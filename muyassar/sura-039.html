Sura 039

Verse 1

تَنْزِيلُ الْكِتَابِ مِنَ اللَّهِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَكِيمِ

The revelation of this Book is from Allah—the Almighty, All-Wise.

The revelation of the Quran is only from Allah, the Almighty in His power and His vengeance, the Wise in His planning and His decrees.

This Book (the Quran) is a revelation from Allah, the Almighty and All-Wise.

Verse 2

إِنَّا أَنْزَلْنَا إِلَيْكَ الْكِتَابَ بِالْحَقِّ فَاعْبُدِ اللَّهَ مُخْلِصًا لَهُ الدِّينَ

Indeed, We have sent down the Book to you ˹O Prophet˺ in truth, so worship Allah ˹alone˺, being sincerely devoted to Him.

Indeed, We have sent down to you - O Messenger - the Quran, commanding truth and justice, so worship Allah alone, and dedicate to Him all your religion.

The Quran has been revealed truthfully, so worship Allah alone with sincere devotion.

Verse 3

أَلَا لِلَّهِ الدِّينُ الْخَالِصُ ۚ وَالَّذِينَ اتَّخَذُوا مِنْ دُونِهِ أَوْلِيَاءَ مَا نَعْبُدُهُمْ إِلَّا لِيُقَرِّبُونَا إِلَى اللَّهِ زُلْفَىٰ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَحْكُمُ بَيْنَهُمْ فِي مَا هُمْ فِيهِ يَخْتَلِفُونَ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِي مَنْ هُوَ كَاذِبٌ كَفَّارٌ

Indeed, sincere devotion is due ˹only˺ to Allah. As for those who take other lords besides Him, ˹saying,˺ “We worship them only so they may bring us closer to Allah,” surely Allah will judge between all regarding what they differed about. Allah certainly does not guide whoever persists in lying and disbelief.

Indeed, to Allah alone belongs the complete obedience, free from polytheism. And those who associate with Allah others and take protectors besides Him say: "We do not worship those deities with Allah except that they may intercede for us with Allah and bring us closer to Him in rank," so they disbelieve in that, because worship and intercession are for Allah alone. Indeed, Allah will judge between the sincere believers and the polytheists with Allah on the Day of Resurrection concerning what they differed about in their worship, and He will recompense each according to what he deserves. Indeed, Allah does not guide to the straight path those who fabricate lies against Allah, and are ungrateful for His signs and proofs.

Sincere devotion is due to Allah alone. Those who take other deities as intercessors are misguided, and Allah will judge their differences. He does not guide those who lie and disbelieve.

Verse 4

لَوْ أَرَادَ اللَّهُ أَنْ يَتَّخِذَ وَلَدًا لَاصْطَفَىٰ مِمَّا يَخْلُقُ مَا يَشَاءُ ۚ سُبْحَانَهُ ۖ هُوَ اللَّهُ الْوَاحِدُ الْقَهَّارُ

Had it been Allah’s Will to have offspring, He could have chosen whatever He willed of His creation. Glory be to Him! He is Allah—the One, the Supreme.

If Allah had desired to take a son, He would have chosen from His creation whatever He willed. Glory be to Allah, and exalted is He above having a son. He is the One, the Unique, the Eternal Refuge, the Irresistible, Who has subjugated His creation with His power, so everything is humble and submissive to Him.

If Allah had willed to have offspring, He could have chosen from His creation. He is the One, Supreme, and above having a son.

Verse 5

خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ بِالْحَقِّ ۖ يُكَوِّرُ اللَّيْلَ عَلَى النَّهَارِ وَيُكَوِّرُ النَّهَارَ عَلَى اللَّيْلِ ۖ وَسَخَّرَ الشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ ۖ كُلٌّ يَجْرِي لِأَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى ۗ أَلَا هُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْغَفَّارُ

He created the heavens and the earth for a purpose. He wraps the night around the day, and wraps the day around the night. And He has subjected the sun and the moon, each orbiting for an appointed term. He is truly the Almighty, Most Forgiving.

Allah created the heavens and the earth and what is in them with truth. He brings the night and takes away the day, and He brings the day and takes away the night, and He has subjected the sun and the moon in an orderly manner for the benefit of His servants. Each runs in its orbit until the establishment of the Hour. Indeed, Allah, Who has done these acts and bestowed these blessings upon His creation, is the Almighty over His creation, the Oft-Forgiving for the sins of His repentant servants.

Allah created the heavens and earth for a purpose. He controls the alternation of night and day, and the sun and moon. He is the Almighty, Oft-Forgiving.

Verse 6

خَلَقَكُمْ مِنْ نَفْسٍ وَاحِدَةٍ ثُمَّ جَعَلَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَأَنْزَلَ لَكُمْ مِنَ الْأَنْعَامِ ثَمَانِيَةَ أَزْوَاجٍ ۚ يَخْلُقُكُمْ فِي بُطُونِ أُمَّهَاتِكُمْ خَلْقًا مِنْ بَعْدِ خَلْقٍ فِي ظُلُمَاتٍ ثَلَاثٍ ۚ ذَٰلِكُمُ اللَّهُ رَبُّكُمْ لَهُ الْمُلْكُ ۖ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ فَأَنَّىٰ تُصْرَفُونَ

He created you ˹all˺ from a single soul, then from it He made its mate. And He produced for you four pairs of cattle. He creates you in the wombs of your mothers ˹in stages˺, one development after another, in three layers of darkness. That is Allah—your Lord! All authority belongs to Him. There is no god ˹worthy of worship˺ except Him. How can you then be turned away?

Your Lord created you - O people - from Adam, and created his spouse from him, and created for you from the livestock eight kinds: male and female of camels, cattle, sheep, and goats. He creates you in the wombs of your mothers, stage after stage of creation, in the darkness of the belly, the womb, and the placenta. That is Allah, who created these things, your Lord, the One in control, the One unique in divinity, the One worthy of worship alone. So how do you turn away from worshipping Him to worship others from His creation?

This verse highlights Allah's creation, starting from one soul (Adam) and then his mate, as well as the creation of livestock. It emphasizes Allah's power in creating humans in stages within the womb and emphasizes that He is the only God deserving of worship. It questions why people turn away from Him.

Verse 7

إِنْ تَكْفُرُوا فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ غَنِيٌّ عَنْكُمْ ۖ وَلَا يَرْضَىٰ لِعِبَادِهِ الْكُفْرَ ۖ وَإِنْ تَشْكُرُوا يَرْضَهُ لَكُمْ ۗ وَلَا تَزِرُ وَازِرَةٌ وِزْرَ أُخْرَىٰ ۗ ثُمَّ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكُمْ مَرْجِعُكُمْ فَيُنَبِّئُكُمْ بِمَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ ۚ إِنَّهُ عَلِيمٌ بِذَاتِ الصُّدُورِ

If you disbelieve, then ˹know that˺ Allah is truly not in need of you, nor does He approve of disbelief from His servants. But if you become grateful ˹through faith˺, He will appreciate that from you. No soul burdened with sin will bear the burden of another. Then to your Lord is your return, and He will inform you of what you used to do. He certainly knows best what is ˹hidden˺ in the heart.

If you disbelieve - O people - in your Lord and do not believe in Him, and do not follow His messengers, then He is free of need of you; He has no need of you, and you are the poor ones in need of Him. And He does not approve of disbelief for His servants, nor does He command them to do so. Rather, He approves of their gratitude for His blessings upon them. And no soul bears the burden of another soul. Then to your Lord is your return, and He will inform you of what you used to do, and hold you accountable for it. Indeed, He is Knowing of the secrets of souls and what the hearts conceal.

This verse explains that Allah is not in need of anyone and does not approve of disbelief. He appreciates gratitude and faith. It also clarifies that no one will bear the burden of another's sins. It reminds people that they will return to Allah, who knows what is hidden in their hearts.

Verse 8

وَإِذَا مَسَّ الْإِنْسَانَ ضُرٌّ دَعَا رَبَّهُ مُنِيبًا إِلَيْهِ ثُمَّ إِذَا خَوَّلَهُ نِعْمَةً مِنْهُ نَسِيَ مَا كَانَ يَدْعُو إِلَيْهِ مِنْ قَبْلُ وَجَعَلَ لِلَّهِ أَنْدَادًا لِيُضِلَّ عَنْ سَبِيلِهِ ۚ قُلْ تَمَتَّعْ بِكُفْرِكَ قَلِيلًا ۖ إِنَّكَ مِنْ أَصْحَابِ النَّارِ

When one is touched with hardship, they cry out to their Lord, turning to Him ˹alone˺. But as soon as He showers them with blessings from Him, they ˹totally˺ forget the One they had cried to earlier, and set up equals to Allah to mislead ˹others˺ from His Way. Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Enjoy your disbelief for a little while! You will certainly be one of the inmates of the Fire.”

And when a person is afflicted with affliction, hardship, and illness, he remembers his Lord, so he seeks His help and supplicates to Him. Then, if He answers him and removes his harm and grants him His blessings, he forgets his supplication to his Lord when he was in need of Him, and he associates others with Him, in order to mislead others away from belief in Allah and obedience to Him. Say to him - O Messenger - threateningly: Enjoy your disbelief for a little while until your death and the end of your term. Indeed, you are among the dwellers of the Fire, abiding therein forever.

This verse describes how people often turn to Allah in times of hardship, but forget Him once they receive blessings. It criticizes setting up equals to Allah and warns that disbelievers will end up in the Fire.

Verse 9

أَمَّنْ هُوَ قَانِتٌ آنَاءَ اللَّيْلِ سَاجِدًا وَقَائِمًا يَحْذَرُ الْآخِرَةَ وَيَرْجُو رَحْمَةَ رَبِّهِ ۗ قُلْ هَلْ يَسْتَوِي الَّذِينَ يَعْلَمُونَ وَالَّذِينَ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ ۗ إِنَّمَا يَتَذَكَّرُ أُولُو الْأَلْبَابِ

˹Are they better˺ or those who worship ˹their Lord˺ devoutly in the hours of the night, prostrating and standing, fearing the Hereafter and hoping for the mercy of their Lord? Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Are those who know equal to those who do not know?” None will be mindful ˹of this˺ except people of reason.

Is this disbeliever, enjoying his disbelief, better, or is the one who is worshipping his Lord, obedient to Him, spending the hours of the night in standing and prostrating to Allah, fearing the punishment of the Hereafter, and hoping for the mercy of his Lord? Say - O Messenger -: Are those who know their Lord and their true religion equal to those who do not know anything of that? They are not equal. Only those with sound minds remember and know the difference.

This verse contrasts those who worship Allah devoutly, fearing the Hereafter and hoping for His mercy, with those who do not. It asks if those who know are equal to those who do not, highlighting the importance of knowledge and understanding.

Verse 10

قُلْ يَا عِبَادِ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اتَّقُوا رَبَّكُمْ ۚ لِلَّذِينَ أَحْسَنُوا فِي هَٰذِهِ الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةٌ ۗ وَأَرْضُ اللَّهِ وَاسِعَةٌ ۗ إِنَّمَا يُوَفَّى الصَّابِرُونَ أَجْرَهُمْ بِغَيْرِ حِسَابٍ

Say ˹O Prophet, that Allah says˺, “O My servants who believe! Be mindful of your Lord. Those who do good in this world will have a good reward. And Allah’s earth is spacious. Only those who endure patiently will be given their reward without limit.”

Say - O Prophet - to My believing servants: Fear your Lord by obeying Him and avoiding disobedience to Him. For those who do good in this world by worshipping their Lord and obeying Him, there is good in the Hereafter, which is Paradise, and good in this world, such as health, sustenance, victory, and other things. And the earth of Allah is vast, so emigrate therein to where you worship your Lord and are able to establish your religion. Indeed, the patient will be given their reward in the Hereafter without limit, count, or measure, and this is a glorification of the reward of the patient.

This verse is a message to believers to be mindful of their Lord and to do good, promising a good reward. It emphasizes that Allah's earth is spacious for those who seek to worship Him, and it highlights the unlimited reward for those who are patient.

Verse 11

قُلْ إِنِّي أُمِرْتُ أَنْ أَعْبُدَ اللَّهَ مُخْلِصًا لَهُ الدِّينَ

Say, “I am commanded to worship Allah, being sincerely devoted to Him ˹alone˺.

Say, [O Prophet], to the people, “Indeed, Allah has commanded me and those who follow me to dedicate worship sincerely to Him alone, without any partner, and He has commanded me to be the first of my nation to submit, so he submitted to Him with monotheism, devoted worship to Him, and disavowed all deities other than Him.”

The Prophet is commanded to worship Allah alone with sincere devotion, setting an example for others.

Verse 12

وَأُمِرْتُ لِأَنْ أَكُونَ أَوَّلَ الْمُسْلِمِينَ

And I am commanded to be the first of those who submit ˹to His Will˺.”

Say, [O Prophet], to the people, “Indeed, Allah has commanded me and those who follow me to dedicate worship sincerely to Him alone, without any partner, and He has commanded me to be the first of my nation to submit, so he submitted to Him with monotheism, devoted worship to Him, and disavowed all deities other than Him.”

The Prophet is also commanded to be the first among his people to submit to Allah's will, showcasing complete surrender.

Verse 13

قُلْ إِنِّي أَخَافُ إِنْ عَصَيْتُ رَبِّي عَذَابَ يَوْمٍ عَظِيمٍ

Say, “I truly fear—if I were to disobey my Lord—the torment of a tremendous Day.”

Say, [O Prophet], to the people, “Indeed, I fear, if I disobey my Lord in what He has commanded me of worship and sincerity in obedience to Him, the punishment of the Day of Resurrection, that Day, the terror of which is immense.”

The Prophet expresses fear of divine punishment on the Day of Judgment if he were to disobey Allah.

Verse 14

قُلِ اللَّهَ أَعْبُدُ مُخْلِصًا لَهُ دِينِي

Say, “It is ˹only˺ Allah that I worship, being sincere in my devotion to Him.

Say, [O Prophet]: “Indeed, I worship Allah alone, without any partners, dedicating my worship and obedience to Him. So you may worship, O polytheists, whatever you wish besides Allah, from idols, statues, and other created things, for that will not harm me at all.” This is a threat and warning to those who worship other than Allah and associate others with Him. Say, [O Prophet]: “Indeed, the true losers are those who lose themselves and their families on the Day of Resurrection, by leading them astray in this world and away from faith. Verily, the loss of these polytheists of themselves and their families on the Day of Resurrection is the clear and evident loss.”

The Prophet declares that he worships Allah alone with sincerity, while others may choose their own paths, emphasizing his commitment.

Verse 15

فَاعْبُدُوا مَا شِئْتُمْ مِنْ دُونِهِ ۗ قُلْ إِنَّ الْخَاسِرِينَ الَّذِينَ خَسِرُوا أَنْفُسَهُمْ وَأَهْلِيهِمْ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ ۗ أَلَا ذَٰلِكَ هُوَ الْخُسْرَانُ الْمُبِينُ

Worship then whatever ˹gods˺ you want instead of Him.” Say, “The ˹true˺ losers are those who will lose themselves and their families on Judgment Day. That is indeed the clearest loss.”

Say, [O Prophet]: “Indeed, I worship Allah alone, without any partners, dedicating my worship and obedience to Him. So you may worship, O polytheists, whatever you wish besides Allah, from idols, statues, and other created things, for that will not harm me at all.” This is a threat and warning to those who worship other than Allah and associate others with Him. Say, [O Prophet]: “Indeed, the true losers are those who lose themselves and their families on the Day of Resurrection, by leading them astray in this world and away from faith. Verily, the loss of these polytheists of themselves and their families on the Day of Resurrection is the clear and evident loss.”

People are warned that those who lose themselves and their families on the Day of Judgment are the true losers, highlighting the severe consequences of rejecting Allah.

Verse 16

لَهُمْ مِنْ فَوْقِهِمْ ظُلَلٌ مِنَ النَّارِ وَمِنْ تَحْتِهِمْ ظُلَلٌ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ يُخَوِّفُ اللَّهُ بِهِ عِبَادَهُ ۚ يَا عِبَادِ فَاتَّقُونِ

They will have layers of fire above and below them. That is what Allah warns His servants with. So fear Me, O My servants!

Those losers will have on the Day of Resurrection in Hell, from above them, layers of torment from the Fire like built shades, and from beneath them as well. That described torment is what Allah warns His servants with, that they might beware of it. O servants, fear Me by fulfilling My commands and avoiding My disobedience.

Disbelievers will be surrounded by fire in Hell as a consequence of their actions, warning all to fear Allah and follow His commands.

Verse 17

وَالَّذِينَ اجْتَنَبُوا الطَّاغُوتَ أَنْ يَعْبُدُوهَا وَأَنَابُوا إِلَى اللَّهِ لَهُمُ الْبُشْرَىٰ ۚ فَبَشِّرْ عِبَادِ

And those who shun the worship of false gods, turning to Allah ˹alone˺, will have good news. So give good news to My servants ˹O Prophet˺—

And those who shun the obedience of Satan and the worship of others than Allah, and repent to Allah by worshiping Him and dedicating religion to Him, for them is glad tidings in the worldly life with good praise and success from Allah, and in the Hereafter, Allah’s pleasure and everlasting bliss in Paradise. So give glad tidings, [O Prophet], to My servants who listen to speech and follow the best of it. The best and most guided speech is the speech of Allah, then the speech of His Messenger. Those are the ones whom Allah has guided to righteousness and rectitude, and has guided them to the best morals and deeds, and those are the people of sound intellect.

Those who shun false gods and turn to Allah will receive good news, both in this life and the Hereafter.

Verse 18

الَّذِينَ يَسْتَمِعُونَ الْقَوْلَ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ أَحْسَنَهُ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ هَدَاهُمُ اللَّهُ ۖ وَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمْ أُولُو الْأَلْبَابِ

those who listen to what is said and follow the best of it. These are the ones ˹rightly˺ guided by Allah, and these are ˹truly˺ the people of reason.

And those who shun the obedience of Satan and the worship of others than Allah, and repent to Allah by worshiping Him and dedicating religion to Him, for them is glad tidings in the worldly life with good praise and success from Allah, and in the Hereafter, Allah’s pleasure and everlasting bliss in Paradise. So give glad tidings, [O Prophet], to My servants who listen to speech and follow the best of it. The best and most guided speech is the speech of Allah, then the speech of His Messenger. Those are the ones whom Allah has guided to righteousness and rectitude, and has guided them to the best morals and deeds, and those are the people of sound intellect.

Those who listen to good advice and follow the best of it are rightly guided by Allah and are considered people of sound intellect.

Verse 19

أَفَمَنْ حَقَّ عَلَيْهِ كَلِمَةُ الْعَذَابِ أَفَأَنْتَ تُنْقِذُ مَنْ فِي النَّارِ

What about those against whom the decree of torment has been justified? Is it you ˹O Prophet˺ who will then save those bound for the Fire?

Then is he against whom the word of punishment has been justified [to be guided], due to his persistence in his error and stubbornness? Then there is no way for you, [O Prophet], to guide him. Can you save those who are in the Fire? You are not capable of that.

Those who are destined for punishment cannot be guided by anyone, highlighting the importance of one's choices.

Verse 20

لَٰكِنِ الَّذِينَ اتَّقَوْا رَبَّهُمْ لَهُمْ غُرَفٌ مِنْ فَوْقِهَا غُرَفٌ مَبْنِيَّةٌ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ ۖ وَعْدَ اللَّهِ ۖ لَا يُخْلِفُ اللَّهُ الْمِيعَادَ

But those mindful of their Lord will have ˹elevated˺ mansions, built one above the other, under which rivers flow. ˹That is˺ the promise of Allah. ˹And˺ Allah never fails in ˹His˺ promise.

But those who fear their Lord - by obeying Him and devoting worship to Him - for them in Paradise are lofty chambers, built one above the other, beneath which rivers flow. Allah has promised it to His righteous servants; a true promise. Allah does not break His promise.

Those who fear their Lord will have lofty mansions in Paradise, which is Allah's promise, and He never breaks His promise.

Verse 21

أَلَمْ تَرَ أَنَّ اللَّهَ أَنْزَلَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً فَسَلَكَهُ يَنَابِيعَ فِي الْأَرْضِ ثُمَّ يُخْرِجُ بِهِ زَرْعًا مُخْتَلِفًا أَلْوَانُهُ ثُمَّ يَهِيجُ فَتَرَاهُ مُصْفَرًّا ثُمَّ يَجْعَلُهُ حُطَامًا ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَذِكْرَىٰ لِأُولِي الْأَلْبَابِ

Do you not see that Allah sends down rain from the sky—channelling it through streams in the earth—then produces with it crops of various colours, then they dry up and you see them wither, and then He reduces them to chaff? Surely in this is a reminder for people of reason.

Do you not see, [O Prophet], that Allah sends down rain from the sky, then causes it to enter the earth, making it springs gushing forth and flowing waters? Then with this water, He brings forth crops of various colors and types. Then it withers after its greenness and freshness, so you see it yellowed in color. Then He turns it into broken, crumbled debris? Indeed, in Allah’s doing that is a reminder and a lesson for those of sound intellect.

Allah’s control over the natural world, from rain to crops, serves as a reminder and lesson for those who use their intellect.

وَسِيقَ الَّذِينَ اتَّقَوْا رَبَّهُمْ إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ زُمَرًا ۖ حَتَّىٰ إِذَا جَاءُوهَا وَفُتِحَتْ أَبْوَابُهَا وَقَالَ لَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَا سَلَامٌ عَلَيْكُمْ طِبْتُمْ فَادْخُلُوهَا خَالِدِينَ

And those who were mindful of their Lord will be led to Paradise in ˹successive˺ groups. When they arrive at its ˹already˺ open gates, its keepers will say, “Peace be upon you! You have done well, so come in, to stay forever.”

And those who feared their Lord by acknowledging His Oneness and acting in obedience to Him will be driven to Paradise in groups, until when they reach it and are given permission to enter, its gates will be opened. The angels in charge of Paradise will welcome them, greeting them with joy and happiness because they are purified from the effects of sins, saying to them: Peace be upon you from all affliction, your state is good, so enter Paradise, to abide therein forever.

Those who were mindful of their Lord will be led to Paradise. The gatekeepers will welcome them with peace and joy, inviting them to enter and reside there eternally.

Verse 74

وَقَالُوا الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي صَدَقَنَا وَعْدَهُ وَأَوْرَثَنَا الْأَرْضَ نَتَبَوَّأُ مِنَ الْجَنَّةِ حَيْثُ نَشَاءُ ۖ فَنِعْمَ أَجْرُ الْعَامِلِينَ

The righteous will say, “Praise be to Allah Who has fulfilled His promise to us, and made us inherit the ˹everlasting˺ land to settle in Paradise wherever we please.” How excellent is the reward of those who work ˹righteousness˺!

And the believers will say: Praise be to Allah, who has fulfilled His promise to us, which He promised us through the tongues of His messengers, and made us inherit the land of Paradise, to settle wherever we wish. How excellent is the reward of the doers of good who strove in obedience to their Lord.

The believers will express gratitude to Allah for fulfilling His promise, granting them Paradise to reside in as they please. The verse highlights the excellent reward for those who performed righteous deeds.

Verse 75

وَتَرَى الْمَلَائِكَةَ حَافِّينَ مِنْ حَوْلِ الْعَرْشِ يُسَبِّحُونَ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّهِمْ ۖ وَقُضِيَ بَيْنَهُمْ بِالْحَقِّ وَقِيلَ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

You will see the angels all around the Throne, glorifying the praises of their Lord, for judgment will have been passed on all with fairness. And it will be said, “Praise be to Allah—Lord of all worlds!”

And you see - O Prophet - the angels surrounding the Throne of the Most Merciful, glorifying their Lord from all that is not befitting Him. And Allah, Glory be to Him, judged between the creatures with truth and justice, so He settled the people of faith in Paradise and the people of disbelief in Hell. And it was said: Praise be to Allah, Lord of the Worlds, for what He has judged between the people of Paradise and the people of Hell, a praise of grace and beneficence, and a praise of justice and wisdom.

This verse describes the scene of the Day of Judgment, where angels glorify Allah around His Throne. After judgment, all will praise Allah as the Lord of all worlds, acknowledging His justice and wisdom.
