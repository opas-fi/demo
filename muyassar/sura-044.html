Verse 1

حم

Ḥâ-Mĩm.

(Ha Mim) The discussion on the disjointed letters at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah has preceded.

This verse begins with the letters 'Ha Mim,' which are among the disjointed letters found at the start of some Quranic chapters. Their precise meaning is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

وَالْكِتَابِ الْمُبِينِ

By the clear Book!

Allah, the Exalted, swore by the clear Quran in word and meaning. We have sent it down on the blessed Night of Decree, which is full of blessings and occurs in Ramadan. We were warners of people with what benefits and harms them, and that is by sending messengers and revealing books, so that Allah's argument is established against His servants. In it, every wise matter is decreed and detailed from the Preserved Tablet to the scribes of the angels, concerning lifespans, sustenance, and other things that happen during that year, which cannot be altered or changed. This wise matter is a command from Us, so all that Allah, the Exalted, causes to happen, and ordains, and all that He inspires, is by His command, permission, and knowledge. We were senders of messengers to people, Muhammad and those before him, as a mercy from your Lord, O Messenger, to those to whom they were sent. Indeed, He is the All-Hearing who hears all voices, and the All-Knowing of all matters of His creation, both apparent and hidden. Creator of the heavens and the earth and all that is between them. If you are certain of this, know that the Lord of creation is its true god. There is no deity worthy of worship except Him alone, without any partners. He gives life and causes death. He is your Lord and the Lord of your forefathers, so worship Him and not your deities who have no power to cause harm or benefit.

Allah swears by the clear Quran, emphasizing its significance as a divine revelation. It was sent down on a blessed night, the Night of Decree (Laylat al-Qadr), in Ramadan. This night is when matters are decreed and the Quran was revealed.
