Sura 101

Verse 1

الْقَارِعَةُ

The Striking Disaster!

The Calamity that strikes people's hearts with its horrors.

This verse speaks of *Al-Qari'ah*, the Striking Disaster, a calamitous event that will shake people's hearts with its terror.

Verse 2

مَا الْقَارِعَةُ

What is the Striking Disaster?

What is the Calamity?

This verse emphasizes the magnitude of *Al-Qari'ah* by asking, 'What is this Striking Disaster?'

Verse 3

وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا الْقَارِعَةُ

And what will make you realize what the Striking Disaster is?

And what will make you know what the Calamity is?

This verse further stresses the enormity of *Al-Qari'ah* by questioning what could make one understand it.

Verse 4

يَوْمَ يَكُونُ النَّاسُ كَالْفَرَاشِ الْمَبْثُوثِ

˹It is˺ the Day people will be like scattered moths,

On that Day, people will be like scattered moths, in their multitude, dispersion, and movement; those who fall into the Fire.

On the Day of *Al-Qari'ah*, people will be like scattered moths, dispersed and disoriented.

Verse 5

وَتَكُونُ الْجِبَالُ كَالْعِهْنِ الْمَنْفُوشِ

and the mountains will be like carded wool.

And the mountains will be like colored wool that is carded by hand, becoming scattered dust and disappearing.

The mountains will be like carded wool, turning into scattered dust and vanishing.

Verse 6

فَأَمَّا مَنْ ثَقُلَتْ مَوَازِينُهُ

So as for those whose scale is heavy ˹with good deeds˺,

Then as for he whose scales of good deeds are heavy, he will be in a pleasing life in Paradise.

Those whose scales of good deeds are heavy will live a life of happiness and bliss.

Verse 7

فَهُوَ فِي عِيشَةٍ رَاضِيَةٍ

they will be in a life of bliss.

Then as for he whose scales of good deeds are heavy, he will be in a pleasing life in Paradise.

This verse reiterates that those with heavy good deeds will be in a pleasing and fulfilling life in Paradise.

Verse 8

وَأَمَّا مَنْ خَفَّتْ مَوَازِينُهُ

And as for those whose scale is light,

But as for he whose scales of good deeds are light, and whose scales of bad deeds are heavy, his dwelling place will be Hell.

Those whose scales of good deeds are light will find their abode in the abyss of Hell.

Verse 9

فَأُمُّهُ هَاوِيَةٌ

their home will be the abyss.

But as for he whose scales of good deeds are light, and whose scales of bad deeds are heavy, his dwelling place will be Hell.

This verse specifies that the final dwelling of those with light good deeds will be in *Hawiyah* (the abyss of Hell).

Verse 10

وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا هِيَهْ

And what will make you realize what that is?

And what will make you know - O Messenger - what this abyss is?

This verse questions what could make one understand the true nature of *Hawiyah*.

Verse 11

نَارٌ حَامِيَةٌ

˹It is˺ a scorching Fire.

It is a Fire that has been intensely heated with fuel.

*Hawiyah* is described as a intensely scorching fire.
