Sura 035

Verse 1

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ فَاطِرِ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ جَاعِلِ الْمَلَائِكَةِ رُسُلًا أُولِي أَجْنِحَةٍ مَثْنَىٰ وَثُلَاثَ وَرُبَاعَ ۚ يَزِيدُ فِي الْخَلْقِ مَا يَشَاءُ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

All praise is for Allah, the Originator of the heavens and the earth, Who made angels ˹as His˺ messengers with wings—two, three, or four. He increases in creation whatever He wills. Surely Allah is Most Capable of everything.

Praise be to Allah, with His attributes, all of which are attributes of perfection, and with His apparent and hidden blessings, both religious and worldly. The Creator of the heavens and the earth and their originator, the One who makes the angels messengers to whomever He wills of His servants, and in whatever He wills of His commands and prohibitions. And from the great power of Allah, He made the angels possess wings, two, three, and four, with which they fly, to convey what Allah has commanded. Allah increases in creation what He wills. Indeed, Allah is over all things competent; nothing is difficult for Him.

This verse begins with praise to Allah, the Creator of the heavens and earth, and describes the angels as messengers with wings, highlighting Allah's unlimited creative power.

Verse 2

مَا يَفْتَحِ اللَّهُ لِلنَّاسِ مِنْ رَحْمَةٍ فَلَا مُمْسِكَ لَهَا ۖ وَمَا يُمْسِكْ فَلَا مُرْسِلَ لَهُ مِنْ بَعْدِهِ ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

Whatever mercy Allah opens up for people, none can withhold it. And whatever He withholds, none but Him can release it. For He is the Almighty, All-Wise.

Whatever Allah opens for people of provision and rain and health and knowledge and other blessings, then no one is able to withhold this mercy. And what He withholds of it, then no one is able to send it after Him, Glory be to Him. And He is the Almighty, the Subduer of all things, the Wise, who sends mercy and withholds it according to His wisdom.

This verse emphasizes Allah's absolute control over mercy. He alone can bestow or withhold it, demonstrating His omnipotence and wisdom.

Verse 3

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ اذْكُرُوا نِعْمَتَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ ۚ هَلْ مِنْ خَالِقٍ غَيْرُ اللَّهِ يَرْزُقُكُمْ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ فَأَنَّىٰ تُؤْفَكُونَ

O humanity! Remember Allah’s favours upon you. Is there any creator other than Allah who provides for you from the heavens and the earth? There is no god ˹worthy of worship˺ except Him. How can you then be deluded ˹from the truth˺?

O mankind, remember the favor of Allah upon you with your hearts, tongues, and limbs. There is no creator for you other than Allah, who provides for you from the sky with rain, and from the earth with water, minerals, and other things. There is no deity worthy of worship except Him alone, without any partner. So how are you turned away from His Oneness and worship?

This verse urges humanity to acknowledge Allah's blessings and His role as the sole Creator and Provider. It questions why they turn away from His worship.

Verse 4

وَإِنْ يُكَذِّبُوكَ فَقَدْ كُذِّبَتْ رُسُلٌ مِنْ قَبْلِكَ ۚ وَإِلَى اللَّهِ تُرْجَعُ الْأُمُورُ

If you are rejected by them, so too were messengers before you. And to Allah ˹all˺ matters will be returned ˹for judgment˺.

And if your people deny you, [O Messenger], then messengers before you were denied. And to Allah return all matters in the Hereafter, and He will recompense each according to what he deserves. And in this is a consolation for the Messenger, may Allah bless him and grant him peace.

This verse consoles the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) by reminding him that previous messengers also faced rejection. It emphasizes that all matters will ultimately return to Allah for judgment.

Verse 5

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ إِنَّ وَعْدَ اللَّهِ حَقٌّ ۖ فَلَا تَغُرَّنَّكُمُ الْحَيَاةُ الدُّنْيَا ۖ وَلَا يَغُرَّنَّكُمْ بِاللَّهِ الْغَرُورُ

O humanity! Indeed, Allah’s promise is true. So do not let the life of this world deceive you, nor let the Chief Deceiver deceive you about Allah.

O mankind, indeed the promise of Allah of resurrection, reward, and punishment is true, so let not the worldly life delude you with its desires and demands, and let not the deceiver deceive you about Allah. Indeed, Satan is an enemy to the children of Adam, so take him as an enemy and do not obey him. He only invites his followers to misguidance, so they may be among the companions of the blazing Fire.

This verse reminds humanity that Allah's promise of the Hereafter is true. It warns against being deceived by the temporary pleasures of this world and the temptations of Satan, 'the Chief Deceiver'.

Verse 6

إِنَّ الشَّيْطَانَ لَكُمْ عَدُوٌّ فَاتَّخِذُوهُ عَدُوًّا ۚ إِنَّمَا يَدْعُو حِزْبَهُ لِيَكُونُوا مِنْ أَصْحَابِ السَّعِيرِ

Surely Satan is an enemy to you, so take him as an enemy. He only invites his followers to become inmates of the Blaze.

O mankind, indeed the promise of Allah of resurrection, reward, and punishment is true, so let not the worldly life delude you with its desires and demands, and let not the deceiver deceive you about Allah. Indeed, Satan is an enemy to the children of Adam, so take him as an enemy and do not obey him. He only invites his followers to misguidance, so they may be among the companions of the blazing Fire.

This verse emphasizes that Satan is a clear enemy to humanity. It warns that his aim is to lead people to the Hellfire.

Verse 7

الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ شَدِيدٌ ۖ وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ لَهُمْ مَغْفِرَةٌ وَأَجْرٌ كَبِيرٌ

Those who disbelieve will have a severe punishment. But those who believe and do good will have forgiveness and a great reward.

Those who denied that Allah is the only true God and denied what His messengers brought will have a severe punishment in the Hereafter, and those who believed in Allah and His Messenger and did righteous deeds will have forgiveness from their Lord and pardon for their sins after concealing them, and they will have a great reward, which is Paradise.

This verse contrasts the fate of disbelievers, who will face severe punishment, with that of believers who do good deeds, who will receive forgiveness and a great reward (Paradise).

Verse 8

أَفَمَنْ زُيِّنَ لَهُ سُوءُ عَمَلِهِ فَرَآهُ حَسَنًا ۖ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ يُضِلُّ مَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيَهْدِي مَنْ يَشَاءُ ۖ فَلَا تَذْهَبْ نَفْسُكَ عَلَيْهِمْ حَسَرَاتٍ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ

Are those whose evil-doing is made so appealing to them that they deem it good ˹like those who are rightly guided˺? ˹It is˺ certainly Allah ˹Who˺ leaves to stray whoever He wills, and guides whoever He wills. So do not grieve yourself to death over them ˹O Prophet˺. Surely Allah is All-Knowing of what they do.

So is he to whom Satan has made pleasing his evil deeds of disobedience to Allah and disbelief and worship of deities and idols other than Him like one whom Allah has guided, so he sees good as good and evil as evil? Indeed, Allah misguides whom He wills of His servants and guides whom He wills, so do not destroy yourself in grief over the disbelief of these misguided ones. Indeed, Allah is All-Knowing of their evils, and He will recompense them for them with the worst of recompense.

This verse highlights how Satan can make evil deeds appealing to some, while Allah guides others. It reminds the Prophet not to grieve excessively over the disbelief of others, as Allah is fully aware of their actions.

Verse 9

وَاللَّهُ الَّذِي أَرْسَلَ الرِّيَاحَ فَتُثِيرُ سَحَابًا فَسُقْنَاهُ إِلَىٰ بَلَدٍ مَيِّتٍ فَأَحْيَيْنَا بِهِ الْأَرْضَ بَعْدَ مَوْتِهَا ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ النُّشُورُ

And it is Allah Who sends the winds, which then stir up ˹vapour, forming˺ clouds, and then We drive them to a lifeless land, giving life to the earth after its death. Similar is the Resurrection.

And Allah is the one who sends the winds, and they stir up clouds, then We drive it to a barren land, and the water descends, and We give life to the earth after its dryness, and it becomes green with vegetation. Similarly, Allah will give life to the dead on the Day of Resurrection.

This verse uses the natural phenomenon of wind, rain, and the revival of dead land as an analogy for the resurrection. Allah, who gives life to the earth, will also give life to the dead.

Verse 10

مَنْ كَانَ يُرِيدُ الْعِزَّةَ فَلِلَّهِ الْعِزَّةُ جَمِيعًا ۚ إِلَيْهِ يَصْعَدُ الْكَلِمُ الطَّيِّبُ وَالْعَمَلُ الصَّالِحُ يَرْفَعُهُ ۚ وَالَّذِينَ يَمْكُرُونَ السَّيِّئَاتِ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ شَدِيدٌ ۖ وَمَكْرُ أُولَٰئِكَ هُوَ يَبُورُ

Whoever seeks honour and power, then ˹let them know that˺ all honour and power belongs to Allah. To Him ˹alone˺ good words ascend, and righteous deeds are raised up by Him. As for those who plot evil, they will suffer a severe punishment. And the plotting of such ˹people˺ is doomed ˹to fail˺.

Whoever seeks honor in this world or the Hereafter, then let him seek it from Allah, and it is not attained except through obedience to Him, for to Allah belongs all honor entirely. So whoever seeks honor from the creation, Allah will humiliate him, and whoever seeks honor from the Creator, Allah will honor him. To Him, Exalted is He, ascends good speech, and righteous action raises it. And those who earn evil deeds will have a severe punishment, and the scheming of those will perish and fail, and it will not benefit them in anything.

This verse states that all honor and power belong to Allah. It emphasizes that good words and righteous actions ascend to Him. Those who plot evil will ultimately fail.

Verse 11

وَاللَّهُ خَلَقَكُمْ مِنْ تُرَابٍ ثُمَّ مِنْ نُطْفَةٍ ثُمَّ جَعَلَكُمْ أَزْوَاجًا ۚ وَمَا تَحْمِلُ مِنْ أُنْثَىٰ وَلَا تَضَعُ إِلَّا بِعِلْمِهِ ۚ وَمَا يُعَمَّرُ مِنْ مُعَمَّرٍ وَلَا يُنْقَصُ مِنْ عُمُرِهِ إِلَّا فِي كِتَابٍ ۚ إِنَّ ذَٰلِكَ عَلَى اللَّهِ يَسِيرٌ

And ˹it is˺ Allah ˹Who˺ created you from dust, then ˹developed you˺ from a sperm-drop, then made you into pairs. No female ever conceives or delivers without His knowledge. And no one’s life is made long or cut short but is ˹written˺ in a Record. That is certainly easy for Allah.

And Allah created your father Adam from dust, then made his progeny from a sperm-drop, then made you men and women. And no female conceives or gives birth except with His knowledge, and no one is granted long life, so his life is lengthened, nor is his life shortened, except it is in a record with Him, which is the Preserved Tablet, before his mother conceives him and before she gives birth to him. Allah has indeed counted all of that and knew it before He created it. Nothing is added to or subtracted from what is written for him. Indeed, your creation and knowledge of your states and recording of them in the Preserved Tablet are easy and simple for Allah.

This verse reminds us that Allah created humans from dust and a sperm-drop, and that every aspect of life, including conception, birth, and lifespan, is under His knowledge and control, all of which is recorded in the Preserved Tablet.

Verse 12

وَمَا يَسْتَوِي الْبَحْرَانِ هَٰذَا عَذْبٌ فُرَاتٌ سَائِغٌ شَرَابُهُ وَهَٰذَا مِلْحٌ أُجَاجٌ ۖ وَمِنْ كُلٍّ تَأْكُلُونَ لَحْمًا طَرِيًّا وَتَسْتَخْرِجُونَ حِلْيَةً تَلْبَسُونَهَا ۖ وَتَرَى الْفُلْكَ فِيهِ مَوَاخِرَ لِتَبْتَغُوا مِنْ فَضْلِهِ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ

The two bodies of water are not alike: one is fresh, palatable, and pleasant to drink and the other is salty and bitter. Yet from them both you eat tender seafood and extract ornaments to wear. And you see the ships ploughing their way through both, so you may seek His bounty and give thanks ˹to Him˺.

And the two seas are not alike: this one is fresh, sweet, easy to swallow, and quenches thirst, while that one is salty, very salty. And from each you eat tender fish, and extract ornaments, such as pearls and coral, which you wear. And you see the ships plowing through the water, that you may seek of His bounty through trade and otherwise. And in this is evidence of the power of Allah and His Oneness; and perhaps you will be grateful to Allah for these blessings that He has bestowed upon you.

This verse contrasts two types of water: fresh and salty. It highlights Allah's blessings by mentioning seafood and ornaments extracted from both, and the ships that sail them, encouraging gratitude.

Verse 13

يُولِجُ اللَّيْلَ فِي النَّهَارِ وَيُولِجُ النَّهَارَ فِي اللَّيْلِ وَسَخَّرَ الشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ كُلٌّ يَجْرِي لِأَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى ۚ ذَٰلِكُمُ اللَّهُ رَبُّكُمْ لَهُ الْمُلْكُ ۚ وَالَّذِينَ تَدْعُونَ مِنْ دُونِهِ مَا يَمْلِكُونَ مِنْ قِطْمِيرٍ

He merges the night into the day and the day into the night, and has subjected the sun and the moon, each orbiting for an appointed term. That is Allah—your Lord! All authority belongs to Him. But those ˹idols˺ you ˹pagans˺ invoke besides Him do not possess even the skin of a date stone.

And Allah causes the night to enter the day, increasing the day by what is taken from the night, and He causes the day to enter the night, increasing the night by what is taken from the day. And He has subjected the sun and the moon, each running [its course] for a specified term. That is Allah, your Lord; to Him belongs all dominion. And those whom you invoke other than Allah do not possess [as much as] a membrane of a date seed, which is the thin white shell that is on the date pit.

Allah controls the alternation of night and day and the movements of the sun and moon, which orbit according to His design. The verse emphasizes Allah's absolute authority, contrasting it with the powerlessness of idols.

Verse 14

إِنْ تَدْعُوهُمْ لَا يَسْمَعُوا دُعَاءَكُمْ وَلَوْ سَمِعُوا مَا اسْتَجَابُوا لَكُمْ ۖ وَيَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ يَكْفُرُونَ بِشِرْكِكُمْ ۚ وَلَا يُنَبِّئُكَ مِثْلُ خَبِيرٍ

If you call upon them, they cannot hear your calls. And if they were to hear, they could not respond to you. On the Day of Judgment they will disown your worship ˹of them˺. And no one can inform you ˹O Prophet˺ like the All-Knowledgeable.

If you invoke - O people - these deities other than Allah, they do not hear your supplication. And if they were to hear, hypothetically, they would not respond to you. And on the Day of Resurrection, they will disown you. And no one can inform you, O Messenger, as truthfully as Allah, the All-Knowing, the All-Aware.

Idols cannot hear or respond to prayers, and will disown their worshipers on the Day of Judgment. Only Allah has true knowledge, contrasting with the ignorance of idols.

Verse 15

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ أَنْتُمُ الْفُقَرَاءُ إِلَى اللَّهِ ۖ وَاللَّهُ هُوَ الْغَنِيُّ الْحَمِيدُ

O humanity! It is you who stand in need of Allah, but Allah ˹alone˺ is the Self-Sufficient, Praiseworthy.

O people, you are those in need of Allah in everything; you cannot do without Him for even the blink of an eye, and He, Glory be to Him, is the Self-Sufficient, free of need of people and of all His creation, the Praiseworthy in His essence, names, and attributes, the Praised for His blessings; for every blessing to people is from Him, so to Him belongs praise and gratitude in every situation.

All of humanity is in need of Allah's support, while He is self-sufficient and worthy of all praise. This verse emphasizes humanity's dependence on God.

Verse 16

إِنْ يَشَأْ يُذْهِبْكُمْ وَيَأْتِ بِخَلْقٍ جَدِيدٍ

If He willed, He could eliminate you and produce a new creation.

If Allah wills, He can destroy you, O people, and bring about another people who will obey Him and worship Him alone.

Allah has the power to replace humanity with a new creation. This illustrates His omnipotence and control over existence.

Verse 17

وَمَا ذَٰلِكَ عَلَى اللَّهِ بِعَزِيزٍ

And that is not difficult for Allah ˹at all˺.

And your destruction and the bringing of another creation is not beyond the power of Allah; rather, that is easy and simple for Allah.

The creation and destruction of worlds is easy for Allah, reinforcing His unlimited power.

Verse 18

وَلَا تَزِرُ وَازِرَةٌ وِزْرَ أُخْرَىٰ ۚ وَإِنْ تَدْعُ مُثْقَلَةٌ إِلَىٰ حِمْلِهَا لَا يُحْمَلْ مِنْهُ شَيْءٌ وَلَوْ كَانَ ذَا قُرْبَىٰ ۗ إِنَّمَا تُنْذِرُ الَّذِينَ يَخْشَوْنَ رَبَّهُمْ بِالْغَيْبِ وَأَقَامُوا الصَّلَاةَ ۚ وَمَنْ تَزَكَّىٰ فَإِنَّمَا يَتَزَكَّىٰ لِنَفْسِهِ ۚ وَإِلَى اللَّهِ الْمَصِيرُ

No soul burdened with sin will bear the burden of another. And if a sin-burdened soul cries for help with its burden, none of it will be carried—even by a close relative. You ˹O Prophet˺ can only warn those who stand in awe of their Lord without seeing Him and establish prayer. Whoever purifies themselves, they only do so for their own good. And to Allah is the final return.

And no burdened soul will bear the burden of another. And if a soul heavily laden with sins asks someone to carry some of its sins for it, it will not find anyone to carry anything for it, even if the one it asked were a close relative, a father, a brother, or someone similar. You only warn, O Messenger, those who fear their Lord in the unseen and perform prayer properly. And whoever purifies himself from polytheism and other sins does so for his own sake. And to Allah is the final return and destination of all creatures; He will recompense everyone according to what they deserve.

No one will bear the burden of another's sins, even relatives. The Prophet's role is to warn those who fear Allah and establish prayer. Purification of the soul is for one's own benefit, and all will return to Allah for judgment.

Verse 19

وَمَا يَسْتَوِي الْأَعْمَىٰ وَالْبَصِيرُ

Those blind ˹to the truth˺ and those who can see are not equal,

The blind to the religion of Allah are not equal to the seeing who have perceived the path of truth and followed it. Nor are the darknesses of disbelief equal to the light of faith, nor the shade to the hot wind, nor are the living hearts through faith equal to the dead hearts through disbelief. Indeed, Allah makes hear whom He wills, hearing of understanding and acceptance. And you, [O Messenger], cannot make those in the graves hear, for just as you cannot make the dead in their graves hear, so too you cannot make these disbelievers hear due to the death of their hearts. You are but a warner to them of the wrath of Allah and His punishment. Indeed, We have sent you with the truth, which is faith in Allah and the laws of religion, as a bringer of good tidings of Paradise for those who believe in you and act upon your guidance, and as a warner of Hellfire for those who deny you and disobey you. And there is no nation from among the nations except that a warner came to them warning them of the consequence of their disbelief and misguidance.

This verse highlights that those who are blind to the truth are not equal to those who can see it. It emphasizes the difference between disbelief and faith.

Verse 22

أَوَلَمْ يَسِيرُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ فَيَنْظُرُوا كَيْفَ كَانَ عَاقِبَةُ الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ وَكَانُوا أَشَدَّ مِنْهُمْ قُوَّةً ۚ وَمَا كَانَ اللَّهُ لِيُعْجِزَهُ مِنْ شَيْءٍ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَلَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۚ إِنَّهُ كَانَ عَلِيمًا قَدِيرًا

Have they not travelled throughout the land to see what was the end of those ˹destroyed˺ before them? They were far superior in might. But there is nothing that can escape Allah in the heavens or the earth. He is certainly All-Knowing, Most Capable.

Have the disbelievers of "Mecca" not travelled through the land and observed how was the end of those before them, like 'Aad and Thamud and their like, and what came upon them of destruction and upon their homes of ruin, when they denied the messengers? And those disbelievers were greater in strength and might than the disbelievers of "Mecca." And Allah is not such that anything in the heavens or in the earth could escape or overcome Him. Indeed, He is Knowing of their actions, Powerful to destroy them.

People are encouraged to reflect on the fate of those destroyed before them, who were stronger. Nothing escapes Allah's knowledge or power in the heavens or earth. He is All-Knowing and Most Capable.

Verse 45

وَلَوْ يُؤَاخِذُ اللَّهُ النَّاسَ بِمَا كَسَبُوا مَا تَرَكَ عَلَىٰ ظَهْرِهَا مِنْ دَابَّةٍ وَلَٰكِنْ يُؤَخِّرُهُمْ إِلَىٰ أَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى ۖ فَإِذَا جَاءَ أَجَلُهُمْ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ كَانَ بِعِبَادِهِ بَصِيرًا

If Allah were to punish people ˹immediately˺ for what they have committed, He would not have left a single living being on earth. But He delays them for an appointed term. And when their time arrives, then surely Allah is All-Seeing of His servants.

And if Allah were to punish the people for what they have earned of sins and transgressions, He would not leave upon the earth any creature that moves upon it. But He delays them, and postpones their punishment to a time known to Him. So when their punishment comes, then Allah is ever of His servants Seeing. Nothing of them is hidden from Him, nor does anything of their affairs escape His knowledge, and He will recompense them for what they have done of good or evil.

This verse explains that Allah's mercy is why He doesn't immediately punish people for their sins. He gives them time until their appointed term, when He will judge them.
