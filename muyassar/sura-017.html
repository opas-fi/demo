Sura 017

Verse 1

سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِ لَيْلًا مِنَ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ إِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ الْأَقْصَى الَّذِي بَارَكْنَا حَوْلَهُ لِنُرِيَهُ مِنْ آيَاتِنَا ۚ إِنَّهُ هُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ

Glory be to the One Who took His servant ˹Muḥammad˺ by night from the Sacred Mosque to the Farthest Mosque whose surroundings We have blessed, so that We may show him some of Our signs. Indeed, He alone is the All-Hearing, All-Seeing.

Allah glorifies Himself and magnifies His status, due to His ability to do what no one else is capable of. There is no god but Him, and no lord besides Him. He is the One Who took His servant Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, on a journey by night with his body and soul, while awake, not in a dream, from the Sacred Mosque in "Mecca" to the Farthest Mosque in "Jerusalem", which Allah has blessed its surroundings with crops, fruits and other things, and made it a place for many prophets, to see the wonders of Allah's power and evidence of His oneness. Indeed, Allah, the Exalted, is the Hearer of all sounds, the Seer of everything seen. He gives each what he deserves in this world and the Hereafter.

This verse refers to the miraculous Night Journey of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) from the Sacred Mosque in Mecca to the Farthest Mosque in Jerusalem. It highlights Allah's power and shows that He is All-Hearing and All-Seeing.

Verse 2

وَآتَيْنَا مُوسَى الْكِتَابَ وَجَعَلْنَاهُ هُدًى لِبَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ أَلَّا تَتَّخِذُوا مِنْ دُونِي وَكِيلًا

And We gave Moses the Scripture and made it a guide for the Children of Israel, ˹stating:˺ “Do not take besides Me any other Trustee of Affairs,

And just as Allah honored Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, with the Night Journey, He honored Moses, peace be upon him, by giving him the Torah, and made it a clear statement of the truth and guidance for the Children of Israel, including their prohibition from taking anyone other than Allah as a guardian or god to whom they entrust their affairs.

Similar to the honor given to Prophet Muhammad, Prophet Moses was given the Scripture (Torah) as a guide for the Children of Israel. It emphasizes that they should not take any guardian or god other than Allah.

Verse 3

ذُرِّيَّةَ مَنْ حَمَلْنَا مَعَ نُوحٍ ۚ إِنَّهُ كَانَ عَبْدًا شَكُورًا

˹O˺ descendants of those We carried with Noah ˹in the Ark˺! He was indeed a grateful servant.”

O descendants of those whom We saved and carried with Noah in the Ark, do not associate anything with Allah in His worship, and be grateful for His blessings, following the example of Noah, peace be upon him; indeed, he was a grateful servant to Allah with his heart, tongue, and limbs.

This verse reminds the descendants of those who were saved with Prophet Noah to be grateful to Allah and to worship Him alone, just like Noah who was a very thankful servant.

Verse 4

وَقَضَيْنَا إِلَىٰ بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ فِي الْكِتَابِ لَتُفْسِدُنَّ فِي الْأَرْضِ مَرَّتَيْنِ وَلَتَعْلُنَّ عُلُوًّا كَبِيرًا

And We warned the Children of Israel in the Scripture, “You will certainly cause corruption in the land twice, and you will become extremely arrogant.

And We informed the Children of Israel in the Torah that was revealed to them that they must commit corruption twice in "Jerusalem" and its vicinity by injustice, killing prophets, arrogance, tyranny, and aggression.

The Children of Israel were warned that they would commit great acts of corruption in Jerusalem twice and act with extreme arrogance.

Verse 5

فَإِذَا جَاءَ وَعْدُ أُولَاهُمَا بَعَثْنَا عَلَيْكُمْ عِبَادًا لَنَا أُولِي بَأْسٍ شَدِيدٍ فَجَاسُوا خِلَالَ الدِّيَارِ ۚ وَكَانَ وَعْدًا مَفْعُولًا

“When the first of the two warnings would come to pass, We would send against you some of Our servants of great might, who would ravage your homes. This would be a warning fulfilled.

So when they commit the first corruption, We will send against them servants of Ours, of great courage and intense power, who will overcome them, kill them, and scatter them, so they will roam through their homes as corrupters, and that was a promise that must come to pass, due to the existence of its cause from them.

When the first act of corruption occurred, Allah sent powerful servants against the Children of Israel to punish them and ravage their homes as a fulfillment of the warning.

Verse 6

ثُمَّ رَدَدْنَا لَكُمُ الْكَرَّةَ عَلَيْهِمْ وَأَمْدَدْنَاكُمْ بِأَمْوَالٍ وَبَنِينَ وَجَعَلْنَاكُمْ أَكْثَرَ نَفِيرًا

Then ˹after your repentance˺ We would give you the upper hand over them and aid you with wealth and offspring, causing you to outnumber them.

Then We restored to you - O Children of Israel - the victory and dominance over your enemies who were sent against you, and We increased your provisions and your children, and We strengthened you and made you more numerous than your enemy; and that was because of your good deeds and submission to Allah.

After their repentance, Allah gave the Children of Israel victory over their enemies and increased their wealth and numbers as a result of their good deeds and submission to Allah.

Verse 7

إِنْ أَحْسَنْتُمْ أَحْسَنْتُمْ لِأَنْفُسِكُمْ ۖ وَإِنْ أَسَأْتُمْ فَلَهَا ۚ فَإِذَا جَاءَ وَعْدُ الْآخِرَةِ لِيَسُوءُوا وُجُوهَكُمْ وَلِيَدْخُلُوا الْمَسْجِدَ كَمَا دَخَلُوهُ أَوَّلَ مَرَّةٍ وَلِيُتَبِّرُوا مَا عَلَوْا تَتْبِيرًا

If you act rightly, it is for your own good, but if you do wrong, it is to your own loss. And when the second warning would come to pass, your enemies would ˹be left to˺ totally disgrace you and enter the Temple ˹of Jerusalem˺ as they entered it the first time, and utterly destroy whatever would fall into their hands.

If you do good in your deeds and words, then you have done good for yourselves, because the reward for that will return to you, and if you do evil, then the punishment for that will return upon you, so when the time for the second corruption comes, We will send against you your enemies again, to humiliate and defeat you, and the signs of disgrace and humiliation will appear on your faces, and so they enter "Jerusalem" and destroy it, as they destroyed it the first time, and to destroy everything that falls under their hands with complete destruction.

This verse states that good actions benefit the doer while evil harms them. When the second act of corruption occurred, Allah sent enemies to humiliate them, enter the Temple in Jerusalem, and utterly destroy everything.

Verse 8

عَسَىٰ رَبُّكُمْ أَنْ يَرْحَمَكُمْ ۚ وَإِنْ عُدْتُمْ عُدْنَا ۘ وَجَعَلْنَا جَهَنَّمَ لِلْكَافِرِينَ حَصِيرًا

Perhaps your Lord will have mercy on you ˹if you repent˺, but if you return ˹to sin˺, We will return ˹to punishment˺. And We have made Hell a ˹permanent˺ confinement for the disbelievers.”

It may be that your Lord - O Children of Israel - will have mercy on you after His retribution if you repent and reform. And if you return to corruption and injustice, We will return to your punishment and humiliation. And We have made Hell a prison for you and for the disbelievers in general, from which there is no escape forever. And in this verse and what preceded it, there is a warning to this nation against committing sins, lest it suffer the same fate as the Children of Israel, for the laws of Allah are one and do not change or alter.

This verse suggests that Allah may show mercy to those who repent, but if they return to sin, punishment will follow. Hell is described as a permanent confinement for disbelievers, emphasizing the consequence of rejecting faith.

Verse 9

إِنَّ هَٰذَا الْقُرْآنَ يَهْدِي لِلَّتِي هِيَ أَقْوَمُ وَيُبَشِّرُ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ الَّذِينَ يَعْمَلُونَ الصَّالِحَاتِ أَنَّ لَهُمْ أَجْرًا كَبِيرًا

Surely this Quran guides to what is most upright, and gives good news to the believers—who do good—that they will have a mighty reward.

Indeed, this Quran that We have revealed to Our servant Muhammad guides people to the best ways, which is the religion of Islam, and gives good tidings to the believers who act upon what Allah has commanded them and refrain from what He has forbidden them, that they will have a great reward. And those who do not believe in the Hereafter and what is in it of recompense, We have prepared for them a painful punishment in the Fire.

The Quran is presented as a guide to the best path, promising believers who do good deeds a great reward.

Verse 10

وَأَنَّ الَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِالْآخِرَةِ أَعْتَدْنَا لَهُمْ عَذَابًا أَلِيمًا

And ˹it warns˺ those who do not believe in the Hereafter ˹that˺ We have prepared for them a painful punishment.

Indeed, this Quran that We have revealed to Our servant Muhammad guides people to the best ways, which is the religion of Islam, and gives good tidings to the believers who act upon what Allah has commanded them and refrain from what He has forbidden them, that they will have a great reward. And those who do not believe in the Hereafter and what is in it of recompense, We have prepared for them a painful punishment in the Fire.

This verse warns those who disbelieve in the Hereafter of a painful punishment prepared for them.

Verse 11

وَيَدْعُ الْإِنْسَانُ بِالشَّرِّ دُعَاءَهُ بِالْخَيْرِ ۖ وَكَانَ الْإِنْسَانُ عَجُولًا

And humans ˹swiftly˺ pray for evil as they pray for good. For humankind is ever hasty.

And man sometimes calls down evil upon himself or his child or his wealth, and that is when angry, just as he calls down good, and this is from man's ignorance and haste. And from Allah's mercy upon him is that He answers him in his supplication for good and not evil, because He knows that he does not intend to desire that. And man is by nature hasty.

Humans sometimes impulsively pray for evil as they pray for good, due to their haste. Allah, in His mercy, often does not grant those ill-intentioned supplications, showcasing His wisdom.

Verse 12

وَجَعَلْنَا اللَّيْلَ وَالنَّهَارَ آيَتَيْنِ ۖ فَمَحَوْنَا آيَةَ اللَّيْلِ وَجَعَلْنَا آيَةَ النَّهَارِ مُبْصِرَةً لِتَبْتَغُوا فَضْلًا مِنْ رَبِّكُمْ وَلِتَعْلَمُوا عَدَدَ السِّنِينَ وَالْحِسَابَ ۚ وَكُلَّ شَيْءٍ فَصَّلْنَاهُ تَفْصِيلًا

We made the day and night as two signs. So We made the sign of the night devoid of light, and We made the sign of the day ˹perfectly˺ bright, so that you may seek the bounty of your Lord and know the number of years and calculation ˹of time˺. And We have explained everything in detail.

And We have made the night and the day two signs indicating Our Oneness and Our Power. So We have obliterated the sign of the night - which is the moon - and We have made the sign of the day - which is the sun - luminous so that man may see in the light of the day how to conduct his affairs of livelihood, and relax at night for rest. And so that people may know - from the alternation of night and day - the number of years and the count of months and days, and thus organize their interests according to it. And We have explained everything with sufficient clarity.

The day and night are described as two signs of Allah's power. The day is made bright for seeking livelihood, and the night is for rest. The alternation of day and night helps people calculate time. Allah has made these things clear for mankind.

Verse 13

وَكُلَّ إِنْسَانٍ أَلْزَمْنَاهُ طَائِرَهُ فِي عُنُقِهِ ۖ وَنُخْرِجُ لَهُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ كِتَابًا يَلْقَاهُ مَنْشُورًا

We have bound every human’s destiny to their neck. And on the Day of Judgment We will bring forth to each ˹person˺ a record which they will find laid open.

And every human being, Allah makes his actions, good or evil, accompany him. So, one is not held accountable for the actions of another, nor is another held accountable for their actions. And Allah will bring out for him on the Day of Resurrection a book in which his actions have been recorded, which he will see open.

Each person's actions are tied to them and they will be presented with a record of their deeds on the Day of Judgment.

Verse 14

اقْرَأْ كِتَابَكَ كَفَىٰ بِنَفْسِكَ الْيَوْمَ عَلَيْكَ حَسِيبًا

˹And it will be said,˺ “Read your record. You ˹alone˺ are sufficient this Day to take account of yourself.”

It will be said to him: 'Read your book of deeds.' So he reads it, even if he did not know how to read in this world. 'Your own soul is sufficient as a reckoner against you this Day,' so you will know what recompense is due for you. And this is of the greatest justice and fairness that it is said to the servant: 'Judge yourself; your soul is sufficient to account for you.'

On the Day of Judgement, individuals will be asked to read their record of deeds, and they alone will be sufficient to take account of themselves.

Verse 15

مَنِ اهْتَدَىٰ فَإِنَّمَا يَهْتَدِي لِنَفْسِهِ ۖ وَمَنْ ضَلَّ فَإِنَّمَا يَضِلُّ عَلَيْهَا ۚ وَلَا تَزِرُ وَازِرَةٌ وِزْرَ أُخْرَىٰ ۗ وَمَا كُنَّا مُعَذِّبِينَ حَتَّىٰ نَبْعَثَ رَسُولًا

Whoever chooses to be guided, it is only for their own good. And whoever chooses to stray, it is only to their own loss. No soul burdened with sin will bear the burden of another. And We would never punish ˹a people˺ until We have sent a messenger ˹to warn them˺.

Whoever is guided and follows the path of truth, then the reward for that is for himself alone. And whoever deviates and follows the path of falsehood, then the punishment for that is for himself alone. And no sinful soul shall bear the sin of another sinful soul. And Allah does not punish anyone except after establishing the proof against them by sending messengers and revealing books.

Guidance is for the benefit of the one who chooses it, and straying is to their own loss. No one bears the burden of another's sins, and Allah does not punish a people until a messenger has been sent to them.

Verse 16

وَإِذَا أَرَدْنَا أَنْ نُهْلِكَ قَرْيَةً أَمَرْنَا مُتْرَفِيهَا فَفَسَقُوا فِيهَا فَحَقَّ عَلَيْهَا الْقَوْلُ فَدَمَّرْنَاهَا تَدْمِيرًا

Whenever We intend to destroy a society, We command its elite ˹to obey Allah˺ but they act rebelliously in it. So the decree ˹of punishment˺ is justified, and We destroy it utterly.

And when We intend to destroy the people of a town for their injustice, We command those of them who are given to luxury to obey Allah, to believe in His Oneness and to believe His messengers, and others follow them. But they disobey the command of their Lord and deny His messengers, so the word of punishment which cannot be averted, becomes due on them, and We utterly destroy them.

When Allah intends to destroy a society, He first commands its leaders to obey Him, but when they disobey, the decree of punishment becomes justified. This highlights the responsibility of leaders.

Verse 17

وَكَمْ أَهْلَكْنَا مِنَ الْقُرُونِ مِنْ بَعْدِ نُوحٍ ۗ وَكَفَىٰ بِرَبِّكَ بِذُنُوبِ عِبَادِهِ خَبِيرًا بَصِيرًا

˹Imagine˺ how many peoples We have destroyed after Noah! And sufficient is your Lord as All-Aware and All-Seeing of the sins of His servants.

And how many generations have We destroyed after the time of the Prophet Noah. And sufficient is your Lord - O Messenger - as One who is aware of all the actions of His servants, nothing is hidden from Him.

Many generations have been destroyed after Noah's time, and Allah is All-Aware and All-Seeing of His servants' sins. This emphasizes that no action escapes His knowledge.

Verse 18

مَنْ كَانَ يُرِيدُ الْعَاجِلَةَ عَجَّلْنَا لَهُ فِيهَا مَا نَشَاءُ لِمَنْ نُرِيدُ ثُمَّ جَعَلْنَا لَهُ جَهَنَّمَ يَصْلَاهَا مَذْمُومًا مَدْحُورًا

Whoever desires this fleeting world ˹alone˺, We hasten in it whatever We please to whoever We will; then We destine them for Hell, where they will burn, condemned and rejected.

Whoever seeks the immediate worldly life and strives for it alone, and does not believe in the Hereafter, nor works for it, Allah hastens for him in it whatever Allah wills and desires of what He has written for him in the Preserved Tablet, then Allah makes for him in the Hereafter Hell, which he enters, blamed and expelled from His mercy, may He be exalted; that is because of his desire for the world and his striving for it without the Hereafter.

Those who focus solely on worldly life will receive what Allah wills, but their ultimate destiny is Hell. This highlights the consequences of prioritizing the temporal over the eternal.

Verse 19

وَمَنْ أَرَادَ الْآخِرَةَ وَسَعَىٰ لَهَا سَعْيَهَا وَهُوَ مُؤْمِنٌ فَأُولَٰئِكَ كَانَ سَعْيُهُمْ مَشْكُورًا

But whoever desires the Hereafter and strives for it accordingly, and is a ˹true˺ believer, it is they whose striving will be appreciated.

And whoever intends with his righteous deed the reward of the Hereafter, which is lasting, and strives for it by obeying Allah Almighty, while believing in Allah and His reward and His great recompense, then those, their deeds are accepted and stored for them with their Lord, and they will be rewarded for it.

Those who strive for the Hereafter with true faith, their efforts will be acknowledged and rewarded by Allah. This emphasizes the value of focusing on eternal rewards.

Verse 20

كُلًّا نُمِدُّ هَٰؤُلَاءِ وَهَٰؤُلَاءِ مِنْ عَطَاءِ رَبِّكَ ۚ وَمَا كَانَ عَطَاءُ رَبِّكَ مَحْظُورًا

We provide both the former and the latter from the bounty of your Lord. And the bounty of your Lord can never be withheld.

Each group of those who work for the fleeting world, and those who work for the lasting Hereafter, We increase from Our provision, so We provide for the believers and the disbelievers in this world; for provision is from the gift of your Lord, as a favor from Him, and the gift of your Lord was never withheld from anyone, whether believer or disbeliever.

Allah provides for both those who seek worldly gains and those who strive for the Hereafter. This emphasizes that Allah's bounty is available to all, and is not limited to believers.

Verse 21

انْظُرْ كَيْفَ فَضَّلْنَا بَعْضَهُمْ عَلَىٰ بَعْضٍ ۚ وَلَلْآخِرَةُ أَكْبَرُ دَرَجَاتٍ وَأَكْبَرُ تَفْضِيلًا

See how We have favoured some over others ˹in this life˺, but the Hereafter is certainly far greater in rank and in favour.

Reflect, O Messenger, on how Allah has favored some people over others in this world in provision and work, and the Hereafter is greater in degrees for the believers and greater in preference.

Allah's favors in this world vary, but the Hereafter surpasses this world in status and benefits. This encourages believers to focus on the lasting rewards of the afterlife.

Verse 22

لَا تَجْعَلْ مَعَ اللَّهِ إِلَٰهًا آخَرَ فَتَقْعُدَ مَذْمُومًا مَخْذُولًا

Do not set up any other god with Allah, or you will end up condemned, abandoned.

Do not make, O human, any partner with Allah in His worship, lest you bring upon yourself blame and disappointment.

Do not associate any partners with Allah in worship. This will lead to condemnation and abandonment.

Verse 23

وَقَضَىٰ رَبُّكَ أَلَّا تَعْبُدُوا إِلَّا إِيَّاهُ وَبِالْوَالِدَيْنِ إِحْسَانًا ۚ إِمَّا يَبْلُغَنَّ عِنْدَكَ الْكِبَرَ أَحَدُهُمَا أَوْ كِلَاهُمَا فَلَا تَقُلْ لَهُمَا أُفٍّ وَلَا تَنْهَرْهُمَا وَقُلْ لَهُمَا قَوْلًا كَرِيمًا

For your Lord has decreed that you worship none but Him. And honour your parents. If one or both of them reach old age in your care, never say to them ˹even˺ ‘ugh,’ nor yell at them. Rather, address them respectfully.

And your Lord has commanded, O human, obligated, and made it binding that He alone, may He be glorified and exalted, be singled out for worship, and He commanded kindness to the father and mother, especially in their old age, so do not become annoyed or consider anything you see from one of them or both of them burdensome, nor let them hear any bad words from you, not even the 'uff' which is the lowest degree of bad words, and do not let any ugly action come from you towards them, but be gentle with them, and always say to them a soft and gentle word.

Allah commands the worship of Him alone and kindness to parents, especially in old age. Treat them with respect and gentleness, avoiding even the slightest annoyance.

Verse 24

وَاخْفِضْ لَهُمَا جَنَاحَ الذُّلِّ مِنَ الرَّحْمَةِ وَقُلْ رَبِّ ارْحَمْهُمَا كَمَا رَبَّيَانِي صَغِيرًا

And be humble with them out of mercy, and pray, “My Lord! Be merciful to them as they raised me when I was young.”

And be humble and submissive to your mother and father out of mercy towards them, and ask your Lord to have mercy on them with His vast mercy, both alive and dead, as they were patient in raising you as a weak child, without strength or power.

Be humble towards your parents and pray for Allah's mercy upon them, just as they were merciful in raising you as a child. This emphasizes the importance of gratitude and humility towards parents.

Verse 25

رَبُّكُمْ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا فِي نُفُوسِكُمْ ۚ إِنْ تَكُونُوا صَالِحِينَ فَإِنَّهُ كَانَ لِلْأَوَّابِينَ غَفُورًا

Your Lord knows best what is within yourselves. If you are righteous, He is certainly All-Forgiving to those who ˹constantly˺ turn to Him.

Your Lord, O people, knows best what is in your hearts of good and evil. If your intentions and purposes are to please Allah and what draws you closer to Him, then He is, glory be to Him, Forgiving to those who return to Him at all times, so whoever Allah knows that there is in his heart nothing but turning to Him and loving Him, then He will forgive him and pardon him for what may appear of minor sins, which are part of human nature.

Allah knows what is in your hearts. If you are righteous and turn to Him, He is Forgiving. This emphasizes the importance of sincerity and repentance.

Verse 26

وَآتِ ذَا الْقُرْبَىٰ حَقَّهُ وَالْمِسْكِينَ وَابْنَ السَّبِيلِ وَلَا تُبَذِّرْ تَبْذِيرًا

Give to close relatives their due, as well as the poor and ˹needy˺ travellers. And do not spend wastefully.

And do good to everyone who is related to you, and give him his right of kindness and righteousness, and give to the needy and the traveler who is cut off from his family and wealth, and do not spend your wealth in anything that does not obey Allah, or in excess and squandering.

وَقُلِ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي لَمْ يَتَّخِذْ وَلَدًا وَلَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُ شَرِيكٌ فِي الْمُلْكِ وَلَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُ وَلِيٌّ مِنَ الذُّلِّ ۖ وَكَبِّرْهُ تَكْبِيرًا

And say, “All praise is for Allah, Who has never had ˹any˺ offspring; nor does He have a partner in ˹governing˺ the kingdom; nor is He pathetic, needing a protector. And revere Him immensely.”

And say - O Messenger -: All praise is due to Allah, to Whom belongs perfection and praise, Who is exalted above having a son or a partner in His divinity, and Who has no ally from His creation, for He is the Self-Sufficient, the Powerful, and they are the poor and needy to Him. And magnify Him completely by praising Him and worshipping Him alone without any partners, and dedicating all of your religion to Him alone.

All praise belongs to Allah, who is free of any offspring or partners in governance. He is self-sufficient and requires no protector. Revere Him by worshiping him alone.
