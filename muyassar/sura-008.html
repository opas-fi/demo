Sura 008

Verse 1

يَسْأَلُونَكَ عَنِ الْأَنْفَالِ ۖ قُلِ الْأَنْفَالُ لِلَّهِ وَالرَّسُولِ ۖ فَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ وَأَصْلِحُوا ذَاتَ بَيْنِكُمْ ۖ وَأَطِيعُوا اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ مُؤْمِنِينَ

They ask you ˹O Prophet˺ regarding the spoils of war. Say, “Their distribution is decided by Allah and His Messenger. So be mindful of Allah, settle your affairs, and obey Allah and His Messenger if you are ˹true˺ believers.”

Your companions will ask you, O Prophet, about the spoils of war on the day of Badr. How will you divide them among them? Tell them: The matter is up to Allah and His Messenger. The Messenger will divide them by the command of his Lord. So fear Allah's punishment and do not rush to disobey Him. Leave disputes and quarrels over this wealth. Reconcile yourselves and adhere to the obedience of Allah and His Messenger if you are believers. Faith calls for obedience to Allah and His Messenger.

This verse clarifies that the distribution of war spoils is determined by Allah and His Messenger. It emphasizes the importance of fearing Allah, resolving disputes, and obeying divine guidance for true believers.

Verse 2

إِنَّمَا الْمُؤْمِنُونَ الَّذِينَ إِذَا ذُكِرَ اللَّهُ وَجِلَتْ قُلُوبُهُمْ وَإِذَا تُلِيَتْ عَلَيْهِمْ آيَاتُهُ زَادَتْهُمْ إِيمَانًا وَعَلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ يَتَوَكَّلُونَ

The ˹true˺ believers are only those whose hearts tremble at the remembrance of Allah, whose faith increases when His revelations are recited to them, and who put their trust in their Lord.

The true believers in God are those whose hearts are filled with fear when God is mentioned, and whose faith is increased when the verses of the Qur'an are recited to them, because they contemplate its meanings. They put their trust in God Almighty, and do not hope for anyone other than Him, nor do they fear anyone other than Him.

This verse describes the characteristics of true believers: their hearts are filled with awe at the mention of Allah, their faith increases when they hear the Quran, and they place their complete trust in Him.

Verse 3

الَّذِينَ يُقِيمُونَ الصَّلَاةَ وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَاهُمْ يُنْفِقُونَ

˹They are˺ those who establish prayer and donate from what We have provided for them.

Those who regularly perform the obligatory prayers at their appointed times, and from what We have provided them of wealth, they spend in that which We have commanded them.

True believers are characterized by their consistent observance of prayer and their generosity in giving from what Allah has provided them.

Verse 4

أُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ حَقًّا ۚ لَهُمْ دَرَجَاتٌ عِنْدَ رَبِّهِمْ وَمَغْفِرَةٌ وَرِزْقٌ كَرِيمٌ

It is they who are the true believers. They will have elevated ranks, forgiveness, and an honourable provision from their Lord.

Those who do these actions are the true believers, outwardly and inwardly, in what God has revealed to them. They will have high positions with God, forgiveness for their sins, and a generous provision, which is Paradise.

Those who embody these qualities are the true believers and are promised high ranks, forgiveness, and an honorable provision from Allah.

Verse 5

كَمَا أَخْرَجَكَ رَبُّكَ مِنْ بَيْتِكَ بِالْحَقِّ وَإِنَّ فَرِيقًا مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ لَكَارِهُونَ

Similarly, when your Lord brought you ˹O Prophet˺ out of your home for a just cause, a group of believers was totally against it.

Just as when you differed over the spoils, God took them from you and made them His portion and the portion of His Messenger, may God bless him and grant him peace, so your Lord commanded you, O Prophet, to leave 'Madinah' to meet the Quraysh caravan, and that was through the revelation that Gabriel brought to you, despite the dislike of a group of the believers to leave.

This verse recalls how Allah commanded the Prophet to leave his home for a righteous cause, despite some believers' initial reluctance. It highlights the importance of trusting divine commands even when they are not immediately understood.

Verse 6

يُجَادِلُونَكَ فِي الْحَقِّ بَعْدَمَا تَبَيَّنَ كَأَنَّمَا يُسَاقُونَ إِلَى الْمَوْتِ وَهُمْ يَنْظُرُونَ

They disputed with you about the truth after it had been made clear, as if they were being driven to death with their eyes wide open.

A group of believers argue with you, O Prophet, about fighting after it has become clear to them that it will happen, as if they were being driven to death while they are seeing it with their own eyes.

Some believers argued about the truth of the matter even after it became clear, as if they were being led to their own death. This shows how difficult it can be to accept divine will.

Verse 7

وَإِذْ يَعِدُكُمُ اللَّهُ إِحْدَى الطَّائِفَتَيْنِ أَنَّهَا لَكُمْ وَتَوَدُّونَ أَنَّ غَيْرَ ذَاتِ الشَّوْكَةِ تَكُونُ لَكُمْ وَيُرِيدُ اللَّهُ أَنْ يُحِقَّ الْحَقَّ بِكَلِمَاتِهِ وَيَقْطَعَ دَابِرَ الْكَافِرِينَ

˹Remember, O believers,˺ when Allah promised ˹to give˺ you the upper hand over either target, you wished to capture the unarmed party. But it was Allah’s Will to establish the truth by His Words and uproot the disbelievers;

And remember - O you who argue - God's promise to you of victory over one of the two groups: the caravan and what it carries of provisions, or the mobilization, which is fighting the enemies and triumphing over them. You love victory over the caravan rather than fighting, and God wants to establish Islam and make it supreme by His command to you to fight the infidels, and to eradicate the infidels by destruction.

Allah promised victory over one of two groups: the caravan or the enemy army. The believers desired the easier victory. However, Allah willed to establish the truth and destroy disbelief through actual combat.

Verse 8

لِيُحِقَّ الْحَقَّ وَيُبْطِلَ الْبَاطِلَ وَلَوْ كَرِهَ الْمُجْرِمُونَ

to firmly establish the truth and wipe out falsehood—even to the dismay of the wicked.

May God glorify Islam and its people, and eliminate polytheism and its people, even if the polytheists hate it.

Allah’s plan was to establish the truth and wipe out falsehood, even if it was displeasing to the wicked, emphasizing that Allah’s ultimate will is supreme.

Verse 9

إِذْ تَسْتَغِيثُونَ رَبَّكُمْ فَاسْتَجَابَ لَكُمْ أَنِّي مُمِدُّكُمْ بِأَلْفٍ مِنَ الْمَلَائِكَةِ مُرْدِفِينَ

˹Remember˺ when you cried out to your Lord for help, He answered, “I will reinforce you with a thousand angels—followed by many others.”

Remember God's blessing upon you on the day of Badr, when you sought victory over your enemy. God answered your prayer, saying, 'I will reinforce you with a thousand angels from heaven, one following the other.'

This verse recalls when believers sought Allah's help in battle, and He responded by sending a thousand angels to support them. This highlights Allah's power and assistance to those who believe.

Verse 10

وَمَا جَعَلَهُ اللَّهُ إِلَّا بُشْرَىٰ وَلِتَطْمَئِنَّ بِهِ قُلُوبُكُمْ ۚ وَمَا النَّصْرُ إِلَّا مِنْ عِنْدِ اللَّهِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَزِيزٌ حَكِيمٌ

And Allah made this a sign of victory and reassurance to your hearts. Victory comes only from Allah. Surely Allah is Almighty, All-Wise.

God did not provide this support except as good news for you of victory, and to calm your hearts with it, and to be certain of God's victory for you, and victory comes only from God, not from your strength and power. God is Almighty in His kingdom, Wise in His management and law.

Allah's aid through angels was a sign of victory and reassurance for believers. True victory comes only from Allah, emphasizing His might and wisdom.

Verse 11

إِذْ يُغَشِّيكُمُ النُّعَاسَ أَمَنَةً مِنْهُ وَيُنَزِّلُ عَلَيْكُمْ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ بِهِ وَيُذْهِبَ عَنْكُمْ رِجْزَ الشَّيْطَانِ وَلِيَرْبِطَ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِكُمْ وَيُثَبِّتَ بِهِ الْأَقْدَامَ

˹Remember˺ when He caused drowsiness to overcome you, giving you serenity. And He sent down rain from the sky to purify you, free you from Satan’s whispers, strengthen your hearts, and make ˹your˺ steps firm.

When God casts drowsiness upon you as a protection from the fear of your enemy that he will overcome you, and sends down upon you pure water from the clouds, to purify you with it from apparent events, and remove from you inwardly the whispers and thoughts of Satan, and to strengthen your hearts with patience in battle, and to make firm the feet of the believers by covering the sandy ground with rain so that the feet do not slip in it.

Allah sent drowsiness as a comfort, and rain to purify and strengthen the believers. This shows how Allah provides both physical and spiritual support in times of struggle.

Verse 12

إِذْ يُوحِي رَبُّكَ إِلَى الْمَلَائِكَةِ أَنِّي مَعَكُمْ فَثَبِّتُوا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا ۚ سَأُلْقِي فِي قُلُوبِ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا الرُّعْبَ فَاضْرِبُوا فَوْقَ الْأَعْنَاقِ وَاضْرِبُوا مِنْهُمْ كُلَّ بَنَانٍ

˹Remember, O Prophet,˺ when your Lord revealed to the angels, “I am with you. So make the believers stand firm. I will cast horror into the hearts of the disbelievers. So strike their necks and strike their fingertips.”

When your Lord, O Prophet, inspired the angels whom God had supported the Muslims with in the Battle of Badr, 'I am with you, helping you and supporting you. So strengthen the resolve of those who believe. I will cast into the hearts of those who disbelieve intense fear, humiliation, and disgrace. So strike, O believers, the heads of the disbelievers, and strike from them every limb and joint. '

Allah inspired the angels to support the believers and instilled fear in the hearts of the disbelievers, commanding them to strike their necks and limbs. This emphasizes divine intervention and support in battle.

Verse 13

ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّهُمْ شَاقُّوا اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ ۚ وَمَنْ يُشَاقِقِ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ شَدِيدُ الْعِقَابِ

This is because they defied Allah and His Messenger. And whoever defies Allah and His Messenger, then ˹know that˺ Allah is surely severe in punishment.

This is what happened to the infidels, their heads, necks and limbs being struck, because they disobeyed the command of God and His Messenger. And whoever disobeys the command of God and His Messenger, God will severely punish him in this world and the Hereafter.

The disbelievers faced punishment because they disobeyed Allah and His Messenger. This highlights the consequences of defying divine commands.

Verse 14

ذَٰلِكُمْ فَذُوقُوهُ وَأَنَّ لِلْكَافِرِينَ عَذَابَ النَّارِ

That ˹worldly punishment˺ is yours, so taste it! Then the disbelievers will suffer the torment of the Fire.

This is the punishment which I have hastened for you - O disbelievers who disobeyed the commands of God and His Messenger in this world - so taste it in this worldly life, and for you in the Hereafter is the punishment of the Fire.

Disbelievers are told to taste the punishment in this world, and they will face the torment of the Fire in the Hereafter, reinforcing the seriousness of rejecting faith.

Verse 15

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا زَحْفًا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ الْأَدْبَارَ

O believers! When you face the disbelievers in battle, never turn your backs to them.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, when you meet those who disbelieve in battle close to you, do not turn your backs to them and flee from them, but stand firm against them, for Allah is with you and will support you against them.

Believers are commanded not to turn their backs in battle. This promotes courage and steadfastness, highlighting the importance of not fleeing from conflict.

Verse 16

وَمَنْ يُوَلِّهِمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ دُبُرَهُ إِلَّا مُتَحَرِّفًا لِقِتَالٍ أَوْ مُتَحَيِّزًا إِلَىٰ فِئَةٍ فَقَدْ بَاءَ بِغَضَبٍ مِنَ اللَّهِ وَمَأْوَاهُ جَهَنَّمُ ۖ وَبِئْسَ الْمَصِيرُ

And whoever does so on such an occasion—unless it is a manoeuvre or to join their own troops—will earn the displeasure of Allah, and their home will be Hell. What an evil destination!

Whoever among you turns his back to them at the time of battle, unless he is turning to the plot of the disbelievers or joining the group of Muslims present at the battle, wherever they may be, has certainly deserved the wrath of God, and his abode is Hell, and wretched is the destination and the place of return.

Turning away from battle is forbidden except for strategic reasons or to join other believers. Those who disobey this will face Allah's displeasure and Hell as a punishment.

Verse 17

فَلَمْ تَقْتُلُوهُمْ وَلَٰكِنَّ اللَّهَ قَتَلَهُمْ ۚ وَمَا رَمَيْتَ إِذْ رَمَيْتَ وَلَٰكِنَّ اللَّهَ رَمَىٰ ۚ وَلِيُبْلِيَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ مِنْهُ بَلَاءً حَسَنًا ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ سَمِيعٌ عَلِيمٌ

It was not you ˹believers˺ who killed them, but it was Allah Who did so. Nor was it you ˹O Prophet˺ who threw ˹a handful of sand at the disbelievers˺, but it was Allah Who did so, rendering the believers a great favour. Surely Allah is All-Hearing, All-Knowing.

You, O believers, did not kill the polytheists on the day of Badr, but Allah killed them, as He helped you to do so. You, O Prophet, did not throw when you threw, but Allah threw, as He delivered the arrow you threw to the faces of the polytheists; and to test the believers in Allah and His Messenger and to bring them through jihad to the highest levels, and to make them aware of His favor upon them, so that they may thank Him, glory be to Him, for that. Indeed, Allah is Hearing of your supplications and your words, whether you keep them secret or make them public, and Knowing of what is good for His servants.

This verse emphasizes that victory comes from Allah. It was not the believers who killed the disbelievers in battle, but Allah who enabled it. Similarly, the Prophet's throw was aided by Allah, showing that all power and success come from Him.

Verse 18

ذَٰلِكُمْ وَأَنَّ اللَّهَ مُوهِنُ كَيْدِ الْكَافِرِينَ

As such, Allah frustrates the evil plans of the disbelievers.

This act of killing the polytheists and throwing them when they were defeated, and the good test of the victory of the believers over their enemies, is from God to the believers, and that God - in the future - will weaken and nullify the plot of the disbelievers until they are humiliated and submit to the truth or perish.

This verse assures that Allah will weaken and nullify the plans of the disbelievers. He frustrates their efforts and ensures their eventual defeat.

Verse 19

إِنْ تَسْتَفْتِحُوا فَقَدْ جَاءَكُمُ الْفَتْحُ ۖ وَإِنْ تَنْتَهُوا فَهُوَ خَيْرٌ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِنْ تَعُودُوا نَعُدْ وَلَنْ تُغْنِيَ عَنْكُمْ فِئَتُكُمْ شَيْئًا وَلَوْ كَثُرَتْ وَأَنَّ اللَّهَ مَعَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

If you ˹Meccans˺ sought judgment, now it has come to you. And if you cease, it will be for your own good. But if you persist, We will persist. And your forces—no matter how numerous they might be—will not benefit you whatsoever. For Allah is certainly with the believers.

If you - O disbelievers - ask Allah to inflict His wrath and punishment on the unjust aggressors, then Allah has answered your request, when He inflicted upon you His punishment that was a deterrent for you and a lesson for the righteous. If you - O disbelievers - cease disbelieving in Allah and His Messenger and fighting His Prophet Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, then that is better for you in this world and the hereafter. But if you return to war and fighting Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and fighting his believing followers, then We promise to defeat you as you were defeated on the day of Badr. Your group will not avail you at all, just as it did not avail you on the day of Badr despite your large number and equipment and the small number and equipment of the believers. And Allah is with the believers with His support and victory.

This verse is directed to the disbelievers, telling them that if they sought a judgment from Allah, it has come to them. If they cease their disbelief, it is better for them, but if they persist, they will face Allah's retribution. Their numbers will not help them, as Allah is with the believers.

Verse 20

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا أَطِيعُوا اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ وَلَا تَوَلَّوْا عَنْهُ وَأَنْتُمْ تَسْمَعُونَ

O believers! Obey Allah and His Messenger and do not turn away from him while you hear ˹his call˺.

O you who have believed in God and His Messenger, obey God and His Messenger in what He has commanded you and forbidden you, and do not abandon obedience to God and obedience to His Messenger, while you listen to what is recited to you in the Qur'an of arguments and proofs.

This verse calls upon believers to obey Allah and His Messenger. They should not turn away from the message while hearing it.

Verse 21

وَلَا تَكُونُوا كَالَّذِينَ قَالُوا سَمِعْنَا وَهُمْ لَا يَسْمَعُونَ

Do not be like those who say, “We hear,” but in fact they are not listening.

And do not be, O believers, in disobeying God and His Messenger Muhammad, may God bless him and grant him peace, like the polytheists and hypocrites who, when they hear the Book of God recited to them, say: We heard with our ears, while in reality they do not contemplate what they hear, nor do they think about it.

Believers are warned not to be like those who claim to hear but do not listen with understanding. True listening involves contemplation and acting upon the message.

Verse 22

إِنَّ شَرَّ الدَّوَابِّ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ الصُّمُّ الْبُكْمُ الَّذِينَ لَا يَعْقِلُونَ

Indeed, the worst of all beings in the sight of Allah are the ˹wilfully˺ deaf and dumb, who do not understand.

The worst of what has crawled on the earth - of God's creation - in God's sight are the deaf whose ears are blocked from hearing the truth and so they do not hear, and the mute whose tongues are mute from uttering it and so they do not speak. These are the ones who do not understand God's commands and prohibitions.

The worst beings in the sight of Allah are those who are deaf and dumb to the truth, not understanding or acting upon His commands.

Verse 23

وَلَوْ عَلِمَ اللَّهُ فِيهِمْ خَيْرًا لَأَسْمَعَهُمْ ۖ وَلَوْ أَسْمَعَهُمْ لَتَوَلَّوْا وَهُمْ مُعْرِضُونَ

Had Allah known any goodness in them, He would have certainly made them hear. ˹But˺ even if He had made them hear, they would have surely turned away heedlessly.

If God had known any good in these people, He would have made them hear the admonitions and lessons of the Qur'an so that they would understand the arguments and proofs of God Almighty. But He knew that there was no good in them and that they would not believe. If He had made them hear - assuming and assuming - they would have turned away from faith intentionally and stubbornly after understanding it, and they would have turned away from it, not paying attention to the truth in any way.

If Allah had found goodness in them, He would have made them understand. But since they have no intention to believe, even if they were given the ability to hear, they would turn away, heedless.

Verse 24

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اسْتَجِيبُوا لِلَّهِ وَلِلرَّسُولِ إِذَا دَعَاكُمْ لِمَا يُحْيِيكُمْ ۖ وَاعْلَمُوا أَنَّ اللَّهَ يَحُولُ بَيْنَ الْمَرْءِ وَقَلْبِهِ وَأَنَّهُ إِلَيْهِ تُحْشَرُونَ

O believers! Respond to Allah and His Messenger when he calls you to that which gives you life. And know that Allah stands between a person and their heart, and that to Him you will all be gathered.

O you who believe in Allah as Lord and in Muhammad as a Prophet and Messenger, respond to Allah and the Messenger with obedience when they call you to that which gives you life from the truth, for in responding is the improvement of your life in this world and the Hereafter. And know - O believers - that Allah, the Most High, is the One who controls all things, and is able to prevent a person from what his heart desires. He, glory be to Him, is the One to whom it is appropriate to respond when he calls you, for in His hand is the dominion of all things. And know that you will be gathered on a Day about which there is no doubt, and He will reward each according to what he deserves.

This verse calls believers to respond to Allah and His Messenger, as it leads to a fulfilling life. It reminds them that Allah has control over their hearts and they will be gathered to Him.

Verse 25

وَاتَّقُوا فِتْنَةً لَا تُصِيبَنَّ الَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا مِنْكُمْ خَاصَّةً ۖ وَاعْلَمُوا أَنَّ اللَّهَ شَدِيدُ الْعِقَابِ

Beware of a trial that will not only affect the wrongdoers among you. And know that Allah is severe in punishment.

O believers, beware of a test and trial that will befall the wrongdoer and others, and will not be limited to those who commit sins or those who have committed the sin. Rather, it will befall the righteous with them if they are able to denounce the injustice and do not denounce it. Know that God is severe in punishment for those who disobey His commands and prohibitions.

Believers are warned of trials that will affect not just the wrongdoers, but everyone if they don't stop injustice. Allah is severe in punishment for those who disobey Him.

Verse 26

وَاذْكُرُوا إِذْ أَنْتُمْ قَلِيلٌ مُسْتَضْعَفُونَ فِي الْأَرْضِ تَخَافُونَ أَنْ يَتَخَطَّفَكُمُ النَّاسُ فَآوَاكُمْ وَأَيَّدَكُمْ بِنَصْرِهِ وَرَزَقَكُمْ مِنَ الطَّيِّبَاتِ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ

Remember when you had been vastly outnumbered and oppressed in the land, constantly in fear of attacks by your enemy, then He sheltered you, strengthened you with His help, and provided you with good things so perhaps you would be thankful.

And remember, O believers, the blessings of God upon you when you were in Mecca, few in number and oppressed, fearing that the infidels would quickly seize you. So He made for you a refuge to which you could flee, which was Medina, and strengthened you with His victory over them on the day of Badr, and fed you from the good things - including the spoils - so that you would thank Him for what He provided for you and bestowed upon you.

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَهَاجَرُوا وَجَاهَدُوا بِأَمْوَالِهِمْ وَأَنْفُسِهِمْ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ وَالَّذِينَ آوَوْا وَنَصَرُوا أُولَٰئِكَ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلِيَاءُ بَعْضٍ ۚ وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَلَمْ يُهَاجِرُوا مَا لَكُمْ مِنْ وَلَايَتِهِمْ مِنْ شَيْءٍ حَتَّىٰ يُهَاجِرُوا ۚ وَإِنِ اسْتَنْصَرُوكُمْ فِي الدِّينِ فَعَلَيْكُمُ النَّصْرُ إِلَّا عَلَىٰ قَوْمٍ بَيْنَكُمْ وَبَيْنَهُمْ مِيثَاقٌ ۗ وَاللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ بَصِيرٌ

Those who believed, emigrated, and strived with their wealth and lives in the cause of Allah, as well as those who gave them shelter and help—they are truly guardians of one another. As for those who believed but did not emigrate, you have no obligations to them until they emigrate. But if they seek your help ˹against persecution˺ in faith, it is your obligation to help them, except against people bound with you in a treaty. Allah is All-Seeing of what you do.

Indeed, those who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law and emigrated to the abode of Islam, or a land where they could worship their Lord, and strove in the cause of Allah with their wealth and lives, and those who accommodated the emigrants in their homes and supported them with their wealth and aided the religion of Allah - those are helpers of one another. As for those who believed and did not emigrate from the abode of disbelief, you are not obligated to protect them and aid them until they emigrate. And if they are wronged by the disbelievers and they seek your aid, then respond to them, except in the case of a people between you and whom there is a firm treaty which they have not broken. And Allah is Seeing of your deeds. He will reward each according to his intention and his work.

This verse outlines the bonds between believers who emigrated and those who provided them shelter, stating they are guardians of one another. It also clarifies that believers who do not emigrate are not entitled to the same level of support unless they are persecuted for their faith. In such cases, aid is obligatory unless it interferes with existing treaties.

Verse 73

وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلِيَاءُ بَعْضٍ ۚ إِلَّا تَفْعَلُوهُ تَكُنْ فِتْنَةٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَفَسَادٌ كَبِيرٌ

As for the disbelievers, they are guardians of one another. And unless you ˹believers˺ act likewise, there will be great oppression and corruption in the land.

And those who disbelieve are supporters of one another. But if you - O believers - are not supporters of one another, there will be on earth a trial for the believers away from the religion of God, and widespread corruption by turning people away from the path of God and strengthening the pillars of disbelief.

This verse highlights the mutual support of disbelievers and warns believers that failing to support one another will result in great corruption and oppression. This underscores the need for believers to unite and protect their faith.

Verse 74

وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَهَاجَرُوا وَجَاهَدُوا فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ وَالَّذِينَ آوَوْا وَنَصَرُوا أُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ حَقًّا ۚ لَهُمْ مَغْفِرَةٌ وَرِزْقٌ كَرِيمٌ

Those who believed, migrated, and struggled in the cause of Allah, and those who gave ˹them˺ shelter and help, they are the true believers. They will have forgiveness and an honourable provision.

And those who believed in God and His Messenger, and left their homes intending to go to the abode of Islam or a country where they could worship their Lord, and strove to exalt the word of God, and those who aided their emigrant brothers and sheltered them and supported them with wealth and support - those are the true believers, for them is forgiveness for their sins and a generous and ample provision in the Gardens of Bliss.

This verse praises those who believed, emigrated, and struggled for Allah's cause, as well as those who gave them shelter and support. These are identified as true believers who will receive forgiveness and a generous reward in Paradise.

Verse 75

وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مِنْ بَعْدُ وَهَاجَرُوا وَجَاهَدُوا مَعَكُمْ فَأُولَٰئِكَ مِنْكُمْ ۚ وَأُولُو الْأَرْحَامِ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلَىٰ بِبَعْضٍ فِي كِتَابِ اللَّهِ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيمٌ

And those who later believed, migrated, and struggled alongside you, they are also with you. But only blood relatives are now entitled to inherit from one another, as ordained by Allah. Surely Allah has ˹full˺ knowledge of everything.

And those who believed after these Muhajireen and Ansar, and emigrated and fought with you in the cause of Allah, they are from you - O believers - they have what you have and are subject to what you are. And those of kinship are more deserving of one another in inheritance in the judgment of Allah than the general Muslims. Indeed, Allah is Knowing of all things. He knows what is best for His servants in terms of inheritance from one another in kinship and lineage, but not inheritance by oath, and other things that were in the beginning of Islam.

This verse states that those who believed, migrated and struggled with the believers later are part of them. It also introduces a ruling on inheritance, stating that blood relatives are more entitled to inherit from one another. This is ordained by Allah, the All-Knowing.
