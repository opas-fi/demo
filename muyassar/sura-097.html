Sura 097

Verse 1

إِنَّا أَنْزَلْنَاهُ فِي لَيْلَةِ الْقَدْرِ

Indeed, ˹it is˺ We ˹Who˺ sent this ˹Quran˺ down on the Night of Glory.

Indeed, We sent down the Quran on the Night of Honor and Virtue, which is one of the nights of the month of Ramadan.

This verse states that the Quran was revealed on the Night of Glory (Laylatul Qadr), which is a night in Ramadan.

Verse 2

وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ

And what will make you realize what the Night of Glory is?

And what will make you know, [O Prophet], what is the Night of Decree and Honor?

This verse emphasizes the importance and greatness of the Night of Glory.

Verse 3

لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌ مِنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍ

The Night of Glory is better than a thousand months.

The Night of Decree is a blessed night; its virtue is better than the virtue of a thousand months in which there is no Night of Decree.

The Night of Glory is better than a thousand months, highlighting its immense spiritual value.

Verse 4

تَنَزَّلُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ وَالرُّوحُ فِيهَا بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِمْ مِنْ كُلِّ أَمْرٍ

That night the angels and the ˹holy˺ spirit descend, by the permission of their Lord, for every ˹decreed˺ matter.

The angels and Gabriel descend therein by permission of their Lord for every matter He decrees in that year.

On this night, angels and the holy spirit descend with God's permission for all decreed matters.

Verse 5

سَلَامٌ هِيَ حَتَّىٰ مَطْلَعِ الْفَجْرِ

It is all peace until the break of dawn.

It is peace until the emergence of dawn.

This night is full of peace and tranquility until the break of dawn.
