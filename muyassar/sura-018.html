Sura 018

Verse 1

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي أَنْزَلَ عَلَىٰ عَبْدِهِ الْكِتَابَ وَلَمْ يَجْعَلْ لَهُ عِوَجًا ۜ

All praise is for Allah Who has revealed the Book to His servant, allowing no crookedness in it,

Praise be to Allah with His attributes, which are all attributes of perfection, and for His apparent and hidden blessings, religious and worldly, Who bestowed His favor and sent down the Quran upon His servant and Messenger Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, and did not place in it any deviation from the truth.

Praise be to Allah, who revealed the Quran to His servant (Prophet Muhammad), with no deviation or falsehood in it.

Verse 2

قَيِّمًا لِيُنْذِرَ بَأْسًا شَدِيدًا مِنْ لَدُنْهُ وَيُبَشِّرَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ الَّذِينَ يَعْمَلُونَ الصَّالِحَاتِ أَنَّ لَهُمْ أَجْرًا حَسَنًا

˹making it˺ perfectly upright, to warn ˹the disbelievers˺ of a severe torment from Him; to give good news to the believers—who do good—that they will have a fine reward,

Allah has made it a straight Book, with no discrepancies or contradictions, so that it may warn the disbelievers of a severe punishment from Him, and give good tidings to those who believe in Allah and His Messenger, those who perform righteous deeds, that they will have a great reward, which is Paradise, wherein they will abide forever and never depart from it.

The Quran serves as a clear guidance, warning disbelievers of severe punishment and promising believers a great reward which is Paradise.

Verse 3

مَاكِثِينَ فِيهِ أَبَدًا

in which they will remain forever;

Allah has made it a straight Book, with no discrepancies or contradictions, so that it may warn the disbelievers of a severe punishment from Him, and give good tidings to those who believe in Allah and His Messenger, those who perform righteous deeds, that they will have a great reward, which is Paradise, wherein they will abide forever and never depart from it.

Believers will remain eternally in Paradise, enjoying its unending blessings.

Verse 4

وَيُنْذِرَ الَّذِينَ قَالُوا اتَّخَذَ اللَّهُ وَلَدًا

and to warn those who claim, “Allah has offspring.”

And warn with it the polytheists who said: Allah has taken a son.

The Quran warns those who falsely claim that Allah has offspring, as this is against His absolute Oneness.

Verse 5

مَا لَهُمْ بِهِ مِنْ عِلْمٍ وَلَا لِآبَائِهِمْ ۚ كَبُرَتْ كَلِمَةً تَخْرُجُ مِنْ أَفْوَاهِهِمْ ۚ إِنْ يَقُولُونَ إِلَّا كَذِبًا

They have no knowledge of this, nor did their forefathers. What a terrible claim that comes out of their mouths! They say nothing but lies.

These polytheists have no knowledge whatsoever of what they falsely attribute to Allah regarding taking a son, just as their ancestors whom they imitated had none. How grave is this heinous statement that comes out of their mouths; they say nothing but a lie.

This verse condemns the false claim that Allah has a son. It highlights the ignorance of those who make this claim and emphasizes that it is a baseless lie.

Verse 6

فَلَعَلَّكَ بَاخِعٌ نَفْسَكَ عَلَىٰ آثَارِهِمْ إِنْ لَمْ يُؤْمِنُوا بِهَٰذَا الْحَدِيثِ أَسَفًا

Now, perhaps you ˹O Prophet˺ will grieve yourself to death over their denial, if they ˹continue to˺ disbelieve in this message.

Perhaps, [O Messenger], you would destroy yourself in grief and sorrow over their turning away from you, if they do not believe in this Quran and act upon it.

This verse shows the Prophet's deep concern and grief over the disbelief of his people. It speaks to the burden the Prophet felt to convey his message.

Verse 7

إِنَّا جَعَلْنَا مَا عَلَى الْأَرْضِ زِينَةً لَهَا لِنَبْلُوَهُمْ أَيُّهُمْ أَحْسَنُ عَمَلًا

We have indeed made whatever is on earth as an adornment for it, in order to test which of them is best in deeds.

Indeed, We have made that which is on the earth adornment for it, and a benefit for its people, in order that We may test them as to which of them is best in deed by obeying Us, and which of them is worst in deed by committing sins, and We will recompense each according to what he deserves.

This verse explains that the beauty and resources on Earth are a test for people. The purpose is to see who will perform the best actions, through obedience to Allah.

Verse 8

وَإِنَّا لَجَاعِلُونَ مَا عَلَيْهَا صَعِيدًا جُرُزًا

And We will certainly reduce whatever is on it to barren ground.

And indeed, We will make all that is on the earth of that adornment, when the world comes to an end, barren dust, with no vegetation in it.

This verse explains that the worldly adornment will eventually turn to barren dust, signifying the end of the world and highlighting the temporary nature of earthly life.

Verse 9

أَمْ حَسِبْتَ أَنَّ أَصْحَابَ الْكَهْفِ وَالرَّقِيمِ كَانُوا مِنْ آيَاتِنَا عَجَبًا

Have you ˹O Prophet˺ thought that the people of the cave and the plaque were ˹the only˺ wonders of Our signs?

Do not think, [O Messenger], that the story of the People of the Cave and the tablet on which their names were written is a strange and wondrous one among Our signs; for the creation of the heavens and the earth and what is in them is more wondrous than that.

This verse tells the Prophet not to view the story of the People of the Cave as the only wondrous sign. Allah's creation is filled with greater signs of His power.

Verse 10

إِذْ أَوَى الْفِتْيَةُ إِلَى الْكَهْفِ فَقَالُوا رَبَّنَا آتِنَا مِنْ لَدُنْكَ رَحْمَةً وَهَيِّئْ لَنَا مِنْ أَمْرِنَا رَشَدًا

˹Remember˺ when those youths took refuge in the cave, and said, “Our Lord! Grant us mercy from Yourself and guide us rightly through our ordeal.”

Remember, [O Messenger], when the believing youths sought refuge in the cave, fearing the trials of their people and their compulsion to worship idols, and said: 'Our Lord, grant us from Yourself mercy to make us steadfast and protect us from evil, and make easy for us the right path that leads us to the action that You love, so we may be rightly guided and not astray.'

This verse tells about the youth seeking refuge in a cave, and praying to Allah for mercy and guidance through their ordeal.

Verse 11

فَضَرَبْنَا عَلَىٰ آذَانِهِمْ فِي الْكَهْفِ سِنِينَ عَدَدًا

So We caused them to fall into a dead sleep in the cave for many years,

So We cast upon them a deep sleep, and they remained in the cave for many years.

This verse reveals that Allah caused the youths to sleep in the cave for many years.

Verse 12

ثُمَّ بَعَثْنَاهُمْ لِنَعْلَمَ أَيُّ الْحِزْبَيْنِ أَحْصَىٰ لِمَا لَبِثُوا أَمَدًا

then We raised them so We may show which of the two groups would make a better estimation of the length of their stay.

Then We awakened them from their sleep so that We might reveal to the people what We knew in eternity; so that it may be distinguished which of the two disputing groups is more accurate in calculating the duration of their stay, and whether they had stayed a day or part of a day, or a long period.

This verse explains that Allah awakened the youths to reveal how long they slept and distinguish the group which was more accurate in estimating the period of time.

Verse 13

نَحْنُ نَقُصُّ عَلَيْكَ نَبَأَهُمْ بِالْحَقِّ ۚ إِنَّهُمْ فِتْيَةٌ آمَنُوا بِرَبِّهِمْ وَزِدْنَاهُمْ هُدًى

We relate to you ˹O Prophet˺ their story in truth. They were youths who truly believed in their Lord, and We increased them in guidance.

We relate to you, [O Messenger], their story in truth. Indeed, the People of the Cave were youths who believed in their Lord and obeyed His command, and We increased them in guidance and steadfastness on the truth.

This verse states the story of the People of the Cave is true. They were youths who believed in Allah and were rewarded with increased guidance.

Verse 14

وَرَبَطْنَا عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِهِمْ إِذْ قَامُوا فَقَالُوا رَبُّنَا رَبُّ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ لَنْ نَدْعُوَ مِنْ دُونِهِ إِلَٰهًا ۖ لَقَدْ قُلْنَا إِذًا شَطَطًا

And We strengthened their hearts when they stood up and declared, “Our Lord is the Lord of the heavens and the earth. We will never call upon any god besides Him, or we would truly be uttering an outrageous lie.”

And We strengthened their hearts with faith and made their resolve firm by it, when they stood before the disbelieving king while he reproached them for abandoning the worship of idols, and said to him: 'Our Lord whom we worship is the Lord of the heavens and the earth; we will not worship any other deity besides Him. If we said otherwise, then we would have spoken an unjust statement far from the truth.'

This verse tells about the youths who openly declared their belief in Allah, affirming Him as the sole Lord and rejecting all other deities.

Verse 15

هَٰؤُلَاءِ قَوْمُنَا اتَّخَذُوا مِنْ دُونِهِ آلِهَةً ۖ لَوْلَا يَأْتُونَ عَلَيْهِمْ بِسُلْطَانٍ بَيِّنٍ ۖ فَمَنْ أَظْلَمُ مِمَّنِ افْتَرَىٰ عَلَى اللَّهِ كَذِبًا

˹Then they said to one another,˺ “These people of ours have taken gods besides Him. Why do they not produce a clear proof of them? Who then does more wrong than those who fabricate lies against Allah?

Then some of them said to others: 'These are our people who have taken gods other than Allah, why do they not bring a clear proof for their worship of them? So there is none more unjust than one who fabricates a lie against Allah by attributing partners to Him in His worship.'

This verse tells how the youths questioned their people's worship of false gods, stating that there is no greater wrong than fabricating lies against Allah.

Verse 16

وَإِذِ اعْتَزَلْتُمُوهُمْ وَمَا يَعْبُدُونَ إِلَّا اللَّهَ فَأْوُوا إِلَى الْكَهْفِ يَنْشُرْ لَكُمْ رَبُّكُمْ مِنْ رَحْمَتِهِ وَيُهَيِّئْ لَكُمْ مِنْ أَمْرِكُمْ مِرْفَقًا

Since you have distanced yourselves from them and what they worship besides Allah, take refuge in the cave. Your Lord will extend His mercy to you and accommodate you in your ordeal.”

And when you separated from your people for your religion, and abandoned what they worshipped of deities except the worship of Allah, then seek refuge in the cave in the mountain to worship your Lord alone. Your Lord will extend to you from His mercy that which covers you in both worlds, and will make easy for you from your affairs that from which you benefit in your lives of the means of livelihood.

This verse advises the believers to seek refuge in a cave after separating from their disbelieving community. It promises Allah's mercy and ease in their difficult situation.

Verse 17

وَتَرَى الشَّمْسَ إِذَا طَلَعَتْ تَزَاوَرُ عَنْ كَهْفِهِمْ ذَاتَ الْيَمِينِ وَإِذَا غَرَبَتْ تَقْرِضُهُمْ ذَاتَ الشِّمَالِ وَهُمْ فِي فَجْوَةٍ مِنْهُ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ مِنْ آيَاتِ اللَّهِ ۗ مَنْ يَهْدِ اللَّهُ فَهُوَ الْمُهْتَدِ ۖ وَمَنْ يُضْلِلْ فَلَنْ تَجِدَ لَهُ وَلِيًّا مُرْشِدًا

And you would have seen the sun, as it rose, inclining away from their cave to the right, and as it set, declining away from them to the left, while they lay in its open space. That is one of the signs of Allah. Whoever Allah guides is truly guided. But whoever He leaves to stray, you will never find for them a guiding mentor.

So when they did that, Allah cast upon them sleep and protected them. And you see - O observer of them - the sun when it rises from the east, it inclines away from their place towards the right, and when it sets, it leaves them towards the left, and they are in a spacious area of the cave, so the heat of the sun does not harm them, and the air does not cease from them. That which We did for these youths is from the signs of the power of Allah. He whom Allah guides to be guided by His verses, he is the one who is guided to the truth, and he who is not guided to that, then you will not find a helper for him to guide him to attaining the truth, because guidance and abandonment are in the hands of Allah alone.

This verse describes a miracle: the sun avoids the cave where the believers rested, indicating Allah's protection. Guidance comes from Allah, and those who stray will find no guide.

Verse 18

وَتَحْسَبُهُمْ أَيْقَاظًا وَهُمْ رُقُودٌ ۚ وَنُقَلِّبُهُمْ ذَاتَ الْيَمِينِ وَذَاتَ الشِّمَالِ ۖ وَكَلْبُهُمْ بَاسِطٌ ذِرَاعَيْهِ بِالْوَصِيدِ ۚ لَوِ اطَّلَعْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ لَوَلَّيْتَ مِنْهُمْ فِرَارًا وَلَمُلِئْتَ مِنْهُمْ رُعْبًا

And you would have thought they were awake, though they were asleep. We turned them over, to the right and left, while their dog stretched his forelegs at the entrance. Had you looked at them, you would have certainly fled away from them, filled with horror.

And you think - O observer - the people of the cave are awake, while they are in fact asleep, and We take care of them, so We turn them while they sleep once to the right side and once to the left side; so that the earth does not consume them, and their dog that accompanied them is stretching out its forelegs in the courtyard of the cave. If you had seen them, you would have turned away from them fleeing, and your soul would have been filled with fear of them.

The believers appeared to be awake, though they were asleep. Allah turned them from side to side. Their dog was also present. The sight of them was so awe-inspiring that anyone who saw them would flee in terror.

Verse 19

وَكَذَٰلِكَ بَعَثْنَاهُمْ لِيَتَسَاءَلُوا بَيْنَهُمْ ۚ قَالَ قَائِلٌ مِنْهُمْ كَمْ لَبِثْتُمْ ۖ قَالُوا لَبِثْنَا يَوْمًا أَوْ بَعْضَ يَوْمٍ ۚ قَالُوا رَبُّكُمْ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا لَبِثْتُمْ فَابْعَثُوا أَحَدَكُمْ بِوَرِقِكُمْ هَٰذِهِ إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ فَلْيَنْظُرْ أَيُّهَا أَزْكَىٰ طَعَامًا فَلْيَأْتِكُمْ بِرِزْقٍ مِنْهُ وَلْيَتَلَطَّفْ وَلَا يُشْعِرَنَّ بِكُمْ أَحَدًا

And so We awakened them so that they might question one another. One of them exclaimed, “How long have you remained ˹asleep˺?” Some replied, “Perhaps a day, or part of a day.” They said ˹to one another˺, “Your Lord knows best how long you have remained. So send one of you with these silver coins of yours to the city, and let him find which food is the purest, and then bring you provisions from it. Let him be ˹exceptionally˺ cautious, and do not let him give you away.

And just as We caused them to sleep and protected them this long period, We awakened them from their sleep in their state without change; so that they may ask each other: How long have we stayed sleeping here? So some of them said: We stayed a day or part of a day, and others, the matter was confusing to them: Entrust the knowledge of that to Allah, your Lord is most knowing of the time that you stayed, so send one of you with this silver money of yours to our city so let him see: which of the people of the city is most lawful and purer in food? So let him bring you provision from it, and let him be gentle in his purchase with the seller so that we do not become exposed, and our matter becomes apparent, and let no one from the people know about you.

After being asleep for a long period, the believers awakened and began to question each other. They decided to send one of them to the city with silver coins to find pure food while being very cautious.

Verse 20

إِنَّهُمْ إِنْ يَظْهَرُوا عَلَيْكُمْ يَرْجُمُوكُمْ أَوْ يُعِيدُوكُمْ فِي مِلَّتِهِمْ وَلَنْ تُفْلِحُوا إِذًا أَبَدًا

For, indeed, if they find out about you, they will stone you ˹to death˺, or force you back into their faith, and then you will never succeed.”

Indeed, your people, if they find out about you, will stone you with stones, so they will kill you, or they will return you to their religion, so you will become disbelievers, and you will not succeed in your aim of entering Paradise - if you do that - ever.

This verse explains the believers' fear of being discovered: they would either be stoned to death or forced back into their old faith, which would lead to their ruin.

Verse 21

وَكَذَٰلِكَ أَعْثَرْنَا عَلَيْهِمْ لِيَعْلَمُوا أَنَّ وَعْدَ اللَّهِ حَقٌّ وَأَنَّ السَّاعَةَ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهَا إِذْ يَتَنَازَعُونَ بَيْنَهُمْ أَمْرَهُمْ ۖ فَقَالُوا ابْنُوا عَلَيْهِمْ بُنْيَانًا ۖ رَبُّهُمْ أَعْلَمُ بِهِمْ ۚ قَالَ الَّذِينَ غَلَبُوا عَلَىٰ أَمْرِهِمْ لَنَتَّخِذَنَّ عَلَيْهِمْ مَسْجِدًا

That is how We caused them to be discovered so that their people might know that Allah’s promise ˹of resurrection˺ is true and that there is no doubt about the Hour. When the people disputed with each other about the case of the youth ˹after their death˺, some proposed, “Build a structure around them. Their Lord knows best about them.” Those who prevailed in the matter said, “We will surely build a place of worship over them.”

And just as We caused them to sleep for many years, and then We awakened them, We made the people of that time aware of them, after the seller revealed the type of dirhams their messenger had brought; so that people would know that Allah's promise of resurrection is true, and that the Hour is coming, there is no doubt about it, when those who learned about the people of the cave disputed about the matter of resurrection: some affirmed it and some denied it. So Allah made their discovery by the people a proof for the believers against the disbelievers. After their affair was revealed and they died, a group of those who knew about them said: Build a building at the entrance of the cave to conceal them, and leave them to their own affair, their Lord knows best about their condition. And those with influence and power among them said: We will surely take their place of rest as a mosque for worship. And the Messenger of Allah, may Allah's peace and blessings be upon him, forbade taking the graves of prophets and righteous people as mosques, and cursed those who did that in his last instructions to his nation, just as he forbade building on graves in general, and plastering and writing on them, because that is excessive veneration that may lead to worship of those in them.

Allah revealed the sleepers of the cave to show people the truth of resurrection. After their discovery, people debated what to do; some suggested building a memorial, while others wanted to build a place of worship, although building on graves is not permitted in Islam.

Verse 22

سَيَقُولُونَ ثَلَاثَةٌ رَابِعُهُمْ كَلْبُهُمْ وَيَقُولُونَ خَمْسَةٌ سَادِسُهُمْ كَلْبُهُمْ رَجْمًا بِالْغَيْبِ ۖ وَيَقُولُونَ سَبْعَةٌ وَثَامِنُهُمْ كَلْبُهُمْ ۚ قُلْ رَبِّي أَعْلَمُ بِعِدَّتِهِمْ مَا يَعْلَمُهُمْ إِلَّا قَلِيلٌ ۗ فَلَا تُمَارِ فِيهِمْ إِلَّا مِرَاءً ظَاهِرًا وَلَا تَسْتَفْتِ فِيهِمْ مِنْهُمْ أَحَدًا

Some will say, “They were three, their dog was the fourth,” while others will say, “They were five, their dog was the sixth,” ˹only˺ guessing blindly. And others will say, “They were seven and their dog was the eighth.” Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “My Lord knows best their ˹exact˺ number. Only a few people know as well.” So do not argue about them except with sure knowledge, nor consult any of those ˹who debate˺ about them.

Some of the People of the Book who delve into their matter will say: They were three, the fourth of them being their dog. And another group will say: They were five, the sixth of them being their dog, and the words of both groups are based on conjecture without evidence. And a third group says: They were seven, and the eighth of them was their dog. Say - O Messenger -: My Lord is most knowing of their number, none knows their number except a few of His creation. So do not argue with the People of the Book about their number except with a clear argument that is not deep, by only relating to them what the revelation has informed you of, and do not ask them about their number and their circumstances, for they do not know that.

People have differing opinions about the number of the sleepers in the cave, with some guessing. Allah emphasizes that only He knows the exact number, and we should not argue about it without knowledge. Instead, focus on the lessons of the story.

Verse 23

وَلَا تَقُولَنَّ لِشَيْءٍ إِنِّي فَاعِلٌ ذَٰلِكَ غَدًا

And never say of anything, “I will definitely do this tomorrow,”

And never say about anything you intend to do: 'I will do that tomorrow,' except [when you qualify it] with 'if Allah wills.' And remember your Lord when you forget by saying: 'if Allah wills,' and whenever you forget, then remember Allah, for the remembrance of Allah removes forgetfulness, and say: 'Perhaps my Lord will guide me to the closest path that leads to guidance and righteousness.'

This verse teaches us to always say 'if Allah wills' (Insha'Allah) when talking about future plans. It emphasizes our reliance on Allah's will and power.

Verse 24

إِلَّا أَنْ يَشَاءَ اللَّهُ ۚ وَاذْكُرْ رَبَّكَ إِذَا نَسِيتَ وَقُلْ عَسَىٰ أَنْ يَهْدِيَنِ رَبِّي لِأَقْرَبَ مِنْ هَٰذَا رَشَدًا

without adding, “if Allah so wills!” But if you forget, then remember your Lord, and say, “I trust my Lord will guide me to what is more right than this.”

And never say about anything you intend to do: 'I will do that tomorrow,' except [when you qualify it] with 'if Allah wills.' And remember your Lord when you forget by saying: 'if Allah wills,' and whenever you forget, then remember Allah, for the remembrance of Allah removes forgetfulness, and say: 'Perhaps my Lord will guide me to the closest path that leads to guidance and righteousness.'

If you forget to say 'if Allah wills,' remember Allah and say it. Ask Allah to guide you to what is best. It is a reminder to rely on Allah and seek His guidance.

Verse 25

وَلَبِثُوا فِي كَهْفِهِمْ ثَلَاثَ مِائَةٍ سِنِينَ وَازْدَادُوا تِسْعًا

They had remained in their cave for three hundred years, adding nine.

And the young men remained asleep in their cave for three hundred years, and [add] nine [more].
