Sura 091

Verse 1

وَالشَّمْسِ وَضُحَاهَا

By the sun and its brightness,

Allah swore by the sun and its brightness, and its morning radiance, and by the moon when it follows it in rising and setting, and by the day when it unveils the darkness and exposes it, and by the night when it covers the earth, so that what is on it becomes dark, and by the sky and its firm construction, and by the earth and its expanse, and by every soul and Allah’s perfection of its creation to perform its task. He clarified for it the path of evil and the path of good. He has succeeded who purifies it and develops it with good, and he has failed who hides himself in sins.

Allah swears by the sun and its radiant light, highlighting its importance as a sign of His creation.

Verse 11

كَذَّبَتْ ثَمُودُ بِطَغْوَاهَا

Thamûd rejected ˹the truth˺ out of arrogance,

Thamud denied their prophet due to their extreme disobedience, when the most wretched of the tribe rose to hamstring the she-camel. So the Messenger of Allah, Salih, peace be upon him, said to them: Beware of harming the she-camel; for it is a sign sent by Allah to you, indicating the truthfulness of your prophet. Beware of attacking her drinking; for she has a drink on one day and you have a drink on a known day. This was difficult for them, so they denied him in what he had warned them of and hamstrung her. Therefore, their Lord inflicted upon them the punishment for their crime, and He made it equal upon them, so that no one escaped. And He - exalted be His might - does not fear the consequences of the severe punishment that He sent upon them.

The people of Thamud rejected the truth due to their arrogance, illustrating the consequences of defying Allah's messengers.
