Sura 061

Verse 1

سَبَّحَ لِلَّهِ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

Whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth glorifies Allah. For He ˹alone˺ is the Almighty, All-Wise.

Allah is Exalted above all that does not befit Him. Whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth glorifies Him, and He is the Mighty, the One Who cannot be overcome, the Wise in His statements and actions.

This verse proclaims that everything in the heavens and earth glorifies Allah, highlighting His supreme power and wisdom.

Verse 2

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لِمَ تَقُولُونَ مَا لَا تَفْعَلُونَ

O believers! Why do you say what you do not do?

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, why do you promise a promise, or say a saying, and not fulfill it?! This is a reproach upon whoever does not match his actions with his words.

Believers are questioned for not aligning their actions with their words, emphasizing the importance of honesty and integrity.

Verse 3

كَبُرَ مَقْتًا عِنْدَ اللَّهِ أَنْ تَقُولُوا مَا لَا تَفْعَلُونَ

How despicable it is in the sight of Allah that you say what you do not do!

Most hateful it is to Allah that you should say that which you do not do.

This verse emphasizes how greatly Allah dislikes the discrepancy between words and deeds.

Verse 4

إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الَّذِينَ يُقَاتِلُونَ فِي سَبِيلِهِ صَفًّا كَأَنَّهُمْ بُنْيَانٌ مَرْصُوصٌ

Surely Allah loves those who fight in His cause in ˹solid˺ ranks as if they were one concrete structure.

Indeed, Allah loves those who fight in His cause in a row as if they are a solid, compact structure through which the enemy cannot penetrate. And in the verse is a statement of the virtue of Jihad and the Mujahideen; because of Allah’s love, Exalted be He, for His believing servants if they line up facing the enemies of Allah, fighting them in His cause.

Allah loves those who fight in His cause, standing united and firm as a solid structure, highlighting the virtue of Jihad and steadfastness in faith.

Verse 5

وَإِذْ قَالَ مُوسَىٰ لِقَوْمِهِ يَا قَوْمِ لِمَ تُؤْذُونَنِي وَقَدْ تَعْلَمُونَ أَنِّي رَسُولُ اللَّهِ إِلَيْكُمْ ۖ فَلَمَّا زَاغُوا أَزَاغَ اللَّهُ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ وَاللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِي الْقَوْمَ الْفَاسِقِينَ

˹Remember, O Prophet,˺ when Moses said to his people, “O my people! Why do you hurt me when you already know I am Allah’s messenger to you?” So when they ˹persistently˺ deviated, Allah caused their hearts to deviate. For Allah does not guide the rebellious people.

And mention to your people - O Messenger - when the prophet of Allah, Moses, peace be upon him, said to his people: Why do you harm me with words and actions, while you know that I am the messenger of Allah to you? So when they turned away from the truth with their knowledge of it, and persisted in that, Allah turned their hearts away from accepting guidance as a punishment for their deviation that they chose for themselves. And Allah does not guide the people who are disobedient and depart from the path of truth.

The verse recounts how Moses was hurt by his people despite knowing he was Allah's messenger. When they persisted in their deviation, Allah turned their hearts away as a consequence, showing that Allah does not guide those who are willfully disobedient.

Verse 6

وَإِذْ قَالَ عِيسَى ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ يَا بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ إِنِّي رَسُولُ اللَّهِ إِلَيْكُمْ مُصَدِّقًا لِمَا بَيْنَ يَدَيَّ مِنَ التَّوْرَاةِ وَمُبَشِّرًا بِرَسُولٍ يَأْتِي مِنْ بَعْدِي اسْمُهُ أَحْمَدُ ۖ فَلَمَّا جَاءَهُمْ بِالْبَيِّنَاتِ قَالُوا هَٰذَا سِحْرٌ مُبِينٌ

And ˹remember˺ when Jesus, son of Mary, said, “O children of Israel! I am truly Allah’s messenger to you, confirming the Torah which came before me, and giving good news of a messenger after me whose name will be Aḥmad.” Yet when the Prophet came to them with clear proofs, they said, “This is pure magic.”

And remember - O Messenger to your people - when Jesus, the son of Mary, said to his people, "Indeed, I am the messenger of Allah to you, confirming what came before me of the Torah and bearing witness to the truthfulness of a messenger who will come after me, whose name is 'Ahmad,' and he is Muhammad, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, and inviting to believe in him." But when Muhammad, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, came to them with clear signs, they said, "This that you have brought us is manifest magic."

This verse recounts Jesus's (Isa) prophecy of Prophet Muhammad (Ahmad), confirming earlier scriptures. Despite the clear signs, some people rejected the message, labeling it as magic.

Verse 7

وَمَنْ أَظْلَمُ مِمَّنِ افْتَرَىٰ عَلَى اللَّهِ الْكَذِبَ وَهُوَ يُدْعَىٰ إِلَى الْإِسْلَامِ ۚ وَاللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِي الْقَوْمَ الظَّالِمِينَ

Who does more wrong than the one who fabricates lies about Allah when invited to submit ˹to Him˺? For Allah does not guide the wrongdoing people.

And who is more unjust than one who invents a lie about Allah and makes partners for Him in worship, while he is invited to enter Islam and dedicate worship to Allah alone? And Allah does not guide the people who are unjust to themselves by disbelief and polytheism, to that which is their success.

The verse asks who is more unjust than those who invent lies about Allah, especially when they're invited to Islam. Allah does not guide those who choose to do wrong.

Verse 8

يُرِيدُونَ لِيُطْفِئُوا نُورَ اللَّهِ بِأَفْوَاهِهِمْ وَاللَّهُ مُتِمُّ نُورِهِ وَلَوْ كَرِهَ الْكَافِرُونَ

They wish to extinguish Allah’s light with their mouths, but Allah will ˹certainly˺ perfect His light, even to the dismay of the disbelievers.

These unjust people want to invalidate the truth that Muhammad, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, was sent with - which is the Quran - by their false statements. But Allah will manifest the truth by completing His religion, even if the disbelieving deniers dislike it.

The disbelievers attempt to extinguish the light of Allah (Islam) with their words, but Allah will perfect His light despite their opposition.

Verse 9

هُوَ الَّذِي أَرْسَلَ رَسُولَهُ بِالْهُدَىٰ وَدِينِ الْحَقِّ لِيُظْهِرَهُ عَلَى الدِّينِ كُلِّهِ وَلَوْ كَرِهَ الْمُشْرِكُونَ

He is the One Who has sent His Messenger with ˹true˺ guidance and the religion of truth, making it prevail over all others, even to the dismay of the polytheists.

Allah is the One who sent His Messenger Muhammad with the Quran and the religion of Islam, to make it prevail over all other religions, even if the polytheists dislike it.

Allah sent Prophet Muhammad with guidance and the true religion (Islam) to prevail over all other religions, even if polytheists disapprove.

Verse 10

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا هَلْ أَدُلُّكُمْ عَلَىٰ تِجَارَةٍ تُنْجِيكُمْ مِنْ عَذَابٍ أَلِيمٍ

O believers! Shall I guide you to an exchange that will save you from a painful punishment?

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, shall I guide you to a great transaction that will save you from a painful torment?

This verse poses a question to the believers, offering a path that will save them from a painful punishment.

Verse 11

تُؤْمِنُونَ بِاللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ وَتُجَاهِدُونَ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ بِأَمْوَالِكُمْ وَأَنْفُسِكُمْ ۚ ذَٰلِكُمْ خَيْرٌ لَكُمْ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ

˹It is to˺ have faith in Allah and His Messenger, and strive in the cause of Allah with your wealth and your lives. That is best for you, if only you knew.

You maintain your belief in Allah and His Messenger, and strive in the cause of Allah to support His religion with your wealth and your lives. That is better for you than the trade of this world, if you know the harms and benefits of things, so adhere to that.

This verse highlights that the best exchange is to have faith in Allah and strive in His cause with wealth and lives.

Verse 12

يَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ ذُنُوبَكُمْ وَيُدْخِلْكُمْ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ وَمَسَاكِنَ طَيِّبَةً فِي جَنَّاتِ عَدْنٍ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ الْفَوْزُ الْعَظِيمُ

He will forgive your sins, and admit you into Gardens under which rivers flow, and ˹house you in˺ splendid homes in the Gardens of Eternity. That is the ultimate triumph.

If you - O believers - do what Allah has commanded you, He will cover your sins for you, and He will admit you into gardens beneath which rivers flow, and pure dwellings in gardens of perpetual residence that will never cease. That is the supreme triumph, after which there is no triumph. And another blessing for you - O believers - that you love is victory from Allah that will come to you, and a speedy conquest that will be accomplished at your hands. And give glad tidings to the believers - O Prophet - of victory and conquest in this world, and Paradise in the Hereafter.

Allah promises forgiveness, gardens in Paradise, and eternal homes as a reward for believers' actions.

Verse 13

وَأُخْرَىٰ تُحِبُّونَهَا ۖ نَصْرٌ مِنَ اللَّهِ وَفَتْحٌ قَرِيبٌ ۗ وَبَشِّرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

˹He will also give you˺ another favour that you long for: help from Allah and an imminent victory. ˹So˺ give good news ˹O Prophet˺ to the believers.

If you - O believers - do what Allah has commanded you, He will cover your sins for you, and He will admit you into gardens beneath which rivers flow, and pure dwellings in gardens of perpetual residence that will never cease. That is the supreme triumph, after which there is no triumph. And another blessing for you - O believers - that you love is victory from Allah that will come to you, and a speedy conquest that will be accomplished at your hands. And give glad tidings to the believers - O Prophet - of victory and conquest in this world, and Paradise in the Hereafter.

Believers are also promised help from Allah and an imminent victory as a blessing in this life. The Prophet is commanded to give glad tidings to the believers.

Verse 14

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا كُونُوا أَنْصَارَ اللَّهِ كَمَا قَالَ عِيسَى ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ لِلْحَوَارِيِّينَ مَنْ أَنْصَارِي إِلَى اللَّهِ ۖ قَالَ الْحَوَارِيُّونَ نَحْنُ أَنْصَارُ اللَّهِ ۖ فَآمَنَتْ طَائِفَةٌ مِنْ بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ وَكَفَرَتْ طَائِفَةٌ ۖ فَأَيَّدْنَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا عَلَىٰ عَدُوِّهِمْ فَأَصْبَحُوا ظَاهِرِينَ

O believers! Stand up for Allah, as Jesus, son of Mary, asked the disciples, “Who will stand up with me for Allah?” The disciples replied, “We will stand up for Allah.” Then a group from the Children of Israel believed while another disbelieved. We then supported the believers against their enemies, so they prevailed.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, be supporters of Allah's religion, as the disciples of Jesus were supporters of Allah's religion when Jesus said to them, "Who among you will be my supporter and helper in what draws near to Allah?" They said, "We are the supporters of Allah's religion." So a group from the Children of Israel were guided, and a group went astray. Then We supported those who believed in Allah and His Messenger and helped them against those who opposed them from the sects of the Christians, and they became dominant over them; that was through the mission of Muhammad, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him.

Believers are encouraged to support Allah's religion as Jesus' disciples did. Some believed and others disbelieved. Ultimately, Allah supported the believers, giving them victory.
