Sura 067

Verse 1

تَبَارَكَ الَّذِي بِيَدِهِ الْمُلْكُ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

Blessed is the One in Whose Hands rests all authority. And He is Most Capable of everything.

The bounty and goodness of Allah have increased on all of His creation, the One in whose hand is the dominion of this world and the Hereafter and their authority, His command and decree are effective in both, and He is capable of all things. It is derived from the verse the affirmation of the attribute of the Hand for Allah, glory be to Him, in a manner that befits His Majesty.

This verse declares that Allah is the source of all authority and power. He controls all aspects of existence, and nothing is beyond His capabilities.

Verse 2

الَّذِي خَلَقَ الْمَوْتَ وَالْحَيَاةَ لِيَبْلُوَكُمْ أَيُّكُمْ أَحْسَنُ عَمَلًا ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْغَفُورُ

˹He is the One˺ Who created death and life in order to test which of you is best in deeds. And He is the Almighty, All-Forgiving.

The One who created death and life, so that He may test you - O people - which of you is best in deeds and most sincere? And He is the Mighty, Whom nothing can overcome, the Forgiving to those who repent from His servants. And in the verse there is encouragement to perform acts of obedience, and discouragement from committing sins.

Allah created life and death to test people through their actions. He is the Almighty, capable of all things, and is also the Most Forgiving for those who repent.

Verse 3

الَّذِي خَلَقَ سَبْعَ سَمَاوَاتٍ طِبَاقًا ۖ مَا تَرَىٰ فِي خَلْقِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ مِنْ تَفَاوُتٍ ۖ فَارْجِعِ الْبَصَرَ هَلْ تَرَىٰ مِنْ فُطُورٍ

˹He is the One˺ Who created seven heavens, one above the other. You will never see any imperfection in the creation of the Most Compassionate. So look again: do you see any flaws?

The One who created seven heavens in layers, one above the other. You see no inconsistencies or disparities in the creation of the Most Gracious - O observer. So look again at the sky, do you see in it any cracks or fissures?

Allah created the seven heavens perfectly, without any flaws. This verse invites reflection on the perfection and harmony of Allah's creation.

Verse 4

ثُمَّ ارْجِعِ الْبَصَرَ كَرَّتَيْنِ يَنْقَلِبْ إِلَيْكَ الْبَصَرُ خَاسِئًا وَهُوَ حَسِيرٌ

Then look again and again—your sight will return frustrated and weary.

Then look again, time after time, your sight will return to you, humbled and weary, unable to find any flaw, and it is tired and fatigued.

Repeated attempts to find imperfections in Allah's creation will ultimately fail, leaving one's gaze humbled and weary, emphasizing the flawless nature of His work.

Verse 5

وَلَقَدْ زَيَّنَّا السَّمَاءَ الدُّنْيَا بِمَصَابِيحَ وَجَعَلْنَاهَا رُجُومًا لِلشَّيَاطِينِ ۖ وَأَعْتَدْنَا لَهُمْ عَذَابَ السَّعِيرِ

And indeed, We adorned the lowest heaven with ˹stars like˺ lamps, and made them ˹as missiles˺ for stoning ˹eavesdropping˺ devils, for whom We have also prepared the torment of the Blaze.

And We have adorned the nearest heaven that the eyes see with great, luminous stars, and We have made them missiles to pelt devils who eavesdrop, and We have prepared for them in the Hereafter the punishment of the blazing Fire, whose heat they will suffer.

The lowest heaven is adorned with stars, which also serve as missiles against devils who try to eavesdrop on divine knowledge. A severe punishment in the Hereafter awaits these devils.

Verse 6

وَلِلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِرَبِّهِمْ عَذَابُ جَهَنَّمَ ۖ وَبِئْسَ الْمَصِيرُ

Those who disbelieve in their Lord will suffer the punishment of Hell. What an evil destination!

And for those who disbelieve in their Creator is the punishment of Hell, and what an evil destination it is.

Those who disbelieve in their Lord will face the punishment of Hell, a terrible and evil destination.

Verse 7

إِذَا أُلْقُوا فِيهَا سَمِعُوا لَهَا شَهِيقًا وَهِيَ تَفُورُ

When they are tossed into it, they will hear its roaring as it boils over,

When these disbelievers are thrown into Hell, they will hear a terrible, horrific sound, as it seethes intensely.

The inhabitants of Hell will hear its terrifying roar as it boils intensely, vividly depicting the torment they face.

Verse 8

تَكَادُ تَمَيَّزُ مِنَ الْغَيْظِ ۖ كُلَّمَا أُلْقِيَ فِيهَا فَوْجٌ سَأَلَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَا أَلَمْ يَأْتِكُمْ نَذِيرٌ

almost bursting in fury. Every time a group is cast into it, its keepers will ask them, “Did a warner not come to you?”

Hell almost bursts with rage at the disbelievers. Every time a group of people are thrown into it, those in charge of it will ask them reproachfully, “Did a messenger not come to you in the world, warning you about this punishment in which you now are?”

Hell almost bursts with rage at the disbelievers, and its keepers will reproach them by asking if they had been warned before.

Verse 9

قَالُوا بَلَىٰ قَدْ جَاءَنَا نَذِيرٌ فَكَذَّبْنَا وَقُلْنَا مَا نَزَّلَ اللَّهُ مِنْ شَيْءٍ إِنْ أَنْتُمْ إِلَّا فِي ضَلَالٍ كَبِيرٍ

They will reply, “Yes, a warner did come to us, but we denied and said, ‘Allah has revealed nothing. You are extremely astray.’”

They will answer, saying: “Yes, a messenger came to us from Allah and warned us, but we denied him, and we said regarding what he brought of verses: 'Allah has not sent down anything to any human being. You - O messengers - are nothing but far astray from the truth.”

The disbelievers confess that they had received warnings but denied them, and that they accused the messengers of being astray.

Verse 10

وَقَالُوا لَوْ كُنَّا نَسْمَعُ أَوْ نَعْقِلُ مَا كُنَّا فِي أَصْحَابِ السَّعِيرِ

And they will lament, “If only we had listened and reasoned, we would not be among the residents of the Blaze!”

And they will say, confessing: “If only we had listened with the listening of those who seek the truth, or if we had pondered over what we were called to, we would not be among the people of the Fire.”

The disbelievers lament their failure to listen and reason, which led them to become inhabitants of Hell.

Verse 11

فَاعْتَرَفُوا بِذَنْبِهِمْ فَسُحْقًا لِأَصْحَابِ السَّعِيرِ

And so they will confess their sins. So away with the residents of the Blaze!

So they have confessed their denial and disbelief, for which they deserved the punishment of the Fire. So away with the people of the Fire from the mercy of Allah.

Having confessed their sins, the disbelievers are condemned to Hell, far removed from Allah's mercy.

Verse 12

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يَخْشَوْنَ رَبَّهُمْ بِالْغَيْبِ لَهُمْ مَغْفِرَةٌ وَأَجْرٌ كَبِيرٌ

Indeed, those in awe of their Lord without seeing Him will have forgiveness and a mighty reward.

Indeed, those who fear their Lord, and worship Him, and do not disobey Him while they are unseen by people, and they fear the punishment in the Hereafter before seeing it, for them is forgiveness from Allah for their sins, and a great reward, which is Paradise.

Those who fear their Lord, even without seeing Him, will receive forgiveness and a great reward in Paradise.

Verse 13

وَأَسِرُّوا قَوْلَكُمْ أَوِ اجْهَرُوا بِهِ ۖ إِنَّهُ عَلِيمٌ بِذَاتِ الصُّدُورِ

Whether you speak secretly or openly—He surely knows best what is ˹hidden˺ in the heart.

And conceal your speech - O people - in any matter of your affairs or declare it; they are equal before Allah. Indeed, He, the Exalted, is All-Knowing of what is within the breasts, so how can your words and deeds be hidden from Him?

Whether you speak quietly or openly, Allah knows what is hidden in your hearts. Nothing is concealed from Him.

Verse 14

أَلَا يَعْلَمُ مَنْ خَلَقَ وَهُوَ اللَّطِيفُ الْخَبِيرُ

How could He not know His Own creation? For He ˹alone˺ is the Most Subtle, All-Aware.

Does not the Lord of the worlds know His creation and their affairs, and He is the One who created them, perfected their creation, and made it beautiful? And He is the Subtle with His servants, the All-Aware of them and their deeds.

How can Allah not know His creation? He is the Most Subtle and All-Aware, knowing everything about His creation.

Verse 15

هُوَ الَّذِي جَعَلَ لَكُمُ الْأَرْضَ ذَلُولًا فَامْشُوا فِي مَنَاكِبِهَا وَكُلُوا مِنْ رِزْقِهِ ۖ وَإِلَيْهِ النُّشُورُ

He is the One Who smoothed out the earth for you, so move about in its regions and eat from His provisions. And to Him is the resurrection ˹of all˺.

Allah alone is the One who made the earth easy and paved for you to settle upon it, so walk in its regions and sides, and eat from the provision of Allah that He brings forth for you from it, and to Him alone is the resurrection from your graves for reckoning and recompense. And in the verse is an indication to seeking provision and earnings, and in it is a sign that Allah is the only true God, without partner, and of His power, and a reminder of His blessings, and a warning against relying on this world.

Allah made the earth easy for you to live on and benefit from. Move about, eat from His provisions, and remember that to Him is the resurrection.

Verse 16

أَأَمِنْتُمْ مَنْ فِي السَّمَاءِ أَنْ يَخْسِفَ بِكُمُ الْأَرْضَ فَإِذَا هِيَ تَمُورُ

Do you feel secure that the One Who is in heaven will not cause the earth to swallow you up as it quakes violently?

Have you felt secure - O disbelievers of "Mecca" - from Allah, who is above the heaven, that He will cause the earth to swallow you, and then it will tremble with you until you perish? Have you felt secure from Allah, who is above the heaven, that He will send upon you a wind that pelts you with small stones? Then you will know - O disbelievers - how was My warning to you when you witness the punishment? And knowledge will not benefit you at that time. And in the verse is an affirmation of the highness of Allah, as befits His Majesty, Glory be to Him.

Do you feel safe from Allah causing the earth to swallow you up? Do you feel secure from His punishment?

Verse 17

أَمْ أَمِنْتُمْ مَنْ فِي السَّمَاءِ أَنْ يُرْسِلَ عَلَيْكُمْ حَاصِبًا ۖ فَسَتَعْلَمُونَ كَيْفَ نَذِيرِ

Or do you feel secure that the One Who is in heaven will not unleash upon you a storm of stones. Only then would you know how ˹serious˺ My warning was!

Have you felt secure - O disbelievers of "Mecca" - from Allah, who is above the heaven, that He will cause the earth to swallow you, and then it will tremble with you until you perish? Have you felt secure from Allah, who is above the heaven, that He will send upon you a wind that pelts you with small stones? Then you will know - O disbelievers - how was My warning to you when you witness the punishment? And knowledge will not benefit you at that time. And in the verse is an affirmation of the highness of Allah, as befits His Majesty, Glory be to Him.

Do you feel secure from Allah sending a storm of stones upon you? You will then realize the seriousness of Allah's warning.

Verse 18

وَلَقَدْ كَذَّبَ الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ فَكَيْفَ كَانَ نَكِيرِ

And certainly those before them denied ˹as well˺, then how severe was My response!

And those before the disbelievers of "Mecca" - such as the people of Noah, 'Aad, and Thamud - denied their messengers, so how was My denial of them, and My changing of what blessings they had by sending the punishment upon them and destroying them?

Those before you also denied the messengers, and their denial resulted in severe punishment. Learn from their fate.

Verse 19

أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا إِلَى الطَّيْرِ فَوْقَهُمْ صَافَّاتٍ وَيَقْبِضْنَ ۚ مَا يُمْسِكُهُنَّ إِلَّا الرَّحْمَٰنُ ۚ إِنَّهُ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ بَصِيرٌ

Have they not seen the birds above them, spreading and folding their wings? None holds them up except the Most Compassionate. Indeed, He is All-Seeing of everything.

Have these disbelievers not observed the birds above them, spreading their wings when they fly in the air and drawing them into their sides sometimes? None keeps them from falling at that time except the Most Merciful. Indeed, He is All-Seeing of everything; no deficiency or discrepancy is seen in His creation. Rather, who is this that, in your opinion - O disbelievers - is a party for you to help you other than the Most Merciful if He intends evil for you? The disbelievers are only in deception and misguidance from Satan. Rather, who is this alleged provider who provides for you if Allah withholds His provision and prevents it from you? Rather, the disbelievers have persisted in their transgression and misguidance in defiance, arrogance, and aversion to the truth, not listening to it, nor following it.

Have they not seen the birds flying? Only Allah's compassion keeps them aloft. He is All-Seeing of everything.

What power can help you other than Allah? The disbelievers are in delusion.

Who will provide for you if Allah withholds His provision? The disbelievers persist in their arrogance and turning away from the truth.

Verse 22

أَفَمَنْ يَمْشِي مُكِبًّا عَلَىٰ وَجْهِهِ أَهْدَىٰ أَمَّنْ يَمْشِي سَوِيًّا عَلَىٰ صِرَاطٍ مُسْتَقِيمٍ

Who is ˹rightly˺ guided: the one who crawls facedown or the one who walks upright on the Straight Path?

Then, is one who walks with his face down, not knowing where he goes nor how he proceeds, more rightly guided on the path and better guided, or one who walks upright, standing tall, safe on a clear path with no crookedness in it? And this is a parable that Allah has given for the disbeliever and the believer.

Is the one who walks blindly, face down, better guided than the one who walks upright on the Straight Path? This is a parable for the disbeliever and the believer.

Verse 23

قُلْ هُوَ الَّذِي أَنْشَأَكُمْ وَجَعَلَ لَكُمُ السَّمْعَ وَالْأَبْصَارَ وَالْأَفْئِدَةَ ۖ قَلِيلًا مَا تَشْكُرُونَ

Little - O disbelievers - do you give thanks for these blessings to your Lord who has bestowed them upon you. Say to them: Allah is the One who created you and dispersed you on the earth, and to Him alone you will be gathered after this dispersion for reckoning and recompense.

Allah brought you into existence and gave you hearing, sight, and intellect. Yet, you are hardly thankful.

Verse 24

˹Also˺ say, “He is the One Who has dispersed you ˹all˺ over the earth, and to Him you will ˹all˺ be gathered.”

Say to them: Allah is the One who created you and dispersed you on the earth, and to Him alone you will be gathered after this dispersion for reckoning and recompense.

Allah is the One who dispersed you on earth, and to Him you will all be gathered.

Verse 25

وَيَقُولُونَ مَتَىٰ هَٰذَا الْوَعْدُ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صَادِقِينَ

˹Still˺ they ask ˹the believers˺, “When will this threat come to pass, if what you say is true?”

Say - O Messenger - to these: Indeed, knowledge of the time of the Hour is specific to Allah, and I am only a warner for you, warning you of the consequence of your disbelief, and I am making clear to you what Allah has commanded me to make clear to the utmost extent.

The disbelievers ask when the threat will come. The timing of that is with Allah, and the Prophet is sent with a clear warning.

Verse 26

قُلْ إِنَّمَا الْعِلْمُ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ وَإِنَّمَا أَنَا نَذِيرٌ مُبِينٌ

Say - O Messenger - to these: Indeed, knowledge of the time of the Hour is specific to Allah, and I am only a warner for you, warning you of the consequence of your disbelief, and I am making clear to you what Allah has commanded me to make clear to the utmost extent.

The knowledge of the Hour is with Allah alone. The Prophet's role is to give a clear warning.

Verse 27

فَلَمَّا رَأَوْهُ زُلْفَةً سِيئَتْ وُجُوهُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا وَقِيلَ هَٰذَا الَّذِي كُنْتُمْ بِهِ تَدَّعُونَ

Then when they see the torment drawing near, the faces of the disbelievers will become gloomy, and it will be said ˹to them˺, “This is what you claimed would never come.”

When the disbelievers saw Allah's punishment approaching them and witnessed it, humiliation and gloom appeared on their faces, and it was said to them in rebuke: “This is what you used to seek to hasten in the world.”

This verse describes the disbelievers' dismay when they witness the approaching punishment they once denied. Their faces will be filled with gloom and they will be rebuked for their disbelief.

Verse 28

قُلْ أَرَأَيْتُمْ إِنْ أَهْلَكَنِيَ اللَّهُ وَمَنْ مَعِيَ أَوْ رَحِمَنَا فَمَنْ يُجِيرُ الْكَافِرِينَ مِنْ عَذَابٍ أَلِيمٍ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Consider this: whether Allah causes me and those with me to die or shows us mercy, who will save the disbelievers from a painful punishment?”

Say, [O Messenger], to these disbelievers: “Inform me, if Allah causes me to die and those with me of the believers as you wish, or if He has mercy on us and delays our end and keeps us safe from His punishment, then who is it that will protect you and prevent you from a painful, agonizing punishment?”

The Prophet is instructed to ask the disbelievers who will protect them from punishment if Allah chooses to cause him and the believers to die or if He has mercy on them instead. This highlights that only Allah has power over life and death and only He can prevent punishment.

Verse 29

قُلْ هُوَ الرَّحْمَٰنُ آمَنَّا بِهِ وَعَلَيْهِ تَوَكَّلْنَا ۖ فَسَتَعْلَمُونَ مَنْ هُوَ فِي ضَلَالٍ مُبِينٍ

Say, “He is the Most Compassionate—in Him ˹alone˺ we believe, and in Him ˹alone˺ we trust. You will soon know who is clearly astray.”

Say, “Allah is the Most Merciful; we have believed in Him, and we have acted according to His law, and we have obeyed Him, and in Him alone we have relied in all our affairs. So you will know - O disbelievers - when the punishment descends: which of the two parties, us or you, is in clear error from Allah’s straight path?”

This verse emphasizes the believers’ faith and trust in Allah, the Most Compassionate. It challenges the disbelievers to recognize who is truly astray, warning them of the consequences of their disbelief.

Verse 30

قُلْ أَرَأَيْتُمْ إِنْ أَصْبَحَ مَاؤُكُمْ غَوْرًا فَمَنْ يَأْتِيكُمْ بِمَاءٍ مَعِينٍ

Say, “Consider this: if your water were to sink ˹into the earth˺, then who ˹else˺ could bring you flowing water?”

Say, [O Messenger], to these polytheists: “Inform me, if your water that you drink from were to recede into the earth so that you cannot reach it by any means, then who other than Allah would bring you flowing water on the surface of the earth, visible to the eyes?”

This verse poses a rhetorical question, asking who else besides Allah could restore their water if it were to vanish. It is intended to highlight Allah’s control over natural resources and the dependence of humans on His power.
