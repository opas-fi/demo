Verse 1

إِذَا الشَّمْسُ كُوِّرَتْ

When the sun is put out,

When the sun is wrapped up and its light is gone, and when the stars are scattered, and their light is gone, and when the mountains are moved from the face of the earth and become scattered dust, and when the pregnant she-camels are left unattended and neglected, and when the wild animals are gathered and mixed; so that Allah may retaliate from some for others, and when the seas are set ablaze, and become on their enormity a raging fire, and when the souls are paired with their like and counterparts, and when the girl buried alive is asked on the Day of Resurrection as a question of solace for her and a rebuke for her burier: for what sin was she buried? And when the scrolls of deeds are displayed, and when the sky is uprooted and removed from its place, and when the Fire is kindled and ignited, and when Paradise, the abode of bliss, is brought near to its people, the righteous, when that occurs, every soul will be certain and find what it has put forth of good or evil.

This verse describes the cataclysmic events of the Day of Judgment, starting with the sun being extinguished.
