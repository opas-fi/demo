Verse 1

الم

Alif-Lãm-Mĩm.

Its discussion has preceded in the beginning of Surat Al-Baqarah.

This verse begins with the Arabic letters *Alif-Lam-Mim*, the meaning of which is known only to Allah. These letters appear at the beginning of several surahs.

Verse 2

اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ

Allah! There is no god ˹worthy of worship˺ except Him—the Ever-Living, All-Sustaining.

He is Allah, there is no deity worthy of worship except Him, the One characterized by complete life as befits His majesty, the Sustainer of all things.

This verse emphasizes the absolute oneness of Allah. He is the Ever-Living, meaning He has eternal life, and the All-Sustaining, meaning He maintains all of creation.

Verse 3

نَزَّلَ عَلَيْكَ الْكِتَابَ بِالْحَقِّ مُصَدِّقًا لِمَا بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ وَأَنْزَلَ التَّوْرَاةَ وَالْإِنْجِيلَ

He has revealed to you ˹O Prophet˺ the Book in truth, confirming what came before it, as He revealed the Torah and the Gospel

He sent down upon you the Quran with the truth in which there is no doubt, confirming what was before it of scriptures and messengers, and He sent down the Torah to Moses, peace be upon him, and the Gospel to Jesus, peace be upon him, before the revelation of the Quran; to guide the righteous to faith, and the righteousness of their religion and worldly life, and He sent down that which differentiates between truth and falsehood. And those who disbelieve in the revealed verses of Allah, for them is a great punishment. And Allah is Mighty, not overcome, Possessor of vengeance for those who deny His proofs and evidence, and His Oneness in divinity.

Allah revealed the Quran to Prophet Muhammad, confirming the truth of previous scriptures like the Torah and the Gospel. The Quran serves as a guide for humanity.

Verse 4

previously, as a guide for people, and ˹also˺ revealed the Standard ˹to distinguish between right and wrong˺. Surely those who reject Allah’s revelations will suffer a severe torment. For Allah is Almighty, capable of punishment.

He sent down upon you the Quran with the truth in which there is no doubt, confirming what was before it of scriptures and messengers, and He sent down the Torah to Moses, peace be upon him, and the Gospel to Jesus, peace be upon him, before the revelation of the Quran; to guide the righteous to faith, and the righteousness of their religion and worldly life, and He sent down that which differentiates between truth and falsehood. And those who disbelieve in the revealed verses of Allah, for them is a great punishment. And Allah is Mighty, not overcome, Possessor of vengeance for those who deny His proofs and evidence, and His Oneness in divinity.

The Quran was revealed as a guide distinguishing right from wrong. Rejecting Allah’s revelations leads to a severe punishment, as Allah is Almighty and capable of retribution.

Verse 5

إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَخْفَىٰ عَلَيْهِ شَيْءٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا فِي السَّمَاءِ

Surely nothing on earth or in the heavens is hidden from Allah.

Indeed, Allah's knowledge encompasses all of creation, and nothing is hidden from Him on earth or in the heavens, whether little or much.

Nothing in the heavens or on Earth is hidden from Allah. His knowledge encompasses everything, highlighting His omniscience.

Verse 6

هُوَ الَّذِي يُصَوِّرُكُمْ فِي الْأَرْحَامِ كَيْفَ يَشَاءُ ۚ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

He is the One Who shapes you in the wombs of your mothers as He wills. There is no god ˹worthy of worship˺ except Him—the Almighty, All-Wise.

He is the One who forms you in the wombs of your mothers as He wills, of male and female, handsome and ugly, wretched and blessed, no deity is worthy of worship except Him, the Mighty who is not overcome, the Wise in His command and management.

Allah shapes each human in the womb as He wills, showcasing His power over creation. He is the Almighty, the All-Wise, and the only God worthy of worship.

Verse 7

هُوَ الَّذِي أَنْزَلَ عَلَيْكَ الْكِتَابَ مِنْهُ آيَاتٌ مُحْكَمَاتٌ هُنَّ أُمُّ الْكِتَابِ وَأُخَرُ مُتَشَابِهَاتٌ ۖ فَأَمَّا الَّذِينَ فِي قُلُوبِهِمْ زَيْغٌ فَيَتَّبِعُونَ مَا تَشَابَهَ مِنْهُ ابْتِغَاءَ الْفِتْنَةِ وَابْتِغَاءَ تَأْوِيلِهِ ۗ وَمَا يَعْلَمُ تَأْوِيلَهُ إِلَّا اللَّهُ ۗ وَالرَّاسِخُونَ فِي الْعِلْمِ يَقُولُونَ آمَنَّا بِهِ كُلٌّ مِنْ عِنْدِ رَبِّنَا ۗ وَمَا يَذَّكَّرُ إِلَّا أُولُو الْأَلْبَابِ

He is the One Who has revealed to you ˹O Prophet˺ the Book, of which some verses are precise—they are the foundation of the Book—while others are elusive. Those with deviant hearts follow the elusive verses seeking ˹to spread˺ doubt through their ˹false˺ interpretations—but none grasps their ˹full˺ meaning except Allah. As for those well-grounded in knowledge, they say, “We believe in this ˹Quran˺—it is all from our Lord.” But none will be mindful ˹of this˺ except people of reason.

He is the One who has sent down the Quran to you: from it are verses of clear meaning, they are the foundation of the Book to which one resorts when confused, and to which what contradicts it is referred. And from it are other verses that are ambiguous and can have several meanings, their intended meaning only becoming clear by combining them with the clear verses. So those whose hearts are diseased and deviant, because of their evil intentions, follow these ambiguous verses alone, to create doubts among the people, so that they mislead them, and to interpret them according to their false doctrines. And no one knows the true meanings of these verses except Allah. And those well-grounded in knowledge say: 'We believe in this Quran, all of it has come to us from our Lord through the tongue of His Messenger Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace', and they refer the ambiguous to the clear. And only those of sound intellect understand, comprehend, and ponder the meanings correctly.

The Quran has clear verses that form its foundation, and others that are open to interpretation. Those with doubts try to misinterpret these, but only Allah knows their true meaning. Those with knowledge believe it all comes from Allah.

Verse 8

رَبَّنَا لَا تُزِغْ قُلُوبَنَا بَعْدَ إِذْ هَدَيْتَنَا وَهَبْ لَنَا مِنْ لَدُنْكَ رَحْمَةً ۚ إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ الْوَهَّابُ

˹They say,˺ “Our Lord! Do not let our hearts deviate after you have guided us. Grant us Your mercy. You are indeed the Giver ˹of all bounties˺.

And they say: 'Our Lord, do not divert our hearts from faith after You have favored us with guidance to Your religion, and grant us from Your bounty a vast mercy, indeed You are the Bestower: the One of great bounty and giving, You give to whom You will without reckoning.'

This verse is a prayer asking Allah to keep believers steadfast in faith, and to grant His mercy. It acknowledges that Allah is the Giver of all bounties.

Verse 9

رَبَّنَا إِنَّكَ جَامِعُ النَّاسِ لِيَوْمٍ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يُخْلِفُ الْمِيعَادَ

Our Lord! You will certainly gather all humanity for the ˹promised˺ Day—about which there is no doubt. Surely Allah does not break His promise.”

Our Lord, we acknowledge and bear witness that You will gather mankind on a day in which there is no doubt, and it is the Day of Resurrection. Indeed, You do not break Your promise to Your servants.

This verse emphasizes the belief that Allah will gather all of humanity on the Day of Judgment. It affirms that Allah never breaks His promises.

Verse 10

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَنْ تُغْنِيَ عَنْهُمْ أَمْوَالُهُمْ وَلَا أَوْلَادُهُمْ مِنَ اللَّهِ شَيْئًا ۖ وَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمْ وَقُودُ النَّارِ

Indeed, neither the wealth nor children of the disbelievers will be of any benefit to them against Allah—and they will be the fuel for the Fire.

Indeed, those who disbelieved in the true religion and denied it, their wealth and their children will not avail them at all against the punishment of Allah if it befalls them in this world, nor will it ward it off from them in the Hereafter. And those are the fuel of the Fire on the Day of Resurrection.

This verse highlights that the wealth and children of disbelievers will not protect them from Allah's punishment. Instead, they will become fuel for the Hellfire.

Verse 11

كَدَأْبِ آلِ فِرْعَوْنَ وَالَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ ۚ كَذَّبُوا بِآيَاتِنَا فَأَخَذَهُمُ اللَّهُ بِذُنُوبِهِمْ ۗ وَاللَّهُ شَدِيدُ الْعِقَابِ

Their fate will be like that of the people of Pharaoh and those before them—they all rejected Our signs, so Allah seized them for their sins. And Allah is severe in punishment.

The case of the disbelievers in their denial and what befalls them is like the case of the people of Pharaoh and those before them from the disbelievers; they denied the clear signs of Allah, so He hastened their punishment because of their denial and stubbornness. And Allah is severe in punishment for those who disbelieve in Him and deny His messengers.

The fate of these disbelievers is likened to the people of Pharaoh, who were destroyed for rejecting God's signs. Allah's punishment is severe for those who disbelieve.

Verse 12

قُلْ لِلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا سَتُغْلَبُونَ وَتُحْشَرُونَ إِلَىٰ جَهَنَّمَ ۚ وَبِئْسَ الْمِهَادُ

˹O Prophet!˺ Tell the disbelievers, “Soon you will be overpowered and driven to Hell—what an evil place to rest!”

Say, [O Messenger], to those who disbelieved from the Jews and others and who belittled your victory at Badr: Indeed, you will be defeated in this world and will die in disbelief, and you will be gathered to Hellfire to be a permanent bed for you, and what a wretched bed.

Disbelievers are warned of their impending defeat and being driven to Hell, which is described as a wretched place of rest.

Verse 13

قَدْ كَانَ لَكُمْ آيَةٌ فِي فِئَتَيْنِ الْتَقَتَا ۖ فِئَةٌ تُقَاتِلُ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ وَأُخْرَىٰ كَافِرَةٌ يَرَوْنَهُمْ مِثْلَيْهِمْ رَأْيَ الْعَيْنِ ۚ وَاللَّهُ يُؤَيِّدُ بِنَصْرِهِ مَنْ يَشَاءُ ۗ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَعِبْرَةً لِأُولِي الْأَبْصَارِ

Indeed, there was a sign for you in the two armies that met in battle—one fighting for the cause of Allah and the other in denial. The believers saw their enemy twice their number. But Allah supports with His victory whoever He wills. Surely in this is a lesson for people of insight.

There was indeed for you [O Jews, the arrogant and defiant] a great sign in two groups that met in the battle of Badr: one group fighting for the sake of the religion of Allah, and they were Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, and his companions, and another group disbelieving in Allah, fighting for falsehood, seeing the believers in number twice as many as they were, with their own eyes. And Allah made that a reason for the Muslims' victory over them. And Allah supports with His victory whom He wills of His servants. Indeed, in this event is a great lesson for the people of insight who are guided to the judgment and deeds of Allah.

The Battle of Badr serves as a sign, where believers, though outnumbered, were supported by Allah to victory. This is a lesson for those with insight into Allah's power.

Verse 14

زُيِّنَ لِلنَّاسِ حُبُّ الشَّهَوَاتِ مِنَ النِّسَاءِ وَالْبَنِينَ وَالْقَنَاطِيرِ الْمُقَنْطَرَةِ مِنَ الذَّهَبِ وَالْفِضَّةِ وَالْخَيْلِ الْمُسَوَّمَةِ وَالْأَنْعَامِ وَالْحَرْثِ ۗ ذَٰلِكَ مَتَاعُ الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا ۖ وَاللَّهُ عِنْدَهُ حُسْنُ الْمَآبِ

The enjoyment of ˹worldly˺ desires—women, children, treasures of gold and silver, fine horses, cattle, and fertile land—has been made appealing to people. These are the pleasures of this worldly life, but with Allah is the finest destination.

Beautified for people is the love of that which they desire - of women and sons, heaped-up sums of gold and silver, fine branded horses, and cattle and tilled land. That is the enjoyment of worldly life, and Allah has with Him the best return, which is Paradise.

Worldly desires like wealth, children, and luxuries are made appealing. However, they are temporary pleasures of this life, and the ultimate reward is with Allah.

Verse 15

قُلْ أَؤُنَبِّئُكُمْ بِخَيْرٍ مِنْ ذَٰلِكُمْ ۚ لِلَّذِينَ اتَّقَوْا عِنْدَ رَبِّهِمْ جَنَّاتٌ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا وَأَزْوَاجٌ مُطَهَّرَةٌ وَرِضْوَانٌ مِنَ اللَّهِ ۗ وَاللَّهُ بَصِيرٌ بِالْعِبَادِ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Shall I inform you of what is better than ˹all of˺ this? Those mindful ˹of Allah˺ will have Gardens with their Lord under which rivers flow, to stay there forever, and pure spouses, along with Allah’s pleasure.” And Allah is All-Seeing of ˹His˺ servants,

Say, [O Messenger]: Shall I inform you of [something] better than that? For those who fear Allah will be gardens beneath which rivers flow, wherein they will abide eternally, and purified spouses, and greater than that: pleasure from Allah. And Allah is observant of [His] servants, knowing of their state, and He will reward them accordingly.

Believers are promised something better than worldly pleasures - Gardens in Paradise with rivers, pure spouses, and Allah's pleasure, highlighting the reward for those who are mindful of Allah.

Verse 16

الَّذِينَ يَقُولُونَ رَبَّنَا إِنَّنَا آمَنَّا فَاغْفِرْ لَنَا ذُنُوبَنَا وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ

These are the pious servants who say: We believe in You, and we follow Your messenger Muhammad, peace be upon him, so erase our sins and save us from the torment of the Fire.

This verse describes the believers' prayer, seeking forgiveness for their sins and protection from Hellfire after acknowledging their faith.

Verse 17

الصَّابِرِينَ وَالصَّادِقِينَ وَالْقَانِتِينَ وَالْمُنْفِقِينَ وَالْمُسْتَغْفِرِينَ بِالْأَسْحَارِ

˹It is they˺ who are patient, sincere, obedient, and charitable, and who pray for forgiveness before dawn.

They are the ones who are characterized by patience in acts of obedience, and abstaining from disobedience, and withstanding the painful decrees of Allah, and by truthfulness in words and deeds, and by complete obedience, and by spending secretly and openly, and by seeking forgiveness in the last part of the night; because it is a place where acceptance and supplication are answered.

The verse lists qualities of the righteous: patience, truthfulness, obedience, charity, and seeking forgiveness before dawn. It highlights the importance of consistent devotion and repentance.

Verse 18

شَهِدَ اللَّهُ أَنَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَالْمَلَائِكَةُ وَأُولُو الْعِلْمِ قَائِمًا بِالْقِسْطِ ۚ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

Allah ˹Himself˺ is a Witness that there is no god ˹worthy of worship˺ except Him—and so are the angels and people of knowledge. He is the Maintainer of justice. There is no god ˹worthy of worship˺ except Him—the Almighty, All-Wise.

Allah bears witness that He is the One and Only God, and He has coupled His testimony with the testimony of the angels and people of knowledge, concerning the greatest thing witnessed, which is His Oneness, may He be exalted, and His upholding of justice. There is no god but He, the Almighty, whom nothing He wills can prevent, the Wise in His words and deeds.

Allah testifies to His oneness, and the angels and people of knowledge also bear witness to this truth. Allah is the maintainer of justice, the Almighty, and the All-Wise. This verse emphasizes monotheism and divine justice.

Verse 19

إِنَّ الدِّينَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ الْإِسْلَامُ ۗ وَمَا اخْتَلَفَ الَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْكِتَابَ إِلَّا مِنْ بَعْدِ مَا جَاءَهُمُ الْعِلْمُ بَغْيًا بَيْنَهُمْ ۗ وَمَنْ يَكْفُرْ بِآيَاتِ اللَّهِ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ سَرِيعُ الْحِسَابِ

Certainly, Allah’s only Way is Islam. Those who were given the Scripture did not dispute ˹among themselves˺ out of mutual envy until knowledge came to them. Whoever denies Allah’s signs, then surely Allah is swift in reckoning.

Indeed, the religion that Allah has approved for His creation and which He sent His messengers with, and He does not accept other than it, is Islam. It is submission to Allah alone through obedience and surrendering to Him through servitude, and following the messengers in what Allah has sent them with at all times until they were sealed with Muhammad, peace be upon him, after whose mission Allah will not accept any religion other than the Islam which he was sent with. And the disagreement between the People of the Book, the Jews and Christians, did not occur, and they did not divide into sects and parties, except after the proof was established against them through the sending of messengers and the revelation of books; out of envy and jealousy seeking the worldly life. And whoever denies the revealed signs of Allah and His signs that indicate His Lordship and Divinity, then Allah is swift in reckoning, and He will recompense them for what they used to do.

Islam is the only true religion accepted by Allah. Disputes among the People of the Book arose out of envy after knowledge was given to them. Those who deny Allah's signs will face swift reckoning.

Verse 20

So if they dispute with you - O Messenger - the People of the Book about monotheism after you have established the proof against them, then say to them: I have devoted myself to Allah alone and do not associate anyone with Him, and so do those who follow me from the believers, they devote themselves to Allah and submit to Him. And say to them, and to the polytheists of the Arabs and others: if you submit, then you are on the straight path and the guidance and the truth, and if you turn away, then your reckoning is with Allah, and it is only incumbent upon me to convey, and I have conveyed to you and established the proof against you. And Allah is All-Seeing of His servants, nothing of their affairs is hidden from Him.

The Prophet is instructed to declare his submission to Allah and invite others to do the same. Guidance comes from submission to Allah, while the Prophet's duty is to deliver the message. Allah is aware of all His servants.

Verse 21

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يَكْفُرُونَ بِآيَاتِ اللَّهِ وَيَقْتُلُونَ النَّبِيِّينَ بِغَيْرِ حَقٍّ وَيَقْتُلُونَ الَّذِينَ يَأْمُرُونَ بِالْقِسْطِ مِنَ النَّاسِ فَبَشِّرْهُمْ بِعَذَابٍ أَلِيمٍ

Indeed, those who deny Allah’s signs, kill the prophets unjustly, and kill people who stand up for justice—give them good news of a painful punishment.

Indeed, those who deny the clear proofs and what the messengers brought, and kill the prophets of Allah unjustly without right, and kill those who command justice and following the path of the prophets, then give them glad tidings of a painful torment.

This verse condemns those who reject Allah's signs, unjustly kill prophets, and kill those who stand for justice. They are warned of a painful punishment.

Verse 22

أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ حَبِطَتْ أَعْمَالُهُمْ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ وَمَا لَهُمْ مِنْ نَاصِرِينَ

They are the ones whose deeds are wasted in this world and the Hereafter. And they will have no helpers.

Those are the ones whose deeds are in vain in this world and the Hereafter, so no deed of theirs is accepted, and they will have no helper to help them from the punishment of Allah.

The deeds of those who deny Allah are in vain, both in this world and the Hereafter. They will have no one to help them from Allah's punishment.

Verse 23

أَلَمْ تَرَ إِلَى الَّذِينَ أُوتُوا نَصِيبًا مِنَ الْكِتَابِ يُدْعَوْنَ إِلَىٰ كِتَابِ اللَّهِ لِيَحْكُمَ بَيْنَهُمْ ثُمَّ يَتَوَلَّىٰ فَرِيقٌ مِنْهُمْ وَهُمْ مُعْرِضُونَ

Have you not seen those who were given a portion of the Scriptures? Yet when they are invited to the Book of Allah to settle their disputes, some of them turn away heedlessly.

Have you not seen - O Messenger - something more amazing than the case of these Jews who were given by Allah a portion of the Scripture and knew that what you have brought is the truth? They are called to what has come in the Book of Allah - which is the Quran - to judge between them in what they differed about, but if it does not agree with their desires, many of them refuse the judgment of Allah, for it is their habit to turn away from the truth?
