Sura 086

Verse 1

وَالسَّمَاءِ وَالطَّارِقِ

By the heaven and the nightly star!

Allah, the Exalted, swears by the sky and the star that appears at night. And what will make you realize the greatness of this star? It is the shining, blazing star. There is no soul but that a guardian angel has been appointed over it, preserving its deeds to be judged on the Day of Resurrection.

This verse begins by Allah swearing by the sky and the star that appears at night. This is to emphasize the importance of what will follow.

Verse 5

فَلْيَنْظُرِ الْإِنْسَانُ مِمَّ خُلِقَ

Let people then consider what they were created from!

So let man, who denies the resurrection, look at what he was created from? To know that the recreation of man is not more difficult than his initial creation. He was created from a fluid discharged quickly in the womb, which comes from between the backbone of a man and the chest of a woman. Indeed, He who created man from this water is surely Able to return him to life after death.

This verse urges people to reflect on their creation, recognizing the power of Allah.

Verse 9

يَوْمَ تُبْلَى السَّرَائِرُ

on the Day all secrets will be disclosed.

On the Day when secrets will be tested, and the righteous will be distinguished from the corrupt, then man will have no power to defend himself, and he will have no helper to protect him from the punishment of Allah.

The Day of Judgment will disclose all secrets, exposing the truth of everyone's deeds.

On that Day, no one will have power or helper, highlighting complete dependence on Allah.

Indeed, the Qur'an is a decisive statement between truth and falsehood, and it is not a jest.
