Sura 025

Verse 1

تَبَارَكَ الَّذِي نَزَّلَ الْفُرْقَانَ عَلَىٰ عَبْدِهِ لِيَكُونَ لِلْعَالَمِينَ نَذِيرًا

Blessed is the One Who sent down the Standard to His servant, so that he may be a warner to the whole world.

Magnificent are the blessings of Allah, and abundant are His favors, and perfect are His attributes, Glory be to Him, the Exalted, Who sent down the Quran, which distinguishes between truth and falsehood, upon His servant Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, to be a messenger to mankind and jinn, warning them of Allah’s punishment.

Allah is blessed and exalted for revealing the Quran, the standard (Al-Furqan) for distinguishing right from wrong. This was sent down to Prophet Muhammad, who serves as a warner to all mankind and jinn.

Verse 2

الَّذِي لَهُ مُلْكُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ وَلَمْ يَتَّخِذْ وَلَدًا وَلَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُ شَرِيكٌ فِي الْمُلْكِ وَخَلَقَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ فَقَدَّرَهُ تَقْدِيرًا

˹Allah is˺ the One to Whom belongs the kingdom of the heavens and the earth, Who has never had ˹any˺ offspring, nor does He have a partner in ˹governing˺ the kingdom. He has created everything, ordaining it precisely.

He to Whom belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth, and Who has not taken a son, and Who has no partner in His dominion. And He is the One Who created everything, and He proportioned it according to what is suitable from creation, in accordance with what His wisdom requires, without deficiency or flaw.

Allah is the sole owner of the heavens and the earth. He has no offspring or partner in His dominion. He created everything with precision and perfect planning, according to His wisdom.

Verse 3

وَاتَّخَذُوا مِنْ دُونِهِ آلِهَةً لَا يَخْلُقُونَ شَيْئًا وَهُمْ يُخْلَقُونَ وَلَا يَمْلِكُونَ لِأَنْفُسِهِمْ ضَرًّا وَلَا نَفْعًا وَلَا يَمْلِكُونَ مَوْتًا وَلَا حَيَاةً وَلَا نُشُورًا

Yet they have taken besides Him gods who cannot create anything but are themselves created. Nor can they protect or benefit themselves. Nor can they control life, death, or resurrection.

The polytheists of the Arabs took deities besides Allah that cannot create anything, while Allah created them and created them, and they do not possess for themselves the power to repel harm or bring benefit, nor can they cause death or give life to the dead, or resurrect anyone from the dead alive from his grave.

This verse highlights the absurdity of worshipping idols. These idols cannot create, protect, or control anything, including life and death, demonstrating their powerlessness.

Verse 4

وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا إِنْ هَٰذَا إِلَّا إِفْكٌ افْتَرَاهُ وَأَعَانَهُ عَلَيْهِ قَوْمٌ آخَرُونَ ۖ فَقَدْ جَاءُوا ظُلْمًا وَزُورًا

The disbelievers say, “This ˹Quran˺ is nothing but a fabrication which he made up with the help of others.” Their claim is totally unjustified and untrue!

And those who disbelieved in Allah said: This Quran is but a lie and a fabrication which Muhammad has invented, and other people have helped him with it. They have committed a terrible injustice, and have come with a shameful falsehood; for the Quran is not something that a human being can invent.

The disbelievers accuse the Quran of being a fabricated lie, claiming the Prophet Muhammad made it up with help. This verse refutes their accusation by stating it is an injustice and a falsehood.

Verse 5

وَقَالُوا أَسَاطِيرُ الْأَوَّلِينَ اكْتَتَبَهَا فَهِيَ تُمْلَىٰ عَلَيْهِ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا

And they say, “˹These revelations are only˺ ancient fables which he has had written down, and they are rehearsed to him morning and evening.”

And they said about the Quran: It is the tales of the ancients written down in their books, which Muhammad has transcribed, and it is recited to him morning and evening.

The disbelievers also dismiss the Quran as ancient fables written down and recited to the Prophet, attempting to discredit its divine nature.

Verse 6

قُلْ أَنْزَلَهُ الَّذِي يَعْلَمُ السِّرَّ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ إِنَّهُ كَانَ غَفُورًا رَحِيمًا

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “This ˹Quran˺ has been revealed by the One Who knows the secrets of the heavens and the earth. Surely He is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.”

Say, [O Messenger], to these disbelievers: The One who revealed the Quran is Allah, whose knowledge encompasses what is in the heavens and the earth. Indeed, He is Forgiving to those who repent from sins and transgressions, and Merciful to them, as He did not hasten to punish them.

This verse emphasizes that the Quran is revealed by Allah, who knows all secrets of the heavens and the earth. Allah is Forgiving and Merciful, even to those who initially disbelieve.

Verse 7

وَقَالُوا مَالِ هَٰذَا الرَّسُولِ يَأْكُلُ الطَّعَامَ وَيَمْشِي فِي الْأَسْوَاقِ ۙ لَوْلَا أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مَلَكٌ فَيَكُونَ مَعَهُ نَذِيرًا

And they say ˹mockingly˺, “What kind of messenger is this who eats food and goes about in market-places ˹for a living˺? If only an angel had been sent down with him to be his co-warner,

And the polytheists said: What is with this one who claims to be the Messenger of Allah (meaning Muhammad, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him), who eats food like us and walks in the markets seeking livelihood? Why was not an angel sent down with him to testify to his truthfulness, or a treasure from wealth sent down to him from the sky, or a great garden for him to eat from its fruits? And these unjust disbelievers said: You believers are following only a man upon whom magic has overcome his mind.

The disbelievers question why the Prophet eats food and walks in markets, challenging his claim as a messenger. They desired an angel to accompany him or other signs as proof.

Verse 8

أَوْ يُلْقَىٰ إِلَيْهِ كَنْزٌ أَوْ تَكُونُ لَهُ جَنَّةٌ يَأْكُلُ مِنْهَا ۚ وَقَالَ الظَّالِمُونَ إِنْ تَتَّبِعُونَ إِلَّا رَجُلًا مَسْحُورًا

or a treasure had been cast down to him, or he had had a garden from which he may eat!” And the wrongdoers say ˹to the believers˺, “You are only following a bewitched man.”

And the polytheists said: What is with this one who claims to be the Messenger of Allah (meaning Muhammad, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him), who eats food like us and walks in the markets seeking livelihood? Why was not an angel sent down with him to testify to his truthfulness, or a treasure from wealth sent down to him from the sky, or a great garden for him to eat from its fruits? And these unjust disbelievers said: You believers are following only a man upon whom magic has overcome his mind.

The disbelievers demand a treasure or garden for the Prophet to prove his authenticity, and they accuse believers of following a bewitched man, showing their rejection of the message.

Verse 9

انْظُرْ كَيْفَ ضَرَبُوا لَكَ الْأَمْثَالَ فَضَلُّوا فَلَا يَسْتَطِيعُونَ سَبِيلًا

See ˹O Prophet˺ how they call you names! So they have gone so ˹far˺ astray that they cannot find the ˹Right˺ Way.

Look, [O Messenger], how the deniers have said such strange things about you, which resemble - in their strangeness - parables; in order to reach a point of denying you? So they have become far removed from the truth, and they will find no way to it; to correct what they have said about you from falsehood and slander.

This verse points out how the disbelievers used strange and baseless accusations against the Prophet, showing how far astray they have become and their inability to find the truth.

Verse 10

تَبَارَكَ الَّذِي إِنْ شَاءَ جَعَلَ لَكَ خَيْرًا مِنْ ذَٰلِكَ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ وَيَجْعَلْ لَكَ قُصُورًا

Blessed is the One Who—if He wills—can give you far better than ˹all˺ that: gardens under which rivers flow, and palaces as well.

Great are the blessings of Allah, and abundant are His bounties, Who, if He willed, would have made for you - O Messenger - better than what they wished for you, for He would have made for you in this world many gardens through which rivers flow, and He would have made for you in them great palaces.

This verse emphasizes Allah's power to grant the Prophet far more than what the disbelievers desired, including beautiful gardens and palaces.

Verse 11

بَلْ كَذَّبُوا بِالسَّاعَةِ ۖ وَأَعْتَدْنَا لِمَنْ كَذَّبَ بِالسَّاعَةِ سَعِيرًا

In fact, they deny the Hour. And for the deniers of the Hour, We have prepared a blazing Fire.

And they did not deny you because you eat food and walk in the markets, but rather they denied the Day of Resurrection and what it contains of recompense, and We have prepared for those who deny the Hour a blazing fire that will be fueled by them.

The core issue is that the disbelievers reject the Day of Judgment. For those who reject it, Allah has prepared a blazing fire.

Verse 12

إِذَا رَأَتْهُمْ مِنْ مَكَانٍ بَعِيدٍ سَمِعُوا لَهَا تَغَيُّظًا وَزَفِيرًا

Once it sees them from a distance, they will hear it fuming and growling.

When the Fire sees these deniers on the Day of Resurrection from a distant place, they will hear the sound of its boiling and roaring, from the intensity of its rage towards them.

This verse describes Hellfire's intense rage and the terrifying sounds it makes when it sees the disbelievers from afar.

Verse 13

وَإِذَا أُلْقُوا مِنْهَا مَكَانًا ضَيِّقًا مُقَرَّنِينَ دَعَوْا هُنَالِكَ ثُبُورًا

And when they are tossed into a narrow place inside ˹Hell˺, chained together, then and there they will cry out for ˹instant˺ destruction.

And when they are thrown into a narrow place of Hell - and their hands are chained to their necks - they will call for destruction upon themselves to escape it.

This verse depicts the disbelievers being thrown into a confined space in Hell, chained, and desperately wishing for death to escape their torment.

Verse 14

لَا تَدْعُوا الْيَوْمَ ثُبُورًا وَاحِدًا وَادْعُوا ثُبُورًا كَثِيرًا

˹They will be told,˺ “Do not cry only once for destruction, but cry many times over!”

Then it will be said to them in despair, 'Do not call for destruction today only once, but many times, for that will only increase your grief, and there is no escape for you.'

The disbelievers are told that their cries for destruction are futile and that they will face unending suffering.

Verse 15

قُلْ أَذَٰلِكَ خَيْرٌ أَمْ جَنَّةُ الْخُلْدِ الَّتِي وُعِدَ الْمُتَّقُونَ ۚ كَانَتْ لَهُمْ جَزَاءً وَمَصِيرًا

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Is this better or the Garden of Eternity which the righteous have been promised, as a reward and ˹an ultimate˺ destination?

Say to them, [O Messenger], 'Is this Fire that has been described to you better, or the Garden of everlasting bliss that those who fear the punishment of their Lord have been promised? It is their reward for their deeds, and a final destination they will return to in the Hereafter?'

The Prophet is instructed to ask whether the torment of Hell is better than the eternal Paradise promised to the righteous.

Verse 16

لَهُمْ فِيهَا مَا يَشَاءُونَ خَالِدِينَ ۚ كَانَ عَلَىٰ رَبِّكَ وَعْدًا مَسْئُولًا

There they will have whatever they wish for, forever. That is a promise ˹to be sought after˺, binding on your Lord.”

For these obedient ones in Paradise is whatever they desire of the delights of bliss, their enjoyment in it is everlasting. Their entry into it is a promise binding on your Lord, [O Messenger], which the righteous servants of Allah ask for, and Allah never breaks His promise.

In Paradise, the righteous will have everything they desire for eternity, a promise from Allah that will be fulfilled.

Verse 17

وَيَوْمَ يَحْشُرُهُمْ وَمَا يَعْبُدُونَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ فَيَقُولُ أَأَنْتُمْ أَضْلَلْتُمْ عِبَادِي هَٰؤُلَاءِ أَمْ هُمْ ضَلُّوا السَّبِيلَ

˹Watch for˺ the Day He will gather them along with what they used to worship besides Allah, and ask ˹the objects of worship˺, “Was it you who misled these servants of Mine, or did they stray from the Way ˹on their own˺?”

And on the Day of Resurrection, Allah will gather the polytheists and what they used to worship besides Him, and He will say to those who were worshipped, 'Did you mislead these servants of Mine from the path of truth and order them to worship you, or did they stray from the path, so they worshipped you of their own accord?'

On the Day of Judgment, Allah will gather the polytheists and their idols and ask the idols if they led people astray.

Verse 18

قَالُوا سُبْحَانَكَ مَا كَانَ يَنْبَغِي لَنَا أَنْ نَتَّخِذَ مِنْ دُونِكَ مِنْ أَوْلِيَاءَ وَلَٰكِنْ مَتَّعْتَهُمْ وَآبَاءَهُمْ حَتَّىٰ نَسُوا الذِّكْرَ وَكَانُوا قَوْمًا بُورًا

They will say, “Glory be to You! It was not right for ˹others like˺ us to take any lords besides You, but You allowed enjoyment for them and their forefathers ˹for so long˺ that they forgot ˹Your˺ remembrance and became a doomed people.”

Those who were worshipped besides Allah will say, 'Glory be to You, O our Lord, far removed from what these people did. It is not right for us to take besides You any allies that we take as friends, but You granted these polytheists and their fathers enjoyment in wealth and well-being in the world until they forgot Your remembrance, and thus associated others with You in worship, and they were a doomed people overcome by misery and abandonment.'

The idols will deny leading people astray, stating that it was not for them to be worshiped alongside Allah, and that those people were led astray because of the enjoyment they were granted in this life.

Verse 19

فَقَدْ كَذَّبُوكُمْ بِمَا تَقُولُونَ فَمَا تَسْتَطِيعُونَ صَرْفًا وَلَا نَصْرًا ۚ وَمَنْ يَظْلِمْ مِنْكُمْ نُذِقْهُ عَذَابًا كَبِيرًا

˹The doomed will be told˺, “Your gods have clearly denied your claims. So now you can neither ward off ˹the punishment˺ nor get any help.” And whoever of you does wrong, We will make them taste a horrible punishment.

Then it will be said to the polytheists, 'These whom you worshipped have indeed proven you liars in your claims against them, so now you are unable to avert the punishment from yourselves, nor can you find support for yourselves. And whoever associates anything with Allah, and thus wrongs himself by worshipping other than Allah, and dies upon that, Allah will punish him with a severe punishment.'

The polytheists will be told their idols are denying them, and they will face punishment. Those who wrong themselves by worshipping other than Allah will receive a severe punishment.

Verse 20

وَمَا أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ مِنَ الْمُرْسَلِينَ إِلَّا إِنَّهُمْ لَيَأْكُلُونَ الطَّعَامَ وَيَمْشُونَ فِي الْأَسْوَاقِ ۗ وَجَعَلْنَا بَعْضَكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ فِتْنَةً أَتَصْبِرُونَ ۗ وَكَانَ رَبُّكَ بَصِيرًا

We never sent any messenger before you ˹O Prophet˺, who did not eat food and go about in market-places. We have made some of you a trial for others. Will you ˹not then˺ be patient? And your Lord is All-Seeing.

And We did not send before you, [O Messenger], any of Our messengers except that they were humans, eating food and walking in the markets. And We have made some of you, [O people], a trial for others through guidance and misguidance, wealth and poverty, and health and sickness. Will you be patient, and fulfill what Allah has obligated upon you, and give thanks to Him, so your Master will reward you? Or will you not be patient, and thus deserve punishment? And your Lord, [O Messenger], is All-Seeing of whoever is impatient or patient, and whoever disbelieves or is grateful.

All messengers were humans who ate and went to marketplaces. Allah tests people through different circumstances, and asks if people will be patient. Allah is All-Seeing.

Verse 21

وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ لَا يَرْجُونَ لِقَاءَنَا لَوْلَا أُنْزِلَ عَلَيْنَا الْمَلَائِكَةُ أَوْ نَرَىٰ رَبَّنَا ۗ لَقَدِ اسْتَكْبَرُوا فِي أَنْفُسِهِمْ وَعَتَوْا عُتُوًّا كَبِيرًا

Those who do not expect to meet Us say, “If only the angels were sent down to us, or we could see our Lord!” They have certainly been carried away by their arrogance and have entirely exceeded all limits.

And those who do not expect to meet their Lord after their death, because of their denial of Him, said: “Why have the angels not been sent down to us, to inform us that Muhammad is truthful, or (why) do we not see our Lord with our own eyes, so He informs us of his truthfulness in his message?” They have become proud of themselves and arrogant, where they have dared to utter such a statement, and they have transgressed the limit in their rebellion and disbelief.

Those who deny the meeting with Allah demand to see angels or Allah Himself as proof. This shows their arrogance and extreme transgression.

Verse 22

يَوْمَ يَرَوْنَ الْمَلَائِكَةَ لَا بُشْرَىٰ يَوْمَئِذٍ لِلْمُجْرِمِينَ وَيَقُولُونَ حِجْرًا مَحْجُورًا

˹But˺ on the Day they will see the angels, there will be no good news for the wicked, who will cry, “Keep away! Away ˹from us˺!”

The Day they see the angels at the time of death, in the grave, and on the Day of Resurrection, in a different form than they proposed - not to give them glad tidings of Paradise, but to say to them: “Allah has made Paradise a forbidden place for you.”

On the Day they see the angels, the wicked will receive no good news but instead be told that Paradise is forbidden to them.

Verse 23

وَقَدِمْنَا إِلَىٰ مَا عَمِلُوا مِنْ عَمَلٍ فَجَعَلْنَاهُ هَبَاءً مَنْثُورًا

Then We will turn to whatever ˹good˺ deeds they did, reducing them to scattered dust.

And We shall approach what they have done of apparent good and righteousness, and We shall make it scattered dust, of no benefit to them, like the scattered motes that are seen in the sunlight; that is because deeds are of no benefit in the Hereafter unless their doer possesses: faith in Allah, sincerity to Him, and adherence to His Messenger Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace.

Apparent good deeds of disbelievers will be rendered useless like scattered dust because they lack faith, sincerity, and adherence to the Prophet Muhammad's teachings.

Verse 24

أَصْحَابُ الْجَنَّةِ يَوْمَئِذٍ خَيْرٌ مُسْتَقَرًّا وَأَحْسَنُ مَقِيلًا

˹But˺ on that Day the residents of Paradise will have the best settlement and the finest place to rest.

The dwellers of Paradise on the Day of Resurrection are in a better abode than the people of Hell, and have better dwellings in Paradise, where their comfort is complete, and their bliss is untainted.

The people of Paradise will have the best dwellings and a place of complete comfort.

Verse 25

وَيَوْمَ تَشَقَّقُ السَّمَاءُ بِالْغَمَامِ وَنُزِّلَ الْمَلَائِكَةُ تَنْزِيلًا

˹Watch for˺ the Day the heavens will burst with clouds, and the angels will be sent down in successive ranks.

And remember, O Messenger, that Day when the sky will be split open, and from its openings, the thin white clouds will appear, and Allah will send down the angels of the heavens on that Day, who will surround all of creation in the gathering place, and Allah, Blessed and Exalted, will come to decide the judgment between His servants, in a manner befitting His Majesty.

On that Day, the heavens will split open, clouds will appear, and angels will descend in ranks, surrounding all of creation.

Verse 26

الْمُلْكُ يَوْمَئِذٍ الْحَقُّ لِلرَّحْمَٰنِ ۚ وَكَانَ يَوْمًا عَلَى الْكَافِرِينَ عَسِيرًا

True authority on that Day will belong ˹only˺ to the Most Compassionate. And it will be a hard day for the disbelievers.

The true dominion on that Day will belong to the Most Compassionate alone, and this Day will be difficult and severe for the disbelievers, because of the punishment and painful torment they will receive.

On that Day, true authority will belong solely to *Ar-Rahman* (The Most Compassionate) and it will be a difficult day for the disbelievers due to the severe punishment.

Verse 27

وَيَوْمَ يَعَضُّ الظَّالِمُ عَلَىٰ يَدَيْهِ يَقُولُ يَا لَيْتَنِي اتَّخَذْتُ مَعَ الرَّسُولِ سَبِيلًا

And ˹beware of˺ the Day the wrongdoer will bite his nails ˹in regret˺ and say, “Oh! I wish I had followed the Way along with the Messenger!

And remember, O Messenger, the Day when the wrongdoer will bite his hands in regret and remorse, saying: “Oh, I wish I had taken the path of the Messenger, Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and had followed him in adopting Islam as a path to Paradise.” And he will lament, saying: “Oh, I wish I had not taken so-and-so, the disbeliever, as a friend to follow and love. Indeed, this friend led me astray from the Quran after it had come to me. And the accursed Satan is ever a betrayer of man.” And in these verses is a warning against keeping bad company, for it may be a reason for leading one’s companion into Hell.

On the Day of Judgment, wrongdoers will bite their hands in regret, wishing they had followed the Messenger’s path.

Verse 28

يَا وَيْلَتَىٰ لَيْتَنِي لَمْ أَتَّخِذْ فُلَانًا خَلِيلًا

Woe to me! I wish I had never taken so-and-so as a close friend.

And remember, O Messenger, the Day when the wrongdoer will bite his hands in regret and remorse, saying: “Oh, I wish I had taken the path of the Messenger, Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and had followed him in adopting Islam as a path to Paradise.” And he will lament, saying: “Oh, I wish I had not taken so-and-so, the disbeliever, as a friend to follow and love. Indeed, this friend led me astray from the Quran after it had come to me. And the accursed Satan is ever a betrayer of man.” And in these verses is a warning against keeping bad company, for it may be a reason for leading one’s companion into Hell.

The wrongdoers will lament their bad choices of taking disbelievers as close friends, who led them away from the Quran.

Verse 29

لَقَدْ أَضَلَّنِي عَنِ الذِّكْرِ بَعْدَ إِذْ جَاءَنِي ۗ وَكَانَ الشَّيْطَانُ لِلْإِنْسَانِ خَذُولًا

It was he who truly made me stray from the Reminder after it had reached me.” And Satan has always betrayed humanity.

And remember, O Messenger, the Day when the wrongdoer will bite his hands in regret and remorse, saying: “Oh, I wish I had taken the path of the Messenger, Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and had followed him in adopting Islam as a path to Paradise.” And he will lament, saying: “Oh, I wish I had not taken so-and-so, the disbeliever, as a friend to follow and love. Indeed, this friend led me astray from the Quran after it had come to me. And the accursed Satan is ever a betrayer of man.” And in these verses is a warning against keeping bad company, for it may be a reason for leading one’s companion into Hell.
