Sura 111

Verse 1

تَبَّتْ يَدَا أَبِي لَهَبٍ وَتَبَّ

May the hands of Abu Lahab perish, and he ˹himself˺ perish!

May the hands of Abu Lahab be ruined and may he be miserable for harming the Messenger of Allah, Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, and indeed, Abu Lahab's loss has been realized.

This verse is a curse and a declaration of ruin upon Abu Lahab for his harm to the Prophet.

Verse 2

مَا أَغْنَىٰ عَنْهُ مَالُهُ وَمَا كَسَبَ

Neither his wealth nor ˹worldly˺ gains will benefit him.

Neither his wealth nor his children will avail him, and they will not ward off any of Allah's punishment when it befalls him.

This verse states that Abu Lahab’s wealth and worldly gains will not save him from Allah's punishment.

Verse 3

سَيَصْلَىٰ نَارًا ذَاتَ لَهَبٍ

He will burn in a flaming Fire,

He and his wife, who used to carry thorns and throw them in the path of the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, to harm him, will enter a blazing fire.

This verse describes Abu Lahab's fate in the blazing Fire (Hell).

Verse 4

وَامْرَأَتُهُ حَمَّالَةَ الْحَطَبِ

and ˹so will˺ his wife, the carrier of ˹thorny˺ kindling,

He and his wife, who used to carry thorns and throw them in the path of the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, to harm him, will enter a blazing fire.

This verse describes Abu Lahab's wife's fate alongside her husband, emphasizing her role in harming the Prophet.

Verse 5

فِي جِيدِهَا حَبْلٌ مِنْ مَسَدٍ

around her neck will be a rope of palm-fibre.

Around her neck will be a rope of tightly twisted palm fiber, with which she will be hoisted in Hell and then thrown to its bottom.

This verse describes the punishment of Abu Lahab's wife in Hell, with a rope around her neck.
