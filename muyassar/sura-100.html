Sura 100

Verse 1

وَالْعَادِيَاتِ ضَبْحًا

By the galloping, panting horses,

Allah Almighty swore by the horses running in His cause toward the enemy, when their sound is heard from the speed of their running. And it is not permissible for the created being to swear except by Allah, for swearing by other than Allah is polytheism.

Allah swears by the horses that gallop towards the enemy, emphasizing their speed and dedication.

Verse 2

فَالْمُورِيَاتِ قَدْحًا

striking sparks of fire ˹with their hoofs˺,

The horses whose hooves strike sparks of fire due to the intensity of their running.

These are the horses that strike sparks with their hooves due to their intense speed.

Verse 3

فَالْمُغِيرَاتِ صُبْحًا

launching raids at dawn,

And raid the enemy at dawn.

These horses raid the enemy at dawn.

Verse 4

فَأَثَرْنَ بِهِ نَقْعًا

stirring up ˹clouds of˺ dust,

And stir up dust thereby.

They stir up dust clouds as they gallop towards the enemy.

Verse 5

فَوَسَطْنَ بِهِ جَمْعًا

and penetrating into the heart of enemy lines!

And penetrate thereby into the midst of the enemy.

These horses penetrate into the heart of the enemy lines with their charge.

Verse 6

إِنَّ الْإِنْسَانَ لِرَبِّهِ لَكَنُودٌ

Surely humankind is ungrateful to their Lord—

Indeed, man is ungrateful to the blessings of his Lord, and indeed, he is a witness to that ingratitude, and indeed, he is vehement in his love of wealth.

This verse highlights humankind's ungratefulness to their Lord.

Verse 7

وَإِنَّهُ عَلَىٰ ذَٰلِكَ لَشَهِيدٌ

and they certainly attest to this—

Indeed, man is ungrateful to the blessings of his Lord, and indeed, he is a witness to that ingratitude, and indeed, he is vehement in his love of wealth.

People themselves are witnesses to their own ingratitude.

Verse 8

وَإِنَّهُ لِحُبِّ الْخَيْرِ لَشَدِيدٌ

and they are truly extreme in their love of ˹worldly˺ gains.

Indeed, man is ungrateful to the blessings of his Lord, and indeed, he is a witness to that ingratitude, and indeed, he is vehement in his love of wealth.

Humankind is extremely attached to worldly gains and material wealth.

Verse 9

أَفَلَا يَعْلَمُ إِذَا بُعْثِرَ مَا فِي الْقُبُورِ

Do they not know that when the contents of the graves will be spilled out,

Does man not know what awaits him when Allah brings the dead out of the graves for reckoning and reward?

This is a reminder that the graves will be emptied, and the dead will be brought forth for reckoning.

Verse 10

وَحُصِّلَ مَا فِي الصُّدُورِ

and the secrets of the hearts will be laid bare—

And what was hidden in the chests of good or evil will be brought forth.

On the Day of Judgment, all hidden thoughts and intentions within people's hearts, both good and bad, will be revealed.

Verse 11

إِنَّ رَبَّهُمْ بِهِمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ لَخَبِيرٌ

surely their Lord is All-Aware of them on that Day.

Indeed, their Lord, on that Day, is well-acquainted with them and their deeds. Nothing of that is hidden from Him.

On that Day, Allah will have full knowledge of everyone and their deeds; nothing will be hidden from Him.
