Sura 011

Verse 1

الر ۚ كِتَابٌ أُحْكِمَتْ آيَاتُهُ ثُمَّ فُصِّلَتْ مِنْ لَدُنْ حَكِيمٍ خَبِيرٍ

Alif-Lãm-Ra. ˹This is˺ a Book whose verses are well perfected and then fully explained. ˹It is˺ from the One ˹Who is˺ All-Wise, All-Aware.

(Alif, Lam, Ra) The discussion of the disjointed letters at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah has preceded.

This verse uses the disjointed letters, *Alif-Lam-Ra*, followed by a description of the Quran as a book with well-perfected and clearly explained verses, originating from Allah, the All-Wise and All-Aware.

Verse 2

أَلَّا تَعْبُدُوا إِلَّا اللَّهَ ۚ إِنَّنِي لَكُمْ مِنْهُ نَذِيرٌ وَبَشِيرٌ

˹Tell them, O Prophet,˺ “Worship none but Allah. Surely I am a warner and deliverer of good news to you from Him.

And the sending down of the Quran, and the clarification of its rulings, and its detailed explanation and perfection are so that you do not worship except Allah alone, without any partner. Indeed, I am to you - O people - from Allah, a warner warning you of His punishment, and a bearer of good tidings, giving you the good news of His reward.

The core message is to worship none but Allah, the One and Only God. The Prophet is a warner against Allah's punishment and a bearer of good news about His rewards.

Verse 3

وَأَنِ اسْتَغْفِرُوا رَبَّكُمْ ثُمَّ تُوبُوا إِلَيْهِ يُمَتِّعْكُمْ مَتَاعًا حَسَنًا إِلَىٰ أَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى وَيُؤْتِ كُلَّ ذِي فَضْلٍ فَضْلَهُ ۖ وَإِنْ تَوَلَّوْا فَإِنِّي أَخَافُ عَلَيْكُمْ عَذَابَ يَوْمٍ كَبِيرٍ

And seek your Lord’s forgiveness and turn to Him in repentance. He will grant you a good provision for an appointed term and graciously reward the doers of good. But if you turn away, then I truly fear for you the torment of a formidable Day.

And ask Him to forgive you your sins, then return to Him repentant. He will grant you good enjoyment in your world with a good life in it, until your appointed term comes, and He will give every person of merit from knowledge and actions the complete reward of his merit, with no deficiency in it. And if you turn away from what I call you to, then I fear for you the punishment of a severe day, which is the Day of Resurrection. And this is a severe threat for whoever turns away from the orders of Allah and denies His messengers.

This verse urges people to seek forgiveness from Allah, repent, and turn to Him. Allah promises good provision and gracious rewards to the righteous. Those who turn away will face severe torment on the Day of Resurrection.

Verse 4

إِلَى اللَّهِ مَرْجِعُكُمْ ۖ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

To Allah is your return. And He is Most Capable of everything.”

To Allah is your return after your death, all of you, so beware of His punishment, and He, glory be to Him, is capable of resurrecting you, gathering you, and recompensing you.

The verse reminds people that everyone will return to Allah. He has the power to resurrect, gather, and recompense everyone for their deeds. It is a warning to be mindful of Allah's power.

Verse 5

أَلَا إِنَّهُمْ يَثْنُونَ صُدُورَهُمْ لِيَسْتَخْفُوا مِنْهُ ۚ أَلَا حِينَ يَسْتَغْشُونَ ثِيَابَهُمْ يَعْلَمُ مَا يُسِرُّونَ وَمَا يُعْلِنُونَ ۚ إِنَّهُ عَلِيمٌ بِذَاتِ الصُّدُورِ

Indeed, they enfold ˹what is in˺ their hearts, ˹trying˺ to hide it from Him! But even when they cover themselves with their clothes, He knows what they conceal and what they reveal. Surely He knows best what is ˹hidden˺ in the heart.

Indeed, these polytheists conceal disbelief in their chests, thinking that what their souls conceal is hidden from Allah. Do they not know that when they cover their bodies with their clothes, Allah is not unaware of their secret and open intentions? Indeed, He is Knowing of all that their chests harbor of intentions, thoughts, and secrets.

This verse addresses those who try to hide their true intentions, reminding them that Allah knows everything, whether it's concealed or revealed. He is aware of all thoughts, secrets, and intentions within the heart.

Verse 6

وَمَا مِنْ دَابَّةٍ فِي الْأَرْضِ إِلَّا عَلَى اللَّهِ رِزْقُهَا وَيَعْلَمُ مُسْتَقَرَّهَا وَمُسْتَوْدَعَهَا ۚ كُلٌّ فِي كِتَابٍ مُبِينٍ

There is no moving creature on earth whose provision is not guaranteed by Allah. And He knows where it lives and where it is laid to rest. All is ˹written˺ in a perfect Record.

Allah has guaranteed the sustenance of all that moves on the face of the earth, as a favor from Him. He knows the place of its settlement during its life and after its death, and He knows the place where it will die. All of that is written in a clear book with Allah, detailing all of that.

This verse highlights Allah's role as the provider for all living creatures, stating that He knows where each creature lives and where it will die. This emphasizes Allah's comprehensive knowledge and control over all aspects of life.

Verse 7

وَهُوَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ فِي سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍ وَكَانَ عَرْشُهُ عَلَى الْمَاءِ لِيَبْلُوَكُمْ أَيُّكُمْ أَحْسَنُ عَمَلًا ۗ وَلَئِنْ قُلْتَ إِنَّكُمْ مَبْعُوثُونَ مِنْ بَعْدِ الْمَوْتِ لَيَقُولَنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا إِنْ هَٰذَا إِلَّا سِحْرٌ مُبِينٌ

He is the One Who created the heavens and the earth in six Days—and His Throne was upon the waters—in order to test which of you is best in deeds. And if you ˹O Prophet˺ say, “Surely you will ˹all˺ be raised up after death,” the disbelievers will certainly say, “That is nothing but pure magic!”

And it is He who created the heavens and the earth and what is between them in six days, and His Throne was upon water before that, so that He may test you which of you is best in obedience to Him and in deeds, which is what is purely for Allah, conforming to what the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, was upon. And if you said - O Messenger - to these polytheists from your people: 'You are resurrected alive after your death,' they would hasten to deny it and say: 'This Quran that you recite to us is nothing but clear magic.'

Allah created the heavens and the earth in six days to test humanity through their actions. The verse mentions that Allah's Throne was upon water before creation, emphasizing His ultimate power. The disbelievers view the concept of resurrection as 'pure magic', showing their denial of the Hereafter.

Verse 8

وَلَئِنْ أَخَّرْنَا عَنْهُمُ الْعَذَابَ إِلَىٰ أُمَّةٍ مَعْدُودَةٍ لَيَقُولُنَّ مَا يَحْبِسُهُ ۗ أَلَا يَوْمَ يَأْتِيهِمْ لَيْسَ مَصْرُوفًا عَنْهُمْ وَحَاقَ بِهِمْ مَا كَانُوا بِهِ يَسْتَهْزِئُونَ

And if We delay their punishment until an appointed time, they will definitely say, “What is holding it back?” Indeed, on the Day it overtakes them, it will not be averted from them, and they will be overwhelmed by what they used to ridicule.

And if We delay the punishment from these polytheists until an appointed time, and they deem it slow, they will say in mockery and denial: 'What is preventing this punishment from falling, if it is true?' Indeed, the day that punishment comes to them, there will be none who can turn it away from them, nor can any repulse it. And they will be surrounded from every side by the punishment for which they used to mock before it befell them.

This verse addresses the disbelievers' mockery of the delayed punishment, stating that when it arrives, there will be no escape. It emphasizes that the punishment will be inescapable when it comes.

Verse 9

وَلَئِنْ أَذَقْنَا الْإِنْسَانَ مِنَّا رَحْمَةً ثُمَّ نَزَعْنَاهَا مِنْهُ إِنَّهُ لَيَئُوسٌ كَفُورٌ

If We give people a taste of Our mercy then take it away from them, they become utterly desperate, ungrateful.

And if We give man a blessing from Us, such as health and security and other things, then We take it away from him, he is surely in despair of the mercy of Allah, ungrateful for the blessings that Allah has bestowed upon him.

This verse points out human nature: when people experience Allah's mercy and then lose it, they often become despairing and ungrateful. This illustrates the fickle nature of humans in dealing with blessings.

Verse 10

وَلَئِنْ أَذَقْنَاهُ نَعْمَاءَ بَعْدَ ضَرَّاءَ مَسَّتْهُ لَيَقُولَنَّ ذَهَبَ السَّيِّئَاتُ عَنِّي ۚ إِنَّهُ لَفَرِحٌ فَخُورٌ

But if We give them a taste of prosperity after being touched with adversity, they say, “My ills are gone,” and become totally prideful and boastful,

And if We extend for man in his worldly life and make his provision abundant after a hardship of living, he will then say: 'The hardship has gone away from me, and the difficulties have ceased.' Indeed, he is boastful of blessings, excessive in pride and arrogance over people.

When people experience ease after hardship, they often forget their past difficulties and become proud and boastful. This demonstrates how worldly blessings can lead to arrogance and self-admiration.

Verse 11

إِلَّا الَّذِينَ صَبَرُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ أُولَٰئِكَ لَهُمْ مَغْفِرَةٌ وَأَجْرٌ كَبِيرٌ

except those who patiently endure and do good. It is they who will have forgiveness and a mighty reward.

But those who are patient in what befalls them of adversity, believing in Allah and seeking reward with Him, and do righteous deeds, giving thanks to Allah for His blessings, they will have forgiveness for their sins and a great reward in the Hereafter.

This verse is an exception to the previous one. It highlights that those who patiently endure hardships and do good deeds will receive forgiveness and a great reward. It stresses the importance of patience and righteousness.

Verse 12

فَلَعَلَّكَ تَارِكٌ بَعْضَ مَا يُوحَىٰ إِلَيْكَ وَضَائِقٌ بِهِ صَدْرُكَ أَنْ يَقُولُوا لَوْلَا أُنْزِلَ عَلَيْهِ كَنْزٌ أَوْ جَاءَ مَعَهُ مَلَكٌ ۚ إِنَّمَا أَنْتَ نَذِيرٌ ۚ وَاللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ وَكِيلٌ

So perhaps you, O Messenger, due to the magnitude of the disbelief and denial you see from them, are abandoning some of what is revealed to you from what Allah has sent down to you and commanded you to convey, and your chest is constricted by it for fear that they might ask you for certain demands out of obstinacy, as if to say: 'Why was no great wealth sent down to him, or why did no angel come with him to confirm his message?' So convey to them what has been revealed to you, for you are only responsible for warning them with what has been revealed to you. And Allah is, over all things, a watchful guardian, managing all the affairs of His creation.

This verse comforts the Prophet, advising him not to be disheartened by the disbelievers' demands for a treasure or an angel. It emphasizes that the Prophet's role is to deliver the message, and Allah manages all affairs.

Verse 13

أَمْ يَقُولُونَ افْتَرَاهُ ۖ قُلْ فَأْتُوا بِعَشْرِ سُوَرٍ مِثْلِهِ مُفْتَرَيَاتٍ وَادْعُوا مَنِ اسْتَطَعْتُمْ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صَادِقِينَ

Say to them: If the matter is as you claim, then bring ten fabricated chapters like it, and call upon whomever you can from all of Allah's creation to help you bring these ten chapters, if you are truthful in your claim.

This verse challenges those who disbelieve in the Quran. If they claim it's fabricated, they are challenged to produce ten chapters like it, and they can seek help from anyone besides Allah if they are truthful.

Verse 14

فَإِلَّمْ يَسْتَجِيبُوا لَكُمْ فَاعْلَمُوا أَنَّمَا أُنْزِلَ بِعِلْمِ اللَّهِ وَأَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ فَهَلْ أَنْتُمْ مُسْلِمُونَ

If these polytheists do not respond to you - O Messenger and those who believe with you - to what you are inviting them to, due to everyone's inability to do so, then know that this Quran was only sent down by Allah to His Messenger with His knowledge, and it is not from the speech of humans. And know that there is no deity worthy of worship except Allah. So, are you - after this proof has been established against you - submitting Muslims, obedient to Allah and His Messenger?

If the disbelievers can't meet the challenge, it proves the Quran's divine origin. It is revealed by Allah with His knowledge and there is no god worthy of worship except Him. This verse urges them to submit to Allah.

Verse 15

مَنْ كَانَ يُرِيدُ الْحَيَاةَ الدُّنْيَا وَزِينَتَهَا نُوَفِّ إِلَيْهِمْ أَعْمَالَهُمْ فِيهَا وَهُمْ فِيهَا لَا يُبْخَسُونَ

Whoever desires by his deeds the worldly life and its enjoyments, We will give them what is apportioned to them of the reward of their deeds in the worldly life, fully and without deficiency.

Those who only seek worldly gains in their actions, will be fully compensated in this life. This compensation is for deeds done with the intention of worldly gains only.

Verse 16

أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ لَيْسَ لَهُمْ فِي الْآخِرَةِ إِلَّا النَّارُ ۖ وَحَبِطَ مَا صَنَعُوا فِيهَا وَبَاطِلٌ مَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

Those, for them, there is nothing in the Hereafter except the Fire of Hell, where they will suffer its heat. The benefit of what they did will be lost from them, and their deeds were void because they were not for the sake of Allah.

Such people will have nothing in the Hereafter except the Fire of Hell. Their efforts in this life will be fruitless and their deeds will be worthless because they weren't done for Allah's sake.

Verse 17

Those believe in this Quran and act upon its rulings. And whoever disbelieves in this Quran from those who have allied against the Messenger of Allah, may Allah's peace and blessings be upon him, his reward is the Fire, which he will inevitably enter. So, do not be - O Messenger - in doubt about the matter of the Quran and its being from Allah Almighty, after the evidence and proofs have testified to that. And know that this religion is the truth from your Lord, but most people do not believe and do not act upon what they have been commanded. This verse contrasts disbelievers with those who have a clear proof from Allah, supported by the Quran and previous scripture (Torah). Those who believe in the Quran are guided, while those who reject it are destined for the Fire. This verse emphasizes the truth of the Quran.

Verse 18

وَمَنْ أَظْلَمُ مِمَّنِ افْتَرَىٰ عَلَى اللَّهِ كَذِبًا ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ يُعْرَضُونَ عَلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ وَيَقُولُ الْأَشْهَادُ هَٰؤُلَاءِ الَّذِينَ كَذَبُوا عَلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ ۚ أَلَا لَعْنَةُ اللَّهِ عَلَى الظَّالِمِينَ

Those who fabricate lies against Allah are the most unjust. They will be brought before Allah, and witnesses will testify against them, showing that Allah's condemnation is upon them because of their injustice.

Verse 19

The unjust are those who hinder others from Allah's path, try to distort it to suit their desires, and disbelieve in the Hereafter. This verse describes actions of disbelievers and their opposition to the truth.

Verse 20

أُولَٰئِكَ لَمْ يَكُونُوا مُعْجِزِينَ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَمَا كَانَ لَهُمْ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ مِنْ أَوْلِيَاءَ ۘ يُضَاعَفُ لَهُمُ الْعَذَابُ ۚ مَا كَانُوا يَسْتَطِيعُونَ السَّمْعَ وَمَا كَانُوا يُبْصِرُونَ

Disbelievers will not escape Allah's power, nor will they have any protectors. Their punishment will be doubled because they rejected the truth, failing to listen and see.

Verse 21

أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ خَسِرُوا أَنْفُسَهُمْ وَضَلَّ عَنْهُمْ مَا كَانُوا يَفْتَرُونَ

Verse 22

In the Hereafter, the disbelievers will be the greatest losers, trading eternal happiness for eternal punishment in Hell.

Verse 23

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ وَأَخْبَتُوا إِلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ أُولَٰئِكَ أَصْحَابُ الْجَنَّةِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَالِدُونَ

Indeed, those who believed in Allah and His Messenger and did righteous deeds, and submitted to Allah in all that they were commanded and forbidden, those are the people of Paradise, they will not die therein, nor will they ever be removed from it.

Believers who do good deeds and submit to Allah will be the residents of Paradise, where they will live forever.

Verse 24
