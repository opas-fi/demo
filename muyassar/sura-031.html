Sura 031

Verse 1

الم

Alif-Lãm-Mĩm.

(Alif Lam Mim) The discussion about the disjointed letters at the beginning of Surat Al-Baqarah has preceded.

This verse begins with the letters *Alif-Lãm-Mĩm*, which are considered among the mysterious letters of the Quran. Their exact meaning is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

تِلْكَ آيَاتُ الْكِتَابِ الْحَكِيمِ

These are the verses of the Book, rich in wisdom.

These are the verses of the Quran, full of profound wisdom.

These verses of the Quran are full of profound wisdom and guidance for humanity.

Verse 3

هُدًى وَرَحْمَةً لِلْمُحْسِنِينَ

˹It is˺ a guide and mercy for the good-doers—

These verses are guidance and mercy for those who do good by what Allah has revealed in the Quran, and what His Messenger Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, has commanded them.

The Quran serves as a guide and mercy for those who do good and follow its teachings.

Verse 4

الَّذِينَ يُقِيمُونَ الصَّلَاةَ وَيُؤْتُونَ الزَّكَاةَ وَهُمْ بِالْآخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ

those who establish prayer, pay alms-tax, and have sure faith in the Hereafter.

Those who perform prayer completely at its proper times, and give the obligatory charity to those who deserve it, and they are certain of the resurrection and recompense in the Hereafter.

Those who benefit from this guidance are those who establish prayer, pay alms, and have firm belief in the Hereafter.

Verse 5

أُولَٰئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًى مِنْ رَبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ

It is they who are ˹truly˺ guided by their Lord, and it is they who will be successful.

Those who are described with the previous attributes are upon guidance and light from their Lord, and they are the ones who are successful in this world and the Hereafter.

Such individuals are upon true guidance from their Lord and are the ones who will ultimately achieve success.

Verse 6

وَمِنَ النَّاسِ مَنْ يَشْتَرِي لَهْوَ الْحَدِيثِ لِيُضِلَّ عَنْ سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ بِغَيْرِ عِلْمٍ وَيَتَّخِذَهَا هُزُوًا ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ مُهِينٌ

But there are some who employ theatrics, only to lead others away from Allah’s Way—without any knowledge—and to make a mockery of it. They will suffer a humiliating punishment.

And among the people is he who purchases idle talk - and that is everything that distracts from obedience to Allah and turns away from His pleasure - to mislead the people from the path of guidance to the path of whims, and he takes the verses of Allah in ridicule. Those will have a humiliating and disgraceful punishment.

However, there are others who seek to mislead people from the path of Allah, without any knowledge, and make a mockery of it. They will face a humiliating punishment.

Verse 7

وَإِذَا تُتْلَىٰ عَلَيْهِ آيَاتُنَا وَلَّىٰ مُسْتَكْبِرًا كَأَنْ لَمْ يَسْمَعْهَا كَأَنَّ فِي أُذُنَيْهِ وَقْرًا ۖ فَبَشِّرْهُ بِعَذَابٍ أَلِيمٍ

Whenever Our revelations are recited to them, they turn away in arrogance as if they did not hear them, as if there is deafness in their ears. So give them good news ˹O Prophet˺ of a painful punishment.

And when the verses of the Quran are recited to him, he turns away from obedience to Allah, and he arrogantly acts as if he heard nothing, as if there is deafness in his ears. And he who is like this, then give him glad tidings, O Messenger, of a painful torment in the Fire on the Day of Resurrection.

When the verses of the Quran are recited to them, they arrogantly turn away as if they didn't hear them, deserving of a painful punishment.

Verse 8

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ لَهُمْ جَنَّاتُ النَّعِيمِ

Surely those who believe and do good will have the Gardens of Bliss,

Indeed, those who believe in Allah and His Messenger and do righteous deeds that they were commanded with, those will have an abiding bliss in the Gardens.

In contrast, those who believe and do good will have eternal bliss in the Gardens of Paradise.

Verse 9

خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا ۖ وَعْدَ اللَّهِ حَقًّا ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

staying there forever. Allah’s promise is true. And He is the Almighty, All-Wise.

And their life in those Gardens is an eternal life that will not cease or disappear. Allah has promised them this with a true promise, and He, glory be to Him, never breaks His promise. And He is the Mighty in His command, the Wise in His planning.

This is a true promise from Allah, who is the Almighty and All-Wise, and He never breaks His promise.

Verse 10

خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ بِغَيْرِ عَمَدٍ تَرَوْنَهَا ۖ وَأَلْقَىٰ فِي الْأَرْضِ رَوَاسِيَ أَنْ تَمِيدَ بِكُمْ وَبَثَّ فِيهَا مِنْ كُلِّ دَابَّةٍ ۚ وَأَنْزَلْنَا مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً فَأَنْبَتْنَا فِيهَا مِنْ كُلِّ زَوْجٍ كَرِيمٍ

He created the heavens without pillars—as you can see—and placed firm mountains upon the earth so it does not shake with you, and scattered throughout it all types of creatures. And We send down rain from the sky, causing every type of fine plant to grow on earth.

Allah created the heavens, and raised them without pillars as you see them, and He cast into the earth firm mountains so that it would not shake and move, thus disrupting your lives. And He spread on the earth various kinds of creatures, and We sent down rain from the clouds, and by it We caused to grow from the earth every pleasant and beneficial pair with a beautiful appearance.

Allah created the heavens without pillars, placed mountains on earth to stabilize it, and scattered various creatures. He sends rain to grow all kinds of beneficial plants.

Verse 11

هَٰذَا خَلْقُ اللَّهِ فَأَرُونِي مَاذَا خَلَقَ الَّذِينَ مِنْ دُونِهِ ۚ بَلِ الظَّالِمُونَ فِي ضَلَالٍ مُبِينٍ

This is Allah’s creation. Now show Me what those ˹gods˺ other than Him have created. In fact, the wrongdoers are clearly astray.

And all that you see is the creation of Allah, so show me - O polytheists - what did your deities whom you worship besides Allah create? Rather, the polytheists are in clear deviation from the truth and uprightness.

This is all Allah's creation. The verse challenges those who worship others to show what their deities have created. Those who associate partners with Allah are clearly misguided.

Verse 12

وَلَقَدْ آتَيْنَا لُقْمَانَ الْحِكْمَةَ أَنِ اشْكُرْ لِلَّهِ ۚ وَمَنْ يَشْكُرْ فَإِنَّمَا يَشْكُرُ لِنَفْسِهِ ۖ وَمَنْ كَفَرَ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ غَنِيٌّ حَمِيدٌ

Indeed, We blessed Luqmân with wisdom, ˹saying˺, “Be grateful to Allah, for whoever is grateful, it is only for their own good. And whoever is ungrateful, then surely Allah is Self-Sufficient, Praiseworthy.”

And We had certainly given to a righteous servant of Our servants (and that is Luqman) wisdom, which is understanding of the religion, soundness of mind, and accuracy in speech. And We said to him: Give thanks to Allah for His favors upon you, and whoever gives thanks to his Lord, then the benefit of that returns to him, and whoever is ungrateful for His favors, then Allah is free from need of his thanks, not in need of it. To Him belongs praise and glorification in every case.

Allah granted Luqman wisdom and advised him to be grateful. Gratitude benefits the grateful person, while Allah is self-sufficient and worthy of praise, regardless of whether people are grateful.

Verse 13

وَإِذْ قَالَ لُقْمَانُ لِابْنِهِ وَهُوَ يَعِظُهُ يَا بُنَيَّ لَا تُشْرِكْ بِاللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّ الشِّرْكَ لَظُلْمٌ عَظِيمٌ

And ˹remember˺ when Luqmân said to his son, while advising him, “O my dear son! Never associate ˹anything˺ with Allah ˹in worship˺, for associating ˹others with Him˺ is truly the worst of all wrongs.”

And remember - O Messenger - Luqman's advice to his son when he said to him, advising: O my son, do not associate anything with Allah, for you would be wronging yourself; indeed, associating partners with Allah is the greatest of the major sins and the most heinous of them.

Luqman advises his son against associating anything with Allah, emphasizing that associating partners with Allah is a grave wrong.

Verse 14

وَوَصَّيْنَا الْإِنْسَانَ بِوَالِدَيْهِ حَمَلَتْهُ أُمُّهُ وَهْنًا عَلَىٰ وَهْنٍ وَفِصَالُهُ فِي عَامَيْنِ أَنِ اشْكُرْ لِي وَلِوَالِدَيْكَ إِلَيَّ الْمَصِيرُ

And We have commanded people to ˹honour˺ their parents. Their mothers bore them through hardship upon hardship, and their weaning takes two years. So be grateful to Me and your parents. To Me is the final return.

And We have enjoined upon man [care] for his parents. His mother carried him, [increasing her] in weakness upon weakness, and his weaning is in two years. [We said], Give thanks to Allah and to your parents; to Me is the [final] destination.

This verse emphasizes honoring parents, particularly mothers, who endure hardship in bearing and raising children. Gratitude is due to both Allah and parents, and all will return to Allah.

Verse 15

وَإِنْ جَاهَدَاكَ عَلَىٰ أَنْ تُشْرِكَ بِي مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِ عِلْمٌ فَلَا تُطِعْهُمَا ۖ وَصَاحِبْهُمَا فِي الدُّنْيَا مَعْرُوفًا ۖ وَاتَّبِعْ سَبِيلَ مَنْ أَنَابَ إِلَيَّ ۚ ثُمَّ إِلَيَّ مَرْجِعُكُمْ فَأُنَبِّئُكُمْ بِمَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ

But if they pressure you to associate with Me what you have no knowledge of, do not obey them. Still keep their company in this world courteously, and follow the way of those who turn to Me ˹in devotion˺. Then to Me you will ˹all˺ return, and then I will inform you of what you used to do.

And if they strive to make you associate with Me that of which you have no knowledge, then do not obey them but accompany them in [this] world with appropriate kindness and follow the way of those who turn back to Me [in repentance]. Then to Me will be your return, and I will inform you about what you used to do.

If parents pressure one to associate partners with Allah, obedience is not required. However, they should be treated kindly. The verse encourages following the path of those devoted to Allah, and everyone will ultimately return to Him.

Verse 16

يَا بُنَيَّ إِنَّهَا إِنْ تَكُ مِثْقَالَ حَبَّةٍ مِنْ خَرْدَلٍ فَتَكُنْ فِي صَخْرَةٍ أَوْ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ أَوْ فِي الْأَرْضِ يَأْتِ بِهَا اللَّهُ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَطِيفٌ خَبِيرٌ

˹Luqmân  added,˺ “O my dear son! ˹Even˺ if a deed were the weight of a mustard seed—be it ˹hidden˺ in a rock or in the heavens or the earth—Allah will bring it forth. Surely Allah is Most Subtle, All-Aware.

O my son, indeed if wrong or good deeds should be the weight of a mustard seed and should be within a rock or in the heavens or in the earth, Allah will bring it forth. Indeed, Allah is Subtle and Aware of the deeds of His servants.

Luqman advises his son that Allah is aware of every deed, no matter how small or hidden. Allah will bring forth all deeds on the Day of Judgment.

Verse 17

يَا بُنَيَّ أَقِمِ الصَّلَاةَ وَأْمُرْ بِالْمَعْرُوفِ وَانْهَ عَنِ الْمُنْكَرِ وَاصْبِرْ عَلَىٰ مَا أَصَابَكَ ۖ إِنَّ ذَٰلِكَ مِنْ عَزْمِ الْأُمُورِ

“O my dear son! Establish prayer, encourage what is good and forbid what is evil, and endure patiently whatever befalls you. Surely this is a resolve to aspire to.

O my son, establish prayer, enjoin what is right, forbid what is wrong, and be patient over what befalls you. Indeed, that is of the matters [requiring] determination.

Luqman advises his son to establish prayer, encourage good, forbid evil, and endure patiently. These are important matters requiring determination.

Verse 18

وَلَا تُصَعِّرْ خَدَّكَ لِلنَّاسِ وَلَا تَمْشِ فِي الْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ كُلَّ مُخْتَالٍ فَخُورٍ

“And do not turn your nose up to people, nor walk pridefully upon the earth. Surely Allah does not like whoever is arrogant, boastful.

And do not turn your cheek [in contempt] toward people and do not walk through the earth in boastful pride. Indeed, Allah does not like everyone self-deluded and arrogant.

This verse advises against arrogance and pride. Allah does not like the arrogant and boastful.

Verse 19

وَاقْصِدْ فِي مَشْيِكَ وَاغْضُضْ مِنْ صَوْتِكَ ۚ إِنَّ أَنْكَرَ الْأَصْوَاتِ لَصَوْتُ الْحَمِيرِ

Be moderate in your pace. And lower your voice, for the ugliest of all voices is certainly the braying of donkeys.”

And be moderate in your pace and lower your voice; indeed, the most disagreeable of sounds is the voice of donkeys, known for their stupidity and their loud voices.

Be moderate in pace and lower your voice, as loud and harsh voices are displeasing, likened to the braying of donkeys.

Verse 20

أَلَمْ تَرَوْا أَنَّ اللَّهَ سَخَّرَ لَكُمْ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ وَأَسْبَغَ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعَمَهُ ظَاهِرَةً وَبَاطِنَةً ۗ وَمِنَ النَّاسِ مَنْ يُجَادِلُ فِي اللَّهِ بِغَيْرِ عِلْمٍ وَلَا هُدًى وَلَا كِتَابٍ مُنِيرٍ

Have you not seen that Allah has subjected for you whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth, and has lavished His favours upon you, both seen and unseen? ˹Still˺ there are some who dispute about Allah without knowledge, or guidance, or an enlightening scripture.

Have you not seen - O people - that Allah has subjected to you all that is in the heavens, of the sun, the moon, the clouds, and other things, and what is in the earth, of the beasts, trees, water, and other things that are countless? And He has encompassed you with His manifest favors upon bodies and limbs, and inner favors in minds and hearts, and what He has stored for you of which you know not. And among the people are those who dispute about the Oneness of Allah and the sincerity of worship to Him without proof or explanation, nor a clear book clarifying the truth of his claim.

Allah has provided everything in the heavens and earth for our benefit and has showered us with blessings, both visible and hidden. Yet, some people still argue about Allah without any true knowledge or guidance.

Verse 21

وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمُ اتَّبِعُوا مَا أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ قَالُوا بَلْ نَتَّبِعُ مَا وَجَدْنَا عَلَيْهِ آبَاءَنَا ۚ أَوَلَوْ كَانَ الشَّيْطَانُ يَدْعُوهُمْ إِلَىٰ عَذَابِ السَّعِيرِ

When it is said to them, “Follow what Allah has revealed,” they reply, “No! We ˹only˺ follow what we found our forefathers practicing.” ˹Would they still do so˺ even if Satan is inviting them to the torment of the Blaze?

And when it is said to these who argue about the Oneness of Allah and His exclusive right to worship: Follow what Allah has revealed to His Prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him, they say: Rather, we will follow what our fathers were upon of polytheism and idol worship. Would they do that, even if Satan were calling them, by making their evil deeds appealing to them, and their disbelief in Allah, to the blazing fire?

When these people are told to follow what Allah has revealed, they reject it, choosing instead to blindly follow the practices of their ancestors, even though this path leads to hellfire.

Verse 22

وَمَنْ يُسْلِمْ وَجْهَهُ إِلَى اللَّهِ وَهُوَ مُحْسِنٌ فَقَدِ اسْتَمْسَكَ بِالْعُرْوَةِ الْوُثْقَىٰ ۗ وَإِلَى اللَّهِ عَاقِبَةُ الْأُمُورِ

Whoever fully submits themselves to Allah and is a good-doer, they have certainly grasped the firmest hand-hold. And with Allah rests the outcome of ˹all˺ affairs.

And whoever sincerely devotes his worship to Allah and directs his intention to his Lord, the Most High, while being righteous in his words, and perfect in his deeds, then he has grasped the firmest handle that leads to the pleasure of Allah and His Paradise. And to Allah alone do all matters return, and He will reward the righteous for their righteousness and the wrongdoer for his wrongdoing.

Those who submit completely to Allah and do good deeds have grasped the most secure handhold, assuring them success. Ultimately, all matters return to Allah.

Verse 23

وَمَنْ كَفَرَ فَلَا يَحْزُنْكَ كُفْرُهُ ۚ إِلَيْنَا مَرْجِعُهُمْ فَنُنَبِّئُهُمْ بِمَا عَمِلُوا ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ بِذَاتِ الصُّدُورِ

But whoever disbelieves, do not let their disbelief grieve you ˹O Prophet˺. To Us is their return, and We will inform them of all they did. Surely Allah knows best what is ˹hidden˺ in the heart.

And whoever disbelieves, then do not grieve for him - O Messenger - and do not be sad, for you have delivered what you were obligated to of the call and the message. To Us is their return and their destination on the Day of Resurrection, then We will inform them of their evil deeds that they committed in the world, then We will recompense them for it. Indeed, Allah is Knowing of what their hearts conceal of disbelief in Allah and preferring obedience to Satan.

Do not be saddened by the disbelief of those who reject faith. They will return to Allah, who will hold them accountable for their actions. Allah knows what is hidden in their hearts.

Verse 24

نُمَتِّعُهُمْ قَلِيلًا ثُمَّ نَضْطَرُّهُمْ إِلَىٰ عَذَابٍ غَلِيظٍ

We allow them enjoyment for a little while, then ˹in time˺ We will force them into a harsh torment.

We give them enjoyment in this fleeting world for a short time, then on the Day of Resurrection We will compel them and drive them to a terrible punishment, which is the punishment of Hell.

The disbelievers are given some worldly enjoyment for a brief time, but ultimately they will be forced into a severe and painful punishment.

Verse 25

وَلَئِنْ سَأَلْتَهُمْ مَنْ خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ لَيَقُولُنَّ اللَّهُ ۚ قُلِ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ ۚ بَلْ أَكْثَرُهُمْ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

And if you ask them who created the heavens and the earth, they will definitely say, “Allah!” Say, “Praise be to Allah!” In fact, most of them do not know.

And if you ask - O Messenger - these polytheists in Allah: Who created the heavens and the earth? they will surely say, 'Allah.' So if they say that, then say to them: 'All praise is due to Allah,' who has manifested the proof to you from yourselves, but most of these polytheists do not consider or contemplate who it is that deserves the praise and thanks, and that is why they associated others with Him.

Even the polytheists acknowledge that Allah created the heavens and earth. Therefore, all praise belongs to Him. Yet, most people fail to understand the implications of this.

Verse 26

لِلَّهِ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ هُوَ الْغَنِيُّ الْحَمِيدُ

To Allah belongs whatever is in the heavens and the earth. Allah is truly the Self-Sufficient, Praiseworthy.

To Allah - Glory be to Him - belongs all that is in the heavens and the earth as possession, slaves, creation, and decree; therefore, none deserves worship except Him. Indeed, Allah is free of need of His creation; to Him is the praise and commendation in every situation.

Everything in the heavens and earth belongs to Allah. He is self-sufficient and truly worthy of all praise.

Verse 27

وَلَوْ أَنَّمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ مِنْ شَجَرَةٍ أَقْلَامٌ وَالْبَحْرُ يَمُدُّهُ مِنْ بَعْدِهِ سَبْعَةُ أَبْحُرٍ مَا نَفِدَتْ كَلِمَاتُ اللَّهِ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَزِيزٌ حَكِيمٌ

If all the trees on earth were pens and the ocean ˹were ink˺, refilled by seven other oceans, the Words of Allah would not be exhausted. Surely Allah is Almighty, All-Wise.

And if all the trees on earth were pens, and the sea was ink, and it was supplied with seven more seas, and the words of Allah were written with those pens and that ink, those pens would be broken and that ink would be exhausted, but the complete words of Allah, which none can encompass, would not be exhausted. Indeed, Allah is Mighty in His retribution against those who associate partners with Him, Wise in His planning for His creation. And in the verse is the affirmation of the attribute of speech for Allah - the Most High - in a manner befitting His majesty and perfection, Glory be to Him.

If all the trees were pens and the ocean was ink, replenished seven times over, it would not suffice to write all the words of Allah. His knowledge and power are infinite and immeasurable.

Verse 28

مَا خَلْقُكُمْ وَلَا بَعْثُكُمْ إِلَّا كَنَفْسٍ وَاحِدَةٍ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ سَمِيعٌ بَصِيرٌ

The creation and resurrection of you ˹all˺ is as simple ˹for Him˺ as that of a single soul. Surely Allah is All-Hearing, All-Seeing.

The creation of you - O people - and your resurrection on the Day of Resurrection in ease and facility are no more than the creation and resurrection of a single soul. Indeed, Allah is Hearing of your sayings, Seeing of your actions, and He will recompense you for them.

The creation and resurrection of all humanity is as easy for Allah as creating and resurrecting a single soul. Allah is All-Hearing and All-Seeing.

Verse 29

أَلَمْ تَرَ أَنَّ اللَّهَ يُولِجُ اللَّيْلَ فِي النَّهَارِ وَيُولِجُ النَّهَارَ فِي اللَّيْلِ وَسَخَّرَ الشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ كُلٌّ يَجْرِي إِلَىٰ أَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى وَأَنَّ اللَّهَ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌ

Do you not see that Allah causes the night to merge into the day and the day into the night, and has subjected the sun and the moon, each orbiting for an appointed term, and that Allah is All-Aware of what you do?

Do you not see that Allah takes from the hours of the night, thus lengthening the day and shortening the night, and takes from the hours of the day, thus lengthening the night and shortening the day? And He has subjected the sun and the moon for you, each running in its orbit for a specified term. And that Allah is aware of all the deeds of creation, whether good or evil, nothing of which is hidden from Him?

This verse highlights Allah's control over the day and night cycle, as well as the sun and moon. It emphasizes that Allah is aware of all actions.

Verse 30

ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّ اللَّهَ هُوَ الْحَقُّ وَأَنَّ مَا يَدْعُونَ مِنْ دُونِهِ الْبَاطِلُ وَأَنَّ اللَّهَ هُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْكَبِيرُ

That is because Allah ˹alone˺ is the Truth and what they invoke besides Him is falsehood, and ˹because˺ Allah ˹alone˺ is the Most High, All-Great.

All of that is from the greatness of My power, so that you may know and acknowledge that Allah is the Truth in His essence, attributes, and actions, and that what they invoke besides Him is falsehood, and that Allah is the Most High in His essence, power, and dominance above all His creation, the Greatest over all things, and everything besides Him is subject to Him. So He alone is the One worthy to be worshipped, not those besides Him.

This verse affirms that Allah is the ultimate truth, while anything else worshipped is false. It also declares that Allah is the Most High and Great.

Verse 31

أَلَمْ تَرَ أَنَّ الْفُلْكَ تَجْرِي فِي الْبَحْرِ بِنِعْمَتِ اللَّهِ لِيُرِيَكُمْ مِنْ آيَاتِهِ ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ لِكُلِّ صَبَّارٍ شَكُورٍ

Do you not see that the ships sail ˹smoothly˺ through the sea by the grace of Allah so that He may show you some of His signs? Surely in this are signs for whoever is steadfast, grateful.

Do you not see - O beholder - that the ships sail through the sea by the command of Allah, a favor from Him upon His creation, so that He may show you from His signs and arguments against you what you should consider? Indeed, in the sailing of ships in the sea are signs for every patient one who abstains from the prohibitions of Allah, and grateful one for His favors.

This verse points out the ships sailing by Allah's grace as a sign for those who are patient and grateful.

Verse 32

وَإِذَا غَشِيَهُمْ مَوْجٌ كَالظُّلَلِ دَعَوُا اللَّهَ مُخْلِصِينَ لَهُ الدِّينَ فَلَمَّا نَجَّاهُمْ إِلَى الْبَرِّ فَمِنْهُمْ مُقْتَصِدٌ ۚ وَمَا يَجْحَدُ بِآيَاتِنَا إِلَّا كُلُّ خَتَّارٍ كَفُورٍ

And as soon as they are overwhelmed by waves like mountains, they cry out to Allah ˹alone˺ in sincere devotion. But when He delivers them ˹safely˺ to shore, only some become relatively grateful. And none rejects Our signs except whoever is deceitful, ungrateful.

And when the polytheists ride the ships, and waves surround them like clouds and mountains, they are overcome by fear and dread of drowning, so they turn in fright to Allah, and sincerely call upon Him. Then, when He saves them to the land, some of them are moderate, not fully thanking Allah, and some of them are ungrateful, denying Allah's favors. And no one denies Our signs and proofs that indicate the perfection of Our power and Our Oneness except every treacherous one who breaks his covenant, ungrateful for the favors of Allah upon him.

When faced with danger at sea, people turn to Allah sincerely, but some become ungrateful after being saved. This highlights human inconsistency.

Verse 33

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ اتَّقُوا رَبَّكُمْ وَاخْشَوْا يَوْمًا لَا يَجْزِي وَالِدٌ عَنْ وَلَدِهِ وَلَا مَوْلُودٌ هُوَ جَازٍ عَنْ وَالِدِهِ شَيْئًا ۚ إِنَّ وَعْدَ اللَّهِ حَقٌّ ۖ فَلَا تَغُرَّنَّكُمُ الْحَيَاةُ الدُّنْيَا وَلَا يَغُرَّنَّكُمْ بِاللَّهِ الْغَرُورُ

O humanity! Be mindful of your Lord, and beware of a Day when no parent will be of any benefit to their child, nor will a child be of any benefit to their parent. Surely Allah’s promise is true. So do not let the life of this world deceive you, nor let the Chief Deceiver deceive you about Allah.

O people, fear your Lord and obey Him by complying with His commands and avoiding His prohibitions, and beware of the Day of Resurrection when no parent will avail their child, nor will a child avail their parent in anything. Indeed, the promise of Allah is true without doubt, so do not be deceived by the life of this world and its adornments, thus making you forget the Hereafter, and let not the deceiver deceive you about Allah from among the devils of jinn and mankind.

This verse warns people to be mindful of Allah and the Day of Judgment, emphasizing that no one can help another on that day. It also cautions against being deceived by worldly life and the devil.

Verse 34

إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ وَيُنَزِّلُ الْغَيْثَ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الْأَرْحَامِ ۖ وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا ۖ وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ خَبِيرٌ

Indeed, Allah ˹alone˺ has the knowledge of the Hour. He sends down the rain, and knows what is in the wombs. No soul knows what it will earn for tomorrow, and no soul knows in what land it will die. Surely Allah is All-Knowing, All-Aware.

Indeed, Allah alone knows when the Hour will be established, and it is He who sends down rain from the clouds; no one besides Him is capable of that. And He knows what is in the wombs of the females, and He knows what every soul earns for tomorrow, and no soul knows in which land it will die. Rather, Allah Almighty is the One exclusively knowing all of that. Indeed, Allah is All-Knowing, All-Aware, encompassing the apparent and the hidden; nothing of it is hidden from Him.

This verse states that only Allah has knowledge of the Hour, when rain will fall, and what is in the wombs. It also states that no one knows where they will die, and that Allah is All-Knowing and All-Aware.
