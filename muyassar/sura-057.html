Sura 057

Verse 1

سَبَّحَ لِلَّهِ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

Whatever is in the heavens and the earth glorifies Allah, for He is the Almighty, All-Wise.

Allah is exalted above all imperfections. Everything in the heavens and the earth glorifies Him, from all His creation. And He is the Almighty over His creation, the Wise in managing their affairs.

Everything in the heavens and earth glorifies Allah, the Almighty and All-Wise.

Verse 2

لَهُ مُلْكُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۖ يُحْيِي وَيُمِيتُ ۖ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

To Him belongs the kingdom of the heavens and the earth. He gives life and causes death. And He is Most Capable of everything.

To Him belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth and what is in them. He is the owner, the disposer of His creation. He gives life and causes death, and He is over all things competent. Nothing is difficult for Him if He wills it; so what He wills happens, and what He does not will does not happen.

Allah owns the kingdom of the heavens and the earth, giving life and causing death, and He is capable of everything.

Verse 3

هُوَ الْأَوَّلُ وَالْآخِرُ وَالظَّاهِرُ وَالْبَاطِنُ ۖ وَهُوَ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيمٌ

He is the First and the Last, the Most High and Most Near, and He has ˹perfect˺ knowledge of all things.

He is the First, before whom there was nothing, and the Last, after whom there is nothing, and the Manifest, above whom there is nothing, and the Hidden, below whom there is nothing, and nothing is hidden from Him in the earth or in the sky. And He is Knowing of all things.

Allah is the First and the Last, the Most High and Most Near, and has perfect knowledge of all things.

Verse 4

هُوَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ فِي سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍ ثُمَّ اسْتَوَىٰ عَلَى الْعَرْشِ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا يَلِجُ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَمَا يَخْرُجُ مِنْهَا وَمَا يَنْزِلُ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ وَمَا يَعْرُجُ فِيهَا ۖ وَهُوَ مَعَكُمْ أَيْنَ مَا كُنْتُمْ ۚ وَاللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ بَصِيرٌ

He is the One Who created the heavens and the earth in six Days, then established Himself on the Throne. He knows whatever goes into the earth and whatever comes out of it, and whatever descends from the sky and whatever ascends into it. And He is with you wherever you are. For Allah is All-Seeing of what you do.

He is the One who created the heavens and the earth and what is between them in six days; then He ascended - meaning rose and elevated - over His Throne, above all His creation, in a manner befitting His Majesty. He knows what enters into the earth of seeds, rain and other things, and what comes out of it of plants, crops and fruits, and what descends from the sky of rain and other things, and what ascends in it of angels and deeds, and He, glory be to Him, is with you in His knowledge wherever you may be, and Allah is Seeing of your deeds which you do, and He will recompense you for them.

Allah created the universe in six days, then established Himself on the Throne. He knows everything that goes into and comes out of the earth, as well as what descends from and ascends to the sky. He is aware of everything you do.

Verse 5

لَهُ مُلْكُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ وَإِلَى اللَّهِ تُرْجَعُ الْأُمُورُ

To Him belongs the kingdom of the heavens and the earth. And to Allah all matters are returned.

To Him belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth, and to Allah is the destination of the affairs of creation in the Hereafter, and He will recompense them for their deeds.

The dominion of the heavens and the earth belongs to Allah alone. All matters will ultimately return to Him.

Verse 6

يُولِجُ اللَّيْلَ فِي النَّهَارِ وَيُولِجُ النَّهَارَ فِي اللَّيْلِ ۚ وَهُوَ عَلِيمٌ بِذَاتِ الصُّدُورِ

He merges the night into day and the day into night. And He knows best what is ˹hidden˺ in the heart.

He causes what decreases from the hours of the night to enter into the day, thus increasing the day, and He causes what decreases from the hours of the day to enter into the night, thus increasing the night, and He, glory be to Him, is All-Knowing of what is in the chests of His creation.

Allah causes the night to merge into day and vice-versa. He knows what is hidden in the hearts of people.

Verse 7

آمِنُوا بِاللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ وَأَنْفِقُوا مِمَّا جَعَلَكُمْ مُسْتَخْلَفِينَ فِيهِ ۖ فَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مِنْكُمْ وَأَنْفَقُوا لَهُمْ أَجْرٌ كَبِيرٌ

Believe in Allah and His Messenger, and donate from what He has entrusted you with. So those of you who believe and donate will have a mighty reward.

Believe in Allah and His Messenger Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and spend from what Allah has provided for you of wealth and made you successors in it. For those who believe among you, O people, and spend from their wealth, there is a great reward for them.

Believe in Allah and His Messenger, and donate from what Allah has entrusted you. Those who believe and donate will receive a great reward.

Verse 8

وَمَا لَكُمْ لَا تُؤْمِنُونَ بِاللَّهِ ۙ وَالرَّسُولُ يَدْعُوكُمْ لِتُؤْمِنُوا بِرَبِّكُمْ وَقَدْ أَخَذَ مِيثَاقَكُمْ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ مُؤْمِنِينَ

Why do you not believe in Allah while the Messenger is inviting you to have faith in your Lord, although He has already taken your covenant, if you will ever believe.

And what excuse do you have not to believe in the Oneness of Allah and act according to His law, while the Messenger calls you to that, and Allah has taken your covenant for that, if you are believers in Allah your Creator?

Why do you not believe when the Messenger calls you to believe in your Lord? Allah has taken a covenant from you, if you are believers.

Verse 9

هُوَ الَّذِي يُنَزِّلُ عَلَىٰ عَبْدِهِ آيَاتٍ بَيِّنَاتٍ لِيُخْرِجَكُمْ مِنَ الظُّلُمَاتِ إِلَى النُّورِ ۚ وَإِنَّ اللَّهَ بِكُمْ لَرَءُوفٌ رَحِيمٌ

He is the One Who sends down clear revelations to His servant to bring you out of darkness and into light. For indeed Allah is Ever Gracious and Most Merciful to you.

He is the One who sends down upon His servant Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, clear, detailed verses from the Quran, to bring you out thereby from the darkness of disbelief to the light of faith. Indeed, Allah is merciful to you in bringing you out from darkness to light, and He will give you a vast mercy in your present and future life, and will reward you with the best reward.

Allah sends down clear verses to His servant to guide you from the darkness of disbelief to the light of faith. Indeed, Allah is Gracious and Merciful to you.

Verse 10

وَمَا لَكُمْ أَلَّا تُنْفِقُوا فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ وَلِلَّهِ مِيرَاثُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ لَا يَسْتَوِي مِنْكُمْ مَنْ أَنْفَقَ مِنْ قَبْلِ الْفَتْحِ وَقَاتَلَ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ أَعْظَمُ دَرَجَةً مِنَ الَّذِينَ أَنْفَقُوا مِنْ بَعْدُ وَقَاتَلُوا ۚ وَكُلًّا وَعَدَ اللَّهُ الْحُسْنَىٰ ۚ وَاللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌ

And why should you not spend in the cause of Allah, while Allah is the ˹sole˺ inheritor of the heavens and the earth? Those of you who donated and fought before the victory ˹over Mecca˺ are unparalleled. They are far greater in rank than those who donated and fought afterwards. Yet Allah has promised each a fine reward. And Allah is All-Aware of what you do.

And what prevents you from spending in the cause of Allah? And to Allah belongs the inheritance of the heavens and the earth; He inherits everything in them, and no one remains the owner of anything in them. Not equal in reward and recompense are those among you who spent before the conquest of "Mecca" and fought the disbelievers. Those are greater in rank with Allah than those who spent in the cause of Allah after the conquest and fought the disbelievers, and both groups Allah has promised Paradise. And Allah is All-Aware of your deeds, nothing of them is hidden from Him, and He will recompense you for them.

Why shouldn't you spend in Allah's cause when Allah is the inheritor of everything? Those who spent and fought before the victory are greater in rank than those who did so afterwards. Yet, Allah has promised a good reward to both groups. And Allah is aware of what you do.

Verse 11

مَنْ ذَا الَّذِي يُقْرِضُ اللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا فَيُضَاعِفَهُ لَهُ وَلَهُ أَجْرٌ كَرِيمٌ

Who is it that will lend to Allah a good loan which Allah will multiply ˹many times over˺ for them, and they will have an honourable reward?

Who is it that will spend in the cause of Allah, seeking reward from his heart, without reminder of favor or harm, so his Lord will multiply his reward and recompense, and he will have a generous reward, which is Paradise?

Who will lend Allah a good loan, for which Allah will multiply the reward and grant an honorable recompense?

Verse 12

يَوْمَ تَرَى الْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَالْمُؤْمِنَاتِ يَسْعَىٰ نُورُهُمْ بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَبِأَيْمَانِهِمْ بُشْرَاكُمُ الْيَوْمَ جَنَّاتٌ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ ذَٰلِكَ هُوَ الْفَوْزُ الْعَظِيمُ

On that Day you will see believing men and women with their light shining ahead of them and on their right. ˹They will be told,˺ “Today you have good news of Gardens, under which rivers flow, ˹for you˺ to stay in forever. This is ˹truly˺ the ultimate triumph.”

On the Day you see the believing men and believing women, their light proceeding before them and on their right, [it will be said], "Your good tidings today are [of] gardens beneath which rivers flow, wherein you will abide eternally." That is what is the great attainment.

On Judgment Day, believers will be distinguished by their light, and they will be given the good news of Paradise, a place of eternal bliss.

Verse 13

يَوْمَ يَقُولُ الْمُنَافِقُونَ وَالْمُنَافِقَاتُ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا انْظُرُونَا نَقْتَبِسْ مِنْ نُورِكُمْ قِيلَ ارْجِعُوا وَرَاءَكُمْ فَالْتَمِسُوا نُورًا فَضُرِبَ بَيْنَهُمْ بِسُورٍ لَهُ بَابٌ بَاطِنُهُ فِيهِ الرَّحْمَةُ وَظَاهِرُهُ مِنْ قِبَلِهِ الْعَذَابُ

On that Day hypocrite men and women will beg the believers, “Wait for us so that we may have some of your light.” It will be said ˹mockingly˺, “Go back ˹to the world˺ and seek a light ˹there˺!” Then a ˹separating˺ wall with a gate will be erected between them. On the near side will be grace and on the far side will be torment.

On the Day the hypocrite men and hypocrite women will say to those who believed, “Wait for us that we may acquire some of your light.” It will be said, “Go back behind you and seek light.” And a wall will be placed between them with a door, within which is mercy and without which is torment.

On that Day, hypocrites will seek help from the believers, but they will be turned away and separated by a wall. Believers will be on the side of mercy, while hypocrites will face torment.

Verse 14

يُنَادُونَهُمْ أَلَمْ نَكُنْ مَعَكُمْ ۖ قَالُوا بَلَىٰ وَلَٰكِنَّكُمْ فَتَنْتُمْ أَنْفُسَكُمْ وَتَرَبَّصْتُمْ وَارْتَبْتُمْ وَغَرَّتْكُمُ الْأَمَانِيُّ حَتَّىٰ جَاءَ أَمْرُ اللَّهِ وَغَرَّكُمْ بِاللَّهِ الْغَرُورُ

The tormented will cry out to those graced, “Were we not with you?” They will reply, “Yes ˹you were˺. But you chose to be tempted ˹by hypocrisy˺, ˹eagerly˺ awaited ˹our demise˺, doubted ˹the truth˺, and were deluded by false hopes until Allah’s decree ˹of your death˺ came to pass. And ˹so˺ the Chief Deceiver deceived you about Allah.

The hypocrites will call to the believers, “Were we not with you?” They will say, “Yes, but you put yourselves in temptation, and you waited and doubted, and wishful thinking deluded you until the command of Allah came. And the deceiver deceived you concerning Allah.”

The hypocrites will question the believers, reminding them of their past association. Believers will respond that the hypocrites were tested, doubted, and deceived by Satan until their death.

Verse 15

فَالْيَوْمَ لَا يُؤْخَذُ مِنْكُمْ فِدْيَةٌ وَلَا مِنَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا ۚ مَأْوَاكُمُ النَّارُ ۖ هِيَ مَوْلَاكُمْ ۖ وَبِئْسَ الْمَصِيرُ

So Today no ransom will be accepted from you ˹hypocrites˺, nor from the disbelievers. Your home is the Fire—it is the ˹only˺ fitting place for you. What an evil destination!”

So today no ransom will be accepted from you, nor from those who disbelieved. Your refuge is the Fire. It is most worthy of you, and how evil the destination.

No ransom will be accepted from the hypocrites or disbelievers on Judgment Day. Their final destination will be the Fire, a fitting place for them.

Verse 16

أَلَمْ يَأْنِ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا أَنْ تَخْشَعَ قُلُوبُهُمْ لِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ وَمَا نَزَلَ مِنَ الْحَقِّ وَلَا يَكُونُوا كَالَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْكِتَابَ مِنْ قَبْلُ فَطَالَ عَلَيْهِمُ الْأَمَدُ فَقَسَتْ قُلُوبُهُمْ ۖ وَكَثِيرٌ مِنْهُمْ فَاسِقُونَ

Has the time not yet come for believers’ hearts to be humbled at the remembrance of Allah and what has been revealed of the truth, and not be like those given the Scripture before—˹those˺ who were spoiled for so long that their hearts became hardened. And many of them are ˹still˺ rebellious.

Has the time not come for those who have believed that their hearts should become humbly submissive at the remembrance of Allah and what has come down of the truth? And let them not be like those who were given the Scripture before, and a long period passed over them, so their hearts hardened; and many of them are defiantly disobedient. And in the verse, urging gentleness and humility to Allah Almighty when hearing what He has revealed from the Book and wisdom, and caution against being like the Jews and Christians, in the hardness of their hearts, and their disobedience to Allah.

This verse urges believers to have humble hearts when remembering Allah and His revelations. It warns against being like those who received scriptures before but became hardened and disobedient over time.

Verse 17

اعْلَمُوا أَنَّ اللَّهَ يُحْيِي الْأَرْضَ بَعْدَ مَوْتِهَا ۚ قَدْ بَيَّنَّا لَكُمُ الْآيَاتِ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَعْقِلُونَ

Know that Allah revives the earth after its death. We have certainly made the signs clear for you so perhaps you will understand.

Know that Allah gives life to the earth after its lifelessness. We have made clear to you the signs; perhaps you will understand.

Just as Allah brings life to the barren earth, He also brings guidance and understanding to hearts. This verse emphasizes the clear signs of Allah for those who reflect.

Verse 18

إِنَّ الْمُصَّدِّقِينَ وَالْمُصَّدِّقَاتِ وَأَقْرَضُوا اللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا يُضَاعَفُ لَهُمْ وَلَهُمْ أَجْرٌ كَرِيمٌ

Indeed, those men and women who give in charity and lend to Allah a good loan will have it multiplied for them, and they will have an honourable reward.

Indeed, the men who practice charity and the women who practice charity and [they who] have loaned Allah a goodly loan - it will be multiplied for them, and they will have a noble reward.

Those who give charity and lend to Allah a good loan will have their reward multiplied and will receive a noble reward in the Hereafter.

Verse 19

وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا بِاللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِ أُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الصِّدِّيقُونَ ۖ وَالشُّهَدَاءُ عِنْدَ رَبِّهِمْ لَهُمْ أَجْرُهُمْ وَنُورُهُمْ ۖ وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا وَكَذَّبُوا بِآيَاتِنَا أُولَٰئِكَ أَصْحَابُ الْجَحِيمِ

˹As for˺ those who believe in Allah and His messengers, it is they who are ˹truly˺ the people of truth. And the martyrs, with their Lord, will have their reward and their light. But ˹as for˺ those who disbelieve and reject Our signs, it is they who will be the residents of the Hellfire.

And those who have believed in Allah and His messengers and have not discriminated between any of them - those are the truthful ones who have perfected their belief in what the messengers brought, in belief, word, and deed, and the martyrs with their Lord will have their due reward with Allah and their great light on the Day of Resurrection. And those who disbelieved and denied Our signs and proofs - those are the companions of Hellfire, so they will have no reward or light.

This verse highlights the distinction between believers and disbelievers. Believers and martyrs will receive their rewards and light from Allah, while those who reject the truth will face the Hellfire.

Verse 20

اعْلَمُوا أَنَّمَا الْحَيَاةُ الدُّنْيَا لَعِبٌ وَلَهْوٌ وَزِينَةٌ وَتَفَاخُرٌ بَيْنَكُمْ وَتَكَاثُرٌ فِي الْأَمْوَالِ وَالْأَوْلَادِ ۖ كَمَثَلِ غَيْثٍ أَعْجَبَ الْكُفَّارَ نَبَاتُهُ ثُمَّ يَهِيجُ فَتَرَاهُ مُصْفَرًّا ثُمَّ يَكُونُ حُطَامًا ۖ وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ شَدِيدٌ وَمَغْفِرَةٌ مِنَ اللَّهِ وَرِضْوَانٌ ۚ وَمَا الْحَيَاةُ الدُّنْيَا إِلَّا مَتَاعُ الْغُرُورِ

Know that this worldly life is no more than play, amusement, luxury, mutual boasting, and competition in wealth and children. This is like rain that causes plants to grow, to the delight of the planters. But later the plants dry up and you see them wither, then they are reduced to chaff. And in the Hereafter there will be either severe punishment or forgiveness and pleasure of Allah, whereas the life of this world is no more than the delusion of enjoyment.

Know that the life of this world is but amusement and diversion, bodies playing and hearts being amused, and adornment with which you beautify yourselves, and boasting among yourselves in its possessions, and rivalry in increase of wealth and children. Its likeness is that of rain whose growth pleases the tillers, then it dries up, and you see it turn yellow after its greenness, then it becomes broken pieces of straw. And in the Hereafter is severe punishment for the disbelievers and forgiveness from Allah and acceptance for the people of faith. And the life of this world for those who work for it, forgetting their hereafter, is only the enjoyment of delusion.

The worldly life is described as temporary and filled with distractions like amusement, boasting, and competition. It's like a plant that grows, then withers. The Hereafter will bring either severe punishment or forgiveness, emphasizing the importance of focusing on the eternal life rather than being consumed by worldly pleasures.

Verse 21

سَابِقُوا إِلَىٰ مَغْفِرَةٍ مِنْ رَبِّكُمْ وَجَنَّةٍ عَرْضُهَا كَعَرْضِ السَّمَاءِ وَالْأَرْضِ أُعِدَّتْ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا بِاللَّهِ وَرُسُلِهِ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ فَضْلُ اللَّهِ يُؤْتِيهِ مَنْ يَشَاءُ ۚ وَاللَّهُ ذُو الْفَضْلِ الْعَظِيمِ

˹So˺ compete with one another for forgiveness from your Lord and a Paradise as vast as the heavens and the earth, prepared for those who believe in Allah and His messengers. This is the favour of Allah. He grants it to whoever He wills. And Allah is the Lord of infinite bounty.

Race one another to forgiveness from your Lord and a Garden whose width is like the width of the heaven and earth, which has been prepared for those who have unified Allah and followed His messengers. That is the bounty of Allah, which He grants to whom He wills of His creation. Paradise is not attained except by the mercy of Allah and His grace, and righteous deeds. And Allah is the possessor of great bounty to His believing servants.

This verse encourages believers to compete in seeking forgiveness from Allah and striving for Paradise, which is as vast as the heavens and the earth. It emphasizes that Paradise is a gift from Allah granted to those who believe and follow His messengers.

Verse 22

مَا أَصَابَ مِنْ مُصِيبَةٍ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا فِي أَنْفُسِكُمْ إِلَّا فِي كِتَابٍ مِنْ قَبْلِ أَنْ نَبْرَأَهَا ۚ إِنَّ ذَٰلِكَ عَلَى اللَّهِ يَسِيرٌ

No calamity ˹or blessing˺ occurs on earth or in yourselves without being ˹written˺ in a Record before We bring it into being. This is certainly easy for Allah.

No disaster strikes upon the earth or among yourselves except that it is written in the Preserved Tablet before it is created. Indeed that, for Allah , is easy.

This verse explains that all events, good or bad, are written in a Divine Record before they occur, highlighting Allah's knowledge and control over everything.

Verse 23

لِكَيْلَا تَأْسَوْا عَلَىٰ مَا فَاتَكُمْ وَلَا تَفْرَحُوا بِمَا آتَاكُمْ ۗ وَاللَّهُ لَا يُحِبُّ كُلَّ مُخْتَالٍ فَخُورٍ

˹We let you know this˺ so that you neither grieve over what you have missed nor boast over what He has granted you. For Allah does not like whoever is arrogant, boastful—

And whoever turns away from the obedience of Allah only harms himself, and he will not harm Allah in the least. For Allah is the Free of need from His creation, the Praiseworthy, to whom every good and perfect attribute belongs, and every beautiful deed worthy of praise.

This verse advises against excessive grief over losses and arrogance over blessings. It emphasizes that Allah dislikes those who are boastful and proud.

Verse 25

لَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا رُسُلَنَا بِالْبَيِّنَاتِ وَأَنْزَلْنَا مَعَهُمُ الْكِتَابَ وَالْمِيزَانَ لِيَقُومَ النَّاسُ بِالْقِسْطِ ۖ وَأَنْزَلْنَا الْحَدِيدَ فِيهِ بَأْسٌ شَدِيدٌ وَمَنَافِعُ لِلنَّاسِ وَلِيَعْلَمَ اللَّهُ مَنْ يَنْصُرُهُ وَرُسُلَهُ بِالْغَيْبِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ قَوِيٌّ عَزِيزٌ

Indeed, We sent Our messengers with clear proofs, and with them We sent down the Scripture and the balance ˹of justice˺ so that people may administer justice. Surely Allah is All-Powerful, Almighty.

We did indeed send Our messengers with clear proofs, and sent down with them the Book and the Balance of Justice so that humanity might establish justice. And We sent down iron, in which is (material for) mighty war, as well as many benefits for humanity. That was so that **Allah** would know who helps Him and His messengers unseen. Verily, **Allah** is All-Powerful, Almighty.

God sent messengers with clear signs, scriptures, and justice so people could establish fairness. He also provided iron, representing strength and benefits, to test who would support Him and His messengers without seeing Him. God is Powerful and Almighty.

Verse 26

وَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا نُوحًا وَإِبْرَاهِيمَ وَجَعَلْنَا فِي ذُرِّيَّتِهِمَا النُّبُوَّةَ وَالْكِتَابَ ۖ فَمِنْهُمْ مُهْتَدٍ ۖ وَكَثِيرٌ مِنْهُمْ فَاسِقُونَ

God sent Noah and Abraham and placed prophethood and scripture among their descendants. While some were guided, many were disobedient.

Verse 27

ثُمَّ قَفَّيْنَا عَلَىٰ آثَارِهِمْ بِرُسُلِنَا وَقَفَّيْنَا بِعِيسَى ابْنِ مَرْيَمَ وَآتَيْنَاهُ الْإِنْجِيلَ وَجَعَلْنَا فِي قُلُوبِ الَّذِينَ اتَّبَعُوهُ رَأْفَةً وَرَحْمَةً وَرَهْبَانِيَّةً ابْتَدَعُوهَا مَا كَتَبْنَاهَا عَلَيْهِمْ إِلَّا ابْتِغَاءَ رِضْوَانِ اللَّهِ فَمَا رَعَوْهَا حَقَّ رِعَايَتِهَا ۖ فَآتَيْنَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مِنْهُمْ أَجْرَهُمْ ۖ وَكَثِيرٌ مِنْهُمْ فَاسِقُونَ

Then in the footsteps of these ˹prophets˺, We sent Our messengers, and ˹after them˺ We sent Jesus, son of Mary, and granted him the Gospel, and instilled compassion and mercy into the hearts of his followers. As for monasticism, they made it up—We never ordained it for them—only seeking to please Allah, yet they did not ˹even˺ observe it strictly. So We rewarded those of them who were faithful. But most of them are rebellious.

Then We (continued to) send Our messengers in their footsteps, and We sent Jesus son of Mary. We gave him the Gospel and instilled in the hearts of his followers compassion and mercy. They invented monasticism, which We did not prescribe for them. It was only to seek the pleasure of **Allah**. But they did not observe it with its proper observance. So We gave those among them who believed their reward. And many of them were deviators.

God continued sending messengers, including Jesus, who received the Gospel. Compassion was instilled in his followers, but monasticism, which was not ordained by God, was invented by them. They did not observe it properly. Some remained faithful while others were rebellious.

Verse 28

O you who have faith! Keep your duty to **Allah** and believe in His Messenger. He will give you twice your reward, grant you light by which you shall walk, and forgive you. And **Allah** is Most-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

Believers are urged to fear God and believe in His Messenger. Those who do will receive a double share of mercy, guidance and forgiveness. God is Forgiving and Merciful.

Verse 29

This is so the People of the Book understand they have no control over God’s grace, which He bestows as He wills. God is the Lord of infinite bounty.
