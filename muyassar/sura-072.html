Sura 072

Verse 1

قُلْ أُوحِيَ إِلَيَّ أَنَّهُ اسْتَمَعَ نَفَرٌ مِنَ الْجِنِّ فَقَالُوا إِنَّا سَمِعْنَا قُرْآنًا عَجَبًا

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “It has been revealed to me that a group of jinn listened ˹to the Quran,˺ and said ˹to their fellow jinn˺: ‘Indeed, we have heard a wondrous recitation.

Say, [O Prophet], “It has been revealed to me that a group of jinn listened and said, ‘Indeed, we have heard an amazing Qur’an. It guides to the right path, so we have believed in it. And we will never associate anyone with our Lord, who created us, in worship.’

This verse reveals that a group of jinn listened to the Quran and were amazed by its recitation, recognizing its divine origin.

Verse 2

يَهْدِي إِلَى الرُّشْدِ فَآمَنَّا بِهِ ۖ وَلَنْ نُشْرِكَ بِرَبِّنَا أَحَدًا

It leads to Right Guidance so we believed in it, and we will never associate anyone with our Lord ˹in worship˺.

Say, [O Prophet], “It has been revealed to me that a group of jinn listened and said, ‘Indeed, we have heard an amazing Qur’an. It guides to the right path, so we have believed in it. And we will never associate anyone with our Lord, who created us, in worship.’

The jinn acknowledged that the Quran guides to the right path, leading them to believe in it and to reject associating partners with Allah.

Verse 3

وَأَنَّهُ تَعَالَىٰ جَدُّ رَبِّنَا مَا اتَّخَذَ صَاحِبَةً وَلَا وَلَدًا

˹Now, we believe that˺ our Lord—Exalted is His Majesty—has neither taken a mate nor offspring,

And that the majesty and grandeur of our Lord is exalted; He has not taken a wife or a son.

The jinn proclaimed that their Lord is exalted and does not have a wife or offspring, affirming His uniqueness.

Verse 4

وَأَنَّهُ كَانَ يَقُولُ سَفِيهُنَا عَلَى اللَّهِ شَطَطًا

and that the foolish of us used to utter ˹outrageous˺ falsehoods about Allah.

And that our foolish one – that is, Iblis – used to say about Allah that which was far from the truth and right, concerning the claim of having a companion and a son.

The jinn admitted that some of them foolishly used to attribute falsehoods to Allah, specifically regarding the claim of Him having a companion or son.

Verse 5

وَأَنَّا ظَنَنَّا أَنْ لَنْ تَقُولَ الْإِنْسُ وَالْجِنُّ عَلَى اللَّهِ كَذِبًا

We certainly thought that humans and jinn would never speak lies about Allah.

And that we thought no one would ever lie about Allah, neither humans nor jinn, by attributing a companion and son to Him.

The jinn confessed that they once believed neither humans nor jinn would lie about Allah, highlighting the gravity of associating partners with Him.

Verse 6

وَأَنَّهُ كَانَ رِجَالٌ مِنَ الْإِنْسِ يَعُوذُونَ بِرِجَالٍ مِنَ الْجِنِّ فَزَادُوهُمْ رَهَقًا

And some men used to seek refuge with some jinn—so they increased each other in wickedness.

And that there were men from among mankind who used to seek refuge with men from among the jinn, so the men of the jinn increased the humans in fear, terror, and dread by their seeking refuge with them. This seeking refuge with other than Allah, which Allah condemned in the people of ignorance, is the greatest form of shirk, which Allah does not forgive except with sincere repentance from it. And in the verse is a strong warning against resorting to sorcerers, charlatans, and their likes.

Some men used to seek refuge with the jinn, which only increased their wickedness and fear. This verse warns against seeking help from other than Allah.

Verse 7

وَأَنَّهُمْ ظَنُّوا كَمَا ظَنَنْتُمْ أَنْ لَنْ يَبْعَثَ اللَّهُ أَحَدًا

And those ˹humans˺ thought, just like you ˹jinn˺, that Allah would not resurrect anyone ˹for judgment˺.

And that the disbelievers among mankind thought, as you thought, O company of jinn, that Allah would never resurrect anyone after death.

The jinn revealed that some humans, like them, thought Allah would not resurrect anyone, highlighting their disbelief in the afterlife.

Verse 8

وَأَنَّا لَمَسْنَا السَّمَاءَ فَوَجَدْنَاهَا مُلِئَتْ حَرَسًا شَدِيدًا وَشُهُبًا

˹Earlier˺ we tried to reach heaven ˹for news˺, only to find it filled with stern guards and shooting stars.

And that we - a company of jinn - sought to reach the sky to listen to the words of its inhabitants, but we found it filled with many angels who guard it and with burning meteors with which those who approach it are shot.

The jinn disclosed their past attempts to reach the sky for news, but found it heavily guarded by angels and shooting stars, preventing their access.

Verse 9

وَأَنَّا كُنَّا نَقْعُدُ مِنْهَا مَقَاعِدَ لِلسَّمْعِ ۖ فَمَنْ يَسْتَمِعِ الْآنَ يَجِدْ لَهُ شِهَابًا رَصَدًا

We used to take up positions there for eavesdropping, but whoever dares eavesdrop now will find a flare lying in wait for them.

And we used to take up positions in the sky before to listen to its news, but whoever tries to eavesdrop now will find a meteor lying in wait for him, which burns and destroys him. And in these two verses is the refutation of the claims of sorcerers and charlatans, who claim to know the unseen and deceive the weak-minded with their lies and fabrications.

Previously, the jinn could eavesdrop in the sky, but now, anyone who tries will be struck by a meteor, thus refuting the claims of sorcerers.

Verse 10

وَأَنَّا لَا نَدْرِي أَشَرٌّ أُرِيدَ بِمَنْ فِي الْأَرْضِ أَمْ أَرَادَ بِهِمْ رَبُّهُمْ رَشَدًا

Now, we have no clue whether evil is intended for those on earth, or their Lord intends for them what is right.

And that we – a company of jinn – do not know whether evil is intended by Allah to befall the inhabitants of the earth or whether He intends for them good and guidance.

The jinn declared their ignorance of whether Allah intends good or evil for the people on earth, acknowledging Allah's complete control over all matters.

Verse 11

وَأَنَّا مِنَّا الصَّالِحُونَ وَمِنَّا دُونَ ذَٰلِكَ ۖ كُنَّا طَرَائِقَ قِدَدًا

Among us are those who are righteous and those who are less so. We have been of different factions.

And that some of us are righteous and pious, and some of us are less than that; disbelievers and wicked. We were divided into different groups and ways.

The jinn admitted that they are a diverse group, some righteous and some less so, highlighting the existence of different factions among them.

Verse 12

وَأَنَّا ظَنَنَّا أَنْ لَنْ نُعْجِزَ اللَّهَ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَنْ نُعْجِزَهُ هَرَبًا

˹Now,˺ we truly know that we cannot frustrate Allah on earth, nor can we escape from Him ˹into heaven˺.

And that we were certain that Allah is able to subdue us, and that we are in His grasp and authority. So we can never escape Him if He wants something for us, wherever we are, and we will not be able to escape His punishment by fleeing to the sky, if He intends evil for us.

The jinn recognized that they cannot escape Allah's power and authority, understanding His complete control over them.

Verse 13

وَأَنَّا لَمَّا سَمِعْنَا الْهُدَىٰ آمَنَّا بِهِ ۖ فَمَنْ يُؤْمِنْ بِرَبِّهِ فَلَا يَخَافُ بَخْسًا وَلَا رَهَقًا

When we heard the guidance ˹of the Quran˺, we ˹readily˺ believed in it. For whoever believes in their Lord will have no fear of being denied ˹a reward˺ or wronged.

And when we heard the Qur'an, we believed in it and acknowledged that it is the truth from Allah. So whoever believes in his Lord will not fear any decrease in his good deeds, nor any injustice that would befall him by an increase in his bad deeds.

The jinn, having heard the Quran, readily believed and affirmed that those who believe in their Lord will not fear any injustice or loss of reward.

Verse 14

وَأَنَّا مِنَّا الْمُسْلِمُونَ وَمِنَّا الْقَاسِطُونَ ۖ فَمَنْ أَسْلَمَ فَأُولَٰئِكَ تَحَرَّوْا رَشَدًا

And among us are those who have submitted ˹to Allah˺ and those who are deviant. So ˹as for˺ those who submitted, it is they who have attained Right Guidance.

And among us are those who submit to Allah in obedience, and among us are the deviators, the unjust who have strayed from the path of truth. So whoever submits and yields to Allah in obedience, then those are the ones who have sought the path of truth and correctness and strived in choosing it, so Allah has guided them to it. As for those who deviate from the path of Islam, they will be fuel for Hell.

This verse speaks about the division of people, some who submit to Allah's will and are rightly guided, and others who deviate from the path of truth.

Verse 15

وَأَمَّا الْقَاسِطُونَ فَكَانُوا لِجَهَنَّمَ حَطَبًا

And as for the deviant, they will be fuel for Hell.’”

And among us are those who submit to Allah in obedience, and among us are the deviators, the unjust who have strayed from the path of truth. So whoever submits and yields to Allah in obedience, then those are the ones who have sought the path of truth and correctness and strived in choosing it, so Allah has guided them to it. As for those who deviate from the path of Islam, they will be fuel for Hell.

Those who choose to deviate from the path of Islam will face the consequences in Hell.

Verse 16

وَأَنْ لَوِ اسْتَقَامُوا عَلَى الطَّرِيقَةِ لَأَسْقَيْنَاهُمْ مَاءً غَدَقًا

Had the deniers followed the Right Way, We would have certainly granted them abundant rain to drink—

And that if the disbelievers among humans and jinn had followed the path of Islam and not deviated from it, We would have sent down upon them abundant water and expanded for them provision in this world, to test them: how they would thank Allah for His favors upon them? And whoever turns away from obeying his Lord and listening to the Quran, pondering it, and acting upon it, He will admit him into a severe and grievous torment.

If disbelievers followed the right path, Allah would provide them with abundant blessings as a test of their gratitude.

Verse 17

لِنَفْتِنَهُمْ فِيهِ ۚ وَمَنْ يُعْرِضْ عَنْ ذِكْرِ رَبِّهِ يَسْلُكْهُ عَذَابًا صَعَدًا

as a test for them. And whoever turns away from the remembrance of their Lord will be admitted by Him into an overwhelming punishment.

And that if the disbelievers among humans and jinn had followed the path of Islam and not deviated from it, We would have sent down upon them abundant water and expanded for them provision in this world, to test them: how they would thank Allah for His favors upon them? And whoever turns away from obeying his Lord and listening to the Quran, pondering it, and acting upon it, He will admit him into a severe and grievous torment.

Those who turn away from remembering Allah and His guidance will face severe punishment.

Verse 18

وَأَنَّ الْمَسَاجِدَ لِلَّهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا مَعَ اللَّهِ أَحَدًا

The places of worship are ˹only˺ for Allah, so do not invoke anyone besides Him.

And that the mosques are for the worship of Allah alone, so do not worship anyone else therein, and dedicate your supplication and worship within them to Him. For the mosques were not built except for Allah alone to be worshipped in them, apart from anyone else. And in this is the obligation to purify the mosques from everything that taints devotion to Allah and following His Messenger Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him.

Mosques are dedicated solely for the worship of Allah. Therefore, all forms of worship should be directed to Him alone.

Verse 19

وَأَنَّهُ لَمَّا قَامَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ يَدْعُوهُ كَادُوا يَكُونُونَ عَلَيْهِ لِبَدًا

Yet when the servant of Allah stood up calling upon Him ˹alone˺, the pagans almost swarmed over him.

And that when Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, stood up to worship his Lord, the jinn almost crowded around him in heaps, some on top of others, because of the intensity of their gathering to hear the Quran from him.

When Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) stood to worship Allah, the jinn were intensely drawn to listen to his recitation of the Quran.

Verse 20

قُلْ إِنَّمَا أَدْعُو رَبِّي وَلَا أُشْرِكُ بِهِ أَحَدًا

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “I call only upon my Lord, associating none with Him ˹in worship˺.”

Say -O Messenger- to these disbelievers: 'I only worship my Lord alone, and I do not associate anyone with Him in worship.'

The Prophet (peace be upon him) declares that he only worships his Lord and does not associate any partners with Him.

Verse 21

Say, “It is not in my power to harm or benefit you.”

Say -O Messenger- to them: 'I have no power to avert any harm from you, nor to bring you any benefit.' Say: 'Indeed, no one can save me from Allah's punishment if I disobey Him, nor can I find any refuge apart from Him to flee to from His torment. But I can only convey to you from Allah what He has commanded me to convey to you, and His message with which He has sent me to you. And whoever disobeys Allah and His Messenger and turns away from the religion of Allah, then his recompense is the Fire of Hell, from which he will never depart.

The Prophet (peace be upon him) clarifies that he has no power to cause harm or bring benefit to anyone.

Verse 23

إِلَّا بَلَاغًا مِنَ اللَّهِ وَرِسَالَاتِهِ ۚ وَمَنْ يَعْصِ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ فَإِنَّ لَهُ نَارَ جَهَنَّمَ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا أَبَدًا

Verse 24

حَتَّىٰ إِذَا رَأَوْا مَا يُوعَدُونَ فَسَيَعْلَمُونَ مَنْ أَضْعَفُ نَاصِرًا وَأَقَلُّ عَدَدًا

Only when they see what they have been threatened with will they know who is weaker in helpers and inferior in manpower.

Until when the polytheists see what they are promised of torment, then they will know at its arrival upon them: who is weaker in support and helpers and less in soldiers?

The disbelievers will realize who is weaker in support and power when they face the promised torment.

Verse 25

Say, “I do not know if what you are promised is near or my Lord has set a distant time for it.

Say -O Messenger- to these polytheists: 'I do not know whether the torment you are promised is near in time, or whether my Lord will make a long duration for it?' He is the Knower of the unseen, and He does not disclose His unseen to anyone of His creation, except whom Allah has chosen for His message and approved of, for indeed He will reveal to them some of the unseen. And He sends from before the Messenger and from behind him angels to protect him from the jinn, lest they eavesdrop and whisper it to the soothsayers. So that the Messenger, peace and blessings be upon him, may know that the messengers before him were in the same state as him, conveying the truth and sincerity, and that he was protected as they were protected from the jinn, and that Allah has encompassed in His knowledge what they have, externally and internally, of laws and rulings and other things, nothing of it escapes Him, and that He has enumerated everything in number, so nothing of it is hidden from Him.

The Prophet (peace be upon him) admits that he does not know when the promised torment will occur, emphasizing Allah's exclusive knowledge of the unseen.
