Sura 009

Verse 1

بَرَاءَةٌ مِنَ اللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ إِلَى الَّذِينَ عَاهَدْتُمْ مِنَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ

˹This is˺ a discharge from all obligations, by Allah and His Messenger, to the polytheists you ˹believers˺ have entered into treaties with:

This is a declaration of innocence from God and His Messenger, and a declaration of abandoning the covenants that were between the Muslims and the polytheists.

This verse declares the termination of treaties between Muslims and polytheists, signifying a new phase in their relationship.

Verse 2

فَسِيحُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ أَرْبَعَةَ أَشْهُرٍ وَاعْلَمُوا أَنَّكُمْ غَيْرُ مُعْجِزِي اللَّهِ ۙ وَأَنَّ اللَّهَ مُخْزِي الْكَافِرِينَ

“You ˹polytheists˺ may travel freely through the land for four months, but know that you will have no escape from Allah, and that Allah will disgrace the disbelievers.”

So travel - O polytheists - in the land for a period of four months, going wherever you wish, safe from the believers, and know that you will not escape punishment, and that God will humiliate the disbelievers and cause them to be disgraced in this world and the Fire in the Hereafter. This verse is for those who have absolute, non-temporary covenants, or for those who have a covenant of less than four months, so that four months may be completed for them, or for those who had a covenant and broke it.

Polytheists are given a four-month grace period to travel safely, but are warned that they cannot escape Allah's punishment.

Verse 3

وَأَذَانٌ مِنَ اللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ إِلَى النَّاسِ يَوْمَ الْحَجِّ الْأَكْبَرِ أَنَّ اللَّهَ بَرِيءٌ مِنَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ ۙ وَرَسُولُهُ ۚ فَإِنْ تُبْتُمْ فَهُوَ خَيْرٌ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِنْ تَوَلَّيْتُمْ فَاعْلَمُوا أَنَّكُمْ غَيْرُ مُعْجِزِي اللَّهِ ۗ وَبَشِّرِ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِعَذَابٍ أَلِيمٍ

A declaration from Allah and His Messenger ˹is made˺ to all people on the day of the greater pilgrimage that Allah and His Messenger are free of the polytheists. So if you ˹pagans˺ repent, it will be better for you. But if you turn away, then know that you will have no escape from Allah. And give good news ˹O Prophet˺ to the disbelievers of a painful punishment.

And a notification from Allah and His Messenger and a warning to the people on the Day of Sacrifice that Allah is free from the polytheists, and His Messenger is free from them as well. So if you - O polytheists - return to the truth and abandon your polytheism, then it is better for you. But if you turn away from accepting the truth and refuse to enter the religion of Allah, then know that you will not escape the punishment of Allah. And warn - O Messenger - those who turn away from Islam of the painful punishment of Allah.

A public announcement is made that Allah and His Messenger are free from obligations to polytheists. Polytheists are urged to repent, or face a painful punishment.

Verse 4

إِلَّا الَّذِينَ عَاهَدْتُمْ مِنَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ ثُمَّ لَمْ يَنْقُصُوكُمْ شَيْئًا وَلَمْ يُظَاهِرُوا عَلَيْكُمْ أَحَدًا فَأَتِمُّوا إِلَيْهِمْ عَهْدَهُمْ إِلَىٰ مُدَّتِهِمْ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الْمُتَّقِينَ

As for the polytheists who have honoured every term of their treaty with you and have not supported an enemy against you, honour your treaty with them until the end of its term. Surely Allah loves those who are mindful ˹of Him˺.

The exception to the previous ruling are the polytheists who entered into a covenant with you for a specific period of time, and did not betray the covenant, and did not assist any of the enemies against you, so complete their covenant with them until its limited end. Indeed, God loves the righteous who did what they were commanded to do, and avoided polytheism, betrayal, and other sins.

Treaties with polytheists who have honored their agreements should be upheld until the end of their term. Allah loves those who are mindful of their obligations.

Verse 5

فَإِذَا انْسَلَخَ الْأَشْهُرُ الْحُرُمُ فَاقْتُلُوا الْمُشْرِكِينَ حَيْثُ وَجَدْتُمُوهُمْ وَخُذُوهُمْ وَاحْصُرُوهُمْ وَاقْعُدُوا لَهُمْ كُلَّ مَرْصَدٍ ۚ فَإِنْ تَابُوا وَأَقَامُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَآتَوُا الزَّكَاةَ فَخَلُّوا سَبِيلَهُمْ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

But once the Sacred Months have passed, kill the polytheists ˹who violated their treaties˺ wherever you find them, capture them, besiege them, and lie in wait for them on every way. But if they repent, perform prayers, and pay alms-tax, then set them free. Indeed, Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

So when the four months in which you granted security to the polytheists have passed, declare war on the enemies of God wherever they may be, and target them by besieging their strongholds, and lie in wait for them on their roads. If they turn away from their disbelief and enter Islam and adhere to its laws of establishing prayer and giving zakat, then leave them alone, for they have become your brothers in Islam. Indeed, God is Forgiving to those who repent and return, and Merciful to them.

After the Sacred Months, fighting is allowed against polytheists who violated their treaties. However, if they repent, perform prayers, and pay alms-tax, they are to be set free.

Verse 6

وَإِنْ أَحَدٌ مِنَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ اسْتَجَارَكَ فَأَجِرْهُ حَتَّىٰ يَسْمَعَ كَلَامَ اللَّهِ ثُمَّ أَبْلِغْهُ مَأْمَنَهُ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّهُمْ قَوْمٌ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

And if anyone from the polytheists asks for your protection ˹O Prophet˺, grant it to them so they may hear the Word of Allah, then escort them to a place of safety, for they are a people who have no knowledge.

If one of the polytheists whose blood and property are permissible asks to enter your protection, O Messenger, and desires safety, then respond to his request so that he may hear the Noble Qur'an and learn of its guidance, then return him to where he came from in safety; this is to establish proof against him; this is because the disbelievers are a people ignorant of the truths of Islam, so they may choose it if ignorance is removed from them.

If a polytheist seeks protection to hear the word of Allah, they should be granted it and then escorted to a place of safety. This highlights the importance of knowledge and the removal of ignorance.

Verse 7

كَيْفَ يَكُونُ لِلْمُشْرِكِينَ عَهْدٌ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ وَعِنْدَ رَسُولِهِ إِلَّا الَّذِينَ عَاهَدْتُمْ عِنْدَ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ ۖ فَمَا اسْتَقَامُوا لَكُمْ فَاسْتَقِيمُوا لَهُمْ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الْمُتَّقِينَ

How can such polytheists have a treaty with Allah and His Messenger, except those you have made a treaty with at the Sacred Mosque? So, as long as they are true to you, be true to them. Indeed Allah loves those who are mindful ˹of Him˺.

The polytheists should not have a covenant with Allah and His Messenger, except for those with whom you made a covenant at the Sacred Mosque in the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah. So long as they continue to fulfill your covenant, then continue to do the same for them. Indeed, Allah loves the righteous, who fulfill their covenants.

This verse discusses treaties with polytheists, stating that only those with existing agreements, like the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, should be honored if they remain faithful. It emphasizes that Allah loves those who are mindful of their obligations.

Verse 8

كَيْفَ وَإِنْ يَظْهَرُوا عَلَيْكُمْ لَا يَرْقُبُوا فِيكُمْ إِلًّا وَلَا ذِمَّةً ۚ يُرْضُونَكُمْ بِأَفْوَاهِهِمْ وَتَأْبَىٰ قُلُوبُهُمْ وَأَكْثَرُهُمْ فَاسِقُونَ

How ˹can they have a treaty˺? If they were to have the upper hand over you, they would have no respect for kinship or treaty. They only flatter you with their tongues, but their hearts are in denial, and most of them are rebellious.

The polytheists are bound by their covenants as long as others are in control. However, if they feel they have power over the believers, they do not respect kinship or covenants. So do not be deceived by how they treat you when they fear you. They say things to you with their tongues so that you will be pleased with them, but their hearts refuse that, and most of them rebel against Islam and break their covenants.

This verse highlights the untrustworthy nature of polytheists. They only abide by treaties when they are weak; if they gain power, they will disregard any agreements and show their true animosity.

Verse 9

اشْتَرَوْا بِآيَاتِ اللَّهِ ثَمَنًا قَلِيلًا فَصَدُّوا عَنْ سَبِيلِهِ ۚ إِنَّهُمْ سَاءَ مَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

They chose a fleeting gain over Allah’s revelations, hindering ˹others˺ from His Way. Evil indeed is what they have done!

They replaced the verses of God with the trivial worldly show, so they turned away from the truth and prevented those who desired Islam from entering it. Their actions were ugly and their deeds were evil.

The verse criticizes the polytheists for prioritizing worldly gains over Allah's revelations and for hindering others from embracing Islam. Their actions are described as wicked and evil.

Verse 10

لَا يَرْقُبُونَ فِي مُؤْمِنٍ إِلًّا وَلَا ذِمَّةً ۚ وَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُعْتَدُونَ

They do not honour the bonds of kinship or treaties with the believers. It is they who are the transgressors.

These polytheists wage war on faith and its people. They do not give any weight to the kinship of the believer or his covenant. Their concern is aggression and injustice.

This verse describes the polytheists as transgressors who disrespect kinship and treaties with believers, emphasizing their hostility and injustice.

Verse 11

فَإِنْ تَابُوا وَأَقَامُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَآتَوُا الزَّكَاةَ فَإِخْوَانُكُمْ فِي الدِّينِ ۗ وَنُفَصِّلُ الْآيَاتِ لِقَوْمٍ يَعْلَمُونَ

But if they repent, perform prayer, and pay alms-tax, then they are your brothers in faith. This is how We make the revelations clear for people of knowledge.

If they stop worshipping other than God, and utter the word of monotheism, and adhere to the laws of Islam, such as establishing prayer and paying zakat, then they are your brothers in Islam. We explain the verses and clarify them for people who will benefit from them.

This verse offers a path to reconciliation. If polytheists repent, pray, and pay alms-tax, they become brothers in faith. It highlights that Allah clarifies His teachings for people of knowledge.

Verse 12

وَإِنْ نَكَثُوا أَيْمَانَهُمْ مِنْ بَعْدِ عَهْدِهِمْ وَطَعَنُوا فِي دِينِكُمْ فَقَاتِلُوا أَئِمَّةَ الْكُفْرِ ۙ إِنَّهُمْ لَا أَيْمَانَ لَهُمْ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَنْتَهُونَ

But if they break their oaths after making a pledge and attack your faith, then fight the champions of disbelief—who never honour their oaths—so perhaps they will desist.

If these polytheists break the covenants you have concluded with them and openly attack the religion of Islam, then fight them, for they are the leaders of misguidance, with whom there is no covenant or protection, until they cease their disbelief and hostility towards Islam.

This verse permits fighting against those who break their oaths and attack the faith. It calls them leaders of disbelief and emphasizes the need to fight them until they cease their hostility towards Islam.

Verse 13

أَلَا تُقَاتِلُونَ قَوْمًا نَكَثُوا أَيْمَانَهُمْ وَهَمُّوا بِإِخْرَاجِ الرَّسُولِ وَهُمْ بَدَءُوكُمْ أَوَّلَ مَرَّةٍ ۚ أَتَخْشَوْنَهُمْ ۚ فَاللَّهُ أَحَقُّ أَنْ تَخْشَوْهُ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ مُؤْمِنِينَ

Will you not fight those who have broken their oaths, conspired to expel the Messenger ˹from Mecca˺, and attacked you first? Do you fear them? Allah is more deserving of your fear, if you are ˹true˺ believers.

Do not hesitate to fight these people who broke their covenants and worked to expel the Messenger from Mecca. They are the ones who started harming you in the first place. Do you fear them or are you afraid to meet them in war? God is more deserving of your fear, if you are true believers.

This verse encourages believers to fight against those who broke their oaths, conspired against the Prophet, and initiated aggression. It emphasizes that fear of Allah should be greater than fear of the enemy.

Verse 14

قَاتِلُوهُمْ يُعَذِّبْهُمُ اللَّهُ بِأَيْدِيكُمْ وَيُخْزِهِمْ وَيَنْصُرْكُمْ عَلَيْهِمْ وَيَشْفِ صُدُورَ قَوْمٍ مُؤْمِنِينَ

˹So˺ fight them and Allah will punish them at your hands, put them to shame, help you overcome them, and soothe the hearts of the believers—

O believers, fight the enemies of Allah, and He will punish them by your hands, humiliate them with defeat and disgrace, give you victory over them, make His word supreme, and heal your hearts that have been filled with sadness and grief because of the plots of these polytheists, and remove the anger from the hearts of the believers. And whoever repents from these stubborn ones, then indeed, Allah accepts the repentance of whomever He wills. And Allah is All-Knowing of the sincerity of the repentant one, and Wise in His planning, making, and laying down His laws for His servants.

This verse commands believers to fight against their enemies, promising that Allah will punish them through the believers, humiliate them, and bring comfort to the believers' hearts.

Verse 15

وَيُذْهِبْ غَيْظَ قُلُوبِهِمْ ۗ وَيَتُوبُ اللَّهُ عَلَىٰ مَنْ يَشَاءُ ۗ وَاللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ حَكِيمٌ

removing rage from their hearts. And Allah pardons whoever He wills. For Allah is All-Knowing, All-Wise.

O believers, fight the enemies of Allah, and He will punish them by your hands, humiliate them with defeat and disgrace, give you victory over them, make His word supreme, and heal your hearts that have been filled with sadness and grief because of the plots of these polytheists, and remove the anger from the hearts of the believers. And whoever repents from these stubborn ones, then indeed, Allah accepts the repentance of whomever He wills. And Allah is All-Knowing of the sincerity of the repentant one, and Wise in His planning, making, and laying down His laws for His servants.

This verse continues the theme of the previous one, further emphasizing Allah's role in healing the believers' hearts and reiterates that Allah is All-Knowing and All-Wise in His judgments.

Verse 16

أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَنْ تُتْرَكُوا وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ جَاهَدُوا مِنْكُمْ وَلَمْ يَتَّخِذُوا مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ وَلَا رَسُولِهِ وَلَا الْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَلِيجَةً ۚ وَاللَّهُ خَبِيرٌ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ

Do you ˹believers˺ think that you will be left without Allah proving who among you ˹truly˺ struggles ˹in His cause˺ and never takes trusted allies other than Allah, His Messenger, or the believers? And Allah is All-Aware of what you do.

It is part of God's law to test, so do not think, O believers, that God will leave you without testing you. God will know with clear knowledge those who were sincere in their struggle and did not take anyone other than God, His Messenger, and the believers as confidants and allies. God is aware of all your deeds and will reward you for them.

This verse reminds believers that Allah tests them to distinguish those who truly strive for His cause and don't seek allies other than Allah, His Messenger, and fellow believers.

Verse 17

مَا كَانَ لِلْمُشْرِكِينَ أَنْ يَعْمُرُوا مَسَاجِدَ اللَّهِ شَاهِدِينَ عَلَىٰ أَنْفُسِهِمْ بِالْكُفْرِ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ حَبِطَتْ أَعْمَالُهُمْ وَفِي النَّارِ هُمْ خَالِدُونَ

It is not for the polytheists to maintain the mosques of Allah while they openly profess disbelief. Their deeds are void, and they will be in the Fire forever.

It is not the business of the polytheists to build the houses of God, and they declare their disbelief in God and associate partners with Him. The deeds of these polytheists will be nullified on the Day of Resurrection, and their destiny will be eternal in Hell.

This verse states that it is not for those who associate partners with Allah (polytheists) to maintain mosques, as their deeds are void, and their fate will be eternal hellfire.

Verse 18

إِنَّمَا يَعْمُرُ مَسَاجِدَ اللَّهِ مَنْ آمَنَ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ وَأَقَامَ الصَّلَاةَ وَآتَى الزَّكَاةَ وَلَمْ يَخْشَ إِلَّا اللَّهَ ۖ فَعَسَىٰ أُولَٰئِكَ أَنْ يَكُونُوا مِنَ الْمُهْتَدِينَ

The mosques of Allah should only be maintained by those who believe in Allah and the Last Day, establish prayer, pay alms-tax, and fear none but Allah. It is right to hope that they will be among the ˹truly˺ guided.

Only those who believe in God and the Last Day, establish prayer, give zakat, and do not fear blame from anyone for God's sake should take care of and maintain the houses of God. These people are the ones who are guided to the truth.

This verse specifies that those who maintain mosques should be believers who pray, pay alms (Zakat), and fear only Allah. These are the people who are guided.

Verse 19

أَجَعَلْتُمْ سِقَايَةَ الْحَاجِّ وَعِمَارَةَ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ كَمَنْ آمَنَ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ وَجَاهَدَ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ ۚ لَا يَسْتَوُونَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ ۗ وَاللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِي الْقَوْمَ الظَّالِمِينَ

Do you ˹pagans˺ consider providing the pilgrims with water and maintaining the Sacred Mosque as equal to believing in Allah and the Last Day and struggling in the cause of Allah? They are not equal in Allah’s sight. And Allah does not guide the wrongdoing people.

O people, do you consider what you do of providing water to the pilgrims and maintaining the Sacred Mosque as the faith of those who believe in Allah and the Last Day and strive in the cause of Allah? The state of believers and the state of disbelievers is not equal before Allah, because Allah does not accept any work without faith. And Allah, the Exalted, does not guide to good deeds the people who wrong themselves through disbelief.

This verse questions the pagans who think providing water to pilgrims and maintaining the Sacred Mosque is equal to believing in Allah, the Last Day, and striving in His cause. True belief is superior.

Verse 20

الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَهَاجَرُوا وَجَاهَدُوا فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ بِأَمْوَالِهِمْ وَأَنْفُسِهِمْ أَعْظَمُ دَرَجَةً عِنْدَ اللَّهِ ۚ وَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْفَائِزُونَ

Those who have believed, emigrated, and strived in the cause of Allah with their wealth and their lives are greater in rank in the sight of Allah. It is they who will triumph.

Those who believed in God and left the land of disbelief, heading for the land of Islam, and spent their wealth and their lives in jihad to raise the word of God, these have the highest degree with God, and they are the ones who are successful in gaining His pleasure.

This verse highlights the high status of those who believed, migrated, and strived in Allah's cause with their wealth and lives. They are the ones who will achieve success in His sight.

Verse 21

يُبَشِّرُهُمْ رَبُّهُمْ بِرَحْمَةٍ مِنْهُ وَرِضْوَانٍ وَجَنَّاتٍ لَهُمْ فِيهَا نَعِيمٌ مُقِيمٌ

Their Lord gives them good news of His mercy, pleasure, and Gardens with everlasting bliss,

These believing immigrants have good news from their Lord of vast mercy and pleasure after which there is no displeasure, and their destiny is the eternal gardens and everlasting bliss.

This verse announces good news for believers who migrate for their faith. They are promised Allah's mercy, pleasure, and eternal gardens of bliss.

Verse 22

خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا أَبَدًا ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ أَجْرٌ عَظِيمٌ

to stay there for ever and ever. Surely with Allah is a great reward.

They will remain in those gardens, their stay and enjoyment will never end. This is the reward for the acts of obedience and righteous deeds they performed in their worldly life. God Almighty has a great reward for those who believe and do righteous deeds by obeying His commands and avoiding His prohibitions.

The believers will live in these gardens forever, enjoying unending blessings. This reward comes from their righteous deeds and obedience to Allah.

Verse 23

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تَتَّخِذُوا آبَاءَكُمْ وَإِخْوَانَكُمْ أَوْلِيَاءَ إِنِ اسْتَحَبُّوا الْكُفْرَ عَلَى الْإِيمَانِ ۚ وَمَنْ يَتَوَلَّهُمْ مِنْكُمْ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الظَّالِمُونَ

O believers! Do not take your parents and siblings as trusted allies if they choose disbelief over belief. And whoever of you does so, they are the ˹true˺ wrongdoers.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, do not take your relatives - fathers, brothers, etc. - as allies, divulging to them the secrets of the Muslims, and consulting them in your affairs, as long as they are disbelievers and hostile to Islam. And whoever takes them as allies and bestows love upon them has disobeyed Allah Almighty and wronged himself greatly.

Believers are warned not to take disbelieving family members as allies. Those who do so are considered wrongdoers for prioritizing worldly ties over faith.

This verse reveals that some hypocrites would have followed the Prophet if the journey and the reward were easier. Their excuses and oaths are rejected, highlighting their insincerity.

Only those who lack faith and harbor doubts seek exemption from striving for Allah. Their hearts are filled with uncertainty, causing them to waver.

If the hypocrites had truly intended to join the battle, they would have made preparations. However, Allah did not want them to go, so they were left behind with those unable to participate.

This verse explains that the hypocrites' participation would have caused discord and trouble. Some believers were prone to listening to them. Allah is fully aware of the wrongdoers.

The reason for the rejection of the hypocrites' donations is their disbelief, their reluctance towards prayer, and their resentful giving. This highlights the importance of sincerity in worship and actions.
