Sura 024

Verse 1

سُورَةٌ أَنْزَلْنَاهَا وَفَرَضْنَاهَا وَأَنْزَلْنَا فِيهَا آيَاتٍ بَيِّنَاتٍ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَذَكَّرُونَ

˹This is˺ a sûrah which We have revealed and made ˹its rulings˺ obligatory, and revealed in it clear commandments so that you may be mindful.

This is a great Surah from the Quran which We have sent down, and made obligatory to act by its rules, and in which We have sent down clear signs, so that you - O believers - may remember with these clear verses, and act by them.

This Surah (chapter) is a divine revelation with clear rules and commandments, intended to guide believers and promote mindfulness of Allah's laws.

Verse 2

الزَّانِيَةُ وَالزَّانِي فَاجْلِدُوا كُلَّ وَاحِدٍ مِنْهُمَا مِائَةَ جَلْدَةٍ ۖ وَلَا تَأْخُذْكُمْ بِهِمَا رَأْفَةٌ فِي دِينِ اللَّهِ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ تُؤْمِنُونَ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ ۖ وَلْيَشْهَدْ عَذَابَهُمَا طَائِفَةٌ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

As for female and male fornicators, give each of them one hundred lashes, and do not let pity for them make you lenient in ˹enforcing˺ the law of Allah, if you ˹truly˺ believe in Allah and the Last Day. And let a number of believers witness their punishment.

The fornicatress and the fornicator who have not previously been married, the punishment of each of them is one hundred lashes with a whip, and it is established in the Sunnah, along with this lashing, exile for a year. And let not compassion for them prevent you from carrying out the punishment or reducing it, if you are believers in Allah and the Last Day, acting according to the rulings of Islam, and let a number of the believers witness the punishment, for denunciation, deterrence, admonition, and consideration.

This verse outlines the punishment for unmarried fornicators: one hundred lashes each. It emphasizes that compassion shouldn't compromise Allah's law, and the punishment should be witnessed by believers as a deterrent.

Verse 3

الزَّانِي لَا يَنْكِحُ إِلَّا زَانِيَةً أَوْ مُشْرِكَةً وَالزَّانِيَةُ لَا يَنْكِحُهَا إِلَّا زَانٍ أَوْ مُشْرِكٌ ۚ وَحُرِّمَ ذَٰلِكَ عَلَى الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

A male fornicator would only marry a female fornicator or idolatress. And a female fornicator would only be married to a fornicator or idolater. This is ˹all˺ forbidden to the believers.

The fornicator does not consent except to marry a fornicatress or a polytheist who does not acknowledge the prohibition of fornication, and the fornicatress does not consent except to marry a fornicator or a polytheist who does not acknowledge the prohibition of fornication. As for the chaste men and chaste women, they do not accept that, and that marriage is forbidden for the believers. This is a clear proof of the prohibition of marrying a fornicatress until she repents, as well as the prohibition of marrying off a fornicator until he repents.

This verse prohibits believers from marrying fornicators and idolaters, highlighting the importance of moral purity in marriage.

Verse 4

وَالَّذِينَ يَرْمُونَ الْمُحْصَنَاتِ ثُمَّ لَمْ يَأْتُوا بِأَرْبَعَةِ شُهَدَاءَ فَاجْلِدُوهُمْ ثَمَانِينَ جَلْدَةً وَلَا تَقْبَلُوا لَهُمْ شَهَادَةً أَبَدًا ۚ وَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْفَاسِقُونَ

Those who accuse chaste women ˹of adultery˺ and fail to produce four witnesses, give them eighty lashes ˹each˺. And do not ever accept any testimony from them—for they are indeed the rebellious—

And those who accuse chaste women and men of fornication, without bringing four just witnesses, then lash them with eighty lashes, and never accept their testimony, and those are the ones who have gone out of the obedience of Allah.

Those who falsely accuse chaste women of adultery must provide four witnesses or face eighty lashes and their testimony will not be accepted. They are considered rebellious in Allah's eyes.

Verse 5

إِلَّا الَّذِينَ تَابُوا مِنْ بَعْدِ ذَٰلِكَ وَأَصْلَحُوا فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

except those who repent afterwards and mend their ways, then surely Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

Except those who repent and regret and return from their accusation and correct their actions, then Allah will forgive their sins and have mercy on them, and accept their repentance.

This verse provides an exception: those who repent and amend their behavior after falsely accusing others can have their past sins forgiven by Allah.

Verse 6

وَالَّذِينَ يَرْمُونَ أَزْوَاجَهُمْ وَلَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُمْ شُهَدَاءُ إِلَّا أَنْفُسُهُمْ فَشَهَادَةُ أَحَدِهِمْ أَرْبَعُ شَهَادَاتٍ بِاللَّهِ ۙ إِنَّهُ لَمِنَ الصَّادِقِينَ

And those who accuse their wives ˹of adultery˺ but have no witness except themselves, the accuser must testify, swearing four times by Allah that he is telling the truth,

And those who accuse their wives of adultery, and have no witnesses to their accusation other than themselves, then each of them must testify before the judge four times saying: 'I bear witness by Allah that I am truthful in what I have accused her of adultery,' and adds in the fifth testimony a plea upon himself to deserve the curse of Allah if he is a liar in his statement.

A man who accuses his wife of adultery without witnesses can testify against her by swearing four times by Allah that he is truthful. He also adds a fifth oath invoking Allah's curse upon himself if he is lying.

Verse 7

وَالْخَامِسَةُ أَنَّ لَعْنَتَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْهِ إِنْ كَانَ مِنَ الْكَاذِبِينَ

and a fifth oath that Allah may condemn him if he is lying.

And those who accuse their wives of adultery, and have no witnesses to their accusation other than themselves, then each of them must testify before the judge four times saying: 'I bear witness by Allah that I am truthful in what I have accused her of adultery,' and adds in the fifth testimony a plea upon himself to deserve the curse of Allah if he is a liar in his statement.

Continuation of the previous verse, highlighting the fifth oath where the husband invokes Allah's curse upon himself if he is lying about the accusation.

Verse 8

وَيَدْرَأُ عَنْهَا الْعَذَابَ أَنْ تَشْهَدَ أَرْبَعَ شَهَادَاتٍ بِاللَّهِ ۙ إِنَّهُ لَمِنَ الْكَاذِبِينَ

For her to be spared the punishment, she must swear four times by Allah that he is telling a lie,

And by his testimony, the wife deserves the punishment for adultery, which is stoning to death, and this punishment is not averted from her unless she testifies in response to his testimony four testimonies by Allah that he is a liar in his accusation of adultery against her, and adds in the fifth testimony a plea upon herself to deserve the wrath of Allah, if her husband is truthful in his accusation against her, and in this case, they are separated.

To avoid punishment, the accused wife can swear four times by Allah that her husband is lying about the accusation. She adds a fifth oath invoking Allah’s wrath upon herself if her husband is truthful.

Verse 9

وَالْخَامِسَةَ أَنَّ غَضَبَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْهَا إِنْ كَانَ مِنَ الصَّادِقِينَ

and a fifth oath that Allah may be displeased with her if he is telling the truth.

And by his testimony, the wife deserves the punishment for adultery, which is stoning to death, and this punishment is not averted from her unless she testifies in response to his testimony four testimonies by Allah that he is a liar in his accusation of adultery against her, and adds in the fifth testimony a plea upon herself to deserve the wrath of Allah, if her husband is truthful in his accusation against her, and in this case, they are separated.

Continuation of the previous verse, highlighting the fifth oath where the wife invokes Allah's wrath upon herself if her husband is truthful about the accusation.

Verse 10

وَلَوْلَا فَضْلُ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَتُهُ وَأَنَّ اللَّهَ تَوَّابٌ حَكِيمٌ

˹You would have suffered,˺ had it not been for Allah’s grace and mercy upon you, and had Allah not been Accepting of Repentance, All-Wise.

And if it were not for the favor of Allah upon you and His mercy – O believers – with this legislation for husbands and wives, the liar among the liars would have been afflicted with what he called upon himself, and that Allah is Oft-Returning with forgiveness to those who repent from His servants, Wise in His legislation and planning.

This verse underscores Allah's grace and mercy, stating that without His guidance in these laws, people would face harsher consequences. It also highlights Allah's acceptance of repentance and His wisdom.

Verse 11

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ جَاءُوا بِالْإِفْكِ عُصْبَةٌ مِنْكُمْ ۚ لَا تَحْسَبُوهُ شَرًّا لَكُمْ ۖ بَلْ هُوَ خَيْرٌ لَكُمْ ۚ لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مِنْهُمْ مَا اكْتَسَبَ مِنَ الْإِثْمِ ۚ وَالَّذِي تَوَلَّىٰ كِبْرَهُ مِنْهُمْ لَهُ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌ

Indeed, those who came up with that ˹outrageous˺ slander are a group of you. Do not think this is bad for you. Rather, it is good for you. They will be punished, each according to their share of the sin. As for their mastermind, he will suffer a tremendous punishment.

Indeed, those who came with the worst falsehood, which is the accusation against the Mother of the Believers, Aisha (may Allah be pleased with her), of immorality, are a group belonging to you - O community of Muslims - do not consider their words evil for you, but rather, it is good for you, for what that included from the acquittal of the Mother of the Believers, her integrity and the glorification of her mention, the raising of ranks, the expiation of sins, and the purification of the believers. Every individual who spoke of the slander has a consequence for his action of sin, and the one who bore the majority of it, who is Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul, the chief of the hypocrites - may Allah curse him - has a great punishment in the Hereafter, which is eternity in the lowest depths of the Fire.

This verse addresses the slander against Aisha (RA). It clarifies that those involved were a group among the believers, and their actions, though seemingly negative, carried good by revealing the truth and purifying the community. Each participant is accountable for their role in the sin, with the mastermind facing severe punishment.

Verse 12

لَوْلَا إِذْ سَمِعْتُمُوهُ ظَنَّ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ وَالْمُؤْمِنَاتُ بِأَنْفُسِهِمْ خَيْرًا وَقَالُوا هَٰذَا إِفْكٌ مُبِينٌ

If only the believing men and women had thought well of one another, when you heard this ˹rumour˺, and said, “This is clearly ˹an outrageous˺ slander!”

Why did the believing men and the believing women not think good of each other when they heard that slander, which is that they are free from what they were accused of, and say: This is an obvious lie against Aisha (may Allah be pleased with her).

This verse questions why the believers did not think well of each other upon hearing the slander. It emphasizes that a good response would have been to recognize the rumor as an obvious falsehood.

Verse 13

لَوْلَا جَاءُوا عَلَيْهِ بِأَرْبَعَةِ شُهَدَاءَ ۚ فَإِذْ لَمْ يَأْتُوا بِالشُّهَدَاءِ فَأُولَٰئِكَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ هُمُ الْكَاذِبُونَ

Why did they not produce four witnesses? Now, since they have failed to produce witnesses, they are ˹truly˺ liars in the sight of Allah.

Why did the accusers not bring four upright witnesses for their statement? Since they did not do so, then it is they who are the liars in the sight of Allah.

This verse challenges the accusers to produce four witnesses, a requirement for such accusations. Their failure to do so proves their lies before Allah.

Verse 14

وَلَوْلَا فَضْلُ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَتُهُ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ لَمَسَّكُمْ فِي مَا أَفَضْتُمْ فِيهِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌ

Had it not been for Allah’s grace and mercy upon you in this world and the Hereafter, you would have certainly been touched with a tremendous punishment for what you plunged into—

And if it were not for the favor of Allah upon you and His mercy towards you, such that His goodness has encompassed you in your religion and your world so He did not hasten your punishment, and accepted the repentance of those who repented amongst you, then a great punishment would have afflicted you because of what you have involved yourselves in.

This verse highlights the mercy of Allah. It acknowledges that His grace and mercy have shielded the believers from the severe punishment they deserved for their involvement in the slander.

Verse 15

إِذْ تَلَقَّوْنَهُ بِأَلْسِنَتِكُمْ وَتَقُولُونَ بِأَفْوَاهِكُمْ مَا لَيْسَ لَكُمْ بِهِ عِلْمٌ وَتَحْسَبُونَهُ هَيِّنًا وَهُوَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ عَظِيمٌ

when you passed it from one tongue to the other, and said with your mouths what you had no knowledge of, taking it lightly while it is ˹extremely˺ serious in the sight of Allah.

When you receive the slander and pass it on with your mouths, which is a false statement, and you have no knowledge of it, both of which are forbidden: speaking falsehood, and speaking without knowledge, and you think that is something insignificant, but it is great in the sight of Allah. And in this is a clear rebuke against being lenient in spreading falsehood.

This verse condemns the act of spreading rumors without knowledge. Believers are reminded that sharing falsehoods, even when perceived as minor, is grave in the eyes of Allah.

Verse 16

وَلَوْلَا إِذْ سَمِعْتُمُوهُ قُلْتُمْ مَا يَكُونُ لَنَا أَنْ نَتَكَلَّمَ بِهَٰذَا سُبْحَانَكَ هَٰذَا بُهْتَانٌ عَظِيمٌ

If only you had said upon hearing it, “How can we speak about such a thing! Glory be to You ˹O Lord˺! This is a heinous slander!”

And why did you not say when you heard it: It is not permissible for us to speak this lie, exalting You - O Lord - from saying such a thing about the wife of Your Messenger Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him), for it is a great lie in sin and deserving of blame.

This verse describes the appropriate response when hearing the slander: recognizing it as a grave falsehood and exalting Allah, declaring that it is not permissible to speak such a thing.

Verse 17

يَعِظُكُمُ اللَّهُ أَنْ تَعُودُوا لِمِثْلِهِ أَبَدًا إِنْ كُنْتُمْ مُؤْمِنِينَ

Allah reminds you and forbids you from ever returning to such an act of false accusation, if you are believers in Him.

This verse is a strong prohibition against repeating false accusations. True believers are commanded to never again engage in such behavior.

Verse 18

This verse states that Allah clearly explains His commandments. Allah is All-Knowing and All-Wise in His decrees and laws.

Verse 19

Indeed, those who love to see indecency spread among the believers will suffer a painful punishment in this life and the Hereafter. Allah knows and you do not know.

Indeed, those who love that immorality should spread among the Muslims, from accusations of adultery or any bad speech, for them is a painful punishment in this world by implementing the prescribed punishment on them, and other worldly calamities, and for them in the Hereafter is the punishment of Fire if they do not repent, and Allah - alone - knows their lies, and knows what is best for His servants, and the consequences of matters, and you do not know that.

This verse condemns those who desire to spread immorality among believers. Such people will face severe punishment both in this life and the Hereafter. Allah's knowledge surpasses that of humans.

Verse 20

This verse emphasizes Allah's immense grace and mercy towards His believing servants. His favor and compassion prevent immediate punishments and guide them through His clear rulings.

Verse 21

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, do not follow the paths of Satan. And whoever follows the paths of Satan, then he enjoins him to do evil deeds and abominations. And were it not for the favor of Allah upon the believers and His mercy toward them, not one of them would have ever been pure from the filth of his sins. But Allah - by His favor - purifies whom He wills. And Allah is Hearing of your sayings, Knowing of your intentions and actions.

This verse warns believers against following Satan, whose path leads to immorality. It emphasizes that only through Allah's grace can one be purified from sin, and that Allah is All-Hearing and All-Knowing.

Verse 22

وَلَا يَأْتَلِ أُولُو الْفَضْلِ مِنْكُمْ وَالسَّعَةِ أَنْ يُؤْتُوا أُولِي الْقُرْبَىٰ وَالْمَسَاكِينَ وَالْمُهَاجِرِينَ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ ۖ وَلْيَعْفُوا وَلْيَصْفَحُوا ۗ أَلَا تُحِبُّونَ أَنْ يَغْفِرَ اللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۗ وَاللَّهُ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

And let not those of virtue and abundance among you swear not to give to their relatives and the needy and the emigrants, and to withhold from them the expenses; because of a sin they committed, but let them pardon and overlook their offenses. Do you not like that Allah should pardon you? Then pardon them. And Allah is Forgiving to His servants, Merciful to them. And in this is an urging to pardon and forgive, even if it is met with wrongdoing.

Those with wealth and virtue are encouraged to forgive their relatives and the needy, and to continue helping them, even if they have done wrong. It is a reminder that Allah is All-Forgiving and Merciful, and we should strive to be as well.

Verse 23

Indeed, those who accuse chaste, unwary, believing women of adultery, who have no thought of it in their hearts, are expelled from the mercy of Allah in this world and the Hereafter, and they will have a great punishment in Hellfire. And in this verse is evidence of the disbelief of one who curses or accuses a wife of the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) of evil.

This verse condemns those who falsely accuse chaste and innocent believing women of adultery. Such people are cursed and will face severe punishment in the Hereafter.

Verse 24

That punishment will be on the Day of Resurrection, when their tongues will testify against them of what they spoke, and their hands and feet will speak of what they did.

On the Day of Judgment, people's tongues, hands, and feet will testify against them, revealing their wrongdoings.

Verse 25

On that Day, Allah will pay them in full their due for their deeds with justice, and they will know in that great standing that Allah is the clear Truth, which is the truth, and His promise is truth, and His warning is truth, and everything from Him is truth, who does not wrong anyone by an atom’s weight.
