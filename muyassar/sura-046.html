Sura 046

Verse 1

حم

Ḥâ-Mĩm.

(Ha Meem) The discussion on the disconnected letters was previously mentioned at the beginning of Surat Al-Baqarah.

This verse begins with the letters 'Ha-Meem', the meaning of which is not definitively known, and is among the mutashabihat (unclear verses) of the Quran.

Verse 2

تَنْزِيلُ الْكِتَابِ مِنَ اللَّهِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَكِيمِ

The revelation of this Book is from Allah—the Almighty, All-Wise.

This Quran is a revelation from Allah, the All-Mighty, Who cannot be overcome, the All-Wise in His planning and creation.

The Quran is a revelation from Allah, the Almighty and All-Wise. This confirms the divine origin of the Quran and emphasizes Allah's power and wisdom.

Verse 3

مَا خَلَقْنَا السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ وَمَا بَيْنَهُمَا إِلَّا بِالْحَقِّ وَأَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى ۚ وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا عَمَّا أُنْذِرُوا مُعْرِضُونَ

We only created the heavens and the earth and everything in between for a purpose and an appointed term. Yet the disbelievers are turning away from what they have been warned about.

We did not create the heavens and the earth and what is between them except in truth, not in vain or futile. Rather, so that the servants may know the greatness of their Creator and worship Him alone, and know that He is able to return the servants after their death, and to establish truth and justice among themselves, and for a specified term with Him. And those who denied that Allah is the True God are turning away from what the Quran has warned them with, they do not take heed, nor do they reflect.

Allah created the heavens and the earth for a purpose, not in vain, and for a specific term. Yet, disbelievers turn away from these warnings, failing to reflect on the purpose of creation.

Verse 4

قُلْ أَرَأَيْتُمْ مَا تَدْعُونَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ أَرُونِي مَاذَا خَلَقُوا مِنَ الْأَرْضِ أَمْ لَهُمْ شِرْكٌ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ ۖ ائْتُونِي بِكِتَابٍ مِنْ قَبْلِ هَٰذَا أَوْ أَثَارَةٍ مِنْ عِلْمٍ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صَادِقِينَ

Ask ˹them, O Prophet˺, “Have you considered whatever ˹idols˺ you invoke besides Allah? Show me what they have created on earth! Or do they have a share in ˹the creation of˺ the heavens? Bring me a scripture ˹revealed˺ before this ˹Quran˺ or a shred of knowledge, if what you say is true.”

Say, [O Muhammad], to these disbelievers: “Have you seen the deities and idols that you worship besides Allah? Show me what they have created from the earth, or do they have a share with Allah in the creation of the heavens? Bring me a scripture from Allah before this Quran or a trace of knowledge, if you are truthful in what you claim.”

This verse challenges the disbelievers by asking them to show what their idols have created, or if they have a share in the creation. It questions the validity of worshipping anything besides Allah.

Verse 5

وَمَنْ أَضَلُّ مِمَّنْ يَدْعُو مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ مَنْ لَا يَسْتَجِيبُ لَهُ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ وَهُمْ عَنْ دُعَائِهِمْ غَافِلُونَ

And who could be more astray than those who call upon others besides Allah—˹others˺ that cannot respond to them until the Day of Judgment, and are ˹even˺ unaware of their calls?

And who is more astray than one who invokes besides Allah those who will not respond to him until the Day of Resurrection, and they, of their invocation, are heedless. For they are among the dead or stones, trees, and the like, and they are heedless of the invocation of those who worship them, incapable of benefiting or harming them.

This verse questions who is more astray than those who call upon others besides Allah, beings who cannot respond to them and are even unaware of their supplications. It highlights the futility of worshipping false deities.

Verse 6

وَإِذَا حُشِرَ النَّاسُ كَانُوا لَهُمْ أَعْدَاءً وَكَانُوا بِعِبَادَتِهِمْ كَافِرِينَ

And when ˹such˺ people will be gathered together, those ˹gods˺ will be their enemies and will disown their worship.

And when people are gathered on the Day of Resurrection for reckoning and recompense, the deities they used to invoke in the world will be their enemies, cursing them, disowning them, and denying any knowledge of their worship of them.

On the Day of Judgment, the false gods that people worshipped will turn against them, denying any association with their worship.

Verse 7

وَإِذَا تُتْلَىٰ عَلَيْهِمْ آيَاتُنَا بَيِّنَاتٍ قَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لِلْحَقِّ لَمَّا جَاءَهُمْ هَٰذَا سِحْرٌ مُبِينٌ

Whenever Our clear revelations are recited to them, the disbelievers say of the truth when it has come to them, “This is pure magic.”

And when Our verses are recited to these polytheists, clear and distinct, those who disbelieved say when the Quran came to them: “This is obvious magic.”

When the Quran is recited to the disbelievers, they reject it, claiming it is just 'magic'.

Verse 8

أَمْ يَقُولُونَ افْتَرَاهُ ۖ قُلْ إِنِ افْتَرَيْتُهُ فَلَا تَمْلِكُونَ لِي مِنَ اللَّهِ شَيْئًا ۖ هُوَ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا تُفِيضُونَ فِيهِ ۖ كَفَىٰ بِهِ شَهِيدًا بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَكُمْ ۖ وَهُوَ الْغَفُورُ الرَّحِيمُ

Or do they say, “He has fabricated this ˹Quran˺!”? Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “If I have done so, then there is nothing whatsoever you can do to save me from Allah. He knows best what ˹slurs˺ you indulge about it. Sufficient is He as a Witness between you and me. And He is the All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.”

Or do these polytheists say: “Muhammad has invented this Quran?” Say to them, [O Messenger]: “If I have invented it upon Allah, then you cannot avert from me anything of Allah's punishment, if He punishes me for that. He is most knowing than anyone else of what you say about this Quran. Sufficient is Allah as a witness between me and you, and He is the Forgiving for those who repent to Him, and the Merciful to His believing servants.”

The disbelievers accuse the Prophet of fabricating the Quran. The Prophet responds that if he had done so, Allah would punish him, and that Allah is the ultimate witness.

Verse 9

قُلْ مَا كُنْتُ بِدْعًا مِنَ الرُّسُلِ وَمَا أَدْرِي مَا يُفْعَلُ بِي وَلَا بِكُمْ ۖ إِنْ أَتَّبِعُ إِلَّا مَا يُوحَىٰ إِلَيَّ وَمَا أَنَا إِلَّا نَذِيرٌ مُبِينٌ

Say, “I am not the first messenger ever sent, nor do I know what will happen to me or you. I only follow what is revealed to me. And I am only sent with a clear warning.”

Say, [O Messenger], to the polytheists of your people: “I am not the first messenger of Allah to His creation, and I do not know what Allah will do with me or with you in this world. I only follow the revelation of Allah that He reveals to me, and I am but a clear warner.”

The Prophet clarifies he is not the first messenger, nor does he know the future. He only follows what is revealed to him and is a clear warner.

Verse 10

قُلْ أَرَأَيْتُمْ إِنْ كَانَ مِنْ عِنْدِ اللَّهِ وَكَفَرْتُمْ بِهِ وَشَهِدَ شَاهِدٌ مِنْ بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ عَلَىٰ مِثْلِهِ فَآمَنَ وَاسْتَكْبَرْتُمْ ۖ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِي الْقَوْمَ الظَّالِمِينَ

Ask ˹them, O Prophet˺, “Consider if this ˹Quran˺ is ˹truly˺ from Allah and you deny it, and a witness from the Children of Israel attests to it and then believes, whereas you act arrogantly. Surely Allah does not guide the wrongdoing people.”

Say, [O Messenger], to the polytheists of your people: “Tell me, if this Quran is from Allah and you disbelieve in it, and a witness from the Children of Israel, like Abdullah bin Salam, testified to a like of this Quran - which is what is in the Torah from the confirmation of the prophethood of Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, and he believed and acted upon what came in the Quran, and you denied that out of arrogance - is this not the greatest injustice and the most severe disbelief? Verily, Allah does not guide to Islam and the attainment of truth the people who have wronged themselves by their disbelief in Allah.”

The disbelievers are challenged to consider the truth of the Quran, especially since witnesses from the Children of Israel (like Abdullah bin Salam) believed in it. Their arrogance prevents them from guidance.

Verse 11

وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَوْ كَانَ خَيْرًا مَا سَبَقُونَا إِلَيْهِ ۚ وَإِذْ لَمْ يَهْتَدُوا بِهِ فَسَيَقُولُونَ هَٰذَا إِفْكٌ قَدِيمٌ

The disbelievers say of the believers, “Had it been ˹something˺ good, they would not have beaten us to it.” Now since they reject its guidance, they will say, “˹This is˺ an ancient fabrication!”

And those who denied the prophethood of Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, said to those who believed in him: “If your belief in Muhammad and what he has brought were good, you would not have preceded us to believing in him.” And since they were not guided by the Quran and did not benefit from the truth in it, they will say: “This is a lie, tales from the ancients.”

The disbelievers mock the believers, claiming that if the message were good, they would have accepted it first. They dismiss it as 'ancient fabrications'.

Verse 12

وَمِنْ قَبْلِهِ كِتَابُ مُوسَىٰ إِمَامًا وَرَحْمَةً ۚ وَهَٰذَا كِتَابٌ مُصَدِّقٌ لِسَانًا عَرَبِيًّا لِيُنْذِرَ الَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا وَبُشْرَىٰ لِلْمُحْسِنِينَ

And before this ˹Quran˺ the Book of Moses was ˹revealed as˺ a guide and mercy. And this Book is a confirmation, in the Arabic tongue, to warn those who do wrong, and as good news to those who do good.

And before this Quran, We sent down the Torah as a guide for the Children of Israel to follow, and a mercy for those who believed in it and acted upon what was in it. And this Quran confirms what came before it of books. We sent it down in the Arabic language, to warn those who have wronged themselves with disbelief and disobedience, and as good tidings for those who obeyed Allah, and did good in their faith and obedience in this world.

Like the Torah before it, the Quran is a guide and mercy. It is in Arabic to warn wrongdoers and give good news to those who do good.

Verse 13

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ قَالُوا رَبُّنَا اللَّهُ ثُمَّ اسْتَقَامُوا فَلَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ

Surely those who say, “Our Lord is Allah,” and then remain steadfast—there will be no fear for them, nor will they grieve.

Indeed, those who say, “Our Lord is Allah,” and then remain steadfast in their faith in Him, then there will be no fear upon them from the terror of the Day of Resurrection and its horrors, nor will they grieve over what they left behind after their death from worldly pleasures.

Those who affirm that Allah is their Lord and remain steadfast will have no fear or grief.

Verse 14

أُولَٰئِكَ أَصْحَابُ الْجَنَّةِ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا جَزَاءً بِمَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

It is they who will be the residents of Paradise, staying there forever, as a reward for what they used to do.

Those are the people of Paradise, abiding therein forever by the mercy of Allah upon them and because of the righteous deeds they presented in their world.

These steadfast believers are the residents of Paradise, where they will remain forever as a reward for their righteous deeds.

Verse 15

وَوَصَّيْنَا الْإِنْسَانَ بِوَالِدَيْهِ إِحْسَانًا ۖ حَمَلَتْهُ أُمُّهُ كُرْهًا وَوَضَعَتْهُ كُرْهًا ۖ وَحَمْلُهُ وَفِصَالُهُ ثَلَاثُونَ شَهْرًا ۚ حَتَّىٰ إِذَا بَلَغَ أَشُدَّهُ وَبَلَغَ أَرْبَعِينَ سَنَةً قَالَ رَبِّ أَوْزِعْنِي أَنْ أَشْكُرَ نِعْمَتَكَ الَّتِي أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيَّ وَعَلَىٰ وَالِدَيَّ وَأَنْ أَعْمَلَ صَالِحًا تَرْضَاهُ وَأَصْلِحْ لِي فِي ذُرِّيَّتِي ۖ إِنِّي تُبْتُ إِلَيْكَ وَإِنِّي مِنَ الْمُسْلِمِينَ

We have commanded people to honour their parents. Their mothers bore them in hardship and delivered them in hardship. Their ˹period of˺ bearing and weaning is thirty months. In time, when the child reaches their prime at the age of forty, they pray, “My Lord! Inspire me to ˹always˺ be thankful for Your favours which You blessed me and my parents with, and to do good deeds that please You. And instil righteousness in my offspring. I truly repent to You, and I truly submit ˹to Your Will˺.”

And We have enjoined upon man to be good in his companionship with his parents, being dutiful to them in their lives and after their death. For his mother carried him as a fetus in her womb with hardship and fatigue, and gave birth to him with hardship and fatigue as well, and the duration of his carrying and weaning is thirty months. And in mentioning these hardships that the mother endures without the father, is evidence that her right upon her child is greater than the right of the father. Until when this person reaches the end of his physical and mental strength, and reaches forty years, he calls upon his Lord, saying: My Lord, inspire me to be grateful for Your favor which You have bestowed upon me and upon my parents, and enable me to do righteous deeds that please You, and make righteous for me my offspring. Indeed, I have repented to You from my sins, and indeed, I am of those submissive to You in obedience, and submitting to Your command and Your prohibition, and yielding to Your judgment.

This verse highlights the importance of honoring parents, especially mothers, acknowledging the hardships of childbirth and child-rearing. It also emphasizes gratitude to Allah and striving for good deeds, including praying for righteous offspring and submitting to Allah's will, especially when one reaches maturity.

Verse 16

أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ نَتَقَبَّلُ عَنْهُمْ أَحْسَنَ مَا عَمِلُوا وَنَتَجَاوَزُ عَنْ سَيِّئَاتِهِمْ فِي أَصْحَابِ الْجَنَّةِ ۖ وَعْدَ الصِّدْقِ الَّذِي كَانُوا يُوعَدُونَ

It is from these ˹people˺ that We will accept the good they did, and overlook their misdeeds—along with the residents of Paradise, ˹in fulfilment of˺ the true promise they have been given.

Those are the ones from whom We will accept the best of what they did of righteous deeds, and We will overlook their misdeeds, among the companions of Paradise. This is the promise which We have promised them, a true promise which is beyond doubt.

This verse describes those who are obedient and righteous. Allah will accept their good deeds and overlook their shortcomings and they will reside in Paradise fulfilling Allah's true promise.

Verse 17

وَالَّذِي قَالَ لِوَالِدَيْهِ أُفٍّ لَكُمَا أَتَعِدَانِنِي أَنْ أُخْرَجَ وَقَدْ خَلَتِ الْقُرُونُ مِنْ قَبْلِي وَهُمَا يَسْتَغِيثَانِ اللَّهَ وَيْلَكَ آمِنْ إِنَّ وَعْدَ اللَّهِ حَقٌّ فَيَقُولُ مَا هَٰذَا إِلَّا أَسَاطِيرُ الْأَوَّلِينَ

But some scold their parents, “Enough with you! Are you warning me that I will be brought forth ˹from the grave˺, while many generations had already perished before me ˹for good˺?” The parents cry to Allah for help, ˹and warn their child,˺ “Pity you. Have faith! Surely Allah’s promise is true.” But the deniers insist, “This is nothing but ancient fables.”

And the one who said to his parents when they called him to believe in Allah and acknowledge the Resurrection: 'Woe to you! Do you promise me that I will be brought forth from my grave alive, when generations of nations before me have passed, and they perished and none of them were resurrected?' And his parents ask Allah for his guidance, saying to him: 'Woe to you! Believe and affirm and do good deeds, for the promise of Allah regarding the Resurrection is true, without doubt.' But he says to them: 'What you are saying is nothing but what the ancients have written of falsehoods, taken from their books.'

This verse describes a disbeliever who scoffs at the idea of resurrection, rejecting the warnings of his parents to believe in Allah, referring to the idea of life after death as ancient fables, showing arrogance and disbelief.

Verse 18

أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ حَقَّ عَلَيْهِمُ الْقَوْلُ فِي أُمَمٍ قَدْ خَلَتْ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ مِنَ الْجِنِّ وَالْإِنْسِ ۖ إِنَّهُمْ كَانُوا خَاسِرِينَ

These are the ones against whom the fate of earlier communities of jinn and humans has been justified, ˹for˺ they were truly losers.

Those are the ones who, such is their description, upon whom Allah's punishment is due, and His penalty and His wrath have befallen them, among nations that passed before them of jinn and mankind in disbelief and denial. Indeed, they were losers by selling guidance for misguidance, and bliss for torment.

This verse explains the fate of those who reject faith. Like previous disbelieving communities of jinn and humans, they are destined for loss because they chose disbelief over guidance, resulting in punishment.

Verse 19

وَلِكُلٍّ دَرَجَاتٌ مِمَّا عَمِلُوا ۖ وَلِيُوَفِّيَهُمْ أَعْمَالَهُمْ وَهُمْ لَا يُظْلَمُونَ

Each ˹of the two groups˺ will be ranked according to what they have done so He may fully reward all. And none will be wronged.

And for every group, of the people of good and the people of evil, are stations with Allah on the Day of Resurrection, based on their deeds which they did in this world, each according to his rank. And Allah will fully recompense them for their deeds, and they will not be wronged by an increase in their misdeeds, nor by a decrease in their good deeds.

This verse establishes that on the Day of Judgment, each person will be assigned a rank based on their deeds. Allah will reward everyone fully and justly, without any unfairness.

Verse 20

وَيَوْمَ يُعْرَضُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا عَلَى النَّارِ أَذْهَبْتُمْ طَيِّبَاتِكُمْ فِي حَيَاتِكُمُ الدُّنْيَا وَاسْتَمْتَعْتُمْ بِهَا فَالْيَوْمَ تُجْزَوْنَ عَذَابَ الْهُونِ بِمَا كُنْتُمْ تَسْتَكْبِرُونَ فِي الْأَرْضِ بِغَيْرِ الْحَقِّ وَبِمَا كُنْتُمْ تَفْسُقُونَ

˹Watch for˺ the Day ˹when˺ the disbelievers will be exposed to the Fire. ˹They will be told,˺ “You ˹already˺ exhausted your ˹share of˺ pleasures during your worldly life, and ˹fully˺ enjoyed them. So Today you will be rewarded with the torment of disgrace for your arrogance throughout the land with no right, and for your rebelliousness.”

And the Day when those who disbelieved will be presented to the Fire for punishment, it will be said to them in rebuke: 'You exhausted your good things in your worldly life and enjoyed them, so today - O disbelievers - you will be recompensed with the punishment of disgrace and humiliation in the Fire, because of what you used to arrogantly behave in the earth without right, and because of what you used to disobey Allah.'

This verse describes the fate of disbelievers on the Day of Judgment. Having indulged in worldly pleasures and acted arrogantly, they will be punished with humiliation in the Fire.

Verse 21

وَاذْكُرْ أَخَا عَادٍ إِذْ أَنْذَرَ قَوْمَهُ بِالْأَحْقَافِ وَقَدْ خَلَتِ النُّذُرُ مِنْ بَيْنِ يَدَيْهِ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِ أَلَّا تَعْبُدُوا إِلَّا اللَّهَ إِنِّي أَخَافُ عَلَيْكُمْ عَذَابَ يَوْمٍ عَظِيمٍ

And remember the brother of ’Ȃd, when he warned his people, who inhabited the sand-hills—there were certainly warners before and after him—˹saying,˺ “Worship none but Allah. I truly fear for you the torment of a tremendous day.”

And remember, O Messenger, the prophet of Allah, Hud, the brother of 'Aad in lineage, not in religion, when he warned his people that the punishment of Allah would befall them, while they were in their homes known as "Al-Ahqaf", which are the numerous sands south of the Arabian Peninsula. And the messengers had passed with warnings to their people before Hud and after him: that you should not associate anything with Allah in your worship of Him. Indeed, I fear for you the punishment of Allah on a day whose terror will be great, which is the Day of Resurrection.

This verse recounts Prophet Hud's warning to his people, the 'Aad, who lived in sand-hills. He urged them to worship Allah alone, cautioning them about a severe punishment to come.

Verse 22

قَالُوا أَجِئْتَنَا لِتَأْفِكَنَا عَنْ آلِهَتِنَا فَأْتِنَا بِمَا تَعِدُنَا إِنْ كُنْتَ مِنَ الصَّادِقِينَ

They argued, “Have you come to turn us away from our gods? Bring us then whatever you threaten us with, if what you say is true.”

They said, "Have you come to us with your call to turn us away from worshiping our gods? Then bring us what you threaten us with of punishment, if you are of the truthful in your words and promise."

The people of 'Aad challenged Prophet Hud, asking if he intended to turn them from their gods. They demanded he bring the punishment he threatened if his message was true.

Verse 23

قَالَ إِنَّمَا الْعِلْمُ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ وَأُبَلِّغُكُمْ مَا أُرْسِلْتُ بِهِ وَلَٰكِنِّي أَرَاكُمْ قَوْمًا تَجْهَلُونَ

He responded, “The knowledge ˹of its time˺ is only with Allah. I only convey to you what I have been sent with. But I can see that you are a people acting ignorantly.”

Hud, peace be upon him, said, "Indeed, the knowledge of when what you are promised of punishment will come is with Allah, and I am only a messenger of Allah to you, conveying to you what He sent me with. But I see you a people ignorant in your hastening of the punishment and your audacity against Allah."

Prophet Hud clarified that the timing of the punishment was known only to Allah. His role was to deliver Allah's message, and he observed their ignorance in demanding the punishment.

Verse 24

فَلَمَّا رَأَوْهُ عَارِضًا مُسْتَقْبِلَ أَوْدِيَتِهِمْ قَالُوا هَٰذَا عَارِضٌ مُمْطِرُنَا ۚ بَلْ هُوَ مَا اسْتَعْجَلْتُمْ بِهِ ۖ رِيحٌ فِيهَا عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ

Then when they saw the torment as a ˹dense˺ cloud approaching their valleys, they said ˹happily˺, “This is a cloud bringing us rain.” ˹But Hûd replied,˺ “No, it is what you sought to hasten: a ˹fierce˺ wind carrying a painful punishment!”

Then when they saw the punishment they had hastened, as a cloud in the sky heading towards their valleys, they said, "This is a cloud raining upon us." Hud, peace be upon him, said to them, "It is not a cloud of rain and mercy as you thought, but rather it is a cloud of the punishment you hastened. It is a wind in which is a painful, agonizing punishment."

When the people of 'Aad saw a cloud approaching, they believed it brought rain. However, Prophet Hud revealed it was the punishment they had sought: a destructive wind.

Verse 25

تُدَمِّرُ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ بِأَمْرِ رَبِّهَا فَأَصْبَحُوا لَا يُرَىٰ إِلَّا مَسَاكِنُهُمْ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ نَجْزِي الْقَوْمَ الْمُجْرِمِينَ

It destroyed everything by the command of its Lord, leaving nothing visible except their ruins. This is how We reward the wicked people.

It will destroy everything it passes by, which has been sent to be destroyed by the command and will of its Lord. So they became, and nothing could be seen in their lands except their dwellings that they used to inhabit. Like this is the recompense We give to the criminal people, because of their crime and transgression.

The wind destroyed everything it encountered by Allah's command, leaving only ruins. This is how Allah punishes those who do evil.

Verse 26

وَلَقَدْ مَكَّنَّاهُمْ فِيمَا إِنْ مَكَّنَّاكُمْ فِيهِ وَجَعَلْنَا لَهُمْ سَمْعًا وَأَبْصَارًا وَأَفْئِدَةً فَمَا أَغْنَىٰ عَنْهُمْ سَمْعُهُمْ وَلَا أَبْصَارُهُمْ وَلَا أَفْئِدَتُهُمْ مِنْ شَيْءٍ إِذْ كَانُوا يَجْحَدُونَ بِآيَاتِ اللَّهِ وَحَاقَ بِهِمْ مَا كَانُوا بِهِ يَسْتَهْزِئُونَ

Indeed, We had established them in a way We have not established you ˹Meccans˺. And We gave them hearing, sight, and intellect. But neither their hearing, sight, nor intellect were of any benefit to them whatsoever, since they persisted in denying Allah’s signs. And ˹so˺ they were overwhelmed by what they used to ridicule.

And We had certainly empowered 'Aad in the world in a way that We did not empower you, O people of Quraish who disbelieve. And We gave them hearing with which they hear, and sight with which they see, and hearts with which they understand. But they used them in what displeased Allah with them, so they were of no avail when they were denying the proofs of Allah. And there befell them of the punishment what they mocked and hastened. This is a threat from Allah, may His Majesty be exalted, and a warning to the disbelievers.

Allah had given the people of 'Aad power and faculties that the people of Mecca did not have. However, their senses did not benefit them, as they rejected the signs of Allah, bringing the punishment they used to mock.

Verse 27

وَلَقَدْ أَهْلَكْنَا مَا حَوْلَكُمْ مِنَ الْقُرَىٰ وَصَرَّفْنَا الْآيَاتِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَرْجِعُونَ

We certainly destroyed the societies around you after having varied the signs so perhaps they would return ˹to the Right Path˺.

And We have already destroyed what was around you, O people of "Mecca", of the towns, like 'Aad and Thamud, and We made them fallen on their foundations. And We have clarified for them types of proofs and indications, so that they might return from what they were upon of disbelief in Allah and His signs.

Allah destroyed previous societies, clarifying his signs hoping they would turn back to the right path.

Verse 28

فَلَوْلَا نَصَرَهُمُ الَّذِينَ اتَّخَذُوا مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ قُرْبَانًا آلِهَةً ۖ بَلْ ضَلُّوا عَنْهُمْ ۚ وَذَٰلِكَ إِفْكُهُمْ وَمَا كَانُوا يَفْتَرُونَ

Why then did those ˹idols˺ they took as gods—hoping to get closer ˹to Him˺—not come to their aid? Instead, they failed them. That is ˹the result of˺ their lies and their fabrications.

Then why did not those whom We destroyed of the past nations, their gods whom they took for worship as a means of drawing near to their Lord, help them, so that they could intercede for them with Him? Rather, their gods disappeared from them, so they did not answer them, nor did they defend them. And that was their lie and what they were fabricating in taking them as gods.

The false gods they worshiped for closeness to Allah were of no help. They failed the people who worshiped them, proving they were just fabrications and lies.

Verse 29

وَإِذْ صَرَفْنَا إِلَيْكَ نَفَرًا مِنَ الْجِنِّ يَسْتَمِعُونَ الْقُرْآنَ فَلَمَّا حَضَرُوهُ قَالُوا أَنْصِتُوا ۖ فَلَمَّا قُضِيَ وَلَّوْا إِلَىٰ قَوْمِهِمْ مُنْذِرِينَ

˹Remember, O Prophet,˺ when We sent a group of jinn your way to listen to the Quran. Then, upon hearing it, they said ˹to one another˺, “Listen quietly!” Then when it was over, they returned to their fellow jinn as warners.

And remember, O Messenger, when We sent to you a group of jinn listening to the Quran from you. And when they were present, while the Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) was reciting, some of them said to others: Be silent, so we can listen to the Quran. And when the Messenger finished reciting the Quran, and they had understood it and it had affected them, they returned to their people as warners, cautioning them about the punishment of Allah if they did not believe in Him.

This verse describes an event where a group of jinn were sent to listen to the Quran. Upon hearing it, they urged each other to be silent and listen attentively, and after the recitation they went back to their people as warners.

Verse 30

قَالُوا يَا قَوْمَنَا إِنَّا سَمِعْنَا كِتَابًا أُنْزِلَ مِنْ بَعْدِ مُوسَىٰ مُصَدِّقًا لِمَا بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ يَهْدِي إِلَى الْحَقِّ وَإِلَىٰ طَرِيقٍ مُسْتَقِيمٍ

They declared, “O our fellow jinn! We have truly heard a scripture revealed after Moses, confirming what came before it. It guides to the truth and the Straight Way.

They said, “O our people, we have heard a Book revealed after Moses, confirming what came before it of the Books of Allah which He revealed to His messengers, guiding to the truth and righteousness, and to a correct straight path.

The jinn acknowledged the Quran as a divine scripture revealed after Moses, confirming previous scriptures, and guiding to truth and the straight path.

Verse 31

يَا قَوْمَنَا أَجِيبُوا دَاعِيَ اللَّهِ وَآمِنُوا بِهِ يَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ مِنْ ذُنُوبِكُمْ وَيُجِرْكُمْ مِنْ عَذَابٍ أَلِيمٍ

O our fellow jinn! Respond to the caller of Allah and believe in him, He will forgive your sins and protect you from a painful punishment.

O our people, respond to the Messenger of Allah, Muhammad, to what he calls you to, and believe in him and act upon what he has brought to you, Allah will forgive you some of your sins and save you from a painful, grievous torment.

The jinn called on their people to respond to the message of the Quran, believe in the messenger Muhammad, and warned that such belief would lead to forgiveness of sins and protection from punishment.

Verse 32

وَمَنْ لَا يُجِبْ دَاعِيَ اللَّهِ فَلَيْسَ بِمُعْجِزٍ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَيْسَ لَهُ مِنْ دُونِهِ أَوْلِيَاءُ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ فِي ضَلَالٍ مُبِينٍ

And whoever does not respond to the caller of Allah will have no escape on earth, nor will they have any protectors against Him. It is they who are clearly astray.”

And whoever does not respond to the Messenger of Allah to what he called to, then he cannot frustrate Allah on earth if He intends punishment for him, and he will have no protectors besides Allah to prevent him from His punishment. Those are in clear error.

This verse clarifies that those who reject the call of the messenger cannot escape Allah's punishment, and will have no protectors besides Him. Their path is described as clear misguidance.

Verse 33

أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا أَنَّ اللَّهَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ وَلَمْ يَعْيَ بِخَلْقِهِنَّ بِقَادِرٍ عَلَىٰ أَنْ يُحْيِيَ الْمَوْتَىٰ ۚ بَلَىٰ إِنَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

Do they not realize that Allah, Who created the heavens and the earth and did not tire in creating them, is able to give life to the dead? Yes ˹indeed˺! He is certainly Most Capable of everything.

Have they not considered and known that Allah, who created the heavens and the earth without any prior example, and was not unable to create them, is able to give life to the dead whom He created initially? Yes, that is an easy matter for Allah the Most High, who nothing can hinder, indeed He has power over all things.

The verse emphasizes Allah's ability to resurrect the dead by highlighting that He is the one who created the heavens and earth without any difficulty, making it easy to understand his power over all things including resurrection.

Verse 34

وَيَوْمَ يُعْرَضُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا عَلَى النَّارِ أَلَيْسَ هَٰذَا بِالْحَقِّ ۖ قَالُوا بَلَىٰ وَرَبِّنَا ۚ قَالَ فَذُوقُوا الْعَذَابَ بِمَا كُنْتُمْ تَكْفُرُونَ

And on the Day the disbelievers will be exposed to the Fire, ˹they will be asked,˺ “Is this ˹Hereafter˺ not the truth?” They will cry, “Absolutely, by our Lord!” It will be said, “Then taste the punishment for your disbelief.”

And on the Day of Resurrection, those who disbelieved will be presented to the fire of Hell for punishment, and it will be said to them: 'Is this punishment not the truth?' They will respond, saying, 'Yes, and by our Lord, it is the truth.' Then it will be said to them, 'Then taste the punishment for what you used to deny, the torment of the fire, and disavow it in the world.'

This verse describes the disbelievers being confronted with the truth of the Hereafter and asked if they recognize it. They will affirm the truth, acknowledging their disbelief and receiving their punishment accordingly.

Verse 35

فَاصْبِرْ كَمَا صَبَرَ أُولُو الْعَزْمِ مِنَ الرُّسُلِ وَلَا تَسْتَعْجِلْ لَهُمْ ۚ كَأَنَّهُمْ يَوْمَ يَرَوْنَ مَا يُوعَدُونَ لَمْ يَلْبَثُوا إِلَّا سَاعَةً مِنْ نَهَارٍ ۚ بَلَاغٌ ۚ فَهَلْ يُهْلَكُ إِلَّا الْقَوْمُ الْفَاسِقُونَ

So endure patiently, as did the Messengers of Firm Resolve. And do not ˹seek to˺ hasten ˹the torment˺ for the deniers. On the Day they see what they have been threatened with, it will be as if they had only stayed ˹in this world˺ for an hour of a day. ˹This is˺ a ˹sufficient˺ warning! Then, will anyone be destroyed except the rebellious people?

So be patient, O Messenger, with what befalls you of harm from your denying people, as were patient the resolute messengers before you - and they are, as is well-known: Noah, Abraham, Moses, and Jesus, and you are one of them - and do not be impatient for your people's punishment. For when it occurs and they see it, it will be as if they had not remained in the world except for an hour of the day. This is a clear communication for them and others. And none will be destroyed by the punishment of Allah except the people who transgress His command and obedience.

This verse encourages patience for the Messenger Muhammad, similar to the resolute prophets before him. It also warns disbelievers about their punishment, which, when they see it, will cause them to realize that their worldly stay had been very short. Only the rebellious will be destroyed.
