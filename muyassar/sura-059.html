Sura 059

Verse 1

سَبَّحَ لِلَّهِ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

Whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth glorifies Allah. For He is the Almighty, All-Wise.

Allah is exalted above all that does not befit Him. All that is in the heavens and all that is on the earth glorify Him, and He is the Almighty, who cannot be overcome, the Wise in His decree, management, creation, and legislation, placing matters in their proper places.

Everything in the heavens and earth glorifies Allah. He is the Almighty, possessing ultimate power and wisdom in all His actions and decrees.

Verse 2

هُوَ الَّذِي أَخْرَجَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا مِنْ أَهْلِ الْكِتَابِ مِنْ دِيَارِهِمْ لِأَوَّلِ الْحَشْرِ ۚ مَا ظَنَنْتُمْ أَنْ يَخْرُجُوا ۖ وَظَنُّوا أَنَّهُمْ مَانِعَتُهُمْ حُصُونُهُمْ مِنَ اللَّهِ فَأَتَاهُمُ اللَّهُ مِنْ حَيْثُ لَمْ يَحْتَسِبُوا ۖ وَقَذَفَ فِي قُلُوبِهِمُ الرُّعْبَ ۚ يُخْرِبُونَ بُيُوتَهُمْ بِأَيْدِيهِمْ وَأَيْدِي الْمُؤْمِنِينَ فَاعْتَبِرُوا يَا أُولِي الْأَبْصَارِ

He is the One Who expelled the disbelievers of the People of the Book from their homes for ˹their˺ first banishment ˹ever˺. You never thought they would go. And they thought their strongholds would put them out of Allah’s reach. But ˹the decree of˺ Allah came upon them from where they never expected. And He cast horror into their hearts so they destroyed their houses with their own hands and the hands of the believers. So take a lesson ˹from this˺, O people of insight!

He - Glory be to Him - is the One who expelled those who denied the prophethood of Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, from the People of the Book, who were the Jews of Banu Nadir, from their dwellings that were near the Muslims around "Medina." That was the first expulsion of them from "the Arabian Peninsula" to "the Levant." You did not think, O Muslims, that they would be expelled from their homes in such humiliation and disgrace because of their strength and power, and the Jews thought that their fortresses would repel the wrath of Allah and that no one could overcome them. But Allah came to them from where they had not expected, and He cast fear and great panic into their hearts. They destroyed their homes with their own hands and the hands of the believers. So take heed, O people of sound insight and sound minds, from what happened to them.

Allah expelled the disbelieving People of the Book from their homes. They felt secure in their strongholds, but Allah's decree came unexpectedly, casting fear into their hearts, and they destroyed their homes. This serves as a lesson for those with insight.

Verse 3

وَلَوْلَا أَنْ كَتَبَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِمُ الْجَلَاءَ لَعَذَّبَهُمْ فِي الدُّنْيَا ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِي الْآخِرَةِ عَذَابُ النَّارِ

Had Allah not decreed exile for them, He would have certainly punished them in this world. And in the Hereafter they will suffer the punishment of the Fire.

And had it not been that Allah had decreed and destined their expulsion from their homes, He would have punished them in this world with killing and captivity, and for them in the Hereafter is the punishment of the Fire.

If Allah had not decreed exile for them, He would have punished them in this world and in the Hereafter they will suffer the punishment of the Fire.

Verse 4

ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّهُمْ شَاقُّوا اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ ۖ وَمَنْ يُشَاقِّ اللَّهَ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ شَدِيدُ الْعِقَابِ

This is because they defied Allah and His Messenger. And whoever defies Allah, then Allah is truly severe in punishment.

That - which befell the Jews in this world and what awaits them in the Hereafter - is because they disobeyed the command of Allah and the command of His Messenger in the most severe manner, and they fought them and sought to disobey them, and whoever disobeys Allah and His Messenger, then Allah is severe in punishment for him.

This verse explains that the consequences faced by those who defy Allah and His Messenger are severe. Disobedience leads to Allah's punishment.

Verse 5

مَا قَطَعْتُمْ مِنْ لِينَةٍ أَوْ تَرَكْتُمُوهَا قَائِمَةً عَلَىٰ أُصُولِهَا فَبِإِذْنِ اللَّهِ وَلِيُخْزِيَ الْفَاسِقِينَ

Whatever palm trees you ˹believers˺ cut down or left standing intact, it was ˹all˺ by Allah’s Will, so that He might disgrace the rebellious.

Whatever palm tree you - O believers - cut down or left standing on its trunk, without harming it, it was by the permission and command of Allah; and in order to humiliate those who rebel against His obedience, who disobey His command and prohibition, as He empowered you to cut down their palm trees and burn them.

The cutting down or leaving of palm trees was by Allah's will, to humiliate those who rebel against Him. It shows that all actions are under Allah's control and serve His purpose.

Verse 6

وَمَا أَفَاءَ اللَّهُ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِ مِنْهُمْ فَمَا أَوْجَفْتُمْ عَلَيْهِ مِنْ خَيْلٍ وَلَا رِكَابٍ وَلَٰكِنَّ اللَّهَ يُسَلِّطُ رُسُلَهُ عَلَىٰ مَنْ يَشَاءُ ۚ وَاللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

As for the gains Allah has turned over to His Messenger from them—you did not ˹even˺ spur on any horse or camel for such gains. But Allah gives authority to His messengers over whoever He wills. For Allah is Most Capable of everything.

And what Allah bestowed on His Messenger from the wealth of the Jews of Banu Nadir, you did not ride horses or camels to obtain it, but Allah gives authority to His messengers over whom He wills of His enemies, so they surrender to them without fighting. And the fay' (booty) is what is taken from the wealth of the disbelievers rightfully without fighting. And Allah is Powerful over all things; nothing can weaken Him.

The gains acquired without fighting were bestowed by Allah to His Messenger. Allah grants authority to His messengers over whom He wills and has power over all things.

Verse 7

مَا أَفَاءَ اللَّهُ عَلَىٰ رَسُولِهِ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْقُرَىٰ فَلِلَّهِ وَلِلرَّسُولِ وَلِذِي الْقُرْبَىٰ وَالْيَتَامَىٰ وَالْمَسَاكِينِ وَابْنِ السَّبِيلِ كَيْ لَا يَكُونَ دُولَةً بَيْنَ الْأَغْنِيَاءِ مِنْكُمْ ۚ وَمَا آتَاكُمُ الرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوهُ وَمَا نَهَاكُمْ عَنْهُ فَانْتَهُوا ۚ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ ۖ إِنَّ اللَّهَ شَدِيدُ الْعِقَابِ

As for gains granted by Allah to His Messenger from the people of ˹other˺ lands, they are for Allah and the Messenger, his close relatives, orphans, the poor, and ˹needy˺ travellers so that wealth may not merely circulate among your rich. Whatever the Messenger gives you, take it. And whatever he forbids you from, leave it. And fear Allah. Surely Allah is severe in punishment.

What Allah has bestowed upon His Messenger from the wealth of the polytheists of the people of the villages without riding horses or camels, is for Allah and His Messenger, to be spent in the public interests of the Muslims, and for the relatives of the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and the orphans, who are the poor children whose fathers have died, and the needy, who are those in need and poverty, and the wayfarer, who is the traveling stranger whose expenses have run out and whose wealth has been cut off; that is so that the money will not be circulating solely amongst the rich, and the poor and needy will be deprived of it. And whatever the Messenger gives you of wealth, or legislates for you of the law, then take it, and whatever he forbids you from taking or doing, then abstain from it, and fear Allah by complying with His commands and abandoning His prohibitions. Indeed, Allah is severe in punishment for those who disobey Him and oppose His command and prohibition. And this verse is a principle in the necessity of acting upon the Sunnah: in words, deeds, or approval.

The gains from other lands are for Allah, His Messenger, his relatives, orphans, the poor, and travelers. This distribution ensures wealth doesn't remain solely with the rich. It also emphasizes the importance of following the Messenger's teachings.

Verse 8

لِلْفُقَرَاءِ الْمُهَاجِرِينَ الَّذِينَ أُخْرِجُوا مِنْ دِيَارِهِمْ وَأَمْوَالِهِمْ يَبْتَغُونَ فَضْلًا مِنَ اللَّهِ وَرِضْوَانًا وَيَنْصُرُونَ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الصَّادِقُونَ

˹Some of the gains will be˺ for poor emigrants who were driven out of their homes and wealth, seeking Allah’s bounty and pleasure, and standing up for Allah and His Messenger. They are the ones true in faith.

And likewise from the wealth that Allah has bestowed upon His Messenger are given the poor emigrants, who were forced by the disbelievers of "Mecca" to leave their homes and their wealth, seeking from Allah that He favors them with provision in this world and with His pleasure in the Hereafter, and supporting the religion of Allah and His Messenger by striving in the way of Allah; those are the truthful ones who have confirmed their words with their actions.

A portion of the gains is for the poor emigrants who left their homes for Allah's sake. These are the true believers, seeking Allah's bounty and supporting His religion.

Verse 9

وَالَّذِينَ تَبَوَّءُوا الدَّارَ وَالْإِيمَانَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ يُحِبُّونَ مَنْ هَاجَرَ إِلَيْهِمْ وَلَا يَجِدُونَ فِي صُدُورِهِمْ حَاجَةً مِمَّا أُوتُوا وَيُؤْثِرُونَ عَلَىٰ أَنْفُسِهِمْ وَلَوْ كَانَ بِهِمْ خَصَاصَةٌ ۚ وَمَنْ يُوقَ شُحَّ نَفْسِهِ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ

As for those who had settled in the city and ˹embraced˺ the faith before ˹the arrival of˺ the emigrants, they love whoever immigrates to them, never having a desire in their hearts for whatever ˹of the gains˺ is given to the emigrants. They give ˹the emigrants˺ preference over themselves even though they may be in need. And whoever is saved from the selfishness of their own souls, it is they who are ˹truly˺ successful.

And those who settled in "Medina", and believed before the emigration of the emigrants - and they are the Ansar - love the emigrants, and support them with their wealth, and do not find in themselves any jealousy towards them for what they were given of the wealth of fay' and other things, and they prioritize the emigrants and those in need over themselves, even if they are in need and poverty, and whoever is saved from stinginess and withholding excess wealth, then those are the successful ones who have achieved what they sought.

Those who settled in Medina and embraced faith before the emigrants love and support them. They prioritize the emigrants even in need and are considered successful for overcoming their selfishness.

Verse 10

وَالَّذِينَ جَاءُوا مِنْ بَعْدِهِمْ يَقُولُونَ رَبَّنَا اغْفِرْ لَنَا وَلِإِخْوَانِنَا الَّذِينَ سَبَقُونَا بِالْإِيمَانِ وَلَا تَجْعَلْ فِي قُلُوبِنَا غِلًّا لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا رَبَّنَا إِنَّكَ رَءُوفٌ رَحِيمٌ

And those who come after them will pray, “Our Lord! Forgive us and our fellow believers who preceded us in faith, and do not allow bitterness into our hearts towards those who believe. Our Lord! Indeed, You are Ever Gracious, Most Merciful.”

And those who came after them, from the believers, after the first of the Ansar and the Muhajireen, say: 'Our Lord, forgive us our sins and forgive our brothers in faith who preceded us in belief, and do not place in our hearts any envy or hatred for any of the people of faith. Our Lord, indeed You are Kind to Your servants, Merciful to them.' And in the verse is an indication that it is appropriate for the Muslim to remember his predecessors well, to pray for them, to love the companions of the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, to mention them well, and to seek Allah's pleasure with them.

This verse describes the believers who pray for forgiveness for themselves and their predecessors in faith. They also pray to be free from any ill feelings towards their fellow believers, showcasing the importance of unity and love within the community of believers.

Verse 11

أَلَمْ تَرَ إِلَى الَّذِينَ نَافَقُوا يَقُولُونَ لِإِخْوَانِهِمُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا مِنْ أَهْلِ الْكِتَابِ لَئِنْ أُخْرِجْتُمْ لَنَخْرُجَنَّ مَعَكُمْ وَلَا نُطِيعُ فِيكُمْ أَحَدًا أَبَدًا وَإِنْ قُوتِلْتُمْ لَنَنْصُرَنَّكُمْ وَاللَّهُ يَشْهَدُ إِنَّهُمْ لَكَاذِبُونَ

Have you ˹O Prophet˺ not seen the hypocrites who say to their fellow disbelievers from the People of the Book, “If you are expelled, we will certainly leave with you, and We will never obey anyone against you. And if you are fought against, we will surely help you.”? But Allah bears witness that they are truly liars.

Have you not seen the hypocrites, saying to their brothers in disbelief among the Jews of Banu Nadir, 'If Muhammad and those with him expel you from your homes, we will surely go out with you, and we will never obey anyone concerning you if we are asked to abandon you or to not leave with you. And if you are fought, we will certainly help you against them'? But Allah bears witness that the hypocrites are liars in what they promised the Jews of Banu Nadir.

This verse exposes the hypocrisy of some who pledged support to the disbelievers, promising to leave with them if expelled and to help them if attacked. Allah attests to the falseness of these promises.

Verse 12

لَئِنْ أُخْرِجُوا لَا يَخْرُجُونَ مَعَهُمْ وَلَئِنْ قُوتِلُوا لَا يَنْصُرُونَهُمْ وَلَئِنْ نَصَرُوهُمْ لَيُوَلُّنَّ الْأَدْبَارَ ثُمَّ لَا يُنْصَرُونَ

Indeed, if they are expelled, the hypocrites will never leave with them. And if they are fought against, the hypocrites will never help them. And even if the hypocrites did so, they would certainly flee, then the disbelievers would be left with no help.

If the Jews are expelled from 'Medina', the hypocrites will not go out with them. And if they are fought, they will not fight with them, as they promised. And if they were to fight with them, they would surely turn their backs in flight, defeated. Then Allah will not help them, but will abandon them and humiliate them.

This verse highlights the reality of the hypocrites' insincere promises; they would not actually support the disbelievers if they were expelled or attacked, and they would flee if there was any confrontation.

Verse 13

لَأَنْتُمْ أَشَدُّ رَهْبَةً فِي صُدُورِهِمْ مِنَ اللَّهِ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّهُمْ قَوْمٌ لَا يَفْقَهُونَ

Indeed, there is more fear in their hearts for you ˹believers˺ than for Allah. That is because they are a people who do not comprehend.

Indeed, the fear and dread the hypocrites have of you – O believers – is greater and more intense in their hearts than their fear and dread of Allah. That is because they are a people who do not comprehend the greatness of Allah and faith in Him, nor do they fear His punishment.

The hypocrites fear the believers more than they fear Allah, due to their lack of understanding of Allah's greatness and power.

Verse 14

لَا يُقَاتِلُونَكُمْ جَمِيعًا إِلَّا فِي قُرًى مُحَصَّنَةٍ أَوْ مِنْ وَرَاءِ جُدُرٍ ۚ بَأْسُهُمْ بَيْنَهُمْ شَدِيدٌ ۚ تَحْسَبُهُمْ جَمِيعًا وَقُلُوبُهُمْ شَتَّىٰ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّهُمْ قَوْمٌ لَا يَعْقِلُونَ

Even united, they would not ˹dare˺ fight against you except ˹from˺ within fortified strongholds or from behind walls. Their malice for each other is intense: you think they are united, yet their hearts are divided. That is because they are a people with no ˹real˺ understanding.

The Jews will not fight you all together except in fortified villages with walls and trenches, or from behind walls. Their enmity among themselves is severe. You think they are united on one word, but their hearts are divided. That is because they are a people who do not understand the command of Allah nor do they ponder His verses.

This verse describes the disbelievers' behavior in battle - they would only fight from fortified positions, highlighting their lack of courage and unity despite their apparent alliance. Their hearts are divided due to a lack of understanding.

Verse 15

كَمَثَلِ الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ قَرِيبًا ۖ ذَاقُوا وَبَالَ أَمْرِهِمْ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ

They are ˹both just˺ like those who recently went down before them: they tasted the evil consequences of their doings. And they will suffer a painful punishment.

The example of these Jews in what befell them of Allah's punishment is like the example of the disbelievers of Quraysh on the day of 'Badr', and the Jews of Banu Qaynuqa, where they tasted the evil consequences of their disbelief and their enmity towards the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, in this world, and for them in the Hereafter is a painful, agonizing torment.

The disbelievers are like those before them who faced consequences for their actions, and they will face a severe punishment in the Hereafter.

Verse 16

كَمَثَلِ الشَّيْطَانِ إِذْ قَالَ لِلْإِنْسَانِ اكْفُرْ فَلَمَّا كَفَرَ قَالَ إِنِّي بَرِيءٌ مِنْكَ إِنِّي أَخَافُ اللَّهَ رَبَّ الْعَالَمِينَ

˹They are˺ like Satan when he lures someone to disbelieve. Then after they have done so, he will say ˹on Judgment Day˺, “I have absolutely nothing to do with you. I truly fear Allah—the Lord of all worlds.”

And the example of these hypocrites in inciting the Jews to fight and promising them victory over the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, is like the example of Satan when he made disbelief appealing to man and called him to it. Then, when he disbelieved, he said, 'Indeed, I am free of you. Indeed, I fear Allah, Lord of all creation.'

The example of the hypocrites and the disbelievers is like that of Satan who tempts people into disbelief, then disowns them on the Day of Judgment, fearing Allah.

Verse 17

فَكَانَ عَاقِبَتَهُمَا أَنَّهُمَا فِي النَّارِ خَالِدَيْنِ فِيهَا ۚ وَذَٰلِكَ جَزَاءُ الظَّالِمِينَ

So they will both end up in the Fire, staying there forever. That is the reward of the wrongdoers.

So the consequence of the affair of Satan and the human who obeyed him and disbelieved was that they are in the Fire, remaining therein forever, and that is the recompense of the transgressors who exceed the limits of Allah.

Those who follow Satan and disobey Allah will end up in Hellfire forever. This is the consequence for those who transgress against Allah's limits.

Verse 18

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اتَّقُوا اللَّهَ وَلْتَنْظُرْ نَفْسٌ مَا قَدَّمَتْ لِغَدٍ ۖ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ خَبِيرٌ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ

O believers! Be mindful of Allah and let every soul look to what ˹deeds˺ it has sent forth for tomorrow. And fear Allah, ˹for˺ certainly Allah is All-Aware of what you do.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, fear Allah and beware of His punishment by doing what He has commanded you and abandoning what He has forbidden you, and let every soul consider what it has put forth of deeds for the Day of Resurrection, and fear Allah in all that you do and leave. Indeed, Allah is All-Aware of what you do; nothing of your deeds is hidden from Him, and He is the one who will recompense you for them.

Believers are urged to be mindful of Allah, to reflect on their actions in preparation for the Day of Judgment, and to fear Allah, knowing He is aware of all they do.

Verse 19

وَلَا تَكُونُوا كَالَّذِينَ نَسُوا اللَّهَ فَأَنْسَاهُمْ أَنْفُسَهُمْ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْفَاسِقُونَ

And do not be like those who forgot Allah, so He made them forget themselves. It is they who are ˹truly˺ rebellious.

And do not be, O believers, like those who abandoned the fulfillment of Allah’s right which He made obligatory upon them, so He caused them, because of that, to forget their own portions of the good deeds that would save them from the punishment of the Day of Resurrection. Those are the ones described as the defiantly disobedient, those who have gone out from the obedience to Allah and His Messenger.

Do not be like those who forget Allah, leading them to forget their own souls and the good deeds they should be doing. They are the rebellious ones who disobey Allah and His Messenger.

Verse 20

لَا يَسْتَوِي أَصْحَابُ النَّارِ وَأَصْحَابُ الْجَنَّةِ ۚ أَصْحَابُ الْجَنَّةِ هُمُ الْفَائِزُونَ

The residents of the Fire cannot be equal to the residents of Paradise. ˹Only˺ the residents of Paradise will be successful.

Not equal are the companions of the Fire, who are punished, and the companions of Paradise, who are blessed. The companions of Paradise are the ones who have attained all that they have sought, and are saved from all that is disliked.

The people of Hellfire and the people of Paradise are not alike. Only the people of Paradise will be successful in attaining their ultimate desires.

Verse 21

لَوْ أَنْزَلْنَا هَٰذَا الْقُرْآنَ عَلَىٰ جَبَلٍ لَرَأَيْتَهُ خَاشِعًا مُتَصَدِّعًا مِنْ خَشْيَةِ اللَّهِ ۚ وَتِلْكَ الْأَمْثَالُ نَضْرِبُهَا لِلنَّاسِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَتَفَكَّرُونَ

Had We sent down this Quran upon a mountain, you would have certainly seen it humbled and torn apart in awe of Allah. We set forth such comparisons for people, ˹so˺ perhaps they may reflect.

If We had sent down this Qur'an upon a mountain, and it understood what is in it of promise and warning, you would have seen it, despite its strength, firmness, and size, humbling itself, and splitting asunder from fear of Allah Almighty. And those examples We strike and make clear for the people that perhaps they may reflect on the power of Allah and His Greatness. And in the verse is an urging to ponder the Qur'an, understand its meanings and act upon it.

If the Quran was revealed to a mountain, it would crumble out of awe for Allah. This is a reminder for people to reflect on the Quran and its meanings.

Verse 22

هُوَ اللَّهُ الَّذِي لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ عَالِمُ الْغَيْبِ وَالشَّهَادَةِ ۖ هُوَ الرَّحْمَٰنُ الرَّحِيمُ

He is Allah—there is no god ˹worthy of worship˺ except Him: Knower of the seen and unseen. He is the Most Compassionate, Most Merciful.

He is Allah, the One truly worshipped, there is no deity except Him, Knower of the unseen and the witnessed. He knows what is absent and what is present, He is the Most Compassionate whose mercy encompasses all things, the Most Merciful to those who believe in Him.

Allah is the only God, He knows the seen and unseen, and He is the Most Compassionate and Most Merciful.

Verse 23

هُوَ اللَّهُ الَّذِي لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْمَلِكُ الْقُدُّوسُ السَّلَامُ الْمُؤْمِنُ الْمُهَيْمِنُ الْعَزِيزُ الْجَبَّارُ الْمُتَكَبِّرُ ۚ سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ عَمَّا يُشْرِكُونَ

He is Allah—there is no god except Him: the King, the Most Holy, the All-Perfect, the Source of Serenity, the Watcher ˹of all˺, the Almighty, the Supreme in Might, the Majestic. Glorified is Allah far above what they associate with Him ˹in worship˺!

He is Allah, the One truly worshipped, there is no deity except Him, the King of all things, the disposer of them without hindrance or opposition, the one who is free from all defects, the One who is safe from all flaws, the confirmer of His messengers and prophets through the clear signs they are sent with, the overseer of all His creation in their deeds, the Mighty who cannot be overcome, the Compeller who has subjugated all the servants, and to whom the rest of creation is obedient, the Possessor of all greatness and grandeur. Allah is Exalted above all that they associate with Him in worship.

Allah is the King, the Most Holy, the All-Perfect, the Source of Serenity, the Watcher, the Almighty, the Supreme in Might, and the Majestic. He is far above anything that is associated with Him.

Verse 24

هُوَ اللَّهُ الْخَالِقُ الْبَارِئُ الْمُصَوِّرُ ۖ لَهُ الْأَسْمَاءُ الْحُسْنَىٰ ۚ يُسَبِّحُ لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

He is Allah: the Creator, the Inventor, the Shaper. He ˹alone˺ has the Most Beautiful Names. Whatever is in the heavens and the earth ˹constantly˺ glorifies Him. And He is the Almighty, All-Wise.

He is Allah, the Creator, the Apportioner of Creation, the Originator, the Initiator who brings them into existence according to His wisdom, the Fashioner of His creation as He wills, to Him, praise be, belong the most beautiful names and the most exalted attributes, all that is in the heavens and the earth exalts Him, and He is the Mighty, Severe in Retribution against His enemies, the All-Wise in managing the affairs of His creation.

Allah is the Creator, the Inventor, the Shaper. He has the most beautiful names and is constantly glorified by all creation. He is the Almighty and the All-Wise.
