Sura 014

Verse 1

الر ۚ كِتَابٌ أَنْزَلْنَاهُ إِلَيْكَ لِتُخْرِجَ النَّاسَ مِنَ الظُّلُمَاتِ إِلَى النُّورِ بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِمْ إِلَىٰ صِرَاطِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَمِيدِ

Alif-Lãm-Ra. ˹This is˺ a Book which We have revealed to you ˹O Prophet˺ so that you may lead people out of darkness and into light, by the Will of their Lord, to the Path of the Almighty, the Praiseworthy—

(Alif, Lam, Ra). The discussion on the disjointed letters has preceded in the beginning of Surat Al-Baqarah.

This verse begins Surah Ibrahim with the letters Alif-Lam-Ra. It states that the Quran was revealed to guide people from darkness to light, by the will of Allah, the Almighty and Praiseworthy.

Verse 2

اللَّهِ الَّذِي لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۗ وَوَيْلٌ لِلْكَافِرِينَ مِنْ عَذَابٍ شَدِيدٍ

Allah, to Whom belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. And woe to the disbelievers because of a severe torment!

(Alif, Lam, Ra). The discussion on the disjointed letters has preceded in the beginning of Surat Al-Baqarah.

This verse emphasizes Allah's ownership of everything in the heavens and the earth, and warns the disbelievers of a severe torment.

Verse 3

الَّذِينَ يَسْتَحِبُّونَ الْحَيَاةَ الدُّنْيَا عَلَى الْآخِرَةِ وَيَصُدُّونَ عَنْ سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ وَيَبْغُونَهَا عِوَجًا ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ فِي ضَلَالٍ بَعِيدٍ

˹They are˺ the ones who favour the life of this world over the Hereafter and hinder ˹others˺ from the Way of Allah, striving to make it ˹appear˺ crooked. It is they who have gone far astray.

And these who turned away and did not believe in Allah and follow His messengers are the ones who choose the fleeting worldly life, and abandon the everlasting Hereafter, and prevent people from following the religion of Allah, and desire it to be a crooked path to suit their desires. Those described with these attributes are in misguidance from the truth, far from all means of guidance.

This verse describes the disbelievers as those who prefer worldly life over the Hereafter, hinder others from the path of Allah, and seek to distort it. They are far astray from guidance.

Verse 4

وَمَا أَرْسَلْنَا مِنْ رَسُولٍ إِلَّا بِلِسَانِ قَوْمِهِ لِيُبَيِّنَ لَهُمْ ۖ فَيُضِلُّ اللَّهُ مَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيَهْدِي مَنْ يَشَاءُ ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

We have not sent a messenger except in the language of his people to clarify ˹the message˺ for them. Then Allah leaves whoever He wills to stray and guides whoever He wills. And He is the Almighty, All-Wise.

And We did not send any messenger before you -O Prophet- except in the language of his people, to make clear to them the law of Allah. Then Allah leads astray whom He wills from guidance, and guides whom He wills to the truth. And He is the Almighty in His dominion, the Wise who puts matters in their places according to wisdom.

This verse explains that messengers are sent with the language of their people to clarify Allah's message. Allah guides whom He wills and misguides whom He wills, and He is Almighty and All-Wise.

Verse 5

وَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا مُوسَىٰ بِآيَاتِنَا أَنْ أَخْرِجْ قَوْمَكَ مِنَ الظُّلُمَاتِ إِلَى النُّورِ وَذَكِّرْهُمْ بِأَيَّامِ اللَّهِ ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ لِكُلِّ صَبَّارٍ شَكُورٍ

Indeed, We sent Moses with Our signs, ˹ordering him,˺ “Lead your people out of darkness and into light, and remind them of Allah’s days ˹of favour˺.” Surely in this are signs for whoever is steadfast, grateful.

And We had sent Moses to the Children of Israel and supported him with miracles indicating his truthfulness, and We commanded him to invite them to faith, to bring them out of misguidance into guidance, and to remind them of the blessings of Allah and His punishments in their days. Indeed in this reminder are signs for every patient one in obedience to Allah and concerning His prohibitions and His decrees, and a grateful one who fulfills the rights of Allah, thanking Allah for His blessings, and He singled them out for that because they are the ones who take heed of them and do not neglect them.

This verse recounts the sending of Moses with signs to lead his people from darkness to light, reminding them of Allah's favors. These reminders are signs for the steadfast and grateful.

Verse 6

وَإِذْ قَالَ مُوسَىٰ لِقَوْمِهِ اذْكُرُوا نِعْمَةَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ إِذْ أَنْجَاكُمْ مِنْ آلِ فِرْعَوْنَ يَسُومُونَكُمْ سُوءَ الْعَذَابِ وَيُذَبِّحُونَ أَبْنَاءَكُمْ وَيَسْتَحْيُونَ نِسَاءَكُمْ ۚ وَفِي ذَٰلِكُمْ بَلَاءٌ مِنْ رَبِّكُمْ عَظِيمٌ

˹Consider˺ when Moses said to his people, “Remember Allah’s favour upon you when He rescued you from the people of Pharaoh, who afflicted you with dreadful torment—slaughtering your sons and keeping your women. That was a severe test from your Lord.

And mention - O Messenger - to your people the story of Moses when he said to the Children of Israel: Remember the favor of Allah upon you when He saved you from Pharaoh and his followers, who were afflicting you with the worst torment, slaughtering your male children, so that none of them would come to take over Pharaoh's kingdom, and keeping the females alive in humiliation. And in that trial and deliverance was a great test for you from your Lord.

Moses reminds his people of Allah's favor in saving them from Pharaoh's oppression. This included the horrific killing of their sons and enslavement of their women, which was a great trial from their Lord.

Verse 7

وَإِذْ تَأَذَّنَ رَبُّكُمْ لَئِنْ شَكَرْتُمْ لَأَزِيدَنَّكُمْ ۖ وَلَئِنْ كَفَرْتُمْ إِنَّ عَذَابِي لَشَدِيدٌ

And ˹remember˺ when your Lord proclaimed, ‘If you are grateful, I will certainly give you more. But if you are ungrateful, surely My punishment is severe.’”

And Moses said to them: And remember when your Lord declared, an emphatic declaration: If you are grateful to Him for His favors, He will surely increase you in bounty; but if you are ungrateful for the favor of Allah, then He will surely punish you with a severe punishment.

Allah declares that gratitude brings more blessings, while ingratitude leads to severe punishment. This verse highlights the importance of being thankful for Allah's favors.

Verse 8

وَقَالَ مُوسَىٰ إِنْ تَكْفُرُوا أَنْتُمْ وَمَنْ فِي الْأَرْضِ جَمِيعًا فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ لَغَنِيٌّ حَمِيدٌ

Moses added, “If you along with everyone on earth were to be ungrateful, then ˹know that˺ Allah is indeed Self-Sufficient, Praiseworthy.”

And he said to them: If you disbelieve in Allah, you and all the people of the earth, then you will not harm Allah at all; indeed, Allah is free of need of His creation, worthy of praise and commendation, praised in every state.

Moses emphasizes that Allah is Self-Sufficient and Praiseworthy, regardless of whether people are grateful or ungrateful. Disbelief does not diminish Allah's greatness.

Verse 9

أَلَمْ يَأْتِكُمْ نَبَأُ الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِكُمْ قَوْمِ نُوحٍ وَعَادٍ وَثَمُودَ ۛ وَالَّذِينَ مِنْ بَعْدِهِمْ ۛ لَا يَعْلَمُهُمْ إِلَّا اللَّهُ ۚ جَاءَتْهُمْ رُسُلُهُمْ بِالْبَيِّنَاتِ فَرَدُّوا أَيْدِيَهُمْ فِي أَفْوَاهِهِمْ وَقَالُوا إِنَّا كَفَرْنَا بِمَا أُرْسِلْتُمْ بِهِ وَإِنَّا لَفِي شَكٍّ مِمَّا تَدْعُونَنَا إِلَيْهِ مُرِيبٍ

Have you not ˹already˺ received the stories of those who were before you: the people of Noah, ’Âd, Thamûd, and those after them? Only Allah knows how many they were. Their messengers came to them with clear proofs, but they put their hands over their mouths and said, “We totally reject what you have been sent with, and we are certainly in alarming doubt about what you are inviting us to.”

Has there not come to you - O nation of Muhammad - the news of the nations that came before you: the people of Noah, and the people of Hud, and the people of Salih, and the nations after them, whose number is known only to Allah? Their messengers came to them with clear proofs, but they bit their hands in rage and disdain at accepting the faith. And they said to their messengers: 'Indeed, we do not believe in that which you have come with, and indeed, we are in doubt about that to which you are calling us of faith and monotheism, causing suspicion.'

The verse recounts stories of past nations, such as the people of Noah, 'Ad, and Thamud, who rejected their messengers despite clear proofs. They disbelieved and expressed doubt about the message.

Verse 10

قَالَتْ رُسُلُهُمْ أَفِي اللَّهِ شَكٌّ فَاطِرِ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۖ يَدْعُوكُمْ لِيَغْفِرَ لَكُمْ مِنْ ذُنُوبِكُمْ وَيُؤَخِّرَكُمْ إِلَىٰ أَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى ۚ قَالُوا إِنْ أَنْتُمْ إِلَّا بَشَرٌ مِثْلُنَا تُرِيدُونَ أَنْ تَصُدُّونَا عَمَّا كَانَ يَعْبُدُ آبَاؤُنَا فَأْتُونَا بِسُلْطَانٍ مُبِينٍ

Their messengers asked ˹them˺, “Is there any doubt about Allah, the Originator of the heavens and the earth? He is inviting you in order to forgive your sins, and delay your end until your appointed term.” They argued, “You are no more than humans like us! You ˹only˺ wish to turn us away from what our forefathers worshipped. So bring us some compelling proof.”

Their messengers said to them: 'Is there doubt about Allah and His worship - alone - while He is the Creator of the heavens and the earth, and their originator from nothing without any previous example? And He is calling you to faith so that He may forgive you your sins and postpone your stay in this world until an appointed term, which is the end of your terms, so He will not punish you in this world?' They said to their messengers: 'We see you only as humans like ourselves, having no superiority over us that qualifies you to be messengers. You want to prevent us from worshipping what our fathers used to worship of idols and false deities. So bring us a clear proof that testifies to the truth of what you are saying.'

The messengers question the disbelief in Allah, the Creator of the heavens and earth. They clarify that the messengers were inviting them to seek forgiveness, but the people dismissed them, asking for proof of their prophethood and rejecting the idea of abandoning the worship of their forefathers.

Verse 11

قَالَتْ لَهُمْ رُسُلُهُمْ إِنْ نَحْنُ إِلَّا بَشَرٌ مِثْلُكُمْ وَلَٰكِنَّ اللَّهَ يَمُنُّ عَلَىٰ مَنْ يَشَاءُ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ ۖ وَمَا كَانَ لَنَا أَنْ نَأْتِيَكُمْ بِسُلْطَانٍ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِ اللَّهِ ۚ وَعَلَى اللَّهِ فَلْيَتَوَكَّلِ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ

Their messengers said to them, “We are ˹indeed˺ only humans like you, but Allah favours whoever He chooses of His servants. It is not for us to bring you any proof without Allah’s permission. And in Allah let the believers put their trust.

When the messengers heard what their people said, they said to them: Indeed, we are only humans like you, as you said. However, Allah bestows His favor upon whom He wills of His servants and chooses them for His message. As for the clear proof you asked for, it is not possible for us, nor can we bring it to you except by the permission and success of Allah. And upon Allah alone the believers rely in all their affairs.

The messengers clarified that they are human but chosen by Allah, and that they can only provide proof with Allah's permission. Believers should place their trust in Allah alone.

Verse 12

وَمَا لَنَا أَلَّا نَتَوَكَّلَ عَلَى اللَّهِ وَقَدْ هَدَانَا سُبُلَنَا ۚ وَلَنَصْبِرَنَّ عَلَىٰ مَا آذَيْتُمُونَا ۚ وَعَلَى اللَّهِ فَلْيَتَوَكَّلِ الْمُتَوَكِّلُونَ

Why should we not put our trust in Allah, when He has truly guided us to ˹the very best of˺ ways? Indeed, we will patiently endure whatever harm you may cause us. And in Allah let the faithful put their trust.”

And how can we not rely on Allah, when He is the One who guided us to the path of salvation from His punishment by following the rules of His religion? And we will surely be patient with your harm to us by bad words and otherwise, and upon Allah alone the believers must rely for their victory and the defeat of their enemies.

The messengers express their reliance on Allah, who has guided them. They affirm their patience in the face of harm and emphasize that believers should trust in Allah for victory.

Verse 13

وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لِرُسُلِهِمْ لَنُخْرِجَنَّكُمْ مِنْ أَرْضِنَا أَوْ لَتَعُودُنَّ فِي مِلَّتِنَا ۖ فَأَوْحَىٰ إِلَيْهِمْ رَبُّهُمْ لَنُهْلِكَنَّ الظَّالِمِينَ

The disbelievers then threatened their messengers, “We will certainly expel you from our land, unless you return to our faith.” So their Lord revealed to them, “We will surely destroy the wrongdoers,

And the disbelievers' chests tightened because of what the messengers said, so they said to them: We will surely expel you from our land until you return to our religion. Then Allah revealed to His messengers that He would destroy the deniers who disbelieved in Him and His messengers.

Disbelievers threaten to expel the messengers unless they return to their old faith. Allah reveals to the messengers that He will destroy the wrongdoers.

Verse 14

وَلَنُسْكِنَنَّكُمُ الْأَرْضَ مِنْ بَعْدِهِمْ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ لِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامِي وَخَافَ وَعِيدِ

and make you reside in the land after them. This is for whoever is in awe of standing before Me and fears My warning.”

And We will surely make the good end for the messengers and their followers by settling them in the land of the disbelievers after destroying them. That destruction of the disbelievers and the settlement of the believers in their land is a confirmed matter for whoever fears his standing before Me on the Day of Resurrection, and fears My warning and My punishment.

Allah promises to make the believers reside in the land after the disbelievers are destroyed. This is a reminder for those who fear Allah's judgment and warnings.

Verse 15

وَاسْتَفْتَحُوا وَخَابَ كُلُّ جَبَّارٍ عَنِيدٍ

And both sides called for judgment, so every stubborn tyrant was doomed.

And the messengers resorted to their Lord and asked Him for victory over their enemies and to judge between them, so He responded to them. And every arrogant person who does not accept the truth, nor submit to it, nor acknowledge the Oneness of Allah and the sincerity of worship to Him, was destroyed.

The messengers asked Allah for victory, and every stubborn, arrogant tyrant was ultimately destroyed for rejecting the truth.

Verse 16

مِنْ وَرَائِهِ جَهَنَّمُ وَيُسْقَىٰ مِنْ مَاءٍ صَدِيدٍ

Awaiting them is Hell, and they will be left to drink oozing pus,

And before this disbeliever is Hell, where he will face its torment; and he will be given to drink from the pus and blood that comes out of the bodies of the people of Hell.

Disbelievers are warned of Hell, where they will be forced to drink oozing pus, highlighting the horrific consequences of their disbelief.

Verse 17

يَتَجَرَّعُهُ وَلَا يَكَادُ يُسِيغُهُ وَيَأْتِيهِ الْمَوْتُ مِنْ كُلِّ مَكَانٍ وَمَا هُوَ بِمَيِّتٍ ۖ وَمِنْ وَرَائِهِ عَذَابٌ غَلِيظٌ

which they will sip with difficulty, and can hardly swallow. Death will overwhelm them from every side, yet they will not ˹be able to˺ die. Awaiting them still is harsher torment.

The arrogant person tries to swallow the pus and blood and other things that flow from the people of Hell repeatedly, but he cannot swallow it because of its filthiness, heat, and bitterness. And severe torment will come to him from every type and from every part of his body, but he will not die to rest, and after this torment, there will be another painful torment.

The disbelievers will struggle to swallow the foul liquid, experiencing torment from all sides. Death will not end their suffering; harsher torment awaits them.

Verse 18

مَثَلُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِرَبِّهِمْ ۖ أَعْمَالُهُمْ كَرَمَادٍ اشْتَدَّتْ بِهِ الرِّيحُ فِي يَوْمٍ عَاصِفٍ ۖ لَا يَقْدِرُونَ مِمَّا كَسَبُوا عَلَىٰ شَيْءٍ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ هُوَ الضَّلَالُ الْبَعِيدُ

The parable of the deeds of those who disbelieve in their Lord is that of ashes fiercely blown away by wind on a stormy day. They will gain nothing from what they have earned. That is ˹truly˺ the farthest one can stray.

The description of the deeds of the disbelievers in this world, such as righteousness and maintaining kinship ties, is like the description of ashes that have been scattered by the wind on a day of strong wind, leaving no trace of it. Likewise, their deeds will not benefit them with Allah, as disbelief has rendered them worthless just like the wind disperses ashes. That endeavor and action that is on a foundation other than truth is a far straying from the straight path.

The deeds of disbelievers are compared to ashes scattered by wind, gaining them no benefit. This emphasizes the futility of their actions without faith in Allah, describing the furthest straying from the path of truth.

Verse 19

أَلَمْ تَرَ أَنَّ اللَّهَ خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ بِالْحَقِّ ۚ إِنْ يَشَأْ يُذْهِبْكُمْ وَيَأْتِ بِخَلْقٍ جَدِيدٍ

Have you not seen that Allah created the heavens and the earth for a reason? If He wills, He can eliminate you and produce a new creation.

Do you not know, O addressee - and what is intended is all people - that Allah created the heavens and the earth in the correct manner that indicates His wisdom, and that He did not create them in vain, but rather to use them as evidence of His Oneness and the perfection of His power, so that they worship Him alone and do not associate anything with Him? If He wills, He can do away with you and bring forth a people other than you who obey Allah.

This verse highlights that Allah created the heavens and earth with purpose and wisdom, not in vain. It emphasizes Allah's power to replace humanity with a new creation if He wills.

Verse 20

وَمَا ذَٰلِكَ عَلَى اللَّهِ بِعَزِيزٍ

And that is not difficult for Allah ˹at all˺.

And your destruction and bringing forth others instead of you is not impossible for Allah, but it is easy and simple.

This verse stresses that destroying and creating is easy for Allah, emphasizing His absolute power and control.

Verse 21

وَبَرَزُوا لِلَّهِ جَمِيعًا فَقَالَ الضُّعَفَاءُ لِلَّذِينَ اسْتَكْبَرُوا إِنَّا كُنَّا لَكُمْ تَبَعًا فَهَلْ أَنْتُمْ مُغْنُونَ عَنَّا مِنْ عَذَابِ اللَّهِ مِنْ شَيْءٍ ۚ قَالُوا لَوْ هَدَانَا اللَّهُ لَهَدَيْنَاكُمْ ۖ سَوَاءٌ عَلَيْنَا أَجَزِعْنَا أَمْ صَبَرْنَا مَا لَنَا مِنْ مَحِيصٍ

They will all appear before Allah, and the lowly ˹followers˺ will appeal to the arrogant ˹leaders˺, “We were your ˹dedicated˺ followers, so will you ˹then˺ protect us from Allah’s torment in any way?” They will reply, “Had Allah guided us, we would have guided you. ˹Now˺ it is all the same for us whether we suffer patiently or impatiently, there is no escape for us.”

And the creatures will come out of their graves, and they will all appear on the Day of Resurrection before Allah, the One, the All-Dominating; to judge between them. Then the followers will say to their leaders: Indeed, we were your followers in the world, following your commands, so are you – today – going to ward off from us any of the punishment of Allah as you used to promise us? Then the leaders will say: If Allah had guided us to faith, we would have guided you to it, but He did not grant us success, so we went astray and caused you to go astray. It is equal upon us, both the lamenting and the patience, for we have no escape from the punishment nor refuge.

On the Day of Judgment, followers will ask their leaders for help, but the leaders will admit their own helplessness and misguidance. No one can escape the consequences of their deeds.

Verse 22

وَقَالَ الشَّيْطَانُ لَمَّا قُضِيَ الْأَمْرُ إِنَّ اللَّهَ وَعَدَكُمْ وَعْدَ الْحَقِّ وَوَعَدْتُكُمْ فَأَخْلَفْتُكُمْ ۖ وَمَا كَانَ لِيَ عَلَيْكُمْ مِنْ سُلْطَانٍ إِلَّا أَنْ دَعَوْتُكُمْ فَاسْتَجَبْتُمْ لِي ۖ فَلَا تَلُومُونِي وَلُومُوا أَنْفُسَكُمْ ۖ مَا أَنَا بِمُصْرِخِكُمْ وَمَا أَنْتُمْ بِمُصْرِخِيَّ ۖ إِنِّي كَفَرْتُ بِمَا أَشْرَكْتُمُونِ مِنْ قَبْلُ ۗ إِنَّ الظَّالِمِينَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ

And Satan will say ˹to his followers˺ after the judgment has been passed, “Indeed, Allah has made you a true promise. I too made you a promise, but I failed you. I did not have any authority over you. I only called you, and you responded to me. So do not blame me; blame yourselves. I cannot save you, nor can you save me. Indeed, I denounce your previous association of me with Allah ˹in loyalty˺. Surely the wrongdoers will suffer a painful punishment.”

Verse 23

Believers who do good will be granted eternal Gardens with flowing rivers, greeted with 'Peace.' This reward is by Allah's will.

Verse 24

This verse uses the metaphor of a good tree with firm roots and high branches to illustrate the nature of a good word. This good word, often interpreted as the declaration of faith (La ilaha illa Allah), is strong and beneficial.

This verse uses the metaphor of a fruitful tree to describe the believer's faith, which continuously produces good deeds. It emphasizes that Allah provides lessons through parables for people to reflect upon.

Verse 26

In contrast, an evil word, like disbelief, is compared to a weak, uprooted tree, signifying its instability and lack of lasting impact.

Verse 27

يُثَبِّتُ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا بِالْقَوْلِ الثَّابِتِ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ ۖ وَيُضِلُّ اللَّهُ الظَّالِمِينَ ۚ وَيَفْعَلُ اللَّهُ مَا يَشَاءُ

Allah strengthens believers with the firm word of faith, helping them remain steadfast in this life and the Hereafter. Conversely, wrongdoers are left to stray as Allah wills.

Verse 28

This verse questions those who respond to Allah's blessings with ingratitude, leading themselves and others to destruction.

Verse 29

Have you not seen, O addressee - and the meaning is general - the state of the deniers from the disbelievers of Quraysh who exchanged disbelief in Allah instead of thanking Him for the blessing of security in the sanctuary and the sending of the Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, among them? Verse 30

Verse 31

Believers are instructed to establish prayer and give charity, both openly and secretly, before the Day of Judgment when no ransom or connections will help.

Verse 32

Allah created the heavens, earth, and rain, which grow fruits as provision. He subjected the ships and rivers for human use, all as a sign of His power and grace.

Allah also subjected the sun and moon, with their constant movement, and the day and night for our benefit, demonstrating His mastery over the universe.

Prophet Abraham acknowledges that idols have led many astray. He declares his followers are with him, and emphasizes Allah's forgiveness and mercy even to those who disobey him in matters other than polytheism.

Verse 37

Prophet Abraham explains his settlement of his family in a barren valley near the Kaaba, seeking Allah's help for them to establish prayer and to attract the hearts of people to them and to provide for them so they may be thankful.

Prophet Abraham expresses gratitude to Allah for blessing him with sons, Ishmael and Isaac, in his old age. He acknowledges Allah as the Hearer of all prayers.

Verse 40

رَبِّ اجْعَلْنِي مُقِيمَ الصَّلَاةِ وَمِنْ ذُرِّيَّتِي ۚ رَبَّنَا وَتَقَبَّلْ دُعَاءِ

Prophet Abraham asks Allah to make him and his descendants steadfast in prayer, seeking Allah's acceptance of their prayers.

Verse 41

Prophet Abraham prays for forgiveness for himself, his parents, and all believers on the Day of Judgment.

Verse 43

This verse describes the Day of Judgment, where wrongdoers will emerge from their graves with their heads raised and eyes unseeing due to the terror, and their hearts will be empty from intense fear.

Verse 44

And warn - O Messenger - the people to whom I sent you about the punishment of Allah on the Day of Resurrection. And at that time, those who wronged themselves with disbelief will say, “Our Lord, grant us respite for a short time so we may believe in You and obey Your messengers.” It will be said to them reproachfully, “Did you not swear in your lives that there would be no end for you from this worldly life to the Hereafter? The verse warns of the Day when punishment will overtake the wicked. They will plead for a short delay to believe and follow the messengers, but will be rebuked for previously denying the afterlife.

Verse 45

And you dwelled in the dwellings of the former disbelievers who wronged themselves, like the people of Hud and Salih, and you knew - from what you saw and were informed of - what We brought down upon them of destruction, and We have set forth examples for you in the Quran, yet did you not take heed?

It reminds that they passed through the dwellings of previous disbelievers and witnessed their destruction as a lesson, yet they failed to take heed.

Verse 46

The verse speaks of the plots of the polytheists against the Prophet, emphasizing that while they plotted, Allah is aware of all their plans and their plots are weak and harmed only themselves.

Verse 47

فَلَا تَحْسَبَنَّ اللَّهَ مُخْلِفَ وَعْدِهِ رُسُلَهُ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَزِيزٌ ذُو انْتِقَامٍ

So do not think - O Messenger - that Allah will fail his messengers in what He has promised them of victory and the destruction of those who deny them. Indeed, Allah is Almighty, nothing can hinder Him, and He is severely avenging His enemies. And although the address is specific to the Prophet, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, it is directed to the entire nation.

This verse assures that Allah will fulfill His promise to His messengers by granting them victory and destroying those who oppose them. Allah is Almighty and capable of punishment.

Verse 48

And the vengeance of Allah Almighty from His enemies on the Day of Resurrection is the Day this earth will be replaced by another earth, white and pure like silver, and the heavens will likewise be replaced by others, and the creations will come out of their graves alive, appearing for the meeting with Allah, the One, the Subduer, unique in His Greatness, His Names, His Attributes, His Actions, and His dominion over all things.

It describes the Day when the earth and heavens will be transformed, and all of creation will appear before Allah, who is the One, the Supreme.

This Quran, which We have revealed to you - O Messenger - is a message and a proclamation to the people, to advise and warn them, and so that they may be certain that Allah is the One God, and worship Him alone, with no partners, and so that those of sound minds may take heed.

The Quran is a message for humanity, to warn them, to believe in the oneness of God, and to guide people of reason.
