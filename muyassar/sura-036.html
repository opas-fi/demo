(Ya-Seen) The discussion on the disjointed letters has already been mentioned at the beginning of Surat al-Baqarah.

The verse begins with the letters 'Ya-Seen,' the meaning of which is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

Allah, the Most High, swears by the Wise Quran, with its rulings, wisdoms, and proofs, that you - O Messenger - are indeed among those sent with the revelation of Allah to His servants, on a straight and balanced path, which is Islam.

Allah swears by the Quran, which is full of wisdom, rulings, and proofs.

Allah, the Most High, swears by the Wise Quran, with its rulings, wisdoms, and proofs, that you - O Messenger - are indeed among those sent with the revelation of Allah to His servants, on a straight and balanced path, which is Islam.

This verse confirms that Prophet Muhammad is one of Allah's messengers.

Allah, the Most High, swears by the Wise Quran, with its rulings, wisdoms, and proofs, that you - O Messenger - are indeed among those sent with the revelation of Allah to His servants, on a straight and balanced path, which is Islam.

Verse 5

تَنْزِيلَ الْعَزِيزِ الرَّحِيمِ

˹This is˺ a revelation from the Almighty, Most Merciful,

This Quran is a revelation from the All-Mighty in His vengeance against the people of disbelief and disobedience, the Most Merciful to those of His servants who repent and do good deeds.

The Quran is a revelation from Allah, who is All-Mighty and Most Merciful.

Verse 6

لِتُنْذِرَ قَوْمًا مَا أُنْذِرَ آبَاؤُهُمْ فَهُمْ غَافِلُونَ

so that you may warn a people whose forefathers were not warned, and so are heedless.

We have sent it down to you - O Messenger - so that you may warn a people whose forefathers were not warned before you, and they are the Arabs. These people are heedless of faith and steadfastness in good deeds. Every nation that is cut off from warning falls into heedlessness, and this is evidence of the obligation of calling to and reminding about Allah and His law upon the scholars, in order to awaken the Muslims from their heedlessness.

The Quran was revealed to warn those whose forefathers were not warned, specifically the Arabs, who are often heedless.

Verse 7

لَقَدْ حَقَّ الْقَوْلُ عَلَىٰ أَكْثَرِهِمْ فَهُمْ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ

The decree ˹of torment˺ has already been justified against most of them, for they will never believe.

The punishment has been justified upon most of these disbelievers, after the truth was presented to them and they rejected it. They do not believe in Allah, nor in His Messenger, nor do they act according to His law.

The decree of punishment is justified for those who reject the truth, and they will not believe.

Verse 8

إِنَّا جَعَلْنَا فِي أَعْنَاقِهِمْ أَغْلَالًا فَهِيَ إِلَى الْأَذْقَانِ فَهُمْ مُقْمَحُونَ

˹It is as if˺ We have put shackles around their necks up to their chins, so their heads are forced up,

Indeed, We have made those disbelievers, who were presented with the truth and rejected it, and persisted in disbelief and lack of faith, like those whose necks are yoked in shackles, so their hands are gathered with their necks beneath their chins, and they are forced to raise their heads to the sky. They are shackled from all good, and do not see the truth nor are they guided to it.

The disbelievers are described as being shackled, unable to turn towards the truth.

Verse 9

And We have placed before the disbelievers a barrier, and behind them a barrier, so they are like those whose path is blocked from before them and from behind them, and We have blinded their sight because of their disbelief and arrogance, so they do not see guidance, nor are they guided. Everyone who meets the call of Islam with aversion and obstinacy is worthy of this punishment.

The disbelievers are also described as having a barrier in front and behind them, blocking them from seeing the truth.

Verse 10

وَسَوَاءٌ عَلَيْهِمْ أَأَنْذَرْتَهُمْ أَمْ لَمْ تُنْذِرْهُمْ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ

It is the same whether you warn them or not—they will never believe.

It is the same with these obstinate disbelievers, whether you warn them - O Messenger - or do not warn them, they will not believe nor will they act.

It is futile to warn those who are obstinate in their disbelief, as they will not believe.

Verse 11

إِنَّمَا تُنْذِرُ مَنِ اتَّبَعَ الذِّكْرَ وَخَشِيَ الرَّحْمَٰنَ بِالْغَيْبِ ۖ فَبَشِّرْهُ بِمَغْفِرَةٍ وَأَجْرٍ كَرِيمٍ

You can only warn those who follow the Reminder and are in awe of the Most Compassionate without seeing Him. So give them good news of forgiveness and an honourable reward.

Indeed, only those who believe in the Quran benefit from your warning, and follow what it contains of the rulings of Allah, and fear the Most Merciful in secret, where no one sees them except Allah. So give them glad tidings of forgiveness from Allah for their sins, and reward from Him in the Hereafter for their good deeds, which is their entering Paradise.

Only those who follow the Quran and fear Allah in secret will benefit from the warning. They will be forgiven and rewarded.

Verse 12

إِنَّا نَحْنُ نُحْيِي الْمَوْتَىٰ وَنَكْتُبُ مَا قَدَّمُوا وَآثَارَهُمْ ۚ وَكُلَّ شَيْءٍ أَحْصَيْنَاهُ فِي إِمَامٍ مُبِينٍ

It is certainly We Who resurrect the dead, and write what they send forth and what they leave behind. Everything is listed by Us in a perfect Record.

Indeed, it is We who bring the dead to life, all of them, on the Day of Resurrection, and We record what they have done of good and evil, and their effects that they were the cause of in their lives and after their deaths of good, such as a righteous child, beneficial knowledge, and ongoing charity, and of evil, such as idolatry and disobedience. And We have counted everything in a clear record which is the Mother of the Books, and to it is its return, and it is the Preserved Tablet. Therefore, a rational person must take account of himself, to be a good example in his life and after his death.

Allah is the one who gives life and takes it away, and He records all deeds, both good and bad, and their effects, in a clear record. This should be a motivation for people to do good.

Verse 13

وَاضْرِبْ لَهُمْ مَثَلًا أَصْحَابَ الْقَرْيَةِ إِذْ جَاءَهَا الْمُرْسَلُونَ

But the people of the town denied the two messengers, so We strengthened and supported them with a third messenger, and the three said to the people of the town: Indeed, we are messengers to you - O people.

This verse presents a parable of a town visited by messengers to emphasize the importance of heeding divine guidance.

Verse 14

And set forth - O Messenger - for the polytheists of your people who reject your call an example by which they may take heed, which is the story of the people of the town, when the messengers went to them, when We sent to them two messengers to invite them to believe in Allah and abandon the worship of others besides Him. But the people of the town denied the two messengers, so We strengthened and supported them with a third messenger, and the three said to the people of the town: Indeed, we are messengers to you - O people.

Initially, two messengers were sent, but when rejected, a third was sent to strengthen their message. This highlights the persistence in conveying God's message.

The people of the town said to the messengers: You are nothing but human beings like us, and the Most Compassionate has not sent down anything of revelation, and you - O messengers - are only lying.

The people of the town dismissed the messengers, stating they were merely humans and that God had not revealed anything to them, thus accusing them of lying.

Verse 16

The messengers said, confirming: Our Lord who sent us knows that we are indeed sent to you, and our duty is only to convey the message clearly, and we do not possess your guidance, for guidance is in the hand of Allah alone.

The messengers affirmed that their Lord knows they were truly sent and that their responsibility is to deliver the message clearly.

The messengers said, confirming: Our Lord who sent us knows that we are indeed sent to you, and our duty is only to convey the message clearly, and we do not possess your guidance, for guidance is in the hand of Allah alone.

The messengers emphasize that their duty is only to convey the message clearly, without being responsible for guiding people, as guidance comes only from God.

Verse 18

The people of the town said: We have taken you as a bad omen, if you do not cease your call to us, we will certainly stone you to death, and a painful and grievous punishment will befall you from us.

The people threatened the messengers, viewing them as a bad omen and threatening to stone them if they continued to preach.

Verse 19

The messengers said: Your bad omen and your deeds of polytheism and evil are with you and will return upon you. Is it that when you are advised with what is good for you, you take it as a bad omen and threaten us with stoning and punishment? Rather, you are a people whose habit is extravagance in disobedience and denial.

The messengers responded that their bad fortune is due to their own actions and disobedience, not the messengers or their message.

Follow those who do not ask you for money for conveying the message, and they are guided in what they call you to of worshipping Allah alone. And in this is a statement of the virtue of whoever strives to enjoin good and forbid evil.

A man from the city urged his people to follow the messengers, highlighting the importance of accepting guidance.

Verse 22

The man questions why he shouldn't worship the one who created him, which is the core principle of monotheism.

Verse 23

Should I take besides Allah other gods that do not possess anything of the matter? If the Most Compassionate intends me harm, these gods will not be able to avert that or prevent it, nor will they be able to save me from what I am in. Indeed, if I did that, I would be in clear and manifest error. Indeed, I have believed in your Lord, so listen to what I have said to you, and obey me by believing. When he said that, his people pounced on him and killed him, so Allah admitted him to Paradise.

The man questions the logic of worshipping false gods who cannot help or protect, asserting that only God can.

Verse 27

From Paradise, the man wished his people knew of God’s forgiveness and honor bestowed upon him, hoping they would find guidance as well.

Verse 28

وَمَا أَنْزَلْنَا عَلَىٰ قَوْمِهِ مِنْ بَعْدِهِ مِنْ جُنْدٍ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ وَمَا كُنَّا مُنْزِلِينَ

And the matter did not require sending down a host from the sky to punish them after they killed the man who advised them and denied their messengers. They are weaker and more insignificant than that. And We would not send down the angels upon the nations when We destroy them, but rather We send upon them a punishment that devastates them.

After the people rejected their messenger, no heavenly army was needed to punish them. Their destruction was a simple matter for Allah.

Verse 29

إِنْ كَانَتْ إِلَّا صَيْحَةً وَاحِدَةً فَإِذَا هُمْ خَامِدُونَ

The disbelievers were destroyed by a single powerful blast. Their end came swiftly and completely.

Verse 30

This verse expresses regret for those who mocked the messengers. Every messenger sent was ridiculed by his people.

Verse 31

Verse 32

Verse 33

And He who gives life to the earth with vegetation will give life to the creation after death.

A sign of Allah's power is seen in how He revives dead land, producing grain for people to eat. This points to the resurrection.

Verse 34

Verse 35

These blessings are given so that people may eat and be thankful to Allah, as these provisions are not due to their own efforts.

Verse 36

Glory be to Allah, who created everything in pairs, including vegetation, humans (male and female), and other things unknown to mankind.

Verse 37

And a sign for them, indicative of the oneness of Allah and His perfect power: this night, We strip the day from it, and behold, the people are in darkness.

A sign of Allah is the night, from which He removes the daylight, leaving the world in darkness.

Verse 38

The sun travels a path fixed by Allah, the All-Powerful and All-Knowing.

Verse 39

Verse 82

إِنَّمَا أَمْرُهُ إِذَا أَرَادَ شَيْئًا أَنْ يَقُولَ لَهُ كُنْ فَيَكُونُ

All it takes, when He wills something ˹to be˺, is simply to say to it: “Be!” And it is!

His command, when He intends a thing, is only that He says to it, 'Be,' and it is. And from that is death and life, resurrection and the raising of the dead.

When Allah wills something, it simply comes into existence by His command 'Be!' This highlights His absolute power.

So exalted is Allah and sanctified from weakness and partnership, for He is the Owner of all things, the Disposer of the affairs of His creation without any rival or resister. Glory be to Allah, who has dominion over all things and to whom all will return for reckoning and recompense.
