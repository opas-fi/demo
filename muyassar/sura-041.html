Sura 041

Verse 1

حم

Ḥâ-Mĩm.

(Ha Mim) The discussion about the disjointed letters at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah has preceded.

The verse begins with the letters 'Ha Mim,' which are among the disjointed letters that appear at the beginning of some surahs. Their exact meaning is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

تَنْزِيلٌ مِنَ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

˹This is˺ a revelation from the Most Compassionate, Most Merciful.

This noble Quran is a revelation from the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful, which He sent down to His Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him.

This verse emphasizes that the Quran is a revelation from Allah, who is *Ar-Rahman* (The Most Compassionate) and *Ar-Rahim* (The Most Merciful), highlighting His grace and mercy in revealing the Quran.

Verse 3

كِتَابٌ فُصِّلَتْ آيَاتُهُ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّا لِقَوْمٍ يَعْلَمُونَ

˹It is˺ a Book whose verses are perfectly explained—a Quran in Arabic for people who know,

A Book whose verses are distinctly explained, its meanings and rulings made clear, an Arabic Quran easy to understand for a people who know the Arabic language.

The Quran is described as a Book whose verses are fully explained and made clear, presented in Arabic, which makes it understandable for those who know the language.

Verse 4

بَشِيرًا وَنَذِيرًا فَأَعْرَضَ أَكْثَرُهُمْ فَهُمْ لَا يَسْمَعُونَ

delivering good news and warning. Yet most of them turn away, so they do not hear.

A bearer of glad tidings of immediate and future reward for those who believe in it and act according to it, and a warner of immediate and future punishment for those who disbelieve in it. But most of the people turn away from it; they do not listen to it with acceptance and response.

The Quran delivers both good news of reward for believers and warnings of punishment for disbelievers. However, most people turn away from it and do not heed its message.

Verse 5

وَقَالُوا قُلُوبُنَا فِي أَكِنَّةٍ مِمَّا تَدْعُونَا إِلَيْهِ وَفِي آذَانِنَا وَقْرٌ وَمِنْ بَيْنِنَا وَبَيْنِكَ حِجَابٌ فَاعْمَلْ إِنَّنَا عَامِلُونَ

They say, “Our hearts are veiled against what you are calling us to, there is deafness in our ears, and there is a barrier between us and you. So do ˹whatever you want˺ and so shall we!”

And these turning-away disbelievers said to the Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him: 'Our hearts are veiled from understanding what you call us to, and our ears are deaf, and between us and you, O Muhammad, there is a barrier that prevents us from responding to your call. So, you act according to your religion, and we will act according to our religion.'

The disbelievers express their rejection of the Quran, stating that their hearts are veiled, their ears are deaf, and there is a barrier between them and the message. They insist on following their own ways.

Verse 6

قُلْ إِنَّمَا أَنَا بَشَرٌ مِثْلُكُمْ يُوحَىٰ إِلَيَّ أَنَّمَا إِلَٰهُكُمْ إِلَٰهٌ وَاحِدٌ فَاسْتَقِيمُوا إِلَيْهِ وَاسْتَغْفِرُوهُ ۗ وَوَيْلٌ لِلْمُشْرِكِينَ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “I am only a man like you, ˹but˺ it has been revealed to me that your God is only One God. So take the Straight Way towards Him, and seek His forgiveness. And woe to the polytheists—

Say to them, [O Messenger]: 'I am only a human being like you, to whom Allah reveals that your God, who deserves to be worshiped, is one God with no partner. So, take the path leading to Him, and seek His forgiveness. And a punishment awaits the polytheists who worship other than Allah, idols that neither benefit nor harm, and those who do not purify themselves by the oneness of their Lord, being sincere to Him, and do not perform prayer nor give charity. So, there is no sincerity from them to the Creator, nor benefit from them to creation, and they do not believe in the Resurrection, nor in Paradise and Hell, nor do they spend in obedience to Allah.'

The Prophet is commanded to tell them that he is a man like them, but with the distinction of receiving revelation that there is only One God. People are urged to turn to Him and seek His forgiveness. The verse also mentions the woe that awaits polytheists.

Verse 7

الَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْتُونَ الزَّكَاةَ وَهُمْ بِالْآخِرَةِ هُمْ كَافِرُونَ

those who do not pay alms-tax and are in denial of the Hereafter.

Say to them, [O Messenger]: 'I am only a human being like you, to whom Allah reveals that your God, who deserves to be worshiped, is one God with no partner. So, take the path leading to Him, and seek His forgiveness. And a punishment awaits the polytheists who worship other than Allah, idols that neither benefit nor harm, and those who do not purify themselves by the oneness of their Lord, being sincere to Him, and do not perform prayer nor give charity. So, there is no sincerity from them to the Creator, nor benefit from them to creation, and they do not believe in the Resurrection, nor in Paradise and Hell, nor do they spend in obedience to Allah.'

This verse specifies that the woe mentioned in the previous verse is for those who do not pay zakat (alms-tax) and deny the Hereafter, signifying their lack of commitment to worship and belief in the Day of Judgment.

Verse 8

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ لَهُمْ أَجْرٌ غَيْرُ مَمْنُونٍ

˹But˺ those who believe and do good will certainly have a never-ending reward.

Indeed, those who believe in Allah, His Messenger, and His Book, and do righteous deeds, being sincere to Allah in them, will have a great reward that will not be cut off or withheld.

In contrast to the disbelievers, those who believe and do good deeds will have an unending reward, highlighting the eternal benefits of faith and righteous actions.

Verse 9

قُلْ أَئِنَّكُمْ لَتَكْفُرُونَ بِالَّذِي خَلَقَ الْأَرْضَ فِي يَوْمَيْنِ وَتَجْعَلُونَ لَهُ أَنْدَادًا ۚ ذَٰلِكَ رَبُّ الْعَالَمِينَ

Ask ˹them, O Prophet˺, “How can you disbelieve in the One Who created the earth in two Days? And how can you set up equals with Him? That is the Lord of all worlds.

Say, [O Messenger], to these polytheists, rebuking and marveling at their actions: 'Do you indeed disbelieve in Allah who created the earth in two days, and do you set up rivals and partners for Him whom you worship with Him? That Creator is the Lord of all the worlds.'

The verse questions the disbelievers' disbelief in Allah, the One who created the earth in two days and how they dare to associate partners with Him. It emphasizes Allah's position as the Lord of all worlds.

Verse 10

وَجَعَلَ فِيهَا رَوَاسِيَ مِنْ فَوْقِهَا وَبَارَكَ فِيهَا وَقَدَّرَ فِيهَا أَقْوَاتَهَا فِي أَرْبَعَةِ أَيَّامٍ سَوَاءً لِلسَّائِلِينَ

He placed on the earth firm mountains, standing high, showered His blessings upon it, and ordained ˹all˺ its means of sustenance—totaling four Days exactly—for all who ask.

And He, glory be to Him, placed firmly set mountains on the earth, blessed it, making it perpetually good for its people, and He determined the sustenance of its people, of food, and what is suitable for their livelihood, in a total of four days: two days in which the earth was created, and two days in which He made firm mountains and determined sustenance in it, equally for the inquirers, i.e., for those who want to inquire about that, so that they may know.

Allah established firm mountains on the earth, blessed it, and ordained all its means of sustenance in four days for all, which demonstrates His power and provision for all beings.

Verse 11

ثُمَّ اسْتَوَىٰ إِلَى السَّمَاءِ وَهِيَ دُخَانٌ فَقَالَ لَهَا وَلِلْأَرْضِ ائْتِيَا طَوْعًا أَوْ كَرْهًا قَالَتَا أَتَيْنَا طَائِعِينَ

Then He turned towards the heaven when it was ˹still like˺ smoke, saying to it and to the earth, ‘Submit, willingly or unwillingly.’ They both responded, ‘We submit willingly.’

Then He, glory be to Him, ascended, meaning He turned to the sky, which was smoke before, and said to the sky and the earth: 'Come, willingly or unwillingly.' They said: 'We come obediently to You, we have no will that contradicts Your will.'

Allah then turned towards the sky when it was smoke, commanding it and the earth to submit, willingly or unwillingly. Both responded by willingly submitting, showing that all of creation is obedient to His will.

Verse 12

فَقَضَاهُنَّ سَبْعَ سَمَاوَاتٍ فِي يَوْمَيْنِ وَأَوْحَىٰ فِي كُلِّ سَمَاءٍ أَمْرَهَا ۚ وَزَيَّنَّا السَّمَاءَ الدُّنْيَا بِمَصَابِيحَ وَحِفْظًا ۚ ذَٰلِكَ تَقْدِيرُ الْعَزِيزِ الْعَلِيمِ

So He formed the heaven into seven heavens in two Days, assigning to each its mandate. And We adorned the lowest heaven with ˹stars like˺ lamps ˹for beauty˺ and for protection. That is the design of the Almighty, All-Knowing.”

So Allah decreed the creation of the seven heavens and their perfection in two days, thus completing the creation of the heavens and the earth in six days, for a wisdom known to Allah, with His ability, glory be to Him, to create them in a single moment. And He revealed in each heaven what He willed and what He commanded therein, and We adorned the lowest heaven with shining stars, and as a protection for it from the devils who eavesdrop. That is the wondrous creation, the decree of the Almighty in His kingdom, the All-Knowing, whose knowledge encompasses everything.

Allah created the seven heavens in two days, each with its purpose, and adorned the lowest heaven with stars for beauty and protection. This is a demonstration of Allah's power and knowledge.

Verse 13

فَإِنْ أَعْرَضُوا فَقُلْ أَنْذَرْتُكُمْ صَاعِقَةً مِثْلَ صَاعِقَةِ عَادٍ وَثَمُودَ

If they turn away, then say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “I warn you of a ˹mighty˺ blast, like the one that befell ’Ȃd and Thamûd.”

So if these deniers turn away after what has been clarified to them of the praiseworthy attributes of the Quran, and of the attributes of Allah, the Great, then say to them: I have warned you of a punishment that will eradicate you like the punishment of 'Aad and Thamud when they disbelieved in their Lord and disobeyed His messengers.

If the disbelievers turn away, the Prophet is to warn them of a severe punishment similar to what befell the people of 'Ad and Thamud.

Verse 14

إِذْ جَاءَتْهُمُ الرُّسُلُ مِنْ بَيْنِ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِمْ أَلَّا تَعْبُدُوا إِلَّا اللَّهَ ۖ قَالُوا لَوْ شَاءَ رَبُّنَا لَأَنْزَلَ مَلَائِكَةً فَإِنَّا بِمَا أُرْسِلْتُمْ بِهِ كَافِرُونَ

The messengers had come to them from all angles, ˹proclaiming,˺ “Worship none but Allah.” They responded, “Had our Lord willed, He could have easily sent down angels ˹instead˺. So we totally reject what you have been sent with.”

When the messengers came to 'Aad and Thamud, following one another in succession, ordering them to worship Allah alone, without any partners, they said to their messengers: If our Lord had willed to be worshipped alone, and not to worship anything other than Him, He would have sent down to us angels from the sky as messengers with what you are calling us to, and He would not have sent you while you are humans like us. Indeed, we are denying what Allah has sent you with to us of belief in Allah alone.

The messengers came to 'Ad and Thamud, urging them to worship Allah alone. They rejected the message, questioning why angels weren't sent instead of human messengers.

Verse 15

فَأَمَّا عَادٌ فَاسْتَكْبَرُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ بِغَيْرِ الْحَقِّ وَقَالُوا مَنْ أَشَدُّ مِنَّا قُوَّةً ۖ أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا أَنَّ اللَّهَ الَّذِي خَلَقَهُمْ هُوَ أَشَدُّ مِنْهُمْ قُوَّةً ۖ وَكَانُوا بِآيَاتِنَا يَجْحَدُونَ

As for ’Ȃd, they acted arrogantly throughout the land with no right, boasting, “Who is superior to us in might?” Did they not see that Allah ˹Himself˺, Who created them, was far superior to them in might? Still they persisted in denying Our signs.

As for 'Aad, the people of Hud, they behaved arrogantly in the land without right, and they said in conceit: Who is mightier than us in strength? Did they not see that Allah, exalted is He, who created them is mightier than them in strength and power? And they were denying Our signs and proofs.

The people of 'Ad were arrogant, boasting of their strength. They failed to acknowledge that Allah, their Creator, is far superior in might and they persisted in denying Allah's signs.

Verse 16

فَأَرْسَلْنَا عَلَيْهِمْ رِيحًا صَرْصَرًا فِي أَيَّامٍ نَحِسَاتٍ لِنُذِيقَهُمْ عَذَابَ الْخِزْيِ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا ۖ وَلَعَذَابُ الْآخِرَةِ أَخْزَىٰ ۖ وَهُمْ لَا يُنْصَرُونَ

So We sent against them a furious wind, for ˹several˺ miserable days, to make them taste a humiliating punishment in this worldly life. But far more humiliating will be the punishment of the Hereafter. And they will not be helped.

So We sent upon them a fiercely cold wind, with a high-pitched sound, in ill-fated days for them, to make them taste the torment of humiliation in the worldly life, and the torment of the Hereafter is far more humiliating, and they will not be helped by preventing the punishment from them.

Allah sent a furious wind upon 'Ad for several miserable days, bringing them a humiliating punishment in this life and a more severe one in the Hereafter.

Verse 17

وَأَمَّا ثَمُودُ فَهَدَيْنَاهُمْ فَاسْتَحَبُّوا الْعَمَىٰ عَلَى الْهُدَىٰ فَأَخَذَتْهُمْ صَاعِقَةُ الْعَذَابِ الْهُونِ بِمَا كَانُوا يَكْسِبُونَ

As for Thamûd, We showed them guidance, but they preferred blindness over guidance. So the blast of a disgracing punishment overtook them for what they used to commit.

And as for Thamud, the people of Salih, We have clarified to them the path of truth and the way of guidance, but they chose blindness over guidance, so a humiliating thunderbolt of torment destroyed them because of what they were committing of sins in their disbelief in Allah and their denial of His messengers.

Allah guided Thamud but they chose to reject it. As a result, a disgraceful punishment overtook them for their disbelief and sins.

Verse 18

وَنَجَّيْنَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَكَانُوا يَتَّقُونَ

And We delivered those who were faithful and were mindful ˹of Allah˺.

And We saved those who believed from the torment that struck 'Aad and Thamud, and these survivors were fearful of Allah and were mindful of Him.

Allah saved the believers who were mindful of Him from the torment that afflicted 'Ad and Thamud.

Verse 19

وَيَوْمَ يُحْشَرُ أَعْدَاءُ اللَّهِ إِلَى النَّارِ فَهُمْ يُوزَعُونَ

˹Consider˺ the Day ˹when˺ the enemies of Allah will be gathered for the Fire, all driven in ranks.

And on the Day when the enemies of Allah are gathered to the Fire of Hell, the keepers of punishment will drive the first of them to the last of them until when they reach the Fire, and they deny their crimes, their hearing and their sight and their skins will bear witness against them for what they used to do in the world of sins and wrongdoings.

The enemies of Allah will be gathered on the Day of Judgment and driven to Hellfire.

Verse 20

حَتَّىٰ إِذَا مَا جَاءُوهَا شَهِدَ عَلَيْهِمْ سَمْعُهُمْ وَأَبْصَارُهُمْ وَجُلُودُهُمْ بِمَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

When they reach it, their ears, eyes, and skin will testify against what they used to do.

And on the Day when the enemies of Allah are gathered to the Fire of Hell, the keepers of punishment will drive the first of them to the last of them until when they reach the Fire, and they deny their crimes, their hearing and their sight and their skins will bear witness against them for what they used to do in the world of sins and wrongdoings.

When they reach the Fire, their own ears, eyes, and skin will testify against them for their sins in the world.

Verse 21

وَقَالُوا لِجُلُودِهِمْ لِمَ شَهِدْتُمْ عَلَيْنَا ۖ قَالُوا أَنْطَقَنَا اللَّهُ الَّذِي أَنْطَقَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ وَهُوَ خَلَقَكُمْ أَوَّلَ مَرَّةٍ وَإِلَيْهِ تُرْجَعُونَ

They will ask their skin ˹furiously˺, “Why have you testified against us?” It will say, “We have been made to speak by Allah, Who causes all things to speak. He ˹is the One Who˺ created you the first time, and to Him you were bound to return.

And those who are driven to the Fire from the enemies of Allah will say to their skins, reproaching them: “Why did you testify against us?” Their skins will answer: “Allah has given us speech, just as He gave speech to all things. He is the One who created you the first time when you were nothing, and to Him is your return after death for reckoning and recompense.”

On the Day of Judgment, wrongdoers will question why their own skin testified against them. The skin will reply that Allah, who enables all things to speak, commanded it to testify. This emphasizes that all actions are known to Allah and that ultimately, everyone will return to Him.

Verse 22

وَمَا كُنْتُمْ تَسْتَتِرُونَ أَنْ يَشْهَدَ عَلَيْكُمْ سَمْعُكُمْ وَلَا أَبْصَارُكُمْ وَلَا جُلُودُكُمْ وَلَٰكِنْ ظَنَنْتُمْ أَنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَعْلَمُ كَثِيرًا مِمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ

You did not ˹bother to˺ hide yourselves from your ears, eyes, and skin to prevent them from testifying against you. Rather, you assumed that Allah did not know much of what you used to do.

You did not conceal yourselves when you committed sins, fearing that your hearing, your sight, or your skins would testify against you on the Day of Resurrection. Rather, you thought, when committing sins, that Allah did not know much of what you were doing in your disobedience to Allah. And that was your evil thought which you thought about your Lord; it has destroyed you, leading you to the Fire, and so today you have become among the losers who have lost themselves and their families.

This verse explains that wrongdoers did not try to conceal their sins from their own senses out of fear of being exposed by them on the Day of Judgment. Instead, they assumed Allah was not fully aware of their deeds. This flawed perception was a mistake that contributed to their downfall.

Verse 23

وَذَٰلِكُمْ ظَنُّكُمُ الَّذِي ظَنَنْتُمْ بِرَبِّكُمْ أَرْدَاكُمْ فَأَصْبَحْتُمْ مِنَ الْخَاسِرِينَ

It was that ˹false˺ assumption you entertained about your Lord that has brought about your doom, so you have become losers.”

You did not conceal yourselves when you committed sins, fearing that your hearing, your sight, or your skins would testify against you on the Day of Resurrection. Rather, you thought, when committing sins, that Allah did not know much of what you were doing in your disobedience to Allah. And that was your evil thought which you thought about your Lord; it has destroyed you, leading you to the Fire, and so today you have become among the losers who have lost themselves and their families.

The verse continues to emphasize the point that having a false perception of Allah, thinking He did not know much of their deeds, led to their destruction and they have become among the losers.

Verse 24

فَإِنْ يَصْبِرُوا فَالنَّارُ مَثْوًى لَهُمْ ۖ وَإِنْ يَسْتَعْتِبُوا فَمَا هُمْ مِنَ الْمُعْتَبِينَ

Even if they endure patiently, the Fire will ˹always˺ be their home. And if they ˹beg to˺ appease ˹their Lord˺, they will never be allowed to.

So if they are patient with the punishment, the Fire will be their abode, and if they ask to return to the world to resume righteous deeds, their request will not be answered, nor will their excuses be accepted.

Even if the wrongdoers patiently endure the punishment of Hell, it will still be their final abode. Their requests for a second chance will be denied, signifying the finality of the consequences of their actions.

Verse 25

وَقَيَّضْنَا لَهُمْ قُرَنَاءَ فَزَيَّنُوا لَهُمْ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ وَحَقَّ عَلَيْهِمُ الْقَوْلُ فِي أُمَمٍ قَدْ خَلَتْ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ مِنَ الْجِنِّ وَالْإِنْسِ ۖ إِنَّهُمْ كَانُوا خَاسِرِينَ

We placed at their disposal ˹evil˺ associates who made their past and future ˹misdeeds˺ appealing to them. ˹So˺ the fate of earlier communities of jinn and humans has been justified against them ˹as well˺, ˹for˺ they were truly losers.

And We have assigned for these wrongdoers who deny the truth, corrupt companions from the devils among humans and jinn, so they made their evil deeds seem attractive to them in this world, and called them to its pleasures and forbidden desires. And they made what was behind them of the matters of the Hereafter seem attractive, so they made them forget its remembrance and called them to deny the Day of Judgment. Thus, they deserved to enter the Fire along with previous communities of disbelieving jinn and humans. Indeed, they were losers of their deeds in this world and of themselves and their families on the Day of Resurrection.

Allah has appointed evil companions for wrongdoers who make their past and future misdeeds seem appealing to them. As a result, their fate is the same as past communities of disbelievers: they are losers in this life and the hereafter.

Verse 26

وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَا تَسْمَعُوا لِهَٰذَا الْقُرْآنِ وَالْغَوْا فِيهِ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَغْلِبُونَ

The disbelievers advised ˹one another˺, “Do not listen to this Quran but drown it out so that you may prevail.”

And the disbelievers will say to one another, advising among themselves: “Do not listen to this Quran, and do not obey it, and do not submit to its commands. Raise your voices with shouting, whistling, and confusion against Muhammad when he recites the Quran so that you may overcome him, causing him to stop reciting, and thus we will be victorious over him.”

The disbelievers counsel one another not to listen to the Quran but to drown it out with noise in an attempt to overcome the message of the truth.

Verse 27

فَلَنُذِيقَنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا عَذَابًا شَدِيدًا وَلَنَجْزِيَنَّهُمْ أَسْوَأَ الَّذِي كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

So We will certainly make the disbelievers taste a severe punishment, and We will surely repay them according to the worst of their deeds.

We will surely make those who said this statement taste a severe punishment in this world and the Hereafter, and We will surely recompense them for the worst of what they used to do of evil deeds.

Allah warns the disbelievers that they will face severe punishment for rejecting the Quran. They will be repaid based on the worst of their deeds, emphasizing the severity of their actions.

Verse 28

And if We cause man to taste a blessing from Us after hardship and affliction, he does not thank Allah Almighty, but rather transgresses and says: 'This has come to me because I am deserving of it,' and does not believe that the Hour is coming. This is denial of the Resurrection from him, and assuming that the Hour is coming and that I will return to my Lord, then I will have Paradise with Him. We will surely inform those who disbelieved on the Day of Resurrection of what they did of evil deeds, and We will surely make them taste severe punishment.

This verse describes people who, when blessed after hardship, attribute it to their own merit and deny the Day of Judgment. They assume they will be rewarded even if they return to Allah, but disbelievers will face severe punishment for their actions.

Verse 51

وَإِذَا أَنْعَمْنَا عَلَى الْإِنْسَانِ أَعْرَضَ وَنَأَىٰ بِجَانِبِهِ وَإِذَا مَسَّهُ الشَّرُّ فَذُو دُعَاءٍ عَرِيضٍ

When We show favour to someone, they turn away, acting arrogantly. And when touched with evil, they make endless prayers ˹for good˺.

And when We bestow favor upon man with health or provision or other things, he turns away and is arrogant about submitting to the truth, but if harm befalls him, he is full of supplications that Allah remove his harm. Thus, he recognizes his Lord in hardship but does not recognize Him in ease.

This verse points out that when people are favored, they turn away arrogantly, yet when faced with trouble, they pray excessively. It highlights the inconsistency of human behavior in times of ease versus hardship.

Verse 52

قُلْ أَرَأَيْتُمْ إِنْ كَانَ مِنْ عِنْدِ اللَّهِ ثُمَّ كَفَرْتُمْ بِهِ مَنْ أَضَلُّ مِمَّنْ هُوَ فِي شِقَاقٍ بَعِيدٍ

Ask ˹them, O Prophet˺, “Imagine if this ˹Quran˺ is ˹truly˺ from Allah and you deny it: who can be more astray than those who have gone too far in opposition ˹to the truth˺?”

Say - O Messenger - to these deniers: Inform me, if this Quran is from Allah, and then you have denied and disbelieved in it, there is no one more astray than you; because you are in a far opposition to the truth due to your disbelief in the Quran and your denial of it.

This verse challenges disbelievers to consider what if the Quran is truly from Allah and they are rejecting it, stating they would be in severe opposition to the truth.

Verse 53

سَنُرِيهِمْ آيَاتِنَا فِي الْآفَاقِ وَفِي أَنْفُسِهِمْ حَتَّىٰ يَتَبَيَّنَ لَهُمْ أَنَّهُ الْحَقُّ ۗ أَوَلَمْ يَكْفِ بِرَبِّكَ أَنَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ شَهِيدٌ

We will show them Our signs in the universe and within themselves until it becomes clear to them that this ˹Quran˺ is the truth. Is it not enough that your Lord is a Witness over all things?

We will show these deniers Our signs from the victories and the rise of Islam over the regions and all other religions, and in the regions of the heavens and the earth, and what Allah brings about in them of great events, and within themselves and what they contain of wondrous signs of Allah and marvels of His work, until it becomes clear to them from those signs a clarity that accepts no doubt that the Noble Quran is the truth revealed by the Lord of the Worlds. Is not Allah Almighty's testimony sufficient for them as proof that the Quran is the truth and he who brought it is truthful? For He has testified to its confirmation, and He is a witness over all things, and nothing is greater a testimony than His testimony, glory be to Him.

This verse explains that Allah will reveal signs in the universe and within people to show that the Quran is the truth. It concludes by stating that Allah's witness is enough for all things.

Verse 54

أَلَا إِنَّهُمْ فِي مِرْيَةٍ مِنْ لِقَاءِ رَبِّهِمْ ۗ أَلَا إِنَّهُ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ مُحِيطٌ

They are truly in doubt of the meeting with their Lord! ˹But˺ He is indeed Fully Aware of everything.

Indeed, these disbelievers are in great doubt about the resurrection after death. Indeed, Allah - Majestic and Exalted - encompasses all things in knowledge, power, and might. Nothing is hidden from Him on earth or in the sky.

This verse affirms that disbelievers are in doubt about the meeting with their Lord (the resurrection) and that Allah fully knows all things.
