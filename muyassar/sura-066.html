Sura 066

Verse 1

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ لِمَ تُحَرِّمُ مَا أَحَلَّ اللَّهُ لَكَ ۖ تَبْتَغِي مَرْضَاتَ أَزْوَاجِكَ ۚ وَاللَّهُ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

O Prophet! Why do you prohibit ˹yourself˺ from what Allah has made lawful to you, seeking to please your wives? And Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

O Prophet, why do you prohibit [yourself] from what Allah has made lawful for you, seeking to please your wives? And Allah is Forgiving and Merciful to you.

This verse addresses the Prophet, questioning why he would abstain from something lawful to please his wives. It highlights Allah's forgiving and merciful nature.

Verse 2

قَدْ فَرَضَ اللَّهُ لَكُمْ تَحِلَّةَ أَيْمَانِكُمْ ۚ وَاللَّهُ مَوْلَاكُمْ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيمُ الْحَكِيمُ

Allah has already ordained for you ˹believers˺ the way to absolve yourselves from your oaths. For Allah is your Guardian. And He is the All-Knowing, All-Wise.

Allah has already ordained for you, [O believers], the annulment of your oaths. And Allah is your protector, and He is the Knowing, the Wise.

This verse states that Allah has provided a way for believers to be released from their oaths. Allah is the protector of believers, All-Knowing and Wise.

Verse 3

وَإِذْ أَسَرَّ النَّبِيُّ إِلَىٰ بَعْضِ أَزْوَاجِهِ حَدِيثًا فَلَمَّا نَبَّأَتْ بِهِ وَأَظْهَرَهُ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ عَرَّفَ بَعْضَهُ وَأَعْرَضَ عَنْ بَعْضٍ ۖ فَلَمَّا نَبَّأَهَا بِهِ قَالَتْ مَنْ أَنْبَأَكَ هَٰذَا ۖ قَالَ نَبَّأَنِيَ الْعَلِيمُ الْخَبِيرُ

˹Remember˺ when the Prophet had ˹once˺ confided something to one of his wives, then when she disclosed it ˹to another wife˺ and Allah made it known to him, he presented ˹to her˺ part of what was disclosed and overlooked a part. So when he informed her of it, she exclaimed, “Who told you this?” He replied, “I was informed by the All-Knowing, All-Aware.”

And [remember] when the Prophet confided to one of his wives a statement; and when she informed [another] of it and Allah made it known to him, he made known part of it and ignored a part. And when he told her of it, she said, "Who informed you of this?" He said, "I was informed by Allah, the Knowing, the Acquainted."

This verse recounts an incident where the Prophet confided in one of his wives, who then disclosed it to another. When confronted, she asked who told him, and the Prophet stated that Allah, the All-Knowing, had informed him.

Verse 4

إِنْ تَتُوبَا إِلَى اللَّهِ فَقَدْ صَغَتْ قُلُوبُكُمَا ۖ وَإِنْ تَظَاهَرَا عَلَيْهِ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ هُوَ مَوْلَاهُ وَجِبْرِيلُ وَصَالِحُ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ ۖ وَالْمَلَائِكَةُ بَعْدَ ذَٰلِكَ ظَهِيرٌ

˹It will be better˺ if you ˹wives˺ both turn to Allah in repentance, for your hearts have certainly faltered. But if you ˹continue to˺ collaborate against him, then ˹know that˺ Allah Himself is his Guardian. And Gabriel, the righteous believers, and the angels are ˹all˺ his supporters as well.

If you two [wives] repent to Allah, [it is best], for your hearts have deviated. But if you cooperate against him - then indeed, Allah is his protector, and Gabriel and the righteous of the believers and the angels, moreover, are [his] assistants.

This verse urges the Prophet's wives to repent to Allah. It warns that if they collaborate against the Prophet, Allah, along with Gabriel, righteous believers and angels, will support him.

Verse 5

عَسَىٰ رَبُّهُ إِنْ طَلَّقَكُنَّ أَنْ يُبْدِلَهُ أَزْوَاجًا خَيْرًا مِنْكُنَّ مُسْلِمَاتٍ مُؤْمِنَاتٍ قَانِتَاتٍ تَائِبَاتٍ عَابِدَاتٍ سَائِحَاتٍ ثَيِّبَاتٍ وَأَبْكَارًا

Perhaps, if he were to divorce you ˹all˺, his Lord would replace you with better wives who are submissive ˹to Allah˺, faithful ˹to Him˺, devout, repentant, dedicated to worship and fasting—previously married or virgins.

Perhaps his Lord, if he divorced you [wives], would substitute for him wives better than you - submitting [to Allah], believing, devoutly obedient, repentant, worshipping, and fasting - [both] previously married and virgins.

This verse indicates that if the Prophet were to divorce his wives, Allah could replace them with wives who are more devoted, believing, obedient, repentant, and dedicated to worship.

Verse 6

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا قُوا أَنْفُسَكُمْ وَأَهْلِيكُمْ نَارًا وَقُودُهَا النَّاسُ وَالْحِجَارَةُ عَلَيْهَا مَلَائِكَةٌ غِلَاظٌ شِدَادٌ لَا يَعْصُونَ اللَّهَ مَا أَمَرَهُمْ وَيَفْعَلُونَ مَا يُؤْمَرُونَ

O believers! Protect yourselves and your families from a Fire whose fuel is people and stones, overseen by formidable and severe angels, who never disobey whatever Allah orders—always doing as commanded.

O you who have believed, protect yourselves and your families from a Fire whose fuel is people and stones, over which are [appointed] angels, harsh and severe; they do not disobey Allah in what He commands them but do what they are commanded.

This verse commands believers to protect themselves and their families from Hellfire, described as having people and stones as fuel, and guarded by angels who always obey Allah.

Verse 7

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَا تَعْتَذِرُوا الْيَوْمَ ۖ إِنَّمَا تُجْزَوْنَ مَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ

˹The deniers will then be told,˺ “O disbelievers! Make no excuses this Day! You are only rewarded for what you used to do.”

It will be said to those who disbelieved, "Make no excuses today. You are only being recompensed for what you used to do."

This verse explains that on the Day of Judgment, disbelievers will be told not to make excuses and will be recompensed for their deeds.

Verse 8

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا تُوبُوا إِلَى اللَّهِ تَوْبَةً نَصُوحًا عَسَىٰ رَبُّكُمْ أَنْ يُكَفِّرَ عَنْكُمْ سَيِّئَاتِكُمْ وَيُدْخِلَكُمْ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ يَوْمَ لَا يُخْزِي اللَّهُ النَّبِيَّ وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مَعَهُ ۖ نُورُهُمْ يَسْعَىٰ بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَبِأَيْمَانِهِمْ يَقُولُونَ رَبَّنَا أَتْمِمْ لَنَا نُورَنَا وَاغْفِرْ لَنَا ۖ إِنَّكَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

O believers! Turn to Allah in sincere repentance, so your Lord may absolve you of your sins and admit you into Gardens, under which rivers flow, on the Day Allah will not disgrace the Prophet or the believers with him. Their light will shine ahead of them and on their right. They will say, “Our Lord! Perfect our light for us, and forgive us. ˹For˺ You are truly Most Capable of everything.”

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, return from your sins to the obedience of Allah, a return after which there is no disobedience. Perhaps your Lord will remove from you the evil of your deeds and admit you to gardens beneath whose palaces rivers flow, on a Day when Allah will not disgrace the Prophet and those who believed with him, nor will He punish them, but will elevate their status. Their light will proceed before them and on their right hands, saying: Our Lord, perfect for us our light until we pass the path, and be guided to Paradise, and pardon us and forgive our sins and conceal them from us, indeed, You are over all things competent.

This verse urges believers to sincerely repent to Allah, promising forgiveness and entry into Paradise. On Judgment Day, their faith will be a source of light, and they will pray for its perfection and for Allah's mercy.

Verse 9

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ جَاهِدِ الْكُفَّارَ وَالْمُنَافِقِينَ وَاغْلُظْ عَلَيْهِمْ ۚ وَمَأْوَاهُمْ جَهَنَّمُ ۖ وَبِئْسَ الْمَصِيرُ

O Prophet! Struggle against the disbelievers and the hypocrites, and be firm with them. Hell will be their home. What an evil destination!

O Prophet, strive against the disbelievers who have shown their disbelief and announced it, and fight them with the sword, and strive against those who have concealed their disbelief and hidden it with argument and establishing the limits and rites of the religion, and use with both groups harshness in striving against them. And their abode to which they will go in the Hereafter is Hell, and how evil is that destination to which they return.

This verse commands the Prophet to strive against both disbelievers and hypocrites, warning that their destination is Hell.

Verse 10

ضَرَبَ اللَّهُ مَثَلًا لِلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا امْرَأَتَ نُوحٍ وَامْرَأَتَ لُوطٍ ۖ كَانَتَا تَحْتَ عَبْدَيْنِ مِنْ عِبَادِنَا صَالِحَيْنِ فَخَانَتَاهُمَا فَلَمْ يُغْنِيَا عَنْهُمَا مِنَ اللَّهِ شَيْئًا وَقِيلَ ادْخُلَا النَّارَ مَعَ الدَّاخِلِينَ

Allah sets forth an example for the disbelievers: the wife of Noah and the wife of Lot. Each was married to one of Our righteous servants, yet betrayed them. So their husbands were of no benefit to them against Allah whatsoever. Both were told, “Enter the Fire, along with the others!”

Allah has presented an example of the state of the disbelievers - in their mixing with Muslims, their closeness to them, and their association with them, and that this does not benefit them because of their disbelief in Allah - like the state of the wife of the prophet of Allah, Noah, and the wife of the prophet of Allah, Lot: where they were under the protection of two righteous servants of Ours, so they betrayed them in religion, as they were disbelieving. So, these two messengers did not avert anything from the punishment of Allah from their wives. And it was said to the two wives: Enter the Fire with those who enter it. And in this example is a proof that closeness to the prophets and the righteous does not benefit anything with bad deeds.

This verse provides the example of the wives of Prophet Noah and Prophet Lot who betrayed their husbands in faith. Their proximity to righteous men did not save them from the Fire, illustrating that faith is personal.

Verse 11

وَضَرَبَ اللَّهُ مَثَلًا لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا امْرَأَتَ فِرْعَوْنَ إِذْ قَالَتْ رَبِّ ابْنِ لِي عِنْدَكَ بَيْتًا فِي الْجَنَّةِ وَنَجِّنِي مِنْ فِرْعَوْنَ وَعَمَلِهِ وَنَجِّنِي مِنَ الْقَوْمِ الظَّالِمِينَ

And Allah sets forth an example for the believers: the wife of Pharaoh, who prayed, “My Lord! Build me a house in Paradise near You, deliver me from Pharaoh and his ˹evil˺ doing, and save me from the wrongdoing people.”

And Allah has presented an example of the state of the believers - those who believed in Allah and worshipped Him alone and acted according to His law, and that they are not harmed by the association of disbelievers in their dealings - like the state of Pharaoh's wife who was under the protection of the most severe of the disbelievers in Allah, while she was a believer in Allah, when she said: My Lord, build for me a house with You in Paradise and save me from the authority and trials of Pharaoh and from what emanates from him of evil deeds, and save me from the people who follow him in injustice and misguidance, and from their punishment.

This verse offers the example of Pharaoh's wife, a believer in Allah who sought refuge in Paradise and was saved from Pharaoh's evil. Her faith shows that a believer's devotion to Allah is the path to salvation.

Verse 12

وَمَرْيَمَ ابْنَتَ عِمْرَانَ الَّتِي أَحْصَنَتْ فَرْجَهَا فَنَفَخْنَا فِيهِ مِنْ رُوحِنَا وَصَدَّقَتْ بِكَلِمَاتِ رَبِّهَا وَكُتُبِهِ وَكَانَتْ مِنَ الْقَانِتِينَ

˹There is˺ also ˹the example of˺ Mary, the daughter of ’Imrân, who guarded her chastity, so We breathed into her ˹womb˺ through Our angel ˹Gabriel˺. She testified to the words of her Lord and His Scriptures, and was one of the ˹sincerely˺ devout.

And Allah has presented an example of those who believed: Mary, the daughter of Imran, who guarded her chastity and protected it from fornication, so Allah the Most High commanded Gabriel, peace be upon him, to blow into the opening of her garment, and the blow reached her womb, so she conceived Jesus, peace be upon him, and she believed in the words of her Lord, and acted according to His laws that He prescribed for His servants, and His books revealed to His messengers, and she was among those who obeyed Him.

This verse presents Mary, the mother of Jesus, as an example of devotion and righteousness. She is praised for her chastity and her belief in Allah's words and scriptures, highlighting her sincere obedience.
