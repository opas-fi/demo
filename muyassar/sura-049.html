Sura 049

Verse 1

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تُقَدِّمُوا بَيْنَ يَدَيِ اللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ ۖ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ سَمِيعٌ عَلِيمٌ

O believers! Do not proceed ˹in any matter˺ before ˹a decree from˺ Allah and His Messenger. And fear Allah. Surely Allah is All-Hearing, All-Knowing.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger, do not put forward any matter before the command of Allah and His Messenger from the laws of your religion, so you innovate, and fear Allah in your words and deeds that you may contradict the command of Allah and His Messenger. Indeed, Allah is Hearing of your words, Knowing of your intentions and deeds. And in this is a warning to the believers not to innovate in the religion, or legislate what Allah has not permitted.

This verse instructs believers not to make decisions or take actions on religious matters before Allah and His Messenger have given guidance. It emphasizes the importance of fearing Allah and adhering to His commands, as Allah is All-Hearing and All-Knowing. It warns against introducing innovations into the religion.

Verse 2

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تَرْفَعُوا أَصْوَاتَكُمْ فَوْقَ صَوْتِ النَّبِيِّ وَلَا تَجْهَرُوا لَهُ بِالْقَوْلِ كَجَهْرِ بَعْضِكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ أَنْ تَحْبَطَ أَعْمَالُكُمْ وَأَنْتُمْ لَا تَشْعُرُونَ

O believers! Do not raise your voices above the voice of the Prophet, nor speak loudly to him as you do to one another, or your deeds will become void while you are unaware.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, do not raise your voices above the voice of the Prophet when addressing him, and do not speak loudly to him as some of you speak loudly to one another. And distinguish him in your speech as he is distinguished from others in his selection to bear the message of his Lord, and the obligation to believe in him, and love him and obey him and follow his example; lest your deeds become worthless, while you do not perceive or feel it.

Believers are commanded not to raise their voices above the Prophet's voice or speak loudly to him, as they would with one another. This instruction highlights the respect and reverence due to the Prophet. Disregarding this guidance can nullify one's good deeds.

Verse 3

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يَغُضُّونَ أَصْوَاتَهُمْ عِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ امْتَحَنَ اللَّهُ قُلُوبَهُمْ لِلتَّقْوَىٰ ۚ لَهُمْ مَغْفِرَةٌ وَأَجْرٌ عَظِيمٌ

Indeed, those who lower their voices in the presence of Allah’s Messenger are the ones whose hearts Allah has refined for righteousness. They will have forgiveness and a great reward.

Indeed, those who lower their voices before the Messenger of Allah - those are the ones whose hearts Allah has tested and purified for His piety. For them is forgiveness from Allah and a great reward, which is Paradise.

Those who lower their voices in the presence of the Prophet are recognized as having pure and righteous hearts, tested and purified by Allah. They are promised forgiveness and a great reward in Paradise for their piety.

Verse 4

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يُنَادُونَكَ مِنْ وَرَاءِ الْحُجُرَاتِ أَكْثَرُهُمْ لَا يَعْقِلُونَ

Indeed, most of those who call out to you ˹O Prophet˺ from outside ˹your˺ private quarters have no understanding ˹of manners˺.

Indeed, those who call you, [O Prophet], from behind the private apartments - most of them do not have the intellect to carry them to good manners with the Messenger of Allah, may Allah's peace and blessings be upon him, and reverence him.

This verse highlights that many who call out to the Prophet from outside his private quarters lack understanding and proper manners. It emphasizes the importance of respecting the Prophet's privacy and position.

Verse 5

وَلَوْ أَنَّهُمْ صَبَرُوا حَتَّىٰ تَخْرُجَ إِلَيْهِمْ لَكَانَ خَيْرًا لَهُمْ ۚ وَاللَّهُ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

Had they been patient until you could come out to them, it would have certainly been better for them. And Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

And if they had been patient until you came out to them, it would have been better for them in the sight of Allah. Indeed, Allah has commanded them to respect you, and Allah is Forgiving of what they did out of ignorance of sins and failure to observe etiquette, Merciful to them in that He did not hasten punishment for them.

This verse advises patience and respect for the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). It states that it would have been better for some people to wait for him to come out instead of calling out to him, and it highlights Allah's forgiveness and mercy.

Verse 6

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِنْ جَاءَكُمْ فَاسِقٌ بِنَبَإٍ فَتَبَيَّنُوا أَنْ تُصِيبُوا قَوْمًا بِجَهَالَةٍ فَتُصْبِحُوا عَلَىٰ مَا فَعَلْتُمْ نَادِمِينَ

O believers, if an evildoer brings you any news, verify ˹it˺ so you do not harm people unknowingly, becoming regretful for what you have done.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted upon His law, if a wicked person comes to you with information, investigate the matter before believing it and spreading it, so that you may know its truth, lest you harm people unjustly, and you become regretful for what you did.

This verse instructs believers to verify information brought by untrustworthy people to prevent harm and regret. It emphasizes the importance of critical thinking and caution in receiving news.

Verse 7

وَاعْلَمُوا أَنَّ فِيكُمْ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ۚ لَوْ يُطِيعُكُمْ فِي كَثِيرٍ مِنَ الْأَمْرِ لَعَنِتُّمْ وَلَٰكِنَّ اللَّهَ حَبَّبَ إِلَيْكُمُ الْإِيمَانَ وَزَيَّنَهُ فِي قُلُوبِكُمْ وَكَرَّهَ إِلَيْكُمُ الْكُفْرَ وَالْفُسُوقَ وَالْعِصْيَانَ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الرَّاشِدُونَ

And keep in mind that Allah’s Messenger is ˹still˺ in your midst. If he were to yield to you in many matters, you would surely suffer ˹the consequences˺. But Allah has endeared faith to you, making it appealing in your hearts. And He has made disbelief, rebelliousness, and disobedience detestable to you. Those are the ones rightly guided.

And know that among you is the Messenger of Allah, so be respectful to him; for he knows better than you what is best for you. He wants good for you, and you may want for yourselves something bad and harmful that the Messenger does not agree with. If he were to obey you in many matters you choose, it would lead to hardship for you. But Allah has made faith beloved to you and beautified it in your hearts, so you believe. And He has made disbelief in Allah and disobedience to Him hateful to you. Those who have these qualities are the rightly guided, following the path of truth.

This verse reminds believers that the Prophet is among them and that his decisions are beneficial. It highlights the blessing of faith and the repulsion of disbelief, rebellion and disobedience.

Verse 8

فَضْلًا مِنَ اللَّهِ وَنِعْمَةً ۚ وَاللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ حَكِيمٌ

˹This is˺ a bounty and a blessing from Allah. And Allah is All-Knowing, All-Wise.

And this good that they have obtained is a favor from Allah upon them and a blessing. And Allah is Knowing of who is grateful for His blessings, Wise in the management of the affairs of His creation.

This verse states that the guidance and faith believers have is a bounty from Allah. It emphasizes that Allah is All-Knowing and All-Wise in His management of affairs.

Verse 9

وَإِنْ طَائِفَتَانِ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ اقْتَتَلُوا فَأَصْلِحُوا بَيْنَهُمَا ۖ فَإِنْ بَغَتْ إِحْدَاهُمَا عَلَى الْأُخْرَىٰ فَقَاتِلُوا الَّتِي تَبْغِي حَتَّىٰ تَفِيءَ إِلَىٰ أَمْرِ اللَّهِ ۚ فَإِنْ فَاءَتْ فَأَصْلِحُوا بَيْنَهُمَا بِالْعَدْلِ وَأَقْسِطُوا ۖ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الْمُقْسِطِينَ

And if two groups of believers fight each other, then make peace between them. But if one of them transgresses against the other, then fight against the transgressing group until they ˹are willing to˺ submit to the rule of Allah. If they do so, then make peace between both ˹groups˺ in all fairness and act justly. Surely Allah loves those who uphold justice.

And if two factions among the believers fight, then make peace between them, O believers, by inviting them to abide by the Book of Allah and the Sunnah of His Messenger, may Allah's peace and blessings be upon him, and to be content with their rulings. And if one of the factions transgresses and refuses to comply, then fight them until they return to the command of Allah and His Messenger. And if they return, then make peace between them with justice and be fair in your judgment, not exceeding the rulings of Allah and His Messenger. Indeed, Allah loves the just in their judgments, who judge among His creation with equity. And in the verse, there is confirmation of the attribute of love for Allah in reality, as befits His Majesty, glory be to Him.

This verse provides guidance on resolving conflicts between groups of believers, urging mediation and fighting the transgressor until they submit to Allah's command. It also emphasizes the importance of justice and fairness in dispute resolution.

Verse 10

إِنَّمَا الْمُؤْمِنُونَ إِخْوَةٌ فَأَصْلِحُوا بَيْنَ أَخَوَيْكُمْ ۚ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُرْحَمُونَ

The believers are but one brotherhood, so make peace between your brothers. And be mindful of Allah so you may be shown mercy.

The believers are but brothers in religion, so make peace between your brothers when they fight and fear Allah in all your affairs, so that you may receive mercy.

This verse emphasizes the brotherhood of believers and calls for them to make peace among each other and to be mindful of Allah in all their affairs, so they may receive His mercy.

Verse 11

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا يَسْخَرْ قَوْمٌ مِنْ قَوْمٍ عَسَىٰ أَنْ يَكُونُوا خَيْرًا مِنْهُمْ وَلَا نِسَاءٌ مِنْ نِسَاءٍ عَسَىٰ أَنْ يَكُنَّ خَيْرًا مِنْهُنَّ ۖ وَلَا تَلْمِزُوا أَنْفُسَكُمْ وَلَا تَنَابَزُوا بِالْأَلْقَابِ ۖ بِئْسَ الِاسْمُ الْفُسُوقُ بَعْدَ الْإِيمَانِ ۚ وَمَنْ لَمْ يَتُبْ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الظَّالِمُونَ

O believers! Do not let some ˹men˺ ridicule others, they may be better than them, nor let ˹some˺ women ridicule other women, they may be better than them. Do not defame one another, nor call each other by offensive nicknames. How evil it is to act rebelliously after having faith! And whoever does not repent, it is they who are the ˹true˺ wrongdoers.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted upon His law, let not a people ridicule [another] people; perhaps they may be better than them; nor let women ridicule [other] women; perhaps they may be better than them. And do not insult one another and do not call each other by [offensive] nicknames. Wretched is the name of wickedness, [that is], mockery, belittling and calling by nicknames, after you have entered into Islam and understood it. And whoever does not repent from this mockery, belittling, nicknaming and wickedness, then they are the ones who have wronged themselves by committing these prohibitions.

This verse prohibits ridiculing, defaming, and using offensive nicknames. It warns against arrogance and stresses the importance of good character after attaining faith.

Verse 12

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اجْتَنِبُوا كَثِيرًا مِنَ الظَّنِّ إِنَّ بَعْضَ الظَّنِّ إِثْمٌ ۖ وَلَا تَجَسَّسُوا وَلَا يَغْتَبْ بَعْضُكُمْ بَعْضًا ۚ أَيُحِبُّ أَحَدُكُمْ أَنْ يَأْكُلَ لَحْمَ أَخِيهِ مَيْتًا فَكَرِهْتُمُوهُ ۚ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ تَوَّابٌ رَحِيمٌ

O believers! Avoid many suspicions, ˹for˺ indeed, some suspicions are sinful. And do not spy, nor backbite one another. Would any of you like to eat the flesh of their dead brother? You would despise that! And fear Allah. Surely Allah is ˹the˺ Accepter of Repentance, Most Merciful.

O you who have believed, those who believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, avoid much suspicion of the believers. Indeed, some suspicion is sin. And do not spy on the private matters of Muslims, and do not backbite one another in absence about what you dislike. Would one of you like to eat the flesh of his brother while he is dead? You would hate that, so hate backbiting. And fear Allah in what He has commanded you and what He has forbidden you. Indeed, Allah is Accepting of repentance for His believing servants, Merciful to them.

This verse advises believers to avoid excessive suspicion, as some suspicions are sinful. It prohibits spying on others and backbiting, comparing it to the repulsive act of eating the flesh of a dead brother. It concludes by urging fear of Allah, highlighting His acceptance of repentance and mercy.

Verse 13

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ إِنَّا خَلَقْنَاكُمْ مِنْ ذَكَرٍ وَأُنْثَىٰ وَجَعَلْنَاكُمْ شُعُوبًا وَقَبَائِلَ لِتَعَارَفُوا ۚ إِنَّ أَكْرَمَكُمْ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ أَتْقَاكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ خَبِيرٌ

O humanity! Indeed, We created you from a male and a female, and made you into peoples and tribes so that you may ˹get to˺ know one another. Surely the most noble of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous among you. Allah is truly All-Knowing, All-Aware.

O mankind, We have created you from one male and female, Adam and Eve, so there is no superiority among you in lineage, and We made you into diverse peoples and tribes for the purpose of knowing one another. Indeed, the most honored of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous of you. Indeed, Allah is Knowing of the righteous, Aware of them.

This verse emphasizes the equality of humanity, stating that all people are created from a single male and female. Different peoples and tribes exist for the purpose of recognition and understanding, with righteousness being the measure of nobility in Allah's sight. Allah is All-Knowing and All-Aware of all.

Verse 14

قَالَتِ الْأَعْرَابُ آمَنَّا ۖ قُلْ لَمْ تُؤْمِنُوا وَلَٰكِنْ قُولُوا أَسْلَمْنَا وَلَمَّا يَدْخُلِ الْإِيمَانُ فِي قُلُوبِكُمْ ۖ وَإِنْ تُطِيعُوا اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ لَا يَلِتْكُمْ مِنْ أَعْمَالِكُمْ شَيْئًا ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

˹Some of˺ the nomadic Arabs say, “We believe.” Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “You have not believed. But say, ‘We have submitted,’ for faith has not yet entered your hearts. But if you obey Allah and His Messenger ˹wholeheartedly˺, He will not discount anything from ˹the reward of˺ your deeds. Allah is truly All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.”

The bedouins said (themselves): We have believed in Allah and His Messenger with complete faith. Say to them - O Prophet - : Do not claim for yourselves complete faith, but say: We have submitted, and faith has not yet entered into your hearts. And if you obey Allah and His Messenger, He will not deprive you of any reward of your deeds. Indeed, Allah is Forgiving to those who repent from their sins, Merciful to them. And in the verse is a rebuke to those who show faith and follow the Sunnah, while their actions testify to the contrary.

This verse addresses some nomadic Arabs who claimed to be believers but were not. It clarifies that true faith is not just a verbal claim, rather it needs to enter the heart. The verse encourages obedience to Allah and His Messenger, promising that Allah will not reduce the reward for good deeds and is All-Forgiving and Most Merciful.

Verse 15

إِنَّمَا الْمُؤْمِنُونَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا بِاللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ ثُمَّ لَمْ يَرْتَابُوا وَجَاهَدُوا بِأَمْوَالِهِمْ وَأَنْفُسِهِمْ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الصَّادِقُونَ

The ˹true˺ believers are only those who believe in Allah and His Messenger—never doubting—and strive with their wealth and their lives in the cause of Allah. They are the ones true in faith.

The believers are only those who believe in Allah and His Messenger and act according to His law, then do not doubt their faith, and spend their precious wealth and souls in striving in the cause of Allah and obeying Him and seeking His pleasure, they are the truthful in their faith.

This verse defines true believers as those who believe in Allah and His Messenger without doubt and strive in Allah's cause with their wealth and lives. It emphasizes that faith should be accompanied by action and sincerity to be considered truthful.

Verse 16

قُلْ أَتُعَلِّمُونَ اللَّهَ بِدِينِكُمْ وَاللَّهُ يَعْلَمُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۚ وَاللَّهُ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيمٌ

Say, “Do you inform Allah of your faith, when Allah ˹already˺ knows whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth? And Allah has ˹perfect˺ knowledge of all things.”

Say - O Prophet - to these bedouins: Do you inform Allah of your religion and what is in your consciences, while Allah knows what is in the heavens and what is on earth? And Allah is Knowing of all things. Nothing is hidden from Him regarding what is in your hearts of belief or disbelief, and righteousness or wickedness.

This verse questions the need to inform Allah of one's faith when He already knows everything in the heavens and the earth. It highlights that Allah has perfect knowledge of all things, including the beliefs and intentions in people's hearts.

Verse 17

يَمُنُّونَ عَلَيْكَ أَنْ أَسْلَمُوا ۖ قُلْ لَا تَمُنُّوا عَلَيَّ إِسْلَامَكُمْ ۖ بَلِ اللَّهُ يَمُنُّ عَلَيْكُمْ أَنْ هَدَاكُمْ لِلْإِيمَانِ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صَادِقِينَ

They regard their acceptance of Islam as a favour to you. Tell ˹them, O Prophet˺, “Do not regard your Islam as a favour to me. Rather, it is Allah Who has done you a favour by guiding you to the faith, if ˹indeed˺ you are faithful.

These bedouins consider their embracing Islam, following you, and supporting you as a favor to you - O Prophet -. Say to them: Do not consider your entering into Islam a favor to me; rather, its benefit is only to you. Allah has bestowed the favor upon you in that He guided you to believe in Him and in His Messenger if you are truthful in your faith.

This verse addresses those who consider their acceptance of Islam a favor to the Prophet. It emphasizes that Allah is the One who has bestowed a favor upon them by guiding them to faith if they are truly faithful.

Verse 18

إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَعْلَمُ غَيْبَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ وَاللَّهُ بَصِيرٌ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ

Surely Allah knows the unseen of the heavens and earth. And Allah is All-Seeing of what you do.”

Indeed, Allah knows the unseen of the heavens and the earth. Nothing of that is hidden from Him. And Allah is Seeing of your deeds, and He will reward you for them; if good, then good, and if evil, then evil.

This verse reaffirms Allah's knowledge of the unseen in the heavens and the earth. It also states that Allah is All-Seeing of all deeds, implying that He will reward or punish accordingly.
