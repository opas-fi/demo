Sura 050

Verse 1

ق ۚ وَالْقُرْآنِ الْمَجِيدِ

Qãf. By the glorious Quran!

(Q) The discussion about the disjointed letters at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah has preceded. Allah has sworn by the Noble Quran, which possesses glory and honor.

This verse begins with the letter 'Qaf' and an oath by the glorious Quran, highlighting its importance and honor.

Verse 2

بَلْ عَجِبُوا أَنْ جَاءَهُمْ مُنْذِرٌ مِنْهُمْ فَقَالَ الْكَافِرُونَ هَٰذَا شَيْءٌ عَجِيبٌ

˹All will be resurrected,˺ yet the deniers are astonished that a warner has come to them from among themselves ˹warning of resurrection˺. So the disbelievers say, “This is an astonishing thing!

Rather, those who disbelieved the Messenger, may Allah’s peace and blessings be upon him, were amazed that a warner from among them came to them, warning them of the punishment of Allah, and the disbelievers in Allah and His Messenger said: This is something strange that one should be amazed by.

The disbelievers are surprised that a warner has come to them from among themselves, warning them about the resurrection.

Verse 3

أَإِذَا مِتْنَا وَكُنَّا تُرَابًا ۖ ذَٰلِكَ رَجْعٌ بَعِيدٌ

˹Will we be returned to life,˺ when we are dead and reduced to dust? Such a return is impossible.”

Is it that when we die and become dust, how can we return to what we were before? That is a far-fetched return.

The disbelievers question how they can be brought back to life after they have died and turned to dust, considering it impossible.

Verse 4

قَدْ عَلِمْنَا مَا تَنْقُصُ الْأَرْضُ مِنْهُمْ ۖ وَعِنْدَنَا كِتَابٌ حَفِيظٌ

We certainly know what the earth consumes of them ˹after their death˺, and with us is a well-preserved Record.

We know what the earth diminishes and destroys from their bodies, and with Us is a book preserved from change and alteration, with everything that happens to them in their lives and after their death.

Allah knows what the earth consumes of their bodies after death, and He has a well-preserved record of everything.

Verse 5

In fact, they reject the truth when it has come to them, so they are in a confused state.

Rather, these polytheists denied the Quran when it came to them, so they are in a confused and mixed state, not settling on anything, nor is there any stability for them.

The disbelievers reject the truth when it comes to them and are in a state of confusion and instability.

Verse 6

أَفَلَمْ يَنْظُرُوا إِلَى السَّمَاءِ فَوْقَهُمْ كَيْفَ بَنَيْنَاهَا وَزَيَّنَّاهَا وَمَا لَهَا مِنْ فُرُوجٍ

Have they neglected, when they disbelieved in the Resurrection, to look at the sky above them, how We have built it with even sides, a firm structure, and adorned it with stars, and it has no rifts or cracks, so it is free from inconsistencies and flaws?

Allah prompts them to look at the sky and how He built it flawlessly and adorned it with stars, a sign of His power.

Verse 7

وَالْأَرْضَ مَدَدْنَاهَا وَأَلْقَيْنَا فِيهَا رَوَاسِيَ وَأَنْبَتْنَا فِيهَا مِنْ كُلِّ زَوْجٍ بَهِيجٍ

And the earth, We have spread it out and furnished it, and We have made mountains in it as stabilizers; so that it does not tilt with its people, and We have caused to grow in it of every kind pleasing to the eye and beneficial, which pleases and delights the one who looks at it.

Allah also points to the Earth, how He spread it out, placed mountains on it, and produced various types of pleasing plants.

Verse 8

تَبْصِرَةً وَذِكْرَىٰ لِكُلِّ عَبْدٍ مُنِيبٍ

˹all as˺ an insight and a reminder to every servant who turns ˹to Allah˺.

Allah created the heavens and the earth and what is in them of great signs, a lesson to be seen with the eyes of the blind of ignorance, and a reminder for every humble servant, fearful, and turning back to Allah, the Mighty and Majestic.

These signs in the heavens and earth are meant to be a lesson for those who are insightful and turn to Allah.

Verse 9

وَنَزَّلْنَا مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً مُبَارَكًا فَأَنْبَتْنَا بِهِ جَنَّاتٍ وَحَبَّ الْحَصِيدِ

And We sent down from the sky rain with many benefits, and We caused to grow thereby gardens with many trees, and the grain of the harvested crops.

Allah sends down blessed rain from the sky that brings forth gardens and grains.

Verse 10

وَالنَّخْلَ بَاسِقَاتٍ لَهَا طَلْعٌ نَضِيدٌ

and towering palm trees ˹loaded˺ with clustered fruit,

And We caused the tall date palms to grow, which have layered spathes of dates, one on top of another.

The rain also grows tall palm trees with clustered fruit as a part of Allah's provision.

Verse 11

رِزْقًا لِلْعِبَادِ ۖ وَأَحْيَيْنَا بِهِ بَلْدَةً مَيْتًا ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ الْخُرُوجُ

˹as˺ a provision for ˹Our˺ servants. And with this ˹rain˺ We revive a lifeless land. Similar is the emergence ˹from the graves˺.

We caused this to grow as sustenance for the servants, which they eat from according to their needs, and We brought to life with this water that We sent down from the sky a town that had become barren and arid, with no crops or vegetation, just as We brought to life the dead land with this water, We will bring you forth on the Day of Resurrection alive after death.

Allah provides these as sustenance and revives lifeless land, showing how He will resurrect people on the Day of Judgment.

Verse 12

كَذَّبَتْ قَبْلَهُمْ قَوْمُ نُوحٍ وَأَصْحَابُ الرَّسِّ وَثَمُودُ

Before these polytheists from Quraysh, the people of Noah, the people of the well and Thamud, and Aad and Pharaoh and the people of Lot, and the people of the thicket, the people of Shuaib, and the people of Tubba the Himyarite, denied their messengers, so the threat that Allah promised them for their disbelief became due upon them.

Many past nations, like the people of Noah, the people of the Water-pit, and Thamud, denied the truth before.

Verse 13

وَعَادٌ وَفِرْعَوْنُ وَإِخْوَانُ لُوطٍ

Before these polytheists from Quraysh, the people of Noah, the people of the well and Thamud, and Aad and Pharaoh and the people of Lot, and the people of the thicket, the people of Shuaib, and the people of Tubba the Himyarite, denied their messengers, so the threat that Allah promised them for their disbelief became due upon them.

Past nations like Aad, Pharaoh, and the kin of Lot also rejected their messengers.

Verse 14

وَأَصْحَابُ الْأَيْكَةِ وَقَوْمُ تُبَّعٍ ۚ كُلٌّ كَذَّبَ الرُّسُلَ فَحَقَّ وَعِيدِ

the residents of the Forest, and the people of Tubba’. Each rejected ˹their˺ messenger, so My warning was fulfilled.

Before these polytheists from Quraysh, the people of Noah, the people of the well and Thamud, and Aad and Pharaoh and the people of Lot, and the people of the thicket, the people of Shuaib, and the people of Tubba the Himyarite, denied their messengers, so the threat that Allah promised them for their disbelief became due upon them.

The residents of the Forest and the people of Tubba all rejected their messengers, fulfilling Allah's warning.

Verse 15

أَفَعَيِينَا بِالْخَلْقِ الْأَوَّلِ ۚ بَلْ هُمْ فِي لَبْسٍ مِنْ خَلْقٍ جَدِيدٍ

Were We incapable of creating ˹them˺ the first time? In fact, they are in doubt about ˹their˺ re-creation.

Did We then become incapable of initiating the first creation that We created when it was nothing, so We become incapable of recreating them as a new creation after their annihilation? We are not incapable of that, but rather, We are able to do so, but they are in perplexity and doubt about the matter of resurrection and rebirth.

Allah questions if He was incapable of the first creation and emphasizes that they are in doubt about their re-creation.

Verse 16

وَلَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا الْإِنْسَانَ وَنَعْلَمُ مَا تُوَسْوِسُ بِهِ نَفْسُهُ ۖ وَنَحْنُ أَقْرَبُ إِلَيْهِ مِنْ حَبْلِ الْوَرِيدِ

Indeed, ˹it is˺ We ˹Who˺ created humankind and ˹fully˺ know what their souls whisper to them, and We are closer to them than ˹their˺ jugular vein.

And We have already created man and know what his soul whispers to him, and We are closer to him than [his] jugular vein (which is a vein in the neck connected to the heart).

Allah created humans and knows their inner thoughts. He is closer to each person than their jugular vein, signifying His absolute awareness.

Verse 17

إِذْ يَتَلَقَّى الْمُتَلَقِّيَانِ عَنِ الْيَمِينِ وَعَنِ الشِّمَالِ قَعِيدٌ

As the two recording-angels—˹one˺ sitting to the right, and ˹the other to˺ the left—note ˹everything˺,

When the two recording angels record, seated on the right and on the left, his deeds. The one on the right records the good deeds, and the one on the left records the evil deeds.

Two angels record every action: one on the right noting good deeds, and another on the left noting bad deeds.

Verse 18

مَا يَلْفِظُ مِنْ قَوْلٍ إِلَّا لَدَيْهِ رَقِيبٌ عَتِيدٌ

not a word does a person utter without having a ˹vigilant˺ observer ready ˹to write it down˺.

Man does not utter any word except that with him is an observer prepared [to record].

Every word uttered is recorded by an angel, emphasizing accountability for one's speech.

Verse 19

وَجَاءَتْ سَكْرَةُ الْمَوْتِ بِالْحَقِّ ۖ ذَٰلِكَ مَا كُنْتَ مِنْهُ تَحِيدُ

˹Ultimately,˺ with the throes of death will come the truth. This is what you were trying to escape!

And the agony of death came in truth, that is what you were trying to evade.

The reality of death will come with its struggles. It is the truth that people try to avoid.

Verse 20

وَنُفِخَ فِي الصُّورِ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ يَوْمُ الْوَعِيدِ

And the Trumpet will be blown. This is the Day ˹you were˺ warned of.

And the Horn will be blown. That is the Day of the threat [coming to pass].

The Trumpet will be blown, signaling the Day of Judgment, which was warned about by prophets.

Verse 21

And every soul will come with it a driver and a witness. One of them will drive it to the gathering place, and the other will bear witness to what it did in the world, of good and evil.

Each soul will be brought forward with an angel driving it and another witnessing its deeds.

Verse 22

لَقَدْ كُنْتَ فِي غَفْلَةٍ مِنْ هَٰذَا فَكَشَفْنَا عَنْكَ غِطَاءَكَ فَبَصَرُكَ الْيَوْمَ حَدِيدٌ

[It will be said], "You were in heedlessness of this, and We have removed from you your cover, so your sight, this Day, is sharp."

Those who denied the truth will realize it on the Day of Judgment. Their vision will become clear, acknowledging what they disregarded.

Verse 23

وَقَالَ قَرِينُهُ هَٰذَا مَا لَدَيَّ عَتِيدٌ

And his companion will say, "This is what I have prepared, ready present."

The angel assigned to each person will present the record of their deeds ready for judgment.

Verse 24

أَلْقِيَا فِي جَهَنَّمَ كُلَّ كَفَّارٍ عَنِيدٍ

˹It will be said to both angels,˺ “Throw into Hell every stubborn disbeliever,

Allah will say to the two angels, the driver and the witness, after He separates between the creatures: Throw into Hell every stubbornly disbelieving denier that Allah is the true God, many times of disbelief and denial, opposing the truth, preventing the fulfillment of his financial obligations, transgressing against the servants of Allah and His limits, doubting His promise and threat, who associated partners with Allah, worshipping other creations besides Him, so cast him into the severe torment of Hell.

Allah commands angels to throw every stubborn disbeliever into Hell. These are people who rejected truth, hindered good, and doubted.

Verse 25

مَنَّاعٍ لِلْخَيْرِ مُعْتَدٍ مُرِيبٍ

withholder of good, transgressor, and doubter,

Allah will say to the two angels, the driver and the witness, after He separates between the creatures: Throw into Hell every stubbornly disbelieving denier that Allah is the true God, many times of disbelief and denial, opposing the truth, preventing the fulfillment of his financial obligations, transgressing against the servants of Allah and His limits, doubting His promise and threat, who associated partners with Allah, worshipping other creations besides Him, so cast him into the severe torment of Hell.

Further description of those who deserve Hell, mentioning they were deniers, transgressors, and doubters.

Verse 26

الَّذِي جَعَلَ مَعَ اللَّهِ إِلَٰهًا آخَرَ فَأَلْقِيَاهُ فِي الْعَذَابِ الشَّدِيدِ

who set up another god with Allah. So cast them into the severe punishment.”

Allah will say to the two angels, the driver and the witness, after He separates between the creatures: Throw into Hell every stubbornly disbelieving denier that Allah is the true God, many times of disbelief and denial, opposing the truth, preventing the fulfillment of his financial obligations, transgressing against the servants of Allah and His limits, doubting His promise and threat, who associated partners with Allah, worshipping other creations besides Him, so cast him into the severe torment of Hell.

These disbelievers associated partners with Allah. They will be cast into the severe punishment.

Verse 27

Verse 28

Allah will say, "Do not dispute before Me, and I had already presented to you the warning to anyone who disbelieves in Me and disobeys Me."

Verse 29

مَا يُبَدَّلُ الْقَوْلُ لَدَيَّ وَمَا أَنَا بِظَلَّامٍ لِلْعَبِيدِ

Allah's word will not change, and He is never unjust to His creation.

Verse 30

Allah will ask Hell if it is full, and Hell will ask for more. This shows the vastness of hell and the number of disbelievers.

Verse 31

Verse 38

Verse 45

نَحْنُ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا يَقُولُونَ ۖ وَمَا أَنْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ بِجَبَّارٍ ۖ فَذَكِّرْ بِالْقُرْآنِ مَنْ يَخَافُ وَعِيدِ

We know best what they say. And you ˹O Prophet˺ are not ˹there˺ to compel them ˹to believe˺. So remind with the Quran ˹only˺ those who fear My warning.

We know best what these polytheists say of slander against Allah and denial of His signs, and you, [O Messenger], are not over them a controller, to compel them to Islam; but you were sent only as a deliverer. So remind by the Quran those who fear My threat; for he who does not fear the threat does not remember.

Allah knows best what the disbelievers say, and the Prophet's role is to remind those who fear Allah's warning using the Quran, not to force belief.
