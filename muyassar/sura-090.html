Sura 090

Verse 1

لَا أُقْسِمُ بِهَٰذَا الْبَلَدِ

I do swear by this city ˹of Mecca˺—

Allah swears by this sacred city, which is "Mecca," and you -O Prophet- are residing in this "sacred city," and He swears by the father of mankind -who is Adam, peace be upon him- and what descended from him of offspring; We have created man in hardship and toil from the struggle of the world.

Allah swears by the sacred city of Mecca.

Verse 2

وَأَنْتَ حِلٌّ بِهَٰذَا الْبَلَدِ

even though you ˹O Prophet˺ are subject to abuse in this city—

Allah swears by this sacred city, which is "Mecca," and you -O Prophet- are residing in this "sacred city," and He swears by the father of mankind -who is Adam, peace be upon him- and what descended from him of offspring; We have created man in hardship and toil from the struggle of the world.

Even though the Prophet Muhammad is subject to hardship there.

Verse 3

وَوَالِدٍ وَمَا وَلَدَ

and by every parent and ˹their˺ child!

Allah swears by this sacred city, which is "Mecca," and you -O Prophet- are residing in this "sacred city," and He swears by the father of mankind -who is Adam, peace be upon him- and what descended from him of offspring; We have created man in hardship and toil from the struggle of the world.

Allah also swears by every parent and their child, highlighting human lineage.

Verse 4

لَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا الْإِنْسَانَ فِي كَبَدٍ

Indeed, We have created humankind in ˹constant˺ struggle.

Allah swears by this sacred city, which is "Mecca," and you -O Prophet- are residing in this "sacred city," and He swears by the father of mankind -who is Adam, peace be upon him- and what descended from him of offspring; We have created man in hardship and toil from the struggle of the world.

Humankind is created to face struggle and hardship in this life.

Verse 5

أَيَحْسَبُ أَنْ لَنْ يَقْدِرَ عَلَيْهِ أَحَدٌ

Do they think that no one has power over them,

Does he think that because of what he has collected of wealth, that Allah will not overpower him?

Does man think he is beyond Allah's power?

Verse 6

يَقُولُ أَهْلَكْتُ مَالًا لُبَدًا

boasting, “I have wasted enormous wealth!”?

He says boastfully: I have spent much wealth. Does he think in this action that Allah Almighty does not see him and will not hold him accountable for the small and the large?

Some boast of wasting wealth, not realizing they are accountable.

Verse 7

أَيَحْسَبُ أَنْ لَمْ يَرَهُ أَحَدٌ

Do they think that no one sees them?

He says boastfully: I have spent much wealth. Does he think in this action that Allah Almighty does not see him and will not hold him accountable for the small and the large?

Do they think that Allah does not see them?

Verse 8

أَلَمْ نَجْعَلْ لَهُ عَيْنَيْنِ

Have We not given them two eyes,

Have We not made for him two eyes by which he sees, and a tongue and two lips by which he speaks, and have We not guided him to the two ways of good and evil?

Allah has given humans eyes to see.

Verse 9

وَلِسَانًا وَشَفَتَيْنِ

a tongue, and two lips;

Have We not made for him two eyes by which he sees, and a tongue and two lips by which he speaks, and have We not guided him to the two ways of good and evil?

And a tongue and lips to speak.

Verse 10

وَهَدَيْنَاهُ النَّجْدَيْنِ

and shown them the two ways ˹of right and wrong˺?

Have We not made for him two eyes by which he sees, and a tongue and two lips by which he speaks, and have We not guided him to the two ways of good and evil?

And Allah has shown them the two ways, good and evil.

Verse 11

فَلَا اقْتَحَمَ الْعَقَبَةَ

If only they had attempted the challenging path ˹of goodness instead˺!

So, why did he not overcome the hardship of the Hereafter by spending his wealth, thus gaining security?

Why did they not choose the challenging path of goodness?

Verse 12

وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا الْعَقَبَةُ

And what will make you realize what ˹attempting˺ the challenging path is?

And what will make you know what is the hardship of the Hereafter, and what helps to overcome it?

What will make you understand what the challenging path is?

Verse 13

فَكُّ رَقَبَةٍ

It is to free a slave,

It is the freeing of a believing slave from the bondage of slavery.

It includes freeing a slave.

Verse 14

أَوْ إِطْعَامٌ فِي يَوْمٍ ذِي مَسْغَبَةٍ

or to give food in times of famine

Or feeding on a day of severe famine an orphan among relatives, in which the virtue of charity and the ties of kinship are combined, or a poor person who has nothing.

And feeding the hungry during famine.

Verse 15

يَتِيمًا ذَا مَقْرَبَةٍ

to an orphaned relative

Or feeding on a day of severe famine an orphan among relatives, in which the virtue of charity and the ties of kinship are combined, or a poor person who has nothing.

Especially when they are an orphaned relative.

Verse 16

أَوْ مِسْكِينًا ذَا مَتْرَبَةٍ

or to a poor person in distress,

Or feeding on a day of severe famine an orphan among relatives, in which the virtue of charity and the ties of kinship are combined, or a poor person who has nothing.

Or a poor person in distress.

Verse 17

ثُمَّ كَانَ مِنَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالصَّبْرِ وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالْمَرْحَمَةِ

and—above all—to be one of those who have faith and urge each other to perseverance and urge each other to compassion.

Then, he was with the doing of what was mentioned of good deeds among those who sincerely believed in Allah, and exhorted one another to patience in obeying Allah and refraining from disobeying Him, and they exhorted one another to have mercy on the creation.

It also includes being among those who believe, encourage patience, and have compassion.

Verse 18

أُولَٰئِكَ أَصْحَابُ الْمَيْمَنَةِ

These are the people of the right.

Those who did these deeds, they are the companions of the right hand, who will be taken on the Day of Resurrection on the right hand to Paradise.

These are the people of the right hand, destined for Paradise.

Verse 19

وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِآيَاتِنَا هُمْ أَصْحَابُ الْمَشْأَمَةِ

As for those who deny Our signs, they are the people of the left.

And those who disbelieved in the Qur’an are those who will be taken on the Day of Resurrection on the left hand to the Fire.

Those who deny Allah's signs are the people of the left, destined for Hell.

Verse 20

عَلَيْهِمْ نَارٌ مُؤْصَدَةٌ

The Fire will be sealed over them.

Their recompense is Hell, which is completely sealed and closed upon them.

The Fire will be sealed over them.
