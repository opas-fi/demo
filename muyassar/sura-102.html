Sura 102

Verse 1

أَلْهَاكُمُ التَّكَاثُرُ

Competition for more ˹gains˺ diverts you ˹from Allah˺,

The boastful rivalry for increase in wealth and children has distracted you from obeying Allah.

The pursuit of worldly gains and rivalry for more distracts people from their obligations to Allah.

Verse 2

حَتَّىٰ زُرْتُمُ الْمَقَابِرَ

until you end up in ˹your˺ graves.

And your preoccupation with that continued until you came to the graveyards and were buried in them.

This pursuit continues until death, when individuals end up in their graves.

Verse 3

كَلَّا سَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ

But no! You will soon come to know.

This is not how you should be distracted by the rivalry for increase in wealth. You will soon know that the Hereafter is better for you.

This verse warns that people will soon realize the error of their ways and the consequences of being distracted by worldly pursuits.

Verse 4

ثُمَّ كَلَّا سَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ

Again, no! You will soon come to know.

Then beware! You will soon know the evil consequences of being preoccupied with it.

This verse serves as a second warning, stating that they will soon come to know the evil consequences of being preoccupied with worldly matters.

Verse 5

كَلَّا لَوْ تَعْلَمُونَ عِلْمَ الْيَقِينِ

Indeed, if you were to know ˹your fate˺ with certainty, ˹you would have acted differently˺.

This is not how you should be distracted by the rivalry for increase in wealth. If you knew with true knowledge, you would have restrained yourselves and hastened to save yourselves from destruction. You will surely see the Hellfire, then you will surely see it with certainty, and then you will surely be asked on the Day of Resurrection about every kind of pleasure.

This verse emphasizes that if people were certain of their fate, they would restrain themselves from worldly distractions and hasten towards salvation.

Verse 6

لَتَرَوُنَّ الْجَحِيمَ

˹But˺ you will surely see the Hellfire.

This is not how you should be distracted by the rivalry for increase in wealth. If you knew with true knowledge, you would have restrained yourselves and hastened to save yourselves from destruction. You will surely see the Hellfire, then you will surely see it with certainty, and then you will surely be asked on the Day of Resurrection about every kind of pleasure.

This verse states that they will surely witness the Hellfire.

Verse 7

ثُمَّ لَتَرَوُنَّهَا عَيْنَ الْيَقِينِ

Again, you will surely see it with the eye of certainty.

This is not how you should be distracted by the rivalry for increase in wealth. If you knew with true knowledge, you would have restrained yourselves and hastened to save yourselves from destruction. You will surely see the Hellfire, then you will surely see it with certainty, and then you will surely be asked on the Day of Resurrection about every kind of pleasure.

This verse emphasizes that their witnessing of Hellfire will be with absolute certainty.

Verse 8

ثُمَّ لَتُسْأَلُنَّ يَوْمَئِذٍ عَنِ النَّعِيمِ

Then, on that Day, you will definitely be questioned about ˹your worldly˺ pleasures.

This is not how you should be distracted by the rivalry for increase in wealth. If you knew with true knowledge, you would have restrained yourselves and hastened to save yourselves from destruction. You will surely see the Hellfire, then you will surely see it with certainty, and then you will surely be asked on the Day of Resurrection about every kind of pleasure.

On the Day of Judgment, people will be questioned about the worldly pleasures they indulged in.
