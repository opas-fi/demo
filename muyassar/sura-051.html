Verse 50

So ˹proclaim, O Prophet˺: “Flee to Allah! I am truly sent by Him with a clear warning to you.

So flee - O people - from the punishment of Allah to His mercy by believing in Him and His Messenger, and following His command and acting in His obedience. Indeed, I am to you a clear warner. And the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, when something distressed him, would turn to prayer, and this is fleeing to Allah.

Prophet Muhammad is commanded to tell people to flee to God for refuge.

Verse 51

وَلَا تَجْعَلُوا مَعَ اللَّهِ إِلَٰهًا آخَرَ ۖ إِنِّي لَكُمْ مِنْهُ نَذِيرٌ مُبِينٌ

And do not set up another god with Allah. I am truly sent by Him with a clear warning to you.”

And do not make with Allah another object of worship. Indeed, I am to you from Allah a clear warner.

People are warned not to associate any partners with God; this message is a clear warning.

Verse 52

كَذَٰلِكَ مَا أَتَى الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ مِنْ رَسُولٍ إِلَّا قَالُوا سَاحِرٌ أَوْ مَجْنُونٌ

Similarly, no messenger came to those before them without being told: “A magician or a madman!”

Just as the Quraysh denied their Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, and said, "He is a poet, a sorcerer, or a madman," the nations that denied their messengers before Quraysh did the same, so Allah inflicted His punishment upon them.

This verse highlights a pattern: messengers of God have historically been accused of being magicians or madmen by those who reject their message.

Verse 53

أَتَوَاصَوْا بِهِ ۚ بَلْ هُمْ قَوْمٌ طَاغُونَ

Have they passed this ˹cliché˺ down to one another? In fact, they have ˹all˺ been a transgressing people.

Did the former and the latter generations enjoin upon one another the denial of the Messenger when they all said that? Rather, they are a transgressing people whose hearts and deeds are similar in disbelief and transgression, so their later generations said that, just as their predecessors said it.

The verse suggests that disbelief and rejection of messengers are recurring patterns across generations, stemming from a shared tendency to transgress.

Verse 54

فَتَوَلَّ عَنْهُمْ فَمَا أَنْتَ بِمَلُومٍ

So ˹now˺ turn away from them ˹O Prophet˺, for you will not be blamed.

So turn away, [O Messenger], from the polytheists until the command of Allah comes concerning them. You are not to be blamed by anyone, for you have delivered what you were sent with.

The Prophet is instructed to turn away from those who disbelieve. He will not be blamed for their rejection.

Verse 55

وَذَكِّرْ فَإِنَّ الذِّكْرَىٰ تَنْفَعُ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

But ˹continue to˺ remind. For certainly reminders benefit the believers.

And despite your turning away from them, [O Messenger], and not paying attention to their discouragement, continue to call to Allah and to admonish those to whom you were sent. For the reminder and admonition benefit the people of believing hearts, and in them is the establishment of proof against those who turn away.

The Prophet is commanded to continue reminding and admonishing, for reminders benefit those with believing hearts.

Verse 56

وَمَا خَلَقْتُ الْجِنَّ وَالْإِنْسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ

I did not create jinn and humans except to worship Me.

And I did not create the jinn and mankind except to a noble purpose, which is to worship Me alone, not anyone else besides Me.

The purpose of creating jinn and humans is solely for the worship of God.

Verse 57

مَا أُرِيدُ مِنْهُمْ مِنْ رِزْقٍ وَمَا أُرِيدُ أَنْ يُطْعِمُونِ

I seek no provision from them, nor do I need them to feed Me.

I do not want from them any provision, nor do I want them to feed Me, for I am the Provider, the Giver. He, glory be to Him, is not in need of creation; rather, they are the ones who are in need of Him in all their states. He is their Creator and Provider, and He is free of need from them.

God does not need provisions or sustenance from His creation, rather He is the Provider.

Verse 58

إِنَّ اللَّهَ هُوَ الرَّزَّاقُ ذُو الْقُوَّةِ الْمَتِينُ

Indeed, Allah ˹alone˺ is the Supreme Provider—Lord of all Power, Ever Mighty.

Indeed, Allah alone is the Provider for His creation, the One who provides for their sustenance, the Possessor of strength, the Powerful, who is not conquered or overcome. All power and strength belong to Him.

God is the ultimate Provider, possessing all strength and power.

Verse 59

فَإِنَّ لِلَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا ذَنُوبًا مِثْلَ ذَنُوبِ أَصْحَابِهِمْ فَلَا يَسْتَعْجِلُونِ

The wrongdoers will certainly have a share ˹of the torment˺ like that of their predecessors. So do not let them ask Me to hasten ˹it˺.

Indeed, for those who did wrong by denying the Messenger Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, there is a portion of Allah's punishment descending upon them like the portion of their companions who passed away before them. So let them not hasten the punishment; it will surely come to them.

Those who do wrong will face a similar punishment to that of their predecessors, and should not ask to hasten it.

Verse 60

فَوَيْلٌ لِلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا مِنْ يَوْمِهِمُ الَّذِي يُوعَدُونَ

Woe then to the disbelievers when they face their Day which they are warned of!

So destruction and misery to those who disbelieved in Allah and His Messenger on their Day which they are promised with the descending of punishment upon them, and that is the Day of Resurrection.

Woe to those who disbelieve when they face the day of punishment they were warned about.
