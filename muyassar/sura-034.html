Sura 034

Verse 1

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ فِي الْآخِرَةِ ۚ وَهُوَ الْحَكِيمُ الْخَبِيرُ

All praise is for Allah, to Whom belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. And praise be to Him in the Hereafter. He is the All-Wise, All-Aware.

Praise be to Allah for His attributes, which are all attributes of perfection, and for His apparent and hidden blessings, both religious and worldly. To Him belongs the dominion of all that is in the heavens and the earth, and to Him belongs all praise in the Hereafter. He is the Wise in His actions, the All-Aware of the affairs of His creation.

This verse declares that all praise belongs to Allah, who owns everything in the heavens and the earth. He is praised in this life and the Hereafter because of His wisdom and knowledge.

Verse 2

يَعْلَمُ مَا يَلِجُ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَمَا يَخْرُجُ مِنْهَا وَمَا يَنْزِلُ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ وَمَا يَعْرُجُ فِيهَا ۚ وَهُوَ الرَّحِيمُ الْغَفُورُ

He knows whatever goes into the earth and whatever comes out of it, and whatever descends from the sky and whatever ascends into it. And He is the Most Merciful, All-Forgiving.

He knows all that enters the earth of drops of water, and what comes out of it of plants, minerals, and water, and what descends from the sky of rain, angels, and books, and what ascends to it of angels and the deeds of creation. And He is the Most Merciful to His servants, so He does not hasten the punishment of those who disobey Him, the Forgiving to the sins of those who repent to Him and rely on Him.

Allah knows everything that goes into and comes out of the earth, as well as what descends from and ascends to the sky. He is Merciful and Forgiving to His servants.

Verse 3

وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَا تَأْتِينَا السَّاعَةُ ۖ قُلْ بَلَىٰ وَرَبِّي لَتَأْتِيَنَّكُمْ عَالِمِ الْغَيْبِ ۖ لَا يَعْزُبُ عَنْهُ مِثْقَالُ ذَرَّةٍ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَلَا فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا أَصْغَرُ مِنْ ذَٰلِكَ وَلَا أَكْبَرُ إِلَّا فِي كِتَابٍ مُبِينٍ

The disbelievers say, “The Hour will never come to us.” Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Yes—by my Lord, the Knower of the unseen—it will certainly come to you!” Not ˹even˺ an atom’s weight is hidden from Him in the heavens or the earth; nor anything smaller or larger than that, but is ˹written˺ in a perfect Record.

And those who disbelieve and deny the Resurrection said, "The Hour will not come to us." Say to them, [O Prophet], "Yes, by my Lord, it will surely come to you. But no one knows the time of its coming except Allah, Knower of the unseen, from whom is not hidden the weight of a small ant in the heavens and the earth, nor anything smaller than that or larger, but it is recorded in a clear book, which is the Preserved Tablet, so that He may reward those who believed in Allah, followed His Messenger, and did righteous deeds. Those will have forgiveness of their sins and a generous provision, which is Paradise."

The disbelievers doubt the coming of the Hour (Day of Judgment), but Allah, the Knower of the unseen, confirms that it will certainly come. Nothing is hidden from Him, and everything is recorded.

Verse 4

لِيَجْزِيَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ لَهُمْ مَغْفِرَةٌ وَرِزْقٌ كَرِيمٌ

So He may reward those who believe and do good. It is they who will have forgiveness and an honourable provision.

And those who disbelieve and deny the Resurrection said, "The Hour will not come to us." Say to them, [O Prophet], "Yes, by my Lord, it will surely come to you. But no one knows the time of its coming except Allah, Knower of the unseen, from whom is not hidden the weight of a small ant in the heavens and the earth, nor anything smaller than that or larger, but it is recorded in a clear book, which is the Preserved Tablet, so that He may reward those who believed in Allah, followed His Messenger, and did righteous deeds. Those will have forgiveness of their sins and a generous provision, which is Paradise."

Allah will reward those who believe and do good with forgiveness and a generous provision (Paradise).

Verse 5

وَالَّذِينَ سَعَوْا فِي آيَاتِنَا مُعَاجِزِينَ أُولَٰئِكَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ مِنْ رِجْزٍ أَلِيمٌ

As for those who strive to discredit Our revelations, it is they who will suffer the ˹worst˺ torment of agonizing pain.

And those who strive to hinder from the path of Allah and deny His messengers and invalidate Our signs, opposing Allah and overcoming His command, those will have the worst and most painful punishment.

Those who strive to discredit Allah's revelations will face a painful punishment.

Verse 6

وَيَرَى الَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْعِلْمَ الَّذِي أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْكَ مِنْ رَبِّكَ هُوَ الْحَقَّ وَيَهْدِي إِلَىٰ صِرَاطِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَمِيدِ

Those gifted with knowledge ˹clearly˺ see that what has been revealed to you from your Lord ˹O Prophet˺ is the truth, and that it guides to the Path of the Almighty, the Praiseworthy.

And those who have been given knowledge know that the Quran which has been revealed to you from your Lord is the truth, and it guides to the path of Allah, the Almighty who cannot be overpowered nor resisted, but has subdued and overcome everything, the Praiseworthy in His words, actions, and law.

Those with knowledge recognize that the Quran revealed to Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) is the truth and guides to the path of the Almighty and Praiseworthy Allah.

Verse 7

وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا هَلْ نَدُلُّكُمْ عَلَىٰ رَجُلٍ يُنَبِّئُكُمْ إِذَا مُزِّقْتُمْ كُلَّ مُمَزَّقٍ إِنَّكُمْ لَفِي خَلْقٍ جَدِيدٍ

The disbelievers say ˹mockingly to one another˺, “Shall we show you a man who claims that when you have been utterly disintegrated you will be raised as a new creation?

And those who disbelieved said to each other in mockery, "Shall we show you a man (meaning Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him) who tells you that when you have died and your bodies have been completely scattered, you will indeed be brought back to life and resurrected from your graves?" They said that out of excessive denial.

The disbelievers mockingly question how they can be resurrected after their bodies have disintegrated. This highlights their denial of the Day of Resurrection.

Verse 8

أَفْتَرَىٰ عَلَى اللَّهِ كَذِبًا أَمْ بِهِ جِنَّةٌ ۗ بَلِ الَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِالْآخِرَةِ فِي الْعَذَابِ وَالضَّلَالِ الْبَعِيدِ

Has he fabricated a lie against Allah or is he insane?” In fact, those who do not believe in the Hereafter are bound for torment and have strayed farthest ˹from the truth˺.

Is this man fabricating a lie against Allah, or is he possessed by madness, so he speaks without knowing? The matter is not as the disbelievers said, but Muhammad is the most truthful of the truthful. And those who do not believe in the Resurrection and do not work for it are in perpetual torment in the Hereafter, and in far straying from the truth in this world.

This verse addresses the disbelievers' accusations that the Prophet Muhammad was either fabricating lies or insane. It asserts that those who deny the Hereafter will face severe punishment and are far from the truth.

Verse 9

أَفَلَمْ يَرَوْا إِلَىٰ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ إِنْ نَشَأْ نَخْسِفْ بِهِمُ الْأَرْضَ أَوْ نُسْقِطْ عَلَيْهِمْ كِسَفًا مِنَ السَّمَاءِ ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَةً لِكُلِّ عَبْدٍ مُنِيبٍ

Have they not then seen all that surrounds them of the heavens and the earth? If We willed, We could cause the earth to swallow them up, or cause ˹deadly˺ pieces of the sky to fall upon them. Surely in this is a sign for every servant who turns ˹to Allah˺.

Do not these disbelievers, who do not believe in the Hereafter, see the great power of Allah in what is before them and behind them of the sky and the earth, which amazes the minds, and that they have encompassed them? If We will, We can cause the earth to swallow them up, as We did to Korah, or We can send down on them pieces of torment, as We did to the people of Shu'aib, for the sky rained down fire on them and burned them. Indeed, in that which We have mentioned of Our power is a clear sign for every servant who turns back to his Lord in repentance, and acknowledges His Oneness, and is sincere to Him in worship.

This verse urges disbelievers to reflect on the signs of Allah's power in the heavens and the earth. It reminds them that Allah could easily destroy them, and that these signs should encourage repentance and turning to Him.

Verse 10

وَلَقَدْ آتَيْنَا دَاوُودَ مِنَّا فَضْلًا ۖ يَا جِبَالُ أَوِّبِي مَعَهُ وَالطَّيْرَ ۖ وَأَلَنَّا لَهُ الْحَدِيدَ

Indeed, We granted David a ˹great˺ privilege from Us, ˹commanding:˺ “O mountains! Echo his hymns! And the birds as well.” We made iron mouldable for him,

And We had certainly given David prophethood, and a book, and knowledge, and We said to the mountains and the birds: Glorify with him, and We softened iron for him, so it was like dough, to be manipulated as he wished.

This verse speaks of the blessings granted to Prophet David, including prophethood, knowledge, and the ability to soften iron. It also mentions that mountains and birds echoed his praises to Allah.

Verse 11

أَنِ اعْمَلْ سَابِغَاتٍ وَقَدِّرْ فِي السَّرْدِ ۖ وَاعْمَلُوا صَالِحًا ۖ إِنِّي بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ بَصِيرٌ

instructing: “Make full-length armour, ˹perfectly˺ balancing the links. And work righteousness ˹O family of David!˺. Indeed, I am All-Seeing of what you do.”

Make full, wide coats of armor, and measure the nails in the rings of the armor, so do not make the ring small, so that it weakens, and the armor is not able to defend, nor make it large, so that it becomes heavy on its wearer, and work, O David, you and your family, in obedience to Allah. Indeed, I am Seeing of what you do; nothing is hidden from Me.

Allah instructed Prophet David to create well-made armor. This highlights the importance of both worldly skills and righteousness for David and his family. Allah emphasizes His constant observation of their deeds.

Verse 12

وَلِسُلَيْمَانَ الرِّيحَ غُدُوُّهَا شَهْرٌ وَرَوَاحُهَا شَهْرٌ ۖ وَأَسَلْنَا لَهُ عَيْنَ الْقِطْرِ ۖ وَمِنَ الْجِنِّ مَنْ يَعْمَلُ بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِ ۖ وَمَنْ يَزِغْ مِنْهُمْ عَنْ أَمْرِنَا نُذِقْهُ مِنْ عَذَابِ السَّعِيرِ

And to Solomon ˹We subjected˺ the wind: its morning stride was a month’s journey and so was its evening stride. And We caused a stream of molten copper to flow for him, and ˹We subjected˺ some of the jinn to work under him by his Lord’s Will. And whoever of them deviated from Our command, We made them taste the torment of the blaze.

And We subjected the wind to Solomon, it traveled from the beginning of the day to its middle a month's journey, and from the middle of the day to the night a month's journey by usual travel, and We made the copper flow for him as water flows, he could make with it what he wished, and We subjected to him from the jinn, those who work before him by the permission of his Lord, and whoever among them deviates from Our command that We commanded him regarding obedience to Solomon, We will make him taste the torment of the blazing Fire.

This verse describes the powers Allah granted to Prophet Solomon, including control over the wind and the ability to use molten copper. It also mentions that jinn were made to work for him, and those who disobeyed were punished.

Verse 13

يَعْمَلُونَ لَهُ مَا يَشَاءُ مِنْ مَحَارِيبَ وَتَمَاثِيلَ وَجِفَانٍ كَالْجَوَابِ وَقُدُورٍ رَاسِيَاتٍ ۚ اعْمَلُوا آلَ دَاوُودَ شُكْرًا ۚ وَقَلِيلٌ مِنْ عِبَادِيَ الشَّكُورُ

They made for him whatever he desired of sanctuaries, statues, basins as large as reservoirs, and cooking pots fixed ˹into the ground˺. ˹We ordered:˺ “Work gratefully, O family of David!” ˹Only˺ a few of My servants are ˹truly˺ grateful.

The jinn made for Solomon what he wished of temples for worship, and images of copper and glass, and large basins like pools in which water gathers, and firm pots that do not move from their places because of their size. And We said, O family of David: Work in gratitude to Allah for what He has given you, and that is by obeying Him and fulfilling His commands, and few of My servants are grateful to Allah. And David and his family were among the few.

The jinn constructed many impressive things for Prophet Solomon, demonstrating the extent of his power. The verse concludes with a reminder to be grateful to Allah, noting that few of His servants are truly so.

Verse 14

فَلَمَّا قَضَيْنَا عَلَيْهِ الْمَوْتَ مَا دَلَّهُمْ عَلَىٰ مَوْتِهِ إِلَّا دَابَّةُ الْأَرْضِ تَأْكُلُ مِنْسَأَتَهُ ۖ فَلَمَّا خَرَّ تَبَيَّنَتِ الْجِنُّ أَنْ لَوْ كَانُوا يَعْلَمُونَ الْغَيْبَ مَا لَبِثُوا فِي الْعَذَابِ الْمُهِينِ

When We decreed Solomon’s death, nothing indicated to the ˹subjected˺ jinn that he was dead except the termites eating away his staff. So when he collapsed, the jinn realized that if they had ˹really˺ known the unseen, they would not have remained in ˹such˺ humiliating servitude.

So when We decreed death for Solomon, nothing indicated his death to the jinn except the woodworm eating his staff that he was leaning on, so Solomon fell to the ground. At that time, the jinn knew that if they had known the unseen, they would not have remained in the humiliating torment and hard labor for Solomon, thinking that he was still alive. And in the verse is a refutation of the belief of some people that the jinn know the unseen, for if they had known the unseen, they would have known of Solomon's death, peace be upon him, and they would not have remained in the humiliating torment.

This verse recounts the death of Prophet Solomon and how the jinn were unaware of it until termites ate his staff, causing him to fall. This event clarified that jinn do not know the unseen and refuted the belief that they possess such knowledge.

Verse 15

لَقَدْ كَانَ لِسَبَإٍ فِي مَسْكَنِهِمْ آيَةٌ ۖ جَنَّتَانِ عَنْ يَمِينٍ وَشِمَالٍ ۖ كُلُوا مِنْ رِزْقِ رَبِّكُمْ وَاشْكُرُوا لَهُ ۚ بَلْدَةٌ طَيِّبَةٌ وَرَبٌّ غَفُورٌ

Indeed, there was a sign for ˹the tribe of˺ Sheba in their homeland: two orchards—one to the right and the other to the left. ˹They were told:˺ “Eat from the provision of your Lord, and be grateful to Him. ˹Yours is˺ a good land and a forgiving Lord.”

For the people of Sheba in their dwelling place, in "Yemen", there was a sign [of Our grace]: two gardens on the right and the left. “Eat from the provision of your Lord and be grateful to Him. A good land and a forgiving Lord.”

The people of Sheba were given fertile lands with two orchards. They were blessed and told to be grateful to Allah for their good land and forgiving Lord.

Verse 16

فَأَعْرَضُوا فَأَرْسَلْنَا عَلَيْهِمْ سَيْلَ الْعَرِمِ وَبَدَّلْنَاهُمْ بِجَنَّتَيْهِمْ جَنَّتَيْنِ ذَوَاتَيْ أُكُلٍ خَمْطٍ وَأَثْلٍ وَشَيْءٍ مِنْ سِدْرٍ قَلِيلٍ

But they turned away. So We sent against them a devastating flood, and replaced their orchards with two others producing bitter fruit, fruitless bushes, and a few ˹sparse˺ thorny trees.

But they turned away [from Allah's commands and gratitude] and denied the messengers, so We sent upon them the devastating flood that destroyed the dam and submerged the gardens. And We substituted for their two fruit-bearing gardens, two gardens bearing bitter fruit, which is a bitter and unpleasant tasting fruit, and tamarisk trees, which are trees similar to willow with no fruit, and a few jujube trees with many thorns. That change from good to evil was because of their disbelief and their lack of gratitude for the blessings of Allah. And We do not punish with this severe punishment except the extremely ungrateful, who are recompensed with the like of their actions.

However, they turned away from gratitude, so Allah sent a flood that destroyed their orchards and replaced them with bitter, thorny vegetation as a consequence.

Verse 17

ذَٰلِكَ جَزَيْنَاهُمْ بِمَا كَفَرُوا ۖ وَهَلْ نُجَازِي إِلَّا الْكَفُورَ

This is how We rewarded them for their ingratitude. Would We ever punish ˹anyone in such a way˺ except the ungrateful?

But they turned away [from Allah's commands and gratitude] and denied the messengers, so We sent upon them the devastating flood that destroyed the dam and submerged the gardens. And We substituted for their two fruit-bearing gardens, two gardens bearing bitter fruit, which is a bitter and unpleasant tasting fruit, and tamarisk trees, which are trees similar to willow with no fruit, and a few jujube trees with many thorns. That change from good to evil was because of their disbelief and their lack of gratitude for the blessings of Allah. And We do not punish with this severe punishment except the extremely ungrateful, who are recompensed with the like of their actions.

This verse emphasizes that their punishment was a direct result of their ingratitude. Only those who are ungrateful are punished in this manner.

Verse 18

وَجَعَلْنَا بَيْنَهُمْ وَبَيْنَ الْقُرَى الَّتِي بَارَكْنَا فِيهَا قُرًى ظَاهِرَةً وَقَدَّرْنَا فِيهَا السَّيْرَ ۖ سِيرُوا فِيهَا لَيَالِيَ وَأَيَّامًا آمِنِينَ

We had also placed between them and the cities We showered with blessings ˹many small˺ towns within sight of one another. And We set moderate travel distances in between, ˹saying,˺ “Travel between them by day and night safely.”

And We placed between them - the people of Sheba, who were in "Yemen" - and the cities which We had blessed - which is "Sham" - continuous cities, one can see some from the other, and We made the journey between them a measured journey from place to place with no hardship. And We said to them: Travel in those cities at any time you wish, whether night or day, in safety not fearing an enemy or hunger or thirst.

Allah had also blessed them with easily accessible towns for safe travel, a sign of His favor and care for them.

Verse 19

فَقَالُوا رَبَّنَا بَاعِدْ بَيْنَ أَسْفَارِنَا وَظَلَمُوا أَنْفُسَهُمْ فَجَعَلْنَاهُمْ أَحَادِيثَ وَمَزَّقْنَاهُمْ كُلَّ مُمَزَّقٍ ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ لِكُلِّ صَبَّارٍ شَكُورٍ

But they said, “Our Lord! Make ˹the distances of˺ our journeys longer,” wronging themselves. So We reduced them to ˹cautionary˺ tales, and scattered them utterly. Surely in this are lessons for whoever is steadfast, grateful.

But because of their transgression, they became bored with comfort, safety and prosperity. They said, “Our Lord, make the distances between our cities greater, so that our journey between them becomes longer.” And they wronged themselves by their disbelief, so We destroyed them and made them an example and stories for those who come after them. And We dispersed them completely and their lands were destroyed. Indeed in what befell "Sheba" is a lesson for every patient one who endures hardship, grateful for the blessings of Allah Almighty.

Out of arrogance, they asked for longer travel distances and thus brought hardship upon themselves. This resulted in their dispersion and became cautionary tales for others.

Verse 20

وَلَقَدْ صَدَّقَ عَلَيْهِمْ إِبْلِيسُ ظَنَّهُ فَاتَّبَعُوهُ إِلَّا فَرِيقًا مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

Indeed, Iblîs’ assumption about them has come true, so they ˹all˺ follow him, except a group of ˹true˺ believers.

And Iblis had certainly thought about them, a conjecture not based on certainty, that he would mislead the children of Adam, and that they would obey him in disobeying Allah, so he found his thought true with them. They obeyed him and disobeyed their Lord, except for a group of believers in Allah. They remained steadfast in obeying Allah.

Iblis's assumption that he could mislead them turned out to be true, as most followed him, except for a small group of believers who remained steadfast.

Verse 21

وَمَا كَانَ لَهُ عَلَيْهِمْ مِنْ سُلْطَانٍ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَنْ يُؤْمِنُ بِالْآخِرَةِ مِمَّنْ هُوَ مِنْهَا فِي شَكٍّ ۗ وَرَبُّكَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ حَفِيظٌ

He does not have any authority over them, but ˹Our Will is˺ only to distinguish those who believe in the Hereafter from those who are in doubt about it. And your Lord is a ˹vigilant˺ Keeper over all things.”

And Iblis did not have any power over these disbelievers to compel them to disbelief, but it was Allah’s wisdom that required him to tempt the children of Adam in order to make manifest what He knew from eternity; to distinguish who believes in the Resurrection and recompense from those who are in doubt about it. And your Lord is a Guardian over all things, He preserves them and rewards them for it.

Iblis had no real power over them; his influence was a test to distinguish believers in the Hereafter from doubters. Allah is a guardian over all things.

Verse 22

قُلِ ادْعُوا الَّذِينَ زَعَمْتُمْ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ ۖ لَا يَمْلِكُونَ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَلَا فِي الْأَرْضِ وَمَا لَهُمْ فِيهِمَا مِنْ شِرْكٍ وَمَا لَهُ مِنْهُمْ مِنْ ظَهِيرٍ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Call upon those you claim ˹to be divine˺ besides Allah. They do not possess ˹even˺ an atom’s weight either in the heavens or the earth, nor do they have any share in ˹governing˺ them. Nor is any of them a helper to Him.”

Say - O Messenger - to the polytheists: “Invoke those you claim to be partners with Allah, whom you worship besides Him, from idols, angels and humans, and address them with your needs. Prophet Muhammad clarifies that he does not seek any worldly reward for his message. His reward is from Allah, who witnesses all things.

Verse 48

Prophet Muhammad states that Allah hurls the truth against falsehood, destroying it. Allah is the Knower of the unseen, and nothing is hidden from Him.

Verse 49

قُلْ جَاءَ الْحَقُّ وَمَا يُبْدِئُ الْبَاطِلُ وَمَا يُعِيدُ

This verse declares the triumph of truth and the inevitable disappearance of falsehood. It emphasizes the finality of truth's victory.

Verse 50

This verse highlights personal responsibility for guidance or misguidance. Guidance comes from Allah's revelation, underscoring His attributes of being All-Hearing and Ever Near.

Verse 51

This verse vividly describes the disbelievers' terror on Judgment Day, when they will have no escape and be seized swiftly.

Verse 52

This verse depicts the disbelievers' belated claim of belief in the Hereafter, which is futile since the opportunity for faith was in the world.

Verse 54
