Sura 012

Verse 1

الر ۚ تِلْكَ آيَاتُ الْكِتَابِ الْمُبِينِ

Alif-Lãm-Ra. These are the verses of the clear Book.

(Alif, Lam, Ra) The discussion of the disjointed letters has preceded at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah.

This verse begins Surah Yusuf with the letters *Alif-Lam-Ra*, which are among the disjointed letters whose meaning is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

إِنَّا أَنْزَلْنَاهُ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّا لَعَلَّكُمْ تَعْقِلُونَ

Indeed, We have sent it down as an Arabic Quran so that you may understand.

Indeed, We have sent it down as an Arabic Qur'an so that you - O Arabs - may understand its meanings and comprehend them, and act upon its guidance.

The Quran has been revealed in clear Arabic to make it understandable for the Arab people, so they can comprehend its meanings and guidance.

Verse 3

نَحْنُ نَقُصُّ عَلَيْكَ أَحْسَنَ الْقَصَصِ بِمَا أَوْحَيْنَا إِلَيْكَ هَٰذَا الْقُرْآنَ وَإِنْ كُنْتَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِ لَمِنَ الْغَافِلِينَ

We relate to you ˹O Prophet˺ the best of stories through Our revelation of this Quran, though before this you were totally unaware ˹of them˺.

We relate to you - O Messenger - the best of stories by Our inspiration to you, this Qur'an, and indeed you were, before its revelation to you, among those who were unaware of these news, not knowing anything about them.

Allah reveals the best of stories to the Prophet Muhammad through the Quran. Before this revelation, he was unaware of these events.

Verse 4

إِذْ قَالَ يُوسُفُ لِأَبِيهِ يَا أَبَتِ إِنِّي رَأَيْتُ أَحَدَ عَشَرَ كَوْكَبًا وَالشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ رَأَيْتُهُمْ لِي سَاجِدِينَ

˹Remember˺ when Joseph said to his father, “O my dear father! Indeed I dreamt of eleven stars, and the sun, and the moon—I saw them prostrating to me!”

Mention - O Messenger - to your people the saying of Joseph to his father: 'Indeed, I saw in a dream eleven stars, and the sun and the moon; I saw them prostrating to me.' And this vision was glad tidings of what Joseph, peace be upon him, attained of high status in this world and the Hereafter.

This verse recounts Prophet Yusuf's (Joseph's) dream in which eleven stars, the sun, and the moon prostrated to him. This dream was a sign of his future high status and influence.

Verse 5

قَالَ يَا بُنَيَّ لَا تَقْصُصْ رُؤْيَاكَ عَلَىٰ إِخْوَتِكَ فَيَكِيدُوا لَكَ كَيْدًا ۖ إِنَّ الشَّيْطَانَ لِلْإِنْسَانِ عَدُوٌّ مُبِينٌ

He replied, “O my dear son! Do not relate your vision to your brothers, or they will devise a plot against you. Surely Satan is a sworn enemy to humankind.

Jacob said to his son Joseph: O my son, do not mention this vision to your brothers, lest they should plot against you and envy you, and seek to destroy you. Indeed, Satan is to man a clear enemy.

Prophet Jacob advises his son Joseph not to share his dream with his brothers, fearing their envy and potential harm. He reminds Joseph that Satan is a clear enemy to humankind, always seeking to sow discord.

Verse 6

وَكَذَٰلِكَ يَجْتَبِيكَ رَبُّكَ وَيُعَلِّمُكَ مِنْ تَأْوِيلِ الْأَحَادِيثِ وَيُتِمُّ نِعْمَتَهُ عَلَيْكَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ يَعْقُوبَ كَمَا أَتَمَّهَا عَلَىٰ أَبَوَيْكَ مِنْ قَبْلُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَإِسْحَاقَ ۚ إِنَّ رَبَّكَ عَلِيمٌ حَكِيمٌ

And so will your Lord choose you ˹O Joseph˺, and teach you the interpretation of dreams, and perfect His favour upon you and the descendants of Jacob—˹just˺ as He once perfected it upon your forefathers, Abraham and Isaac. Surely your Lord is All-Knowing, All-Wise.”

And as your Lord has shown you this vision, so will He choose you and teach you the interpretation of what people see in their sleep from the visions to which they will come to pass in reality, and perfect His favor upon you and upon the family of Jacob with prophethood and messengership, as He perfected it before upon your two forefathers, Abraham and Isaac, with prophethood and messengership. Indeed, your Lord is Knowing of whom He chooses of His servants, Wise in managing the affairs of His creation.

Jacob assures Joseph that Allah will choose him, grant him the ability to interpret dreams, and perfect His blessings upon him and his family, as He did with their forefathers Abraham and Isaac. This highlights Allah's knowledge and wisdom in His choices.

Verse 7

لَقَدْ كَانَ فِي يُوسُفَ وَإِخْوَتِهِ آيَاتٌ لِلسَّائِلِينَ

Indeed, in the story of Joseph and his brothers there are lessons for all who ask.

Indeed, in the story of Joseph and his brothers, there are lessons and signs that indicate the power of Allah and His wisdom for those who inquire about their news and wish to know it.

The story of Joseph and his brothers contains valuable lessons and signs for those who seek to understand Allah's power and wisdom.

Verse 8

إِذْ قَالُوا لَيُوسُفُ وَأَخُوهُ أَحَبُّ إِلَىٰ أَبِينَا مِنَّا وَنَحْنُ عُصْبَةٌ إِنَّ أَبَانَا لَفِي ضَلَالٍ مُبِينٍ

˹Remember˺ when they said ˹to one another˺, “Surely Joseph and his brother ˹Benjamin˺ are more beloved to our father than we, even though we are a group of so many. Indeed, our father is clearly mistaken.

When Joseph’s brothers said among themselves: “Joseph and his brother are more beloved to our father than we are, he prefers them over us, and we are a large group. Indeed, our father is in clear error in preferring them over us without any reason we see.”

Joseph's brothers express their jealousy, believing that their father favors Joseph and his younger brother. They perceive their father's preference as a mistake.

Verse 9

اقْتُلُوا يُوسُفَ أَوِ اطْرَحُوهُ أَرْضًا يَخْلُ لَكُمْ وَجْهُ أَبِيكُمْ وَتَكُونُوا مِنْ بَعْدِهِ قَوْمًا صَالِحِينَ

Kill Joseph or cast him out to some ˹distant˺ land so that our father’s attention will be only ours, then after that you may ˹repent and˺ become righteous people!”

Kill Joseph or cast him into a remote, unknown land, so that your father’s love will be exclusively for you and he will be devoted to you, and he will not turn away from you to others, and after killing Joseph or removing him, you will be repentant to Allah, asking forgiveness from Him after your sin.

The brothers plot to either kill Joseph or abandon him in a distant land, aiming to secure their father's undivided affection. They naively believe they can repent later for their wrongdoing.

Verse 10

قَالَ قَائِلٌ مِنْهُمْ لَا تَقْتُلُوا يُوسُفَ وَأَلْقُوهُ فِي غَيَابَتِ الْجُبِّ يَلْتَقِطْهُ بَعْضُ السَّيَّارَةِ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ فَاعِلِينَ

One of them said, “Do not kill Joseph. But if you must do something, throw him into the bottom of a well so perhaps he may be picked up by some travellers.”

One of Joseph’s brothers said: Do not kill Joseph, but throw him into the bottom of a well; some passersby of travelers will pick him up and you will be rid of him, and there is no need to kill him, if you are determined to do what you say.

One of the brothers suggests throwing Joseph into a well instead of killing him, hoping that passing travelers might find him. This displays a lesser degree of cruelty.

Verse 11

قَالُوا يَا أَبَانَا مَا لَكَ لَا تَأْمَنَّا عَلَىٰ يُوسُفَ وَإِنَّا لَهُ لَنَاصِحُونَ

They said, “O our father! Why do you not trust us with Joseph, although we truly wish him well?

Joseph’s brothers said—after agreeing to remove him—: O our father, why do you not trust us with Joseph, even though he is our brother, and we want good for him and are compassionate towards him, and we look after him, and we are sincerely advising him?

The brothers question their father's distrust, asserting their good intentions and care for Joseph. This is part of their manipulative attempt to deceive him.

Verse 12

أَرْسِلْهُ مَعَنَا غَدًا يَرْتَعْ وَيَلْعَبْ وَإِنَّا لَهُ لَحَافِظُونَ

Send him out with us tomorrow so that he may enjoy himself and play. And we will really watch over him.”

Send him with us tomorrow when we go out to our pastures, he will enjoy himself, be active and joyful, and play in races and other permissible games, and indeed we will be his protectors from everything you fear for him.

The brothers request that Joseph be allowed to accompany them to enjoy himself and play, promising to watch over him. Their request conceals their true, malicious plan.

Verse 13

قَالَ إِنِّي لَيَحْزُنُنِي أَنْ تَذْهَبُوا بِهِ وَأَخَافُ أَنْ يَأْكُلَهُ الذِّئْبُ وَأَنْتُمْ عَنْهُ غَافِلُونَ

He responded, “It would truly sadden me if you took him away with you, and I fear that a wolf may devour him while you are negligent of him.”

Jacob said: Indeed, it grieves me that he will be separated from me if you take him to the pastures, and I fear that the wolf will eat him, while you are heedless and preoccupied.

Jacob expresses his sadness at the prospect of Joseph being separated from him and voices his fear that a wolf might attack him while the brothers are negligent.

Verse 14

قَالُوا لَئِنْ أَكَلَهُ الذِّئْبُ وَنَحْنُ عُصْبَةٌ إِنَّا إِذًا لَخَاسِرُونَ

They said, “If a wolf were to devour him, despite our strong group, then we would certainly be losers!”

Joseph's brothers said to their father: If the wolf eats him, while we are a strong group, then indeed we would be losers, there is no good in us, and no benefit to be expected from us.

The brothers dismiss their father’s concerns, stating that if a wolf were to harm Joseph despite their large group, they would be considered worthless and incapable.

Verse 15

فَلَمَّا ذَهَبُوا بِهِ وَأَجْمَعُوا أَنْ يَجْعَلُوهُ فِي غَيَابَتِ الْجُبِّ ۚ وَأَوْحَيْنَا إِلَيْهِ لَتُنَبِّئَنَّهُمْ بِأَمْرِهِمْ هَٰذَا وَهُمْ لَا يَشْعُرُونَ

And so, when they took him away and decided to throw him into the bottom of the well, We inspired him: “˹One day˺ you will remind them of this deed of theirs while they are unaware ˹of who you are˺.”

So he sent him with them. And when they took him away and agreed to throw him into the bottom of the well, We inspired to Joseph, “You will surely inform them of this deed of theirs in the future, while they do not perceive it.”

Joseph's brothers threw him in a well. Allah revealed to Joseph that one day he would remind them of this act when they least expect it.

Verse 16

وَجَاءُوا أَبَاهُمْ عِشَاءً يَبْكُونَ

Then they returned to their father in the evening, weeping.

And the brothers of Joseph came to their father in the time of evening, from the beginning of the night, weeping and showing sorrow and anxiety.

The brothers returned to their father Jacob in the evening, pretending to be sad and weeping.

Verse 17

قَالُوا يَا أَبَانَا إِنَّا ذَهَبْنَا نَسْتَبِقُ وَتَرَكْنَا يُوسُفَ عِنْدَ مَتَاعِنَا فَأَكَلَهُ الذِّئْبُ ۖ وَمَا أَنْتَ بِمُؤْمِنٍ لَنَا وَلَوْ كُنَّا صَادِقِينَ

They cried, “Our father! We went racing and left Joseph with our belongings, and a wolf devoured him! But you will not believe us, no matter how truthful we are.”

They said, “O our father, indeed, we went racing, and we left Joseph with our things and belongings, and we did not neglect his safety, rather we left him in our care, and we did not leave him except for a short time, and then the wolf ate him, and you will not believe us even if we were described as truthful, due to your intense love for Joseph.”

They lied to their father, claiming a wolf had eaten Joseph while they were racing and not watching him. They knew their father may not believe their fabricated story.

Verse 18

وَجَاءُوا عَلَىٰ قَمِيصِهِ بِدَمٍ كَذِبٍ ۚ قَالَ بَلْ سَوَّلَتْ لَكُمْ أَنْفُسُكُمْ أَمْرًا ۖ فَصَبْرٌ جَمِيلٌ ۖ وَاللَّهُ الْمُسْتَعَانُ عَلَىٰ مَا تَصِفُونَ

And they brought his shirt, stained with false blood. He responded, “No! Your souls must have tempted you to do something ˹evil˺. So ˹I can only endure with˺ beautiful patience! It is Allah’s help that I seek to bear your claims.”

And they came with his shirt stained with blood other than Joseph’s blood; to testify to their truthfulness, but it was evidence of their falsehood; because the shirt was not torn. So their father Jacob, peace be upon him, said to them: “It is not as you say, but your own souls have enticed you to a wicked deed concerning Joseph, and you saw it as good and did it. So my patience is a beautiful patience with no complaint to any of creation, and I seek help from Allah to endure what you describe of falsehood, not through my own power and strength.”

The brothers showed their father Joseph's shirt with fake blood. Jacob immediately knew this was a lie. He showed patience and sought Allah's help with his sorrow.

Verse 19

وَجَاءَتْ سَيَّارَةٌ فَأَرْسَلُوا وَارِدَهُمْ فَأَدْلَىٰ دَلْوَهُ ۖ قَالَ يَا بُشْرَىٰ هَٰذَا غُلَامٌ ۚ وَأَسَرُّوهُ بِضَاعَةً ۚ وَاللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ بِمَا يَعْمَلُونَ

And there came some travellers, and they sent their water-boy who let down his bucket into the well. He cried out, “Oh, what a great find! Here is a boy!” And they took him secretly ˹to be sold˺ as merchandise, but Allah is All-Knowing of what they did.

And a group of travelers came, and they sent one who was seeking water for them. So when he lowered his bucket into the well, Joseph clung to it. And their water-drawer said, “Good news! Here is a precious boy.” And the water-drawer and his companions hid Joseph from the rest of the travelers and did not reveal him to them, and they said, “Indeed, this is merchandise we obtained.” And Allah knows well what they were doing with Joseph.

Some travelers found Joseph in the well. They hid him from others, regarding him as merchandise, and Allah was aware of their actions.

Verse 20

وَشَرَوْهُ بِثَمَنٍ بَخْسٍ دَرَاهِمَ مَعْدُودَةٍ وَكَانُوا فِيهِ مِنَ الزَّاهِدِينَ

And his brothers sold him to the travelers for a small price of dirhams, and they were uninterested in him, desiring to get rid of him; that was because they did not know his status with Allah.

The brothers sold Joseph for a very low price, showing they did not value him at all.

Verse 21

The man from Egypt who bought him said to his wife, “Take good care of him, perhaps he may be useful to us or we may adopt him as a son.” This is how We established Joseph in the land, so that We might teach him the interpretation of dreams. Allah’s Will always prevails, but most people do not know.

And when the travelers took Joseph to “Egypt”, the Aziz of it, who was the minister, bought him from them, and said to his wife: “Treat him well, and make his stay with us honorable, perhaps we will benefit from his service, or adopt him as a son.” And just as We saved Joseph and made the Aziz of “Egypt” kind to him, so We established him in the land of “Egypt”, and made him in charge of its treasuries, and so that we teach him the interpretation of visions so that he knows from them what will occur in the future. And Allah is predominant over His affair, so His decree is executed and none can invalidate it, but most people do not know that all matters are in the hand of Allah.

An Egyptian official bought Joseph and told his wife to care for him. Allah established Joseph in Egypt and planned to teach him dream interpretation. Allah's will always prevails.

Verse 22

وَلَمَّا بَلَغَ أَشُدَّهُ آتَيْنَاهُ حُكْمًا وَعِلْمًا ۚ وَكَذَٰلِكَ نَجْزِي الْمُحْسِنِينَ

And when Joseph reached his full strength in his youth, We gave him understanding and knowledge, and like this reward that We gave to Joseph for his good doing, We reward those who do good for their good doing. And in this is a consolation for the Messenger, peace be upon him.

When Joseph reached maturity, Allah granted him wisdom and knowledge. This is how Allah rewards those who do good.

Verse 23

وَرَاوَدَتْهُ الَّتِي هُوَ فِي بَيْتِهَا عَنْ نَفْسِهِ وَغَلَّقَتِ الْأَبْوَابَ وَقَالَتْ هَيْتَ لَكَ ۚ قَالَ مَعَاذَ اللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّهُ رَبِّي أَحْسَنَ مَثْوَايَ ۖ إِنَّهُ لَا يُفْلِحُ الظَّالِمُونَ

The wife of Al-Aziz invited Joseph, who was in her house, to herself - gently and softly - because of her intense love for him and his handsome appearance. She closed the doors on herself and Joseph and said, 'Come to me.' He said, 'I seek refuge in Allah; I seek protection from what you are inviting me to, from betraying my master who has honored my position and treated me well, so I will not betray him in his family. Indeed, those who do wrong and do what they have no right to do will not succeed.'

The wife of the man in whose house Joseph lived tried to seduce him. Joseph refused, stating that he would not betray his master's trust, and that wrongdoers never succeed.

Verse 24

And she had desired to commit the immoral act, and Joseph's soul had also entertained fleeting thoughts of responding, had he not seen a sign from his Lord that restrained him from what his soul suggested. And indeed, We showed him that so that We would avert evil and immorality from him in all his affairs. Indeed, he is one of Our purified servants, chosen for the message, who are sincere in their worship of Allah and His Oneness.

Both Joseph and the wife desired the act. However, Joseph was protected by a sign from God, which prevented him from acting on his desire. This highlights Joseph's purity and devotion.

Verse 25

They raced for the door and she tore his shirt from the back, only to find her husband at the door. She cried, “What is the penalty for someone who tried to violate your wife, except imprisonment or a painful punishment?”

And Joseph rushed towards the door, wanting to leave, and she rushed to try and catch him, and she pulled his shirt from behind to prevent him from leaving, so she tore it. And they found her husband at the door, so she said, 'What is the punishment for one who intends to commit immorality with your wife except that he be imprisoned or punished with a painful punishment?'

As Joseph tried to flee, the wife tore his shirt from the back. She then falsely accused him of trying to violate her, demanding punishment.

Verse 26

Joseph said, 'She is the one who asked me for that.' Then a baby in the cradle, from her family, testified and said, 'If his shirt is torn from the front, then she has told the truth in her accusation against him, and he is one of the liars.'

Joseph stated that she had tried to seduce him. A witness from her family pointed out that if the shirt was torn from the front, she was telling the truth, but if torn from the back, Joseph was the truthful one.

Verse 27

This verse provides a continuation of the previous verse, stating that if his shirt is torn from the back, she has lied and he is truthful.

Verse 28

The Prophet declares his path: inviting to Allah with clear understanding. He affirms Allah's oneness and distances himself from those who associate partners with Him.

Verse 109

Messengers before the Prophet were also men from their societies. The consequences for past deniers should be a lesson. The Hereafter is far better for the mindful.

When messengers despaired, Allah's help came to them. Allah's punishment cannot be avoided by the wicked. This serves as consolation for the Prophet.

The stories in the Quran provide lessons for thoughtful people. It confirms previous scriptures, clarifies important matters, and is guidance and mercy for believers.
