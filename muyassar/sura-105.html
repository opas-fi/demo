Sura 105

Verse 1

أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِأَصْحَابِ الْفِيلِ

Have you not seen ˹O Prophet˺ how your Lord dealt with the Army of the Elephant?

Have you not known - O Messenger - how your Lord dealt with the companions of the elephant: Abraha the Abyssinian and his army who wanted to destroy the blessed Kaaba?

This verse prompts reflection on Allah's action against the Army of the Elephant, a historical event where Allah protected the Kaaba.

Verse 2

أَلَمْ يَجْعَلْ كَيْدَهُمْ فِي تَضْلِيلٍ

Did He not frustrate their scheme?

Did He not make their plotting of evil in vain and lost?

This verse highlights how Allah thwarted the plans of the Army of the Elephant, showing the futility of their schemes against His will.

Verse 3

وَأَرْسَلَ عَلَيْهِمْ طَيْرًا أَبَابِيلَ

For He sent against them flocks of birds,

And He sent upon them birds in successive groups, striking them with stones of hardened clay.

This verse describes how Allah sent flocks of birds against the Army of the Elephant, showcasing His power and ability to send divine intervention.

Verse 4

تَرْمِيهِمْ بِحِجَارَةٍ مِنْ سِجِّيلٍ

that pelted them with stones of baked clay,

And He sent upon them birds in successive groups, striking them with stones of hardened clay.

This verse elaborates on how the birds pelted the Army of the Elephant with stones of baked clay, emphasizing the divine nature of the punishment.

Verse 5

فَجَعَلَهُمْ كَعَصْفٍ مَأْكُولٍ

leaving them like chewed up straw.

So He made them like crushed leaves of crops that have been eaten by beasts and then discarded.

This verse describes how the Army of the Elephant was left like chewed up straw, demonstrating the complete destruction of Allah's enemies.
