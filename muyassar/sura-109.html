Sura 109

Verse 1

قُلْ يَا أَيُّهَا الْكَافِرُونَ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “O you disbelievers!

Say -O Messenger- to those who disbelieve in Allah and His Messenger: O you disbelievers in Allah.

This verse is a clear declaration to the disbelievers, emphasizing a separation of beliefs.

Verse 2

لَا أَعْبُدُ مَا تَعْبُدُونَ

I do not worship what you worship,

I do not worship what you worship of idols and false deities.

This verse clarifies that the Prophet does not worship the idols of the disbelievers.

Verse 3

وَلَا أَنْتُمْ عَابِدُونَ مَا أَعْبُدُ

nor do you worship what I worship.

Nor do you worship what I worship of One God, who is Allah, Lord of the worlds, the only one worthy of worship.

This verse clarifies that the disbelievers do not worship the One God that the Prophet worships.

Verse 4

وَلَا أَنَا عَابِدٌ مَا عَبَدْتُمْ

I will never worship what you worship,

Nor will I worship what you have worshiped of false idols and deities.

This verse reaffirms the Prophet's unwavering commitment to not worship the disbelievers' idols in the future.

Verse 5

وَلَا أَنْتُمْ عَابِدُونَ مَا أَعْبُدُ

nor will you ever worship what I worship.

Nor will you worship in the future what I worship. This verse was revealed about specific people from the polytheists, whom Allah knew would never believe.

This verse foretells that the disbelievers will not worship Allah. It was revealed about specific people whom Allah knew would not believe.

Verse 6

لَكُمْ دِينُكُمْ وَلِيَ دِينِ

You have your way, and I have my Way.”

For you is your religion that you have insisted on following, and for me is my religion which I do not seek to change.

This verse highlights the clear distinction between the beliefs of the disbelievers and the Prophet, emphasizing that each will follow their own path.
