Sura 004

Verse 1

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ اتَّقُوا رَبَّكُمُ الَّذِي خَلَقَكُمْ مِنْ نَفْسٍ وَاحِدَةٍ وَخَلَقَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَبَثَّ مِنْهُمَا رِجَالًا كَثِيرًا وَنِسَاءً ۚ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ الَّذِي تَسَاءَلُونَ بِهِ وَالْأَرْحَامَ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَانَ عَلَيْكُمْ رَقِيبًا

O humanity! Be mindful of your Lord Who created you from a single soul, and from it He created its mate, and through both He spread countless men and women. And be mindful of Allah—in Whose Name you appeal to one another—and ˹honour˺ family ties. Surely Allah is ever Watchful over you.

O mankind, fear Allah and adhere to His commands, and avoid His prohibitions; for He is the One who created you from one soul, which is Adam, peace be upon him, and created from it his mate, which is Eve, and spread from them throughout the earth many men and many women, and be mindful of Allah, by whom you ask of one another, and beware of severing your family ties. Indeed, Allah is watchful over all your conditions.

This verse reminds humanity of their common origin from Adam and Eve, urging them to be mindful of Allah and to honor family ties. It emphasizes Allah's watchfulness over everyone.

Verse 2

وَآتُوا الْيَتَامَىٰ أَمْوَالَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا تَتَبَدَّلُوا الْخَبِيثَ بِالطَّيِّبِ ۖ وَلَا تَأْكُلُوا أَمْوَالَهُمْ إِلَىٰ أَمْوَالِكُمْ ۚ إِنَّهُ كَانَ حُوبًا كَبِيرًا

Give orphans their wealth ˹when they reach maturity˺, and do not exchange your worthless possessions for their valuables, nor cheat them by mixing their wealth with your own. For this would indeed be a great sin.

And give to those whose fathers died while they were under the age of puberty, and you were their guardians, their wealth when they reach the age of puberty, and you see in them the ability to protect their wealth. And do not take the good from their wealth and replace it with the bad from your wealth, and do not mix their wealth with your wealth in order to deceive and consume their wealth. Indeed, whoever dares to do that has committed a great sin.

This verse instructs guardians to give orphans their wealth when they reach maturity. It forbids the exchange of good for bad or mixing their wealth with the guardians' own, warning against this as a great sin.

Verse 3

وَإِنْ خِفْتُمْ أَلَّا تُقْسِطُوا فِي الْيَتَامَىٰ فَانْكِحُوا مَا طَابَ لَكُمْ مِنَ النِّسَاءِ مَثْنَىٰ وَثُلَاثَ وَرُبَاعَ ۖ فَإِنْ خِفْتُمْ أَلَّا تَعْدِلُوا فَوَاحِدَةً أَوْ مَا مَلَكَتْ أَيْمَانُكُمْ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ أَدْنَىٰ أَلَّا تَعُولُوا

If you fear you might fail to give orphan women their ˹due˺ rights ˹if you were to marry them˺, then marry other women of your choice—two, three, or four. But if you are afraid you will fail to maintain justice, then ˹content yourselves with˺ one or those ˹bondwomen˺ in your possession. This way you are less likely to commit injustice.

And if you fear that you will not be just to the orphan girls under your care by not giving them their dowries like others, then leave them and marry what is lawful for you of women other than them, two or three or four. But if you fear that you will not be just, then be content with one, or what your right hands possess of slaves. That which has been legislated for you regarding orphan girls and marriage from one to four, or limiting yourself to one, or what your right hands possess, is more likely to prevent injustice and transgression.

This verse permits men to marry up to four wives, but emphasizes the need for justice and fairness. If there is a fear of not being just, then they should marry only one or be content with their bondwomen to avoid injustice.

Verse 4

وَآتُوا النِّسَاءَ صَدُقَاتِهِنَّ نِحْلَةً ۚ فَإِنْ طِبْنَ لَكُمْ عَنْ شَيْءٍ مِنْهُ نَفْسًا فَكُلُوهُ هَنِيئًا مَرِيئًا

Give women ˹you wed˺ their due dowries graciously. But if they waive some of it willingly, then you may enjoy it freely with a clear conscience.

And give the women their dowries, a gift obligatory and a duty incumbent, with a good heart from you. But if they willingly give up to you anything of it, then take it, and dispose of it; it is lawful and good.

This verse instructs men to give women their dowries willingly and graciously. If a woman chooses to waive some of the dowry, it is permissible to accept it.

Verse 5

وَلَا تُؤْتُوا السُّفَهَاءَ أَمْوَالَكُمُ الَّتِي جَعَلَ اللَّهُ لَكُمْ قِيَامًا وَارْزُقُوهُمْ فِيهَا وَاكْسُوهُمْ وَقُولُوا لَهُمْ قَوْلًا مَعْرُوفًا

Do not entrust the incapable ˹among your dependants˺ with your wealth which Allah has made a means of support for you—but feed and clothe them from it, and speak to them kindly.

And do not give – O guardians – those who waste among the men, women, and children their wealth that is under your control, so that they may spend it in the wrong way, for this wealth is what sustains the life of the people. And spend on them from it and clothe them, and speak to them words of kindness, good speech, and good manners.

This verse advises against entrusting the wealth to those who are incapable of managing it wisely. Instead, guardians should provide for their needs and speak kindly to them.

Verse 6

وَابْتَلُوا الْيَتَامَىٰ حَتَّىٰ إِذَا بَلَغُوا النِّكَاحَ فَإِنْ آنَسْتُمْ مِنْهُمْ رُشْدًا فَادْفَعُوا إِلَيْهِمْ أَمْوَالَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا تَأْكُلُوهَا إِسْرَافًا وَبِدَارًا أَنْ يَكْبَرُوا ۚ وَمَنْ كَانَ غَنِيًّا فَلْيَسْتَعْفِفْ ۖ وَمَنْ كَانَ فَقِيرًا فَلْيَأْكُلْ بِالْمَعْرُوفِ ۚ فَإِذَا دَفَعْتُمْ إِلَيْهِمْ أَمْوَالَهُمْ فَأَشْهِدُوا عَلَيْهِمْ ۚ وَكَفَىٰ بِاللَّهِ حَسِيبًا

Test ˹the competence of˺ the orphans until they reach a marriageable age. Then if you feel they are capable of sound judgment, return their wealth to them. And do not consume it wastefully and hastily before they grow up ˹to demand it˺. If the guardian is well-off, they should not take compensation; but if the guardian is poor, let them take a reasonable provision. When you give orphans back their property, call in witnesses. And sufficient is Allah as a ˹vigilant˺ Reckoner.

And test those under your care among the orphans to know their ability to manage their wealth well, until when they reach the age of puberty, and you know of their righteousness in their religion and ability to protect their wealth, then hand it over to them, and do not transgress against it by spending it in the wrong way, wasting it and rushing to consume it before they take it from you. And whoever is wealthy among you, let him abstain with his wealth, and not take anything from the orphan’s wealth, and whoever is poor, let him take according to his need when necessary. So when you know that they are capable of protecting their wealth after reaching puberty, and you have handed it over to them, then have witnesses over them to guarantee that their right has reached them completely so that they do not deny that. And it is sufficient for you that Allah is a witness over you, and holds you accountable for what you have done.

This verse commands testing orphans' competence until they reach marriageable age. If they are deemed capable, their wealth should be returned. Guardians are not to waste their wealth, and if poor, may take a reasonable amount. Witnesses are to be present when returning the wealth.

Verse 7

لِلرِّجَالِ نَصِيبٌ مِمَّا تَرَكَ الْوَالِدَانِ وَالْأَقْرَبُونَ وَلِلنِّسَاءِ نَصِيبٌ مِمَّا تَرَكَ الْوَالِدَانِ وَالْأَقْرَبُونَ مِمَّا قَلَّ مِنْهُ أَوْ كَثُرَ ۚ نَصِيبًا مَفْرُوضًا

For men there is a share in what their parents and close relatives leave, and for women there is a share in what their parents and close relatives leave—whether it is little or much. ˹These are˺ obligatory shares.

For males - whether young or old - is a share decreed by Allah from what the parents and relatives leave behind of wealth, whether it be little or much, in specific, clear portions that Allah Almighty has prescribed for them, and for women as well.

This verse establishes that both men and women have a right to inherit from their parents and close relatives. These are obligatory shares, regardless of whether the inheritance is small or large.

Verse 8

وَإِذَا حَضَرَ الْقِسْمَةَ أُولُو الْقُرْبَىٰ وَالْيَتَامَىٰ وَالْمَسَاكِينُ فَارْزُقُوهُمْ مِنْهُ وَقُولُوا لَهُمْ قَوْلًا مَعْرُوفًا

If ˹non-inheriting˺ relatives, orphans, or the needy are present at the time of distribution, offer them a ˹small˺ provision from it and speak to them kindly.

And if relatives of the deceased who have no right to the inheritance are present at the division of the inheritance, or if orphans are present who lost their fathers when they were young, or those who have no wealth, then give them something of the wealth as a matter of preference before dividing the inheritance among its rightful recipients, and speak to them kindly, without being obscene or offensive.

When distributing inheritance, it's recommended to provide for relatives who are not legal heirs, orphans, and the needy present, and to speak to them kindly.

Verse 9

وَلْيَخْشَ الَّذِينَ لَوْ تَرَكُوا مِنْ خَلْفِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّةً ضِعَافًا خَافُوا عَلَيْهِمْ فَلْيَتَّقُوا اللَّهَ وَلْيَقُولُوا قَوْلًا سَدِيدًا

Let the guardians be as concerned ˹for the orphans˺ as they would if they were to ˹die and˺ leave ˹their own˺ helpless children behind. So let them be mindful of Allah and speak equitably.

And let those fear who, if they left behind weak offspring, would fear for them [the consequences]. So let them fear Allah regarding those under their care, orphans and others, by protecting their wealth, raising them well, and keeping harm away from them. And let them speak to them justly and fairly.

Guardians of orphans should be as concerned for them as they would be for their own children, treating them fairly and protecting their rights.

Verse 10

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يَأْكُلُونَ أَمْوَالَ الْيَتَامَىٰ ظُلْمًا إِنَّمَا يَأْكُلُونَ فِي بُطُونِهِمْ نَارًا ۖ وَسَيَصْلَوْنَ سَعِيرًا

Indeed, those who unjustly consume orphans’ wealth ˹in fact˺ consume nothing but fire into their bellies. And they will be burned in a blazing Hell!

Indeed, those who consume the wealth of orphans unjustly are only swallowing fire into their bellies, and they will enter a blazing Fire.

Those who unjustly consume the wealth of orphans are warned of severe consequences in the afterlife, facing the fire of Hell.

Verse 11

يُوصِيكُمُ اللَّهُ فِي أَوْلَادِكُمْ ۖ لِلذَّكَرِ مِثْلُ حَظِّ الْأُنْثَيَيْنِ ۚ فَإِنْ كُنَّ نِسَاءً فَوْقَ اثْنَتَيْنِ فَلَهُنَّ ثُلُثَا مَا تَرَكَ ۖ وَإِنْ كَانَتْ وَاحِدَةً فَلَهَا النِّصْفُ ۚ وَلِأَبَوَيْهِ لِكُلِّ وَاحِدٍ مِنْهُمَا السُّدُسُ مِمَّا تَرَكَ إِنْ كَانَ لَهُ وَلَدٌ ۚ فَإِنْ لَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُ وَلَدٌ وَوَرِثَهُ أَبَوَاهُ فَلِأُمِّهِ الثُّلُثُ ۚ فَإِنْ كَانَ لَهُ إِخْوَةٌ فَلِأُمِّهِ السُّدُسُ ۚ مِنْ بَعْدِ وَصِيَّةٍ يُوصِي بِهَا أَوْ دَيْنٍ ۗ آبَاؤُكُمْ وَأَبْنَاؤُكُمْ لَا تَدْرُونَ أَيُّهُمْ أَقْرَبُ لَكُمْ نَفْعًا ۚ فَرِيضَةً مِنَ اللَّهِ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَانَ عَلِيمًا حَكِيمًا

Allah commands you regarding your children: the share of the male will be twice that of the female. If you leave only two ˹or more˺ females, their share is two-thirds of the estate. But if there is only one female, her share will be one-half. Each parent is entitled to one-sixth if you leave offspring. But if you are childless and your parents are the only heirs, then your mother will receive one-third. But if you leave siblings, then your mother will receive one-sixth—after the fulfilment of bequests and debts. ˹Be fair to˺ your parents and children, as you do not ˹fully˺ know who is more beneficial to you. ˹This is˺ an obligation from Allah. Surely Allah is All-Knowing, All-Wise.

Allah instructs you concerning your children: for the male, what is equal to the share of two females. But if there are [only] daughters, two or more, for them is two thirds of one’s estate; and if there is only one, for her is half. And for one’s parents, to each one of them is a sixth of the estate if he had children. But if he had no children and the parents are his [only] heirs, then for his mother is one third, and for his father is the remainder. And if the deceased had brothers and sisters, two or more, then for the mother is one sixth, and the rest goes to the father, and the siblings get nothing. This division of the inheritance is only after the deceased’s will has been fulfilled to the limit of one third or the payment of any debt. Your parents or your children, you do not know which of them is closer to you in benefit in your worldly life and in the Hereafter, so do not prefer one over the other. This is what Allah has decreed for you. Indeed, Allah is ever Knowing and Wise in what He has prescribed for them.

This verse outlines the specific inheritance shares. Males receive double the share of females. Parents receive one-sixth each if there are children. If there are no children, the mother receives one-third, while if there are siblings, she receives one-sixth. These are divinely ordained proportions after any bequests or debts have been settled. The verse underscores that one does not know who is closer in benefit, stressing fairness in inheritance distribution.

Verse 12

وَلَكُمْ نِصْفُ مَا تَرَكَ أَزْوَاجُكُمْ إِنْ لَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُنَّ وَلَدٌ ۚ فَإِنْ كَانَ لَهُنَّ وَلَدٌ فَلَكُمُ الرُّبُعُ مِمَّا تَرَكْنَ ۚ مِنْ بَعْدِ وَصِيَّةٍ يُوصِينَ بِهَا أَوْ دَيْنٍ ۚ وَلَهُنَّ الرُّبُعُ مِمَّا تَرَكْتُمْ إِنْ لَمْ يَكُنْ لَكُمْ وَلَدٌ ۚ فَإِنْ كَانَ لَكُمْ وَلَدٌ فَلَهُنَّ الثُّمُنُ مِمَّا تَرَكْتُمْ ۚ مِنْ بَعْدِ وَصِيَّةٍ تُوصُونَ بِهَا أَوْ دَيْنٍ ۗ وَإِنْ كَانَ رَجُلٌ يُورَثُ كَلَالَةً أَوِ امْرَأَةٌ وَلَهُ أَخٌ أَوْ أُخْتٌ فَلِكُلِّ وَاحِدٍ مِنْهُمَا السُّدُسُ ۚ فَإِنْ كَانُوا أَكْثَرَ مِنْ ذَٰلِكَ فَهُمْ شُرَكَاءُ فِي الثُّلُثِ ۚ مِنْ بَعْدِ وَصِيَّةٍ يُوصَىٰ بِهَا أَوْ دَيْنٍ غَيْرَ مُضَارٍّ ۚ وَصِيَّةً مِنَ اللَّهِ ۗ وَاللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ حَلِيمٌ

You will inherit half of what your wives leave if they are childless. But if they have children, then ˹your share is˺ one-fourth of the estate—after the fulfilment of bequests and debts. And your wives will inherit one-fourth of what you leave if you are childless. But if you have children, then your wives will receive one-eighth of your estate—after the fulfilment of bequests and debts. And if a man or a woman leaves neither parents nor children but only a brother or a sister ˹from their mother’s side˺, they will each inherit one-sixth, but if they are more than one, they ˹all˺ will share one-third of the estate—after the fulfilment of bequests and debts without harm ˹to the heirs˺. ˹This is˺ a commandment from Allah. And Allah is All-Knowing, Most Forbearing.

And for you - O men - is half of what your wives leave behind if they have no child, whether male or female. But if they have a child, then for you is a quarter of what they leave, which you inherit after the execution of their permissible will, or what is due from them as debt to its claimants. And for your wives - O men - is a quarter of what you leave if you have no son or daughter from them or from others. But if you have a son or a daughter, then for them is an eighth of what you leave, divided between them. If there is only one wife, then this is her inheritance, after the execution of what you have bequeathed of permissible bequests, or settlement of what is due from you as a debt. And if a man or woman dies and has no child or parent, and has a brother or sister from the mother's side, then for each of them is a sixth. But if the brothers or sisters from the mother's side are more than that, then they are partners in a third, divided among them equally, with no difference between male and female. And this that Allah has prescribed for the brothers and sisters from the mother's side, they take it as inheritance for them after the settlement of the deceased's debts, and the execution of his will if he has made a will that does not harm the heirs. With this, your Lord advises you, a beneficial advice for you. And Allah is Knowing of what is best for His creation, Forbearing, not hastening with punishment.

This verse outlines inheritance laws for spouses and siblings. Husbands inherit half of their childless wives' estate or one-fourth if children exist, after debts and bequests. Wives inherit one-fourth of their childless husbands' estate or one-eighth if children exist, after debts and bequests. Siblings from the mother's side inherit one-sixth each if only one, or share one-third equally if more than one, after debts and bequests. These are commandments from Allah.

Verse 13

تِلْكَ حُدُودُ اللَّهِ ۚ وَمَنْ يُطِعِ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ يُدْخِلْهُ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ وَذَٰلِكَ الْفَوْزُ الْعَظِيمُ

These ˹entitlements˺ are the limits set by Allah. Whoever obeys Allah and His Messenger will be admitted into Gardens under which rivers flow, to stay there forever. That is the ultimate triumph!

Those are the divine laws which Allah has prescribed regarding orphans, women, and inheritance, His laws indicating that they are from Allah, the All-Knowing, the Wise. And whoever obeys Allah and His Messenger in what He has prescribed for His servants of these laws and others, He will admit him into gardens with many trees and palaces, beneath which rivers flow with their fresh water, and they will abide in this bliss, never departing from it, and that reward is the great success.

These prescribed laws are limits set by Allah. Those who obey Allah and His Messenger by adhering to these laws will be rewarded with eternal Paradise. This is the ultimate achievement and success.

Verse 14

وَمَنْ يَعْصِ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ وَيَتَعَدَّ حُدُودَهُ يُدْخِلْهُ نَارًا خَالِدًا فِيهَا وَلَهُ عَذَابٌ مُهِينٌ

But whoever disobeys Allah and His Messenger and exceeds their limits will be cast into Hell, to stay there forever. And they will suffer a humiliating punishment.

And whoever disobeys Allah and His Messenger, by denying the laws of Allah, and exceeding what Allah has prescribed for His servants by changing them, or suspending work with them, He will admit him into a fire, abiding therein, and for him is a humiliating and degrading punishment.

Those who disobey Allah and His Messenger by violating these laws will be punished with eternal Hell. They will suffer a humiliating and degrading punishment for their transgression.

Verse 15

وَاللَّاتِي يَأْتِينَ الْفَاحِشَةَ مِنْ نِسَائِكُمْ فَاسْتَشْهِدُوا عَلَيْهِنَّ أَرْبَعَةً مِنْكُمْ ۖ فَإِنْ شَهِدُوا فَأَمْسِكُوهُنَّ فِي الْبُيُوتِ حَتَّىٰ يَتَوَفَّاهُنَّ الْمَوْتُ أَوْ يَجْعَلَ اللَّهُ لَهُنَّ سَبِيلًا

˹As for˺ those of your women who commit illegal intercourse—call four witnesses from among yourselves. If they testify, confine the offenders to their homes until they die or Allah ordains a ˹different˺ way for them.

And those of your women who commit adultery, then bring against them four witnesses from among you. And if they testify, confine them to houses until death overtakes them or Allah ordains for them a way [out].

This verse outlines the initial legal consequence for women who commit adultery. It mandates that if four witnesses testify against them, they are to be confined to their homes until death or until Allah provides another way. This was a temporary measure before later rulings.

Verse 16

وَاللَّذَانِ يَأْتِيَانِهَا مِنْكُمْ فَآذُوهُمَا ۖ فَإِنْ تَابَا وَأَصْلَحَا فَأَعْرِضُوا عَنْهُمَا ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَانَ تَوَّابًا رَحِيمًا

And the two among you who commit this sin—discipline them. If they repent and mend their ways, relieve them. Surely Allah is ever Accepting of Repentance, Most Merciful.

And the two who commit it among you, then harm them with beating, desertion and rebuke. But if they repent and correct themselves by offering good deeds, then turn away from harming them. It is understood from this verse and the one before it that if men commit fornication, they are harmed, and women are confined and harmed. The confinement ends in death, and the harm ends in repentance and righteousness. This was in the early days of Islam, then it was abrogated by what Allah and His Messenger legislated, which is stoning for the married man and woman, who are free, adult, and sane, and who have had intercourse in a valid marriage, and one hundred lashes and banishment for a year for others. Indeed, Allah is ever accepting of repentance from His repentant servants, Merciful to them.

This verse discusses the punishment for two individuals who commit adultery. It suggests that they should be disciplined.
