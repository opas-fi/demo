Sura 043

Verse 1

حم

Ḥâ-Mĩm.

(Ha Mim) The discussion about the disconnected letters has preceded in the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah.

This verse starts with disconnected letters (Ha Mim) which are not fully understood but signify the miraculous nature of the Quran.

Verse 2

وَالْكِتَابِ الْمُبِينِ

By the clear Book!

Allah Almighty swore by the Quran, clear in wording and meaning.

Allah swears by the clear Book (Quran), emphasizing its clarity in both wording and meaning.

Verse 3

إِنَّا جَعَلْنَاهُ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّا لَعَلَّكُمْ تَعْقِلُونَ

Certainly, We have made it a Quran in Arabic so perhaps you will understand.

Indeed, We have sent down the Quran to Muhammad, peace be upon him, in the Arabic language; so that you may understand, and contemplate its meanings and proofs. And it is in the Preserved Tablet with Us, Exalted in its status and honor, and decisive, with no contradiction or discrepancy in it.

The Quran was revealed in Arabic to make it understandable for its initial audience, urging them to contemplate its meanings and proofs.

Verse 4

وَإِنَّهُ فِي أُمِّ الْكِتَابِ لَدَيْنَا لَعَلِيٌّ حَكِيمٌ

And indeed, it is—in the Master Record with Us—highly esteemed, rich in wisdom.

Indeed, We have sent down the Quran to Muhammad, peace be upon him, in the Arabic language; so that you may understand, and contemplate its meanings and proofs. And it is in the Preserved Tablet with Us, Exalted in its status and honor, and decisive, with no contradiction or discrepancy in it.

This verse explains that the Quran is preserved in the Master Record with Allah, indicating its high status, honor, and freedom from any contradictions.

Verse 5

أَفَنَضْرِبُ عَنْكُمُ الذِّكْرَ صَفْحًا أَنْ كُنْتُمْ قَوْمًا مُسْرِفِينَ

Should We then turn the ˹Quranic˺ Reminder away from you ˹simply˺ because you have been a transgressing people?

Shall We then turn away from you, and cease sending down the Quran to you because of your turning away, your lack of submission, and your excess in disbelieving in it?

This verse questions whether the Quranic reminder should be withdrawn simply because of people's transgressions, emphasizing Allah's continued guidance despite human failings.

Verse 6

وَكَمْ أَرْسَلْنَا مِنْ نَبِيٍّ فِي الْأَوَّلِينَ

˹Imagine˺ how many prophets We sent to those ˹destroyed˺ before!

We sent many prophets in the early generations that passed before your people, O Prophet. And there never came to them a prophet but they used to ridicule him, like your people ridicule you. So We destroyed those who denied Our messengers, and they were stronger in might and power than your people, O Muhammad. And the punishment of the former people passed by them, by being destroyed because of their disbelief, their transgression and their mockery of their prophets. And in this is solace for the Prophet, peace be upon him.

This verse illustrates how many prophets were sent to prior generations, highlighting that the rejection and mockery of prophets is not unique to the time of Prophet Muhammad.

Verse 7

وَمَا يَأْتِيهِمْ مِنْ نَبِيٍّ إِلَّا كَانُوا بِهِ يَسْتَهْزِئُونَ

But no prophet ever came to them without being mocked.

We sent many prophets in the early generations that passed before your people, O Prophet. And there never came to them a prophet but they used to ridicule him, like your people ridicule you. So We destroyed those who denied Our messengers, and they were stronger in might and power than your people, O Muhammad. And the punishment of the former people passed by them, by being destroyed because of their disbelief, their transgression and their mockery of their prophets. And in this is solace for the Prophet, peace be upon him.

This verse states that no prophet was ever sent to a community without facing mockery, a constant challenge faced by the messengers of God.

Verse 8

فَأَهْلَكْنَا أَشَدَّ مِنْهُمْ بَطْشًا وَمَضَىٰ مَثَلُ الْأَوَّلِينَ

So We destroyed those who were far mightier than these ˹Meccans˺. The examples of ˹their˺ predecessors have ˹already˺ been related.

We sent many prophets in the early generations that passed before your people, O Prophet. And there never came to them a prophet but they used to ridicule him, like your people ridicule you. So We destroyed those who denied Our messengers, and they were stronger in might and power than your people, O Muhammad. And the punishment of the former people passed by them, by being destroyed because of their disbelief, their transgression and their mockery of their prophets. And in this is solace for the Prophet, peace be upon him.

This verse reminds the people that Allah destroyed previous generations who were far mightier than them, serving as a warning and lesson from the past.

Verse 9

وَلَئِنْ سَأَلْتَهُمْ مَنْ خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ لَيَقُولُنَّ خَلَقَهُنَّ الْعَزِيزُ الْعَلِيمُ

If you ask them ˹O Prophet˺ who created the heavens and the earth, they will certainly say, “The Almighty, All-Knowing did.”

And if you - O Messenger - ask these polytheists among your people, 'Who created the heavens and the earth?' they will surely say, 'They were created by the Almighty in His dominion, the All-Knowing of them and what is in them of things; nothing is hidden from Him.'

Even the polytheists acknowledge that Allah is the creator of the heavens and earth, confirming that this recognition does not translate into their worshiping Him alone.

Verse 10

الَّذِي جَعَلَ لَكُمُ الْأَرْضَ مَهْدًا وَجَعَلَ لَكُمْ فِيهَا سُبُلًا لَعَلَّكُمْ تَهْتَدُونَ

˹He is the One˺ Who has laid out the earth for you, and set in it pathways for you so that you may find your way.

He Who made the earth for you a bed and a carpet, and facilitated for you within it paths for your livelihoods and trades; so that you may be guided by those paths to your religious and worldly benefits.

Allah has made the earth habitable and set pathways for people to navigate, highlighting His provision and care for humanity.

Verse 11

وَالَّذِي نَزَّلَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً بِقَدَرٍ فَأَنْشَرْنَا بِهِ بَلْدَةً مَيْتًا ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ تُخْرَجُونَ

And ˹He is the One˺ Who sends down rain from the sky in perfect measure, with which We give life to a lifeless land. And so will you be brought forth ˹from the grave˺.

And He who sends down rain from the sky in due measure, not a flood to drown nor less than needed, so that it may be a means of sustenance for you and your livestock. And We have given life thereby to a lifeless land, just as We bring forth from this dead land, with the water We sent down from the sky, vegetation and crops. You will be brought forth – O people – from your graves after your annihilation.

Allah sends down rain in perfect measure, bringing life to barren lands. This is a sign of how He will resurrect people from their graves.

Verse 12

وَالَّذِي خَلَقَ الْأَزْوَاجَ كُلَّهَا وَجَعَلَ لَكُمْ مِنَ الْفُلْكِ وَالْأَنْعَامِ مَا تَرْكَبُونَ

And ˹He is the One˺ Who created all ˹things in˺ pairs, and made for you ships and animals to ride

And He who created all pairs, of animals and plants, and made for you of the ships what you ride in the sea, and of the livestock such as camels, horses, mules and donkeys what you ride on land.

Allah created all things in pairs and provided ships and animals for transportation.

Verse 13

لِتَسْتَوُوا عَلَىٰ ظُهُورِهِ ثُمَّ تَذْكُرُوا نِعْمَةَ رَبِّكُمْ إِذَا اسْتَوَيْتُمْ عَلَيْهِ وَتَقُولُوا سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي سَخَّرَ لَنَا هَٰذَا وَمَا كُنَّا لَهُ مُقْرِنِينَ

so that you may sit firmly on their backs, and remember your Lord’s blessings once you are settled on them, saying, “Glory be to the One Who has subjected these for us, for we could have never done so ˹on our own˺.

That you may settle yourselves upon their backs, and then remember the favor of your Lord when you have settled upon them, and say: “Glory be to Allah, who has subjected this to us, and we could not have managed it. And also say: Indeed, to our Lord after our death we are surely returning to Him.” And in this is a declaration that Allah, the Bestower of countless blessings upon His servants, is the One worthy of worship in every circumstance.

When using these means of transport, we should remember Allah's blessings and acknowledge that He has made them subservient to us.

Verse 14

وَإِنَّا إِلَىٰ رَبِّنَا لَمُنْقَلِبُونَ

And surely to our Lord we will ˹all˺ return.”

That you may settle yourselves upon their backs, and then remember the favor of your Lord when you have settled upon them, and say: “Glory be to Allah, who has subjected this to us, and we could not have managed it. And also say: Indeed, to our Lord after our death we are surely returning to Him.” And in this is a declaration that Allah, the Bestower of countless blessings upon His servants, is the One worthy of worship in every circumstance.

We will ultimately return to Allah. This is a reminder to be grateful and worship Him.

Verse 15

وَجَعَلُوا لَهُ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ جُزْءًا ۚ إِنَّ الْإِنْسَانَ لَكَفُورٌ مُبِينٌ

Still the pagans have made some of His creation out to be a part of Him. Indeed, humankind is clearly ungrateful.

And these polytheists have assigned to Allah a portion of His creation, and that is their saying about the angels: 'The daughters of Allah.' Indeed, man is ungrateful for the blessings of his Lord that He has bestowed upon him, showing his denial and disbelief, enumerating the misfortunes, and forgetting the blessings.

The disbelievers falsely attribute some of Allah's creation as being part of Him. Humankind is ungrateful for Allah's blessings.

Verse 16

أَمِ اتَّخَذَ مِمَّا يَخْلُقُ بَنَاتٍ وَأَصْفَاكُمْ بِالْبَنِينَ

Has He taken ˹angels as His˺ daughters from what He created, and favoured you ˹O pagans˺ with sons?

Or do you claim - O ignorant ones - that your Lord has taken from what He creates daughters, while you do not approve of that for yourselves, and has singled you out with sons, making them for you? And in this is a rebuke for them.

The disbelievers claim that Allah has daughters while they prefer sons for themselves. This highlights the illogical nature of their beliefs.

Verse 17

وَإِذَا بُشِّرَ أَحَدُهُمْ بِمَا ضَرَبَ لِلرَّحْمَٰنِ مَثَلًا ظَلَّ وَجْهُهُ مُسْوَدًّا وَهُوَ كَظِيمٌ

Whenever one of them is given the good news of what they attribute to the Most Compassionate, his face grows gloomy, as he suppresses his rage.

And when one of them is given glad tidings of a female, which he has attributed to the Most Gracious, as he claimed that the angels are the daughters of Allah, his face becomes darkened due to the bad news of the female, and he is sad, filled with worry and distress. (So how can they accept for Allah what they do not accept for themselves? Exalted is Allah and far above what the disbelievers say, by a great transcendence).

When they receive news of a baby girl, they are upset, yet they attribute daughters to Allah. This shows the hypocrisy of their claim.

Verse 18

أَوَمَنْ يُنَشَّأُ فِي الْحِلْيَةِ وَهُوَ فِي الْخِصَامِ غَيْرُ مُبِينٍ

˹Do they attribute to Him˺ those who are brought up in fineries and are not commanding in disputes?

Do you dare attribute to Allah, the Most High, one who is brought up in adornment and is not clear in argument due to her femininity?

How can they ascribe to Allah those who are brought up in luxury and are not strong in disputes?

Verse 19

وَجَعَلُوا الْمَلَائِكَةَ الَّذِينَ هُمْ عِبَادُ الرَّحْمَٰنِ إِنَاثًا ۚ أَشَهِدُوا خَلْقَهُمْ ۚ سَتُكْتَبُ شَهَادَتُهُمْ وَيُسْأَلُونَ

Still they have labelled the angels, who are servants of the Most Compassionate, as female. Did they witness their creation? Their statement will be recorded, and they will be questioned!

And these polytheists have made the angels, who are the servants of the Most Gracious, females. Did they witness their creation so they can judge that they are females? Their testimony will be written, and they will be questioned about it in the Hereafter.

They falsely label the angels as female, even though they have no evidence of this. They will be held accountable for this statement.

Verse 20

وَقَالُوا لَوْ شَاءَ الرَّحْمَٰنُ مَا عَبَدْنَاهُمْ ۗ مَا لَهُمْ بِذَٰلِكَ مِنْ عِلْمٍ ۖ إِنْ هُمْ إِلَّا يَخْرُصُونَ

And they argue, “Had the Most Compassionate willed, we would have never worshipped them.” They have no knowledge ˹in support˺ of this ˹claim˺. They do nothing but lie.

And these polytheists from Quraysh said, “If the Most Gracious willed, we would not have worshipped any besides Him.” And this is a false argument, as Allah has established the proof upon the servants by sending the messengers and revealing the books. So, their argument about decree and destiny is the most false of falsehoods after the messengers have warned them. They have no knowledge of the truth of what they say, and they only say it through conjecture and lying, because they have no news from Allah about that or any proof.

They argue that their worship would not occur if Allah did not will it, but this is a false claim. Allah has given them guidance and they are responsible for their actions.

Verse 21

أَمْ آتَيْنَاهُمْ كِتَابًا مِنْ قَبْلِهِ فَهُمْ بِهِ مُسْتَمْسِكُونَ

Or have We given them a Book ˹for proof˺, before this ˹Quran˺, to which they are holding firm?

Did they witness the creation of the angels, or have We given them a book before the Quran which We have sent down, and they are adhering to it, acting by what is in it, and using it as an argument against you, O Messenger?

Have they been given any scripture or evidence to support their beliefs? No, they follow no divine guidance.

Verse 22

بَلْ قَالُوا إِنَّا وَجَدْنَا آبَاءَنَا عَلَىٰ أُمَّةٍ وَإِنَّا عَلَىٰ آثَارِهِمْ مُهْتَدُونَ

In fact, they say, “We found our forefathers following a ˹particular˺ way, and we are following in their footsteps.”

Rather they say: "Indeed, we found our fathers upon a way and a doctrine and a religion, and we are following in the footsteps of our fathers in what they were upon, emulating them and taking them as our example."

They follow the path of their forefathers blindly, without proper knowledge or reflection.

Verse 23

وَكَذَٰلِكَ مَا أَرْسَلْنَا مِنْ قَبْلِكَ فِي قَرْيَةٍ مِنْ نَذِيرٍ إِلَّا قَالَ مُتْرَفُوهَا إِنَّا وَجَدْنَا آبَاءَنَا عَلَىٰ أُمَّةٍ وَإِنَّا عَلَىٰ آثَارِهِمْ مُقْتَدُونَ

Similarly, whenever We sent a warner to a society before you ˹O Prophet˺, its ˹spoiled˺ elite would say, “We found our forefathers following a ˹particular˺ way, and we are walking in their footsteps.”

And similarly, We did not send before you - O Messenger - to any town a warner warning them of Our punishment for their disbelief in Us, and they were warned and cautioned about Our wrath and the coming of Our punishment, except that those who were spoiled by luxury among the leaders and elders said: "Indeed, we found our fathers upon a religion and faith, and we are following their path and manner."

This verse explains that when messengers came to previous societies, the wealthy and elite would reject the message, claiming they were simply following the traditions of their forefathers.

Verse 24

قَالَ أَوَلَوْ جِئْتُكُمْ بِأَهْدَىٰ مِمَّا وَجَدْتُمْ عَلَيْهِ آبَاءَكُمْ ۖ قَالُوا إِنَّا بِمَا أُرْسِلْتُمْ بِهِ كَافِرُونَ

Each ˹warner˺ asked, “Even if what I brought you is better guidance than what you found your forefathers practicing?” They replied, “We totally reject whatever you have been sent with.”

Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and those before him from the messengers said to those who opposed him with this false doubt: "Do you follow your fathers, even if I came to you from your Lord with guidance that is more guiding to the path of truth and more indicative of the way of righteousness than what you found your fathers upon in religion and faith?" They said in defiance: "Indeed, we are disbelievers in that which you have been sent with."

The messengers would ask if they would still follow old ways even if the new guidance was better. The people would defiantly reject the new message.

Verse 25

فَانْتَقَمْنَا مِنْهُمْ ۖ فَانْظُرْ كَيْفَ كَانَ عَاقِبَةُ الْمُكَذِّبِينَ

So We took vengeance on these nations who denied their messengers by bringing down punishment upon them through engulfment and drowning and other things. So look - O Messenger - how was the end of their matter when they denied the signs of Allah and His messengers? And let your people beware lest they continue in their denial, and they befall like what befell them.

Due to their denial, these people faced divine punishment. This serves as a warning about the consequences of rejecting messengers and truth.

Verse 26

وَإِذْ قَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ لِأَبِيهِ وَقَوْمِهِ إِنَّنِي بَرَاءٌ مِمَّا تَعْبُدُونَ

˹Remember, O Prophet˺ when Abraham declared to his father and his people, “I am totally free of whatever ˹gods˺ you worship,

And remember - O Messenger - when Abraham said to his father and his people who were worshipping what your people worship: "Indeed, I am free from what you worship besides Allah."

Prophet Abraham declared his rejection of the idols worshipped by his father and people.

Verse 27

إِلَّا الَّذِي فَطَرَنِي فَإِنَّهُ سَيَهْدِينِ

except the One Who originated me, and He will surely guide me.”

Except for the One who created me, for He will guide me to follow the path of righteousness.

Abraham affirmed his worship solely to Allah, his Creator, and recognized that Allah is the one who guides.

Verse 28

وَجَعَلَهَا كَلِمَةً بَاقِيَةً فِي عَقِبِهِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَرْجِعُونَ

And Abraham, peace be upon him, made the word of monotheism (la ilaha illa Allah - there is no god but Allah) remain among those after him, so that they may return to obedience to their Lord and His oneness, and repent from their disbelief and sins.

Abraham established the declaration of monotheism (belief in one God) to remain among his descendants so they may always return to Allah.

Verse 29

Allah allowed the people of Mecca and their forefathers to enjoy life, delaying their punishment, until a clear message and messenger arrived.

Verse 30

وَلَمَّا جَاءَهُمُ الْحَقُّ قَالُوا هَٰذَا سِحْرٌ وَإِنَّا بِهِ كَافِرُونَ

When the truth came, they rejected it, calling the Quran magic.

Verse 31

وَقَالُوا لَوْلَا نُزِّلَ هَٰذَا الْقُرْآنُ عَلَىٰ رَجُلٍ مِنَ الْقَرْيَتَيْنِ عَظِيمٍ

The disbelievers questioned why the Quran was not revealed to a prominent man from Mecca or Ta'if, implying they felt it should have gone to someone of greater worldly status.

Verse 32

أَهُمْ يَقْسِمُونَ رَحْمَتَ رَبِّكَ ۚ نَحْنُ قَسَمْنَا بَيْنَهُمْ مَعِيشَتَهُمْ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا ۚ وَرَفَعْنَا بَعْضَهُمْ فَوْقَ بَعْضٍ دَرَجَاتٍ لِيَتَّخِذَ بَعْضُهُمْ بَعْضًا سُخْرِيًّا ۗ وَرَحْمَتُ رَبِّكَ خَيْرٌ مِمَّا يَجْمَعُونَ

Is it they who distribute prophethood, placing it wherever they wish? We have distributed their livelihood among them in their worldly life from sustenance and food, and We have raised some of them above others in degrees: this one is rich and this one is poor, this one is strong and this one is weak; so that some of them are subservient to others in livelihood. And the mercy of your Lord - O Messenger - in admitting them to Paradise is better than what they gather from the fleeting debris of this world.

This verse emphasizes that Allah distributes His blessings and sustenance as He wills, not based on human desires. Divine mercy is far better than any worldly wealth they accumulate.

Verse 33

وَلَوْلَا أَنْ يَكُونَ النَّاسُ أُمَّةً وَاحِدَةً لَجَعَلْنَا لِمَنْ يَكْفُرُ بِالرَّحْمَٰنِ لِبُيُوتِهِمْ سُقُفًا مِنْ فِضَّةٍ وَمَعَارِجَ عَلَيْهَا يَظْهَرُونَ

Were it not that people might ˹be tempted to˺ become one community ˹of disbelievers˺, We would have supplied the homes of ˹only˺ those who disbelieve in the Most Compassionate with silver roofs and ˹silver˺ stairways to ascend,

And were it not that the people would become one community upon disbelief, We would have made for those who disbelieve in the Most Gracious roofs of silver for their houses and stairs upon which they ascend.

If not for the fear that all people would become disbelievers, Allah could have given disbelievers great wealth and luxury in this world, but he withholds this to not tempt believers.

Verse 34

وَلِبُيُوتِهِمْ أَبْوَابًا وَسُرُرًا عَلَيْهَا يَتَّكِئُونَ

as well as ˹silver˺ gates and thrones to recline on,

And We made for their houses doors of silver and couches [of silver] upon which they could recline, and We made for them gold. And all that is not but the enjoyment of worldly life, and the Hereafter with your Lord is for the righteous, not for others.
