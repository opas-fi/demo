Sura 042

Verse 1

حم

Ḥâ-Mĩm.

(Ha Mim * Ayn Sin Qaf) The discussion about the detached letters at the beginning of Surat Al-Baqarah has preceded.

These are the detached letters of the Quran (Ha Mim), the meaning of which is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

عسق

’Aĩn-Sĩn-Qãf.

(Ha Mim * Ayn Sin Qaf) The discussion about the detached letters at the beginning of Surat Al-Baqarah has preceded.

These are the detached letters of the Quran (Ayn Sin Qaf), the meaning of which is known only to Allah.

Verse 3

كَذَٰلِكَ يُوحِي إِلَيْكَ وَإِلَى الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِكَ اللَّهُ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

And so you ˹O Prophet˺ are sent revelation, just like those before you, by Allah—the Almighty, All-Wise.

Just as Allah has revealed this Quran to you, O Prophet, He revealed the Books and Scriptures to the prophets before you. And He is the Mighty in His vengeance, the Wise in His sayings and actions.

Just as Allah revealed to previous prophets, He is revealing the Quran to Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). He is the Almighty and Wise.

Verse 4

لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ

To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. And He is the Most High, the Greatest.

To Allah alone belongs all that is in the heavens and all that is on the earth, and He is the Most High in His essence, His power, and His domination, the Great, to Whom belongs greatness and majesty.

Everything in the heavens and the earth belongs to Allah. He is the Most High and the Greatest.

Verse 5

تَكَادُ السَّمَاوَاتُ يَتَفَطَّرْنَ مِنْ فَوْقِهِنَّ ۚ وَالْمَلَائِكَةُ يُسَبِّحُونَ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّهِمْ وَيَسْتَغْفِرُونَ لِمَنْ فِي الْأَرْضِ ۗ أَلَا إِنَّ اللَّهَ هُوَ الْغَفُورُ الرَّحِيمُ

The heavens nearly burst, one above the other, ˹in awe of Him˺. And the angels glorify the praises of their Lord, and seek forgiveness for those on earth. Surely Allah alone is the All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

The heavens are about to burst open, each one above the one below it, from the greatness and majesty of the Most Gracious. And the angels glorify their Lord with praise, and they exalt Him above what does not befit Him, and they ask their Lord for forgiveness for the sins of those on earth who believe in Him. Indeed, Allah is the Forgiving of the sins of His believing servants, the Merciful to them.

The heavens are in awe of Allah's majesty, and the angels glorify Him and seek forgiveness for those on Earth. Allah is the All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

Verse 6

وَالَّذِينَ اتَّخَذُوا مِنْ دُونِهِ أَوْلِيَاءَ اللَّهُ حَفِيظٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَمَا أَنْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ بِوَكِيلٍ

As for those who take other protectors besides Him, Allah is Watchful over them. And you ˹O Prophet˺ are not a keeper over them.

And those who have taken besides Allah protectors, they take them as allies and worship them. Allah keeps watch over their actions so that He may recompense them on the Day of Resurrection. And you, O Messenger, are not a guardian over them to keep watch over their actions. You are only a warner, so it is your duty to deliver the message and Our duty to take account.

Allah is watching those who take protectors other than Him. The Prophet's duty is only to warn them, not to control them.

Verse 7

وَكَذَٰلِكَ أَوْحَيْنَا إِلَيْكَ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّا لِتُنْذِرَ أُمَّ الْقُرَىٰ وَمَنْ حَوْلَهَا وَتُنْذِرَ يَوْمَ الْجَمْعِ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ ۚ فَرِيقٌ فِي الْجَنَّةِ وَفَرِيقٌ فِي السَّعِيرِ

And so We have revealed to you a Quran in Arabic, so you may warn the Mother of Cities and everyone around it, and warn of the Day of Gathering—about which there is no doubt—˹when˺ a group will be in Paradise and another in the Blaze.

And just as We revealed to the prophets before you, We have revealed to you an Arabic Quran so that you may warn the people of "Mecca" and those around it from all people, and warn of the torment of the Day of Assembly, which is the Day of Resurrection, there is no doubt about its coming. On that day, people will be in two groups: a group in Paradise, and they are the ones who believed in Allah and followed what was brought to them by His Messenger Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and a group in the Blazing Fire, and they are the ones who disbelieved in Allah and opposed what was brought to them by His Messenger Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace.

The Quran in Arabic is revealed to warn the people of Mecca and the surrounding areas about the Day of Gathering when people will be divided into those in Paradise and those in the Hellfire.

Verse 8

وَلَوْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ لَجَعَلَهُمْ أُمَّةً وَاحِدَةً وَلَٰكِنْ يُدْخِلُ مَنْ يَشَاءُ فِي رَحْمَتِهِ ۚ وَالظَّالِمُونَ مَا لَهُمْ مِنْ وَلِيٍّ وَلَا نَصِيرٍ

Had Allah willed, He could have easily made all ˹humanity˺ into a single community ˹of believers˺. But He admits into His mercy whoever He wills. And the wrongdoers will have no protector or helper.

And if Allah had willed, He would have gathered all His creation on guidance and made them on one guided religion, but He wanted to admit into His mercy whom He wills from His special creation. And the wrongdoers to themselves by associating partners with Allah have no protector to take care of their affairs on the Day of Resurrection, nor any helper to defend them from the punishment of Allah.

Allah could have made all people believers, but He chooses to grant His mercy to whom He wills. The wrongdoers will have no protector or helper.

Verse 9

أَمِ اتَّخَذُوا مِنْ دُونِهِ أَوْلِيَاءَ ۖ فَاللَّهُ هُوَ الْوَلِيُّ وَهُوَ يُحْيِي الْمَوْتَىٰ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

How can they take protectors besides Him? Allah alone is the Protector. He ˹alone˺ gives life to the dead. And He ˹alone˺ is Most Capable of everything.

Rather, these polytheists have taken protectors besides Allah who take care of them, while Allah alone is the Protector, whom His servant should worship and obey, and He protects His believing servants by bringing them out of darkness into light and assisting them in all their affairs. And He gives life to the dead at the Resurrection, and He is Able to do all things, nothing can overcome Him.

Why do people seek protectors besides Allah? He is the only true protector, the giver of life, and capable of all things.

Verse 10

وَمَا اخْتَلَفْتُمْ فِيهِ مِنْ شَيْءٍ فَحُكْمُهُ إِلَى اللَّهِ ۚ ذَٰلِكُمُ اللَّهُ رَبِّي عَلَيْهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ وَإِلَيْهِ أُنِيبُ

˹Say to the believers, O Prophet,˺ “Whatever you may differ about, its judgment rests with Allah. That is Allah—my Lord. In Him I put my trust, and to Him I ˹always˺ turn.”

And in whatever you differ, O people, regarding your religious affairs, its judgment is to be referred to Allah in His Book and the Sunnah of His Messenger, may Allah bless him and grant him peace. That is Allah, my Lord and your Lord, upon Him alone I rely in my affairs, and to Him I return in all my concerns.

Any matter of dispute should be referred to Allah's judgment, as revealed in His Book and the Sunnah. The Prophet places his trust in Allah alone.

Verse 11

فَاطِرُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ جَعَلَ لَكُمْ مِنْ أَنْفُسِكُمْ أَزْوَاجًا وَمِنَ الْأَنْعَامِ أَزْوَاجًا ۖ يَذْرَؤُكُمْ فِيهِ ۚ لَيْسَ كَمِثْلِهِ شَيْءٌ ۖ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ

˹He is˺ the Originator of the heavens and the earth. He has made for you spouses from among yourselves, and ˹made˺ mates for cattle ˹as well˺—multiplying you ˹both˺. There is nothing like Him, for He ˹alone˺ is the All-Hearing, All-Seeing.

Allah, the Exalted, is the Creator of the heavens and the earth, and their Initiator with His power, will, and wisdom. He made for you from yourselves spouses, so that you may find tranquility in them, and He made for you from the livestock pairs, males and females, increasing you by that through procreation. Nothing from His creation resembles or is like Him, neither in His essence, nor in His names, nor in His attributes, nor in His actions; because His names are all beautiful, His attributes are attributes of perfection and greatness, and His actions, the Exalted, brought into existence the magnificent creations without any partner. And He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing. Nothing of the actions and sayings of His creation is hidden from Him, and He will recompense them for that.

Allah is the Originator of the heavens and the earth. He created spouses for humans and pairs for animals, ensuring procreation. Nothing is comparable to Him; He is the All-Hearing and All-Seeing.

Verse 12

لَهُ مَقَالِيدُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۖ يَبْسُطُ الرِّزْقَ لِمَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيَقْدِرُ ۚ إِنَّهُ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيمٌ

To Him belong the keys ˹of the treasuries˺ of the heavens and the earth. He gives abundant or limited provisions to whoever He wills. Indeed, He has ˹perfect˺ knowledge of all things.

To Him, the Exalted, belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth, and in His hand are the keys of mercy and sustenance. He expands His provision for whom He wills among His servants and restricts it for whom He wills. Indeed, blessed and exalted is He, the All-Knowing of everything; nothing from the affairs of His creation is hidden from Him.

The keys to all treasures in the heavens and the earth belong to Allah. He controls the provision of all, giving abundantly or sparingly as He wills. He has perfect knowledge of all things.

Verse 13

شَرَعَ لَكُمْ مِنَ الدِّينِ مَا وَصَّىٰ بِهِ نُوحًا وَالَّذِي أَوْحَيْنَا إِلَيْكَ وَمَا وَصَّيْنَا بِهِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَمُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَىٰ ۖ أَنْ أَقِيمُوا الدِّينَ وَلَا تَتَفَرَّقُوا فِيهِ ۚ كَبُرَ عَلَى الْمُشْرِكِينَ مَا تَدْعُوهُمْ إِلَيْهِ ۚ اللَّهُ يَجْتَبِي إِلَيْهِ مَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيَهْدِي إِلَيْهِ مَنْ يُنِيبُ

He has ordained for you ˹believers˺ the Way which He decreed for Noah, and what We have revealed to you ˹O Prophet˺ and what We decreed for Abraham, Moses, and Jesus, ˹commanding:˺ “Uphold the faith, and make no divisions in it.” What you call the polytheists to is unbearable for them. Allah chooses for Himself whoever He wills, and guides to Himself whoever turns ˹to Him˺.

Allah has ordained for you – O people – from the religion that We have revealed to you -O Messenger, which is Islam- what He enjoined upon Noah to do and to convey, and what We enjoined upon Abraham, Moses, and Jesus (these five are the resolute messengers according to the famous view), that: Establish the religion by believing in the Oneness of Allah and obeying Him and worshiping Him alone, without others, and do not differ in the religion that I have commanded you with. Great is what you call them to, from the Oneness of Allah and sincerity in worshiping Him, to the polytheists. Allah chooses for the Oneness of Allah whom He wills from His creation, and He enables those who return to Him to act in obedience to Him.

Allah has ordained for believers the same faith as for Noah, Abraham, Moses, and Jesus, emphasizing monotheism and unity. The polytheists find this difficult to accept. Allah guides to Himself whom He wills.

Verse 14

وَمَا تَفَرَّقُوا إِلَّا مِنْ بَعْدِ مَا جَاءَهُمُ الْعِلْمُ بَغْيًا بَيْنَهُمْ ۚ وَلَوْلَا كَلِمَةٌ سَبَقَتْ مِنْ رَبِّكَ إِلَىٰ أَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى لَقُضِيَ بَيْنَهُمْ ۚ وَإِنَّ الَّذِينَ أُورِثُوا الْكِتَابَ مِنْ بَعْدِهِمْ لَفِي شَكٍّ مِنْهُ مُرِيبٍ

They did not split ˹into sects˺ out of mutual envy until knowledge came to them. Had it not been for a prior decree from your Lord for an appointed term, the matter would have certainly been settled between them ˹at once˺. And surely those who were made to inherit the Scripture after them are truly in alarming doubt about this ˹Quran˺.

And the polytheists did not divide in their religions, becoming sects and parties, except after the knowledge came to them and the proof was established against them. And what motivated them to do that was nothing but transgression and stubbornness. And were it not for a word that had preceded from your Lord -O Messenger- to delay the punishment from them until a specified time, which is the Day of Resurrection, the matter would have been decided between them by hastening the punishment of the disbelievers among them. And indeed, those who inherited the Torah and the Gospel after these disputants of the truth are in doubt about the religion and faith, trapped in suspicion and the blameworthy disagreement.

The people divided into sects out of envy after knowledge came to them. If not for a prior decree, Allah would have settled matters immediately. Those who inherited the scriptures after them are in doubt about the truth of this Quran.

Verse 15

فَلِذَٰلِكَ فَادْعُ ۖ وَاسْتَقِمْ كَمَا أُمِرْتَ ۖ وَلَا تَتَّبِعْ أَهْوَاءَهُمْ ۖ وَقُلْ آمَنْتُ بِمَا أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ مِنْ كِتَابٍ ۖ وَأُمِرْتُ لِأَعْدِلَ بَيْنَكُمُ ۖ اللَّهُ رَبُّنَا وَرَبُّكُمْ ۖ لَنَا أَعْمَالُنَا وَلَكُمْ أَعْمَالُكُمْ ۖ لَا حُجَّةَ بَيْنَنَا وَبَيْنَكُمُ ۖ اللَّهُ يَجْمَعُ بَيْنَنَا ۖ وَإِلَيْهِ الْمَصِيرُ

Because of that, you ˹O Prophet˺ will invite ˹all˺. Be steadfast as you are commanded, and do not follow their desires. And say, “I believe in every Scripture Allah has revealed. And I am commanded to judge fairly among you. Allah is our Lord and your Lord. We will be accountable for our deeds and you for yours. There is no ˹need for˺ contention between us. Allah will gather us together ˹for judgment˺. And to Him is the final return.”

So, to that upright religion that Allah ordained for the prophets and enjoined upon them, invite – O Messenger – the servants of Allah, and be steadfast as Allah has commanded you, and do not follow the desires of those who doubt the truth and deviate from the religion, and say: I have believed in all the books sent down from the sky to the prophets, and my Lord has commanded me to be just between you in judgment. Allah is our Lord and your Lord; we will have the reward for our righteous deeds, and you will have the recompense for your evil deeds. There is no dispute or argument between us and you after the truth has become clear. Allah will gather us together on the Day of Resurrection, then He will judge between us justly in what we differed about, and to Him is the return and the destination, and He will reward each one as they deserve.

The Prophet is commanded to invite all to the upright religion. He believes in all revealed scriptures and is commanded to judge justly. Allah is the Lord of all, and everyone will be held accountable for their deeds. There should be no contention; Allah will gather all for judgment.

Verse 16

وَالَّذِينَ يُحَاجُّونَ فِي اللَّهِ مِنْ بَعْدِ مَا اسْتُجِيبَ لَهُ حُجَّتُهُمْ دَاحِضَةٌ عِنْدَ رَبِّهِمْ وَعَلَيْهِمْ غَضَبٌ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ شَدِيدٌ

As for those who dispute about Allah after He is ˹already˺ acknowledged ˹by many˺, their argument is futile in the sight of their Lord. Upon them is wrath, and they will suffer a severe punishment.

And those who argue about the religion of Allah, which Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, was sent with, after the people have responded to it and submitted, their argument and dispute are invalid and lost with their Lord, and upon them is the wrath of Allah in this world, and for them in the Hereafter is a severe punishment, which is the Fire.

This verse warns those who argue against the truth of Allah's religion after it has been made clear. Their arguments are worthless in God's sight, and they will face His wrath and a severe punishment.

Verse 17

اللَّهُ الَّذِي أَنْزَلَ الْكِتَابَ بِالْحَقِّ وَالْمِيزَانَ ۗ وَمَا يُدْرِيكَ لَعَلَّ السَّاعَةَ قَرِيبٌ

It is Allah Who has revealed the Book with the truth and the balance ˹of justice˺. You never know, perhaps the Hour is near.

Allah is the One who has sent down the Quran and all the other revealed books with truth, and sent down the balance, which is justice, to judge between people with fairness. And what can inform you that perhaps the Hour, in which the Resurrection will occur, is near?

Allah has revealed the Quran with truth and justice. The Day of Judgment could be closer than one might think, emphasizing the urgency of faith and good deeds.

Verse 18

يَسْتَعْجِلُ بِهَا الَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِهَا ۖ وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مُشْفِقُونَ مِنْهَا وَيَعْلَمُونَ أَنَّهَا الْحَقُّ ۗ أَلَا إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يُمَارُونَ فِي السَّاعَةِ لَفِي ضَلَالٍ بَعِيدٍ

Those who disbelieve in it ˹ask to˺ hasten it ˹mockingly˺. But the believers are fearful of it, knowing that it is the truth. Surely those who dispute about the Hour have gone far astray.

Those who do not believe in the Hour ask to hasten its arrival, mockingly and scornfully, while those who believe in it are fearful of its occurrence, and they know that it is the truth in which there is no doubt. Indeed, those who argue about the occurrence of the Hour are in extreme error, far from the truth.

Disbelievers mock the idea of the Day of Judgment, while believers are mindful of its reality. Those who argue against it are deeply misguided.

Verse 19

اللَّهُ لَطِيفٌ بِعِبَادِهِ يَرْزُقُ مَنْ يَشَاءُ ۖ وَهُوَ الْقَوِيُّ الْعَزِيزُ

Allah is Ever Kind to His servants. He provides ˹abundantly˺ to whoever He wills. And He is the All-Powerful, Almighty.

Allah is gracious to His servants; He increases the provision for whomever He wills and decreases it for whomever He wills according to His wisdom, and He is the Strong, to whom all strength belongs, the Mighty in His vengeance against those who disobey Him.

Allah is kind and generous to His servants. He provides for them as He wills and possesses all power and might.

Verse 20

مَنْ كَانَ يُرِيدُ حَرْثَ الْآخِرَةِ نَزِدْ لَهُ فِي حَرْثِهِ ۖ وَمَنْ كَانَ يُرِيدُ حَرْثَ الدُّنْيَا نُؤْتِهِ مِنْهَا وَمَا لَهُ فِي الْآخِرَةِ مِنْ نَصِيبٍ

Whoever desires the harvest of the Hereafter, We will increase their harvest. And whoever desires ˹only˺ the harvest of this world, We will give them some of it, but they will have no share in the Hereafter.

Whoever desires the reward of the Hereafter through his deeds, and fulfills the rights of Allah and spends in the call to religion, We will increase his good deeds, so We will multiply the reward of the good deed to ten times its like up to what Allah wills of increase. And whoever desires only this worldly life through his deeds, We will give him from it what We have apportioned for him, and he will not have any reward in the Hereafter.

Those who strive for the Hereafter will have their rewards increased by God. Those who focus solely on this world will receive some of its blessings, but will have no share in the Hereafter.

Verse 21

أَمْ لَهُمْ شُرَكَاءُ شَرَعُوا لَهُمْ مِنَ الدِّينِ مَا لَمْ يَأْذَنْ بِهِ اللَّهُ ۚ وَلَوْلَا كَلِمَةُ الْفَصْلِ لَقُضِيَ بَيْنَهُمْ ۗ وَإِنَّ الظَّالِمِينَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ

Or do they have associate-gods who have ordained for them some ˹polytheistic˺ beliefs, which Allah has not authorized? Had it not been for ˹prior˺ decree on Judgment, the matter would have certainly been settled between them ˹at once˺. And surely the wrongdoers will suffer a painful punishment.

Or do these polytheists with Allah have partners in their polytheism and misguidance, who have innovated for them from religion and polytheism what Allah has not permitted? And if it were not for Allah's decree and His predestination in giving them respite, and not hastening the punishment for them in this world, judgment would have been passed between them by hastening the punishment for them. And indeed, the disbelievers in Allah will have a painful and agonizing punishment on the Day of Resurrection.

This verse challenges the polytheists and their false gods, asking if they have any divine authority for their practices. If it were not for a set term, punishment would be immediate for the wrongdoers.

Verse 22

تَرَى الظَّالِمِينَ مُشْفِقِينَ مِمَّا كَسَبُوا وَهُوَ وَاقِعٌ بِهِمْ ۗ وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ فِي رَوْضَاتِ الْجَنَّاتِ ۖ لَهُمْ مَا يَشَاءُونَ عِنْدَ رَبِّهِمْ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ هُوَ الْفَضْلُ الْكَبِيرُ

You will see the wrongdoers fearful ˹of the punishment˺ for what they committed but it will be inevitable for them, whereas those who believe and do good will be in the lush Gardens of Paradise. They will have whatever they desire from their Lord. That is ˹truly˺ the greatest bounty.

You see, O Messenger, the disbelievers on the Day of Resurrection fearful of Allah's punishment for what they earned in the world of evil deeds, and the punishment is descending upon them, and they will inevitably taste it, and those who believed in Allah and obeyed Him are in the gardens of Paradise, its palaces and the bliss of the Hereafter, they will have whatever they desire with their Lord. That which Allah has given them of grace and honor is the grace that cannot be described, and the minds cannot comprehend it.

On the Day of Judgment, wrongdoers will be fearful of the inevitable punishment. Believers, however, will dwell in Paradise and enjoy all that they desire, which is the ultimate bounty.

Verse 23

Allah responds to those who believe and do good, increasing their reward from His bounty. Disbelievers will face severe punishment.

This verse explains that if Allah were to provide abundant sustenance to everyone, they would become arrogant and transgress. Instead, He provides for His servants in perfect measure, aware of what is best for them.

Verse 28

وَهُوَ الَّذِي يُنَزِّلُ الْغَيْثَ مِنْ بَعْدِ مَا قَنَطُوا وَيَنْشُرُ رَحْمَتَهُ ۚ وَهُوَ الْوَلِيُّ الْحَمِيدُ

Allah is the One who sends down rain after people have lost hope, spreading His mercy. He is the Guardian and Praiseworthy.

The creation of the heavens and the earth, and the diverse living beings within them, are signs of Allah's power. He is capable of gathering all of creation for the Day of Resurrection.

Verse 30

Any misfortune that befalls people is often a result of their own actions, but Allah pardons many of their misdeeds.

Verse 31

Verse 34

Allah can destroy ships because of people's sins, but He also forgives much. This highlights His justice and mercy.

Believers avoid major sins and forgive others when angered, seeking Allah's reward.

Allah may grant both sons and daughters or make someone infertile. He is All-Knowing and Most Capable.

Verse 51

وَمَا كَانَ لِبَشَرٍ أَنْ يُكَلِّمَهُ اللَّهُ إِلَّا وَحْيًا أَوْ مِنْ وَرَاءِ حِجَابٍ أَوْ يُرْسِلَ رَسُولًا فَيُوحِيَ بِإِذْنِهِ مَا يَشَاءُ ۚ إِنَّهُ عَلِيٌّ حَكِيمٌ

It is not ˹possible˺ for a human being to have Allah communicate with them, except through inspiration, or from behind a veil, or by sending a messenger-angel to reveal whatever He wills by His permission. He is surely Most High, All-Wise.

It is not for any human being that Allah should speak to him except by revelation which Allah reveals to him, or that He speaks from behind a veil, as He spoke to Moses, peace be upon him, or He sends a messenger as Gabriel, peace be upon him, descends to the one sent to, and reveals by the permission of his Lord, not by his own whim, what Allah wills to reveal. Indeed, He is Most High in His Essence, Names, Attributes and Actions, He has subjugated all things and the creatures are submissive to Him, All-Wise in managing the affairs of His creation. And in the verse is confirmation of the attribute of speech for Allah, Most High, in a manner befitting His Majesty and Great Authority.

Allah communicates with humans through inspiration, from behind a veil, or through a messenger-angel. He is Most High and All-Wise.

Verse 52

وَكَذَٰلِكَ أَوْحَيْنَا إِلَيْكَ رُوحًا مِنْ أَمْرِنَا ۚ مَا كُنْتَ تَدْرِي مَا الْكِتَابُ وَلَا الْإِيمَانُ وَلَٰكِنْ جَعَلْنَاهُ نُورًا نَهْدِي بِهِ مَنْ نَشَاءُ مِنْ عِبَادِنَا ۚ وَإِنَّكَ لَتَهْدِي إِلَىٰ صِرَاطٍ مُسْتَقِيمٍ

And so We have sent to you ˹O Prophet˺ a revelation by Our command. You did not know of ˹this˺ Book and faith ˹before˺. But We have made it a light, by which We guide whoever We will of Our servants. And you are truly leading ˹all˺ to the Straight Path—

And just as We revealed to the prophets before you - O Prophet - We revealed to you a Quran from Us. You did not know before it what the previous scriptures, nor faith, nor divine laws were. But We made the Quran a light for people, guiding by it whom We will among Our servants to the straight path. And you - O Messenger - indeed guide and direct, by the permission of Allah, to a straight path - which is Islam - the path of Allah, to whom belongs the dominion of all that is in the heavens and all that is on the earth; He has no partner in that. This verse highlights that the Quran is a revelation from Allah to Prophet Muhammad. It emphasizes that the Prophet did not know the details of the scriptures and faith before the revelation, and the Quran now serves as a guiding light.
