Verse 1

يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ

O you wrapped ˹in your clothes˺!

O you who have wrapped yourself [in clothing], arise [to pray] the night, except for a little. Arise [for prayer] half of it - or subtract from it a little, so that it reaches a third, or add to the half, until it reaches two-thirds, and recite the Quran with measured recitation, making clear the letters and the pauses.

This verse addresses the Prophet, instructing him to pray during the night.

Verse 5

إِنَّا سَنُلْقِي عَلَيْكَ قَوْلًا ثَقِيلًا

˹For˺ We will soon send upon you a weighty revelation.

Indeed, We will cast upon you, [O Prophet], a weighty Quran, containing commands, prohibitions, and legal rulings.

This verse explains that a weighty revelation, the Quran, is soon to be sent to the Prophet.

Verse 6

إِنَّ نَاشِئَةَ اللَّيْلِ هِيَ أَشَدُّ وَطْئًا وَأَقْوَمُ قِيلًا

Indeed, worship in the night is more impactful and suitable for recitation.

Indeed, worship that arises in the depths of the night is more effective in the heart and clearer in speech due to the heart being free from the concerns of the world.

Night worship is highlighted as more impactful and conducive to clear recitation due to its tranquility.

Verse 7

إِنَّ لَكَ فِي النَّهَارِ سَبْحًا طَوِيلًا

For during the day you are over-occupied ˹with worldly duties˺.

Indeed, you have during the day a diversion and a turning in your interests, and a wide engagement in matters of the message, so devote yourself at night to the worship of your Lord.

The verse notes that the day is filled with worldly duties, making the night a better time for devotion.

Verse 8

وَاذْكُرِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ وَتَبَتَّلْ إِلَيْهِ تَبْتِيلًا

˹Always˺ remember the Name of your Lord, and devote yourself to Him wholeheartedly.

And remember, [O Prophet], the name of your Lord, invoke Him by it, and devote yourself to Him with complete devotion in your worship and put your trust in Him. He is the Owner of the East and the West; there is no deity worthy of worship except Him, so rely on Him, and entrust your affairs to Him.

This verse urges remembering Allah's name and dedicating oneself wholeheartedly to Him.

Verse 9

And remember, [O Prophet], the name of your Lord, invoke Him by it, and devote yourself to Him with complete devotion in your worship and put your trust in Him. He is the Owner of the East and the West; there is no deity worthy of worship except Him, so rely on Him, and entrust your affairs to Him.

Allah is declared as the Lord of the east and west, and the only one to rely upon as Trustee of Affairs.

Verse 10

وَاصْبِرْ عَلَىٰ مَا يَقُولُونَ وَاهْجُرْهُمْ هَجْرًا جَمِيلًا

Be patient ˹O Prophet˺ with what they say, and depart from them courteously.

And be patient over what the polytheists say about you and your religion, and oppose them in their false deeds, while turning away from them, and leaving vengeance against them.

The Prophet is advised to be patient with the disbelievers and to depart from them courteously.

Verse 11

Leave Me, [O Messenger], with those who deny My verses, the people of pleasure and luxury in the world, and give them a little respite by delaying the punishment from them until the decree reaches its term with their punishment.

Allah says to leave the disbelievers who are in luxury to Him and bear with them for a little while.

Verse 12

This verse describes the severe punishment awaiting the deniers: shackles and a raging fire.

Verse 14

Verse 15

Indeed, We have sent to you - O people of Mecca - Muhammad as a messenger, a witness over you for what has come from you of disbelief and disobedience, just as We sent Moses as a messenger to the tyrant Pharaoh. And Pharaoh denied Moses and did not believe in his message, and disobeyed his command, so We destroyed him with a severe destruction. And in this is a warning against disobeying the Messenger Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, for fear that the disobedient will befall like what befell Pharaoh and his people.

Allah sent a messenger (Prophet Muhammad) to the people as a witness, just as He sent a messenger to Pharaoh.

Verse 20

إِنَّ رَبَّكَ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ تَقُومُ أَدْنَىٰ مِنْ ثُلُثَيِ اللَّيْلِ وَنِصْفَهُ وَثُلُثَهُ وَطَائِفَةٌ مِنَ الَّذِينَ مَعَكَ ۚ وَاللَّهُ يُقَدِّرُ اللَّيْلَ وَالنَّهَارَ ۚ عَلِمَ أَنْ لَنْ تُحْصُوهُ فَتَابَ عَلَيْكُمْ ۖ فَاقْرَءُوا مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنَ الْقُرْآنِ ۚ عَلِمَ أَنْ سَيَكُونُ مِنْكُمْ مَرْضَىٰ ۙ وَآخَرُونَ يَضْرِبُونَ فِي الْأَرْضِ يَبْتَغُونَ مِنْ فَضْلِ اللَّهِ ۙ وَآخَرُونَ يُقَاتِلُونَ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ ۖ فَاقْرَءُوا مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنْهُ ۚ وَأَقِيمُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَآتُوا الزَّكَاةَ وَأَقْرِضُوا اللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا ۚ وَمَا تُقَدِّمُوا لِأَنْفُسِكُمْ مِنْ خَيْرٍ تَجِدُوهُ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ هُوَ خَيْرًا وَأَعْظَمَ أَجْرًا ۚ وَاسْتَغْفِرُوا اللَّهَ ۖ إِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

Surely your Lord knows that you ˹O Prophet˺ stand ˹in prayer˺ for nearly two-thirds of the night, or ˹sometimes˺ half of it, or a third, as do some of those with you. Allah ˹alone˺ keeps a ˹precise˺ measure of the day and night. He knows that you ˹believers˺ are unable to endure this, and has turned to you in mercy. So recite ˹in prayer˺ whatever you can from the Quran. He knows that some of you will be sick, some will be travelling throughout the land seeking Allah’s bounty, and some fighting in the cause of Allah. So recite whatever you can from it. And ˹continue to˺ perform ˹regular˺ prayers, pay alms-tax, and lend to Allah a good loan. Whatever good you send forth for yourselves, you will find it with Allah far better and more rewarding. And seek Allah’s forgiveness. Surely Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

Indeed, your Lord - O Prophet - knows that you stand for prayer at night for less than two-thirds of it, and sometimes half of it, and sometimes one-third of it, and a group of your companions stand with you. And Allah alone is the One who determines the night and the day, and knows their measures, and what passes and remains of them. Allah knows that you cannot keep the whole night in prayer, so He has made it easy for you, so recite in the night prayer what is easy for you to recite from the Quran. Allah knows that there will be among you those whom illness will prevent from praying at night, and other people who travel throughout the land for trade and work, seeking from Allah's lawful provision, and other people who strive in the cause of Allah to exalt His word and spread His religion. So recite in your prayer what is easy for you from the Quran, and be steadfast in the obligatory prayers, and give the obligatory charity, and give charity in good and virtuous ways from your wealth seeking the pleasure of Allah. And whatever good you do, of acts of virtue and obedience, you will receive its reward and recompense from Allah on the Day of Resurrection, better than what you have presented in this world, and a greater reward, and seek Allah's forgiveness in all your affairs. Indeed, Allah is Forgiving and Merciful towards you.

Allah knows the Prophet's dedication in night prayers, sometimes two-thirds, half, or a third of the night. He has made it easier due to believers' limitations and other responsibilities, like illness, travel, and fighting for His cause. The verse encourages reciting the Quran as much as possible, performing prayers, paying Zakat, and giving charity. These good deeds will be rewarded greatly by Allah. It also emphasizes seeking forgiveness, as Allah is All-Forgiving and Most Merciful.
