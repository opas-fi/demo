Sura 007

Verse 1

المص

Alif-Lãm-Mĩm-Ṣãd.

The discussion on the disjointed letters at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah has preceded.

This verse begins with the disjointed letters *Alif-Lãm-Mĩm-Ṣãd*. The meaning of these letters is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

كِتَابٌ أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْكَ فَلَا يَكُنْ فِي صَدْرِكَ حَرَجٌ مِنْهُ لِتُنْذِرَ بِهِ وَذِكْرَىٰ لِلْمُؤْمِنِينَ

˹This is˺ a Book sent down to you ˹O Prophet˺—do not let anxiety into your heart regarding it—so with it you may warn ˹the disbelievers˺, and as a reminder to the believers.

This Quran is a great book that Allah has revealed to you - O Messenger - so let there be no doubt in your heart that it was revealed from Allah, and do not hesitate in conveying it and warning with it. We have revealed it to you so that you may warn the disbelievers and remind the believers.

This verse emphasizes that the Quran is a divine book revealed to Prophet Muhammad. It serves as a warning to disbelievers and a reminder to believers.

Verse 3

اتَّبِعُوا مَا أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْكُمْ مِنْ رَبِّكُمْ وَلَا تَتَّبِعُوا مِنْ دُونِهِ أَوْلِيَاءَ ۗ قَلِيلًا مَا تَذَكَّرُونَ

Follow what has been sent down to you from your Lord, and do not take others as guardians besides Him. How seldom are you mindful!

Follow - O people - what has been revealed to you from your Lord from the Book and the Sunnah, by complying with the commands and avoiding the prohibitions, and do not follow other than Allah any allies such as devils, rabbis, and monks. Indeed, little do you take heed and consider, so that you return to the truth.

People are urged to follow the teachings of the Quran and Sunnah, and to avoid taking any guides other than Allah. The verse highlights that many people are not mindful of this guidance.

Verse 4

وَكَمْ مِنْ قَرْيَةٍ أَهْلَكْنَاهَا فَجَاءَهَا بَأْسُنَا بَيَاتًا أَوْ هُمْ قَائِلُونَ

˹Imagine˺ how many societies We have destroyed! Our torment took them by surprise ˹while sleeping˺ at night or midday.

And many a town We destroyed its people because of their opposition to Our messengers and their denial of them, which resulted in the disgrace of this world followed by the humiliation of the Hereafter. So Our punishment came to them once while they were sleeping at night and once while they were sleeping during the day. Allah specified these two times because they are times of tranquility and rest, so the coming of the punishment at these times is more awful and severe.

The verse mentions that many communities were destroyed when they rejected messengers. The punishment came to them unexpectedly, either at night or midday.

Verse 5

فَمَا كَانَ دَعْوَاهُمْ إِذْ جَاءَهُمْ بَأْسُنَا إِلَّا أَنْ قَالُوا إِنَّا كُنَّا ظَالِمِينَ

Their only cry—when overwhelmed by Our torment—was, “We have indeed been wrongdoers.”

So their only utterance when the punishment came was the admission of their sins and wrongdoing, and that they were deserving of the punishment that befell them.

When faced with Allah's punishment, the only words of the wrongdoers were admissions of their own wrong actions.

Verse 6

فَلَنَسْأَلَنَّ الَّذِينَ أُرْسِلَ إِلَيْهِمْ وَلَنَسْأَلَنَّ الْمُرْسَلِينَ

We will surely question those who received messengers and We will question the messengers ˹themselves˺.

So We will surely question the nations to whom the messengers were sent: What was your response to Our messengers to you? And We will surely question the messengers about their conveyance of the messages of their Lord, and about what their nations answered them with.

Allah will question both the messengers and the people who received their messages on the Day of Judgement.

Verse 7

فَلَنَقُصَّنَّ عَلَيْهِمْ بِعِلْمٍ ۖ وَمَا كُنَّا غَائِبِينَ

Then We will give them a full account with sure knowledge—for We were never absent.

Then We will surely relate to all of creation what they did with Our knowledge of their deeds in the world regarding what We commanded them, and what We forbade them, and We were not absent from them in any situation.

Allah will present a full and accurate account of people's deeds with complete knowledge, as He was never absent from any situation.

Verse 8

وَالْوَزْنُ يَوْمَئِذٍ الْحَقُّ ۚ فَمَنْ ثَقُلَتْ مَوَازِينُهُ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ

The weighing on that Day will be just. As for those whose scale will be heavy ˹with good deeds˺, ˹only˺ they will be successful.

And the weighing of people's deeds on the Day of Resurrection will be with a real balance with justice and fairness in which there is no injustice. So those whose scales of deeds are heavy - due to their many good deeds - then those are the successful ones.

On the Day of Judgment, the weighing of deeds will be just. Those whose scales are heavy with good deeds will be successful.

Verse 9

وَمَنْ خَفَّتْ مَوَازِينُهُ فَأُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ خَسِرُوا أَنْفُسَهُمْ بِمَا كَانُوا بِآيَاتِنَا يَظْلِمُونَ

But those whose scale is light, they have doomed themselves for wrongfully denying Our signs.

And those whose scales of deeds are light - due to their many bad deeds - then those are the ones who have lost their share of the pleasure of Allah, due to their transgression by denying the verses of Allah and not submitting to them.

Those whose scales are light with good deeds due to their denial of Allah's signs have lost their share of Allah's pleasure.

Verse 10

وَلَقَدْ مَكَّنَّاكُمْ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَجَعَلْنَا لَكُمْ فِيهَا مَعَايِشَ ۗ قَلِيلًا مَا تَشْكُرُونَ

We have indeed established you on earth and provided you with a means of livelihood. ˹Yet˺ you seldom give any thanks.

And We have indeed established you - O people - in the earth and made it a place of settlement for you, and We have made for you in it what you live by of foods and drinks. Yet, your gratitude to the blessings of Allah is little.

Allah reminds humanity that He has established them on Earth and provided for their sustenance, yet they are rarely thankful for these blessings.

Verse 11

وَلَقَدْ خَلَقْنَاكُمْ ثُمَّ صَوَّرْنَاكُمْ ثُمَّ قُلْنَا لِلْمَلَائِكَةِ اسْجُدُوا لِآدَمَ فَسَجَدُوا إِلَّا إِبْلِيسَ لَمْ يَكُنْ مِنَ السَّاجِدِينَ

Surely We created you, then shaped you, then said to the angels, “Prostrate before Adam,” so they all did—but not Iblîs, who refused to prostrate with the others.

And We have indeed bestowed upon you the blessing of creating your origin - which is your father Adam from nothingness - then We fashioned him in his preferred form over much of the creation. Then We ordered Our angels, peace be upon them, to prostrate to him - as an honor and respect and a demonstration of Adam's excellence - so they all prostrated. But Iblis, who was with them, was not among those who prostrated to Adam, due to his envy of him for this great honor.

Allah recounts the creation of Adam, then the angels' prostration to him, except for Iblis who refused out of envy.

Verse 12

قَالَ مَا مَنَعَكَ أَلَّا تَسْجُدَ إِذْ أَمَرْتُكَ ۖ قَالَ أَنَا خَيْرٌ مِنْهُ خَلَقْتَنِي مِنْ نَارٍ وَخَلَقْتَهُ مِنْ طِينٍ

Allah asked, “What prevented you from prostrating when I commanded you?” He replied, “I am better than he is: You created me from fire and him from clay.”

Allah the Almighty said, rebuking Iblis for his refusal to prostrate: "What prevented you from prostrating when I commanded you?" Iblis said: "I am better than him in creation; for I was created from fire, and he was created from clay." So he saw that fire is more noble than clay.

Allah questions Iblis (Satan) for not prostrating to Adam. Iblis refuses, citing his creation from fire as superior to Adam's from clay, displaying arrogance.

Verse 13

قَالَ فَاهْبِطْ مِنْهَا فَمَا يَكُونُ لَكَ أَنْ تَتَكَبَّرَ فِيهَا فَاخْرُجْ إِنَّكَ مِنَ الصَّاغِرِينَ

Allah said, “Then get down from Paradise! It is not for you to be arrogant here. So get out! You are truly one of the disgraced.”

Allah said to Iblis: "Descend from Paradise, for it is not for you to be arrogant therein. So get out of Paradise, indeed, you are of those debased and disgraced."

Allah orders Iblis to leave Paradise for his arrogance, declaring him disgraced.

Verse 14

قَالَ أَنْظِرْنِي إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ

He appealed, “Then delay my end until the Day of their resurrection.”

Iblis said to Allah - Majestic and Exalted - when he despaired of His mercy: "Grant me respite until the Day of Resurrection; so that I may be able to mislead those of the children of Adam whom I can."

Iblis asks Allah to delay his death until the Day of Resurrection, seeking more time to mislead humanity.

Verse 15

قَالَ إِنَّكَ مِنَ الْمُنْظَرِينَ

Allah said, “You are delayed ˹until the appointed Day˺.”

Allah the Almighty said: "Indeed, you are of those upon whom I have decreed respite until the first blowing of the horn, when all of creation dies."

Allah grants Iblis respite until the appointed time, the blowing of the horn, a time when all creation will perish.

Verse 16

قَالَ فَبِمَا أَغْوَيْتَنِي لَأَقْعُدَنَّ لَهُمْ صِرَاطَكَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ

He said, “For leaving me to stray I will lie in ambush for them on Your Straight Path.

Iblis, may Allah curse him, said: "Because You have caused me to go astray, I will surely lie in wait for them on Your straight path, and I will surely turn them away from Islam which is the natural disposition You have created them upon."

Iblis vows to obstruct humanity from Allah's Straight Path as retribution for his own misguidance.

Verse 17

ثُمَّ لَآتِيَنَّهُمْ مِنْ بَيْنِ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِمْ وَعَنْ أَيْمَانِهِمْ وَعَنْ شَمَائِلِهِمْ ۖ وَلَا تَجِدُ أَكْثَرَهُمْ شَاكِرِينَ

I will approach them from their front, their back, their right, their left, and then You will find most of them ungrateful.”

Then I will come to them from all directions and sides, so I will turn them away from the truth, and I will make falsehood appealing to them, and I will make them desire this world, and I will make them doubt the Hereafter, and You will not find most of the children of Adam thankful for Your blessings.

Iblis declares he will approach humans from all directions, tempting them away from righteousness and gratitude.

Verse 18

قَالَ اخْرُجْ مِنْهَا مَذْءُومًا مَدْحُورًا ۖ لَمَنْ تَبِعَكَ مِنْهُمْ لَأَمْلَأَنَّ جَهَنَّمَ مِنْكُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ

Allah said, “Get out of Paradise! You are disgraced and rejected! I will certainly fill up Hell with you and your followers all together.”

Allah the Almighty said to Iblis: "Get out of Paradise, despised and banished. I will surely fill Hell with you and with all those among the children of Adam who follow you."

Allah declares Iblis banished and warns that Hell will be filled with him and his followers.

Verse 19

وَيَا آدَمُ اسْكُنْ أَنْتَ وَزَوْجُكَ الْجَنَّةَ فَكُلَا مِنْ حَيْثُ شِئْتُمَا وَلَا تَقْرَبَا هَٰذِهِ الشَّجَرَةَ فَتَكُونَا مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ

˹Allah said,˺ “O Adam! Live with your wife in Paradise and eat from wherever you please, but do not approach this tree, or else you will be wrongdoers.”

And O Adam, dwell, you and your wife Hawa, in Paradise and eat from its fruits wherever you wish, but do not eat from the fruit of a tree (He designated for them), for if you do so, you will be among the wrongdoers who transgress the limits of Allah.

Allah allows Adam and his wife to live in Paradise and enjoy its fruits, but forbids them from approaching a specific tree.

Verse 20

فَوَسْوَسَ لَهُمَا الشَّيْطَانُ لِيُبْدِيَ لَهُمَا مَا وُورِيَ عَنْهُمَا مِنْ سَوْآتِهِمَا وَقَالَ مَا نَهَاكُمَا رَبُّكُمَا عَنْ هَٰذِهِ الشَّجَرَةِ إِلَّا أَنْ تَكُونَا مَلَكَيْنِ أَوْ تَكُونَا مِنَ الْخَالِدِينَ

Then Satan tempted them in order to expose what was hidden of their nakedness. He said, “Your Lord has forbidden this tree to you only to prevent you from becoming angels or immortals.”

So Satan whispered to Adam and Hawa to cause them to disobey Allah the Almighty by eating from that tree which Allah had forbidden them; so that the consequence would be the uncovering of what had been concealed of their private parts, and he said to them, in an attempt to deceive them: "Your Lord has only forbidden you from eating the fruit of this tree lest you become angels or lest you become among the immortal ones."

Satan tempts Adam and his wife by suggesting the forbidden tree would make them angels or grant them immortality.

Verse 21

وَقَاسَمَهُمَا إِنِّي لَكُمَا لَمِنَ النَّاصِحِينَ

And he swore to them, “I am truly your sincere advisor.”

And Satan swore to Adam and Hawa by Allah that he was of those who advised them in his counsel to them to eat from the tree, and he was a liar in that.

Satan swears to Adam and his wife that he is their sincere advisor, a false claim aimed at deceiving them.

Verse 22

فَدَلَّاهُمَا بِغُرُورٍ ۚ فَلَمَّا ذَاقَا الشَّجَرَةَ بَدَتْ لَهُمَا سَوْآتُهُمَا وَطَفِقَا يَخْصِفَانِ عَلَيْهِمَا مِنْ وَرَقِ الْجَنَّةِ ۖ وَنَادَاهُمَا رَبُّهُمَا أَلَمْ أَنْهَكُمَا عَنْ تِلْكُمَا الشَّجَرَةِ وَأَقُلْ لَكُمَا إِنَّ الشَّيْطَانَ لَكُمَا عَدُوٌّ مُبِينٌ

So he brought about their fall through deception. And when they tasted of the tree, their nakedness was exposed to them, prompting them to cover themselves with leaves from Paradise. Then their Lord called out to them, “Did I not forbid you from that tree and ˹did I not˺ tell you that Satan is your sworn enemy?”

So he emboldened them and deceived them, and they ate from the tree which Allah had forbidden them to approach. So, when they ate from it, their private parts were exposed, and that which Allah had concealed from them before the transgression was removed, so they began to fasten some of the leaves of Paradise on their private parts. And their Lord, Majestic and Exalted, called out to them, "Did I not forbid you from eating from that tree, and did I not say to you: indeed, Satan is to you a clear enemy?" And in this verse is evidence that the uncovering of the private parts is of the great matters, and that it was and still is reprehensible in human nature, and ugly to the minds.

Satan's deception leads Adam and his wife to eat from the forbidden tree, exposing their nakedness. Allah reminds them of His warning and of Satan's enmity.

Verse 23

قَالَا رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَا أَنْفُسَنَا وَإِنْ لَمْ تَغْفِرْ لَنَا وَتَرْحَمْنَا لَنَكُونَنَّ مِنَ الْخَاسِرِينَ

They replied, “Our Lord! We have wronged ourselves. If You do not forgive us and have mercy on us, we will certainly be losers.”

Adam and Eve said: Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves by eating from the tree, and if You do not forgive us and have mercy on us, we will surely be among those who have lost their share in this world and the Hereafter. (And these are the words that Adam received from his Lord, so he called upon Him with them, and Allah accepted his repentance).

Adam and Eve acknowledge their wrongdoing and seek Allah's forgiveness and mercy, understanding that without it they would be among the losers.

Verse 24

قَالَ اهْبِطُوا بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِي الْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّ وَمَتَاعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍ

Allah said, “Descend as enemies to each other. You will find in the earth a residence and provision for your appointed stay.”

Allah Almighty said, addressing Adam, Eve, and Iblis: Descend from the heaven to the earth, and some of you will be enemies to others, and for you on earth will be a place to settle, and you will enjoy yourselves until the end of your terms.

Allah commands Adam, Eve, and Iblis to descend to Earth, where they will be in conflict with each other. Earth is also designated as their temporary residence and source of provision.

Verse 25

قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ

He added, “There you will live, there you will die, and from there you will be resurrected.”

Allah Almighty said to Adam, Eve, and their offspring: In it you shall live, that is, on earth you will spend the days of your worldly lives, and in it will be your death, and from it your Lord will bring you forth, and gather you resurrected on the Day of Resurrection.

On Earth, humans will live, die, and be resurrected on the Day of Judgment. This emphasizes the cycle of life, death, and the afterlife.

Verse 26

يَا بَنِي آدَمَ قَدْ أَنْزَلْنَا عَلَيْكُمْ لِبَاسًا يُوَارِي سَوْآتِكُمْ وَرِيشًا ۖ وَلِبَاسُ التَّقْوَىٰ ذَٰلِكَ خَيْرٌ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ مِنْ آيَاتِ اللَّهِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَذَّكَّرُونَ

O children of Adam! We have provided for you clothing to cover your nakedness and as an adornment. However, the best clothing is righteousness. This is one of Allah’s bounties, so perhaps you will be mindful.

O children of Adam, We have made for you clothing to cover your private parts, which is the clothing of necessity, and clothing for adornment and beautification, which is from perfection and pleasure. And the clothing of the fear of Allah Almighty by doing what is commanded and avoiding what is prohibited is the best clothing for the believer. That is what Allah has bestowed upon you of the signs of Allah's Lordship, Oneness, favor, and mercy to His servants; so that you may remember these blessings and be grateful to Allah for them. And in that is a favor from Allah Almighty to His creation with these blessings.

Allah provides clothing for both covering and adornment, yet the best garment is righteousness. This verse highlights Allah's blessings and encourages mindfulness of Him.

Verse 27

يَا بَنِي آدَمَ لَا يَفْتِنَنَّكُمُ الشَّيْطَانُ كَمَا أَخْرَجَ أَبَوَيْكُمْ مِنَ الْجَنَّةِ يَنْزِعُ عَنْهُمَا لِبَاسَهُمَا لِيُرِيَهُمَا سَوْآتِهِمَا ۗ إِنَّهُ يَرَاكُمْ هُوَ وَقَبِيلُهُ مِنْ حَيْثُ لَا تَرَوْنَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّا جَعَلْنَا الشَّيَاطِينَ أَوْلِيَاءَ لِلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ

O children of Adam! Do not let Satan deceive you as he tempted your parents out of Paradise and caused their cover to be removed in order to expose their nakedness. Surely he and his soldiers watch you from where you cannot see them. We have made the devils allies of those who disbelieve.

O children of Adam, let not Satan deceive you, making sin fair-seeming to you, as he made it fair-seeming to your parents, Adam and Eve, and caused them to be expelled from Paradise because of it, stripping them of their clothing with which Allah had covered them, so that their private parts would be exposed to them. Indeed, Satan sees you, he and his progeny and his kind, while you do not see them, so beware of them. Indeed, We have made the devils allies of those who disbelieve, those who do not believe in the Oneness of Allah, do not believe in His messengers, and do not act according to His guidance.

Satan's deception led to Adam and Eve's expulsion from Paradise and the exposure of their private parts. He and his followers can see humans, but humans cannot see them, serving as a warning about their influence.

Verse 28

وَإِذَا فَعَلُوا فَاحِشَةً قَالُوا وَجَدْنَا عَلَيْهَا آبَاءَنَا وَاللَّهُ أَمَرَنَا بِهَا ۗ قُلْ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَأْمُرُ بِالْفَحْشَاءِ ۖ أَتَقُولُونَ عَلَى اللَّهِ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ

Whenever they commit a shameful deed, they say, “We found our forefathers doing it and Allah has commanded us to do it.” Say, “No! Allah never commands what is shameful. How can you attribute to Allah what you do not know?”

And when the disbelievers commit an ugly deed, they excuse their deed by saying that it is what they inherited from their forefathers, and that it is what Allah has commanded. Say to them, [O Messenger]: Indeed, Allah Almighty does not command His servants to do ugly deeds and their evils. Do you say about Allah - O polytheists - what you do not know as a lie and a fabrication?

Disbelievers justify their wrongdoings by claiming it's tradition or that Allah commanded it. This verse clarifies that Allah never commands shameful deeds and encourages understanding and proper attribution.

Verse 29

قُلْ أَمَرَ رَبِّي بِالْقِسْطِ ۖ وَأَقِيمُوا وُجُوهَكُمْ عِنْدَ كُلِّ مَسْجِدٍ وَادْعُوهُ مُخْلِصِينَ لَهُ الدِّينَ ۚ كَمَا بَدَأَكُمْ تَعُودُونَ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “My Lord has commanded uprightness and dedication ˹to Him alone˺ in worship, calling upon Him with sincere devotion. Just as He first brought you into being, you will be brought to life again.”

Say, [O Messenger], to these polytheists: My Lord has commanded justice, and has commanded you to be devoted to His worship in every place of worship, especially in the mosques, and to call upon Him, being sincere to Him in obedience and worship, and to believe in the resurrection after death. And just as Allah brought you into existence from nothing, He is able to restore life to you once again.

Allah commands uprightness, sincere devotion, and belief in the resurrection. Just as He created humans initially, He is able to bring them back to life.

Verse 30

فَرِيقًا هَدَىٰ وَفَرِيقًا حَقَّ عَلَيْهِمُ الضَّلَالَةُ ۗ إِنَّهُمُ اتَّخَذُوا الشَّيَاطِينَ أَوْلِيَاءَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ وَيَحْسَبُونَ أَنَّهُمْ مُهْتَدُونَ

He has guided some, while others are destined to stray. They have taken devils as their masters instead of Allah—thinking they are ˹rightly˺ guided.

Allah made His servants into two groups: a group that He guided to the Straight Path, and a group upon whom straying from the Straight Path was decreed. Verse 195
