Sura 062

Verse 1

يُسَبِّحُ لِلَّهِ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ الْمَلِكِ الْقُدُّوسِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَكِيمِ

Whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth ˹constantly˺ glorifies Allah—the King, the Most Holy, the Almighty, the All-Wise.

Allah is exalted above all that is not befitting of Him. Everything in the heavens and the earth glorifies Him, and He alone is the Owner of all things, the One who disposes of them without rival, the One who is free from all defects, the Mighty who cannot be overcome, the Wise in His planning and creation.

Everything in the heavens and earth glorifies Allah, the King, the Most Holy, the Almighty, and the All-Wise. This highlights Allah's absolute power and wisdom.

Verse 2

هُوَ الَّذِي بَعَثَ فِي الْأُمِّيِّينَ رَسُولًا مِنْهُمْ يَتْلُو عَلَيْهِمْ آيَاتِهِ وَيُزَكِّيهِمْ وَيُعَلِّمُهُمُ الْكِتَابَ وَالْحِكْمَةَ وَإِنْ كَانُوا مِنْ قَبْلُ لَفِي ضَلَالٍ مُبِينٍ

He is the One Who raised for the illiterate ˹people˺ a messenger from among themselves—reciting to them His revelations, purifying them, and teaching them the Book and wisdom, for indeed they had previously been clearly astray—

Allah, the Glorified, is the One who sent among the Arabs, who do not read, and who have no book or trace of a message, a messenger from among them to all people, reciting to them the Quran, purifying them from corrupt beliefs and bad morals, and teaching them the Quran and the Sunnah. Indeed, before his sending, they were in clear deviation from the truth. And He sent him to other peoples who have not yet come, and who will come from the Arabs and from others. And Allah - alone - is the Mighty, the Conqueror of all things, the Wise in His words and deeds.

Allah sent a messenger to the unlettered Arabs, who were astray, to recite the Quran, purify them, and teach them wisdom. This emphasizes the transformative impact of the message.

Verse 3

وَآخَرِينَ مِنْهُمْ لَمَّا يَلْحَقُوا بِهِمْ ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

along with others of them who have not yet joined them ˹in faith˺. For He is the Almighty, All-Wise.

Allah, the Glorified, is the One who sent among the Arabs, who do not read, and who have no book or trace of a message, a messenger from among them to all people, reciting to them the Quran, purifying them from corrupt beliefs and bad morals, and teaching them the Quran and the Sunnah. Indeed, before his sending, they were in clear deviation from the truth. And He sent him to other peoples who have not yet come, and who will come from the Arabs and from others. And Allah - alone - is the Mighty, the Conqueror of all things, the Wise in His words and deeds.

This message extends to those who have not yet joined the believers, emphasizing that the Prophet's mission is universal, and Allah is the Almighty, All-Wise.

Verse 4

ذَٰلِكَ فَضْلُ اللَّهِ يُؤْتِيهِ مَنْ يَشَاءُ ۚ وَاللَّهُ ذُو الْفَضْلِ الْعَظِيمِ

This is the favour of Allah. He grants it to whoever He wills. And Allah is the Lord of infinite bounty.

That sending of the Messenger, peace be upon him, to the Arab nation and others is a favor from Allah, He grants it to whom He wills of His servants. And He - alone - is the Possessor of immense goodness and bestowal.

The sending of the Messenger is a favor from Allah, granted to whom He wills, highlighting His infinite bounty and grace.

Verse 5

مَثَلُ الَّذِينَ حُمِّلُوا التَّوْرَاةَ ثُمَّ لَمْ يَحْمِلُوهَا كَمَثَلِ الْحِمَارِ يَحْمِلُ أَسْفَارًا ۚ بِئْسَ مَثَلُ الْقَوْمِ الَّذِينَ كَذَّبُوا بِآيَاتِ اللَّهِ ۚ وَاللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِي الْقَوْمَ الظَّالِمِينَ

The example of those who were entrusted with ˹observing˺ the Torah but failed to do so, is that of a donkey carrying books. How evil is the example of those who reject Allah’s signs! For Allah does not guide the wrongdoing people.

The likeness of those Jews who were charged with the Torah and then did not act upon it is like the likeness of a donkey carrying books that it does not know what is in them. Wretched is the likeness of the people who denied the verses of Allah, and did not benefit from them. And Allah does not guide the wrongdoing people who transgress His limits, and go out of His obedience.

Those who were entrusted with the Torah but did not act upon it are like a donkey carrying books, illustrating the futility of having knowledge without application. Allah does not guide wrongdoers.

Verse 6

قُلْ يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ هَادُوا إِنْ زَعَمْتُمْ أَنَّكُمْ أَوْلِيَاءُ لِلَّهِ مِنْ دُونِ النَّاسِ فَتَمَنَّوُا الْمَوْتَ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صَادِقِينَ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “O Jews! If you claim to be Allah’s chosen ˹people˺ out of all humanity, then wish for death, if what you say is true.”

Say - O Messenger - to those who adhere to the distorted Jewish faith: If you falsely claim that you are the beloved of Allah to the exclusion of other people, then wish for death if you are truthful in your claim that Allah loves you.

The Jews are challenged to wish for death if they truly believe they are Allah's chosen people. This exposes their fear of the afterlife.

Verse 7

وَلَا يَتَمَنَّوْنَهُ أَبَدًا بِمَا قَدَّمَتْ أَيْدِيهِمْ ۚ وَاللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ بِالظَّالِمِينَ

But they will never wish for that because of what their hands have done. And Allah has ˹perfect˺ knowledge of the wrongdoers.

And these Jews will never wish for death, preferring the life of this world over the hereafter, and out of fear of the punishment of Allah for what they have put forth of disbelief and evil deeds. And Allah is All-Knowing of the wrongdoers, nothing of their wrongdoing is hidden from Him.

The Jews will never wish for death because of their misdeeds, and Allah is fully aware of the wrongdoers' actions. This emphasizes Allah's knowledge of their hidden fears and guilt.

Verse 8

قُلْ إِنَّ الْمَوْتَ الَّذِي تَفِرُّونَ مِنْهُ فَإِنَّهُ مُلَاقِيكُمْ ۖ ثُمَّ تُرَدُّونَ إِلَىٰ عَالِمِ الْغَيْبِ وَالشَّهَادَةِ فَيُنَبِّئُكُمْ بِمَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ

Say, “The death you are running away from will inevitably come to you. Then you will be returned to the Knower of the seen and unseen, and He will inform you of what you used to do.”

Say: Indeed, the death that you flee from is inevitable, for it is coming to you when your terms arrive. Then you will be returned on the Day of Resurrection to Allah, the Knower of the unseen and the seen, and He will inform you of your deeds, and He will recompense you for them.

Death, which people try to avoid, is inevitable. Everyone will return to Allah, the Knower of the unseen and seen, to be informed of their deeds. This serves as a reminder of accountability.

Verse 9

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا نُودِيَ لِلصَّلَاةِ مِنْ يَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ فَاسْعَوْا إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ وَذَرُوا الْبَيْعَ ۚ ذَٰلِكُمْ خَيْرٌ لَكُمْ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ

O believers! When the call to prayer is made on Friday, then proceed ˹diligently˺ to the remembrance of Allah and leave off ˹your˺ business. That is best for you, if only you knew.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, when the call to prayer is made on Friday, hasten to the remembrance of Allah and to prayer, and leave off trade. That is better for you, if you should know. And in the verse is evidence of the obligation to attend Friday prayer and listen to the sermon.

This verse instructs believers to hasten to the remembrance of Allah (prayer) and cease business when the Friday prayer call is made. This is emphasized as being better for them, highlighting the importance of prioritizing worship.

Verse 10

فَإِذَا قُضِيَتِ الصَّلَاةُ فَانْتَشِرُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ وَابْتَغُوا مِنْ فَضْلِ اللَّهِ وَاذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ كَثِيرًا لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ

Once the prayer is over, disperse throughout the land and seek the bounty of Allah. And remember Allah often so you may be successful.

And when you have completed the prayer, disperse through the land and seek of the bounty of Allah, and remember Allah often that you may succeed.

After the prayer, Muslims are encouraged to disperse and seek Allah's bounty, but they are also reminded to remember Allah frequently for success in life.

Verse 11

وَإِذَا رَأَوْا تِجَارَةً أَوْ لَهْوًا انْفَضُّوا إِلَيْهَا وَتَرَكُوكَ قَائِمًا ۚ قُلْ مَا عِنْدَ اللَّهِ خَيْرٌ مِنَ اللَّهْوِ وَمِنَ التِّجَارَةِ ۚ وَاللَّهُ خَيْرُ الرَّازِقِينَ

When they saw the fanfare along with the caravan, they ˹almost all˺ flocked to it, leaving you ˹O Prophet˺ standing ˹on the pulpit˺. Say, “What is with Allah is far better than amusement and merchandise. And Allah is the Best Provider.”

And when they see a transaction or a diversion, they rush to it and leave you standing. Say, "What is with Allah is better than diversion and than a transaction, and Allah is the best of providers." seek from Him, and seek help in His obedience to attain what He has of good in this life and the Hereafter.

This verse criticizes those who abandon the Prophet's sermon for worldly gains. It emphasizes that what Allah provides is far better than any material amusement or trade.
