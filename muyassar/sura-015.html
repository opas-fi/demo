Sura 015

Verse 1

الر ۚ تِلْكَ آيَاتُ الْكِتَابِ وَقُرْآنٍ مُبِينٍ

Alif-Lãm-Ra. These are the verses of the Book; the clear Quran.

(Alif, Lam, Ra). The discussion about the disconnected letters in the beginning of Surat Al-Baqarah has preceded.

The verse begins with disconnected letters, followed by an affirmation that these are verses of the clear Quran.

Verse 2

رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ

˹The day will come when˺ the disbelievers will certainly wish they had submitted ˹to Allah˺.

The disbelievers will wish, when they see the sinful believers coming out of the Fire, that they had been monotheists, so that they could come out as they did.

This verse speaks of a time when disbelievers will regret not having submitted to Allah, especially when they see believers being saved.

Verse 3

ذَرْهُمْ يَأْكُلُوا وَيَتَمَتَّعُوا وَيُلْهِهِمُ الْأَمَلُ ۖ فَسَوْفَ يَعْلَمُونَ

˹So˺ let them eat and enjoy themselves and be diverted by ˹false˺ hope, for they will soon know.

Leave, O Messenger, the disbelievers to eat and enjoy their worldly life, and let their greed distract them from obeying Allah, for they will soon know the consequence of their losing affair in this world and the Hereafter.

The disbelievers are warned to enjoy their temporary worldly life while they are distracted by false hope, but they will soon face consequences.

Verse 4

وَمَا أَهْلَكْنَا مِنْ قَرْيَةٍ إِلَّا وَلَهَا كِتَابٌ مَعْلُومٌ

We have never destroyed a society without a destined term.

And if they ask for the torment to be sent down upon them as a denial of you, O Messenger, then We do not destroy any town except that it has a predetermined term for its destruction. We do not destroy them until they reach it, like those who came before them.

Allah emphasizes that He does not destroy any society without a predetermined time. This highlights divine justice and planning.

Verse 5

مَا تَسْبِقُ مِنْ أُمَّةٍ أَجَلَهَا وَمَا يَسْتَأْخِرُونَ

No people can advance their doom, nor can they delay it.

No nation exceeds its term, adding to it, nor does it precede it, decreasing from it.

No people can advance or delay their appointed time of destruction. Everything is under Allah's precise control.

Verse 6

وَقَالُوا يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِي نُزِّلَ عَلَيْهِ الذِّكْرُ إِنَّكَ لَمَجْنُونٌ

They say, “O you to whom the Reminder is revealed! You must be insane!

And those who deny Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, said mockingly: O you upon whom the Quran has been sent down, you are surely mad. Why do you not bring us the angels - if you are truthful - to testify that Allah has sent you?

The disbelievers mockingly accuse the Prophet Muhammad, upon whom the Quran was revealed, of being insane.

Verse 7

لَوْ مَا تَأْتِينَا بِالْمَلَائِكَةِ إِنْ كُنْتَ مِنَ الصَّادِقِينَ

Why do you not bring us the angels, if what you say is true?”

And those who deny Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, said mockingly: O you upon whom the Quran has been sent down, you are surely mad. Why do you not bring us the angels - if you are truthful - to testify that Allah has sent you?

The disbelievers challenge the Prophet to bring angels to testify to his truthfulness, showing their disbelief.

Verse 8

مَا نُنَزِّلُ الْمَلَائِكَةَ إِلَّا بِالْحَقِّ وَمَا كَانُوا إِذًا مُنْظَرِينَ

We do not send the angels down except for a just cause, and then ˹the end of˺ the disbelievers will not be delayed.

And Allah replied to them: We do not send down the angels except with the torment, for which there is no respite for those who do not believe, and they will not be given respite when the angels descend with the torment.

Angels are not sent down except for a just cause, and when they descend with torment, the disbelievers will not be given respite.

Verse 9

إِنَّا نَحْنُ نَزَّلْنَا الذِّكْرَ وَإِنَّا لَهُ لَحَافِظُونَ

It is certainly We Who have revealed the Reminder, and it is certainly We Who will preserve it.

Indeed, it is We who have sent down the Quran to the Prophet Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and indeed, We are its guardians to protect it from being added to, or subtracted from, or anything being lost from it.

Allah affirms that He has revealed the Quran and that He is the guardian of its preservation from any alteration.

Verse 10

وَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا مِنْ قَبْلِكَ فِي شِيَعِ الْأَوَّلِينَ

Indeed, We sent messengers before you ˹O Prophet˺ among the groups of early peoples,

And We have certainly sent before you, O Messenger, Messengers among the groups of the ancients, and no Messenger came to them except that they used to mock him. And in this is a consolation for the Messenger, may Allah bless him and grant him peace. So just as these polytheists did to you, so was done to those before you of the Messengers.

Allah reminds the Prophet that He sent messengers to earlier peoples, who were also mocked. This is a source of consolation for the Prophet.

Verse 11

وَمَا يَأْتِيهِمْ مِنْ رَسُولٍ إِلَّا كَانُوا بِهِ يَسْتَهْزِئُونَ

but no messenger ever came to them without being mocked.

And We have certainly sent before you, O Messenger, Messengers among the groups of the ancients, and no Messenger came to them except that they used to mock him. And in this is a consolation for the Messenger, may Allah bless him and grant him peace. So just as these polytheists did to you, so was done to those before you of the Messengers.

This verse reiterates that no messenger was ever sent to a people without being mocked, emphasizing a recurring pattern of disbelief.

Verse 12

كَذَٰلِكَ نَسْلُكُهُ فِي قُلُوبِ الْمُجْرِمِينَ

This is how We allow disbelief ˹to steep˺ into the hearts of the wicked.

Just as We inserted disbelief into the hearts of the previous nations by mocking the Messengers and denying them, so We do that in the hearts of the polytheists of your people who have committed a crime by disbelieving in Allah and denying His Messenger. They do not believe in the Reminder which has been sent down to you, and the way of the ancients has passed concerning the destruction of the disbelievers, and these are like them; those of them who persist in disbelief and denial will be destroyed.

Allah states that disbelief is instilled into the hearts of the wicked, similar to how it was done with previous nations who mocked their messengers.

Verse 13

لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِهِ ۖ وَقَدْ خَلَتْ سُنَّةُ الْأَوَّلِينَ

They would not believe in this ˹Quran˺ despite the ˹many˺ examples of those ˹destroyed˺ before.

Just as We inserted disbelief into the hearts of the previous nations by mocking the Messengers and denying them, so We do that in the hearts of the polytheists of your people who have committed a crime by disbelieving in Allah and denying His Messenger. They do not believe in the Reminder which has been sent down to you, and the way of the ancients has passed concerning the destruction of the disbelievers, and these are like them; those of them who persist in disbelief and denial will be destroyed.

Despite the examples of those destroyed before them, the disbelievers will not believe in the Quran due to their ingrained disbelief.

Verse 14

وَلَوْ فَتَحْنَا عَلَيْهِمْ بَابًا مِنَ السَّمَاءِ فَظَلُّوا فِيهِ يَعْرُجُونَ

And even if We opened for them a gate to heaven, through which they continued to ascend,

And even if We opened a gate of the sky for the disbelievers of "Mecca", and they continued ascending in it until they witnessed what is in the sky of the wonders of the kingdom of Allah, they would not believe, and they would say: Our eyes have been bewitched, so that we saw what we did not see, and we are only bewitched in our minds by Muhammad.

Even if Allah were to open a gate to the sky for them and they ascended, the disbelievers would still not believe.

Verse 15

لَقَالُوا إِنَّمَا سُكِّرَتْ أَبْصَارُنَا بَلْ نَحْنُ قَوْمٌ مَسْحُورُونَ

still they would say, “Our eyes have truly been dazzled! In fact, we must have been bewitched.”

And even if We opened a gate of the sky for the disbelievers of "Mecca", and they continued ascending in it until they witnessed what is in the sky of the wonders of the kingdom of Allah, they would not believe, and they would say: Our eyes have been bewitched, so that we saw what we did not see, and we are only bewitched in our minds by Muhammad.

The disbelievers would rationalize their disbelief by saying their eyes were deceived or that they were bewitched. This shows their refusal to accept the truth.

Verse 16

وَلَقَدْ جَعَلْنَا فِي السَّمَاءِ بُرُوجًا وَزَيَّنَّاهَا لِلنَّاظِرِينَ

Indeed, We have placed constellations in the sky, and adorned it for all to see.

And among the proofs of Our power: that We have placed in the lowest heaven constellations for the planets to descend into, and this is used to determine the roads, times, fertility and drought, and We have adorned this heaven with stars for those who look at them and contemplate, so they may take heed.

Allah has placed constellations in the sky, making it beautiful for those who observe and contemplate. These celestial bodies are used for navigation and understanding seasons.

Verse 17

وَحَفِظْنَاهَا مِنْ كُلِّ شَيْطَانٍ رَجِيمٍ

And We protected it from every accursed devil,

And We have protected the heaven from every accursed devil, expelled from the mercy of Allah, so that they cannot reach it.

The sky is protected by Allah from devils who have been cursed and expelled from His mercy.

Verse 18

إِلَّا مَنِ اسْتَرَقَ السَّمْعَ فَأَتْبَعَهُ شِهَابٌ مُبِينٌ

except the one eavesdropping, who is then pursued by a visible flare.

Except he who steals a hearing from the speech of the exalted assembly at some times, then a bright star overtakes him and catches up to him, burning him. And the devil may throw to his ally some of what he has stolen before the shooting star burns him.

If a devil tries to eavesdrop on the heavenly realm, they are chased away by a shooting star, which burns them.

Verse 19

وَالْأَرْضَ مَدَدْنَاهَا وَأَلْقَيْنَا فِيهَا رَوَاسِيَ وَأَنْبَتْنَا فِيهَا مِنْ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ مَوْزُونٍ

As for the earth, We spread it out and placed upon it firm mountains, and caused everything to grow there in perfect balance.

And the earth, We have spread it out wide, and cast upon it mountains to stabilize it, and We have grown in it of every kind of plant that is measured and known, from what the servants need.

Allah has spread out the earth, placed mountains to stabilize it, and caused balanced growth of plants for human needs.

Verse 20

وَجَعَلْنَا لَكُمْ فِيهَا مَعَايِشَ وَمَنْ لَسْتُمْ لَهُ بِرَازِقِينَ

And We made in it means of sustenance for you and others, who you do not provide for.

And We have made for you therein what you live by, of cultivation, livestock, and various types of earnings and other things, and We have created for you of offspring, servants, and beasts of burden that you benefit from, and their sustenance is not your responsibility, but it is from Allah, Lord of the Worlds, as a favor and honor from Him.

Allah has provided various means of sustenance, not only for humans, but also for other creatures, emphasizing that He is the ultimate provider.

Verse 21

وَإِنْ مِنْ شَيْءٍ إِلَّا عِنْدَنَا خَزَائِنُهُ وَمَا نُنَزِّلُهُ إِلَّا بِقَدَرٍ مَعْلُومٍ

There is not any means ˹of sustenance˺ whose reserves We do not hold, only bringing it forth in precise measure.

And there is not a single thing of the benefits for the servants except that its treasures are with Us, of all kinds, and We do not send it down except in a measured amount as We will and as We desire, for the treasures are in the hands of Allah, He gives to whom He wills and withholds from whom He wills, according to His vast mercy, and His supreme wisdom.

All resources are with Allah, and He distributes them in precise measure according to His wisdom and will.

Verse 22

وَأَرْسَلْنَا الرِّيَاحَ لَوَاقِحَ فَأَنْزَلْنَا مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً فَأَسْقَيْنَاكُمُوهُ وَمَا أَنْتُمْ لَهُ بِخَازِنِينَ

We send fertilizing winds, and bring down rain from the sky for you to drink. It is not you who hold its reserves.

And We have sent the winds and subjected them to pollinate the clouds, carrying the rain, goodness, and benefit, then We sent down from the clouds water We have prepared for your drinking, your land, and your livestock, and you are not able to store it and save it, but We store it for you as mercy from Us, and as kindness to you.

Allah sends fertilizing winds to bring rain, providing water for drinking and sustenance. He controls these natural resources, not humans.

Verse 23

وَإِنَّا لَنَحْنُ نُحْيِي وَنُمِيتُ وَنَحْنُ الْوَارِثُونَ

Surely it is We Who give life and cause death. And We are the ˹Eternal˺ Successor.

And indeed, it is We who give life to whoever was dead by creating him from nothingness, and We cause death to whoever was alive after the end of his term, and We are the inheritors of the earth and whoever is on it.

Allah is the One who gives life and causes death, and He is the ultimate inheritor of all creation.

Verse 24

وَلَقَدْ عَلِمْنَا الْمُسْتَقْدِمِينَ مِنْكُمْ وَلَقَدْ عَلِمْنَا الْمُسْتَأْخِرِينَ

We certainly know those who have gone before you and those who will come after ˹you˺.

And We have indeed known who has perished from you since Adam, and who is alive, and who will come until the Day of Resurrection.

Allah has full knowledge of all those who have lived before and those who will come in the future.

Verse 25

وَإِنَّ رَبَّكَ هُوَ يَحْشُرُهُمْ ۚ إِنَّهُ حَكِيمٌ عَلِيمٌ

Surely your Lord ˹alone˺ will gather them together ˹for judgment˺. He is truly All-Wise, All-Knowing.

And indeed, your Lord is the One who will gather them for reckoning and recompense, for He is Wise in His planning, All-Knowing, nothing is hidden from Him.

Allah will gather all of humanity on the Day of Judgment for reckoning. He is all-wise and all-knowing.

Verse 26

وَلَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا الْإِنْسَانَ مِنْ صَلْصَالٍ مِنْ حَمَإٍ مَسْنُونٍ

Indeed, We created man from sounding clay moulded from black mud.

And We have certainly created Adam from dry clay that if it is struck, a sound is heard, and this dry clay is from black mud that has changed in color and smell, from being left for a long time.

Allah created man from sounding clay molded from black mud, signifying the humble origins of humanity.

Verse 27

وَالْجَانَّ خَلَقْنَاهُ مِنْ قَبْلُ مِنْ نَارِ السَّمُومِ

As for the jinn, We created them earlier from smokeless fire.

And We created the father of the jinn, who is Iblis, from before the creation of Adam from intense heatless fire.

Allah created the jinn from smokeless fire, prior to the creation of man.

Verse 28

وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَائِكَةِ إِنِّي خَالِقٌ بَشَرًا مِنْ صَلْصَالٍ مِنْ حَمَإٍ مَسْنُونٍ

˹Remember, O Prophet˺ when your Lord said to the angels, “I am going to create a human being from sounding clay moulded from black mud.

And remember - O Prophet - when your Lord said to the angels: "Indeed, I am going to create a human from dry clay, and this dry clay is from black mud that has changed in color."

Allah informed the angels of His intention to create man from sounding clay molded from black mud.

Verse 29

فَإِذَا سَوَّيْتُهُ وَنَفَخْتُ فِيهِ مِنْ رُوحِي فَقَعُوا لَهُ سَاجِدِينَ

So when I have fashioned him and had a spirit of My Own ˹creation˺ breathed into him, fall down in prostration to him.”

So when I have fashioned him and completed his form and breathed into him of My spirit, then fall down in prostration to him as a greeting and honor, not as a prostration of worship.

Allah commanded the angels to prostrate to Adam, as a sign of honor, once he was fully formed and given life.

Verse 30

فَسَجَدَ الْمَلَائِكَةُ كُلُّهُمْ أَجْمَعُونَ

So the angels prostrated all together—

So the angels prostrated, all of them together as their Lord commanded them, none of them refused, but Iblis refused to prostrate to Adam with the prostrating angels.

The angels obeyed Allah and prostrated before Adam.

Verse 31

إِلَّا إِبْلِيسَ أَبَىٰ أَنْ يَكُونَ مَعَ السَّاجِدِينَ

but not Iblîs, who refused to prostrate with the others.

So the angels prostrated, all of them together as their Lord commanded them, none of them refused, but Iblis refused to prostrate to Adam with the prostrating angels.

Iblis (Satan) refused to prostrate to Adam, disobeying Allah's command.

Verse 32

قَالَ يَا إِبْلِيسُ مَا لَكَ أَلَّا تَكُونَ مَعَ السَّاجِدِينَ

Allah asked, “O Iblîs! What is the matter with you that you did not join others in prostration?”

Allah said to Iblis: What prevents you from prostrating with the angels?

Allah asks Iblis (Satan) why he didn't prostrate with the angels, showing a direct questioning of his disobedience.

Verse 33

قَالَ لَمْ أَكُنْ لِأَسْجُدَ لِبَشَرٍ خَلَقْتَهُ مِنْ صَلْصَالٍ مِنْ حَمَإٍ مَسْنُونٍ

He replied, “It is not for me to prostrate to a human You created from sounding clay moulded from black mud.”

Iblis, displaying his arrogance and envy, said: It is not befitting for me to prostrate to a human whom You created from dry clay, which was black mud that had changed.

Iblis refuses to prostrate to Adam, a human created from clay, demonstrating arrogance and considering himself superior.

Verse 34

قَالَ فَاخْرُجْ مِنْهَا فَإِنَّكَ رَجِيمٌ

Allah commanded, “Then get out of Paradise, for you are truly cursed.

Allah the Exalted said to him: Then get out of Paradise, for you are expelled from all good, and upon you is the curse and distance from My mercy until the Day the people are resurrected for reckoning and recompense.

Allah expels Iblis from Paradise due to his disobedience and declares him cursed, marking his fall from grace.

Verse 35

وَإِنَّ عَلَيْكَ اللَّعْنَةَ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ

And surely upon you is condemnation until the Day of Judgment.”

Allah the Exalted said to him: Then get out of Paradise, for you are expelled from all good, and upon you is the curse and distance from My mercy until the Day the people are resurrected for reckoning and recompense.

Allah further condemns Iblis, stating that the curse will remain on him until the Day of Judgment.

Verse 36

قَالَ رَبِّ فَأَنْظِرْنِي إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ

Satan appealed, “My Lord! Then delay my end until the Day of their resurrection.”

Iblis said: My Lord, grant me respite in this world until the day when You resurrect Your servants, which is the Day of Resurrection.

Iblis requests a delay in his end until the Day of Resurrection, seeking to prolong his existence and his ability to mislead.

Verse 37

قَالَ فَإِنَّكَ مِنَ الْمُنْظَرِينَ

Allah said, “You will be delayed

Allah said to him: Indeed, you are among those whose destruction I have delayed until the day when all of creation dies after the first blowing of the trumpet, not until the Day of Resurrection. And I only granted that as a deception for him, a respite, and a trial for mankind and jinn.

Allah grants Iblis a delay, but only until a specific, appointed time, not until the Day of Resurrection.

Verse 38

إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ الْوَقْتِ الْمَعْلُومِ

until the appointed Day.”

Allah said to him: Indeed, you are among those whose destruction I have delayed until the day when all of creation dies after the first blowing of the trumpet, not until the Day of Resurrection. And I only granted that as a deception for him, a respite, and a trial for mankind and jinn.

This verse reaffirms that Iblis's respite is temporary, until the appointed Day, set by Allah.

Verse 39

قَالَ رَبِّ بِمَا أَغْوَيْتَنِي لَأُزَيِّنَنَّ لَهُمْ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَأُغْوِيَنَّهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ

Satan responded, “My Lord! For allowing me to stray I will surely tempt them on earth and mislead them all together,

Iblis said: My Lord, because of what You have caused me to deviate and go astray, I will surely make the disobedience to You appear pleasing to the descendants of Adam on earth, and I will surely lead them all astray from the path of guidance, except for Your servants whom You have guided, who have devoted their worship to You alone, apart from all Your other creation.

Iblis vows to tempt and mislead all of mankind on Earth, blaming his own deviation on Allah.

Verse 40

إِلَّا عِبَادَكَ مِنْهُمُ الْمُخْلَصِينَ

except Your chosen servants among them.”

Iblis said: My Lord, because of what You have caused me to deviate and go astray, I will surely make the disobedience to You appear pleasing to the descendants of Adam on earth, and I will surely lead them all astray from the path of guidance, except for Your servants whom You have guided, who have devoted their worship to You alone, apart from all Your other creation.

Iblis acknowledges that he will not be able to mislead Allah's chosen servants, those who are sincere in their worship.

Verse 41

قَالَ هَٰذَا صِرَاطٌ عَلَيَّ مُسْتَقِيمٌ

Allah said, “This is the Way, binding on Me:

Allah said: This is a straight path, a moderate path that leads to Me and to the abode of My honor. Indeed, My servants who are devoted to Me, I will not give you any authority over their hearts to mislead them from the straight path, but your authority is over those who follow you from the misguided polytheists who are pleased with your patronage and obedience instead of My obedience.

Allah declares the 'straight path' as the way that leads to Him and clarifies that He will protect His devoted servants.

Verse 42

إِنَّ عِبَادِي لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَانٌ إِلَّا مَنِ اتَّبَعَكَ مِنَ الْغَاوِينَ

you will certainly have no authority over My servants, except the deviant who follow you,

Allah said: This is a straight path, a moderate path that leads to Me and to the abode of My honor. Indeed, My servants who are devoted to Me, I will not give you any authority over their hearts to mislead them from the straight path, but your authority is over those who follow you from the misguided polytheists who are pleased with your patronage and obedience instead of My obedience.

Allah clarifies that Iblis has no power over His sincere servants, but his influence is only over those who choose to follow him.

Verse 43

وَإِنَّ جَهَنَّمَ لَمَوْعِدُهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ

and surely Hell is their destined place, all together.

And indeed, Hell is the appointed place for Iblis and his followers all together, it has seven gates, each gate lower than the other, for each gate of the followers of Iblis is a portion and share according to their deeds.

Hell is the final destination for Iblis and his followers, highlighting the severe consequences of disobedience.

Verse 44

لَهَا سَبْعَةُ أَبْوَابٍ لِكُلِّ بَابٍ مِنْهُمْ جُزْءٌ مَقْسُومٌ
