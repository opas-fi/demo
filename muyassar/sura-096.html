Sura 096

Verse 1

اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ

Read, ˹O Prophet,˺ in the Name of your Lord Who created—

Read, O Prophet, what has been revealed to you from the Quran, starting with the name of your Lord, the One and Only in creation, who created every human being from a clot of thick red blood. Read, O Prophet, what has been revealed to you, for your Lord is most gracious and generous. He taught His creation writing with the pen. He taught man what he did not know, and brought him out of the darkness of ignorance to the light of knowledge.

The Prophet is commanded to read in the name of Allah, the Creator, which is the beginning of revelation.

Verse 6

كَلَّا إِنَّ الْإِنْسَانَ لَيَطْغَىٰ

Most certainly, one exceeds all bounds

Humans often transgress when they feel self-sufficient and independent.

Verse 9

أَرَأَيْتَ الَّذِي يَنْهَىٰ

Have you seen the man who prevents

Have you seen the wonder of this man's transgression (Abu Jahl) who forbids Our servant when he prays to his Lord (Muhammad, peace be upon him)? Have you seen that if the one forbidden from prayer is on guidance, then how can he be forbidden? Or, if he is commanding others to piety, then should he be forbidden from that? Have you seen that if this one who forbids denies what he is invited to and turns away from it, did he not know that Allah sees everything he does? It is not as Abu Jahl claims; if he does not stop his opposition and harm, then We will surely seize him by the forelock, a violent seizure, and cast him into the Fire. His forelock is a lying forelock in its statements, a sinful one in its actions. So let this tyrant summon the people of his assembly whom he seeks help from; We will call on the angels of punishment. It is not as Abu Jahl thinks; no harm will come to you, O Messenger, so do not obey him in what he called you to of abandoning prayer; and prostrate to your Lord and draw near to Him by endearing yourself to Him with obedience.

This verse questions the actions of those who prevent others from prayer.
