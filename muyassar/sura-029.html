Sura 029

Verse 1

الم

Alif-Lãm-Mĩm.

Alif, Lam, Meem: The discussion on the disjointed letters has already been mentioned at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah.

These are the disjointed letters (*Alif, Lam, Meem*) whose meaning is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

أَحَسِبَ النَّاسُ أَنْ يُتْرَكُوا أَنْ يَقُولُوا آمَنَّا وَهُمْ لَا يُفْتَنُونَ

Do people think once they say, “We believe,” that they will be left without being put to the test?

Did the people think that just because they say, 'We believe,' they will be left without being tested?

Do people think they will be left alone after they say they believe, without being tested?

Verse 3

وَلَقَدْ فَتَنَّا الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ ۖ فَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ صَدَقُوا وَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ الْكَاذِبِينَ

We certainly tested those before them. And ˹in this way˺ Allah will clearly distinguish between those who are truthful and those who are liars.

And We had certainly tested those before them from the nations and We had tested them, from among those whom We sent Our messengers to. So Allah will surely make apparent to the creation, the truthfulness of the truthful in their faith, and the falsehood of the liars; to distinguish each group from the other.

Allah has tested those before you. Allah will reveal who is truthful in their faith and who is a liar.

Verse 4

أَمْ حَسِبَ الَّذِينَ يَعْمَلُونَ السَّيِّئَاتِ أَنْ يَسْبِقُونَا ۚ سَاءَ مَا يَحْكُمُونَ

Or do the evildoers ˹simply˺ think that they will escape Us? How wrong is their judgment!

Or do those who do evil deeds, of polytheism and other sins, think that they can elude Us, escaping from Us so We cannot seize them? How evil is their judgment which they judge!

Do those who do evil think they can escape Allah? How wrong is their judgment!

Verse 5

مَنْ كَانَ يَرْجُو لِقَاءَ اللَّهِ فَإِنَّ أَجَلَ اللَّهِ لَآتٍ ۚ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ

Whoever hopes for the meeting with Allah, ˹let them know that˺ Allah’s appointed time is sure to come. He is the All-Hearing, All-Knowing.

Whoever hopes for the meeting with Allah and is eager for His reward, then indeed, Allah's appointed time for the resurrection of His creation for reward and punishment is surely coming soon, and He is the All-Hearing of statements, All-Knowing of actions.

Whoever hopes to meet Allah, should know that His appointed time will surely come. He is the All-Hearing and All-Knowing.

Verse 6

وَمَنْ جَاهَدَ فَإِنَّمَا يُجَاهِدُ لِنَفْسِهِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَغَنِيٌّ عَنِ الْعَالَمِينَ

And whoever strives ˹in Allah’s cause˺, only does so for their own good. Surely Allah is not in need of ˹any of˺ His creation.

And whoever strives in the cause of exalting the word of Allah, and strives against his own self in compelling it to obedience, then he only strives for himself; because he does that seeking reward for his striving. Indeed, Allah is free of need of the deeds of all His creation, to Him belongs the dominion, the creation, and the command.

Whoever strives for Allah, does so for their own benefit. Allah is not in need of anything from His creation.

Verse 7

وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ لَنُكَفِّرَنَّ عَنْهُمْ سَيِّئَاتِهِمْ وَلَنَجْزِيَنَّهُمْ أَحْسَنَ الَّذِي كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

As for those who believe and do good, We will certainly absolve them of their sins, and reward them according to the best of what they used to do.

And those who believed in Allah and His Messenger, and did righteous deeds - We will surely remove from them their misdeeds, and We will surely reward them for the best of what they used to do.

This verse promises that believers who do good deeds will have their sins forgiven and be rewarded for their righteous actions.

Verse 8

وَوَصَّيْنَا الْإِنْسَانَ بِوَالِدَيْهِ حُسْنًا ۖ وَإِنْ جَاهَدَاكَ لِتُشْرِكَ بِي مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِ عِلْمٌ فَلَا تُطِعْهُمَا ۚ إِلَيَّ مَرْجِعُكُمْ فَأُنَبِّئُكُمْ بِمَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ

We have commanded people to honour their parents. But if they urge you to associate with Me what you have no knowledge of, then do not obey them. To Me you will ˹all˺ return, and then I will inform you of what you used to do.

And We have enjoined upon man [care] for his parents. But if they endeavor to make you associate with Me that of which you have no knowledge, do not obey them. And what is akin to the demand of associating partners with Allah is all other sins. There is no obedience to a creature, whoever he may be, in disobedience to Allah, as it has been proven from the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace. To Me is your return on the Day of Resurrection, and I will inform you of what you used to do in the world, of good and bad deeds, and I will recompense you for it.

While honoring parents is important, this verse states that obedience to them is not required if they urge you to associate others with Allah. Ultimately, everyone will return to Allah for judgment.

Verse 9

وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ لَنُدْخِلَنَّهُمْ فِي الصَّالِحِينَ

Those who believe and do good will surely be admitted by Us into ˹the company of˺ the righteous.

And those who believed in Allah and His Messenger and did righteous deeds, We will surely admit them to Paradise among the righteous servants of Allah.

Those who believe and do good will be admitted into Paradise among the righteous.

Verse 10

وَمِنَ النَّاسِ مَنْ يَقُولُ آمَنَّا بِاللَّهِ فَإِذَا أُوذِيَ فِي اللَّهِ جَعَلَ فِتْنَةَ النَّاسِ كَعَذَابِ اللَّهِ وَلَئِنْ جَاءَ نَصْرٌ مِنْ رَبِّكَ لَيَقُولُنَّ إِنَّا كُنَّا مَعَكُمْ ۚ أَوَلَيْسَ اللَّهُ بِأَعْلَمَ بِمَا فِي صُدُورِ الْعَالَمِينَ

There are some who say, “We believe in Allah,” but when they suffer in the cause of Allah, they mistake ˹this˺ persecution at the hands of people for the punishment of Allah. But when victory comes from your Lord, they surely say ˹to the believers˺, “We have always been with you.” Does Allah not know best what is in the hearts of all beings?

And of the people are some who say, "We believe in Allah," but when they are harmed for [the cause of] Allah, they consider the trial of the people as [if it were] the punishment of Allah. And if victory comes from your Lord, they will surely say, "Indeed, we were with you." Is not Allah most knowing of what is within the breasts of [all] beings?

This verse describes people who claim to believe but falter when facing hardship. They view trials as punishment from Allah, while seeking to align with the believers when victory is achieved. Allah knows what is in their hearts.

Verse 11

وَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ الْمُنَافِقِينَ

Allah will certainly distinguish between those who have ˹sure˺ faith and the hypocrites.

And Allah will surely make evident those who believed, and He will surely make evident the hypocrites, so that each group may be distinguished from the other.

Allah will distinguish between those who truly believe and those who are hypocrites.

Verse 12

وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اتَّبِعُوا سَبِيلَنَا وَلْنَحْمِلْ خَطَايَاكُمْ وَمَا هُمْ بِحَامِلِينَ مِنْ خَطَايَاهُمْ مِنْ شَيْءٍ ۖ إِنَّهُمْ لَكَاذِبُونَ

The disbelievers say to the believers, “˹Just˺ follow our way, and we will bear ˹the burden of˺ your sins.” But they would never ˹want to˺ bear any of the believers’ sins. They are simply lying.

And those who disbelieved, who denied the Oneness of Allah from Quraysh, and did not believe in Allah's threats and promises, said to those who believed in Allah among them and acted according to His law: Abandon the religion of Muhammad, and follow our religion, and we will bear the burden of your sins. But they will not bear anything of their sins. Indeed, they are liars in what they said.

Disbelievers may encourage believers to abandon their faith, promising to bear the burden of their sins. However, this is a lie, as no one can bear another's sins.

Verse 13

وَلَيَحْمِلُنَّ أَثْقَالَهُمْ وَأَثْقَالًا مَعَ أَثْقَالِهِمْ ۖ وَلَيُسْأَلُنَّ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ عَمَّا كَانُوا يَفْتَرُونَ

Yet they will certainly ˹be made to˺ carry their own burdens, as well as other burdens along with their own. And they will surely be questioned on Judgment Day about what they used to fabricate.

And they will surely bear their own burdens and [other] burdens with their burdens. And they will surely be questioned on the Day of Resurrection about what they used to invent.

Disbelievers will be held accountable for their own sins and the sins of those they misled. They will be questioned about their false claims.

Verse 14

وَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا نُوحًا إِلَىٰ قَوْمِهِ فَلَبِثَ فِيهِمْ أَلْفَ سَنَةٍ إِلَّا خَمْسِينَ عَامًا فَأَخَذَهُمُ الطُّوفَانُ وَهُمْ ظَالِمُونَ

Indeed, We sent Noah to his people, and he remained among them for a thousand years, less fifty. Then the Flood overtook them, while they persisted in wrongdoing.

And We had sent Noah to his people, and he remained among them a thousand years except fifty years, calling them to monotheism and forbidding them from polytheism. But they did not respond to him, so Allah destroyed them by the flood, while they were wrongdoers to themselves by their disbelief and transgression.

This verse recounts the story of Prophet Noah, who preached for 950 years. His people, however, persisted in their wrongdoing and were eventually destroyed by the Flood.

Verse 15

فَأَنْجَيْنَاهُ وَأَصْحَابَ السَّفِينَةِ وَجَعَلْنَاهَا آيَةً لِلْعَالَمِينَ

But We delivered him and those in the Ark, making it a sign for all people.

So We saved Noah and those who were with him in the ship, and We made that a sign and a lesson for the worlds.

This verse highlights the saving of Noah and those with him in the Ark, presenting it as a sign for humanity. It serves as a reminder of Allah's power to save believers and a warning to those who reject His message.

Verse 16

وَإِبْرَاهِيمَ إِذْ قَالَ لِقَوْمِهِ اعْبُدُوا اللَّهَ وَاتَّقُوهُ ۖ ذَٰلِكُمْ خَيْرٌ لَكُمْ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ

And ˹remember˺ when Abraham said to his people, “Worship Allah, and fear Him. This is better for you, if only you knew.

And mention - O Messenger - Abraham, peace be upon him, when he said to his people: 'Worship Allah alone, and fear His wrath by performing His obligations and avoiding His sins; that is better for you, if you know what is better for you than what is evil for you.'

The verse recalls Abraham's call to his people to worship Allah alone and to fear Him. It emphasizes that worshipping Allah and having piety is ultimately in their best interest.

Verse 17

إِنَّمَا تَعْبُدُونَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ أَوْثَانًا وَتَخْلُقُونَ إِفْكًا ۚ إِنَّ الَّذِينَ تَعْبُدُونَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ لَا يَمْلِكُونَ لَكُمْ رِزْقًا فَابْتَغُوا عِنْدَ اللَّهِ الرِّزْقَ وَاعْبُدُوهُ وَاشْكُرُوا لَهُ ۖ إِلَيْهِ تُرْجَعُونَ

You worship besides Allah nothing but idols, simply creating lies ˹about them˺. Those you worship besides Allah certainly cannot give you any provision. So seek provision from Allah ˹alone˺, worship Him, and be grateful to Him. To Him you will ˹all˺ be returned.”

You worship, O people, other than Allah only idols, and you invent a lie by calling them gods. Indeed, your idols that you worship besides Allah cannot provide you with any sustenance, so seek sustenance from Allah, not from your idols, and devote your worship and gratitude to Him for His provision to you. To Allah you will be returned after your death, and He will recompense you for what you have done.

This verse criticizes idol worship and emphasizes that idols cannot provide sustenance. It directs believers to seek provision from Allah alone, worship Him, and be grateful, knowing that all will return to Him.

Verse 18

وَإِنْ تُكَذِّبُوا فَقَدْ كَذَّبَ أُمَمٌ مِنْ قَبْلِكُمْ ۖ وَمَا عَلَى الرَّسُولِ إِلَّا الْبَلَاغُ الْمُبِينُ

If you persist in denial, so did ˹many˺ communities before you. The Messenger’s duty is only to deliver ˹the message˺ clearly.

And if you deny - O people - Our Messenger Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, in what he calls you to of worshiping Allah alone, then groups before you have denied their messengers in what they called them to of the truth, and Allah's wrath befell them. And the Messenger Muhammad is only obliged to deliver to you from Allah His clear message, and he has done so.

The verse acknowledges that some may reject the message, as previous communities did. It clarifies that the messenger's role is to deliver the message clearly, and that those who deny will face consequences.

Verse 19

أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا كَيْفَ يُبْدِئُ اللَّهُ الْخَلْقَ ثُمَّ يُعِيدُهُ ۚ إِنَّ ذَٰلِكَ عَلَى اللَّهِ يَسِيرٌ

Have they not seen how Allah originates the creation then resurrects it? That is certainly easy for Allah.

Do these people not know how Allah initiates the creation from nothing, then He brings it back after its annihilation, just as He began it the first time, a new creation? That is not difficult for Him. Indeed, that is easy for Allah, just as it was easy for Him to initiate it.

This verse poses a question to disbelievers, asking if they have not considered how Allah initiates creation and then resurrects it. The verse affirms that this process is easy for Allah, highlighting His power over life and death.

Verse 20

قُلْ سِيرُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ فَانْظُرُوا كَيْفَ بَدَأَ الْخَلْقَ ۚ ثُمَّ اللَّهُ يُنْشِئُ النَّشْأَةَ الْآخِرَةَ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Travel throughout the land and see how He originated the creation, then Allah will bring it into being one more time. Surely Allah is Most Capable of everything.

Say - O Messenger - to those who deny the resurrection after death: 'Travel through the earth, and see how Allah initiated the creation, and its initiation was not difficult for Him? Likewise, the re-creation of the Hereafter will not be difficult for Him. Indeed, Allah is powerful over all things; nothing He wills can overwhelm Him.'

This verse instructs the Prophet to tell disbelievers to travel the earth and observe how Allah initiated creation, making it clear that Allah has the power to recreate just as He initiated creation.

Verse 21

يُعَذِّبُ مَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيَرْحَمُ مَنْ يَشَاءُ ۖ وَإِلَيْهِ تُقْلَبُونَ

He punishes whoever He wills, and shows mercy to whoever He wills. And you will ˹all˺ be returned to Him.

He punishes whom He wills of His creation for the sins they committed during their lives, and He has mercy on whom He wills of them who repent, believe, and do righteous deeds. And to Him you will be returned, and He will recompense you for what you have done.

This verse describes Allah's authority to punish whom He wills and show mercy to whom He wills. It reminds all that they will return to Him, highlighting accountability.

Verse 22

وَمَا أَنْتُمْ بِمُعْجِزِينَ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا فِي السَّمَاءِ ۖ وَمَا لَكُمْ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ مِنْ وَلِيٍّ وَلَا نَصِيرٍ

And you cannot escape Him on earth or in heaven. Nor have you any protector or helper besides Allah.”

And you - O people - will not frustrate Allah in the earth or in the heavens if you disobey Him, and you have no protector besides Allah to manage your affairs, nor a helper to protect you from Allah if He intends evil for you.

The verse states that no one can escape Allah's power, either on Earth or in the heavens, emphasizing that there is no protector or helper other than Allah.

Verse 23

وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِآيَاتِ اللَّهِ وَلِقَائِهِ أُولَٰئِكَ يَئِسُوا مِنْ رَحْمَتِي وَأُولَٰئِكَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ

As for those who disbelieve in Allah’s signs and the meeting with Him, it is they who will have no hope in His mercy. And it is they who will suffer a painful punishment.

And those who reject the proofs of Allah and deny His signs, and the meeting with Him on the Day of Resurrection, those have no hope in My mercy when they witness what has been prepared for them of punishment, and those will have a painful, agonizing punishment.

This verse emphasizes that those who disbelieve in Allah's signs and the meeting with Him will have no hope in His mercy and will face a painful punishment.

Verse 24

فَمَا كَانَ جَوَابَ قَوْمِهِ إِلَّا أَنْ قَالُوا اقْتُلُوهُ أَوْ حَرِّقُوهُ فَأَنْجَاهُ اللَّهُ مِنَ النَّارِ ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ لِقَوْمٍ يُؤْمِنُونَ

But the only response of Abraham’s people was to say: “Kill him or burn him!” But Allah saved him from the fire. Surely in this are signs for people who believe.

So the answer of Abraham's people was nothing but that some of them said to others: Kill him or burn him with fire. So they threw him into it, and Allah saved him from it and made it cool and safe for him. Indeed, in Our saving of Abraham from the fire are signs and proofs for a people who believe in Allah and act according to His law.

Prophet Abraham's people tried to kill him by burning him, but Allah saved him from the fire. This is a clear sign for believers of Allah's power and protection.

Verse 25

وَقَالَ إِنَّمَا اتَّخَذْتُمْ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ أَوْثَانًا مَوَدَّةَ بَيْنِكُمْ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا ۖ ثُمَّ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ يَكْفُرُ بَعْضُكُمْ بِبَعْضٍ وَيَلْعَنُ بَعْضُكُمْ بَعْضًا وَمَأْوَاكُمُ النَّارُ وَمَا لَكُمْ مِنْ نَاصِرِينَ

He said ˹to his people˺, “You have taken idols ˹for worship˺ instead of Allah, only to keep ˹the bond of˺ harmony among yourselves in this worldly life. But on the Day of Judgment you will disown and curse one another. Your home will be the Fire, and you will have no helper!”

And Abraham said to his people: O my people, you have only worshipped, instead of Allah, false deities, which you have taken as affection among yourselves in this worldly life; you love one another for their worship, and you are friendly to each other for their service. Then on the Day of Resurrection, some of you will disown others, and some of you will curse others, and your destination will all be the Fire, and you will have no helper to prevent you from entering it.

Prophet Abraham criticized his people for worshipping idols for worldly harmony. On the Day of Judgment, they will disown each other and face the Fire without any help.

Verse 26

فَآمَنَ لَهُ لُوطٌ ۘ وَقَالَ إِنِّي مُهَاجِرٌ إِلَىٰ رَبِّي ۖ إِنَّهُ هُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

So Lot believed in him. And Abraham said, “I am emigrating ˹in obedience˺ to my Lord. He ˹alone˺ is indeed the Almighty, All-Wise.”

So Lot believed Abraham and followed his religion. And Abraham said, “Indeed, I am leaving the home of my people for the blessed land, which is 'Sham'. Indeed, Allah is the Mighty, who cannot be overcome, the Wise in His planning.

Prophet Lot believed in Abraham. Abraham then declared his emigration for the sake of Allah, acknowledging Allah as the Almighty and All-Wise.

Verse 27

وَوَهَبْنَا لَهُ إِسْحَاقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ وَجَعَلْنَا فِي ذُرِّيَّتِهِ النُّبُوَّةَ وَالْكِتَابَ وَآتَيْنَاهُ أَجْرَهُ فِي الدُّنْيَا ۖ وَإِنَّهُ فِي الْآخِرَةِ لَمِنَ الصَّالِحِينَ

We blessed him with Isaac and ˹later˺ Jacob, and reserved prophethood and revelation for his descendants. We gave him his reward in this life, and in the Hereafter he will certainly be among the righteous.

And We gave him Isaac as a son, and Jacob as a grandson, and We placed in his descendants the prophets and the scriptures, and We gave him the reward of his trials for Us, in this world, good mention and righteous offspring, and indeed, in the Hereafter, he will be among the righteous.

Allah blessed Abraham with Isaac and Jacob, and made prophethood and revelation among his descendants. Abraham was rewarded both in this life and will be among the righteous in the Hereafter.

Verse 28

وَلُوطًا إِذْ قَالَ لِقَوْمِهِ إِنَّكُمْ لَتَأْتُونَ الْفَاحِشَةَ مَا سَبَقَكُمْ بِهَا مِنْ أَحَدٍ مِنَ الْعَالَمِينَ

And ˹remember˺ when Lot rebuked ˹the men of˺ his people: “You certainly commit a shameful deed that no man has ever done before you.

And mention, O Messenger, Lot when he said to his people, “Indeed, you commit an abomination that no one in the worlds has ever done before. Do you approach men in their rear and cut off the way for travelers with your evil deeds, and do you commit despicable acts in your gatherings such as mocking people, stoning passersby, and harming them with unbecoming words and actions? And in this is a declaration that it is not permissible for people to gather on the evil that Allah and His Messenger have forbidden. So the answer of Lot's people was nothing but that they said: Bring us the punishment of Allah, if you are of the truthful in what you say, and the fulfillers of what you promise.

وَيَسْتَعْجِلُونَكَ بِالْعَذَابِ ۚ وَلَوْلَا أَجَلٌ مُسَمًّى لَجَاءَهُمُ الْعَذَابُ وَلَيَأْتِيَنَّهُمْ بَغْتَةً وَهُمْ لَا يَشْعُرُونَ

They challenge you ˹O Prophet˺ to hasten the punishment. Had it not been for a time already set, the punishment would have certainly come to them ˹at once˺. But it will definitely take them by surprise when they least expect it.

And they hasten you - O Messenger - those polytheists among your people with the punishment in mockery. And were it not that Allah has set for their punishment in this world a time that neither precedes nor delays, the punishment would have come to them when they requested it, and it would surely come to them suddenly while they are unaware of it and do not perceive it.

The disbelievers challenge the Prophet to bring on the punishment they mockingly seek. Allah has set a time for punishment, which will come suddenly when they least expect it.

Verse 54

يَسْتَعْجِلُونَكَ بِالْعَذَابِ وَإِنَّ جَهَنَّمَ لَمُحِيطَةٌ بِالْكَافِرِينَ

They urge you to hasten the punishment. And Hell will certainly encompass the disbelievers

They hasten you to bring the punishment in this world, and it will inevitably come to them, either in this world or in the Hereafter. And indeed, the punishment of Hell in the Hereafter will encompass them, with no escape for them from it.

The disbelievers are warned again about their hastening the punishment. Hell will encompass the disbelievers, leaving no way to escape.

Verse 55

يَوْمَ يَغْشَاهُمُ الْعَذَابُ مِنْ فَوْقِهِمْ وَمِنْ تَحْتِ أَرْجُلِهِمْ وَيَقُولُ ذُوقُوا مَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ

on the Day the punishment will overwhelm them from above them and from below their feet. And it will be said, “Reap what you sowed.”

On the Day of Resurrection, the punishment of Hell will cover the disbelievers from above their heads and from beneath their feet. So the fire will cover them from all directions, and Allah will say to them at that time: 'Taste the recompense for what you used to do in the world: associating partners with Allah, and committing crimes and sins.'

On the Day of Judgment, the disbelievers will be overwhelmed by the punishment from all directions. They will be told to taste the consequences of their disbelief and actions.

Verse 56

يَا عِبَادِيَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِنَّ أَرْضِي وَاسِعَةٌ فَإِيَّايَ فَاعْبُدُونِ

O My believing servants! My earth is truly spacious, so worship Me ˹alone˺.

O My servants who have believed, if you are in hardship due to showing faith and worshipping Allah alone, then emigrate to the wide land of Allah, and devote your worship to Me alone.

Believers are reminded that if they face hardship for worshipping Allah, they can migrate to another place on Allah's vast earth where they can worship Him alone.

Verse 57

كُلُّ نَفْسٍ ذَائِقَةُ الْمَوْتِ ۖ ثُمَّ إِلَيْنَا تُرْجَعُونَ

Every soul will taste death, then to Us you will ˹all˺ be returned.

Every living soul will taste death. Then to Us you will be returned for reckoning and recompense.

Every soul will taste death. After death, all will return to Allah for judgment.

Verse 58

وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ لَنُبَوِّئَنَّهُمْ مِنَ الْجَنَّةِ غُرَفًا تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ نِعْمَ أَجْرُ الْعَامِلِينَ

˹As for˺ those who believe and do good, We will certainly house them in ˹elevated˺ mansions in Paradise, under which rivers flow, to stay there forever. How excellent is the reward for those who work ˹righteousness!˺—

And those who believed in Allah and His Messenger and did what they were commanded of righteous deeds, We will surely settle them in the Garden, in high chambers beneath which rivers flow, abiding therein eternally. Excellent is the reward of the workers in obedience to Allah, these chambers in the Gardens of Bliss.

Those who believe and do good will be housed in Paradise with high chambers and flowing rivers, to abide there forever. This is an excellent reward for their righteous deeds.

Verse 59

الَّذِينَ صَبَرُوا وَعَلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ يَتَوَكَّلُونَ

those who patiently endure, and put their trust in their Lord!

Indeed, those mentioned Gardens are for the believers who were patient in worshipping Allah, held fast to their religion, and relied on Allah for their sustenance and their struggle against their enemies.

Paradise is prepared for those who are patient, steadfast in their faith, and put their trust in Allah.

Verse 60

وَكَأَيِّنْ مِنْ دَابَّةٍ لَا تَحْمِلُ رِزْقَهَا اللَّهُ يَرْزُقُهَا وَإِيَّاكُمْ ۚ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ

How many are the creatures that cannot secure their provisions! ˹It is˺ Allah ˹Who˺ provides for them and you ˹as well˺. He is indeed the All-Hearing, All-Knowing.

And how many creatures there are that do not store their food for tomorrow, as the son of Adam does. But Allah, glory be to Him, provides for them as He provides for you. And He is the All-Hearing of your words, the All-Knowing of your actions and the thoughts in your hearts.

Many creatures do not store their food, yet Allah provides for them. Similarly, Allah is the provider for all, including humans, and He is All-Hearing and All-Knowing.

Verse 61

وَلَئِنْ سَأَلْتَهُمْ مَنْ خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ وَسَخَّرَ الشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ لَيَقُولُنَّ اللَّهُ ۖ فَأَنَّىٰ يُؤْفَكُونَ

If you ask them ˹O Prophet˺ who created the heavens and the earth and subjected the sun and the moon ˹for your benefit˺, they will certainly say, “Allah!” How can they then be deluded ˹from the truth˺?

And if you ask - O Messenger - the polytheists: 'Who created the heavens and the earth in this wondrous order, and subjected the sun and the moon?' they will surely say: 'Allah alone created them.' So how are they turned away from belief in Allah, the Creator of everything and its Manager, and they worship others with Him? How amazing their falsehood and lies are!!

Even polytheists acknowledge that Allah created the heavens and the earth, and subjected the sun and moon. Yet, they still turn away from worshipping Allah. This shows their hypocrisy.

Verse 62

اللَّهُ يَبْسُطُ الرِّزْقَ لِمَنْ يَشَاءُ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ وَيَقْدِرُ لَهُ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيمٌ

Allah gives abundant or limited provisions to whoever He wills of His servants. Surely Allah has ˹full˺ knowledge of everything.

Allah, glory be to Him, expands the provision for whom He wills of His creation and restricts it from others among them, for His knowledge of what is best for His servants. Indeed, Allah is All-Knowing of everything about your states and affairs, nothing is hidden from Him.

Allah provides for His servants abundantly or in limited measure, according to His perfect knowledge and wisdom.

Verse 63

وَلَئِنْ سَأَلْتَهُمْ مَنْ نَزَّلَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً فَأَحْيَا بِهِ الْأَرْضَ مِنْ بَعْدِ مَوْتِهَا لَيَقُولُنَّ اللَّهُ ۚ قُلِ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ ۚ بَلْ أَكْثَرُهُمْ لَا يَعْقِلُونَ

And if you ask them who sends down rain from the sky, giving life to the earth after its death, they will surely say, “Allah!” Say, “Praise be to Allah!” In fact, most of them do not understand.

And if you ask - O Messenger - the polytheists: 'Who sends down water from the clouds and thereby gives life to the earth after its dryness?' they will surely say to you, acknowledging it: 'Allah alone is the One Who sends that down.' Say: 'Praise be to Allah, who has made your argument clear to them.' But most of them do not understand what benefits them or what harms them. And if they did understand, they would not associate with Allah others besides Him.

The polytheists admit that Allah sends rain and revives the earth. The verse urges, “Praise be to Allah!” Most people still fail to understand this truth and associate partners with Allah despite recognizing His power and blessings.

Verse 64

وَمَا هَٰذِهِ الْحَيَاةُ الدُّنْيَا إِلَّا لَهْوٌ وَلَعِبٌ ۚ وَإِنَّ الدَّارَ الْآخِرَةَ لَهِيَ الْحَيَوَانُ ۚ لَوْ كَانُوا يَعْلَمُونَ

This worldly life is no more than play and amusement. But the Hereafter is indeed the real life, if only they knew.

This worldly life is nothing but diversion and amusement, hearts are diverted by it, and bodies are played with because of its adornment and desires. Then it quickly vanishes. And the Hereafter is indeed the true lasting life in which there is no death. If people knew this, they would not prefer the abode of annihilation over the abode of eternity.

This verse explains that worldly life is temporary and full of distractions, unlike the Hereafter, which is the true and lasting life. This should encourage people to prioritize the eternal over the fleeting.

Verse 65

فَإِذَا رَكِبُوا فِي الْفُلْكِ دَعَوُا اللَّهَ مُخْلِصِينَ لَهُ الدِّينَ فَلَمَّا نَجَّاهُمْ إِلَى الْبَرِّ إِذَا هُمْ يُشْرِكُونَ

If they happen to be aboard a ship ˹caught in a storm˺, they cry out to Allah ˹alone˺ in sincere devotion. But as soon as He delivers them ˹safely˺ to shore, they associate ˹others with Him once again˺.

And when the disbelievers embark on ships in the sea and fear drowning, they single out Allah, and sincerely supplicate to Him in their distress. But when He delivers them to the land and the distress is removed from them, they revert to their polytheism. Indeed, they contradict themselves. They single out Allah in the hour of distress, and associate partners with Him in times of ease. And their polytheism after our favor upon them by deliverance from the sea will result in the denial of what We have bestowed upon them in themselves and their wealth, and so they complete their enjoyment in this world. They will soon know the corruption of their deeds, and what Allah has prepared for them of a painful punishment on the Day of Resurrection. And in that is a threat and a warning for them.

This verse describes how people turn to Allah alone in times of distress, but then revert to associating partners with Him once the danger is gone. This hypocrisy highlights a lack of genuine faith.

Verse 66

لِيَكْفُرُوا بِمَا آتَيْنَاهُمْ وَلِيَتَمَتَّعُوا ۖ فَسَوْفَ يَعْلَمُونَ

So let them be ungrateful for all We have given them, and ˹let them˺ enjoy themselves ˹for now˺! For they will soon know.

And when the disbelievers embark on ships in the sea and fear drowning, they single out Allah, and sincerely supplicate to Him in their distress. But when He delivers them to the land and the distress is removed from them, they revert to their polytheism. Indeed, they contradict themselves. They single out Allah in the hour of distress, and associate partners with Him in times of ease. And their polytheism after our favor upon them by deliverance from the sea will result in the denial of what We have bestowed upon them in themselves and their wealth, and so they complete their enjoyment in this world. They will soon know the corruption of their deeds, and what Allah has prepared for them of a painful punishment on the Day of Resurrection. And in that is a threat and a warning for them.

This verse is a warning to those who are ungrateful for Allah's blessings and who indulge in worldly pleasures. They will soon face the consequences of their actions.

Verse 67

أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا أَنَّا جَعَلْنَا حَرَمًا آمِنًا وَيُتَخَطَّفُ النَّاسُ مِنْ حَوْلِهِمْ ۚ أَفَبِالْبَاطِلِ يُؤْمِنُونَ وَبِنِعْمَةِ اللَّهِ يَكْفُرُونَ

Have they not seen how We have made ˹Mecca˺ a safe haven, whereas people ˹all˺ around them are snatched away? How can they then believe in falsehood and deny Allah’s favours?

Do the disbelievers of Mecca not see that Allah has made Mecca a sanctuary, secure for them? In it its people are safe in their lives and wealth, while people around them outside the sanctuary are snatched away, insecure? Do they believe in polytheism and disbelieve in Allah's favor that he has favored them with, not worshiping Him alone without others?

This verse questions why people deny Allah's favors, specifically highlighting the safety of Mecca as a sign of His grace. It contrasts this with the instability and danger that others experience.

Verse 68

وَمَنْ أَظْلَمُ مِمَّنِ افْتَرَىٰ عَلَى اللَّهِ كَذِبًا أَوْ كَذَّبَ بِالْحَقِّ لَمَّا جَاءَهُ ۚ أَلَيْسَ فِي جَهَنَّمَ مَثْوًى لِلْكَافِرِينَ

And who does more wrong than those who fabricate lies against Allah or reject the truth after it has reached them? Is Hell not a ˹fitting˺ home for the disbelievers?

And who is more unjust than one who invents a lie against Allah, attributing his misguidance and falsehood to Allah, or denies the truth that Allah has sent with His Messenger Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him? Indeed, in the Fire is a dwelling for those who disbelieve in Allah, deny His Oneness, and reject His Messenger Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him.

This verse identifies those who invent lies about Allah or deny the truth as the most unjust of people. Their abode is Hellfire.

Verse 69

وَالَّذِينَ جَاهَدُوا فِينَا لَنَهْدِيَنَّهُمْ سُبُلَنَا ۚ وَإِنَّ اللَّهَ لَمَعَ الْمُحْسِنِينَ

As for those who struggle in Our cause, We will surely guide them along Our Way. And Allah is certainly with the good-doers.

And those who have believed and struggled against the enemies of Allah, themselves, and Satan, and were patient in trials and tribulations in the way of Allah, Allah will guide them to the paths of goodness and make them steadfast on the straight path. And whoever is like that is an doer of good to himself and others. And indeed, Allah, the Most High, is surely with those who do good from his creation with victory, support, preservation, and guidance.

This verse promises guidance to those who strive for Allah's cause, emphasizing that Allah is always with those who do good. It encourages continuous effort and good deeds.
