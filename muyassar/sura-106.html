Sura 106

Verse 1

لِإِيلَافِ قُرَيْشٍ

˹At least˺ for ˹the favour of˺ making Quraysh habitually secure—

Be amazed at the familiarity of Quraysh, their security, the stability of their interests, the regularity of their journeys in the winter to "Yemen", and in the summer to "Syria", and the facilitation of that to bring what they need.

This verse highlights the favor of Allah in granting security and stability to the Quraysh tribe, particularly their trade caravans.

Verse 2

إِيلَافِهِمْ رِحْلَةَ الشِّتَاءِ وَالصَّيْفِ

secure in their trading caravan ˹to Yemen˺ in the winter and ˹Syria˺ in the summer—

Be amazed at the familiarity of Quraysh, their security, the stability of their interests, the regularity of their journeys in the winter to "Yemen", and in the summer to "Syria", and the facilitation of that to bring what they need.

It specifically mentions the security of the Quraysh's trade journeys to Yemen in winter and Syria in summer, underscoring Allah's provision for their needs.

Verse 3

فَلْيَعْبُدُوا رَبَّ هَٰذَا الْبَيْتِ

let them worship the Lord of this ˹Sacred˺ House,

So let them give thanks and worship the Lord of this House - which is the Kaaba - by which they were honored, and let them unify Him and dedicate their worship to Him.

This verse calls on the Quraysh to worship Allah alone, the Lord of the Kaaba, as a sign of gratitude for His blessings.

Verse 4

الَّذِي أَطْعَمَهُمْ مِنْ جُوعٍ وَآمَنَهُمْ مِنْ خَوْفٍ

Who has fed them against hunger and made them secure against fear.

He who fed them from severe hunger, and secured them from terror and great fear.

This emphasizes Allah's role in providing food and security, urging the Quraysh to recognize their dependence on Him.
