Verse 1

يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُدَّثِّرُ

O you covered up ˹in your clothes˺!

O you who are wrapped up in your garments, arise from your bed, and warn people of the punishment of Allah, and single out your Lord alone for glorification, unification, and worship, and purify your garments from impurities, for the purity of the outward is part of the perfection of the inward, and persevere in forsaking idols, false gods, and all acts of polytheism, so do not approach them, and do not give a gift in order to seek more than it, and for the pleasure of your Lord, be patient with commands and prohibitions.

This verse addresses the Prophet, telling him to arise from his bed, and prepare himself to warn people.

(And the one meant by this is Al-Waleed ibn al-Mugheera, the opponent of truth, who challenged Allah and His Messenger with hostility, and this is the punishment of everyone who opposes and defies the truth).

Allah tells the Prophet to leave to Him the one He created uniquely, referring to a disbeliever who was given much wealth and children, but still rejected faith.
