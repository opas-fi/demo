Sura 005

Verse 1

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا أَوْفُوا بِالْعُقُودِ ۚ أُحِلَّتْ لَكُمْ بَهِيمَةُ الْأَنْعَامِ إِلَّا مَا يُتْلَىٰ عَلَيْكُمْ غَيْرَ مُحِلِّي الصَّيْدِ وَأَنْتُمْ حُرُمٌ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَحْكُمُ مَا يُرِيدُ

O believers! Honour your obligations. All grazing livestock has been made lawful to you—except what is hereby announced to you and hunting while on pilgrimage. Indeed, Allah commands what He wills.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, fulfill the confirmed covenants of Allah, from belief in the laws of religion, and submission to them, and fulfill the covenants to one another of trusts, transactions, and other matters that do not contradict the Book of Allah, and the Sunnah of His Messenger Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace. And Allah has made lawful for you the beast of cattle, which are camels, cows, and sheep, except what He has clarified to you of the prohibition of dead animals, blood, and other such things, and the prohibition of hunting while you are in the state of Ihram. Indeed, Allah judges what He wills in accordance with His wisdom and justice.

This verse instructs believers to fulfill their obligations and contracts. It declares that grazing livestock (camels, cows, and sheep) are lawful to eat, except what is specifically forbidden, such as hunting while on pilgrimage (Ihram). This highlights the importance of obeying Allah's commands and honoring commitments.

Verse 2

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تُحِلُّوا شَعَائِرَ اللَّهِ وَلَا الشَّهْرَ الْحَرَامَ وَلَا الْهَدْيَ وَلَا الْقَلَائِدَ وَلَا آمِّينَ الْبَيْتَ الْحَرَامَ يَبْتَغُونَ فَضْلًا مِنْ رَبِّهِمْ وَرِضْوَانًا ۚ وَإِذَا حَلَلْتُمْ فَاصْطَادُوا ۚ وَلَا يَجْرِمَنَّكُمْ شَنَآنُ قَوْمٍ أَنْ صَدُّوكُمْ عَنِ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ أَنْ تَعْتَدُوا ۘ وَتَعَاوَنُوا عَلَى الْبِرِّ وَالتَّقْوَىٰ ۖ وَلَا تَعَاوَنُوا عَلَى الْإِثْمِ وَالْعُدْوَانِ ۚ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ ۖ إِنَّ اللَّهَ شَدِيدُ الْعِقَابِ

O believers! Do not violate Allah’s rituals ˹of pilgrimage˺, the sacred months, the sacrificial animals, the ˹offerings decorated with˺ garlands, nor those ˹pilgrims˺ on their way to the Sacred House seeking their Lord’s bounty and pleasure. When pilgrimage has ended, you are allowed to hunt. Do not let the hatred of a people who once barred you from the Sacred Mosque provoke you to transgress. Cooperate with one another in goodness and righteousness, and do not cooperate in sin and transgression. And be mindful of Allah. Surely Allah is severe in punishment.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, do not violate the limits of Allah and His landmarks, and do not permit fighting in the sacred months, which are: Dhu al-Qa'dah, Dhu al-Hijjah, Muharram, and Rajab. That was at the beginning of Islam, and do not violate the sanctity of the Hadi (sacrificial animal) or what is marked of it; as they would place collars, which were braids of wool or hair, on the necks as a sign that the animal was a Hadi and that the man intended to perform Hajj. And do not permit fighting those heading to the Sacred House who seek from the bounty of Allah that which will make their livelihood and please their Lord. And when you have ended your Ihram, then hunting is lawful for you. And let not hatred of a people for having prevented you from reaching the Sacred Mosque - as happened in the year of "Hudaybiyah" - incite you to abandon justice toward them. And cooperate - O believers among yourselves - in doing good deeds, and in the fear of Allah, and do not cooperate in what contains sin, disobedience and transgression of the limits of Allah, and beware of disobeying the command of Allah, for He is severe in punishment.

This verse emphasizes respect for the rituals of pilgrimage, including the sacred months, sacrificial animals, and those traveling to the Sacred House. It warns against letting past conflicts incite injustice and promotes cooperation in righteousness and avoiding sin. This verse also highlights the gravity of disobeying Allah's commands.

Verse 3

حُرِّمَتْ عَلَيْكُمُ الْمَيْتَةُ وَالدَّمُ وَلَحْمُ الْخِنْزِيرِ وَمَا أُهِلَّ لِغَيْرِ اللَّهِ بِهِ وَالْمُنْخَنِقَةُ وَالْمَوْقُوذَةُ وَالْمُتَرَدِّيَةُ وَالنَّطِيحَةُ وَمَا أَكَلَ السَّبُعُ إِلَّا مَا ذَكَّيْتُمْ وَمَا ذُبِحَ عَلَى النُّصُبِ وَأَنْ تَسْتَقْسِمُوا بِالْأَزْلَامِ ۚ ذَٰلِكُمْ فِسْقٌ ۗ الْيَوْمَ يَئِسَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا مِنْ دِينِكُمْ فَلَا تَخْشَوْهُمْ وَاخْشَوْنِ ۚ الْيَوْمَ أَكْمَلْتُ لَكُمْ دِينَكُمْ وَأَتْمَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعْمَتِي وَرَضِيتُ لَكُمُ الْإِسْلَامَ دِينًا ۚ فَمَنِ اضْطُرَّ فِي مَخْمَصَةٍ غَيْرَ مُتَجَانِفٍ لِإِثْمٍ ۙ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

Forbidden to you are carrion, blood, and swine; what is slaughtered in the name of any other than Allah; what is killed by strangling, beating, a fall, or by being gored to death; what is partly eaten by a predator unless you slaughter it; and what is sacrificed on altars. You are also forbidden to draw lots for decisions. This is all evil. Today the disbelievers have given up all hope of ˹undermining˺ your faith. So do not fear them; fear Me! Today I have perfected your faith for you, completed My favour upon you, and chosen Islam as your way. But whoever is compelled by extreme hunger—not intending to sin—then surely Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

Allah has forbidden you dead animals, which are those animals that die without being ritually slaughtered, and He has forbidden you flowing blood that has been shed, the flesh of swine, and that which has been slaughtered in the name of other than Allah. Also forbidden are animals that have been strangled, which are those whose breath is constricted until they die; animals that have been beaten to death, which are those struck by a stick or stone until they die; animals that have fallen from a high place or into a well and died; and animals that have been gored by another animal and died. Allah has also forbidden you animals that have been eaten by wild beasts, such as lions, tigers, and wolves, and similar creatures. He, glory be to Him, has made an exception from the forbidden strangled animals and those mentioned after them that you are permitted to eat what you ritually slaughter before it dies. Allah has forbidden you what has been sacrificed to other than Allah on what is set up for worship, such as stones or other objects. Allah has forbidden you to seek knowledge of what has been allocated to you or not by arrows, which are the arrows they used to draw lots when they wanted to do something before they undertook it. That which is mentioned in the verse of forbidden things -if committed- is a departure from the command of Allah and His obedience to His disobedience. Now, the disbelievers have lost hope that you will turn away from your religion to polytheism, after I have given you victory over them, so do not fear them and fear Me. Today I have perfected for you your religion, Islam, by achieving victory and completing the Sharia, and I have completed My favor upon you by bringing you out of the darkness of ignorance into the light of faith, and I have approved Islam for you as a religion, so adhere to it and do not abandon it. But whoever is compelled by hunger to eat the dead, without being deliberately inclined to sin, then he is allowed to eat it, for Allah is Forgiving to him, Merciful to him.

This verse outlines dietary prohibitions, including carrion, blood, and swine. It also forbids animals slaughtered in the name of other than Allah, or killed by strangling, beating, falling, or goring, unless properly slaughtered before death. It also prohibits divination by lots. The verse emphasizes the completion of the faith and encourages fear of Allah, not disbelievers. It permits consuming forbidden things only in extreme hunger, without intending sin.

Verse 4

يَسْأَلُونَكَ مَاذَا أُحِلَّ لَهُمْ ۖ قُلْ أُحِلَّ لَكُمُ الطَّيِّبَاتُ ۙ وَمَا عَلَّمْتُمْ مِنَ الْجَوَارِحِ مُكَلِّبِينَ تُعَلِّمُونَهُنَّ مِمَّا عَلَّمَكُمُ اللَّهُ ۖ فَكُلُوا مِمَّا أَمْسَكْنَ عَلَيْكُمْ وَاذْكُرُوا اسْمَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْهِ ۖ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ سَرِيعُ الْحِسَابِ

They ask you, ˹O Prophet,˺ what is permissible for them ˹to eat˺. Say, “What is good and lawful. Also what is caught by your hunting animals and birds of prey which you have trained as instructed by Allah. So eat what they catch for you, but mention the Name of Allah over it ˹first˺.” And be mindful of Allah. Surely Allah is swift in reckoning.

Your companions ask you, O Prophet, what has been made lawful for them to eat. Say to them, “Lawful for you are the good things and what you have trained of birds and beasts of prey such as dogs, leopards, and falcons and the like, which are taught. You train them to hunt for you, taught to you by Allah. So, eat of what they catch for you and mention the name of Allah when you release them for hunting, and fear Allah in what He has commanded you to do and what He has forbidden you to do. Indeed, Allah is swift in account.”

This verse clarifies that lawful foods include those caught by trained hunting animals or birds. When releasing the trained animals to hunt, mentioning the name of Allah is necessary. The verse encourages mindfulness of Allah, emphasizing that He is swift in reckoning.

Verse 5

الْيَوْمَ أُحِلَّ لَكُمُ الطَّيِّبَاتُ ۖ وَطَعَامُ الَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْكِتَابَ حِلٌّ لَكُمْ وَطَعَامُكُمْ حِلٌّ لَهُمْ ۖ وَالْمُحْصَنَاتُ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنَاتِ وَالْمُحْصَنَاتُ مِنَ الَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْكِتَابَ مِنْ قَبْلِكُمْ إِذَا آتَيْتُمُوهُنَّ أُجُورَهُنَّ مُحْصِنِينَ غَيْرَ مُسَافِحِينَ وَلَا مُتَّخِذِي أَخْدَانٍ ۗ وَمَنْ يَكْفُرْ بِالْإِيمَانِ فَقَدْ حَبِطَ عَمَلُهُ وَهُوَ فِي الْآخِرَةِ مِنَ الْخَاسِرِينَ

Today all good, pure foods have been made lawful for you. Similarly, the food of the People of the Book is permissible for you and yours is permissible for them. And ˹permissible for you in marriage˺ are chaste believing women as well as chaste women of those given the Scripture before you—as long as you pay them their dowries in wedlock, neither fornicating nor taking them as mistresses. And whoever rejects the faith, all their good deeds will be void ˹in this life˺ and in the Hereafter they will be among the losers.

And from the perfection of Allah’s blessings upon you today, O believers, is that He has made lawful for you the good lawful things, and the sacrifices of Jews and Christians - if they sacrifice according to their law - are lawful for you, and your sacrifices are lawful for them. And lawful for you, O believers, is marriage to chaste free women who are believers, who are abstinent from fornication, and also marriage to chaste free women from among the Jews and Christians if you give them their dowries, and if you are chaste and not committing fornication, nor taking secret lovers, and you are safe from being influenced by their religion. And whoever denies the laws of faith, then his deeds are in vain, and he will be among the losers on the Day of Resurrection.

This verse declares that all good and pure foods are lawful. It also permits eating the food of the People of the Book (Jews and Christians) and marriage with chaste believing women, as well as chaste women from the People of the Book, as long as dowries are paid in wedlock, with no fornication or secret mistresses. It warns that those who reject the faith will have their good deeds void and will be among the losers in the Hereafter.

Verse 6

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى الصَّلَاةِ فَاغْسِلُوا وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى الْمَرَافِقِ وَامْسَحُوا بِرُءُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى الْكَعْبَيْنِ ۚ وَإِنْ كُنْتُمْ جُنُبًا فَاطَّهَّرُوا ۚ وَإِنْ كُنْتُمْ مَرْضَىٰ أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ أَوْ جَاءَ أَحَدٌ مِنْكُمْ مِنَ الْغَائِطِ أَوْ لَامَسْتُمُ النِّسَاءَ فَلَمْ تَجِدُوا مَاءً فَتَيَمَّمُوا صَعِيدًا طَيِّبًا فَامْسَحُوا بِوُجُوهِكُمْ وَأَيْدِيكُمْ مِنْهُ ۚ مَا يُرِيدُ اللَّهُ لِيَجْعَلَ عَلَيْكُمْ مِنْ حَرَجٍ وَلَٰكِنْ يُرِيدُ لِيُطَهِّرَكُمْ وَلِيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُ عَلَيْكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ

O believers! When you rise up for prayer, wash your faces and your hands up to the elbows, wipe your heads, and wash your feet to the ankles. And if you are in a state of ˹full˺ impurity, then take a full bath. But if you are ill, on a journey, or have relieved yourselves, or have been intimate with your wives and cannot find water, then purify yourselves with clean earth by wiping your faces and hands. It is not Allah’s Will to burden you, but to purify you and complete His favour upon you, so perhaps you will be grateful.

O you who have believed, when you rise to [perform] prayer while you are in a state of impurity, then wash your faces and your hands to the elbows (and the elbow: the joint between the forearm and the upper arm) and wipe over your heads and wash your feet to the ankles (which are: the two prominent bones at the meeting point of the leg with the foot). And if you are in a state of major impurity, then purify yourselves by bathing from it before prayer. But if you are ill or on a journey while in good health, or one of you has relieved himself, or has had sexual intercourse with his wife, and you find no water, then strike with your hands the surface of the earth and wipe your faces and hands with it. Allah does not intend in the matter of purification to make difficulty for you, but rather He has permitted dry ablution as a leniency upon you, and as a mercy to you, as He made it a substitute for water in purification, so the permission of dry ablution was from the completeness of the blessings which necessitate thanking the Bestower of blessings; by obeying Him in what He commanded and what He forbade.

This verse outlines the rules for ritual purification before prayer. It describes how to perform ablution (wudu) with water, washing specific body parts. If water is unavailable, dry ablution (tayammum) with clean earth is an alternative, showing Allah's ease and mercy.

Verse 7

وَاذْكُرُوا نِعْمَةَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَمِيثَاقَهُ الَّذِي وَاثَقَكُمْ بِهِ إِذْ قُلْتُمْ سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ بِذَاتِ الصُّدُورِ

Remember Allah’s favour upon you and the covenant He made with you when you said, “We hear and obey.” And be mindful of Allah. Surely Allah knows best what is ˹hidden˺ in the heart.

And remember the favor of Allah upon you in what He has legislated for you, and remember His covenant that He, Exalted is He, took from you regarding belief in Allah and His Messenger Muhammad, peace be upon him, and hearing and obedience to them, and fear Allah in what He has commanded you and what He has forbidden you from. Indeed, Allah is Knowing of what you conceal within yourselves.

This verse reminds believers of Allah's favors and their covenant to obey Him. It emphasizes mindfulness and that Allah knows what is hidden in their hearts, highlighting the importance of sincere intentions.

Verse 8

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا كُونُوا قَوَّامِينَ لِلَّهِ شُهَدَاءَ بِالْقِسْطِ ۖ وَلَا يَجْرِمَنَّكُمْ شَنَآنُ قَوْمٍ عَلَىٰ أَلَّا تَعْدِلُوا ۚ اعْدِلُوا هُوَ أَقْرَبُ لِلتَّقْوَىٰ ۖ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ خَبِيرٌ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ

O believers! Stand firm for Allah and bear true testimony. Do not let the hatred of a people lead you to injustice. Be just! That is closer to righteousness. And be mindful of Allah. Surely Allah is All-Aware of what you do.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger Muhammad, peace be upon him, be persistently standing firm for justice, seeking the face of Allah, bearing witness to fairness, and let not the hatred of a people prevent you from being just. Be just between enemies and loved ones to the same degree, for that justice is nearest to fear of Allah, and beware of being unjust. Indeed, Allah is Aware of what you do, and He will recompense you for it.

This verse commands believers to stand firmly for justice for Allah's sake, even if they dislike or hate certain people. It emphasizes that being just is closer to righteousness and that Allah is aware of all actions.

Verse 9

وَعَدَ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ ۙ لَهُمْ مَغْفِرَةٌ وَأَجْرٌ عَظِيمٌ

Allah has promised those who believe and do good ˹His˺ forgiveness and a great reward.

Allah has promised those who believed in Allah and His Messenger and did righteous deeds that He will forgive them their sins, and that He will reward them for that with Paradise, and Allah does not break His promise.

This verse assures believers that Allah has promised forgiveness and a great reward for those who believe and do good deeds, reminding them of the blessings for righteous behavior.

Verse 10

وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا وَكَذَّبُوا بِآيَاتِنَا أُولَٰئِكَ أَصْحَابُ الْجَحِيمِ

As for those who disbelieve and deny Our signs, they are the residents of the Hellfire.

And those who denied the Oneness of Allah, which indicates the clear truth, and rejected His proofs that the messengers brought, they are the people of the Fire, who will abide therein.

This verse speaks about the fate of those who reject faith and deny Allah's signs. They are destined to reside in Hellfire.

Verse 11

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اذْكُرُوا نِعْمَتَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ إِذْ هَمَّ قَوْمٌ أَنْ يَبْسُطُوا إِلَيْكُمْ أَيْدِيَهُمْ فَكَفَّ أَيْدِيَهُمْ عَنْكُمْ ۖ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ ۚ وَعَلَى اللَّهِ فَلْيَتَوَكَّلِ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ

O believers! Remember Allah’s favour upon you: when a people sought to harm you, but He held their hands back from you. Be mindful of Allah. And in Allah let the believers put their trust.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, remember the favor of Allah upon you, the favor of security, and casting fear into the hearts of your enemies who intended to strike you, but Allah turned them away from you, and He intervened between them and what they intended for you. And fear Allah and be wary of Him, and rely upon Allah alone in your religious and worldly affairs, and trust in His help and victory.

This verse reminds believers of Allah's favor in protecting them from harm when others plotted against them. It emphasizes mindfulness of Allah and putting trust in Him alone.

Verse 12

وَلَقَدْ أَخَذَ اللَّهُ مِيثَاقَ بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ وَبَعَثْنَا مِنْهُمُ اثْنَيْ عَشَرَ نَقِيبًا ۖ وَقَالَ اللَّهُ إِنِّي مَعَكُمْ ۖ لَئِنْ أَقَمْتُمُ الصَّلَاةَ وَآتَيْتُمُ الزَّكَاةَ وَآمَنْتُمْ بِرُسُلِي وَعَزَّرْتُمُوهُمْ وَأَقْرَضْتُمُ اللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا لَأُكَفِّرَنَّ عَنْكُمْ سَيِّئَاتِكُمْ وَلَأُدْخِلَنَّكُمْ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ ۚ فَمَنْ كَفَرَ بَعْدَ ذَٰلِكَ مِنْكُمْ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ سَوَاءَ السَّبِيلِ

Allah made a covenant with the Children of Israel and appointed twelve leaders from among them and ˹then˺ said, “I am truly with you. If you establish prayer, pay alms-tax, believe in My messengers, support them, and lend to Allah a good loan, I will certainly forgive your sins and admit you into Gardens under which rivers flow. And whoever among you disbelieves afterwards has truly strayed from the Right Way.”

And Allah had taken the confirmed covenant from the Children of Israel that they should devote worship to Him alone, and Allah commanded Moses to appoint twelve leaders over them according to the number of their tribes, taking from them the covenant of hearing and obedience to Allah, His Messenger, and His Book. And Allah said to the Children of Israel: I am with you in My protection and victory. If you establish prayer and give the obligatory charity to those who deserve it, and believe in My messengers in what they have informed you, and support them, and spend in My cause, I will surely expiate your evil deeds from you, and I will surely admit you to gardens beneath whose palaces rivers flow. But whoever denies this covenant from among you has deviated from the path of truth to the path of misguidance.

This verse recounts a covenant Allah made with the Children of Israel, promising forgiveness and Paradise in return for establishing prayer, paying alms, believing in messengers, supporting them, and lending to Allah a good loan. That is why We ordained for the Children of Israel that whoever takes a life—unless as a punishment for murder or mischief in the land—it will be as if they killed all of humanity; and whoever saves a life, it will be as if they saved all of humanity. ˹Although˺ Our messengers already came to them with clear proofs, many of them still transgressed afterwards through the land.

Because of this crime of murder, We prescribed for the Children of Israel that whoever kills a soul for other than a cause of retaliation or corruption [done] in the land - by any type of corruption that warrants killing, such as polytheism or warring - it is as if he had killed all mankind. In what concerns the severe punishment from Allah, and whoever refrains from killing a soul which Allah has forbidden, it is as if he has given life to all mankind. So, preserving the sanctity of one human is preserving the sanctity of all people. And certainly, Our messengers came to the Children of Israel with clear proofs and evidences of the validity of what they called them to, of faith in their Lord, and performing what was ordained upon them, then many of them, after the coming of the messengers to them, were transgressors of the limits of Allah by committing the prohibitions of Allah and neglecting his commands.

This verse highlights the sanctity of human life. Taking one life unjustly is likened to killing all of humanity, while saving one life is like saving all of humanity. It also mentions that even with clear proofs, many transgressed.

Verse 33

إِنَّمَا جَزَاءُ الَّذِينَ يُحَارِبُونَ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ وَيَسْعَوْنَ فِي الْأَرْضِ فَسَادًا أَنْ يُقَتَّلُوا أَوْ يُصَلَّبُوا أَوْ تُقَطَّعَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَأَرْجُلُهُمْ مِنْ خِلَافٍ أَوْ يُنْفَوْا مِنَ الْأَرْضِ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ لَهُمْ خِزْيٌ فِي الدُّنْيَا ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِي الْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌ

Indeed, the penalty for those who wage war against Allah and His Messenger and spread mischief in the land is death, crucifixion, cutting off their hands and feet on opposite sides, or exile from the land. This ˹penalty˺ is a disgrace for them in this world, and they will suffer a tremendous punishment in the Hereafter.

Indeed, the penalty for those who wage war against Allah and challenge Him with enmity, and transgress His judgments and the judgments of His Messenger, and cause corruption in the land by killing people and seizing wealth, is that they should be killed, or crucified along with the killing (and crucifixion is: that the perpetrator be tied to a piece of wood), or their right hand and left foot should be cut off. If he does not repent, his left hand and right foot should be cut off, or they should be exiled to a country other than their own and imprisoned in the prison of that country until their repentance becomes evident. And this penalty which Allah has prepared for the warriors is humiliation in the world, and in the Hereafter they will have a severe torment if they do not repent.

This verse outlines the penalties for those who wage war against Allah and His Messenger and spread corruption, which includes death, crucifixion, amputation, or exile. It also mentions a severe punishment in the Hereafter.

Verse 34

إِلَّا الَّذِينَ تَابُوا مِنْ قَبْلِ أَنْ تَقْدِرُوا عَلَيْهِمْ ۖ فَاعْلَمُوا أَنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

As for those who repent before you seize them, then know that Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

But those who come from the warriors before you overcome them, and come willingly, regretting, then what was due to Allah is dropped from him. So, know - O believers - that Allah is Forgiving to His servants, Merciful to them.

This verse speaks of Allah's forgiveness and mercy. If those who waged war repent before being captured, they are forgiven by Allah.

Verse 35

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اتَّقُوا اللَّهَ وَابْتَغُوا إِلَيْهِ الْوَسِيلَةَ وَجَاهِدُوا فِي سَبِيلِهِ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ

O believers! Be mindful of Allah and seek what brings you closer to Him and struggle in His Way, so you may be successful.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, fear Allah, and draw near to Him by obeying Him and acting in accordance with what pleases Him, and strive in His cause, that you may be successful in His gardens.

This verse urges believers to be mindful of Allah, seek closeness to Him through obedience, and strive in His cause to achieve success.

Verse 36

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ أَنَّ لَهُمْ مَا فِي الْأَرْضِ جَمِيعًا وَمِثْلَهُ مَعَهُ لِيَفْتَدُوا بِهِ مِنْ عَذَابِ يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ مَا تُقُبِّلَ مِنْهُمْ ۖ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ

As for the disbelievers, even if they were to possess everything in the world twice over ˹and offer it all˺ to ransom themselves from the punishment of the Day of Judgment, it would never be accepted from them. And they will suffer a painful punishment.

Indeed, those who deny the oneness of Allah and His law, if they possessed all that is in the earth, and possessed its like with it, and wanted to ransom themselves on the Day of Resurrection from the punishment of Allah with what they possessed, Allah would not accept that from them, and for them is a painful punishment.

This verse states that disbelievers will not be able to ransom themselves from Allah’s punishment on the Day of Judgment even with all the world’s wealth. They will face a painful punishment.

Verse 37

يُرِيدُونَ أَنْ يَخْرُجُوا مِنَ النَّارِ وَمَا هُمْ بِخَارِجِينَ مِنْهَا ۖ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ مُقِيمٌ

They will be desperate to get out of the Fire but they will never be able to. And they will suffer an everlasting punishment.

These disbelievers want to get out of the Fire because of what they encounter of its horrors, but there is no way for them to do that, and they will have a lasting punishment.

This verse describes the desperation of disbelievers in Hell, who will want to escape, but they will be there forever and suffer unending punishment.

Verse 38

وَالسَّارِقُ وَالسَّارِقَةُ فَاقْطَعُوا أَيْدِيَهُمَا جَزَاءً بِمَا كَسَبَا نَكَالًا مِنَ اللَّهِ ۗ وَاللَّهُ عَزِيزٌ حَكِيمٌ

As for male and female thieves, cut off their hands for what they have done—a deterrent from Allah. And Allah is Almighty, All-Wise.

As for the thief, male and female, cut off their hands as prescribed by the law, as a recompense for what they have taken of people's wealth unjustly and as a deterrent penalty by which Allah prevents others from committing the like of their deed. And Allah is Exalted in Might, Wise in His command and prohibition.

This verse outlines the punishment for theft: cutting off the hands of both male and female thieves. It emphasizes that this is a deterrent punishment from Allah, who is Almighty and Wise.

Verse 39

فَمَنْ تَابَ مِنْ بَعْدِ ظُلْمِهِ وَأَصْلَحَ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ يَتُوبُ عَلَيْهِ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

But whoever repents after their wrongdoing and mends their ways, Allah will surely turn to them in forgiveness. Indeed, Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

But whoever repents after his wrongdoing and reforms in all his deeds, then Allah will accept his repentance. Indeed, Allah is Forgiving to His servants, Merciful to them.

This verse highlights Allah's mercy and forgiveness. Those who repent after wrongdoing and reform their behavior will be forgiven by Allah, who is All-Forgiving and Most Merciful.

Verse 40

أَلَمْ تَعْلَمْ أَنَّ اللَّهَ لَهُ مُلْكُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ يُعَذِّبُ مَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيَغْفِرُ لِمَنْ يَشَاءُ ۗ وَاللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

Do you not know that the kingdom of the heavens and the earth belongs to Allah ˹alone˺? He punishes whoever He wills and forgives whoever He wills. And Allah is Most Capable of everything.

Do you not know, [O Messenger], that to Allah belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth? He punishes whom He wills and forgives whom He wills, and Allah is over all things competent.

This verse asserts Allah's absolute dominion over the heavens and earth. He has the power to punish or forgive whomever He wills, and He is capable of all things.

Verse 41

يَا أَيُّهَا الرَّسُولُ لَا يَحْزُنْكَ الَّذِينَ يُسَارِعُونَ فِي الْكُفْرِ مِنَ الَّذِينَ قَالُوا آمَنَّا بِأَفْوَاهِهِمْ وَلَمْ تُؤْمِنْ قُلُوبُهُمْ ۛ وَمِنَ الَّذِينَ هَادُوا ۛ سَمَّاعُونَ لِلْكَذِبِ سَمَّاعُونَ لِقَوْمٍ آخَرِينَ لَمْ يَأْتُوكَ ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ الْكَلِمَ مِنْ بَعْدِ مَوَاضِعِهِ ۖ يَقُولُونَ إِنْ أُوتِيتُمْ هَٰذَا فَخُذُوهُ وَإِنْ لَمْ تُؤْتَوْهُ فَاحْذَرُوا ۚ وَمَنْ يُرِدِ اللَّهُ فِتْنَتَهُ فَلَنْ تَمْلِكَ لَهُ مِنَ اللَّهِ شَيْئًا ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ لَمْ يُرِدِ اللَّهُ أَنْ يُطَهِّرَ قُلُوبَهُمْ ۚ لَهُمْ فِي الدُّنْيَا خِزْيٌ ۖ وَلَهُمْ فِي الْآخِرَةِ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌ

O Messenger! Do not grieve for those who race to disbelieve—those who say, “We believe” with their tongues, but their hearts are in disbelief. Nor those among the Jews who eagerly listen to lies, attentive to those who are too arrogant to come to you. They distort the Scripture, taking rulings out of context, then say, “If this is the ruling you get ˹from Muḥammad˺, accept it. If not, beware!” Whoever Allah allows to be deluded, you can never be of any help to them against Allah. It is not Allah’s Will to purify their hearts. For them there is disgrace in this world, and they will suffer a tremendous punishment in the Hereafter.

O Messenger, do not be grieved by those who hasten into disbelief of your prophethood from among the hypocrites who have shown Islam while their hearts are empty of it, for I will support you against them. And do not be grieved by the Jews' hastening to deny your prophethood, for they are people who listen to lies and accept what their rabbis fabricate, and they respond to other people who do not attend your assembly. And these others alter the words of Allah after they have understood them, and they say: "If what comes to you from Muhammad agrees with what we have altered and distorted from the rulings of the Torah, then act upon it, and if what comes to you from him contradicts it, then beware of accepting it and acting upon it." And whom Allah wills to misguide, you will never be able, [O Messenger], to repel that from him, nor can you guide him. And indeed, these hypocrites and Jews, Allah did not intend to purify their hearts from the filth of disbelief, for them is humiliation and disgrace in this world, and for them in the Hereafter is a great punishment.

This verse tells the Prophet not to be saddened by those who rush to disbelief, particularly hypocrites and Jews. It highlights the deceptive nature of those who distort the scripture and warns that those who are misguided by Allah will face disgrace in this world and severe punishment in the Hereafter.

Verse 42

سَمَّاعُونَ لِلْكَذِبِ أَكَّالُونَ لِلسُّحْتِ ۚ فَإِنْ جَاءُوكَ فَاحْكُمْ بَيْنَهُمْ أَوْ أَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ ۖ وَإِنْ تُعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ فَلَنْ يَضُرُّوكَ شَيْئًا ۖ وَإِنْ حَكَمْتَ فَاحْكُمْ بَيْنَهُمْ بِالْقِسْطِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الْمُقْسِطِينَ

They eagerly listen to falsehood and consume forbidden gain. So if they come to you ˹O Prophet˺, either judge between them or turn away from them. If you turn away from them, they cannot harm you whatsoever. But if you judge between them, then do so with justice. Surely Allah loves those who are just.

These Jews gather between listening to lies and eating forbidden things. If they come to you for judgment, then judge between them, or leave them. If you do not judge between them, they will not be able to harm you in any way. But if you do judge, then judge between them with justice. Indeed, Allah loves those who are just.

This verse describes those who listen to lies and consume what is forbidden. It advises the Prophet to either judge between them with justice or turn away, emphasizing that they cannot harm him. It also highlights that Allah loves those who judge fairly.

Verse 43

وَكَيْفَ يُحَكِّمُونَكَ وَعِنْدَهُمُ التَّوْرَاةُ فِيهَا حُكْمُ اللَّهِ ثُمَّ يَتَوَلَّوْنَ مِنْ بَعْدِ ذَٰلِكَ ۚ وَمَا أُولَٰئِكَ بِالْمُؤْمِنِينَ

But why do they come to you for judgment when they ˹already˺ have the Torah containing Allah’s judgment, then they turn away after all? They are not ˹true˺ believers.

The deed of these Jews is strange. They come to you for judgment, O Messenger, while they do not believe in you, nor in your book, even though they have the Torah, in which is the judgment of Allah. Then they turn away after your judgment if it does not please them. So, they have combined disbelief in their law and turning away from your judgment. And those who are described by these qualities are not believers in Allah, in you, and in what you judge.

This verse questions why people seek judgment from the Prophet when they already have the Torah, which contains Allah's rulings. It criticizes their inconsistency and lack of true faith, as they turn away if the judgment doesn't align with their desires.

Verse 44

إِنَّا أَنْزَلْنَا التَّوْرَاةَ فِيهَا هُدًى وَنُورٌ ۚ يَحْكُمُ بِهَا النَّبِيُّونَ الَّذِينَ أَسْلَمُوا لِلَّذِينَ هَادُوا وَالرَّبَّانِيُّونَ وَالْأَحْبَارُ بِمَا اسْتُحْفِظُوا مِنْ كِتَابِ اللَّهِ وَكَانُوا عَلَيْهِ شُهَدَاءَ ۚ فَلَا تَخْشَوُا النَّاسَ وَاخْشَوْنِ وَلَا تَشْتَرُوا بِآيَاتِي ثَمَنًا قَلِيلًا ۚ وَمَنْ لَمْ يَحْكُمْ بِمَا أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْكَافِرُونَ

Indeed, We revealed the Torah, containing guidance and light, by which the prophets, who submitted themselves to Allah, made judgments for Jews. So too did the rabbis and scholars judge according to Allah’s Book, with which they were entrusted and of which they were made keepers. So do not fear the people; fear Me! Nor trade my revelations for a fleeting gain. And those who do not judge by what Allah has revealed are ˹truly˺ the disbelievers.

Indeed, We sent down the Torah, in which was guidance from error and clarification of laws. The prophets—who submitted to the judgment of Allah and acknowledged it—judged by it among the Jews, and did not deviate from its judgment nor distort it. And the worshipers of the Jews and their scholars who educated people according to the law of Allah also judged by it. That is because their prophets entrusted them with conveying the Torah and understanding the book of Allah and acting by it, and the rabbis and scholars were witnesses that their prophets had judged the Jews by the book of Allah. And Allah says to the scholars and rabbis of the Jews: 'Do not fear the people in carrying out My judgment, for they are not capable of benefiting or harming you, but fear Me, for I am the Benefiter and the Harmer. And do not take in exchange for the judgment of what I have sent down a paltry price. Judging by other than what Allah has revealed is among the deeds of the people of disbelief. So, those who change the judgment of Allah which He revealed in His Book, and conceal it, deny it, and judge by other than it, believing it to be lawful and permissible, then those are the disbelievers.'

This verse explains that Allah revealed the Torah as a source of guidance and light, through which prophets and scholars judged with Allah's laws. It warns against fearing people rather than Allah, and against trading divine revelations for worldly gain. It also states that those who do not judge by Allah's revelations are disbelievers.

Verse 45

وَكَتَبْنَا عَلَيْهِمْ فِيهَا أَنَّ النَّفْسَ بِالنَّفْسِ وَالْعَيْنَ بِالْعَيْنِ وَالْأَنْفَ بِالْأَنْفِ وَالْأُذُنَ بِالْأُذُنِ وَالسِّنَّ بِالسِّنِّ وَالْجُرُوحَ قِصَاصٌ ۚ فَمَنْ تَصَدَّقَ بِهِ فَهُوَ كَفَّارَةٌ لَهُ ۚ وَمَنْ لَمْ يَحْكُمْ بِمَا أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الظَّالِمُونَ

We ordained for them in the Torah, “A life for a life, an eye for an eye, a nose for a nose, an ear for an ear, a tooth for a tooth—and for wounds equal retaliation.” But whoever waives it charitably, it will be atonement for them. And those who do not judge by what Allah has revealed are ˹truly˺ the wrongdoers.

And We ordained for them in the Torah that a life is for a life, an eye for an eye, a nose for a nose, an ear for an ear, a tooth for a tooth, and that there is retaliation in wounds. But whoever forgoes his right to retaliation from the aggressor, that is an expiation for some of the sins of the wronged one and an elimination of them. And whoever does not judge by what Allah has revealed in retaliation and other matters, then those are the transgressors of the limits of Allah.

This verse outlines the law of retaliation in the Torah: a life for a life, an eye for an eye, etc. It states that forgiving the aggressor is an act of charity that can be an expiation for the wronged person's sins. It emphasizes that those who do not judge by what Allah has revealed are wrongdoers.

Verse 46

وَقَفَّيْنَا عَلَىٰ آثَارِهِمْ بِعِيسَى ابْنِ مَرْيَمَ مُصَدِّقًا لِمَا بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ مِنَ التَّوْرَاةِ ۖ وَآتَيْنَاهُ الْإِنْجِيلَ فِيهِ هُدًى وَنُورٌ وَمُصَدِّقًا لِمَا بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ مِنَ التَّوْرَاةِ وَهُدًى وَمَوْعِظَةً لِلْمُتَّقِينَ

Then in the footsteps of the prophets, We sent Jesus, son of Mary, confirming the Torah revealed before him. And We gave him the Gospel containing guidance and light and confirming what was revealed in the Torah—a guide and a lesson to the God-fearing.

And We sent, following in their footsteps, Jesus, the son of Mary, confirming that which came before him in the Torah; and We gave him the Gospel, in which was guidance and light and confirming that which preceded it of the Torah as a guidance and instruction for those conscious of Allah. And We made it a clarification for those who fear Allah and a deterrent for them from committing forbidden things.

Following the prophets, Allah sent Jesus, son of Mary, with the Gospel, confirming the Torah. The Gospel contained guidance, light, and lessons for the God-fearing.

Verse 47

وَلْيَحْكُمْ أَهْلُ الْإِنْجِيلِ بِمَا أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ فِيهِ ۚ وَمَنْ لَمْ يَحْكُمْ بِمَا أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْفَاسِقُونَ

So let the people of the Gospel judge by what Allah has revealed in it. And those who do not judge by what Allah has revealed are ˹truly˺ the rebellious.

And let the People of the Gospel judge by what Allah has revealed therein. And whoever does not judge by what Allah has revealed - then it is those who are the defiantly disobedient.

The people of the Gospel were commanded to judge by its teachings. Those who reject Allah's revelations are considered rebellious.

Verse 48

وَأَنْزَلْنَا إِلَيْكَ الْكِتَابَ بِالْحَقِّ مُصَدِّقًا لِمَا بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ مِنَ الْكِتَابِ وَمُهَيْمِنًا عَلَيْهِ ۖ فَاحْكُمْ بَيْنَهُمْ بِمَا أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ ۖ وَلَا تَتَّبِعْ أَهْوَاءَهُمْ عَمَّا جَاءَكَ مِنَ الْحَقِّ ۚ لِكُلٍّ جَعَلْنَا مِنْكُمْ شِرْعَةً وَمِنْهَاجًا ۚ وَلَوْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ لَجَعَلَكُمْ أُمَّةً وَاحِدَةً وَلَٰكِنْ لِيَبْلُوَكُمْ فِي مَا آتَاكُمْ ۖ فَاسْتَبِقُوا الْخَيْرَاتِ ۚ إِلَى اللَّهِ مَرْجِعُكُمْ جَمِيعًا فَيُنَبِّئُكُمْ بِمَا كُنْتُمْ فِيهِ تَخْتَلِفُونَ

We have revealed to you ˹O Prophet˺ this Book with the truth, as a confirmation of previous Scriptures and a supreme authority on them.
