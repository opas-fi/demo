Sura 019

Verse 1

كهيعص

Kãf-Ha-Ya-’Aĩn- Ṣãd.

(Kaf Ha Ya 'Ain Sad) The discussion on the disjointed letters at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah has preceded.

This verse begins with disjointed letters, the meaning of which is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

ذِكْرُ رَحْمَتِ رَبِّكَ عَبْدَهُ زَكَرِيَّا

˹This is˺ a reminder of your Lord’s mercy to His servant Zachariah,

This is a mention of the mercy of your Lord to His servant Zechariah. We will recount it to you, for in that is a lesson for those who consider.

This verse introduces the story of Prophet Zechariah and highlights Allah's mercy towards His servant.

Verse 3

إِذْ نَادَىٰ رَبَّهُ نِدَاءً خَفِيًّا

when he cried out to his Lord privately,

When he called to his Lord in secret, so that it would be more complete and perfect in sincerity to Allah, and more hopeful for a response.

Prophet Zechariah prayed to Allah in secret, showing his sincerity and hope for his prayer to be answered.

Verse 4

قَالَ رَبِّ إِنِّي وَهَنَ الْعَظْمُ مِنِّي وَاشْتَعَلَ الرَّأْسُ شَيْبًا وَلَمْ أَكُنْ بِدُعَائِكَ رَبِّ شَقِيًّا

saying, “My Lord! Surely my bones have become brittle, and grey hair has spread across my head, but I have never been disappointed in my prayer to You, my Lord!

He said, "My Lord, indeed my bones have weakened, and my head has filled with white hair, and I have never been deprived in the past of having my prayer answered."

Prophet Zechariah expresses his old age and physical weakness but recalls that his prayers had always been answered by Allah.

Verse 5

وَإِنِّي خِفْتُ الْمَوَالِيَ مِنْ وَرَائِي وَكَانَتِ امْرَأَتِي عَاقِرًا فَهَبْ لِي مِنْ لَدُنْكَ وَلِيًّا

And I am concerned about ˹the faith of˺ my relatives after me, since my wife is barren. So grant me, by Your grace, an heir,

And I fear my relatives and my clan after my death, that they will not uphold your religion as it should be upheld, nor will they call your servants to you, and my wife has been barren, unable to bear children, so grant me from you a son, an heir and a helper.

Prophet Zechariah expresses his concern for his family after him and asks Allah to grant him an heir because his wife is barren.

Verse 6

يَرِثُنِي وَيَرِثُ مِنْ آلِ يَعْقُوبَ ۖ وَاجْعَلْهُ رَبِّ رَضِيًّا

who will inherit ˹prophethood˺ from me and the family of Jacob, and make him, O Lord, pleasing ˹to You˺!”

Who will inherit my prophethood and the prophethood of the family of Jacob. And make this son pleasing to you and your servants.

Prophet Zechariah prays that his heir will inherit his prophethood and be pleasing to Allah.

Verse 7

يَا زَكَرِيَّا إِنَّا نُبَشِّرُكَ بِغُلَامٍ اسْمُهُ يَحْيَىٰ لَمْ نَجْعَلْ لَهُ مِنْ قَبْلُ سَمِيًّا

˹The angels announced,˺ “O Zachariah! Indeed, We give you the good news of ˹the birth of˺ a son, whose name will be John—a name We have not given to anyone before.”

O Zechariah, We give you glad tidings of the answer to your prayer. We have granted you a boy whose name will be John. We have never named anyone before him with this name.

The angels give Prophet Zechariah the good news of a son named John, a name not used before.

Verse 8

قَالَ رَبِّ أَنَّىٰ يَكُونُ لِي غُلَامٌ وَكَانَتِ امْرَأَتِي عَاقِرًا وَقَدْ بَلَغْتُ مِنَ الْكِبَرِ عِتِيًّا

He wondered, “My Lord! How can I have a son when my wife is barren, and I have become extremely old?”

Zechariah said in astonishment: My Lord, how can I have a son when my wife has been barren, unable to bear children, and I have reached the end of old age and the weakness of bones?

Prophet Zechariah wonders how he can have a son given his old age and his wife's barrenness.

Verse 9

قَالَ كَذَٰلِكَ قَالَ رَبُّكَ هُوَ عَلَيَّ هَيِّنٌ وَقَدْ خَلَقْتُكَ مِنْ قَبْلُ وَلَمْ تَكُ شَيْئًا

An angel replied, “So will it be! Your Lord says, ‘It is easy for Me, just as I created you before, when you were nothing!’”

The angel said, answering Zechariah about what he was astonished at: This is how the matter is, as you say about your wife being barren, and you reaching extreme old age, but your Lord said: The creation of John in this manner is an easy matter for Me. Then Allah mentioned to Zechariah what is more astonishing than what he asked about, and said: And I created you before John, and you were nothing mentioned or existing.

Allah responds that creating a son is easy for Him, just as He created Zechariah when he was nothing.

Verse 10

قَالَ رَبِّ اجْعَلْ لِي آيَةً ۚ قَالَ آيَتُكَ أَلَّا تُكَلِّمَ النَّاسَ ثَلَاثَ لَيَالٍ سَوِيًّا

Zachariah said, “My Lord! Grant me a sign.” He responded, “Your sign is that you will not ˹be able to˺ speak to people for three nights, despite being healthy.”

Zechariah said, increasing his reassurance: My Lord, make for me a sign of the fulfillment of what the angels have given me good tidings of. He said: Your sign is that you will not be able to speak to people for three nights and days, while you are healthy and well.

Prophet Zechariah asks for a sign and is told that he will be unable to speak for three nights despite being healthy.

Verse 11

فَخَرَجَ عَلَىٰ قَوْمِهِ مِنَ الْمِحْرَابِ فَأَوْحَىٰ إِلَيْهِمْ أَنْ سَبِّحُوا بُكْرَةً وَعَشِيًّا

So he came out to his people from the sanctuary, signalling to them to glorify ˹Allah˺ morning and evening.

So Zechariah went out to his people from his prayer place, which is the place where he was given the glad tidings of the son. Then he gestured to them: to glorify Allah morning and evening, in gratitude to Him, Most High.

Prophet Zechariah signals to his people to glorify Allah morning and evening.

Verse 12

يَا يَحْيَىٰ خُذِ الْكِتَابَ بِقُوَّةٍ ۖ وَآتَيْنَاهُ الْحُكْمَ صَبِيًّا

˹It was later said,˺ “O John! Hold firmly to the Scriptures.” And We granted him wisdom while ˹he was still˺ a child,

So when John was born and reached an age where he could understand speech, Allah commanded him to take the Torah with seriousness and diligence, saying: 'O John, take the Torah with seriousness and diligence,' by memorizing its words, understanding its meanings, and acting upon it. And We gave him wisdom and good understanding, while he was young in age.

Prophet John (Yahya) was commanded to hold firmly to the Scriptures and was granted wisdom from a young age.

Verse 13

وَحَنَانًا مِنْ لَدُنَّا وَزَكَاةً ۖ وَكَانَ تَقِيًّا

as well as purity and compassion from Us. And he was God-fearing,

And We granted him mercy and love from Us, and purity from sins. And he was fearful, obedient to Allah the Most High, fulfilling His obligations, and avoiding His prohibitions.

John was blessed with purity, compassion, and fear of God, fulfilling his religious obligations.

Verse 14

وَبَرًّا بِوَالِدَيْهِ وَلَمْ يَكُنْ جَبَّارًا عَصِيًّا

and kind to his parents. He was neither arrogant nor disobedient.

And he was dutiful to his parents, obedient to them, and was not arrogant to obey his Lord, nor to obey his parents, and was not disobedient to his Lord or to his parents.

John was kind and dutiful to his parents and was neither arrogant nor disobedient to God or his parents.

Verse 15

وَسَلَامٌ عَلَيْهِ يَوْمَ وُلِدَ وَيَوْمَ يَمُوتُ وَيَوْمَ يُبْعَثُ حَيًّا

Peace be upon him the day he was born, and the day of his death, and the day he will be raised back to life!

And peace from Allah be upon John, and security for him the day he was born, and the day he dies, and the day he is raised alive from his grave.

Peace was granted to John on the day he was born, the day he dies, and the day he will be resurrected.

Verse 16

وَاذْكُرْ فِي الْكِتَابِ مَرْيَمَ إِذِ انْتَبَذَتْ مِنْ أَهْلِهَا مَكَانًا شَرْقِيًّا

And mention in the Book ˹O Prophet, the story of˺ Mary when she withdrew from her family to a place in the east,

And mention - O Messenger - in this Quran the story of Mary when she withdrew from her family, and took for herself a place toward the east from them.

The story of Mary is mentioned in the Quran, highlighting when she withdrew from her family to a place in the east.

Verse 17

فَاتَّخَذَتْ مِنْ دُونِهِمْ حِجَابًا فَأَرْسَلْنَا إِلَيْهَا رُوحَنَا فَتَمَثَّلَ لَهَا بَشَرًا سَوِيًّا

screening herself off from them. Then We sent to her Our angel, ˹Gabriel,˺ appearing before her as a man, perfectly formed.

And she placed a screen between her and her family, to conceal her from them and from the people, so We sent to her the angel Gabriel, who appeared to her in the form of a perfect human being.

Mary secluded herself, and God sent angel Gabriel in the form of a man to her.

Verse 18

قَالَتْ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِالرَّحْمَٰنِ مِنْكَ إِنْ كُنْتَ تَقِيًّا

She appealed, “I truly seek refuge in the Most Compassionate from you! ˹So leave me alone˺ if you are God-fearing.”

Mary said to him: 'Indeed, I seek refuge in the Most Gracious from you, lest you harm me, if you are one who fears Allah.'

Mary sought refuge in God from the angel, thinking he might harm her, if he was God-fearing.

Verse 19

قَالَ إِنَّمَا أَنَا رَسُولُ رَبِّكِ لِأَهَبَ لَكِ غُلَامًا زَكِيًّا

He responded, “I am only a messenger from your Lord, ˹sent˺ to bless you with a pure son.”

The angel said to her: 'I am only a messenger of your Lord, sent to you to bestow upon you a pure son, free from sin.'

The angel revealed his identity as a messenger sent to bless her with a pure son.

Verse 20

قَالَتْ أَنَّىٰ يَكُونُ لِي غُلَامٌ وَلَمْ يَمْسَسْنِي بَشَرٌ وَلَمْ أَكُ بَغِيًّا

She wondered, “How can I have a son when no man has ever touched me, nor am I unchaste?”

Mary said to the angel: 'How can I have a son, when no man has touched me in lawful marriage, and I am not unchaste?'

Mary questioned how she could have a son, as she had not been touched by any man nor was unchaste.

Verse 21

قَالَ كَذَٰلِكِ قَالَ رَبُّكِ هُوَ عَلَيَّ هَيِّنٌ ۖ وَلِنَجْعَلَهُ آيَةً لِلنَّاسِ وَرَحْمَةً مِنَّا ۚ وَكَانَ أَمْرًا مَقْضِيًّا

He replied, “So will it be! Your Lord says, ‘It is easy for Me. And so will We make him a sign for humanity and a mercy from Us.’ It is a matter ˹already˺ decreed.”

The angel said to her: 'Thus is the matter, as you describe, that no man has touched you, and that you have not been unchaste. But your Lord said, “It is easy for Me. ” And that this boy shall be a sign for the people, demonstrating Allah the Most High's power, and a mercy from Us to him and to his mother and to the people. And the existence of Jesus in this condition was a pre-ordained decree, written in the Preserved Tablet, so it must come to pass.'

The angel responded that it was God's will, and the birth of her son would be a sign for humanity and a mercy from God.

Verse 22

فَحَمَلَتْهُ فَانْتَبَذَتْ بِهِ مَكَانًا قَصِيًّا

So she conceived him and withdrew with him to a remote place.

So Mary conceived the boy after Gabriel breathed into the opening of her garment, and the breath reached her womb, and thus the pregnancy occurred because of it. Then she withdrew with him to a distant place, away from people.

Mary conceived Jesus and withdrew to a remote place during her pregnancy.

Verse 23

فَأَجَاءَهَا الْمَخَاضُ إِلَىٰ جِذْعِ النَّخْلَةِ قَالَتْ يَا لَيْتَنِي مِتُّ قَبْلَ هَٰذَا وَكُنْتُ نَسْيًا مَنْسِيًّا

Then the pains of labour drove her to the trunk of a palm tree. She cried, “Alas! I wish I had died before this, and was a thing long forgotten!”

Then the labor pains drove her to the trunk of a palm tree. She said, 'Oh, I wish I had died before this day, and had been something not known, not mentioned, and not recognized who I was?'

The pain of childbirth led Mary to wish she had died before this and been forgotten.

Verse 24

فَنَادَاهَا مِنْ تَحْتِهَا أَلَّا تَحْزَنِي قَدْ جَعَلَ رَبُّكِ تَحْتَكِ سَرِيًّا

So a voice reassured her from below her, “Do not grieve! Your Lord has provided a stream at your feet.

So Gabriel or Jesus called to her: 'Do not grieve. Your Lord has placed a stream of water beneath you.'

A voice reassured Mary not to grieve, and informed her that God had provided a stream for her.

Verse 25

وَهُزِّي إِلَيْكِ بِجِذْعِ النَّخْلَةِ تُسَاقِطْ عَلَيْكِ رُطَبًا جَنِيًّا

And shake the trunk of this palm tree towards you, it will drop fresh, ripe dates upon you.

And shake the trunk of the palm tree; it will drop upon you fresh, ripe dates, picked at their time.

Mary was instructed to shake the palm tree, which would drop fresh, ripe dates for her to eat.

Verse 26

فَكُلِي وَاشْرَبِي وَقَرِّي عَيْنًا ۖ فَإِمَّا تَرَيِنَّ مِنَ الْبَشَرِ أَحَدًا فَقُولِي إِنِّي نَذَرْتُ لِلرَّحْمَٰنِ صَوْمًا فَلَنْ أُكَلِّمَ الْيَوْمَ إِنْسِيًّا

So eat and drink, and put your heart at ease. But if you see any of the people, say, ‘I have vowed silence to the Most Compassionate, so I am not talking to anyone today.’”

So eat from the ripe dates, drink from the water, and be consoled by the newborn. And if you see anyone from the people and they ask you about your matter, then say to him: 'I have vowed to Allah silence, so I will not speak to anyone today.' And silence was an act of worship in their law, not in the law of Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him.

This verse instructs Mary to eat, drink, and be comforted after giving birth. If questioned, she is to indicate she has vowed silence to God, a form of worship at that time, and therefore will not speak.

Verse 27

فَأَتَتْ بِهِ قَوْمَهَا تَحْمِلُهُ ۖ قَالُوا يَا مَرْيَمُ لَقَدْ جِئْتِ شَيْئًا فَرِيًّا

Then she returned to her people, carrying him. They said ˹in shock˺, “O Mary! You have certainly done a horrible thing!

Then Mary came to her people, carrying her baby from a distant place. When they saw her like that, they said to her: 'O Mary, you have certainly brought a tremendous thing, a fabricated one.'

Mary returns to her people carrying the baby Jesus. Her people are shocked and accuse her of committing a grave wrong because she has a baby without being married.

Verse 28

يَا أُخْتَ هَارُونَ مَا كَانَ أَبُوكِ امْرَأَ سَوْءٍ وَمَا كَانَتْ أُمُّكِ بَغِيًّا

O sister of Aaron! Your father was not an indecent man, nor was your mother unchaste.”

O sister of the righteous man Harun, your father was not a man of evil, committing immoralities, nor was your mother a wicked woman, committing fornication.

Mary's people question her lineage by referencing the name of a righteous man *Harun*. They say that her parents were not of immoral character, implying her pregnancy is unusual and uncharacteristic of her family.

Verse 29

فَأَشَارَتْ إِلَيْهِ ۖ قَالُوا كَيْفَ نُكَلِّمُ مَنْ كَانَ فِي الْمَهْدِ صَبِيًّا

So she pointed to the baby. They exclaimed, “How can we talk to someone who is an infant in the cradle?”

So Mary pointed to her baby Jesus for them to ask him and speak to him. They said, denying her: 'How can we speak to one who is still a baby in the cradle?'

Mary points to baby Jesus when asked about the matter. Her people are confused and say, how can we ask and speak to an infant?

Verse 30

قَالَ إِنِّي عَبْدُ اللَّهِ آتَانِيَ الْكِتَابَ وَجَعَلَنِي نَبِيًّا

Jesus declared, “I am truly a servant of Allah. He has destined me to be given the Scripture and to be a prophet.

Jesus said, while in his cradle suckling: 'Indeed, I am a servant of Allah. He has decreed to grant me the Scripture, which is the Gospel, and has made me a prophet.'

Jesus, as an infant, declares that he is a servant of God, destined to receive scripture and be a prophet.

Verse 31

وَجَعَلَنِي مُبَارَكًا أَيْنَ مَا كُنْتُ وَأَوْصَانِي بِالصَّلَاةِ وَالزَّكَاةِ مَا دُمْتُ حَيًّا

He has made me a blessing wherever I go, and bid me to establish prayer and give alms-tax as long as I live,

And He has made me blessed wherever I am and has enjoined upon me prayer and Zakat as long as I live.

Jesus declares he has been blessed wherever he is and has been commanded by God to establish prayer and give Zakat as long as he lives.

Verse 32

وَبَرًّا بِوَالِدَتِي وَلَمْ يَجْعَلْنِي جَبَّارًا شَقِيًّا

and to be kind to my mother. He has not made me arrogant or defiant.

And He has made me dutiful to my mother, and He has not made me arrogant or wretched, disobedient to my Lord.

Jesus further states that God has made him dutiful to his mother and not arrogant or disobedient.

Verse 33

وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَيَّ يَوْمَ وُلِدْتُ وَيَوْمَ أَمُوتُ وَيَوْمَ أُبْعَثُ حَيًّا

Peace be upon me the day I was born, the day I die, and the day I will be raised back to life!”

And peace be upon me from Allah the day I was born, and the day I die, and the day I am raised alive on the Day of Resurrection.

Jesus proclaims peace upon himself from the day of his birth, the day of his death, and the day of his resurrection.

Verse 34

ذَٰلِكَ عِيسَى ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ ۚ قَوْلَ الْحَقِّ الَّذِي فِيهِ يَمْتَرُونَ

That is Jesus, son of Mary. ˹And this is˺ a word of truth, about which they dispute.

That is what we have narrated to you - O Messenger - his description and his story. It is Jesus, the son of Mary, without doubt or ambiguity, but rather it is the truth about which the Jews and Christians doubt.

This verse confirms that the story of Jesus, son of Mary, is the truth. It also clarifies that is a subject of dispute between different groups.

Verse 35

مَا كَانَ لِلَّهِ أَنْ يَتَّخِذَ مِنْ وَلَدٍ ۖ سُبْحَانَهُ ۚ إِذَا قَضَىٰ أَمْرًا فَإِنَّمَا يَقُولُ لَهُ كُنْ فَيَكُونُ

It is not for Allah to take a son! Glory be to Him. When He decrees a matter, He simply tells it, “Be!” And it is!

It is not befitting for Allah, the Most High, to take a son from among His servants and creation. He is exalted and sanctified above that. When He decrees a matter of affairs and intends it, small or big, it is not impossible for Him. He only says to it, 'Be,' and it is as He wills and intends.

It is emphasized that it is not befitting for God to have a son, and when God decrees a matter, it comes into being simply by His command.

Verse 36

وَإِنَّ اللَّهَ رَبِّي وَرَبُّكُمْ فَاعْبُدُوهُ ۚ هَٰذَا صِرَاطٌ مُسْتَقِيمٌ

˹Jesus also declared,˺ “Surely Allah is my Lord and your Lord, so worship Him ˹alone˺. This is the Straight Path.”

And Jesus said to his people: 'Indeed, Allah, to whom I call you, is my Lord and your Lord, so worship Him alone without associating any partners with Him. So I and you are equal in servitude and submission to Him. This is the path with no deviation in it.'

Jesus also declared that God is his Lord and the Lord of all, and thus all should worship God alone. This is the straight path.

Verse 37

فَاخْتَلَفَ الْأَحْزَابُ مِنْ بَيْنِهِمْ ۖ فَوَيْلٌ لِلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا مِنْ مَشْهَدِ يَوْمٍ عَظِيمٍ

Yet their ˹various˺ groups have differed among themselves ˹about him˺, so woe to the disbelievers when they face a tremendous Day!

The sects from the People of the Book differed among themselves about the matter of Jesus, peace be upon him. Some of them exaggerated about him, and they are the Christians; some of them said: 'He is Allah,' and some of them said: 'He is the son of Allah,' and some of them said: 'The third of three'- Exalted is Allah above what they say - and some of them were indifferent to him, and they are the Jews. They said: 'He is a sorcerer,' and they said: 'He is the son of Joseph the carpenter.' So, destruction is for those who disbelieved from witnessing a day of great terror, which is the Day of Resurrection.

Different groups have varied and differed in their beliefs about Jesus. Those who disbelieve face severe punishment on a tremendous Day.

Verse 38

أَسْمِعْ بِهِمْ وَأَبْصِرْ يَوْمَ يَأْتُونَنَا ۖ لَٰكِنِ الظَّالِمُونَ الْيَوْمَ فِي ضَلَالٍ مُبِينٍ

How clearly will they hear and see on the Day they will come to Us! But today the wrongdoers are clearly astray.

How keen will be their hearing and their seeing on the Day of Resurrection, the day they will appear before Allah, when that will not benefit them! But the wrongdoers today in this world are in clear error away from the truth.

On the Day of Resurrection, the disbelievers will clearly hear and see, but it will not benefit them then. Today, they are clearly in the wrong and astray.

Verse 39

وَأَنْذِرْهُمْ يَوْمَ الْحَسْرَةِ إِذْ قُضِيَ الْأَمْرُ وَهُمْ فِي غَفْلَةٍ وَهُمْ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ

And warn them ˹O Prophet˺ of the Day of Regret, when all matters will be settled, while they are ˹engrossed˺ in heedlessness and disbelief.

And warn - O Messenger - the people of the Day of Regret when the matter is decided, and death is brought as if it were a speckled ram, and it is slaughtered, and judgment is passed between the creation, so the people of faith will go to Paradise, and the people of disbelief to Hell, and they are today in this world heedless of what they were warned about, so they do not believe, nor do they do good deeds.

This verse warns about the Day of Regret when all will be judged. It emphasizes that people are currently heedless of this impending reality, engaging in disbelief and not doing good deeds.
