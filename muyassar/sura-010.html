Sura 010

Verse 1

الر ۚ تِلْكَ آيَاتُ الْكِتَابِ الْحَكِيمِ

Alif-Lãm-Ra. These are the verses of the Book, rich in wisdom.

(Alif, Lam, Ra). The discussion about the disjointed letters at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah has preceded.

This verse begins with the letters *Alif-Lam-Ra*, which are among the disjointed letters that appear at the start of some surahs. This verse asserts that the Quran is a book full of wisdom.

Verse 2

أَكَانَ لِلنَّاسِ عَجَبًا أَنْ أَوْحَيْنَا إِلَىٰ رَجُلٍ مِنْهُمْ أَنْ أَنْذِرِ النَّاسَ وَبَشِّرِ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا أَنَّ لَهُمْ قَدَمَ صِدْقٍ عِنْدَ رَبِّهِمْ ۗ قَالَ الْكَافِرُونَ إِنَّ هَٰذَا لَسَاحِرٌ مُبِينٌ

Is it astonishing to people that We have sent revelation to a man from among themselves, ˹instructing him,˺ “Warn humanity and give good news to the believers that they will have an honourable status with their Lord.”? Yet the disbelievers said, “Indeed, this ˹man˺ is clearly a magician!”

Was it a wonder to the people that We revealed the Quran to a man from among them, to warn them of the punishment of Allah, and to give glad tidings to those who believe in Allah and His messengers that they will have a good reward for what they have put forth of righteous deeds? But when the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, came to them with the revelation of Allah and recited it to them, those who denied said: Indeed, Muhammad is a magician, and what he has brought is obvious magic that is invalid.

This verse highlights that Allah sent a messenger to warn people and give glad tidings to believers. The disbelievers, however, called the messenger a magician.

Verse 3

إِنَّ رَبَّكُمُ اللَّهُ الَّذِي خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ فِي سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍ ثُمَّ اسْتَوَىٰ عَلَى الْعَرْشِ ۖ يُدَبِّرُ الْأَمْرَ ۖ مَا مِنْ شَفِيعٍ إِلَّا مِنْ بَعْدِ إِذْنِهِ ۚ ذَٰلِكُمُ اللَّهُ رَبُّكُمْ فَاعْبُدُوهُ ۚ أَفَلَا تَذَكَّرُونَ

Surely your Lord is Allah Who created the heavens and the earth in six Days, then established Himself on the Throne, conducting every affair. None can intercede except by His permission. That is Allah—your Lord, so worship Him ˹alone˺. Will you not then be mindful?

Indeed, your Lord is Allah, who created the heavens and the earth in six days, then He ascended - meaning He rose and elevated - over the Throne in a manner befitting His majesty and greatness. He manages the affairs of His creation; no one opposes Him in His decree, and no intercessor can intercede with Him on the Day of Resurrection except after He permits them to intercede. So worship Allah, your Lord, who is described with these attributes, and dedicate your worship to Him alone. Will you not then take heed and learn from these signs and proofs?

Allah created the heavens and the earth in six days and then established Himself on the Throne. He manages all affairs and only He permits intercession. This verse emphasizes Allah's Oneness and calls for worship.

Verse 4

إِلَيْهِ مَرْجِعُكُمْ جَمِيعًا ۖ وَعْدَ اللَّهِ حَقًّا ۚ إِنَّهُ يَبْدَأُ الْخَلْقَ ثُمَّ يُعِيدُهُ لِيَجْزِيَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ بِالْقِسْطِ ۚ وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَهُمْ شَرَابٌ مِنْ حَمِيمٍ وَعَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ بِمَا كَانُوا يَكْفُرُونَ

To Him is your return all together. Allah’s promise is ˹always˺ true. Indeed, He originates the creation then resurrects it so that He may justly reward those who believe and do good. But those who disbelieve will have a boiling drink and a painful punishment for their disbelief.

To your Lord is your return, all of you, on the Day of Resurrection, and this is the true promise of Allah. He is the One who begins the creation and then brings it back after death, bringing it to life as it was initially, to reward those who believed Allah and His Messenger, and did good deeds with the best of rewards in justice. And those who denied the oneness of Allah and the message of His Messenger will have a drink of intensely hot water that scalds faces and cuts intestines, and they will have a painful punishment because of their disbelief and misguidance.

All will return to Allah on the Day of Resurrection. He originates creation and will resurrect it. Believers will be justly rewarded while disbelievers will face severe punishment.

Verse 5

هُوَ الَّذِي جَعَلَ الشَّمْسَ ضِيَاءً وَالْقَمَرَ نُورًا وَقَدَّرَهُ مَنَازِلَ لِتَعْلَمُوا عَدَدَ السِّنِينَ وَالْحِسَابَ ۚ مَا خَلَقَ اللَّهُ ذَٰلِكَ إِلَّا بِالْحَقِّ ۚ يُفَصِّلُ الْآيَاتِ لِقَوْمٍ يَعْلَمُونَ

He is the One Who made the sun a radiant source and the moon a reflected light, with precisely ordained phases, so that you may know the number of years and calculation ˹of time˺. Allah did not create all this except for a purpose. He makes the signs clear for people of knowledge.

Allah is the One who made the sun a radiant light, and made the moon a [reflected] light, and ordained for the moon phases, so you know the days by the sun, and the months and years by the moon. Allah did not create the sun and the moon except with great wisdom, and as a sign of the perfection of Allah's power and knowledge. He clarifies the proofs and evidence for a people who know the wisdom in the creation of all things.

Allah made the sun a source of light and the moon a reflected light with ordained phases. This verse highlights Allah's wisdom in creation and provides signs for those who seek knowledge.

Verse 6

إِنَّ فِي اخْتِلَافِ اللَّيْلِ وَالنَّهَارِ وَمَا خَلَقَ اللَّهُ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ لَآيَاتٍ لِقَوْمٍ يَتَّقُونَ

Surely in the alternation of the day and the night, and in all that Allah has created in the heavens and the earth, there are truly signs for those mindful ˹of Him˺.

Indeed, in the alternation of night and day, and what Allah has created in the heavens and the earth of the wonders of creation, and what is in them of creativity and order, are clear signs and proofs for a people who fear the punishment of Allah, His anger, and His torment.

The alternation of day and night and all that Allah has created in the heavens and earth are clear signs for those who are mindful of Him.

Verse 7

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ لَا يَرْجُونَ لِقَاءَنَا وَرَضُوا بِالْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَاطْمَأَنُّوا بِهَا وَالَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنْ آيَاتِنَا غَافِلُونَ

Indeed, those who do not expect to meet Us, being pleased and content with this worldly life, and who are heedless of Our signs,

Indeed, those who do not expect to meet Us in the Hereafter for reckoning, and what follows of recompense for deeds because of their denial of resurrection, and are satisfied with the worldly life instead of the Hereafter, and are content with it, and those who are heedless of Our cosmic and legislative signs.

This verse describes those who are too focused on worldly life and do not believe in the Hereafter. They are heedless of Allah's signs.

Verse 8

أُولَٰئِكَ مَأْوَاهُمُ النَّارُ بِمَا كَانُوا يَكْسِبُونَ

they will have the Fire as a home because of what they have committed.

Those, their abode will be the Fire of Hell in the Hereafter; a recompense for what they used to earn in their world of sins and wrongdoings.

As a consequence of their disbelief and misdeeds, their final abode will be the Hellfire.

Verse 9

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ يَهْدِيهِمْ رَبُّهُمْ بِإِيمَانِهِمْ ۖ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهِمُ الْأَنْهَارُ فِي جَنَّاتِ النَّعِيمِ

Surely those who believe and do good, their Lord will guide them ˹to Paradise˺ through their faith, rivers will flow under their feet in the Gardens of Bliss,

Indeed, those who believe in Allah and His Messenger and do righteous deeds, their Lord will guide them to the path of Paradise and enable them to do the work that leads to it, because of their faith, then He will reward them with entrance to Paradise and bestowing His pleasure upon them, rivers flow beneath them in the Gardens of Bliss.

Those who believe and do good will be guided by Allah to Paradise. They will be blessed with rivers flowing in the Gardens of Bliss.

Verse 10

دَعْوَاهُمْ فِيهَا سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ وَتَحِيَّتُهُمْ فِيهَا سَلَامٌ ۚ وَآخِرُ دَعْوَاهُمْ أَنِ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

in which their prayer will be, “Glory be to You, O Allah!” and their greeting will be, “Peace!” and their closing prayer will be, “All praise is for Allah—Lord of all worlds!”

Their call in Paradise will be glorification (Glory be to You, O Allah), and the greeting of Allah and His angels to them, and the greeting of one another in Paradise will be (peace), and their last call will be: "Praise be to Allah, Lord of the worlds," that is: the thanks and praise to Allah, the Creator of the creatures and their Sustainer with His blessings.

In Paradise, their prayer will be glorifying Allah, their greeting will be 'Peace,' and their closing prayer will be praising Allah, the Lord of all worlds.

Verse 11

وَلَوْ يُعَجِّلُ اللَّهُ لِلنَّاسِ الشَّرَّ اسْتِعْجَالَهُمْ بِالْخَيْرِ لَقُضِيَ إِلَيْهِمْ أَجَلُهُمْ ۖ فَنَذَرُ الَّذِينَ لَا يَرْجُونَ لِقَاءَنَا فِي طُغْيَانِهِمْ يَعْمَهُونَ

If Allah were to hasten evil for people as they wish to hasten good, they would have certainly been doomed. But We leave those who do not expect to meet Us to wander blindly in their defiance.

And if Allah were to hasten for people the response to their supplication for evil as He hastens for them the response in good, they would be destroyed. So We leave those who do not fear Our punishment and do not believe in the resurrection and the raising up, in their rebellion and transgression, wandering in confusion.

Allah does not hasten evil for people as they impatiently seek good. Those who deny the Hereafter are left to wander in their defiance.

Verse 12

وَإِذَا مَسَّ الْإِنْسَانَ الضُّرُّ دَعَانَا لِجَنْبِهِ أَوْ قَاعِدًا أَوْ قَائِمًا فَلَمَّا كَشَفْنَا عَنْهُ ضُرَّهُ مَرَّ كَأَنْ لَمْ يَدْعُنَا إِلَىٰ ضُرٍّ مَسَّهُ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ زُيِّنَ لِلْمُسْرِفِينَ مَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

Whenever someone is touched by hardship, they cry out to Us, whether lying on their side, sitting, or standing. But when We relieve their hardship, they return to their old ways as if they had never cried to Us to remove any hardship! This is how the misdeeds of the transgressors have been made appealing to them.

And when harm touches man, he calls upon Us to remove it, lying on his side or sitting or standing, according to the condition he is in when that harm befalls him. But when We remove from him the harm that touched him, he continues on his previous path before the harm afflicted him, and forgets what he was in of hardship and affliction, and abandons gratitude to his Lord who relieved him of what had befallen him of affliction. Just as it has been made fair-seeming to this man his persistence in his denial and obstinacy after Allah relieved him of his harm, it has been made fair-seeming to those who have transgressed in lying about Allah and His prophets what they used to do of disobedience to Allah and associating partners with Him.

When people face hardship, they turn to Allah, but when relieved, they often forget and return to their old ways. This shows how misdeeds become appealing to them.

Verse 13

وَلَقَدْ أَهْلَكْنَا الْقُرُونَ مِنْ قَبْلِكُمْ لَمَّا ظَلَمُوا ۙ وَجَاءَتْهُمْ رُسُلُهُمْ بِالْبَيِّنَاتِ وَمَا كَانُوا لِيُؤْمِنُوا ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ نَجْزِي الْقَوْمَ الْمُجْرِمِينَ

We surely destroyed ˹other˺ peoples before you when they did wrong, and their messengers came to them with clear proofs but they would not believe! This is how We reward the wicked people.

And We have already destroyed the generations who denied the Messengers of Allah before you - O polytheists in their Lord - when they associated partners with Allah, and their Messengers came to them from Allah with clear miracles and proofs that demonstrate the truth of the one who came with them, but these nations that We destroyed were not to believe their Messengers and submit to them, so they deserved destruction, and such is the recompense We give to every criminal who transgresses the limits of Allah.

Allah destroyed previous nations who did wrong and rejected their messengers. This serves as a warning of how Allah rewards the wicked.

Verse 14

ثُمَّ جَعَلْنَاكُمْ خَلَائِفَ فِي الْأَرْضِ مِنْ بَعْدِهِمْ لِنَنْظُرَ كَيْفَ تَعْمَلُونَ

Then We made you their successors in the land to see how you would act.

Then We made you - O people - successors on earth after the destroyed generations, to see how you will act: good or evil, and We will recompense you for that according to your deeds.

Allah made the current generation successors on earth to see how they would act and be accountable for their deeds.

Verse 15

وَإِذَا تُتْلَىٰ عَلَيْهِمْ آيَاتُنَا بَيِّنَاتٍ ۙ قَالَ الَّذِينَ لَا يَرْجُونَ لِقَاءَنَا ائْتِ بِقُرْآنٍ غَيْرِ هَٰذَا أَوْ بَدِّلْهُ ۚ قُلْ مَا يَكُونُ لِي أَنْ أُبَدِّلَهُ مِنْ تِلْقَاءِ نَفْسِي ۖ إِنْ أَتَّبِعُ إِلَّا مَا يُوحَىٰ إِلَيَّ ۖ إِنِّي أَخَافُ إِنْ عَصَيْتُ رَبِّي عَذَابَ يَوْمٍ عَظِيمٍ

When Our clear revelations are recited to them, those who do not expect to meet Us say ˹to the Prophet˺, “Bring us a different Quran or make some changes in it.” Say ˹to them˺, “It is not for me to change it on my own. I only follow what is revealed to me. I fear, if I were to disobey my Lord, the punishment of a tremendous Day.”

And when Our clear verses are recited to the polytheists—which We have revealed to you, [O Prophet]—those who do not fear the reckoning, nor hope for reward, nor believe in the Day of Resurrection, say, “Bring a Quran other than this, or change it,” by making the lawful unlawful, the unlawful lawful, the promise a threat, the threat a promise, and by omitting what it contains of criticism of our gods and denigration of our dreams. Say to them, [O Prophet], “That is not for me to do. I only follow in everything I command you and forbid you what my Lord reveals to me and commands me to do. I fear from Allah—if I disobey His command—the punishment of a tremendous Day, which is the Day of Resurrection.”

When the Quran is recited, disbelievers demand a different scripture or alterations. The Prophet clarifies that he cannot change the Quran, as he only follows divine revelation and fears Allah's punishment for disobedience.

Verse 16

قُلْ لَوْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ مَا تَلَوْتُهُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَلَا أَدْرَاكُمْ بِهِ ۖ فَقَدْ لَبِثْتُ فِيكُمْ عُمُرًا مِنْ قَبْلِهِ ۚ أَفَلَا تَعْقِلُونَ

Say, “Had Allah willed, I would not have recited it to you, nor would He have made it known to you. I had lived my whole life among you before this ˹revelation˺. Do you not understand?”

Say to them, [O Prophet], “If Allah had willed, I would not have recited this Quran to you, nor would Allah have made it known to you. Know that it is the truth from Allah, for you know that I lived among you a long time before my Lord revealed it to me, and before I recited it to you. Do you not use your minds for contemplation and reflection?”

The Prophet emphasizes that if Allah had not willed it, the Quran would not have been revealed to him or made known to them. He reminds them of his life among them before the revelation, questioning their lack of understanding.

Verse 17

فَمَنْ أَظْلَمُ مِمَّنِ افْتَرَىٰ عَلَى اللَّهِ كَذِبًا أَوْ كَذَّبَ بِآيَاتِهِ ۚ إِنَّهُ لَا يُفْلِحُ الْمُجْرِمُونَ

Who does more wrong than those who fabricate lies against Allah or deny His revelations? Indeed, the wicked will never succeed.

No one is more unjust than he who invents a lie against Allah or denies His verses. Indeed, those who deny the prophets and messengers of Allah will not succeed, nor will they attain salvation.

This verse condemns those who fabricate lies against Allah or deny His revelations, stating that such wrongdoers will never achieve success.

Verse 18

وَيَعْبُدُونَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ مَا لَا يَضُرُّهُمْ وَلَا يَنْفَعُهُمْ وَيَقُولُونَ هَٰؤُلَاءِ شُفَعَاؤُنَا عِنْدَ اللَّهِ ۚ قُلْ أَتُنَبِّئُونَ اللَّهَ بِمَا لَا يَعْلَمُ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَلَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۚ سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ عَمَّا يُشْرِكُونَ

They worship besides Allah others who can neither harm nor benefit them, and say, “These are our intercessors with Allah.” Ask ˹them, O Prophet˺, “Are you informing Allah of something He does not know in the heavens or the earth? Glorified and Exalted is He above what they associate ˹with Him˺!”

And these polytheists worship besides Allah what can neither harm them nor benefit them in this world or the Hereafter, and they say, “We only worship them so that they may intercede for us with Allah.” Say to them, [O Prophet], “Do you inform Allah of something He does not know about these intercessors in the heavens or on the earth? For if there were intercessors in them who could intercede for you with Him, He would have known about them more than you. So Allah is exalted above what these polytheists do of associating with Him in worship those who can neither harm nor benefit.”

Disbelievers worship idols, claiming they are intercessors with Allah. This verse challenges this belief, asking if they are informing Allah of something He does not know, and affirming that Allah is far above having any partners.

Verse 19

وَمَا كَانَ النَّاسُ إِلَّا أُمَّةً وَاحِدَةً فَاخْتَلَفُوا ۚ وَلَوْلَا كَلِمَةٌ سَبَقَتْ مِنْ رَبِّكَ لَقُضِيَ بَيْنَهُمْ فِيمَا فِيهِ يَخْتَلِفُونَ

Humanity was once nothing but a single community ˹of believers˺, but then they differed. Had it not been for a prior decree from your Lord, their differences would have been settled ˹at once˺.

Mankind were one community [following one religion, which is Islam], then they differed after that, so some of them disbelieved, and some of them remained steadfast on the truth. And had it not been for a word that had preceded from Allah to grant respite to the disobedient and not hasten their punishment for their sins, judgment would have been passed between them: so that the people of falsehood would have been destroyed, and the people of truth would have been saved.

Humanity was once united, but then they differed. If not for Allah's prior decree, their differences would have been settled immediately, indicating that God's plan allows for a period of trial and testing.

Verse 20

وَيَقُولُونَ لَوْلَا أُنْزِلَ عَلَيْهِ آيَةٌ مِنْ رَبِّهِ ۖ فَقُلْ إِنَّمَا الْغَيْبُ لِلَّهِ فَانْتَظِرُوا إِنِّي مَعَكُمْ مِنَ الْمُنْتَظِرِينَ

They ask, “Why has no ˹other˺ sign been sent down to him from his Lord?” Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “˹The knowledge of˺ the unseen is with Allah alone. So wait! I too am waiting with you.”

And these stubborn disbelievers say, “Why has not an evidence and sign been sent down to Muhammad from his Lord, by which we may know that he is upon the truth in what he says?” Say to them, [O Prophet], “No one knows the unseen except Allah. So if He wills, He will do it, and if He wills, He will not do it. So wait, O people, for the judgment of Allah between us and you by hastening His punishment for the wrongdoer among us and victory for the one who is upon the truth. I am waiting for that.”

Disbelievers ask for a sign from Allah. The Prophet responds that knowledge of the unseen belongs to Allah alone, urging them to wait for Allah's judgment and making it clear that the Prophet is also waiting.

Verse 21

وَإِذَا أَذَقْنَا النَّاسَ رَحْمَةً مِنْ بَعْدِ ضَرَّاءَ مَسَّتْهُمْ إِذَا لَهُمْ مَكْرٌ فِي آيَاتِنَا ۚ قُلِ اللَّهُ أَسْرَعُ مَكْرًا ۚ إِنَّ رُسُلَنَا يَكْتُبُونَ مَا تَمْكُرُونَ

When We give people a taste of mercy after being afflicted with a hardship, they swiftly devise plots against Our revelations! Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Allah is swifter in devising ˹punishment˺. Surely Our messenger-angels record whatever you devise.”

And when We let the polytheists taste ease, relief, and prosperity after hardship, severity, and distress had afflicted them, they immediately deny and mock the verses of Allah. Say, [O Messenger], to these mocking polytheists: Allah is swifter in planning, and in drawing you on gradually, and in punishing you. Indeed, Our keepers whom We send to you write down what you are plotting against Our verses, then We will hold you accountable for that.

This verse highlights how people, upon receiving blessings after hardship, often plot against God's messages. It warns that God's plan is far swifter and His messengers record all actions.

Verse 22

The Prophet (pbuh) is encouraged to seek confirmation from those who had prior scriptures, emphasizing the consistency of the divine message and reinforcing the truth of his prophethood.

Verse 95

وَلَا تَكُونَنَّ مِنَ الَّذِينَ كَذَّبُوا بِآيَاتِ اللَّهِ فَتَكُونَ مِنَ الْخَاسِرِينَ

and do not be one of those who deny Allah’s signs or you will be one of the losers.

And do not be, O Messenger, among those who deny Allah's proofs and evidences, for then you will be among the losers upon whom Allah has become displeased and who have attained His punishment.

The Prophet (pbuh) is warned against denying Allah's signs. This emphasizes the importance of acknowledging the truth, otherwise leading to loss and punishment.

Verse 96

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ حَقَّتْ عَلَيْهِمْ كَلِمَتُ رَبِّكَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ

Indeed, those against whom Allah’s decree ˹of torment˺ is justified will not believe—

Indeed, those upon whom the word of your Lord has been fulfilled - O Messenger - by their banishment from His mercy and their punishment, will not believe in Allah’s proofs, nor will they acknowledge His oneness, nor will they act in accordance with His law.

Those who have been decreed not to believe will not do so, demonstrating that some hearts are sealed, and they will persist in their denial despite clear signs.

Verse 97

وَلَوْ جَاءَتْهُمْ كُلُّ آيَةٍ حَتَّىٰ يَرَوُا الْعَذَابَ الْأَلِيمَ

even if every sign were to come to them—until they see the painful punishment.

Even if every admonition and sign came to them until they witness the painful punishment, then at that time they would believe, but their faith will not benefit them.

Even if these deniers were presented with every sign, they would not believe until they see the painful punishment, underscoring the limitations of witnessing alone and the necessity of sincere acceptance of faith.

Verse 98

فَلَوْلَا كَانَتْ قَرْيَةٌ آمَنَتْ فَنَفَعَهَا إِيمَانُهَا إِلَّا قَوْمَ يُونُسَ لَمَّا آمَنُوا كَشَفْنَا عَنْهُمْ عَذَابَ الْخِزْيِ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَمَتَّعْنَاهُمْ إِلَىٰ حِينٍ

If only there had been a society which believed ˹before seeing the torment˺ and, therefore, benefited from its belief, like the people of Jonah. When they believed, We lifted from them the torment of disgrace in this world and allowed them enjoyment for a while.

Faith did not benefit the people of any town who believed when they saw the punishment, except the people of the town of Jonah, son of Matta. For when they were certain that the punishment was coming upon them, they repented to Allah Almighty with sincere repentance. Then, when the truthfulness of their repentance became clear to them, Allah removed the disgraceful punishment from them after it had approached them, and left them in this world to enjoy until the end of their appointed terms.

This verse highlights that faith is valuable when it comes before punishment. The people of Jonah repented sincerely, so Allah removed their impending punishment. This shows the importance of believing before it's too late.

Verse 99

وَلَوْ شَاءَ رَبُّكَ لَآمَنَ مَنْ فِي الْأَرْضِ كُلُّهُمْ جَمِيعًا ۚ أَفَأَنْتَ تُكْرِهُ النَّاسَ حَتَّىٰ يَكُونُوا مُؤْمِنِينَ

Had your Lord so willed ˹O Prophet˺, all ˹people˺ on earth would have certainly believed, every single one of them! Would you then force people to become believers?

And if your Lord had willed, O Messenger, all the people of the earth would have believed, all of them, in what you brought them. But He has wisdom in that. He guides whom He wills and misguides whom He wills, according to His wisdom, and it is not within your power to force people to believe.

This verse explains that if Allah had willed, everyone would believe, but He allows free will. It emphasizes that the Prophet (peace be upon him) cannot force faith; guidance comes from Allah.

Verse 100

وَمَا كَانَ لِنَفْسٍ أَنْ تُؤْمِنَ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِ اللَّهِ ۚ وَيَجْعَلُ الرِّجْسَ عَلَى الَّذِينَ لَا يَعْقِلُونَ

It is not for any soul to believe except by Allah’s leave, and He will bring His wrath upon those who are unmindful.

And it was not for a soul to believe in Allah except with His permission and enabling, so do not exhaust yourself in that, for their matter is with Allah. And Allah places punishment and disgrace on those who do not understand His command and prohibition.

Belief is granted by Allah's permission. Those who are heedless of Allah's signs will face His punishment, highlighting divine control over faith and its consequences.

Verse 101

قُلِ انْظُرُوا مَاذَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ وَمَا تُغْنِي الْآيَاتُ وَالنُّذُرُ عَنْ قَوْمٍ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Consider all that is in the heavens and the earth!” Yet neither signs nor warners are of any benefit to those who refuse to believe.

Say, O Messenger, to your people: Ponder and consider what is in the heavens and the earth of Allah's clear signs, but the signs and lessons, and the messengers who warn the servants of Allah of His punishment, do not benefit a people who do not believe in any of that, because of their turning away and obstinacy.

The verse urges reflection on the signs in the heavens and the earth, but these signs do not benefit those who are obstinate in their disbelief. It stresses that faith requires openness and receptiveness.

Verse 102

فَهَلْ يَنْتَظِرُونَ إِلَّا مِثْلَ أَيَّامِ الَّذِينَ خَلَوْا مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ ۚ قُلْ فَانْتَظِرُوا إِنِّي مَعَكُمْ مِنَ الْمُنْتَظِرِينَ

Are they waiting for ˹anything˺ except the same torments that befell those before them? Say, “Keep waiting then! I too am waiting with you.”

Then, are these people waiting for anything but a day on which they will witness the punishment of Allah, like the days of their denying ancestors who passed away before them? Say to them, O Messenger: Then wait for the punishment of Allah; indeed, I am with you among those who are waiting for your punishment.

This verse questions if disbelievers are waiting for the same fate as past generations, warning them of an inevitable punishment for their denial. It also reinforces the idea of divine justice.

Verse 103

ثُمَّ نُنَجِّي رُسُلَنَا وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ حَقًّا عَلَيْنَا نُنْجِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

Then We saved Our messengers and those who believed. For it is Our duty to save the believers.

Then We save Our messengers and those who believe with them. And just as We saved those, We will save you, O Messenger, and those who believe in you as a favor from Us and mercy.

Allah always saves His messengers and the believers, emphasizing His promise to protect those who adhere to His teachings. It serves as reassurance for the faithful.

Verse 104

قُلْ يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ فِي شَكٍّ مِنْ دِينِي فَلَا أَعْبُدُ الَّذِينَ تَعْبُدُونَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ وَلَٰكِنْ أَعْبُدُ اللَّهَ الَّذِي يَتَوَفَّاكُمْ ۖ وَأُمِرْتُ أَنْ أَكُونَ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “O humanity! If you are in doubt of my faith, then ˹know that˺ I do not worship those ˹idols˺ you worship instead of Allah. But I worship Allah, Who has the power to cause your death. And I have been commanded, ‘Be one of the believers,’

Say, O Messenger, to these people: If you are in doubt about the validity of my religion to which I have called you, which is Islam, and of my steadfastness and uprightness upon it, and you hope for my turning away from it, then I do not worship under any circumstances any of those whom you worship from what you have taken of idols and false deities, but I worship Allah alone, who causes you to die and takes your souls, and I have been commanded to be among those who affirm Him, acting according to His law.

The Prophet (peace be upon him) declares that he only worships Allah, the One who gives life and takes it away. He rejects the idols of the disbelievers, affirming his commitment to monotheism.

Verse 105

وَأَنْ أَقِمْ وَجْهَكَ لِلدِّينِ حَنِيفًا وَلَا تَكُونَنَّ مِنَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ

and, ‘Be steadfast in faith in all uprightness, and do not be one of the polytheists,’

And that you, O Messenger, establish yourself upon the religion of Islam, being steadfast upon it, not inclining away from it to Judaism nor Christianity nor the worship of other than Him. And do not be of those who associate in the worship of their Lord, partners and rivals, so you would be of the destroyed ones. And this, even though it is addressed to the Messenger, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, it is directed to the entire nation.

The Prophet (peace be upon him) is commanded to be steadfast in his faith and to avoid polytheism. This directive applies to all Muslims, emphasizing the importance of maintaining the purity of faith.

Verse 106

وَلَا تَدْعُ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ مَا لَا يَنْفَعُكَ وَلَا يَضُرُّكَ ۖ فَإِنْ فَعَلْتَ فَإِنَّكَ إِذًا مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ

and ‘Do not invoke, instead of Allah, what can neither benefit nor harm you—for if you do, then you will certainly be one of the wrongdoers,’

And do not invoke, O Messenger, besides Allah anything of idols and false deities, because they neither benefit nor harm. For if you did that and invoked them besides Allah, then you would be of those who associate partners with Allah, doing injustice to themselves by shirk and disobedience. And this, even though it is addressed to the Messenger, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, it is directed to the entire nation.

This verse prohibits invoking anything besides Allah because only Allah can provide benefit or harm. It warns against associating partners with Allah (shirk), which is a grave injustice.

Verse 107

وَإِنْ يَمْسَسْكَ اللَّهُ بِضُرٍّ فَلَا كَاشِفَ لَهُ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ وَإِنْ يُرِدْكَ بِخَيْرٍ فَلَا رَادَّ لِفَضْلِهِ ۚ يُصِيبُ بِهِ مَنْ يَشَاءُ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ ۚ وَهُوَ الْغَفُورُ الرَّحِيمُ

and ‘If Allah touches you with harm, none can undo it except Him. And if He intends good for you, none can withhold His bounty. He grants it to whoever He wills of His servants. And He is the All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.’”

And if Allah afflicts you - O Messenger - with hardship or a calamity, there is no remover of it except Him, Glory and Exalted is He. And if He intends for you ease or a blessing, no one can withhold it from you. Allah, the Mighty and Majestic, afflicts with ease and hardship whomever He wills of His servants, and He is the Forgiving of the sins of whoever repents, the Merciful to whoever believes in Him and obeys Him.

This verse highlights Allah's absolute power. If He brings harm, only He can remove it; if He intends good, no one can prevent it. Allah is the All-Forgiving and Most Merciful to those who repent and believe.

Verse 108

قُلْ يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ قَدْ جَاءَكُمُ الْحَقُّ مِنْ رَبِّكُمْ ۖ فَمَنِ اهْتَدَىٰ فَإِنَّمَا يَهْتَدِي لِنَفْسِهِ ۖ وَمَنْ ضَلَّ فَإِنَّمَا يَضِلُّ عَلَيْهَا ۖ وَمَا أَنَا عَلَيْكُمْ بِوَكِيلٍ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “O humanity! The truth has surely come to you from your Lord. So whoever chooses to be guided, it is only for their own good. And whoever chooses to stray, it is only to their own loss. And I am not a keeper over you.”

Say - O Messenger - to these people: The Messenger of Allah has come to you with the Quran, which contains the explanation of your guidance. So, whoever is guided by the guidance of Allah, then the fruit of his action returns to him. And whoever deviates from the truth and insists on misguidance, then his misguidance and harm are upon himself. And I am not entrusted with you to make you believers. I am only a Messenger, delivering what I have been sent with.

Prophet Muhammad is told to declare that the truth has come from Allah. Whoever follows guidance does so for their own benefit, and whoever strays does so to their own detriment. The Prophet is a messenger, not a keeper of people's faith.

Verse 109

وَاتَّبِعْ مَا يُوحَىٰ إِلَيْكَ وَاصْبِرْ حَتَّىٰ يَحْكُمَ اللَّهُ ۚ وَهُوَ خَيْرُ الْحَاكِمِينَ

And follow what is revealed to you, and be patient until Allah passes His judgment. For He is the Best of Judges.

And follow - O Messenger - the revelation of Allah that is revealed to you and act upon it, and be patient in obeying Allah, and in refraining from disobeying Him, and in the harm of those who harm you in conveying His message, until Allah decides His command concerning them and you. And He - the Mighty and Majestic - is the best of judges, for His judgment contains complete justice.

The Prophet is commanded to follow divine revelations and be patient until Allah's judgment is passed. Allah is the Best of Judges, and His judgment is completely just.
