Sura 016

Verse 1

أَتَىٰ أَمْرُ اللَّهِ فَلَا تَسْتَعْجِلُوهُ ۚ سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ عَمَّا يُشْرِكُونَ

The command of Allah is at hand, so do not hasten it. Glorified and Exalted is He above what they associate ˹with Him in worship˺!

The establishment of the Hour and Allah's decree of your punishment - O disbelievers - has drawn near, so do not hasten the punishment in mockery of the Messenger’s warning to you. Allah is exalted and free of partners and associates.

This verse emphasizes that the Day of Judgment is near and not to be hastened as a jest, and that Allah is above any partners or associations.

Verse 2

يُنَزِّلُ الْمَلَائِكَةَ بِالرُّوحِ مِنْ أَمْرِهِ عَلَىٰ مَنْ يَشَاءُ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ أَنْ أَنْذِرُوا أَنَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنَا فَاتَّقُونِ

He sends down the angels with revelation by His command to whoever He wills of His servants, ˹stating:˺ “Warn ˹humanity˺ that there is no god ˹worthy of worship˺ except Me, so be mindful of Me ˹alone˺.”

Allah sends down the angels with the revelation by His command upon whom He wills of His Messengers: to warn people against polytheism, and that there is no deity worthy of worship except Me, so fear Me by performing My obligations and singling Me out in worship and sincerity.

Allah sends angels with revelation to warn against polytheism and to declare that He is the only God worthy of worship. People should be mindful of Allah alone.

Verse 3

خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ بِالْحَقِّ ۚ تَعَالَىٰ عَمَّا يُشْرِكُونَ

He created the heavens and the earth for a purpose. Exalted is He above what they associate with Him ˹in worship˺!

Allah created the heavens and earth in truth, so that the servants may use them to infer the greatness of their Creator, and that He alone is worthy of worship. He is exalted and glorified above their association.

Allah created the heavens and earth with a purpose so people can recognize the greatness of their Creator. Allah is exalted above all that they associate with Him.

Verse 4

خَلَقَ الْإِنْسَانَ مِنْ نُطْفَةٍ فَإِذَا هُوَ خَصِيمٌ مُبِينٌ

He created humans from a sperm-drop, then—behold!—they openly challenge ˹Him˺.

He created man from a despised fluid, and then he becomes strong and arrogant, and becomes fiercely argumentative with his Lord in denying the resurrection, and other things, such as his saying: "Who will give life to bones while they are decayed?" And he forgot Allah who created him from nothing.

This verse highlights the human's humble beginning as a sperm-drop, and then they become arrogant, openly challenging God while forgetting their creation from nothing.

Verse 5

وَالْأَنْعَامَ خَلَقَهَا ۗ لَكُمْ فِيهَا دِفْءٌ وَمَنَافِعُ وَمِنْهَا تَأْكُلُونَ

And He created the cattle for you as a source of warmth, food, and ˹many other˺ benefits.

And the livestock of camels, cattle, and sheep, Allah created them for you - O people - and He has placed in their wool and fur warmth, and other benefits in their milk, hides, and riding, and from them you eat.

Allah created livestock for human benefit, providing warmth, food, and various other uses.

Verse 6

وَلَكُمْ فِيهَا جَمَالٌ حِينَ تُرِيحُونَ وَحِينَ تَسْرَحُونَ

They are also pleasing to you when you bring them home and when you take them out to graze.

And for you in them is beauty, bringing joy upon you when you return them to their homes in the evening, and when you take them out to pasture in the morning.

This verse points out the beauty and joy that livestock brings, both when returning home in the evening and going out to pasture in the morning.

Verse 7

وَتَحْمِلُ أَثْقَالَكُمْ إِلَىٰ بَلَدٍ لَمْ تَكُونُوا بَالِغِيهِ إِلَّا بِشِقِّ الْأَنْفُسِ ۚ إِنَّ رَبَّكُمْ لَرَءُوفٌ رَحِيمٌ

And they carry your loads to ˹distant˺ lands which you could not otherwise reach without great hardship. Surely your Lord is Ever Gracious, Most Merciful.

And these cattle carry your loads to a distant land, which you could not have reached except with great difficulty and hardship. Indeed, your Lord is Kind and Merciful to you, as He has subjected to you what you need, so to Him is the praise and to Him is the gratitude.

This verse highlights how animals carry heavy loads to distant places, which would be difficult for humans to reach otherwise. It emphasizes God's grace and mercy in facilitating human life.

Verse 8

وَالْخَيْلَ وَالْبِغَالَ وَالْحَمِيرَ لِتَرْكَبُوهَا وَزِينَةً ۚ وَيَخْلُقُ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ

˹He also created˺ horses, mules, and donkeys for your transportation and adornment. And He creates what you do not know.

And He created horses, mules, and donkeys for you to ride, and for them to be beauty for you and a good sight; and He creates for you means of riding and others which you do not know; so that you increase in faith in Him and gratitude to Him.

God created horses, mules, and donkeys for transportation and as a source of beauty. He continues to create things beyond human knowledge.

Verse 9

وَعَلَى اللَّهِ قَصْدُ السَّبِيلِ وَمِنْهَا جَائِرٌ ۚ وَلَوْ شَاءَ لَهَدَاكُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ

It is upon Allah ˹alone˺ to ˹clearly˺ show the Straight Way. Other ways are deviant. Had He willed, He would have easily imposed guidance upon all of you.

And upon Allah is the explanation of the straight path for your guidance, which is Islam, and among the paths are those that are inclined and do not lead to guidance, which is everything that contradicts Islam from sects and creeds. And if Allah had willed to guide you, He would have guided you all to faith.

It is God's role to show the clear path of guidance. Other paths deviate from this straight path. If God had willed, He could have guided everyone, but He has given humans free will.

Verse 10

هُوَ الَّذِي أَنْزَلَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً ۖ لَكُمْ مِنْهُ شَرَابٌ وَمِنْهُ شَجَرٌ فِيهِ تُسِيمُونَ

He is the One Who sends down rain from the sky, from which you drink and by which plants grow for your cattle to graze.

It is He who sends down rain from the sky for you, and from it you have water to drink, and He brings forth for you trees in which you pasture your animals, and their yield and benefit return to you.

God sends down rain from the sky, which provides drinking water and nourishes plants for humans and animals.

Verse 11

يُنْبِتُ لَكُمْ بِهِ الزَّرْعَ وَالزَّيْتُونَ وَالنَّخِيلَ وَالْأَعْنَابَ وَمِنْ كُلِّ الثَّمَرَاتِ ۗ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَةً لِقَوْمٍ يَتَفَكَّرُونَ

With it He produces for you ˹various˺ crops, olives, palm trees, grapevines, and every type of fruit. Surely in this is a sign for those who reflect.

He brings forth for you from the earth with this one water diverse crops, and brings forth with it olives, palm trees, and grapes, and brings forth with it all kinds of fruits and produce. Indeed, in that bringing forth is a clear sign for a people who contemplate, and take heed.

With rainwater, God produces diverse crops like olives, dates, grapes, and other fruits. This is a sign for those who reflect on God's creation.

Verse 12

وَسَخَّرَ لَكُمُ اللَّيْلَ وَالنَّهَارَ وَالشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ ۖ وَالنُّجُومُ مُسَخَّرَاتٌ بِأَمْرِهِ ۗ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ لِقَوْمٍ يَعْقِلُونَ

And He has subjected for your benefit the day and the night, the sun and the moon. And the stars have been subjected by His command. Surely in this are signs for those who understand.

And He has subjected for you the night for your rest, and the day for your livelihood, and He has subjected for you the sun for light, and the moon for illumination and for knowing the years and the reckoning, and other benefits, and the stars in the sky are made subservient to you by the command of Allah for knowing times, the ripening of fruits and crops, and for guidance in the darkness. Indeed, in that subjection are clear signs for a people who will understand from Allah His proofs and evidence.

God has made the day and night, sun and moon, and stars beneficial to humans. The stars serve as guides and markers of time. These are signs for those who understand.

Verse 13

وَمَا ذَرَأَ لَكُمْ فِي الْأَرْضِ مُخْتَلِفًا أَلْوَانُهُ ۗ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَةً لِقَوْمٍ يَذَّكَّرُونَ

And ˹He subjected˺ for you whatever He has created on earth of varying colours. Surely in this is a sign for those who are mindful.

And He has subjected what He created for you on the earth, from animals, fruits, minerals, and other things of which the colors and benefits differ. Indeed, in that creation and the difference of colors and benefits is a lesson for a people who take heed, and know that in the subjection of these things are signs of the Oneness of Allah and His being singled out for worship.

God has created diverse things on Earth, including animals, fruits, and minerals, all with different colors and benefits, providing lessons for those who are mindful of His signs.

Verse 14

وَهُوَ الَّذِي سَخَّرَ الْبَحْرَ لِتَأْكُلُوا مِنْهُ لَحْمًا طَرِيًّا وَتَسْتَخْرِجُوا مِنْهُ حِلْيَةً تَلْبَسُونَهَا وَتَرَى الْفُلْكَ مَوَاخِرَ فِيهِ وَلِتَبْتَغُوا مِنْ فَضْلِهِ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ

And He is the One Who has subjected the sea, so from it you may eat tender seafood and extract ornaments to wear. And you see the ships ploughing their way through it, so you may seek His bounty and give thanks ˹to Him˺.

And it is He who has subjected the sea for you, so that you may eat from what you catch of its fish as fresh meat, and extract from it ornaments that you wear like pearls and coral, and you see the great ships cleaving the surface of the water going and coming, and you ride them in order to seek the provision of Allah by trade and profit in them, and perhaps you will be thankful to Allah for His great blessings upon you, so you do not worship other than Him.

God has subjected the sea for humans to eat seafood, extract ornaments, and travel on ships, encouraging people to seek His bounty and be thankful for His blessings.

Verse 15

وَأَلْقَىٰ فِي الْأَرْضِ رَوَاسِيَ أَنْ تَمِيدَ بِكُمْ وَأَنْهَارًا وَسُبُلًا لَعَلَّكُمْ تَهْتَدُونَ

He has placed into the earth firm mountains, so it does not shake with you, as well as rivers, and pathways so you may find your way.

And He placed upon the earth mountains to stabilize it so that it would not sway with you, and made rivers in it so that you may drink from them, and made pathways in it so that you may be guided in reaching the places you intend.

Allah has placed firm mountains on Earth to stabilize it, along with rivers and pathways to help people find their way.

Verse 16

وَعَلَامَاتٍ ۚ وَبِالنَّجْمِ هُمْ يَهْتَدُونَ

Also by landmarks and stars do people find their way.

And He placed landmarks on the earth by which you are guided on the paths by day, as He placed the stars to be guided by them at night.

Landmarks and stars are also provided as guides for people to navigate by day and night.

Verse 17

أَفَمَنْ يَخْلُقُ كَمَنْ لَا يَخْلُقُ ۗ أَفَلَا تَذَكَّرُونَ

Can the One Who creates be equal to those who do not? Will you not then be mindful?

Do you make Allah, who creates all these things and others, equal in the right to be worshiped to the alleged deities that do not create anything? Will you not remember the greatness of Allah, so you single Him out in worship?

Can the Creator be compared to those who cannot create anything? This is a reminder to be mindful and worship Allah alone.

Verse 18

وَإِنْ تَعُدُّوا نِعْمَةَ اللَّهِ لَا تُحْصُوهَا ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَغَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

If you tried to count Allah’s blessings, you would never be able to number them. Surely Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

And if you try to count the favors of Allah upon you, you will not be able to count them, because of their abundance and variety. Indeed, Allah is Forgiving to you, Merciful to you; as He overlooks your shortcomings in fulfilling the thanks for the blessings, and does not cut them off from you for your negligence, and does not hasten punishment for you.

The blessings of Allah are countless. Allah is Forgiving and Merciful, overlooking our shortcomings in gratitude.

Verse 19

وَاللَّهُ يَعْلَمُ مَا تُسِرُّونَ وَمَا تُعْلِنُونَ

And Allah knows what you conceal and what you reveal.

And Allah, the Exalted, knows all your deeds, whether what you conceal of them within yourselves or what you reveal to others, and He will recompense you for them.

Allah knows everything that is concealed and revealed, and will recompense all deeds.

Verse 20

وَالَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ لَا يَخْلُقُونَ شَيْئًا وَهُمْ يُخْلَقُونَ

But those ˹idols˺ they invoke besides Allah cannot create anything—they themselves are created.

And the deities that the polytheists worship do not create anything, even if it is small, for they are creations made by the disbelievers with their own hands, so how do they worship them?

The idols that are worshipped besides Allah cannot create anything; they are themselves created.

Verse 21

أَمْوَاتٌ غَيْرُ أَحْيَاءٍ ۖ وَمَا يَشْعُرُونَ أَيَّانَ يُبْعَثُونَ

They are dead, not alive—not even knowing when their followers will be resurrected.

They are all inanimate objects with no life in them and do not feel the time when Allah will resurrect their worshippers, and they are with them to be cast together into the Fire on the Day of Resurrection.

These idols are lifeless objects, unable to know when their worshippers will be resurrected.

Verse 22

إِلَٰهُكُمْ إِلَٰهٌ وَاحِدٌ ۚ فَالَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِالْآخِرَةِ قُلُوبُهُمْ مُنْكِرَةٌ وَهُمْ مُسْتَكْبِرُونَ

Your God is ˹only˺ One God. As for those who do not believe in the Hereafter, their hearts are in denial, and they are too proud.

Your God, the only One worthy of worship, is Allah, the One God. So, those who do not believe in the resurrection, their hearts deny His Oneness, glory be to Him, because of their lack of fear of His punishment. They are arrogant against accepting the truth and worshiping Allah alone.

There is only One God, Allah. Those who deny the Hereafter are arrogant and reject the truth.

Verse 23

لَا جَرَمَ أَنَّ اللَّهَ يَعْلَمُ مَا يُسِرُّونَ وَمَا يُعْلِنُونَ ۚ إِنَّهُ لَا يُحِبُّ الْمُسْتَكْبِرِينَ

Without a doubt, Allah knows what they conceal and what they reveal. He certainly does not like those who are too proud.

Verily, Allah knows what they conceal of beliefs, sayings, and actions, and what they reveal of them, and He will recompense them for that. Indeed, He, the Exalted, does not love those who are arrogant about His worship and submission to Him, and He will recompense them for that.

Allah knows what is concealed and revealed and dislikes the proud, who refuse to submit to Him.

Verse 24

وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ مَاذَا أَنْزَلَ رَبُّكُمْ ۙ قَالُوا أَسَاطِيرُ الْأَوَّلِينَ

And when it is said to them, “What has your Lord revealed?” They say, “Ancient fables!”

And when these polytheists are asked about what was revealed to the Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, they say falsely and deceitfully: He has only come with the stories of the ancients and their falsehoods.

When asked about what Allah has revealed, disbelievers falsely claim it to be ancient fables.

Verse 25

لِيَحْمِلُوا أَوْزَارَهُمْ كَامِلَةً يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ ۙ وَمِنْ أَوْزَارِ الَّذِينَ يُضِلُّونَهُمْ بِغَيْرِ عِلْمٍ ۗ أَلَا سَاءَ مَا يَزِرُونَ

Let them bear their burdens in full on the Day of Judgment as well as some of the burdens of those they mislead without knowledge. Evil indeed is what they will bear!

Their consequence will be that they will bear their full burdens on the Day of Resurrection - nothing will be forgiven of it - and they will bear some of the burdens of those they deceived to turn them away from Islam without any reduction from their own burdens. How wretched is what they bear of burdens.

These disbelievers will bear their own burdens on the Day of Judgment, as well as the burden of misleading others.

Verse 26

Indeed, the disbelievers before these polytheists plotted against their messengers and what they brought of the call to truth, so Allah came to their building from its foundation and base, and the roof fell upon them from above them, and the destruction came to them from where they felt safe, from where they did not expect it to come to them.

Those before them plotted, but Allah destroyed their structures, bringing torment from unexpected places.

Verse 27

On the Day of Judgment, disbelievers will be humiliated and asked about their false gods. Those with knowledge will declare that disgrace and misery are upon the disbelievers.

Verse 29

The disbelievers will be ordered to enter Hell and remain there forever. This is a miserable abode for the arrogant who rejected faith.

Verse 30

When the righteous are asked about Allah's revelations, they acknowledge it as goodness. The righteous will receive goodness in this life and a far better home in the Hereafter.

the Gardens of Eternity which they will enter, under which rivers flow. In it they will have whatever they desire. This is how Allah rewards the righteous—

Disbelievers choose the temporary pleasures of this world over the eternal rewards of the Hereafter. This preference is why they are not guided by Allah.

The hearts, ears, and eyes of the disbelievers are sealed by Allah due to their choices. They are heedless and do not see or hear the truth.

Those who reject the truth will ultimately be the losers in the Hereafter, having wasted their lives on what leads to punishment.

Verse 110

ثُمَّ إِنَّ رَبَّكَ لِلَّذِينَ هَاجَرُوا مِنْ بَعْدِ مَا فُتِنُوا ثُمَّ جَاهَدُوا وَصَبَرُوا إِنَّ رَبَّكَ مِنْ بَعْدِهَا لَغَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

This verse provides comfort to those who were compelled to renounce Islam but then emigrated, struggled, and persevered in Allah's cause. Allah is forgiving and merciful towards them.

Verse 111

يَوْمَ تَأْتِي كُلُّ نَفْسٍ تُجَادِلُ عَنْ نَفْسِهَا وَتُوَفَّىٰ كُلُّ نَفْسٍ مَا عَمِلَتْ وَهُمْ لَا يُظْلَمُونَ

This verse describes the Day of Judgment when every soul will be accountable for its actions, pleading for itself. Each person will receive full recompense without any injustice.

Verse 112

And Allah presents an example of a city, "Mecca," which was secure from aggression and in tranquility from hardship in life, its provision came easily from every direction, but its people denied the favors of Allah upon them, associated partners with Him, and were ungrateful to Him, so Allah punished them with hunger and fear of the raids of the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and his armies, which were frightening them; and that was because of their disbelief and their false actions.

This verse provides an example of a society that was prosperous but became ungrateful to Allah, leading to their suffering. It serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude.

Verse 113

A messenger came to them, but they denied him, resulting in punishment. This highlights the consequences of rejecting divine guidance and persisting in wrongdoing.

Verse 114

Believers are urged to eat from the good, lawful provisions Allah has given them, and to be grateful for these blessings as an act of worship.

Verse 115

Allah has forbidden certain foods (carrion, blood, swine, and what is sacrificed to other than Allah). However, there is an exception for those in dire need, provided they do not transgress beyond necessity. Allah is forgiving and merciful.

People are warned against falsely declaring things as lawful or unlawful, thereby fabricating lies against Allah. Those who do so will not succeed.

Verse 118

Allah is forgiving and merciful to those who commit sins out of ignorance or recklessness, then repent and correct their ways.
