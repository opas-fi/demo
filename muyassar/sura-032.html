Sura 032

Verse 1

الم

Alif-Lãm-Mĩm.

(Alif Lam Mim) The discussion about the disjointed letters at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah has preceded.

This verse begins with the letters 'Alif-Lam-Mim', which are among the mysterious letters appearing at the start of some surahs. Their precise meaning is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

تَنْزِيلُ الْكِتَابِ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ مِنْ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

The revelation of this Book is—beyond doubt—from the Lord of all worlds.

This Quran, which Muhammad, peace be upon him, brought, there is no doubt that it is revealed from Allah, Lord of all creation.

This verse affirms the Quran's divine origin, stating it is a revelation from Allah, the Lord of all creation, without any doubt.

Verse 3

أَمْ يَقُولُونَ افْتَرَاهُ ۚ بَلْ هُوَ الْحَقُّ مِنْ رَبِّكَ لِتُنْذِرَ قَوْمًا مَا أَتَاهُمْ مِنْ نَذِيرٍ مِنْ قَبْلِكَ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَهْتَدُونَ

Or do they say, “He has fabricated it!”? No! It is the truth from your Lord in order for you to warn a people to whom no warner has come before you, so they may be ˹rightly˺ guided.

Rather, do the polytheists say: Muhammad, peace be upon him, fabricated the Quran? They have lied. Rather, it is the established truth revealed to you - O Messenger - from your Lord; to warn thereby a people to whom no warner has come before you, so that they may be guided, recognize the truth, believe in it, and prefer it, and believe in you.

The verse challenges those who claim the Quran is fabricated, asserting it is the truth from Allah to warn a people who had not received a warner before, so they may be guided.

Verse 4

اللَّهُ الَّذِي خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ وَمَا بَيْنَهُمَا فِي سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍ ثُمَّ اسْتَوَىٰ عَلَى الْعَرْشِ ۖ مَا لَكُمْ مِنْ دُونِهِ مِنْ وَلِيٍّ وَلَا شَفِيعٍ ۚ أَفَلَا تَتَذَكَّرُونَ

It is Allah Who has created the heavens and the earth and everything in between in six Days, then established Himself on the Throne. You have no protector or intercessor besides Him. Will you not then be mindful?

Allah is the One who created the heavens and the earth and what is between them in six days, for a wisdom He knows, and He is able to create them with a word "Be", and it would be. Then He ascended, Glory be to Him - meaning He rose high - over His Throne, an ascension befitting His Majesty, not to be qualified, nor likened to the ascension of created beings. You do not have - O people - any guardian who manages your affairs, or intercessor who intercedes for you with Allah, so that you may be saved from His punishment. Then, will you not take heed and ponder - O people - and single out Allah in worship and be sincere in your worship of Him?

Allah created the heavens, the earth, and everything in between in six days and then established Himself on the Throne. He is the only protector and intercessor; therefore, people should be mindful of Him.

Verse 5

يُدَبِّرُ الْأَمْرَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ إِلَى الْأَرْضِ ثُمَّ يَعْرُجُ إِلَيْهِ فِي يَوْمٍ كَانَ مِقْدَارُهُ أَلْفَ سَنَةٍ مِمَّا تَعُدُّونَ

He conducts every affair from the heavens to the earth, then it all ascends to Him on a Day whose length is a thousand years by your counting.

Allah Almighty manages the affairs of creation from the heaven to the earth, then that matter and management ascends to Allah in a day the measure of which is a thousand years of the days of the world you count.

Allah manages all affairs from the heavens to the earth, and everything returns to Him in a day whose length is like a thousand years by human reckoning, emphasizing His control over time and creation.

Verse 6

ذَٰلِكَ عَالِمُ الْغَيْبِ وَالشَّهَادَةِ الْعَزِيزُ الرَّحِيمُ

That is the Knower of the seen and unseen—the Almighty, Most Merciful,

That Creator, the Manager of the affairs of the worlds, knows all that is unseen from the eyes, what the hearts conceal and the souls hide, and knows what the eyes have witnessed. And He is the Strong, the Dominant, who cannot be overcome, the Merciful to His believing servants.

Allah knows both the seen and the unseen. He is the Almighty, the Most Merciful, indicating His power and compassion.

Verse 7

الَّذِي أَحْسَنَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ خَلَقَهُ ۖ وَبَدَأَ خَلْقَ الْإِنْسَانِ مِنْ طِينٍ

Who has perfected everything He created. And He originated the creation of humankind from clay.

Allah is the One who perfected the creation of everything, and began the creation of man, who is Adam, peace be upon him, from clay.

Allah perfected everything He created. He began the creation of humanity from clay, referring to the creation of Adam (peace be upon him).

Verse 8

ثُمَّ جَعَلَ نَسْلَهُ مِنْ سُلَالَةٍ مِنْ مَاءٍ مَهِينٍ

Then He made his descendants from an extract of a humble fluid,

Then He made the offspring of Adam to be from a weak, thin, and despicable sperm drop.

Then, Allah made the descendants of Adam from a humble fluid (sperm drop), highlighting the humble origin of human life.

Verse 9

ثُمَّ سَوَّاهُ وَنَفَخَ فِيهِ مِنْ رُوحِهِ ۖ وَجَعَلَ لَكُمُ السَّمْعَ وَالْأَبْصَارَ وَالْأَفْئِدَةَ ۚ قَلِيلًا مَا تَشْكُرُونَ

then He fashioned them and had a spirit of His Own ˹creation˺ breathed into them. And He gave you hearing, sight, and intellect. ˹Yet˺ you hardly give any thanks.

Then He perfected the creation of man and originated it, and perfected his form, and breathed into him from His spirit by sending the angel to him, to breathe the spirit into him. And He made for you - O people - the blessing of hearing and sight by which to distinguish between sounds, colors, entities and persons, and the blessing of intellect by which to distinguish between good and evil, the beneficial and the harmful. Little do you thank your Lord for what He has bestowed upon you.

Allah perfected the human form and breathed a spirit into them. He gave humans hearing, sight, and intellect as gifts. However, humans are often ungrateful and offer little thanks.

Verse 10

وَقَالُوا أَإِذَا ضَلَلْنَا فِي الْأَرْضِ أَإِنَّا لَفِي خَلْقٍ جَدِيدٍ ۚ بَلْ هُمْ بِلِقَاءِ رَبِّهِمْ كَافِرُونَ

˹Still˺ they ask ˹mockingly˺, “When we are disintegrated into the earth, will we really be raised as a new creation?” In fact, they are in denial of the meeting with their Lord.

And the polytheists in Allah who deny the resurrection said: When our flesh and bones become dust in the earth, shall we be raised as a new creation? They find that unlikely without seeking to reach the truth, but rather it is from them injustice and stubbornness; because they are disbelievers in the meeting with their Lord - on the Day of Resurrection.

The disbelievers mockingly question how they will be resurrected after they are reduced to dust. They deny the meeting with their Lord on the Day of Judgment.

Verse 11

قُلْ يَتَوَفَّاكُمْ مَلَكُ الْمَوْتِ الَّذِي وُكِّلَ بِكُمْ ثُمَّ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكُمْ تُرْجَعُونَ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Your soul will be taken by the Angel of Death, who is in charge of you. Then to your Lord you will ˹all˺ be returned.”

Say - O Messenger - to these polytheists: The angel of death, who is entrusted with you, will take you, and he will seize your souls when your terms come to an end, and you will not be delayed for a single moment, then you will be returned to your Lord, and He will recompense you for all your deeds: if good, then good, and if evil, then evil.

The Prophet is told to inform the disbelievers that the Angel of Death will take their souls when their appointed time comes, and then they will all be returned to their Lord for judgment.

Verse 12

وَلَوْ تَرَىٰ إِذِ الْمُجْرِمُونَ نَاكِسُو رُءُوسِهِمْ عِنْدَ رَبِّهِمْ رَبَّنَا أَبْصَرْنَا وَسَمِعْنَا فَارْجِعْنَا نَعْمَلْ صَالِحًا إِنَّا مُوقِنُونَ

If only you could see the wicked hanging their heads ˹in shame˺ before their Lord, ˹crying:˺ “Our Lord! We have now seen and heard, so send us back and we will do good. We truly have sure faith ˹now˺!”

If only you could see - O addressee - when the criminals who denied the Resurrection lower their heads before their Lord in shame and disgrace, saying: Our Lord, we have seen our evils, and we have heard from you the confirmation of what your messengers used to command us in the world, and we have repented to you, so return us to the world to do in it by obeying you, we are now certain of what we used to deny in the world of your Oneness, and that you will resurrect those in the graves. If you, O addressee, saw all of that, you would see a great matter and a huge calamity.

This verse describes the regret of the wicked on the Day of Judgment. They will lower their heads in shame, confessing their disbelief and asking to be sent back to do good, showing their now sure faith.

Verse 13

وَلَوْ شِئْنَا لَآتَيْنَا كُلَّ نَفْسٍ هُدَاهَا وَلَٰكِنْ حَقَّ الْقَوْلُ مِنِّي لَأَمْلَأَنَّ جَهَنَّمَ مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ أَجْمَعِينَ

Had We willed, We could have easily imposed guidance on every soul. But My Word will come to pass: I will surely fill up Hell with jinn and humans all together.

And if We had willed, We could have given these polytheists in Allah their guidance and their success to faith, but the Word from Me was justified and due to fulfill, “I will surely fill Hell with the people of disbelief and disobedience, from among jinn and men all together,” and that is for their choosing misguidance over guidance.

This verse explains that Allah could have guided everyone, but His word is that Hell will be filled with those who chose misguidance from both jinn and humans.

Verse 14

فَذُوقُوا بِمَا نَسِيتُمْ لِقَاءَ يَوْمِكُمْ هَٰذَا إِنَّا نَسِينَاكُمْ ۖ وَذُوقُوا عَذَابَ الْخُلْدِ بِمَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ

So taste ˹the punishment˺ for neglecting the meeting of this Day of yours. We ˹too˺ will certainly neglect you. And taste the torment of eternity for what you used to do!

It will be said to these polytheists - upon their entry into the Fire -: So taste the punishment because of your negligence of the Hereafter and your indulgence in the pleasures of the world. Indeed, We have left you today in the punishment, and taste the punishment of Hell that never ends, because of what you used to do in the world of disbelief in Allah and His disobediences.

The disbelievers will be told to taste the punishment for ignoring the Day of Judgment, and they will face eternal torment for their disbelief and disobedience.

Verse 15

إِنَّمَا يُؤْمِنُ بِآيَاتِنَا الَّذِينَ إِذَا ذُكِّرُوا بِهَا خَرُّوا سُجَّدًا وَسَبَّحُوا بِحَمْدِ رَبِّهِمْ وَهُمْ لَا يَسْتَكْبِرُونَ ۩

The only ˹true˺ believers in Our revelation are those who—when it is recited to them—fall into prostration and glorify the praises of their Lord and are not too proud.

Only those who believe in the verses of the Qur'an and act upon them are the ones who, when they are admonished by them or they are recited to them, prostrate to their Lord in humility and obedience, and glorify Allah in their prostrations with His praise, and they are not arrogant to prostrate and glorify Him, and worship Him alone without any partner.

This verse describes the characteristics of true believers. When they hear the Quran, they prostrate in humility, glorify Allah, and are not arrogant in their worship.

Verse 16

تَتَجَافَىٰ جُنُوبُهُمْ عَنِ الْمَضَاجِعِ يَدْعُونَ رَبَّهُمْ خَوْفًا وَطَمَعًا وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَاهُمْ يُنْفِقُونَ

They abandon their beds, invoking their Lord with hope and fear, and donate from what We have provided for them.

The sides of these who believe in the verses of Allah rise from their sleeping beds, they pray to their Lord in the night prayer, they supplicate to their Lord in fear of punishment and in hope of reward, and from what We have provided them, they spend in the obedience of Allah and in His cause.

True believers are described as those who rise at night to pray, supplicate to their Lord with hope and fear, and donate to charity from what they have been given.

Verse 17

فَلَا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَا أُخْفِيَ لَهُمْ مِنْ قُرَّةِ أَعْيُنٍ جَزَاءً بِمَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

No soul can imagine what delights are kept in store for them as a reward for what they used to do.

No soul knows what Allah has stored for these believers of what will please the eye and expand the chest; a reward for their righteous deeds.

This verse emphasizes that the rewards in Paradise are beyond human comprehension, kept as a pleasure for the believers' righteous actions.

Verse 18

أَفَمَنْ كَانَ مُؤْمِنًا كَمَنْ كَانَ فَاسِقًا ۚ لَا يَسْتَوُونَ

Is the one who is a believer equal ˹before Allah˺ to the one who is rebellious? They are not equal!

Is one who is obedient to Allah and His Messenger, believing in His promise and His threat, like one who disbelieves in Allah and His messengers and denies the Last Day? They are not equal in the sight of Allah.

This verse asks if a believer can be equal to a disbeliever, making it clear they are not equal in the sight of Allah.

Verse 19

أَمَّا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ فَلَهُمْ جَنَّاتُ الْمَأْوَىٰ نُزُلًا بِمَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

As for those who believe and do good, they will have the Gardens of ˹Eternal˺ Residence—as an accommodation for what they used to do.

As for those who believed in Allah and did what they were commanded to do, their reward is gardens in which they will dwell and reside in its bliss as a welcoming for them; a reward for what they used to do in the world by obeying Him.

This verse assures those who believe and do good that they will have the Gardens of Eternal Residence as a welcoming and reward for their obedience in the world.

Verse 20

وَأَمَّا الَّذِينَ فَسَقُوا فَمَأْوَاهُمُ النَّارُ ۖ كُلَّمَا أَرَادُوا أَنْ يَخْرُجُوا مِنْهَا أُعِيدُوا فِيهَا وَقِيلَ لَهُمْ ذُوقُوا عَذَابَ النَّارِ الَّذِي كُنْتُمْ بِهِ تُكَذِّبُونَ

But as for those who are rebellious, the Fire will be their home. Whenever they try to escape from it, they will be forced back into it, and will be told, “Taste the Fire’s torment, which you used to deny.”

And as for those who went out from the obedience of Allah and did His disobediences, then their abode is Hell, whenever they want to get out of it, they will be returned to it, and it will be said to them - as a rebuke and scolding -: Taste the punishment of the Fire that you used to deny in the world.

This verse explains that those who disobeyed Allah will have Hell as their abode. They will try to escape but will be forced back and will be told to taste the fire they used to deny.

Verse 21

وَلَنُذِيقَنَّهُمْ مِنَ الْعَذَابِ الْأَدْنَىٰ دُونَ الْعَذَابِ الْأَكْبَرِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَرْجِعُونَ

We will certainly make them taste some of the minor torment ˹in this life˺ before the major torment ˹of the Hereafter˺, so perhaps they will return ˹to the Right Path˺.

And We will surely make these wicked liars taste the lesser punishment of affliction, trials, and calamities in this world before the greater punishment on the Day of Resurrection, where they will be punished in the Hellfire; perhaps they will return and repent from their sins.

Allah warns that He will inflict minor worldly punishments to encourage people to return to the right path before the major punishment of the Hereafter.

Verse 22

وَمَنْ أَظْلَمُ مِمَّنْ ذُكِّرَ بِآيَاتِ رَبِّهِ ثُمَّ أَعْرَضَ عَنْهَا ۚ إِنَّا مِنَ الْمُجْرِمِينَ مُنْتَقِمُونَ

And who does more wrong than the one who is reminded of Allah’s revelations then turns away from them? We will surely inflict punishment upon the wicked.

And no one is more unjust to himself than the one who is reminded by the proofs of Allah, then turns away from all of that, not taking heed of His admonitions, but rather is arrogant about them. Indeed, We are avengers of the criminals who turned away from the verses and proofs of Allah, and did not benefit from them.

The greatest injustice is to be reminded of Allah's signs and then turn away. Allah promises to punish those who reject His guidance.

Verse 23

وَلَقَدْ آتَيْنَا مُوسَى الْكِتَابَ فَلَا تَكُنْ فِي مِرْيَةٍ مِنْ لِقَائِهِ ۖ وَجَعَلْنَاهُ هُدًى لِبَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ

Indeed, We gave the Scripture to Moses—so let there be no doubt ˹O Prophet˺ that you ˹too˺ are receiving revelations—and We made it a guide for the Children of Israel.

And We have given Moses the Torah as We have given you - O Messenger - the Quran, so do not be in doubt of meeting Moses on the Night of Isra and Mi'raj, and We made the Torah a guidance for the Children of Israel, calling them to the truth and to a straight path.

Just as the Torah was given to Moses, the Quran is revealed to Prophet Muhammad. The Quran is a guide, as was the Torah, for the Children of Israel.

Verse 24

وَجَعَلْنَا مِنْهُمْ أَئِمَّةً يَهْدُونَ بِأَمْرِنَا لَمَّا صَبَرُوا ۖ وَكَانُوا بِآيَاتِنَا يُوقِنُونَ

We raised from among them leaders, guiding by Our command, when they patiently endured and firmly believed in Our signs.

And We made from the Children of Israel guides and callers to good, whom people follow, and they call them to the Oneness and worship of Allah alone and obedience to Him. They only attained this high rank when they were patient with the commands of Allah, abandoning His prohibitions, calling to Him, and enduring harm in His cause, and they were certain of the verses and proofs of Allah.

Allah made leaders from among the Children of Israel who guided others by Allah's command, because they were patient, enduring, and believed firmly in His signs.

Verse 25

إِنَّ رَبَّكَ هُوَ يَفْصِلُ بَيْنَهُمْ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ فِيمَا كَانُوا فِيهِ يَخْتَلِفُونَ

Indeed, your Lord will decide between them on the Day of Judgment regarding their differences.

Indeed, your Lord - O Messenger - will judge between the believers and the disbelievers from the Children of Israel and others on the Day of Resurrection with justice in what they differed about in matters of religion, and will recompense each person according to his deeds by admitting the people of Paradise to Paradise and the people of Hell to Hell.

Allah will judge all people on the Day of Judgment regarding their differences, and each will be recompensed justly for their deeds.

Verse 26

أَوَلَمْ يَهْدِ لَهُمْ كَمْ أَهْلَكْنَا مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ مِنَ الْقُرُونِ يَمْشُونَ فِي مَسَاكِنِهِمْ ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ ۖ أَفَلَا يَسْمَعُونَ

Is it not yet clear to them how many peoples We destroyed before them, whose ruins they still pass by? Surely in this are signs. Will they not then listen?

Has it not become clear to these deniers of the Messenger: how many of the previous nations We have destroyed before them? They walk in their dwellings and see them clearly like the people of Hud, Salih, and Lot? Indeed, in that are signs and lessons that can be used to demonstrate the truthfulness of the messengers who came to them, and the falsehood of what they are upon of polytheism. Then will these deniers of the messengers not listen to the admonitions and proofs of Allah and benefit from them?

The ruins of past nations serve as signs, demonstrating the consequences of rejecting messengers and the truth. People should take heed and learn from the past.

Verse 27

أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا أَنَّا نَسُوقُ الْمَاءَ إِلَى الْأَرْضِ الْجُرُزِ فَنُخْرِجُ بِهِ زَرْعًا تَأْكُلُ مِنْهُ أَنْعَامُهُمْ وَأَنْفُسُهُمْ ۖ أَفَلَا يُبْصِرُونَ

Do they not see how We drive rain to parched land, producing ˹various˺ crops from which they and their cattle eat? Will they not then see?

Have not those who deny resurrection after death seen that We drive the water to the dry, hard land that has no vegetation, and We bring forth by it plants of various colors, from which their livestock eat and by which their bodies are nourished, so they live by it? Do they not see these blessings with their own eyes and realize that Allah who did that is able to revive the dead and bring them forth from their graves?

Allah provides for His creation by sending rain to dry land, which brings forth vegetation for people and animals to eat. This is a clear sign of Allah's power and provision.

Verse 28

وَيَقُولُونَ مَتَىٰ هَٰذَا الْفَتْحُ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صَادِقِينَ

They ask ˹mockingly˺, “When is this ˹Day of final˺ Decision, if what you say is true?”

These polytheists with Allah seek to hasten the punishment, saying, 'When will this judgment be that judges between us and you by punishing us according to your claim, if you are truthful in your claim?'

Disbelievers mockingly ask when the Day of Judgment will occur, challenging the truth of the message.

Verse 29

قُلْ يَوْمَ الْفَتْحِ لَا يَنْفَعُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا إِيمَانُهُمْ وَلَا هُمْ يُنْظَرُونَ

Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “On the Day of Decision it will not benefit the disbelievers to believe then, nor will they be delayed ˹from punishment˺.”

Say to them - O Messenger -: On the Day of Judgment when your punishment occurs, and you witness death, the disbelievers will not benefit from their faith, nor will they be given respite to repent and return.

On the Day of Judgment, disbelief will be of no benefit, nor will disbelievers be given respite or the opportunity to repent.

Verse 30

فَأَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ وَانْتَظِرْ إِنَّهُمْ مُنْتَظِرُونَ

So turn away from them, and wait! They too are waiting.

So turn away - O Messenger - from these polytheists and do not pay attention to their denial, and wait for what Allah will do with them. Indeed, they are waiting and watching for misfortunes to befall you, but Allah will disgrace and humiliate them, and give you victory over them. And He has done so, so to Him belongs praise and favor.

Prophet Muhammad is instructed to turn away from those who deny the message and wait for Allah's decree, as Allah will deal with them as they plan and wait to see misfortune befall believers.
