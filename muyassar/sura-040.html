Sura 040

Verse 1

حم

Ḥâ-Mĩm.

(Ha Mim) The discussion on the disjointed letters has preceded in the beginning of Surat Al-Baqarah.

This verse begins with the letters *Ha Mim*. These are disjointed letters whose meaning is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

تَنْزِيلُ الْكِتَابِ مِنَ اللَّهِ الْعَزِيزِ الْعَلِيمِ

The revelation of this Book is from Allah—the Almighty, All-Knowing,

The revelation of the Quran to the Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, is from Allah - the Mighty and Majestic - the Almighty, who has subdued every creature with His might, the All-Knowing of everything.

This verse states that the Quran is a revelation from Allah, who is the Almighty and All-Knowing. This highlights the divine origin of the Quran.

Verse 3

غَافِرِ الذَّنْبِ وَقَابِلِ التَّوْبِ شَدِيدِ الْعِقَابِ ذِي الطَّوْلِ ۖ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ إِلَيْهِ الْمَصِيرُ

the Forgiver of sin and Accepter of repentance, the Severe in punishment, and Infinite in bounty. There is no god ˹worthy of worship˺ except Him. To Him ˹alone˺  is the final return.

The Forgiver of sin for the sinners, and the Accepter of repentance from the repentant, severe in punishment for those who dared to commit sins and did not repent from them, and He, Glory be to Him, is the Possessor of bounty and favor upon His obedient servants. There is no deity worthy of worship except Him. To Him is the destination of all creatures on the Day of Reckoning, where each will be rewarded according to what they deserve.

This verse describes Allah's attributes: He forgives sins, accepts repentance, is severe in punishment, and is generous in bounty. It also affirms that there is no god except Him, and all will return to Him.

Verse 4

مَا يُجَادِلُ فِي آيَاتِ اللَّهِ إِلَّا الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا فَلَا يَغْرُرْكَ تَقَلُّبُهُمْ فِي الْبِلَادِ

None disputes the signs of Allah except the disbelievers, so do not be deceived by their prosperity throughout the land.

None dispute the verses of the Quran and its proofs of the oneness of Allah, and oppose it with falsehood except the deniers who have denied that He is the true God, the only one worthy of worship. So do not be deceived - O Messenger - by their moving about in the lands with various kinds of trade and gains, and the delights and allurements of this world.

This verse states that only disbelievers dispute the signs of Allah. It cautions believers not to be deceived by the temporary prosperity of those who reject faith.

Verse 5

كَذَّبَتْ قَبْلَهُمْ قَوْمُ نُوحٍ وَالْأَحْزَابُ مِنْ بَعْدِهِمْ ۖ وَهَمَّتْ كُلُّ أُمَّةٍ بِرَسُولِهِمْ لِيَأْخُذُوهُ ۖ وَجَادَلُوا بِالْبَاطِلِ لِيُدْحِضُوا بِهِ الْحَقَّ فَأَخَذْتُهُمْ ۖ فَكَيْفَ كَانَ عِقَابِ

Before them, the people of Noah denied ˹the truth˺, as did ˹other˺ enemy forces afterwards. Every community plotted against its prophet to seize him, and argued in falsehood, ˹hoping˺ to discredit the truth with it. So I seized them. And how ˹horrible˺ was My punishment!

The people of Noah and those who came after them from the nations that declared war on the messengers, like Aad and Thamud, denied before these disbelievers, as they resolved to harm them and gathered against them with torture or killing. And every nation of these denying nations intended to kill their messenger and argued with falsehood in order to invalidate the truth with their debate. So I punished them. And how was My punishment a lesson for the creation and an admonition for those who come after them?

This verse recounts how past nations, like the people of Noah, plotted against their prophets and denied the truth. It highlights the severe punishment they faced as a warning.

Verse 6

وَكَذَٰلِكَ حَقَّتْ كَلِمَتُ رَبِّكَ عَلَى الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا أَنَّهُمْ أَصْحَابُ النَّارِ

And so your Lord’s decree has been proven true against the disbelievers—that they will be the inmates of the Fire.

And just as the punishment was justified for the previous nations that denied their messengers, it is justified for those who disbelieve that they are the companions of the Fire.

This verse confirms that Allah's decree of punishment is justified for the disbelievers. They are destined to be inhabitants of the Hellfire.

Verse 7

الَّذِينَ يَحْمِلُونَ الْعَرْشَ وَمَنْ حَوْلَهُ يُسَبِّحُونَ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّهِمْ وَيُؤْمِنُونَ بِهِ وَيَسْتَغْفِرُونَ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا رَبَّنَا وَسِعْتَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ رَحْمَةً وَعِلْمًا فَاغْفِرْ لِلَّذِينَ تَابُوا وَاتَّبَعُوا سَبِيلَكَ وَقِهِمْ عَذَابَ الْجَحِيمِ

Those ˹angels˺ who carry the Throne and those around it glorify the praises of their Lord, have faith in Him, and seek forgiveness for the believers, ˹praying:˺ “Our Lord! You encompass everything in ˹Your˺ mercy and knowledge. So forgive those who repent and follow Your Way, and protect them from the torment of the Hellfire.

Those who bear the Throne of the Most Merciful from the angels, and those around the Throne who surround it, glorify Allah from every deficiency, praise Him as He deserves to be praised, believe in Him with true faith, and ask Him to forgive the believers, saying: Our Lord, You encompass all things in mercy and knowledge, so forgive those who have repented from polytheism and sins and have followed the path that You commanded them to follow, which is Islam, and protect them from the punishment of the Fire and its horrors.

This verse describes the angels who carry and surround Allah's Throne. They glorify Allah, believe in Him, and seek forgiveness for the believers, praying for their protection from Hellfire, acknowledging Allah's all encompassing mercy and knowledge.

Verse 8

رَبَّنَا وَأَدْخِلْهُمْ جَنَّاتِ عَدْنٍ الَّتِي وَعَدْتَهُمْ وَمَنْ صَلَحَ مِنْ آبَائِهِمْ وَأَزْوَاجِهِمْ وَذُرِّيَّاتِهِمْ ۚ إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

Our Lord! Admit them into the Gardens of Eternity which You have promised them, along with the righteous among their parents, spouses, and descendants. You ˹alone˺ are truly the Almighty, All-Wise.

Our Lord, and admit the believers to gardens of perpetual residence which You have promised them and whoever was righteous among their fathers, their spouses and their offspring. Indeed, You are the Exalted in Might, the All-Wise.

Believers pray for themselves and their righteous families to be admitted into the eternal Gardens of Paradise, acknowledging Allah as the Almighty and All-Wise.

Verse 9

وَقِهِمُ السَّيِّئَاتِ ۚ وَمَنْ تَقِ السَّيِّئَاتِ يَوْمَئِذٍ فَقَدْ رَحِمْتَهُ ۚ وَذَٰلِكَ هُوَ الْفَوْزُ الْعَظِيمُ

And protect them from ˹the consequences of their˺ evil deeds. For whoever You protect from the evil of their deeds on that Day will have been shown Your mercy. That is ˹truly˺ the ultimate triumph.”

And protect them from the evil consequences of their sins, so do not take them to account for it, and whom You protect from evil consequences on the Day of Account - You have surely had mercy on him and bestowed upon him salvation from Your torment. And that is the great attainment which no victory is like it.

This verse asks for protection from the consequences of evil deeds, stating that those who are protected by Allah on the Day of Judgment have received His mercy and achieved ultimate triumph.

Verse 10

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا يُنَادَوْنَ لَمَقْتُ اللَّهِ أَكْبَرُ مِنْ مَقْتِكُمْ أَنْفُسَكُمْ إِذْ تُدْعَوْنَ إِلَى الْإِيمَانِ فَتَكْفُرُونَ

Indeed, it will be announced to the disbelievers, “Allah’s contempt for you—as you disbelieved when invited to belief—was far worse than your contempt for one another ˹Today˺.”

Indeed, those who denied that Allah is the true God and directed worship to other than Him, when they witness the horrors of the Fire with their own eyes, they will despise themselves with the utmost disgust. And at that time the keepers of Hell will call to them: 'The loathing of Allah for you in the world - when He asked you to believe in Him and follow His messengers, but you refused - is greater than your hatred for yourselves now, after you have realized that you deserve the wrath and punishment of Allah.'

Disbelievers will be reminded that Allah's contempt for them in this world when they rejected faith is far greater than their self-loathing in the Hereafter.

Verse 11

قَالُوا رَبَّنَا أَمَتَّنَا اثْنَتَيْنِ وَأَحْيَيْتَنَا اثْنَتَيْنِ فَاعْتَرَفْنَا بِذُنُوبِنَا فَهَلْ إِلَىٰ خُرُوجٍ مِنْ سَبِيلٍ

They will plead, “Our Lord! You made us lifeless twice, and gave us life twice. Now we confess our sins. So is there any way out?”

The disbelievers will say: 'Our Lord, You gave us death twice: when we were in the wombs of our mothers as semen before the soul was breathed into us, and when our term in the life of this world ended, and You gave us life twice: in this world, the day we were born, and the day we are resurrected from our graves. So now we confess our past mistakes, is there any way for us to leave the Fire, and that You would return us to the world, so that we can work in Your obedience? But alas that this confession is of no benefit to them.

The disbelievers acknowledge having experienced two deaths and two lives, confessing their sins and asking for a way out, though it will be to no avail.

Verse 12

ذَٰلِكُمْ بِأَنَّهُ إِذَا دُعِيَ اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ كَفَرْتُمْ ۖ وَإِنْ يُشْرَكْ بِهِ تُؤْمِنُوا ۚ فَالْحُكْمُ لِلَّهِ الْعَلِيِّ الْكَبِيرِ

˹They will be told,˺ “˹No!˺ This is because when Allah alone was invoked, you ˹staunchly˺ disbelieved. But when others were associated with Him ˹in worship˺, you ˹readily˺ believed. So ˹Today˺ judgment belongs to Allah ˹alone˺—the Most High, All-Great.”

That is the punishment for you - O disbelievers - because you, when you were called to the Oneness of Allah and sincerity of action for Him, you disbelieved in Him, but if a partner is set for Allah you would believe in it and follow it. So Allah, Glory be to Him, is the judge over His creation, the Just who does not do injustice, He guides whom He wills and misguides whom He wills, and has mercy upon whom He wills and punishes whom He wills, there is no deity worthy of worship except Him who has highness of Self and rank and power, and to Him belongs the pride and grandeur.

The disbelievers are told that their punishment is because they rejected Allah's Oneness but readily believed in false deities. Judgment belongs to Allah alone, the Most High and All-Great.

Verse 13

هُوَ الَّذِي يُرِيكُمْ آيَاتِهِ وَيُنَزِّلُ لَكُمْ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ رِزْقًا ۚ وَمَا يَتَذَكَّرُ إِلَّا مَنْ يُنِيبُ

He is the One Who shows you His signs and sends down ˹rain as˺ a provision for you from the sky. ˹But˺ none will be mindful except those who turn ˹to Him˺.

It is He who shows you - O people - His power with what you see of the great signs indicating the perfection of their Creator and originator, and sends down rain for you from the sky by which you are provided for, and none remembers these signs except he who returns to the obedience of Allah, and is sincere in worship to Him.

Allah shows His signs and provides for humanity through rain from the sky. Only those who turn to Him in obedience and remembrance will truly benefit from these signs.

Verse 14

فَادْعُوا اللَّهَ مُخْلِصِينَ لَهُ الدِّينَ وَلَوْ كَرِهَ الْكَافِرُونَ

So call upon Allah with sincere devotion, even to the dismay of the disbelievers.

So be sincere, O believers, to Allah alone in worship and supplication, and oppose the polytheists in their ways, even if it angers them, and do not mind them.

Believers are commanded to worship Allah alone with sincere devotion, even if it angers the disbelievers.

Verse 15

رَفِيعُ الدَّرَجَاتِ ذُو الْعَرْشِ يُلْقِي الرُّوحَ مِنْ أَمْرِهِ عَلَىٰ مَنْ يَشَاءُ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ لِيُنْذِرَ يَوْمَ التَّلَاقِ

˹He is˺ Highly Exalted in rank, Lord of the Throne. He sends down the revelation by His command to whoever He wills of His servants to warn ˹all˺ of the Day of Meeting—

Indeed, Allah is the Most High, Whose ranks are so high that He has become distinct from His creations, and His status is elevated by it. And He is the Possessor of the Great Throne, and among His mercy to His servants is that He sends messengers to them and sends down revelations with which they live, so that they would have insight regarding their affairs; so that the messengers can warn the servants of Allah, and caution them against the Day of Resurrection in which the first and the last will meet.

Allah is of the highest rank, the Lord of the Throne. He sends down revelation to warn people of the Day of Meeting (Day of Judgement).

Verse 16

يَوْمَ هُمْ بَارِزُونَ ۖ لَا يَخْفَىٰ عَلَى اللَّهِ مِنْهُمْ شَيْءٌ ۚ لِمَنِ الْمُلْكُ الْيَوْمَ ۖ لِلَّهِ الْوَاحِدِ الْقَهَّارِ

the Day all will appear ˹before Allah˺. Nothing about them will be hidden from Him. ˹He will ask,˺ “Who does all authority belong to this Day? To Allah—the One, the Supreme!

On the Day of Resurrection, the creatures will appear before their Lord, and nothing of them or their deeds which they did in the world will be hidden from Allah. Allah Almighty says: To whom belongs the dominion and control on this Day? Then He answers Himself: To Allah, the One and Unique in His names, attributes and actions, the Subduer who has subdued all creatures with His power and might.

On the Day of Judgment, all will appear before Allah, with nothing hidden from Him. All authority belongs to Allah, the One and Supreme.

Verse 17

الْيَوْمَ تُجْزَىٰ كُلُّ نَفْسٍ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ ۚ لَا ظُلْمَ الْيَوْمَ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ سَرِيعُ الْحِسَابِ

Today every soul will be rewarded for what it has done. No injustice Today! Surely Allah is swift in reckoning.”

Today, every soul will be recompensed for what it earned in the world, good or evil. There is no injustice for anyone today, by an increase in his evil deeds or a decrease in his good deeds. Indeed, Allah, the Exalted, is swift in reckoning, so do not deem that Day as slow; for it is near.

This verse describes the Day of Judgment when every soul will be justly recompensed for their deeds. Allah is swift in reckoning, emphasizing the certainty and imminence of this day.

Verse 18

وَأَنْذِرْهُمْ يَوْمَ الْآزِفَةِ إِذِ الْقُلُوبُ لَدَى الْحَنَاجِرِ كَاظِمِينَ ۚ مَا لِلظَّالِمِينَ مِنْ حَمِيمٍ وَلَا شَفِيعٍ يُطَاعُ

Warn them ˹O Prophet˺ of the approaching Day when the hearts will jump into the throats, suppressing distress. The wrongdoers will have neither a close friend nor intercessor to be heard.

And warn - O Messenger - the people of the approaching Day of Judgment, even if they consider it far off, when the hearts of the servants, out of fear of Allah's punishment, will have risen from their chests, reaching their throats, and they will be filled with grief and sorrow. The wrongdoers will have no close friend nor intercessor who will intercede for them with their Lord, and be answered.

This verse warns of the approaching Day of Judgment, where fear will overwhelm the wrongdoers. They will have no close friends or intercessors to help them.

Verse 19

يَعْلَمُ خَائِنَةَ الْأَعْيُنِ وَمَا تُخْفِي الصُّدُورُ

Allah ˹even˺ knows the sly glances of the eyes and whatever the hearts conceal.

Allah, the Exalted, knows what the eyes steal in glances, and what man conceals in his soul, good or evil.

Allah knows everything, including the hidden glances of the eyes and the secrets of the hearts. This emphasizes His comprehensive knowledge and awareness of all that humans do and conceal.

Verse 20

وَاللَّهُ يَقْضِي بِالْحَقِّ ۖ وَالَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ مِنْ دُونِهِ لَا يَقْضُونَ بِشَيْءٍ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ هُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ

And Allah judges with the truth, while those ˹idols˺ they invoke besides Him cannot judge at all. Indeed, Allah ˹alone˺ is the All-Hearing, All-Seeing.

And Allah, the Exalted, judges between people with justice, regarding what they deserve, and those who are worshipped besides Allah from the deities do not judge with anything; due to their inability to do so. Indeed, Allah is the Hearing of what your tongues utter, the Seeing of your actions and deeds, and He will recompense you for them.

Allah judges with truth and justice, unlike the false idols that people worship. He is the All-Hearing and All-Seeing, highlighting His perfect knowledge and ability to judge.

Verse 21

أَوَلَمْ يَسِيرُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ فَيَنْظُرُوا كَيْفَ كَانَ عَاقِبَةُ الَّذِينَ كَانُوا مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ ۚ كَانُوا هُمْ أَشَدَّ مِنْهُمْ قُوَّةً وَآثَارًا فِي الْأَرْضِ فَأَخَذَهُمُ اللَّهُ بِذُنُوبِهِمْ وَمَا كَانَ لَهُمْ مِنَ اللَّهِ مِنْ وَاقٍ

Have they not travelled throughout the land to see what was the end of those ˹destroyed˺ before them? They were far superior in might and ˹richer in˺ monuments throughout the land. But Allah seized them for their sins, and they had no protector from Allah.

Have these deniers of your message - O Messenger - not traveled through the earth, and seen how was the end of the previous nations before them? They were greater than them in strength, and left more traces in the earth, but the intensity of their power and the greatness of their bodies did not benefit them, so Allah seized them with His punishment; because of their disbelief and their earning of sins, and they had no protector from the punishment of Allah to protect them from it, so that he would ward it off from them.

This verse urges reflection on the fate of past nations who were powerful but were destroyed for their sins. They had no protection from Allah's punishment, emphasizing His power and the consequences of disobedience.

Verse 22

ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّهُمْ كَانَتْ تَأْتِيهِمْ رُسُلُهُمْ بِالْبَيِّنَاتِ فَكَفَرُوا فَأَخَذَهُمُ اللَّهُ ۚ إِنَّهُ قَوِيٌّ شَدِيدُ الْعِقَابِ

That was because their messengers used to come to them with clear proofs, but they persisted in disbelief. So Allah seized them. Surely He is All-Powerful, severe in punishment.

That punishment that befell the previous deniers was because of their stance towards the messengers of Allah who came with decisive proofs of the truth of their claim, but they disbelieved in them, and denied them, so Allah seized them with His punishment. Indeed, He, the Exalted, is Powerful, none can overcome Him, Severe in punishment for those who disbelieve in Him and disobey Him.

The destruction of past nations occurred because they rejected the clear proofs brought by the messengers of Allah. Allah's power and severe punishment are highlighted as consequences of disbelief.

Verse 23

وَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا مُوسَىٰ بِآيَاتِنَا وَسُلْطَانٍ مُبِينٍ

Indeed, We sent Moses with Our signs and compelling proof

And We have certainly sent Moses with Our great signs indicating the reality of what he was sent with, and a clear argument and evidence of his truthfulness in his call, and the falsehood of what those to whom he was sent were upon.

This verse introduces the story of Prophet Moses, who was sent with clear signs and compelling proof to guide people.

Verse 24

إِلَىٰ فِرْعَوْنَ وَهَامَانَ وَقَارُونَ فَقَالُوا سَاحِرٌ كَذَّابٌ

to Pharaoh, Hamân, and Korah. But they responded: “Magician! Total liar!”

To Pharaoh, the king of "Egypt", and Haman, his minister, and Qarun, the owner of wealth and treasures, but they denied his message and were arrogant, and said about him: that he is a lying magician, so how does he claim that he was sent as a messenger to the people?

Prophet Moses was sent to Pharaoh, Haman, and Korah, but they rejected him, calling him a magician and a liar. This verse demonstrates the arrogance and denial of those who oppose God's messengers.

Verse 25

فَلَمَّا جَاءَهُمْ بِالْحَقِّ مِنْ عِنْدِنَا قَالُوا اقْتُلُوا أَبْنَاءَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مَعَهُ وَاسْتَحْيُوا نِسَاءَهُمْ ۚ وَمَا كَيْدُ الْكَافِرِينَ إِلَّا فِي ضَلَالٍ

Then, when he came to them with the truth from Us, they said, “Kill the sons of those who believe with him and keep their women.” But the plotting of the disbelievers was only in vain.

So when Moses came to Pharaoh, Haman and Qarun with clear miracles from Us, they did not just oppose and deny them, but they said: kill the sons of those who believed with him, and keep their women for servitude and enslavement. And the schemes of the people of disbelief are only in loss and destruction.

Upon receiving clear signs from God, the disbelievers plotted to kill the sons of the believers and enslave their women. This highlights the futile and destructive nature of disbelief.

Verse 26

وَقَالَ فِرْعَوْنُ ذَرُونِي أَقْتُلْ مُوسَىٰ وَلْيَدْعُ رَبَّهُ ۖ إِنِّي أَخَافُ أَنْ يُبَدِّلَ دِينَكُمْ أَوْ أَنْ يُظْهِرَ فِي الْأَرْضِ الْفَسَادَ

And Pharaoh said, “Let me kill Moses, and let him call upon his Lord! I truly fear that he may change your traditions or cause mischief in the land.”

And Pharaoh said to the chiefs of his people, “Let me kill Moses, and let him call upon his Lord, whom he claims has sent him to us, to prevent him from us. Indeed, I fear that he will change your religion that you are upon, or that he will cause corruption in the land of 'Egypt'.

Pharaoh, fearing Moses' influence, proposed killing him, claiming Moses would disrupt their traditions and cause chaos.

Verse 27

وَقَالَ مُوسَىٰ إِنِّي عُذْتُ بِرَبِّي وَرَبِّكُمْ مِنْ كُلِّ مُتَكَبِّرٍ لَا يُؤْمِنُ بِيَوْمِ الْحِسَابِ

Moses replied, “I seek refuge in my Lord and your Lord from every arrogant person who does not believe in the Day of Reckoning.”

And Moses said to Pharaoh and his assembly, “Indeed, I have sought refuge in my Lord and your Lord - O people - from every arrogant one who does not believe in the Oneness of Allah and obedience to Him, and does not believe in a Day when Allah will hold His creation accountable.”
