Sura 065

Verse 1

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ إِذَا طَلَّقْتُمُ النِّسَاءَ فَطَلِّقُوهُنَّ لِعِدَّتِهِنَّ وَأَحْصُوا الْعِدَّةَ ۖ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ رَبَّكُمْ ۖ لَا تُخْرِجُوهُنَّ مِنْ بُيُوتِهِنَّ وَلَا يَخْرُجْنَ إِلَّا أَنْ يَأْتِينَ بِفَاحِشَةٍ مُبَيِّنَةٍ ۚ وَتِلْكَ حُدُودُ اللَّهِ ۚ وَمَنْ يَتَعَدَّ حُدُودَ اللَّهِ فَقَدْ ظَلَمَ نَفْسَهُ ۚ لَا تَدْرِي لَعَلَّ اللَّهَ يُحْدِثُ بَعْدَ ذَٰلِكَ أَمْرًا

O Prophet! ˹Instruct the believers:˺ When you ˹intend to˺ divorce women, then divorce them with concern for their waiting period, and count it accurately. And fear Allah, your Lord. Do not force them out of their homes ˹during the waiting period˺, nor should they leave—unless they commit a blatant misconduct. These are the limits set by Allah. And whoever transgresses Allah’s limits has truly wronged his own soul. You never know, perhaps Allah will bring about a change ˹of heart˺ later.

O Prophet, when you - you and the believers - intend to divorce women, then divorce them at the beginning of their waiting period - that is, in a period of purity in which no intercourse has occurred, or in an apparent pregnancy - and keep count of the waiting period, so that you may know the time of return if you wish to reconcile with them, and fear Allah, your Lord. Do not expel the divorced women from the houses they dwell in until their waiting period has expired, which is three menstrual cycles for those other than the young, the menopausal, and the pregnant. And it is not permissible for them to leave them on their own, unless they commit a clear, immoral act, such as adultery. And those are the laws of Allah that He has legislated for His servants. And whoever transgresses the laws of Allah has wronged himself and brought himself to destruction. You do not know - O divorcee - perhaps Allah will bring about after that divorce a matter you do not expect, and you reconcile with her.

This verse instructs on the proper way to divorce, emphasizing the importance of timing it with regard to the waiting period ('iddah). It prohibits forcing women out of their homes during this period unless they commit a clear wrong. The verse also highlights that divorce can be a time for reflection, with the possibility of reconciliation.

Verse 2

فَإِذَا بَلَغْنَ أَجَلَهُنَّ فَأَمْسِكُوهُنَّ بِمَعْرُوفٍ أَوْ فَارِقُوهُنَّ بِمَعْرُوفٍ وَأَشْهِدُوا ذَوَيْ عَدْلٍ مِنْكُمْ وَأَقِيمُوا الشَّهَادَةَ لِلَّهِ ۚ ذَٰلِكُمْ يُوعَظُ بِهِ مَنْ كَانَ يُؤْمِنُ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ ۚ وَمَنْ يَتَّقِ اللَّهَ يَجْعَلْ لَهُ مَخْرَجًا

Then when they have ˹almost˺ reached the end of their waiting period, either retain them honourably or separate from them honourably. And call two of your reliable men to witness ˹either way˺—and ˹let the witnesses˺ bear true testimony for ˹the sake of˺ Allah. This is enjoined on whoever has faith in Allah and the Last Day. And whoever is mindful of Allah, He will make a way out for them,

So when the divorced women approach the end of their waiting period, then either retain them with good companionship and spending on them, or part with them with full fulfillment of their rights, without harming them. And call to witness two just men from among you over the reconciliation or separation, and give the testimony - O witnesses - purely for Allah, not for anything else. That which Allah has commanded you is that with which is advised he who believes in Allah and the Last Day. And whoever fears Allah, acts upon what He has commanded him, and avoids what He has forbidden him, He will make for him a way out of every distress, and make easy for him the means of sustenance from where he does not expect or take into account. And whoever puts his trust in Allah, then He is sufficient for him in all his affairs. Indeed, Allah will accomplish His command; nothing escapes Him and nothing He seeks can defeat Him. Allah has made for everything a term to which it reaches its end, and a measure it does not exceed.

This verse explains that at the end of the waiting period, the husband must choose between retaining the wife with kindness or separating with dignity and without harm. Witnesses are required for either decision. The verse also states that those who fear Allah will find solutions to their problems and be provided for by Him.

Verse 3

وَيَرْزُقْهُ مِنْ حَيْثُ لَا يَحْتَسِبُ ۚ وَمَنْ يَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَى اللَّهِ فَهُوَ حَسْبُهُ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ بَالِغُ أَمْرِهِ ۚ قَدْ جَعَلَ اللَّهُ لِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدْرًا

and provide for them from sources they could never imagine. And whoever puts their trust in Allah, then He ˹alone˺ is sufficient for them. Certainly Allah achieves His Will. Allah has already set a destiny for everything.

So when the divorced women approach the end of their waiting period, then either retain them with good companionship and spending on them, or part with them with full fulfillment of their rights, without harming them. And call to witness two just men from among you over the reconciliation or separation, and give the testimony - O witnesses - purely for Allah, not for anything else. That which Allah has commanded you is that with which is advised he who believes in Allah and the Last Day. And whoever fears Allah, acts upon what He has commanded him, and avoids what He has forbidden him, He will make for him a way out of every distress, and make easy for him the means of sustenance from where he does not expect or take into account. And whoever puts his trust in Allah, then He is sufficient for him in all his affairs. Indeed, Allah will accomplish His command; nothing escapes Him and nothing He seeks can defeat Him. Allah has made for everything a term to which it reaches its end, and a measure it does not exceed.

This verse emphasizes that those who trust in Allah will find Him sufficient for their needs. It reiterates that Allah will fulfill His will and that He has set a measure for all things. This underscores the importance of reliance on God during times of difficulty.

Verse 4

وَاللَّائِي يَئِسْنَ مِنَ الْمَحِيضِ مِنْ نِسَائِكُمْ إِنِ ارْتَبْتُمْ فَعِدَّتُهُنَّ ثَلَاثَةُ أَشْهُرٍ وَاللَّائِي لَمْ يَحِضْنَ ۚ وَأُولَاتُ الْأَحْمَالِ أَجَلُهُنَّ أَنْ يَضَعْنَ حَمْلَهُنَّ ۚ وَمَنْ يَتَّقِ اللَّهَ يَجْعَلْ لَهُ مِنْ أَمْرِهِ يُسْرًا

As for your women past the age of menstruation, in case you do not know, their waiting period is three months, and those who have not menstruated as well. As for those who are pregnant, their waiting period ends with delivery. And whoever is mindful of Allah, He will make their matters easy for them.

And the divorced women who have ceased to menstruate due to their old age - if you are in doubt and do not know what is the ruling regarding them, then their waiting period is three months. And the young ones who have not yet menstruated, their waiting period is three months as well. And those with pregnancy from the women, their waiting period is that they give birth to their pregnancies. And whoever fears Allah, and implements His laws, He will make for him ease in his affairs in this world and the Hereafter.

This verse clarifies the waiting period for women who no longer menstruate, those who have not yet menstruated, and those who are pregnant. It indicates that the waiting period for the first two groups is three months and for pregnant women, it is until delivery. It also highlights that fearing Allah leads to ease in one's affairs.

Verse 5

ذَٰلِكَ أَمْرُ اللَّهِ أَنْزَلَهُ إِلَيْكُمْ ۚ وَمَنْ يَتَّقِ اللَّهَ يُكَفِّرْ عَنْهُ سَيِّئَاتِهِ وَيُعْظِمْ لَهُ أَجْرًا

This is the commandment of Allah, which He has revealed to you. And whoever is mindful of Allah, He will absolve them of their sins and reward them immensely.

That which was mentioned regarding the matter of divorce and the waiting period, is the command of Allah that He has revealed to you, O people, that you may act upon it. And whoever fears Allah by being mindful of Him through avoiding His disobedience, and fulfilling his obligations, He will remove his sins from him, give him a great reward in the Hereafter, and enter him into Paradise.

This verse emphasizes that the laws concerning divorce and the waiting period are Allah’s commandments. Those who fear Allah and adhere to His commandments will have their sins forgiven and receive immense rewards in the Hereafter, including entrance to Paradise.

Verse 6

أَسْكِنُوهُنَّ مِنْ حَيْثُ سَكَنْتُمْ مِنْ وُجْدِكُمْ وَلَا تُضَارُّوهُنَّ لِتُضَيِّقُوا عَلَيْهِنَّ ۚ وَإِنْ كُنَّ أُولَاتِ حَمْلٍ فَأَنْفِقُوا عَلَيْهِنَّ حَتَّىٰ يَضَعْنَ حَمْلَهُنَّ ۚ فَإِنْ أَرْضَعْنَ لَكُمْ فَآتُوهُنَّ أُجُورَهُنَّ ۖ وَأْتَمِرُوا بَيْنَكُمْ بِمَعْرُوفٍ ۖ وَإِنْ تَعَاسَرْتُمْ فَسَتُرْضِعُ لَهُ أُخْرَىٰ

Let them live where you live ˹during their waiting period˺, according to your means. And do not harass them to make their stay unbearable. If they are pregnant, then maintain them until they deliver. And if they nurse your child, compensate them, and consult together courteously. But if you fail to reach an agreement, then another woman will nurse ˹the child˺ for the father.

Settle your divorced women during their waiting period in the same manner you yourselves dwell, according to your means and capacity. And do not harm them in order to make it difficult for them in their dwellings. And if your divorced women are pregnant, then spend on them until they give birth. And if they breastfeed your children for you for a fee, then give them their dues. And let some of you instruct others in what is known of generosity and goodwill. And if you do not agree on breastfeeding, then another breastfeeding woman other than the divorced mother will breastfeed for the father.

This verse outlines the rights of divorced women during their waiting period, emphasizing the husband's responsibility to provide housing and financial support. It also covers childcare and breastfeeding arrangements, encouraging mutual agreement and fair compensation.

Verse 7

لِيُنْفِقْ ذُو سَعَةٍ مِنْ سَعَتِهِ ۖ وَمَنْ قُدِرَ عَلَيْهِ رِزْقُهُ فَلْيُنْفِقْ مِمَّا آتَاهُ اللَّهُ ۚ لَا يُكَلِّفُ اللَّهُ نَفْسًا إِلَّا مَا آتَاهَا ۚ سَيَجْعَلُ اللَّهُ بَعْدَ عُسْرٍ يُسْرًا

Let the man of wealth provide according to his means. As for the one with limited resources, let him provide according to whatever Allah has given him. Allah does not require of any soul beyond what He has given it. After hardship, Allah will bring about ease.

Let the husband spend according to what Allah has bestowed upon him, on his divorced wife, and on his child if the husband is affluent in provision. And whoever is restricted in provision, who is poor, let him spend according to what Allah has given him of provision. The poor are not burdened like the wealthy. Allah will bring about ease and wealth after hardship and difficulty.

This verse emphasizes that financial support should be proportional to one's means. Allah does not burden anyone beyond their capacity and promises ease after hardship. This is particularly applicable in the context of divorce and financial obligations.

Verse 8

وَكَأَيِّنْ مِنْ قَرْيَةٍ عَتَتْ عَنْ أَمْرِ رَبِّهَا وَرُسُلِهِ فَحَاسَبْنَاهَا حِسَابًا شَدِيدًا وَعَذَّبْنَاهَا عَذَابًا نُكْرًا

˹Imagine˺ how many societies rebelled against the commandments of their Lord and His messengers, so We called each ˹society˺ to a severe account and subjected them to a horrible punishment.

And many a town rebelled against the command of Allah and the command of His Messenger, and persisted in their transgression and disbelief. So We took them to severe account in this world for their deeds, and We punished them with a great and terrible punishment. So they tasted the evil consequence of their defiance and disbelief, and the consequence of their disbelief was destruction and loss, after which there is no loss.

This verse serves as a warning, highlighting the fate of past societies that disobeyed their Lord and His messengers. They faced severe consequences for their rebellion and disbelief.

Verse 9

So they tasted the evil consequences of their doings, and the outcome of their doings was ˹total˺ loss.

So We took them to severe account in this world for their deeds, and We punished them with a great and terrible punishment. So they tasted the evil consequence of their defiance and disbelief, and the consequence of their disbelief was destruction and loss, after which there is no loss.

This verse reiterates the consequences of disbelief and transgression, leading to loss and destruction. It emphasizes the negative impact of rejecting divine guidance.

Verse 10

أَعَدَّ اللَّهُ لَهُمْ عَذَابًا شَدِيدًا ۖ فَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ يَا أُولِي الْأَلْبَابِ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا ۚ قَدْ أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ إِلَيْكُمْ ذِكْرًا

Allah has ˹also˺ prepared for them a severe punishment. So fear Allah, O people of reason and faith. Allah has indeed revealed to you a Reminder,

Allah has prepared for these people who transgressed, and disobeyed His command and the command of His messengers, a severe punishment. So fear Allah and beware of His wrath, O people of sound minds, who have believed in Allah and His messengers and acted according to His law. Allah has sent down to you, O believers, a reminder with which He reminds you, and alerts you to your share of faith in Allah and acting in obedience to Him. And this reminder is the Messenger who recites to you the verses of Allah, clarifying for you the truth from falsehood; so that He may bring out those who believe in Allah and His Messenger, and act according to what Allah has commanded them and obeyed Him, from the darkness of disbelief into the light of faith. And whoever believes in Allah and does righteous deeds, He will admit him into gardens beneath which rivers flow, wherein they will abide forever. Allah has indeed made good the provision in Paradise for the righteous believer.

This verse warns of a severe punishment for those who disobey and calls on people of reason and faith to fear Allah. It highlights the importance of heeding the reminder (the Quran) sent down by Allah.

Verse 11

رَسُولًا يَتْلُو عَلَيْكُمْ آيَاتِ اللَّهِ مُبَيِّنَاتٍ لِيُخْرِجَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ مِنَ الظُّلُمَاتِ إِلَى النُّورِ ۚ وَمَنْ يُؤْمِنْ بِاللَّهِ وَيَعْمَلْ صَالِحًا يُدْخِلْهُ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا أَبَدًا ۖ قَدْ أَحْسَنَ اللَّهُ لَهُ رِزْقًا

˹and sent˺ a messenger reciting to you Allah’s revelations, making things clear so that He may bring those who believe and do good out of darkness and into light. Verse 12

اللَّهُ الَّذِي خَلَقَ سَبْعَ سَمَاوَاتٍ وَمِنَ الْأَرْضِ مِثْلَهُنَّ يَتَنَزَّلُ الْأَمْرُ بَيْنَهُنَّ لِتَعْلَمُوا أَنَّ اللَّهَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ وَأَنَّ اللَّهَ قَدْ أَحَاطَ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عِلْمًا

Allah is the One Who created seven heavens ˹in layers˺, and likewise for the earth. The ˹divine˺ command descends between them so you may know that Allah is Most Capable of everything and that Allah certainly encompasses all things in ˹His˺ knowledge.

Allah alone is the One who created seven heavens, and created seven earths, and sent down the command of what Allah revealed to His messengers, and what He manages His creation with, between the heavens and the earth; so that you may know, O people, that Allah is over all things Powerful, nothing can make Him helpless, and that Allah has encompassed everything in knowledge, so nothing escapes His knowledge and power.

This verse emphasizes Allah's power and knowledge through His creation of the heavens and the earth, and the divine command that operates within them. It reinforces that Allah encompasses everything in His knowledge and is fully capable of all things.
