Verse 1

الم

Alif-Lãm-Mĩm.

(Alif, Lam, Meem) The discussion on the isolated letters at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah has preceded.

This verse begins with the letters 'Alif, Lam, Meem.' These are isolated letters that appear at the beginning of some Surahs. Their precise meaning is known only to Allah.

Verse 2

غُلِبَتِ الرُّومُ

The Romans have been defeated

The Persians have been defeated by the Romans in the nearest land to Persia, and the Romans will be victorious over the Persians in a period of time not exceeding ten years nor less than three. To Allah, the Most High, belongs the command before the victory of the Romans and after it. And on the day the Romans will be victorious over the Persians, the believers will rejoice in Allah's victory for the Romans over the Persians. And Allah, the Most High, gives victory to whomever He wills, and brings defeat upon whomever He wills. He is the Exalted in Might, who cannot be defeated, the Most Merciful to whomever He wills from his creation. And that was realized, and the Romans defeated the Persians after seven years, and the Muslims rejoiced at that, because the Romans were people of the Book, even if they distorted it.

This verse speaks of a past defeat of the Romans. It foretells that the Romans will be victorious over the Persians. This highlights Allah's control over all affairs.

Verse 6

وَعْدَ اللَّهِ ۖ لَا يُخْلِفُ اللَّهُ وَعْدَهُ وَلَٰكِنَّ أَكْثَرَ النَّاسِ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

˹This is˺ the promise of Allah. ˹And˺ Allah never fails in His promise. But most people do not know.

Allah promised the believers a firm promise that will not be broken, with the victory of the Christian Romans over the pagan Persians. But most of the disbelievers of "Mecca" do not know that what Allah promised is the truth, and they only know the appearances and ornaments of this world, and they are heedless of the affairs of the Hereafter and what benefits them therein, and they do not think about them.

This verse highlights Allah's unwavering promise, contrasting it with people's lack of awareness. It emphasizes the certainty of Allah's word and the ignorance of many who don't grasp it.

This verse points out that people are often engrossed in worldly matters, overlooking the significance of the Hereafter. It stresses that excessive focus on the present life leads to negligence of eternal life.

Verse 8

أَوَلَمْ يَتَفَكَّرُوا فِي أَنْفُسِهِمْ ۗ مَا خَلَقَ اللَّهُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ وَمَا بَيْنَهُمَا إِلَّا بِالْحَقِّ وَأَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى ۗ وَإِنَّ كَثِيرًا مِنَ النَّاسِ بِلِقَاءِ رَبِّهِمْ لَكَافِرُونَ

Have they not reflected upon their own being? Allah only created the heavens and the earth and everything in between for a purpose and an appointed term. Yet most people are truly in denial of the meeting with their Lord!

Have these deniers of Allah's messengers and the meeting with Him not pondered over Allah's creation of them, and that He created them, and they were nothing? Allah did not create the heavens and the earth and what is between them except to establish justice, reward and punishment, and to indicate His oneness and power, and a specified term to which it ends, which is the Day of Resurrection? And many of the people are deniers of meeting their Lord; ignorant that their return will be to Allah after their annihilation, and neglectful of the Hereafter.

This verse encourages reflection on the purpose of creation, which is not without reason. It reminds readers that everything, including the world, has an appointed term, and many deny their eventual return to Allah.

Verse 9

أَوَلَمْ يَسِيرُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ فَيَنْظُرُوا كَيْفَ كَانَ عَاقِبَةُ الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ ۚ كَانُوا أَشَدَّ مِنْهُمْ قُوَّةً وَأَثَارُوا الْأَرْضَ وَعَمَرُوهَا أَكْثَرَ مِمَّا عَمَرُوهَا وَجَاءَتْهُمْ رُسُلُهُمْ بِالْبَيِّنَاتِ ۖ فَمَا كَانَ اللَّهُ لِيَظْلِمَهُمْ وَلَٰكِنْ كَانُوا أَنْفُسَهُمْ يَظْلِمُونَ

Have they not travelled throughout the land to see what was the end of those ˹destroyed˺ before them? They were far superior in might; they cultivated the land and developed it more than these ˹Meccans˺ ever have. Their messengers came to them with clear proofs. Allah would have never wronged them, but it was they who wronged themselves.

Have not these deniers of Allah, heedless of the Hereafter, traveled through the land with contemplation and consideration, to see what was the fate of the nations who denied Allah's messengers, such as 'Aad and Thamud? They were stronger than them in body, and more capable of enjoying life, as they cultivated the land, planted it, built palaces, and lived in them, and they developed their world more than the people of "Mecca" developed their world. But their development did not benefit them, nor did their long duration, and their messengers came to them with clear arguments and shining proofs, but they denied them, so Allah destroyed them, and Allah did not wrong them by that destruction, but they wronged themselves with polytheism and disobedience.

This verse urges people to learn from past civilizations that were destroyed for their disobedience despite their strength. It highlights that past nations’ power did not save them from divine punishment when they rejected their messengers.

Verse 10

Then most evil was the end of the evildoers for denying and mocking the signs of Allah.

Then the end of the evil people, the tyrants and disbelievers, was the worst and ugliest of ends, because they denied Allah and mocked His verses that He revealed to His messengers.

This verse describes the terrible end of those who deny and mock Allah's signs. It serves as a warning that disbelief and ridicule have severe consequences.

Verse 11

Allah alone is the One who creates all creatures, and He alone is able to bring them back again, then to Him all of creation will return, and He will recompense the righteous for their righteousness and the evildoer for their evil.

This verse emphasizes Allah as the sole originator of creation and the one who will resurrect it. It underscores the inevitability of returning to Allah for judgment and accountability.

This verse describes the Day of Judgment when the wicked will be speechless and in despair, without any ability to defend themselves.

Verse 13

This verse states that on the Day of Judgment, there will be no intercessors from the false gods. Those gods whom people worshipped will themselves deny and disown the idolaters.

This verse describes how people will be separated into two groups on the Day of Judgment, the believers and the disbelievers.

Verse 16

This verse describes the fate of those who disbelieve and deny the signs of Allah and the Hereafter. They will be confined to punishment as a consequence of their denial.

Verse 19

Allah brings forth the living from the dead, like the human from the sperm drop and the bird from the egg, and He brings forth the dead from the living, like the sperm drop from the human and the egg from the bird. Allah brings forth the living from the dead and the dead from the living, and revives the earth after its death. This verse reminds that just as He does these things, He will bring people forth from the graves.

Verse 20

One of Allah's signs is that He created humankind from dust. From this origin, humans have multiplied and spread across the earth.

Verse 21

Indeed, in the creation of Allah, there are signs indicating the power of Allah and His Oneness for a people who reflect and ponder.

Another of Allah's signs is that He created spouses for people so that they may find comfort in each other. He has also placed compassion and mercy between them.

Verse 22

The creation of the heavens and the earth and the diversity of languages and colors are also signs of Allah's power, providing lessons for those with knowledge and understanding.

Verse 23

Sleep by night and by day as well as the seeking of Allah's bounty are signs for those who listen and contemplate. Sleep is a time for rest and the day is for seeking provision.

Verse 24

Allah showing the lightning, inspiring hope and fear, and sending rain to revive the earth are signs for people who understand. This shows Allah's power, wisdom and beneficence.

Verse 25

This verse highlights Allah's power through the stability of the heavens and earth. It also speaks of the swift resurrection on the Day of Judgment, when all will rise upon His call.

Verse 26

Verse 27

And Allah alone is the One who begins creation from nothing, then brings it back to life after death. And the return of creation to life after death is easier for Allah than the beginning of their creation, and both are easy for Him. And to Him, Glory be to Him, belongs the highest description in all that He is described with. There is nothing like Him, and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing. And He is the Almighty who cannot be overcome, the Wise in His words, deeds, and management of the affairs of His creation.

Allah is the originator of creation and will resurrect it. Resurrection is as easy for Him as the initial creation. He possesses the highest attributes and is the Almighty and All-Wise.

This is how We make the signs clear for people who understand.

Allah sets forth an example for you - O polytheists - from yourselves: Do any of your slaves and bondswomen share with you in your provision, so that you see yourselves and them as equals in it, fearing them as you fear free partners in the division of your wealth? You would not accept that, so how do you accept it concerning Allah by associating partners from His creation with Him? And with such explanation We make clear the proofs and arguments for those of sound minds who benefit from them.

This verse provides an example from daily life: Just as people wouldn't accept slaves as equal partners, Allah also does not accept partners in worship. This analogy highlights the absurdity of polytheism for those who understand.

Verse 29

بَلِ اتَّبَعَ الَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا أَهْوَاءَهُمْ بِغَيْرِ عِلْمٍ ۖ فَمَنْ يَهْدِي مَنْ أَضَلَّ اللَّهُ ۖ وَمَا لَهُمْ مِنْ نَاصِرِينَ

Rather, the polytheists followed their desires in imitating their fathers without knowledge, so they shared with them in ignorance and misguidance. And no one can guide those whom Allah has caused to stray because of their persistence in disbelief and defiance. And those have no helpers to save them from the punishment of Allah.

Wrongdoers follow their desires without knowledge, thus going astray. Those whom Allah leaves to stray will have no helpers.

Verse 30

فَأَقِمْ وَجْهَكَ لِلدِّينِ حَنِيفًا ۚ فِطْرَتَ اللَّهِ الَّتِي فَطَرَ النَّاسَ عَلَيْهَا ۚ لَا تَبْدِيلَ لِخَلْقِ اللَّهِ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ الدِّينُ الْقَيِّمُ وَلَٰكِنَّ أَكْثَرَ النَّاسِ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

So set your face - O Messenger, you and those who follow you - towards the religion that Allah has prescribed for you, which is Islam, upon which Allah has created mankind. So your remaining on it and your holding fast to it is holding fast to the nature of Allah of belief in Allah alone. There is no altering the creation and religion of Allah. It is the straight path that leads to the pleasure of Allah, the Lord of the worlds, and His Paradise, but most people do not know that what I have commanded you - O Messenger - is the true religion and none other.

This verse urges steadfastness in Islam, the natural way of Allah, which He has instilled in people. It is the Straight Path, although many do not realize it.

Verse 31

مُنِيبِينَ إِلَيْهِ وَاتَّقُوهُ وَأَقِيمُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَلَا تَكُونُوا مِنَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ

˹O believers!˺ Always turn to Him ˹in repentance˺, be mindful of Him, and establish prayers. And do not be polytheists—

And turn back to Allah in repentance and sincerity of deeds for Him, and fear Him by fulfilling commands and avoiding prohibitions, and establish prayer perfectly with its pillars, obligations, and conditions, and do not be of those who associate others with Allah in worship.

This verse calls believers to turn to Allah in repentance, be mindful of Him, establish prayer, and avoid polytheism.

Verse 32

مِنَ الَّذِينَ فَرَّقُوا دِينَهُمْ وَكَانُوا شِيَعًا ۖ كُلُّ حِزْبٍ بِمَا لَدَيْهِمْ فَرِحُونَ

˹like˺ those who have divided their faith and split into sects, each rejoicing in what they have.

And do not be of the polytheists and the people of desires and innovations who changed their religion and altered it, taking some of it and leaving some of it, following their desires, so they became sects and parties, following their leaders, parties, and opinions, helping each other in falsehood, each party rejoicing in what they have, considering themselves to be on the truth and others on falsehood.

Believers are warned not to be like those who divided their religion into sects, each rejoicing in their own beliefs. This emphasizes the importance of unity and adherence to the true faith.

Verse 33

وَإِذَا مَسَّ النَّاسَ ضُرٌّ دَعَوْا رَبَّهُمْ مُنِيبِينَ إِلَيْهِ ثُمَّ إِذَا أَذَاقَهُمْ مِنْهُ رَحْمَةً إِذَا فَرِيقٌ مِنْهُمْ بِرَبِّهِمْ يُشْرِكُونَ

When people are touched with hardship, they cry out to their Lord, turning to Him ˹alone˺. But as soon as He gives them a taste of His mercy, a group of them associates ˹others˺ with their Lord ˹in worship˺,

And when people are afflicted with hardship and calamity, they invoke their Lord, sincerely turning to Him, that He may remove the harm from them. Then, when He has mercy on them and removes the harm from them, a group of them return to associating partners with Allah once again, worshipping besides Allah other deities.

When people face hardship, they turn to Allah alone for help. However, once He relieves their suffering, some of them start associating partners with Him in worship, showing ingratitude.

Verse 34

لِيَكْفُرُوا بِمَا آتَيْنَاهُمْ ۚ فَتَمَتَّعُوا فَسَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ

becoming ungrateful for whatever ˹favours˺ We have given them. So enjoy yourselves, for soon you will know.

So that they may deny what We have given them, and the favor We have bestowed upon them by removing harm, and the passing of hardship from them. So enjoy yourselves, O polytheists, with prosperity and ease in this world. Soon you will know what you will receive of punishment and retribution.

This verse criticizes those who deny Allah's favors after He has helped them. It warns them of the consequences of their ingratitude.

Verse 35

أَمْ أَنْزَلْنَا عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَانًا فَهُوَ يَتَكَلَّمُ بِمَا كَانُوا بِهِ يُشْرِكُونَ

Or have We sent down to them an authority which attests to what they associate ˹with Him˺?

Or have We sent down to these polytheists a clear proof and a decisive book, speaking to the validity of their polytheism and their disbelief in Allah and His signs?

This verse questions whether the polytheists have any valid authority or proof for associating others with Allah. It highlights the lack of evidence for their beliefs.

Verse 36

وَإِذَا أَذَقْنَا النَّاسَ رَحْمَةً فَرِحُوا بِهَا ۖ وَإِنْ تُصِبْهُمْ سَيِّئَةٌ بِمَا قَدَّمَتْ أَيْدِيهِمْ إِذَا هُمْ يَقْنَطُونَ

And when We let people taste mercy from Us, such as health, well-being, and ease, they rejoice in it with an arrogant and boastful joy, not the joy of gratitude. And if they are afflicted with illness, poverty, fear, and distress because of their sins and disobedience, they immediately despair of its removal, and this is the nature of most people in prosperity and adversity.

When people are blessed with good fortune, they often become arrogant. Yet, when hardship befalls them due to their actions, they quickly despair, revealing their lack of faith and patience.

Verse 37

أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا أَنَّ اللَّهَ يَبْسُطُ الرِّزْقَ لِمَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيَقْدِرُ ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ لِقَوْمٍ يُؤْمِنُونَ

Have they not seen that Allah gives abundant or limited provisions to whoever He wills? Surely in this are signs for people who believe.

Do they not know that Allah extends provision to whom He wills as a test, to see if they will be grateful or ungrateful? And He restricts it for whom He wills as a test, to see if they will be patient or impatient? Indeed, in that expansion and restriction are signs for a people who believe in Allah and know His wisdom and mercy.

This verse reminds people that Allah is the One who controls the distribution of provisions. He gives abundantly to some and restricts for others as a test of their gratitude and patience. This is a sign for believers.

Verse 38

فَآتِ ذَا الْقُرْبَىٰ حَقَّهُ وَالْمِسْكِينَ وَابْنَ السَّبِيلِ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ خَيْرٌ لِلَّذِينَ يُرِيدُونَ وَجْهَ اللَّهِ ۖ وَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ

So give, O believer, to your relative his due of kinship ties and charity and all other good deeds, and give to the poor and needy who have been cut off from their resources, from zakat and charity. That giving is better for those who desire with their deeds the face of Allah, and those who do these deeds and other good deeds, those are the successful ones who have won the reward of Allah and escaped His punishment.

This verse encourages believers to give relatives their due rights, as well as to help the poor and needy travelers. These acts of charity and kindness are rewarded by Allah and lead to success.

Verse 39

وَمَا آتَيْتُمْ مِنْ رِبًا لِيَرْبُوَ فِي أَمْوَالِ النَّاسِ فَلَا يَرْبُو عِنْدَ اللَّهِ ۖ وَمَا آتَيْتُمْ مِنْ زَكَاةٍ تُرِيدُونَ وَجْهَ اللَّهِ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُضْعِفُونَ

Whatever loans you give, ˹only˺ seeking interest at the expense of people’s wealth will not increase with Allah. But whatever charity you give, ˹only˺ seeking the pleasure of Allah—it is they whose reward will be multiplied.

And what you give as a loan of money with the intention of usury, seeking to increase that loan so that it may increase and grow in people's wealth, it does not increase with Allah. Rather, He erases and nullifies it. And what you give of zakat and charity to those who deserve it, seeking the pleasure of Allah and seeking His reward, this is what Allah accepts and multiplies for you many times over.

Loans given with the intention of usury (interest) do not increase with Allah, rather, Allah nullifies them. However, charity given solely for the sake of Allah is accepted by Him and will be multiplied.

Verse 40

It is Allah Who created you, then gives you provisions, then will cause you to die, and then will bring you back to life. Can any of your associate-gods do any of this? Glorified and Exalted is He above what they associate with Him ˹in worship˺!

Allah alone is the One who created you, O people, then provided for you in this life, then will cause you to die when your terms end, then will resurrect you from the graves alive for the reckoning and the recompense. Is there any of your partners who can do any of that? Exalted and glorified is Allah above the polytheism of these polytheists.

This verse emphasizes that Allah alone is the Creator, the Provider, the One who causes death, and the One who will resurrect. It challenges the polytheists by asking if any of their gods can do any of these.

Verse 41

ظَهَرَ الْفَسَادُ فِي الْبَرِّ وَالْبَحْرِ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ أَيْدِي النَّاسِ لِيُذِيقَهُمْ بَعْضَ الَّذِي عَمِلُوا لَعَلَّهُمْ يَرْجِعُونَ

Corruption has spread on land and sea as a result of what people’s hands have done, so that Allah may cause them to taste ˹the consequences of˺ some of their deeds and perhaps they might return ˹to the Right Path˺.

Corruption has appeared in the land and sea, such as drought, lack of rainfall, and the abundance of diseases and epidemics; and that is because of the sins that mankind commits; so that He may inflict upon them the punishment of some of their deeds that they did in this world; so that they may repent to Allah - Exalted is He - and turn away from sins, and thus their conditions are rectified, and their affairs are straightened.

Corruption and disorder on land and sea are a result of human actions. Allah allows people to experience the consequences of their deeds so that they may turn back to Him and mend their ways.

Verse 42

Say - O Messenger - to those who deny what you have brought: Travel throughout the earth for contemplation and reflection, and see what was the end of the previous nations who denied, such as the people of Noah, 'Ad, and Thamud. You will find their end to be the worst of ends, and their destination to be the worst of destinations. Most of them were polytheists with Allah.

This verse urges people to reflect on the history of past civilizations that were destroyed because of their disbelief. It points out that most of them were polytheists, associating partners with God.

Verse 43

فَأَقِمْ وَجْهَكَ لِلدِّينِ الْقَيِّمِ مِنْ قَبْلِ أَنْ يَأْتِيَ يَوْمٌ لَا مَرَدَّ لَهُ مِنَ اللَّهِ ۖ يَوْمَئِذٍ يَصَّدَّعُونَ

So be steadfast in the Upright Faith ˹O Prophet˺, before the coming of a Day from Allah that cannot be averted. On that Day the people will be divided:

So direct your face - O Messenger - towards the upright religion, which is Islam, implementing its commands and avoiding its prohibitions, and adhere to it before the coming of the Day of Resurrection. When that Day comes, which no one can repel, the creatures will be scattered in differing groups, so that they will be shown their deeds.

The verse instructs the Prophet to remain steadfast in Islam before the Day of Judgment comes. On that day, people will be divided based on their beliefs and actions.

Verse 44

مَنْ كَفَرَ فَعَلَيْهِ كُفْرُهُ ۖ وَمَنْ عَمِلَ صَالِحًا فَلِأَنْفُسِهِمْ يَمْهَدُونَ

Whoever disbelieves, then upon him is the penalty of his disbelief, which is his eternity in the Fire, and whoever believes and does righteous deeds, then for themselves they are preparing the abodes of Paradise, due to their adherence to the obedience of their Lord.

Those who reject faith will face the consequences of their disbelief. In contrast, those who do good deeds will be preparing a place for themselves in Paradise.

Verse 45

لِيَجْزِيَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ مِنْ فَضْلِهِ ۚ إِنَّهُ لَا يُحِبُّ الْكَافِرِينَ

so that He may ˹generously˺ reward those who believe and do good, out of His grace. He truly does not like the disbelievers.

That Allah may reward those who believed in Allah and His Messenger and did righteous deeds from His bounty and favor. Indeed, He does not love the disbelievers due to His displeasure and anger towards them.

Allah will reward the believers who do good deeds out of His grace. He does not like those who disbelieve because of their rejection of truth.

Verse 46

وَمِنْ آيَاتِهِ أَنْ يُرْسِلَ الرِّيَاحَ مُبَشِّرَاتٍ وَلِيُذِيقَكُمْ مِنْ رَحْمَتِهِ وَلِتَجْرِيَ الْفُلْكُ بِأَمْرِهِ وَلِتَبْتَغُوا مِنْ فَضْلِهِ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ

And one of His signs is that He sends the winds, ushering in good news ˹of rain˺ so that He may give you a taste of His mercy, and that ships may sail by His command, and that you may seek His bounty, and perhaps you will be grateful.

And among the signs of Allah, indicating that He is the only true God, without partner, and of His great power, is the sending of the winds before the rain, as bearers of glad tidings by stirring up the clouds, so that souls rejoice at that, and that He may cause you to taste of His mercy by sending down the rain by which the lands and the servants are revived, and that ships may sail in the sea by the command of Allah and His will, and that you may seek His bounty through trade and other means; Allah did that so that you may thank Him for His blessings and worship Him alone.

This verse highlights some of Allah's signs: He sends winds to bring rain, allows ships to sail, and provides means for people to seek His bounty. These blessings should encourage gratitude.

Verse 47

وَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا مِنْ قَبْلِكَ رُسُلًا إِلَىٰ قَوْمِهِمْ فَجَاءُوهُمْ بِالْبَيِّنَاتِ فَانْتَقَمْنَا مِنَ الَّذِينَ أَجْرَمُوا ۖ وَكَانَ حَقًّا عَلَيْنَا نَصْرُ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

And We have already sent before you - O Messenger - messengers to their people as bringers of good tidings and as warners, calling them to monotheism, and warning them against polytheism. Then, they came to them with miracles and clear proofs, but most of them disbelieved in their Lord, so We took vengeance on those who earned evil deeds among them, and We destroyed them, and We gave victory to the believers, the followers of the messengers, and that is what We will do with those who deny you if they continue in their denial of you, and do not believe.

Allah sent messengers to their respective peoples with clear proofs. Those who persisted in wickedness were punished, while the believers were supported, showing Allah's promise to help those who believe.

Verse 48

اللَّهُ الَّذِي يُرْسِلُ الرِّيَاحَ فَتُثِيرُ سَحَابًا فَيَبْسُطُهُ فِي السَّمَاءِ كَيْفَ يَشَاءُ وَيَجْعَلُهُ كِسَفًا فَتَرَى الْوَدْقَ يَخْرُجُ مِنْ خِلَالِهِ ۖ فَإِذَا أَصَابَ بِهِ مَنْ يَشَاءُ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ إِذَا هُمْ يَسْتَبْشِرُونَ

It is Allah Who sends the winds, which then stir up ˹vapour, forming˺ clouds, which He then spreads out in the sky or piles up into masses as He wills, from which you see rain come forth. Then as soon as He causes it to fall on whoever He wills of His servants, they rejoice,

Allah - Exalted is He - is the One Who sends the winds, which stir up clouds laden with water, then Allah spreads it in the sky as He wills, and makes it separate pieces, and you see the rain coming forth from within the clouds, so when Allah directs it to His servants, they rejoice and are glad that Allah has directed that to them.

Allah sends the winds, forms clouds, and brings down rain. When it rains, people rejoice, acknowledging it as a blessing from Him.

Verse 49

وَإِنْ كَانُوا مِنْ قَبْلِ أَنْ يُنَزَّلَ عَلَيْهِمْ مِنْ قَبْلِهِ لَمُبْلِسِينَ

although they had utterly lost hope just before it was sent down to them.

And indeed they were, before the sending down of the rain, in despair and hopelessness because of its withholding from them.

Before the rain comes, people were hopeless, but they rejoice when Allah sends the rain, highlighting the change in their state.

Verse 50

فَانْظُرْ إِلَىٰ آثَارِ رَحْمَتِ اللَّهِ كَيْفَ يُحْيِي الْأَرْضَ بَعْدَ مَوْتِهَا ۚ إِنَّ ذَٰلِكَ لَمُحْيِي الْمَوْتَىٰ ۖ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

So look - O observer - with contemplation and reflection at the effects of rain on plants, crops, and trees, how Allah revives the earth with it after its death, and He causes it to grow and be green. Indeed, the One who was able to revive this earth is surely the One who will revive the dead, and He is over all things Powerful; nothing is beyond His ability.

This verse urges contemplation on how Allah revives the earth after it has died. Just as He revives the earth, He is also able to bring back the dead, showing His power and capability.

Verse 51

وَلَئِنْ أَرْسَلْنَا رِيحًا فَرَأَوْهُ مُصْفَرًّا لَظَلُّوا مِنْ بَعْدِهِ يَكْفُرُونَ

And if We were to send a destructive wind upon their crops and vegetation, and they saw their vegetation had become ruined by that wind, and it turned yellow after being green, they would surely, after seeing it, disbelieve in Allah and deny His favors.

This verse highlights human ingratitude. If people face hardship, like ruined crops, they tend to forget previous blessings and deny Allah's favors.

Verse 52

فَإِنَّكَ لَا تُسْمِعُ الْمَوْتَىٰ وَلَا تُسْمِعُ الصُّمَّ الدُّعَاءَ إِذَا وَلَّوْا مُدْبِرِينَ

So, O Messenger, you cannot make those whose hearts are dead hear, or those who have blocked their ears from hearing the truth. So do not fret or grieve over the disbelief of these polytheists in you, for they are like the deaf and the dead; they do not hear, nor do they feel, even if they are present. So how about if they are absent from you and turned away?

This verse uses the metaphor of the dead and deaf to illustrate those who reject the truth. The Prophet cannot make those with closed hearts hear or believe.

Verse 53

وَمَا أَنْتَ بِهَادِ الْعُمْيِ عَنْ ضَلَالَتِهِمْ ۖ إِنْ تُسْمِعُ إِلَّا مَنْ يُؤْمِنُ بِآيَاتِنَا فَهُمْ مُسْلِمُونَ

And you, O Messenger, are not a guide for those whom Allah has blinded from the path of guidance. You can only make hear with beneficial hearing those who believe in Our verses, for they are submissive and obedient to Allah’s command.

Similar to the previous verse, this verse explains that only those who willingly submit to Allah's revelations can be guided. The Prophet cannot force guidance on those who are spiritually blind.

Verse 54

اللَّهُ الَّذِي خَلَقَكُمْ مِنْ ضَعْفٍ ثُمَّ جَعَلَ مِنْ بَعْدِ ضَعْفٍ قُوَّةً ثُمَّ جَعَلَ مِنْ بَعْدِ قُوَّةٍ ضَعْفًا وَشَيْبَةً ۚ يَخْلُقُ مَا يَشَاءُ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيمُ الْقَدِيرُ

It is Allah Who created you in a state of weakness, then developed ˹your˺ weakness into strength, then developed ˹your˺ strength into weakness and old age. He creates whatever He wills. For He is the All-Knowing, Most Capable.

Allah is the One who created you from a weak, insignificant fluid, which is the sperm, then after the weakness of childhood, He granted you the strength of manhood, then after this strength, He granted you the weakness of old age and decrepitude. Allah creates what He wills of weakness and strength, and He is the All-Knowing of His creation, the All-Powerful over all things.

This verse describes the stages of human life from weakness to strength, then back to weakness. It emphasizes Allah's power and knowledge in creating these changes.

Verse 55

وَيَوْمَ تَقُومُ السَّاعَةُ يُقْسِمُ الْمُجْرِمُونَ مَا لَبِثُوا غَيْرَ سَاعَةٍ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ كَانُوا يُؤْفَكُونَ

And on the Day the Hour will arrive, the wicked will swear that they did not stay ˹in this world˺ more than an hour. In this way they were always deluded ˹in the world˺.

And on the Day the Hour arrives and Allah resurrects the creation from their graves, the polytheists will swear that they did not remain in the world but for a short period of time. They lied in their oath, just as they used to lie in the world, denying the truth that the messengers came with.

On the Day of Judgment, the wicked will falsely claim they stayed in the world for only a short time, illustrating their delusions and denial.

Verse 56

وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْعِلْمَ وَالْإِيمَانَ لَقَدْ لَبِثْتُمْ فِي كِتَابِ اللَّهِ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ الْبَعْثِ ۖ فَهَٰذَا يَوْمُ الْبَعْثِ وَلَٰكِنَّكُمْ كُنْتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ

But those gifted with knowledge and faith will say ˹to them˺, “You did actually stay—as destined by Allah—until the Day of Resurrection. So here is the Day of Resurrection ˹which you denied˺! But you did not know ˹it was true˺.”

And those who were given knowledge and faith in Allah from among the angels, prophets, and believers will say: “You have remained in what Allah has decreed in His knowledge, from the day you were created until you were resurrected. So, this is the Day of Resurrection, but you did not know, so you denied it in the world, and you rejected it as falsehood.”

Those with knowledge and faith will refute the wicked's claims, reminding them that they stayed as long as Allah had decreed until the Day of Resurrection.

Verse 57

فَيَوْمَئِذٍ لَا يَنْفَعُ الَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا مَعْذِرَتُهُمْ وَلَا هُمْ يُسْتَعْتَبُونَ

So on that Day the wrongdoers’ excuses will not benefit them, nor will they be allowed to appease ˹their Lord˺.

On the Day of Resurrection, the wrongdoers will not be benefited by their excuses, nor will they be asked to please Allah by repentance and obedience. Rather, they will be punished for their misdeeds and sins.

On the Day of Judgment, the wrongdoers' excuses will be futile and they will not be given another chance to please Allah. This is a final judgment for their disbelief and actions.

Verse 58

وَلَقَدْ ضَرَبْنَا لِلنَّاسِ فِي هَٰذَا الْقُرْآنِ مِنْ كُلِّ مَثَلٍ ۚ وَلَئِنْ جِئْتَهُمْ بِآيَةٍ لَيَقُولَنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا إِنْ أَنْتُمْ إِلَّا مُبْطِلُونَ

We have certainly set forth every ˹kind of˺ lesson for people in this Quran. And no matter what sign you bring to them ˹O Prophet˺, the disbelievers will definitely say ˹to the believers˺, “You are only a people of falsehood.”

And We have certainly set forth for people in this Quran every kind of example in order to establish proof against them and affirm the Oneness of Allah, Glory and Exalted be He. And if you, O Messenger, were to come to them with any proof that indicates your truthfulness, those who disbelieve in you will surely say: “You, O Messenger and your followers, are nothing but falsifiers in what you bring us of matters.”

Despite the clear lessons and signs in the Quran, disbelievers will accuse believers of falsehood. This highlights their stubborn refusal to accept the truth.

Verse 59

كَذَٰلِكَ يَطْبَعُ اللَّهُ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِ الَّذِينَ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

This is how Allah seals the hearts of those unwilling to know ˹the truth˺.

And like that seal, Allah seals the hearts of those who do not know the truth of what you bring them, O Messenger, from Allah, of these lessons and clear signs.

Allah seals the hearts of those who are unwilling to accept the truth. This emphasizes the consequence of rejecting guidance.

Verse 60

فَاصْبِرْ إِنَّ وَعْدَ اللَّهِ حَقٌّ ۖ وَلَا يَسْتَخِفَّنَّكَ الَّذِينَ لَا يُوقِنُونَ

So be patient, for the promise of Allah certainly is true. And do not be disturbed by those who have no sure faith.

So be patient, O Messenger, with what befalls you from the harm of your people and their denial of you. Indeed, what Allah has promised you of victory, empowerment, and reward is true, without a doubt. And let not those who do not have certainty in the appointment and do not believe in the resurrection and the recompense provoke you from your religion.

This verse encourages patience for the Prophet and reminds him that Allah's promise is true. It also advises him to not be disturbed by the lack of faith from those who do not believe.
