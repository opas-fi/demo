Sura 033

Verse 1

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ اتَّقِ اللَّهَ وَلَا تُطِعِ الْكَافِرِينَ وَالْمُنَافِقِينَ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَانَ عَلِيمًا حَكِيمًا

O Prophet! ˹Always˺ be mindful of Allah, and do not yield to the disbelievers and the hypocrites. Indeed, Allah is All-Knowing, All-Wise.

O Prophet, persevere in fearing Allah by acting upon His commands and avoiding His prohibitions, and let the believers follow your example, for they are more in need of that than you. And do not obey the disbelievers and the hypocrites. Indeed, Allah is ever Knowing of all things, Wise in His creation, command, and planning.

This verse advises Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) to remain mindful of Allah and not to obey disbelievers and hypocrites. It emphasizes Allah’s knowledge and wisdom.

Verse 2

Follow what is revealed to you from your Lord. Surely Allah is All-Aware of what you ˹all˺ do.

And follow what is revealed to you from your Lord of the Quran and the Sunnah. Indeed, Allah is fully aware of all that you do and will recompense you for it. Nothing of that is hidden from Him.

This verse instructs the Prophet (peace be upon him) to follow the divine revelations from Allah, emphasizing that Allah is aware of all actions.

Verse 3

And put your trust in Allah, for Allah is sufficient as a Trustee of Affairs.

And rely upon your Lord, and entrust all your affairs to Him, and sufficient is He as a Guardian for whoever relies upon Him and turns to Him in repentance.

This verse encourages reliance on Allah and trusting Him as the ultimate guardian and disposer of affairs.

Verse 4

مَا جَعَلَ اللَّهُ لِرَجُلٍ مِنْ قَلْبَيْنِ فِي جَوْفِهِ ۚ وَمَا جَعَلَ أَزْوَاجَكُمُ اللَّائِي تُظَاهِرُونَ مِنْهُنَّ أُمَّهَاتِكُمْ ۚ وَمَا جَعَلَ أَدْعِيَاءَكُمْ أَبْنَاءَكُمْ ۚ ذَٰلِكُمْ قَوْلُكُمْ بِأَفْوَاهِكُمْ ۖ وَاللَّهُ يَقُولُ الْحَقَّ وَهُوَ يَهْدِي السَّبِيلَ

Allah does not place two hearts in any person’s chest. Nor does He regard your wives as ˹unlawful for you like˺ your real mothers, ˹even˺ if you say they are. Nor does He regard your adopted children as your real children. These are only your baseless assertions. But Allah declares the truth, and He ˹alone˺ guides to the ˹Right˺ Way.

Allah has not made for any man two hearts in his chest, nor has He made your wives whom you declare to be like your mothers (in prohibition) (and 'Zihar' is when a man says to his wife, 'You are to me like the back of my mother,' and this used to be considered a divorce in the pre-Islamic era, so Allah clarified that a wife does not become a mother in any situation). And Allah has not made adopted sons actual sons in the law, rather Zihar and adoption have no reality in perpetual prohibition. So a wife from whom Zihar is made is not like a mother in prohibition, nor is lineage established through adoption from a person saying to an adopted one, 'This is my son.' It is speech from the mouth that has no reality and is not taken into account, and Allah, the Exalted, says the truth and clarifies His path for His servants and guides them to the path of righteousness.

This verse clarifies that Allah has not given any person two hearts, nor does saying your wife is like your mother make her so, and adopted children are not like real children. Allah clarifies the truth and guides to the right path.

Verse 5

ادْعُوهُمْ لِآبَائِهِمْ هُوَ أَقْسَطُ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ ۚ فَإِنْ لَمْ تَعْلَمُوا آبَاءَهُمْ فَإِخْوَانُكُمْ فِي الدِّينِ وَمَوَالِيكُمْ ۚ وَلَيْسَ عَلَيْكُمْ جُنَاحٌ فِيمَا أَخْطَأْتُمْ بِهِ وَلَٰكِنْ مَا تَعَمَّدَتْ قُلُوبُكُمْ ۚ وَكَانَ اللَّهُ غَفُورًا رَحِيمًا

Let your adopted children keep their family names. That is more just in the sight of Allah. But if you do not know their fathers, then they are ˹simply˺ your fellow believers and close associates. There is no blame on you for what you do by mistake, but ˹only˺ for what you do intentionally. And Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

Ascribe your adopted children to their fathers; that is more just in the sight of Allah. But if you do not know their true fathers, then they are your brothers in religion and your allies in it. And there is no blame upon you for what you mistakenly did, but Allah will hold you accountable if you do so intentionally. And Allah is Forgiving to those who err, Merciful to those who repent from their sins.

This verse instructs that adopted children should be known by their biological fathers' names. If the fathers are unknown, they are considered as brothers in faith. Mistakes are forgiven, but intentional wrongdoings are not. Allah is All-Forgiving and Most Merciful.

Verse 6

النَّبِيُّ أَوْلَىٰ بِالْمُؤْمِنِينَ مِنْ أَنْفُسِهِمْ ۖ وَأَزْوَاجُهُ أُمَّهَاتُهُمْ ۗ وَأُولُو الْأَرْحَامِ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلَىٰ بِبَعْضٍ فِي كِتَابِ اللَّهِ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَالْمُهَاجِرِينَ إِلَّا أَنْ تَفْعَلُوا إِلَىٰ أَوْلِيَائِكُمْ مَعْرُوفًا ۚ كَانَ ذَٰلِكَ فِي الْكِتَابِ مَسْطُورًا

The Prophet has a stronger affinity to the believers than they do themselves. And his wives are their mothers. As ordained by Allah, blood relatives are more entitled ˹to inheritance˺ than ˹other˺ believers and immigrants, unless you ˹want to˺ show kindness to your ˹close˺ associates ˹through bequest˺. This is decreed in the Record.

The Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, is more worthy of the believers and closer to them than their own selves in matters of religion and worldly affairs. And the wives of the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, are as inviolable to his nation as their own mothers, so it is not permissible to marry the wives of the Messenger, peace and blessings be upon him, after him. And blood relatives among Muslims are more entitled to inherit from one another in Allah’s rule and law than inheritance through faith and emigration (and the Muslims in the beginning of Islam used to inherit through emigration and faith without blood relation, then that was abrogated by the verse of inheritance), unless you do - O Muslims - a good deed to those who are not heirs through support, righteousness, kinship, beneficence, and bequest. This mentioned ruling has been decreed and written in the Preserved Tablet, so it is obligatory for you to act according to it. And in the verse is the obligation of the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, being more beloved to a servant than his own self, and the obligation of complete submission to him, and in it is the obligation of respecting the mothers of the believers, his wives, peace and blessings be upon him, and that whoever insults them has brought upon himself loss.

This verse highlights that the Prophet (peace be upon him) has a stronger connection with believers than they do with themselves, and his wives are regarded as mothers of the believers. Blood relatives have priority in inheritance, except for acts of kindness towards others. These rules are ordained by Allah.

Verse 7

وَإِذْ أَخَذْنَا مِنَ النَّبِيِّينَ مِيثَاقَهُمْ وَمِنْكَ وَمِنْ نُوحٍ وَإِبْرَاهِيمَ وَمُوسَىٰ وَعِيسَى ابْنِ مَرْيَمَ ۖ وَأَخَذْنَا مِنْهُمْ مِيثَاقًا غَلِيظًا

This verse highlights the covenant Allah took from the prophets, including Noah, Abraham, Moses, and Jesus, and Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon them all), emphasizing their responsibility to deliver the divine message faithfully.

Verse 8

لِيَسْأَلَ الصَّادِقِينَ عَنْ صِدْقِهِمْ ۚ وَأَعَدَّ لِلْكَافِرِينَ عَذَابًا أَلِيمًا

(Allah took that covenant from those messengers) to question the messengers about what their nations responded to them, so Allah will reward the believers with Paradise, and He has prepared for the disbelievers on the Day of Resurrection a severe punishment in Hell.

Allah will question the messengers about their delivery of the truth and their people’s response. This verse also warns of a severe punishment for those who disbelieve.

Verse 9

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اذْكُرُوا نِعْمَةَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ إِذْ جَاءَتْكُمْ جُنُودٌ فَأَرْسَلْنَا عَلَيْهِمْ رِيحًا وَجُنُودًا لَمْ تَرَوْهَا ۚ وَكَانَ اللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ بَصِيرًا

O believers, remember the favor of Allah the Exalted which He bestowed upon you in "Medina" during the days of the Battle of the Confederates - which is the Battle of the Trench - when the polytheists gathered against you from outside "Medina", and the Jews and the hypocrites from "Medina" and its surroundings, so they surrounded you, then We sent upon the confederates a severe wind that uprooted their tents and threw their pots, and We sent angels from the sky that you did not see, and terror fell into their hearts. And Allah is ever Seeing of what you do, nothing of that is hidden from Him.

This verse reminds believers of Allah’s favor during the Battle of the Trench, where He sent a wind and unseen forces to help them against their enemies.

Verse 10

إِذْ جَاءُوكُمْ مِنْ فَوْقِكُمْ وَمِنْ أَسْفَلَ مِنْكُمْ وَإِذْ زَاغَتِ الْأَبْصَارُ وَبَلَغَتِ الْقُلُوبُ الْحَنَاجِرَ وَتَظُنُّونَ بِاللَّهِ الظُّنُونَا

˹Remember˺ when they came at you from east and west, when your eyes grew wild ˹in horror˺ and your hearts jumped into your throats, and you entertained ˹conflicting˺ thoughts about Allah.

Remember when they came upon you from above you, from the upper part of the valley from the direction of the east, and from below you from the bottom of the valley from the direction of the west, and when the eyes were staring in extreme bewilderment and astonishment, and the hearts reached the throats due to the intensity of terror, and despair overcame the hypocrites, and the rumors increased, and you were thinking evil thoughts about Allah that He would not support His religion, nor exalt His word.

This verse describes the intense fear and confusion during the Battle of the Trench, when enemies attacked from all directions, testing the believers' faith.

Verse 11

هُنَالِكَ ابْتُلِيَ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ وَزُلْزِلُوا زِلْزَالًا شَدِيدًا

In that difficult situation, the faith of the believers was tested, and the people were examined, and the believer was distinguished from the hypocrite, and they were severely shaken by fear and anxiety; so that their faith would become clear and their certainty would increase.

The difficult circumstances of the Battle of the Trench served as a test of the believers' faith, shaking them severely but ultimately strengthening their certainty.

Verse 12

This verse reveals the doubts of the hypocrites, who claimed that Allah's and His Messenger’s promises of victory were deceptive.

Verse 13

And ˹remember˺ when a group of them said, “O people of Yathrib! There is no point in you staying ˹here˺, so retreat!” Another group of them asked the Prophet’s permission ˹to leave˺, saying, “Our homes are vulnerable,” while ˹in fact˺ they were not vulnerable. They only wished to flee.

And remember - O Prophet - the saying of a group of the hypocrites calling out to the believers from the people of "Medina": 'O people of "Yathrib" (which is the old name for "Medina"), you have no standing in a losing battle, so return to your homes inside "Medina".' And another group of the hypocrites asked permission from the Messenger, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, to return to their homes under the pretext that they were not fortified, and they feared for them, but the truth is that they were not, and they did not intend by that except to flee from the fighting.

Some hypocrites urged believers to retreat, while others sought permission to leave under false pretenses, revealing their lack of commitment.

Verse 14

وَلَوْ دُخِلَتْ عَلَيْهِمْ مِنْ أَقْطَارِهَا ثُمَّ سُئِلُوا الْفِتْنَةَ لَآتَوْهَا وَمَا تَلَبَّثُوا بِهَا إِلَّا يَسِيرًا

And if the army of the confederates had entered "Medina" from its sides, and then these hypocrites were asked to associate partners with Allah and return from Islam, they would have responded to that quickly, and they would not have delayed from associating partners except for a little.

This verse highlights the hypocrisy of those who, if faced with an attack, would quickly abandon their faith, showing their lack of true belief.

Verse 15

وَلَقَدْ كَانُوا عَاهَدُوا اللَّهَ مِنْ قَبْلُ لَا يُوَلُّونَ الْأَدْبَارَ ۚ وَكَانَ عَهْدُ اللَّهِ مَسْئُولًا

They had already pledged to Allah earlier never to turn their backs ˹in retreat˺. And a pledge to Allah must be answered for.

And indeed, these hypocrites had pledged to Allah through His Messenger before the Battle of the Trench, that they would not flee if they witnessed the war, and they would not lag behind if they were called to jihad, but they betrayed their pledge, and Allah will hold them accountable for that, and He will ask them about that pledge, and the pledge of Allah was a responsibility, accounted for.

The hypocrites had pledged to not retreat, yet they did so, highlighting the importance of keeping one's pledge to Allah.

Verse 16

قُلْ لَنْ يَنْفَعَكُمُ الْفِرَارُ إِنْ فَرَرْتُمْ مِنَ الْمَوْتِ أَوِ الْقَتْلِ وَإِذًا لَا تُمَتَّعُونَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا

And if you flee, you will not enjoy this world except for the extent of your limited lifespan, which is a very short time compared to the Hereafter.

This verse emphasizes that fleeing from death will not change destiny. If the time of death has not arrived, one will only enjoy life for a short while, highlighting the inevitability of death.

Verse 17

For He is the Giver, the Withholder, the Harmer, the Benefactor. And these hypocrites will not find for themselves any ally other than Allah to befriend them, nor any helper to support them.

This verse asks who can protect from Allah’s will. It clarifies that only Allah can bring harm or mercy, and there is no protector besides Him.

Verse 18

This verse speaks about those who discourage others from fighting and seek to avoid conflict. Allah knows those who secretly try to hinder others and their intentions.

˹They are˺ totally unwilling to assist you. When danger comes, you see them staring at you with their eyes rolling like someone in the throes of death. Those have not believed in their hearts, so Allah has nullified their deeds, and that is easy for Allah.

This verse describes the behavior of hypocrites: they are unwilling to help, show fear during conflict, and are greedy after the danger is gone. Their actions render their deeds void because they lack true faith.

Verse 20

This verse shows that hypocrites are in constant fear, wishing to be away from conflict. They are cowardly and would barely participate in fighting, even if they were present.

Verse 21

This verse highlights the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) as the best example for believers. Those who believe in Allah, the Last Day, and remember Allah frequently should strive to follow his way.
