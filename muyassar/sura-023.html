Sura 023

Verse 1

قَدْ أَفْلَحَ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ

Successful indeed are the believers:

Successful indeed are the believers who believe in Allah and His Messenger and act according to His law.

This verse states that true success belongs to the believers, those who have faith in Allah and follow His guidance.

Verse 2

الَّذِينَ هُمْ فِي صَلَاتِهِمْ خَاشِعُونَ

those who humble themselves in prayer;

Those who are characterized by humility in their prayers, with their hearts focused and their limbs still.

A characteristic of believers is their humility during prayer, with their hearts and bodies focused on Allah.

Verse 3

وَالَّذِينَ هُمْ عَنِ اللَّغْوِ مُعْرِضُونَ

those who avoid idle talk;

And those who avoid all that is not good in words and deeds.

Believers avoid idle and meaningless talk, focusing on beneficial words and actions.

Verse 4

وَالَّذِينَ هُمْ لِلزَّكَاةِ فَاعِلُونَ

those who pay alms-tax;

And those who purify themselves and their wealth by paying Zakat on their wealth of various kinds.

Believers purify themselves and their wealth by paying Zakat, the obligatory alms.

Verse 5

وَالَّذِينَ هُمْ لِفُرُوجِهِمْ حَافِظُونَ

those who guard their chastity

And those who guard their private parts from what Allah has forbidden of adultery, sodomy, and all immoral acts.

Believers protect their chastity by avoiding unlawful sexual acts.

Verse 6

إِلَّا عَلَىٰ أَزْوَاجِهِمْ أَوْ مَا مَلَكَتْ أَيْمَانُهُمْ فَإِنَّهُمْ غَيْرُ مَلُومِينَ

except with their wives or those ˹bondwomen˺ in their possession, for then they are free from blame,

Except with their wives or what their right hands possess of slave women, for there is no blame or restriction upon them for intercourse and enjoyment with them, because Allah Almighty has made them lawful.

Marital relations with spouses and lawful relationships with bondwomen are permissible and not considered immoral.

Verse 7

فَمَنِ ابْتَغَىٰ وَرَاءَ ذَٰلِكَ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْعَادُونَ

but whoever seeks beyond that are the transgressors;

So whoever seeks enjoyment other than with his wife or his slave woman, then he is among those who transgress from the lawful to the unlawful, and he has exposed himself to the punishment and wrath of Allah.

Seeking sexual gratification outside the bounds of marriage or lawful ownership is a transgression.

Verse 8

وَالَّذِينَ هُمْ لِأَمَانَاتِهِمْ وَعَهْدِهِمْ رَاعُونَ

˹the believers are also˺ those who are true to their trusts and covenants;

And those who are keepers of what they have been entrusted with, fulfilling all their covenants.

Believers are faithful to their trusts and fulfill their covenants.

Verse 9

وَالَّذِينَ هُمْ عَلَىٰ صَلَوَاتِهِمْ يُحَافِظُونَ

and those who are ˹properly˺ observant of their prayers.

And those who regularly perform their prayers at their times, in the prescribed manner, as reported from the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him.

Believers observe their prayers regularly and at the prescribed times.

Verse 10

أُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْوَارِثُونَ

These are the ones who will be awarded

These believers are the inheritors of Paradise.

These believers are the inheritors of Paradise.

Verse 11

الَّذِينَ يَرِثُونَ الْفِرْدَوْسَ هُمْ فِيهَا خَالِدُونَ

Paradise as their own. They will be there forever.

Those who will inherit the highest and middle levels of Paradise; they will abide therein eternally, their blessings will not cease nor will they vanish.

They will inherit Paradise and live there eternally.

Verse 12

وَلَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا الْإِنْسَانَ مِنْ سُلَالَةٍ مِنْ طِينٍ

And indeed, We created humankind from an extract of clay,

And We have certainly created Adam from clay extracted from all the earth.

Allah created humankind, starting with Adam, from an extract of clay.

Verse 13

ثُمَّ جَعَلْنَاهُ نُطْفَةً فِي قَرَارٍ مَكِينٍ

then placed each ˹human˺ as a sperm-drop in a secure place,

Then We made his progeny from a sperm-drop: that is, the semen of men that comes out from their loins, settling firmly in the wombs of women.

Human procreation begins with a sperm drop placed in the womb.

Verse 14

ثُمَّ خَلَقْنَا النُّطْفَةَ عَلَقَةً فَخَلَقْنَا الْعَلَقَةَ مُضْغَةً فَخَلَقْنَا الْمُضْغَةَ عِظَامًا فَكَسَوْنَا الْعِظَامَ لَحْمًا ثُمَّ أَنْشَأْنَاهُ خَلْقًا آخَرَ ۚ فَتَبَارَكَ اللَّهُ أَحْسَنُ الْخَالِقِينَ

then We developed the drop into a clinging clot ˹of blood˺, then developed the clot into a lump ˹of flesh˺, then developed the lump into bones, then clothed the bones with flesh, then We brought it into being as a new creation. So Blessed is Allah, the Best of Creators.

Then We made the sperm-drop into a clinging clot, that is, red blood; then We made the clinging clot after forty days into a lump, that is, a piece of flesh the size of a bite; then We made the soft lump into bones, then We covered the bones with flesh; then We developed it into another creation by breathing the soul into it. So blessed is Allah, the best of creators.

This verse details the stages of human development from a sperm drop to a fully formed being, highlighting Allah's creative power.

Verse 15

ثُمَّ إِنَّكُمْ بَعْدَ ذَٰلِكَ لَمَيِّتُونَ

After that you will surely die,

Then indeed you, O mankind, after the stages of life and the passing of your ages, will die.

After life, all humans will surely die.

Verse 16

ثُمَّ إِنَّكُمْ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ تُبْعَثُونَ

then on the Day of Judgment you will be resurrected.

Then indeed you, after death and the end of this world, will be resurrected on the Day of Resurrection alive from your graves for reckoning and recompense.

Then they will be resurrected on the Day of Judgment for reckoning.

Verse 17

وَلَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا فَوْقَكُمْ سَبْعَ طَرَائِقَ وَمَا كُنَّا عَنِ الْخَلْقِ غَافِلِينَ

And indeed, We created above you seven levels ˹of heaven˺. We are never unmindful of ˹Our˺ creation.

And We have certainly created above you seven heavens, one above another, and We were never heedless of creation, so We do not neglect any creature, nor do We forget it.

Allah created seven heavens above, and He is never neglectful of His creation.

Verse 18

وَأَنْزَلْنَا مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً بِقَدَرٍ فَأَسْكَنَّاهُ فِي الْأَرْضِ ۖ وَإِنَّا عَلَىٰ ذَهَابٍ بِهِ لَقَادِرُونَ

We send down rain from the sky in perfect measure, causing it to soak into the earth. And We are surely able to take it away.

And We sent down from the sky water in due measure for the needs of the creatures, and We made the earth a resting place for this water, and indeed We are able to take away the settled water. And in this is a threat and warning to the wrongdoers.

Allah sends down rain in perfect measure, which soaks into the earth. He has the power to take it away, demonstrating His control over natural resources.

Verse 19

فَأَنْشَأْنَا لَكُمْ بِهِ جَنَّاتٍ مِنْ نَخِيلٍ وَأَعْنَابٍ لَكُمْ فِيهَا فَوَاكِهُ كَثِيرَةٌ وَمِنْهَا تَأْكُلُونَ

With it We produce for you gardens of palm trees and grapevines, in which there are abundant fruits, and from which you may eat,

Then We produced for you with this water gardens of palm trees and grapevines, in which you have many kinds and forms of fruits, and from them you eat.

Through this rain, Allah produces gardens of palm trees and grapevines, providing abundant fruits for sustenance.

Verse 20

وَشَجَرَةً تَخْرُجُ مِنْ طُورِ سَيْنَاءَ تَنْبُتُ بِالدُّهْنِ وَصِبْغٍ لِلْآكِلِينَ

as well as ˹olive˺ trees which grow at Mount Sinai, providing oil and a condiment to eat.

And We produced for you with it the olive tree that grows around Mount Sinai, from which oil is extracted, which is used for anointing and as a condiment.

Allah also provides olive trees, especially those around Mount Sinai, which yield oil for various uses.

Verse 21

وَإِنَّ لَكُمْ فِي الْأَنْعَامِ لَعِبْرَةً ۖ نُسْقِيكُمْ مِمَّا فِي بُطُونِهَا وَلَكُمْ فِيهَا مَنَافِعُ كَثِيرَةٌ وَمِنْهَا تَأْكُلُونَ

And there is certainly a lesson for you in cattle, from whose bellies We give you ˹milk˺ to drink, and in them are many other benefits for you, and from them you may eat.

And indeed, for you - O people - in the camels, cattle, and sheep is a lesson to be learned by their creation. We give you to drink from what is in their bellies of milk, and you have in them many other benefits, such as wool and hides, and the like, and from them you eat.

Cattle are another blessing, providing milk, wool, hides, and food, demonstrating Allah's provision for humanity.

Verse 22

وَعَلَيْهَا وَعَلَى الْفُلْكِ تُحْمَلُونَ

And you are carried upon ˹some of˺ them and upon ships.

And upon the camels and ships in the land and sea you are carried.

Humans are carried on animals and ships, highlighting Allah's provision for transportation and travel.

Verse 23

وَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا نُوحًا إِلَىٰ قَوْمِهِ فَقَالَ يَا قَوْمِ اعْبُدُوا اللَّهَ مَا لَكُمْ مِنْ إِلَٰهٍ غَيْرُهُ ۖ أَفَلَا تَتَّقُونَ

Indeed, We sent Noah to his people. He declared, “O my people! Worship Allah ˹alone˺. You have no god other than Him. Will you not then fear ˹Him˺?”

And We had sent Noah to his people, with the call to Tawhid, and he said to them: Worship Allah alone, you have no deity worthy of worship other than Him, Glorified and Exalted, so dedicate your worship to Him. Do you not fear His punishment?

Allah sent Noah to his people, calling them to worship Him alone and warning them of His punishment if they did not.

Verse 24

فَقَالَ الْمَلَأُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا مِنْ قَوْمِهِ مَا هَٰذَا إِلَّا بَشَرٌ مِثْلُكُمْ يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَتَفَضَّلَ عَلَيْكُمْ وَلَوْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ لَأَنْزَلَ مَلَائِكَةً مَا سَمِعْنَا بِهَٰذَا فِي آبَائِنَا الْأَوَّلِينَ

But the disbelieving chiefs of his people said ˹to the masses˺, “This is only a human like you, who wants to be superior to you. Had Allah willed, He could have easily sent down angels ˹instead˺. We have never heard of this in ˹the history of˺ our forefathers.

So the leaders of his people denied him and said to their common people: 'He is just a man like you, he is not distinguished from you in any way, and he only wants leadership and superiority over you. If Allah had willed to send a messenger to us, He would have sent one of the angels. We have not heard of such a thing from those who preceded us among fathers and forefathers. And Noah is only a man possessed by madness, so wait until he recovers, then he will abandon his call, or die, and you will be relieved of him.'

The disbelieving leaders of Noah's people rejected his message, claiming he was just a human seeking superiority and that Allah would have sent angels if He desired.

Verse 25

إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا رَجُلٌ بِهِ جِنَّةٌ فَتَرَبَّصُوا بِهِ حَتَّىٰ حِينٍ

He is simply insane, so bear with him for a while.”

So the leaders of his people denied him and said to their common people: 'He is just a man like you, he is not distinguished from you in any way, and he only wants leadership and superiority over you. If Allah had willed to send a messenger to us, He would have sent one of the angels. We have not heard of such a thing from those who preceded us among fathers and forefathers. And Noah is only a man possessed by madness, so wait until he recovers, then he will abandon his call, or die, and you will be relieved of him.'

They further dismissed Noah as insane and asked others to bear with him for a while, implying his message was temporary and would fade away.

Verse 26

قَالَ رَبِّ انْصُرْنِي بِمَا كَذَّبُونِ

Noah prayed, “My Lord! Help me, because they have denied ˹me˺.”

Noah said: 'My Lord, support me against my people because of their denial of what I conveyed to them of Your message.'

Noah prayed to Allah for help against his people, who had rejected his message.

Verse 27

فَأَوْحَيْنَا إِلَيْهِ أَنِ اصْنَعِ الْفُلْكَ بِأَعْيُنِنَا وَوَحْيِنَا فَإِذَا جَاءَ أَمْرُنَا وَفَارَ التَّنُّورُ ۙ فَاسْلُكْ فِيهَا مِنْ كُلٍّ زَوْجَيْنِ اثْنَيْنِ وَأَهْلَكَ إِلَّا مَنْ سَبَقَ عَلَيْهِ الْقَوْلُ مِنْهُمْ ۖ وَلَا تُخَاطِبْنِي فِي الَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا ۖ إِنَّهُمْ مُغْرَقُونَ

So We inspired him: “Build the Ark under Our ˹watchful˺ Eyes and directions. Then when Our command comes and the oven bursts ˹with water˺, take on board a pair from every species along with your family—except those against whom the decree ˹to drown˺ has already been passed. And do not plead with Me for those who have done wrong, for they will surely be drowned.”

So We revealed to him: 'Construct the ship under Our observation and Our command to you, with Our help and protection. So when Our command comes for your people to be punished with drowning, and the flood begins, and water gushes out powerfully from the oven - which is the place where bread is baked - a sign of the arrival of the punishment, then take into the ship of each living thing a male and a female so the lineage may remain, and take your family except those who deserve the punishment for their disbelief, such as your wife and your son, and do not ask Me for the salvation of your wrongdoing people, for they will be drowned without doubt.' And in this verse is the affirmation of the attribute of the Eye of Allah, Glory be to Him, in a manner that befits Him, without likening or describing it.

Allah instructed Noah to build an Ark under His guidance. When the flood came, he was to take a pair of every species and his family (except those who were decreed to be punished) on board, with no intercession allowed for those who had done wrong.

Verse 28

فَإِذَا اسْتَوَيْتَ أَنْتَ وَمَنْ مَعَكَ عَلَى الْفُلْكِ فَقُلِ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي نَجَّانَا مِنَ الْقَوْمِ الظَّالِمِينَ

Then when you and those with you have settled in the Ark, say, “All praise is for Allah, Who saved us from the wrongdoing people.”

So when you have boarded the ship, you and those with you, secure from drowning, say: "Praise be to Allah, who has saved us from the disbelieving people."

After boarding the ark, Noah and the believers are instructed to praise Allah for their salvation from the disbelievers.

Verse 29

وَقُلْ رَبِّ أَنْزِلْنِي مُنْزَلًا مُبَارَكًا وَأَنْتَ خَيْرُ الْمُنْزِلِينَ

And pray, “My Lord! Allow me a blessed landing, for You are the best accommodator.”

And say: "My Lord, grant me a blessed and safe landing, and You are the best of those who grant landing." And in this is a teaching from Allah, the Almighty, to His servants that when they land they should say this.

Noah is taught to pray for a blessed and safe landing, acknowledging Allah as the best provider of accommodation.

Verse 30

إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ وَإِنْ كُنَّا لَمُبْتَلِينَ

Surely in this are lessons. And We ˹always˺ put ˹people˺ to the test.

Indeed, in the saving of the believers and the destruction of the disbelievers are clear signs of the truthfulness of the messengers of Allah in what they brought from Allah, and We were testing the nations by sending messengers to them before the punishment befell them.

The story of Noah's ark is a lesson, and Allah tests people through trials and messengers.

Verse 31

ثُمَّ أَنْشَأْنَا مِنْ بَعْدِهِمْ قَرْنًا آخَرِينَ

Then We raised another generation after them,

Then We brought into being after the people of Noah another generation, who were the people of 'Aad.

After the people of Noah, Allah created another generation: the people of Aad.

Verse 32

فَأَرْسَلْنَا فِيهِمْ رَسُولًا مِنْهُمْ أَنِ اعْبُدُوا اللَّهَ مَا لَكُمْ مِنْ إِلَٰهٍ غَيْرُهُ ۖ أَفَلَا تَتَّقُونَ

and sent to them a messenger from among themselves, ˹declaring,˺ “Worship Allah ˹alone˺. You have no god other than Him. Will you not then fear ˹Him˺?”

So We sent among them a messenger from among themselves, who was Hud, peace be upon him. He said to them: "Worship Allah alone; you have no true deity other than Him. Will you not then fear His punishment if you worship other than Him?"

Allah sent a messenger, Hud, to the people of Aad, urging them to worship Allah alone.

Verse 33

وَقَالَ الْمَلَأُ مِنْ قَوْمِهِ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا وَكَذَّبُوا بِلِقَاءِ الْآخِرَةِ وَأَتْرَفْنَاهُمْ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا مَا هَٰذَا إِلَّا بَشَرٌ مِثْلُكُمْ يَأْكُلُ مِمَّا تَأْكُلُونَ مِنْهُ وَيَشْرَبُ مِمَّا تَشْرَبُونَ

But the chiefs of his people—who disbelieved, denied the meeting ˹with Allah˺ in the Hereafter, and were spoiled by the worldly luxuries We had provided for them—said ˹to the masses˺, “This is only a human like you. He eats what you eat, and drinks what you drink.

And the notables and dignitaries of his people, who disbelieved in Allah and denied the Hereafter, and were made arrogant by what they were granted in the world of luxurious living, said: "This one who is calling you to the oneness of Allah is but a human like you, eating of your kind of food and drinking of your kind of drink."

The leaders of Aad, who were arrogant due to their worldly possessions, rejected Hud's message, arguing that he was just a regular human.

Verse 34

وَلَئِنْ أَطَعْتُمْ بَشَرًا مِثْلَكُمْ إِنَّكُمْ إِذًا لَخَاسِرُونَ

And if you ˹ever˺ obey a human like yourselves, then you would certainly be losers.

And if you were to follow a human like yourselves, then you would indeed be losers in abandoning your gods and following him.

The leaders of Aad claimed that following a human like themselves would lead to their loss.

Verse 35

أَيَعِدُكُمْ أَنَّكُمْ إِذَا مِتُّمْ وَكُنْتُمْ تُرَابًا وَعِظَامًا أَنَّكُمْ مُخْرَجُونَ

Does he promise you that once you are dead and reduced to dust and bones, you will be brought forth ˹alive˺?

How can you believe what he promises you, that when you have died and become dust and disintegrated bones, you will be brought out of your graves alive?

They questioned the possibility of resurrection after death.

Verse 36

هَيْهَاتَ هَيْهَاتَ لِمَا تُوعَدُونَ

Impossible, simply impossible is what you are promised!

Far, far is that which you are promised, O people, that after your death you will be brought out alive from your graves.

The disbelievers dismissed the idea of resurrection as impossible.

Verse 37

إِنْ هِيَ إِلَّا حَيَاتُنَا الدُّنْيَا نَمُوتُ وَنَحْيَا وَمَا نَحْنُ بِمَبْعُوثِينَ

There is nothing beyond our worldly life. We die, others are born, and none will be resurrected.

Our life is only this worldly life. Fathers die among us and sons live, and we will not be brought forth alive again.

The disbelievers believed that life is only limited to the worldly existence, with no afterlife.

Verse 38

إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا رَجُلٌ افْتَرَىٰ عَلَى اللَّهِ كَذِبًا وَمَا نَحْنُ لَهُ بِمُؤْمِنِينَ

He is no more than a man who has fabricated a lie about Allah, and we will never believe in him.”

And this one who is calling you to believe is but a man who has fabricated a lie against Allah, and we are not believers in what he has said to us.

They accused Hud of fabricating lies about Allah and refused to believe him.

Verse 39

قَالَ رَبِّ انْصُرْنِي بِمَا كَذَّبُونِ

The messenger prayed, “My Lord! Help me, because they have denied ˹me˺.”

So their messenger invoked his Lord, saying: "My Lord, grant me victory over them because of their denial of me."

Hud prayed to Allah for help against his disbelieving people.

Verse 40

قَالَ عَمَّا قَلِيلٍ لَيُصْبِحُنَّ نَادِمِينَ

Allah responded, “Soon they will be truly regretful.”

And Allah, responding to his invocation, said: "In a little while, they will surely become regretful," meaning: after a short time, these deniers will become regretful.

Allah assured Hud that the disbelievers would soon regret their actions.

Verse 41

فَأَخَذَتْهُمُ الصَّيْحَةُ بِالْحَقِّ فَجَعَلْنَاهُمْ غُثَاءً ۚ فَبُعْدًا لِلْقَوْمِ الظَّالِمِينَ

Then the ˹mighty˺ blast overtook them with justice, and We reduced them to rubble. So away with the wrongdoing people!

And it was not long before they were struck by a mighty blast with a wind, and Allah destroyed them with it. So they all died, and became like the scum of the flood which floats on water. So away with these wrongdoers, and far be it from them to have the mercy of Allah! So let the listeners beware lest they deny their messenger and have befall them what befell those before them.

The people of Aad were destroyed by a mighty blast as a just punishment for their disbelief. This serves as a warning.

Verse 42

ثُمَّ أَنْشَأْنَا مِنْ بَعْدِهِمْ قُرُونًا آخَرِينَ

Then We raised other generations after them.

Then We brought into being after these deniers other nations and creatures, like the peoples of Lot, Shuaib, Ayub, and Yunus, may the blessings and peace of Allah be upon them all.
