Sura 013

Verse 1

المر ۚ تِلْكَ آيَاتُ الْكِتَابِ ۗ وَالَّذِي أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْكَ مِنْ رَبِّكَ الْحَقُّ وَلَٰكِنَّ أَكْثَرَ النَّاسِ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ

Alif-Lãm-Mĩm-Ra. These are the verses of the Book. What has been revealed to you ˹O Prophet˺ from your Lord is the truth, but most people do not believe.

(Alif, Lam, Mim, Ra). The discussion of the separate letters at the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah has preceded.

This verse begins with the letters *Alif-Lam-Mim-Ra*, which are unique to the Quran and their meaning is known to Allah. It emphasizes that the Quran is the truth revealed by Allah, though many disbelieve.

Verse 2

اللَّهُ الَّذِي رَفَعَ السَّمَاوَاتِ بِغَيْرِ عَمَدٍ تَرَوْنَهَا ۖ ثُمَّ اسْتَوَىٰ عَلَى الْعَرْشِ ۖ وَسَخَّرَ الشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ ۖ كُلٌّ يَجْرِي لِأَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى ۚ يُدَبِّرُ الْأَمْرَ يُفَصِّلُ الْآيَاتِ لَعَلَّكُمْ بِلِقَاءِ رَبِّكُمْ تُوقِنُونَ

It is Allah Who has raised the heavens without pillars—as you can see—then established Himself on the Throne. He has subjected the sun and the moon, each orbiting for an appointed term. He conducts the whole affair. He makes the signs clear so that you may be certain of the meeting with your Lord.

Allah, the Most High, is the One who raised the seven heavens with His power without pillars, as you see them, then He rose - meaning He ascended and elevated Himself - over the Throne in a manner that befits His Majesty and Greatness. And He subjected the sun and the moon for the benefit of the servants. Each of them revolves in its orbit until the Day of Judgment. He, Glory be to Him, manages the affairs of this world and the Hereafter, He clarifies for you the verses indicating His power and that there is no god but Him, so that you may be certain of Allah and the return to Him, and believe in His promise and His warning and devote worship to Him alone.

Allah is the One who raised the heavens without any visible pillars, established Himself on the Throne, and subjected the sun and moon to His laws. These signs are meant to assure belief in Allah and the meeting with Him.

Verse 3

وَهُوَ الَّذِي مَدَّ الْأَرْضَ وَجَعَلَ فِيهَا رَوَاسِيَ وَأَنْهَارًا ۖ وَمِنْ كُلِّ الثَّمَرَاتِ جَعَلَ فِيهَا زَوْجَيْنِ اثْنَيْنِ ۖ يُغْشِي اللَّيْلَ النَّهَارَ ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ لِقَوْمٍ يَتَفَكَّرُونَ

And He is the One Who spread out the earth and placed firm mountains and rivers upon it, and created fruits of every kind in pairs. He covers the day with night. Surely in this are signs for those who reflect.

And He, Glory be to Him, is the One who made the earth vast and wide, and prepared it for your sustenance, and placed mountains therein to stabilize it, and rivers for your drinking and benefit, and He made in it of all fruits two kinds, so some are white and black, sweet and sour, and He made the night cover the day with its darkness. Indeed, in all of that are lessons for a people who reflect upon them, and take heed.

Allah spread out the earth, placed mountains and rivers, and created diverse fruits. The alternation of day and night serves as signs for those who reflect on the power and wisdom of the Creator.

Verse 4

وَفِي الْأَرْضِ قِطَعٌ مُتَجَاوِرَاتٌ وَجَنَّاتٌ مِنْ أَعْنَابٍ وَزَرْعٌ وَنَخِيلٌ صِنْوَانٌ وَغَيْرُ صِنْوَانٍ يُسْقَىٰ بِمَاءٍ وَاحِدٍ وَنُفَضِّلُ بَعْضَهَا عَلَىٰ بَعْضٍ فِي الْأُكُلِ ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ لِقَوْمٍ يَعْقِلُونَ

And on the earth there are ˹different˺ neighbouring tracts, gardens of grapevines, ˹various˺ crops, palm trees—some stemming from the same root, others standing alone. They are all irrigated with the same water, yet We make some taste better than others. Surely in this are signs for those who understand.

And in the earth are tracts adjoining each other, some of which are good, yielding what benefits people, and some are barren, salty, not producing anything. And in the good earth are gardens of grapes, and He made in it various crops and palm trees, clustered in one place and not clustered in another, all of that in the same soil, and watered with one water, but it differs in fruits, size, taste, and so on. This one is sweet and this one is sour, and some of them are better than others in eating. Indeed, in that are signs for those who have a heart that understands from Allah, the Most High, His command and prohibition.

Despite the earth having adjacent tracts, diverse gardens and crops, and being watered by the same water, Allah gives each of them a unique taste. This serves as signs for those who understand Allah's power.

Verse 5

وَإِنْ تَعْجَبْ فَعَجَبٌ قَوْلُهُمْ أَإِذَا كُنَّا تُرَابًا أَإِنَّا لَفِي خَلْقٍ جَدِيدٍ ۗ أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِرَبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُولَٰئِكَ الْأَغْلَالُ فِي أَعْنَاقِهِمْ ۖ وَأُولَٰئِكَ أَصْحَابُ النَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَالِدُونَ

˹Now,˺ if anything should amaze you ˹O Prophet˺, then it is their question: “When we are reduced to dust, will we really be raised as a new creation?” It is they who have disbelieved in their Lord. It is they who will have shackles around their necks. And it is they who will be the residents of the Fire. They will be there forever.

And if you wonder - O Messenger - at their disbelief after these evidences, then the most astonishing thing is the saying of the disbelievers: 'When we have died and become dust, shall we be raised anew?' Those are the ones who deny their Lord who brought them into being from nothing, and those will have chains of fire around their necks on the Day of Resurrection, and those will enter the Fire, and will never come out of it.

This verse expresses astonishment at those who disbelieve in resurrection after being reduced to dust. It warns that those who disbelieve will face the consequences of the fire of hell.

Verse 6

وَيَسْتَعْجِلُونَكَ بِالسَّيِّئَةِ قَبْلَ الْحَسَنَةِ وَقَدْ خَلَتْ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمُ الْمَثُلَاتُ ۗ وَإِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَذُو مَغْفِرَةٍ لِلنَّاسِ عَلَىٰ ظُلْمِهِمْ ۖ وَإِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَشَدِيدُ الْعِقَابِ

They ask you ˹O Prophet˺ to hasten the torment rather than grace, though there have ˹already˺ been ˹many˺ torments before them. Surely your Lord is full of forgiveness for people, despite their wrongdoing, and your Lord is truly severe in punishment.

And the deniers of the punishment hasten you, the one that I did not hasten for them before the faith with which security and good deeds are hoped for. And the punishments of the deniers before them have passed, so how do they not take heed of them? And indeed, your Lord - O Messenger - is indeed full of forgiveness for the sins of those who repent from their sins from among the people despite their wrongdoing, opening the door of forgiveness for them and calling them to it, while they wrong themselves by disobeying their Lord. And indeed, your Lord is severe in punishment for whoever persists in disbelief, misguidance, and disobedience to Allah.

The disbelievers challenge the Prophet by asking for immediate punishment, yet Allah is forgiving to those who repent but severe in punishment to those who persist in disbelief.

Verse 7

وَيَقُولُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْلَا أُنْزِلَ عَلَيْهِ آيَةٌ مِنْ رَبِّهِ ۗ إِنَّمَا أَنْتَ مُنْذِرٌ ۖ وَلِكُلِّ قَوْمٍ هَادٍ

The disbelievers say, “If only a sign could be sent down to him from his Lord.” You ˹O Prophet˺ are only a warner. And every people had a guide.

And the disbelievers of Mecca say: Why did a tangible miracle not come to him like the staff of Moses and the she-camel of Saleh? And that is not in your hand - O Messenger - for you are only a deliverer to them and a warner of the might of Allah. And for every nation, there is a messenger to guide them to Allah the Exalted.

The disbelievers demand a sign, but the Prophet's role is to warn. Every community has been guided by a messenger of Allah.

Verse 8

اللَّهُ يَعْلَمُ مَا تَحْمِلُ كُلُّ أُنْثَىٰ وَمَا تَغِيضُ الْأَرْحَامُ وَمَا تَزْدَادُ ۖ وَكُلُّ شَيْءٍ عِنْدَهُ بِمِقْدَارٍ

Allah knows what every female bears and what increases and decreases in the wombs. And with Him everything is determined with precision.

Allah the Exalted knows what every female carries in her womb, whether it is male or female, and whether it is miserable or happy. And He knows what the wombs decrease, so it is miscarried or born before nine months, and what its carrying increases. And everything is determined with Allah by a measure of decrease or increase that it does not exceed.

Allah has complete knowledge of everything a female bears, including the development within the womb. Everything is determined by His precise measure.

Verse 9

عَالِمُ الْغَيْبِ وَالشَّهَادَةِ الْكَبِيرُ الْمُتَعَالِ

˹He is the˺ Knower of the seen and the unseen—the All-Great, Most Exalted.

Allah is All-Knowing of what is hidden from the eyes and of what is witnessed, the Great in His essence, names, and attributes, the Exalted above all of His creation in His essence, power, and might.

Allah knows both the seen and the unseen, He is the All-Great, Most Exalted, and nothing is hidden from Him.

Verse 10

سَوَاءٌ مِنْكُمْ مَنْ أَسَرَّ الْقَوْلَ وَمَنْ جَهَرَ بِهِ وَمَنْ هُوَ مُسْتَخْفٍ بِاللَّيْلِ وَسَارِبٌ بِالنَّهَارِ

It is the same ˹to Him˺ whether any of you speaks secretly or openly, whether one hides in the darkness of night or goes about in broad daylight.

Equal in His knowledge is whoever among you conceals the speech and whoever declares it, and equal to Him is whoever hides his deeds in the darkness of the night and whoever declares them in the light of day.

Whether someone speaks secretly or openly, or acts in darkness or broad daylight, all are the same in Allah's knowledge. He is aware of everything.

Verse 11

لَهُ مُعَقِّبَاتٌ مِنْ بَيْنِ يَدَيْهِ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِ يَحْفَظُونَهُ مِنْ أَمْرِ اللَّهِ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يُغَيِّرُ مَا بِقَوْمٍ حَتَّىٰ يُغَيِّرُوا مَا بِأَنْفُسِهِمْ ۗ وَإِذَا أَرَادَ اللَّهُ بِقَوْمٍ سُوءًا فَلَا مَرَدَّ لَهُ ۚ وَمَا لَهُمْ مِنْ دُونِهِ مِنْ وَالٍ

For each one there are successive angels before and behind, protecting them by Allah’s command. Indeed, Allah would never change a people’s state ˹of favour˺ until they change their own state ˹of faith˺. And if it is Allah’s Will to torment a people, it can never be averted, nor can they find a protector other than Him.

For Allah the Exalted are angels who alternate over man from before him and from behind him, guarding him by the command of Allah and counting what comes from him of good or evil. Indeed, Allah, Glory be to Him, does not change a blessing He has bestowed upon a people unless they change what He commanded them of disobedience. And if Allah wills a calamity upon a group, then there is no escape from it, and they have no protector other than Allah who manages their affairs, bringing them what is loved and warding off from them what is disliked.

Angels protect individuals by Allah's command. Allah does not change a people's condition until they change themselves. His will is unpreventable, and He is the only true protector.

Verse 12

هُوَ الَّذِي يُرِيكُمُ الْبَرْقَ خَوْفًا وَطَمَعًا وَيُنْشِئُ السَّحَابَ الثِّقَالَ

He is the One Who shows you lightning, inspiring ˹you with˺ hope and fear, and produces heavy clouds.

He is the One Who shows you from His signs the lightning - and it is the shining light from within the clouds - so you fear that the scorching thunderbolts will descend upon you from it, and you hope that rain will descend with it. And by His power, Glory be to Him, He creates clouds laden with much water for your benefit.

Allah shows the lightning, inspiring hope and fear. He produces heavy clouds, a sign of His power and mercy.

Verse 13

وَيُسَبِّحُ الرَّعْدُ بِحَمْدِهِ وَالْمَلَائِكَةُ مِنْ خِيفَتِهِ وَيُرْسِلُ الصَّوَاعِقَ فَيُصِيبُ بِهَا مَنْ يَشَاءُ وَهُمْ يُجَادِلُونَ فِي اللَّهِ وَهُوَ شَدِيدُ الْمِحَالِ

The thunder glorifies His praises, as do the angels in awe of Him. He sends thunderbolts, striking with them whoever He wills. Yet they dispute about Allah. And He is tremendous in might.

And the thunder glorifies Allah with praise, a glorification that indicates its submission to its Lord, and the angels praise their Lord out of fear of Allah. And Allah sends down the destructive thunderbolts, destroying by them whom He wills of His creation, and the disbelievers argue about the Oneness of Allah and His power over resurrection, and He is severe in might, strength, and striking those who disobey Him.

The thunder glorifies Allah, and angels are in awe of Him. Allah sends thunderbolts to strike whomever He wills. Yet, disbelievers still argue about Allah, who is mighty and powerful.

Verse 14

لَهُ دَعْوَةُ الْحَقِّ ۖ وَالَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ مِنْ دُونِهِ لَا يَسْتَجِيبُونَ لَهُمْ بِشَيْءٍ إِلَّا كَبَاسِطِ كَفَّيْهِ إِلَى الْمَاءِ لِيَبْلُغَ فَاهُ وَمَا هُوَ بِبَالِغِهِ ۚ وَمَا دُعَاءُ الْكَافِرِينَ إِلَّا فِي ضَلَالٍ

Calling upon Him ˹alone˺ is the truth. But those ˹idols˺ the pagans invoke besides Him ˹can˺ never respond to them in any way. ˹It is˺ just like someone who stretches out their hands to water, ˹asking it˺ to reach their mouths, but it can never do so. The calls of the disbelievers are only in vain.

To Allah, the Exalted, belongs alone the call of monotheism (La ilaha illa Allah - there is no deity except Allah), so none is to be worshipped or invoked except Him. And the deities they worship besides Allah do not respond to the supplication of those who invoke them. Their state with them is like the state of a thirsty person extending his hand to water from afar; to reach his mouth but not reaching it. And the disbelievers’ asking of them is but an extreme in distance from the truth due to their associating others with Allah.

This verse emphasizes that only Allah should be invoked because He is the only one who can respond. Those who call upon idols are like a thirsty person reaching for water that is out of reach, their calls are futile.

Verse 15

وَلِلَّهِ يَسْجُدُ مَنْ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ طَوْعًا وَكَرْهًا وَظِلَالُهُمْ بِالْغُدُوِّ وَالْآصَالِ ۩

To Allah ˹alone˺ bow down ˹in submission˺ all those in the heavens and the earth—willingly or unwillingly—as do their shadows, morning and evening.

And to Allah alone prostrates in submission and obedience all that is in the heavens and the earth. So the believers prostrate and submit to Him willingly and by choice, and the disbelievers submit to Him against their will because they are arrogant towards His worship, while their state and nature belie them in that. And the shadows of created beings submit to His greatness, moving by His will at the beginning and end of the day.

Everything in the heavens and earth, willingly or unwillingly, submits to Allah. Believers submit consciously while disbelievers' very existence and nature demonstrate their submission. Even the shadows of created beings move according to His will, demonstrating His authority.

Verse 16

قُلْ مَنْ رَبُّ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ قُلِ اللَّهُ ۚ قُلْ أَفَاتَّخَذْتُمْ مِنْ دُونِهِ أَوْلِيَاءَ لَا يَمْلِكُونَ لِأَنْفُسِهِمْ نَفْعًا وَلَا ضَرًّا ۚ قُلْ هَلْ يَسْتَوِي الْأَعْمَىٰ وَالْبَصِيرُ أَمْ هَلْ تَسْتَوِي الظُّلُمَاتُ وَالنُّورُ ۗ أَمْ جَعَلُوا لِلَّهِ شُرَكَاءَ خَلَقُوا كَخَلْقِهِ فَتَشَابَهَ الْخَلْقُ عَلَيْهِمْ ۚ قُلِ اللَّهُ خَالِقُ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ وَهُوَ الْوَاحِدُ الْقَهَّارُ

Ask ˹them, O Prophet˺, “Who is the Lord of the heavens and the earth?” Say, “Allah!” Ask ˹them˺, “Why ˹then˺ have you taken besides Him lords who cannot even benefit or protect themselves?” Say, “Can the blind and the sighted be equal? Or can darkness and light be equal?” Or have they associated with Allah partners who ˹supposedly˺ produced a creation like His, leaving them confused between the two creations? Say, “Allah is the Creator of all things, and He is the One, the Supreme.”

Say - O Messenger - to the polytheists: Who is the Creator of the heavens and the earth and their manager? Say: Allah is their Creator and Manager, and you acknowledge that. Then say to them, compelling with proof: Have you taken besides Him gods for yourselves, and they are not able to benefit themselves or harm them, let alone benefit or harm you, and you have abandoned the worship of their Owner? Say to them - O Messenger: Are the disbeliever - who is like the blind - and the believer - who is like the seeing - equal in your view? Or is disbelief - which is like darkness - equal to belief - which is like light? Or do their allies whom they made partners with Allah create like His creation, so that the creation of the partners became similar to the creation of Allah, and they believed they deserve to be worshipped? Say to them - O Messenger: Allah is the Creator of every being from nothing, and He alone is the One worthy of worship, and He is the One, the Subduer who deserves divinity and worship, not idols and statues that neither harm nor benefit.

This verse challenges polytheists by asking who is the Lord of the heavens and earth. It highlights the absurdity of worshipping gods that can't benefit or harm, unlike Allah, the sole creator. It also compares belief and disbelief to light and darkness and the impossibility of equality between them.

Verse 17

أَنْزَلَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً فَسَالَتْ أَوْدِيَةٌ بِقَدَرِهَا فَاحْتَمَلَ السَّيْلُ زَبَدًا رَابِيًا ۚ وَمِمَّا يُوقِدُونَ عَلَيْهِ فِي النَّارِ ابْتِغَاءَ حِلْيَةٍ أَوْ مَتَاعٍ زَبَدٌ مِثْلُهُ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ يَضْرِبُ اللَّهُ الْحَقَّ وَالْبَاطِلَ ۚ فَأَمَّا الزَّبَدُ فَيَذْهَبُ جُفَاءً ۖ وَأَمَّا مَا يَنْفَعُ النَّاسَ فَيَمْكُثُ فِي الْأَرْضِ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ يَضْرِبُ اللَّهُ الْأَمْثَالَ

He sends down rain from the sky, causing the valleys to flow, each according to its capacity. The currents then carry along rising foam, similar to the slag produced from metal that people melt in the fire for ornaments or tools. This is how Allah compares truth to falsehood. The ˹worthless˺ residue is then cast away, but what benefits people remains on the earth. This is how Allah sets forth parables.

Then Allah, the Exalted, gave an example for truth and falsehood by water He sent down from the sky, so that the valleys of the earth flowed according to their size. And the torrent carried a floating scum above it with no benefit. And He gave another example: it is metals that they kindle fire upon to melt them, seeking adornment as in gold and silver, or seeking benefits as in copper, so out of them comes their dross which is of no benefit, like that which was with the water. With this, Allah gives an example for truth and falsehood: Falsehood is like the scum of the water, which vanishes or is thrown away as there is no benefit from it, and the truth is like the clear water, and the pure metals remain in the earth for benefit. Just as He clarified these parables to you, thus He strikes them for people, so that the truth is clarified from falsehood and guidance from misguidance.

This verse uses the analogy of rain and metal refining to compare truth and falsehood. Just as water carries foam and smelting produces dross which are discarded, falsehood is temporary and will vanish, while truth, like the beneficial water and pure metal, will remain.

Verse 18

لِلَّذِينَ اسْتَجَابُوا لِرَبِّهِمُ الْحُسْنَىٰ ۚ وَالَّذِينَ لَمْ يَسْتَجِيبُوا لَهُ لَوْ أَنَّ لَهُمْ مَا فِي الْأَرْضِ جَمِيعًا وَمِثْلَهُ مَعَهُ لَافْتَدَوْا بِهِ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ لَهُمْ سُوءُ الْحِسَابِ وَمَأْوَاهُمْ جَهَنَّمُ ۖ وَبِئْسَ الْمِهَادُ

Those who respond to ˹the call of˺ their Lord will have the finest reward. As for those who do not respond to Him, even if they were to possess everything in the world twice over, they would certainly offer it to ransom themselves. They will face strict judgment, and Hell will be their home. What an evil place to rest!

For the believers who obeyed Allah and His Messenger is Paradise, and those who did not obey and disbelieved in Him, for them is Hell. Even if they owned all that is in the earth and twice as much with it, they would give it as a ransom for themselves from the punishment of Allah on the Day of Resurrection, and it will not be accepted from them. Those will be held accountable for all that they have done of evil deeds, and their abode and residence will be Hell which will be their bed, and how wretched the bed they have prepared for themselves.

Those who respond to Allah will receive Paradise as a reward. Those who reject Him will face severe punishment in Hell, and no amount of worldly possessions could ransom them from it. Their fate is a consequence of their deeds.

Verse 19

أَفَمَنْ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّمَا أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْكَ مِنْ رَبِّكَ الْحَقُّ كَمَنْ هُوَ أَعْمَىٰ ۚ إِنَّمَا يَتَذَكَّرُ أُولُو الْأَلْبَابِ

Can the one who knows that your Lord’s revelation to you ˹O Prophet˺ is the truth be like the one who is blind? None will be mindful ˹of this˺ except people of reason.

Is the one who knows that what has come to you - O Messenger - from Allah is the truth and believes in it, like the blind to the truth who does not believe? Only those of sound mind take heed, those who fulfill the covenant of Allah which He commanded them, and do not break the confirmed covenant which they covenanted with Allah.

This verse contrasts those who recognize the truth of the Quran with those who are blind to it. It emphasizes that only people of sound reason and intellect will take heed.

Verse 20

الَّذِينَ يُوفُونَ بِعَهْدِ اللَّهِ وَلَا يَنْقُضُونَ الْمِيثَاقَ

˹They are˺ those who honour Allah’s covenant, never breaking the pledge;

Is the one who knows that what has come to you - O Messenger - from Allah is the truth and believes in it, like the blind to the truth who does not believe? Only those of sound mind take heed, those who fulfill the covenant of Allah which He commanded them, and do not break the confirmed covenant which they covenanted with Allah.

This verse describes the characteristics of those who are mindful, highlighting their commitment to fulfilling Allah's covenant and never breaking their pledges.

Verse 21

وَالَّذِينَ يَصِلُونَ مَا أَمَرَ اللَّهُ بِهِ أَنْ يُوصَلَ وَيَخْشَوْنَ رَبَّهُمْ وَيَخَافُونَ سُوءَ الْحِسَابِ

and those who maintain whatever ˹ties˺ Allah has ordered to be maintained, stand in awe of their Lord, and fear strict judgment.

And they are those who join what Allah has commanded to be joined, such as kinships and the needy, and they fear their Lord, and they fear that He will hold them accountable for all their sins, and not forgive them any of it.

This verse further describes the righteous as those who maintain the relationships and bonds that Allah has commanded and fear His judgment.

Verse 22

وَالَّذِينَ صَبَرُوا ابْتِغَاءَ وَجْهِ رَبِّهِمْ وَأَقَامُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَأَنْفَقُوا مِمَّا رَزَقْنَاهُمْ سِرًّا وَعَلَانِيَةً وَيَدْرَءُونَ بِالْحَسَنَةِ السَّيِّئَةَ أُولَٰئِكَ لَهُمْ عُقْبَى الدَّارِ

And ˹they are˺ those who endure patiently, seeking their Lord’s pleasure, establish prayer, donate from what We have provided for them—secretly and openly—and respond to evil with good. It is they who will have the ultimate abode:

And they are those who patiently endure harm, and obedience, and from disobedience seeking the pleasure of their Lord, and perform prayer in its most complete forms, and give from their wealth the prescribed Zakat, and the recommended expenditures in secret and in public, and they repel evil with good, so it erases it. Those described with these characteristics will have a praiseworthy outcome in the Hereafter.

The righteous patiently endure, seek Allah's pleasure, establish prayer, give charity, and respond to evil with good. These qualities lead to a praiseworthy outcome.

Verse 23

جَنَّاتُ عَدْنٍ يَدْخُلُونَهَا وَمَنْ صَلَحَ مِنْ آبَائِهِمْ وَأَزْوَاجِهِمْ وَذُرِّيَّاتِهِمْ ۖ وَالْمَلَائِكَةُ يَدْخُلُونَ عَلَيْهِمْ مِنْ كُلِّ بَابٍ

the Gardens of Eternity, which they will enter along with the righteous among their parents, spouses, and descendants. And the angels will enter upon them from every gate, ˹saying,˺

That outcome is the Gardens of Eden, where they will abide, never departing from it, and with them the righteous from among their fathers, spouses, and offspring of males and females, and the angels will enter upon them from every gate, to congratulate them on entering Paradise.

The ultimate abode for the righteous is the Gardens of Eternity, where they will be joined by their righteous families and greeted by angels.

Verse 24

سَلَامٌ عَلَيْكُمْ بِمَا صَبَرْتُمْ ۚ فَنِعْمَ عُقْبَى الدَّارِ

“Peace be upon you for your perseverance. How excellent is the ultimate abode!”

The angels will say to them: 'Peace be upon you, for you have been safe from all evil because of your patience in obeying Allah. So, how excellent is the final abode, Paradise.'

Angels will welcome the righteous into Paradise, congratulating them for their perseverance and highlighting the excellence of their final destination.

Verse 25

وَالَّذِينَ يَنْقُضُونَ عَهْدَ اللَّهِ مِنْ بَعْدِ مِيثَاقِهِ وَيَقْطَعُونَ مَا أَمَرَ اللَّهُ بِهِ أَنْ يُوصَلَ وَيُفْسِدُونَ فِي الْأَرْضِ ۙ أُولَٰئِكَ لَهُمُ اللَّعْنَةُ وَلَهُمْ سُوءُ الدَّارِ

And those who violate Allah’s covenant after it has been affirmed, break whatever ˹ties˺ Allah has ordered to be maintained, and spread corruption in the land—it is they who will be condemned and will have the worst abode.

As for the wretched, they have been described with the opposite of the believers' qualities, they are the ones who do not fulfill the covenant of Allah by singling Him out in worship, after they had affirmed it upon themselves, and they are the ones who sever what Allah has commanded to be joined, such as kinship ties and others, and they cause corruption in the land by committing sins, those described with these ugly qualities will be expelled from the mercy of Allah, and for them is what will displease them of severe torment in the Hereafter.

This verse contrasts the righteous with those who violate Allah's covenant, break relationships, and spread corruption. Their actions will lead to condemnation and a terrible fate.

Verse 26

اللَّهُ يَبْسُطُ الرِّزْقَ لِمَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيَقْدِرُ ۚ وَفَرِحُوا بِالْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَمَا الْحَيَاةُ الدُّنْيَا فِي الْآخِرَةِ إِلَّا مَتَاعٌ

Allah gives abundant or limited provisions to whoever He wills. And the disbelievers become prideful of ˹the pleasures of˺ this worldly life. But the life of this world, compared to the Hereafter, is nothing but a fleeting enjoyment.

Allah alone expands the provision for whom He wills of His servants, and restricts it for whom He wills among them, and the disbelievers rejoice in the abundance of the worldly life, and this worldly life in comparison to the Hereafter is but a little thing to be enjoyed, which will soon vanish.

Allah provides for whom He wills, but disbelievers are prideful of the worldly life. However, the worldly life is only temporary compared to the everlasting Hereafter.

Verse 27

وَيَقُولُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْلَا أُنْزِلَ عَلَيْهِ آيَةٌ مِنْ رَبِّهِ ۗ قُلْ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُضِلُّ مَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيَهْدِي إِلَيْهِ مَنْ أَنَابَ

The disbelievers say, “If only a sign could be sent down to him from his Lord.” Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Indeed, Allah leaves to stray whoever He wills, and guides to Himself whoever turns to Him—

And the disbelievers say defiantly: 'Why has there not been sent down upon Muhammad a perceptible miracle like the miracle of Moses and Jesus?' Say to them: 'Indeed, Allah leads astray whom He wills of the defiant from guidance, and miracles will not benefit him, and He guides to His true religion whoever turns to Him and seeks His pleasure.'

Disbelievers demand miracles, but Allah guides those who turn to Him while allowing others to stray. Miracles alone won't guide those who are defiant.

Verse 28

الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَتَطْمَئِنُّ قُلُوبُهُمْ بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ تَطْمَئِنُّ الْقُلُوبُ

those who believe and whose hearts find comfort in the remembrance of Allah. Surely in the remembrance of Allah do hearts find comfort.

And He guides those whose hearts find peace through the oneness of Allah and His remembrance, so they become tranquil. Indeed, through obedience to Allah and His remembrance and His reward, hearts find tranquility and comfort.

True believers find comfort and peace in the remembrance of Allah. This remembrance provides tranquility to their hearts.

Verse 29

الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ طُوبَىٰ لَهُمْ وَحُسْنُ مَآبٍ

Those who believe and do good, for them will be bliss and an honourable destination.”

Those who believed in Allah and His Messenger, and did righteous deeds, for them is joy and peace of mind, a good state, and a good return to the Paradise of Allah and His pleasure.

This verse describes the reward for believers who do good deeds: they will experience happiness and a blessed return to Paradise.

Verse 30

كَذَٰلِكَ أَرْسَلْنَاكَ فِي أُمَّةٍ قَدْ خَلَتْ مِنْ قَبْلِهَا أُمَمٌ لِتَتْلُوَ عَلَيْهِمُ الَّذِي أَوْحَيْنَا إِلَيْكَ وَهُمْ يَكْفُرُونَ بِالرَّحْمَٰنِ ۚ قُلْ هُوَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ عَلَيْهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ وَإِلَيْهِ مَتَابِ

And so We have sent you ˹O Prophet˺ to a community, like ˹We did with˺ earlier communities, so that you may recite to them what We have revealed to you. Yet they deny the Most Compassionate. Just as We sent messengers before you, We sent you - O Messenger - to a nation before which nations of messengers have passed, so that you recite to this nation the Quran revealed to you, while your people are denying the Oneness of the Most Gracious. Say to them - O Messenger -: The Most Gracious, whom you have not taken as a single god, is my Lord alone, there is no true deity except Him, upon Him I rely and trust, and to Him is my return and repentance.

The Prophet Muhammad is sent to his community to recite the Quran, despite their denial of Allah's oneness. The verse emphasizes that Allah is the only Lord worthy of worship, and all trust and repentance should be directed to Him.

Verse 31

وَلَوْ أَنَّ قُرْآنًا سُيِّرَتْ بِهِ الْجِبَالُ أَوْ قُطِّعَتْ بِهِ الْأَرْضُ أَوْ كُلِّمَ بِهِ الْمَوْتَىٰ ۗ بَلْ لِلَّهِ الْأَمْرُ جَمِيعًا ۗ أَفَلَمْ يَيْأَسِ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا أَنْ لَوْ يَشَاءُ اللَّهُ لَهَدَى النَّاسَ جَمِيعًا ۗ وَلَا يَزَالُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا تُصِيبُهُمْ بِمَا صَنَعُوا قَارِعَةٌ أَوْ تَحُلُّ قَرِيبًا مِنْ دَارِهِمْ حَتَّىٰ يَأْتِيَ وَعْدُ اللَّهِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يُخْلِفُ الْمِيعَادَ

If there were a recitation that could cause mountains to move, or the earth to split, or the dead to speak, ˹it would have been this Quran˺. But all matters are by Allah’s Will. Have the believers not yet realized that had Allah willed, He could have guided all of humanity? And disasters will continue to afflict the disbelievers or strike close to their homes for their misdeeds, until Allah’s promise comes to pass. Surely Allah never fails in His promise.

Allah - the Exalted - responds to the disbelievers who requested the sending down of tangible miracles to the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, saying to them: If there were a Quran that is recited by which mountains would be moved from their places, or by which the earth would be split into rivers, or by which the dead would be brought to life and speak - as they requested from you - this Quran would be characterized by that and none other, yet they would not believe in it. Rather, to Allah alone belongs the whole matter of miracles and everything else. Have not the believers known that if Allah willed, all the people of the earth would have believed without a miracle? The disbelievers will continue to be struck by calamity because of their disbelief, such as killing and captivity in the battles of the Muslims, or that calamity will descend near their homes, until the promise of Allah comes with victory over them. Indeed, Allah does not break His promise.

This verse highlights the Quran's power and Allah's absolute control over all matters. Even if the Quran could move mountains or raise the dead, faith is a matter of divine will. Disbelievers will face consequences for their actions, fulfilling Allah's promise.

Verse 32

وَلَقَدِ اسْتُهْزِئَ بِرُسُلٍ مِنْ قَبْلِكَ فَأَمْلَيْتُ لِلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا ثُمَّ أَخَذْتُهُمْ ۖ فَكَيْفَ كَانَ عِقَابِ

Other messengers had already been ridiculed before you, but I delayed the disbelievers ˹for a while˺ then seized them. And how ˹horrible˺ was My punishment!

And if they have mocked your call - O Messenger - then nations before you have mocked their messengers, so do not grieve, for I have given respite to those who disbelieved, then I seized them with My punishment, and it was a severe punishment.

This verse consoles the Prophet, reminding him that previous messengers were also ridiculed. Allah gives disbelievers respite, but ultimately they will face severe punishment.

Verse 33

أَفَمَنْ هُوَ قَائِمٌ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ نَفْسٍ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ ۗ وَجَعَلُوا لِلَّهِ شُرَكَاءَ قُلْ سَمُّوهُمْ ۚ أَمْ تُنَبِّئُونَهُ بِمَا لَا يَعْلَمُ فِي الْأَرْضِ أَمْ بِظَاهِرٍ مِنَ الْقَوْلِ ۗ بَلْ زُيِّنَ لِلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا مَكْرُهُمْ وَصُدُّوا عَنِ السَّبِيلِ ۗ وَمَنْ يُضْلِلِ اللَّهُ فَمَا لَهُ مِنْ هَادٍ

Is ˹there any equal to˺ the Vigilant One Who knows what every soul commits? Yet the pagans have associated others with Allah ˹in worship˺. Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Name them! Or do you ˹mean to˺ inform Him of something He does not know on the earth? Or are these ˹gods˺ just empty words?” In fact, the disbelievers’ falsehood has been made so appealing to them that they have been turned away from the Path. And whoever Allah leaves to stray will be left with no guide.

Is He who is standing over every soul, keeping track of what it does, more worthy of worship, or these incapable creatures? And they - out of their ignorance - have made partners for Allah from His creation whom they worship. Say to them - O Messenger -: Mention their names and attributes, and they will not find among their attributes anything that makes them worthy of worship. Or do you inform Allah of partners in His earth whom He does not know, or do you call them partners merely by a statement of the tongue without them having any reality? Rather, Satan has made fair-seeming to the disbelievers their false statements and has averted them from the path of Allah. And whoever Allah does not grant guidance to, then there is no one to guide him and make him successful in finding the truth and the right path.

This verse questions the validity of associating partners with Allah, the All-Knowing. Such associations are empty words, and those who follow them are led astray. Guidance is solely from Allah.

Verse 34

لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا ۖ وَلَعَذَابُ الْآخِرَةِ أَشَقُّ ۖ وَمَا لَهُمْ مِنَ اللَّهِ مِنْ وَاقٍ

For them is punishment in this worldly life, but the punishment of the Hereafter is truly far worse. And none can shield them from Allah.

For these disbelievers who hinder from the path of Allah, there is a painful punishment in the life of this world by killing, captivity, and disgrace, and their punishment in the Hereafter is heavier and more severe, and they have no preventer to prevent them from the punishment of Allah.

Disbelievers will face punishment in this life and a far worse punishment in the Hereafter. No one can protect them from Allah's retribution.

Verse 35

مَثَلُ الْجَنَّةِ الَّتِي وُعِدَ الْمُتَّقُونَ ۖ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ ۖ أُكُلُهَا دَائِمٌ وَظِلُّهَا ۚ تِلْكَ عُقْبَى الَّذِينَ اتَّقَوْا ۖ وَعُقْبَى الْكَافِرِينَ النَّارُ

The description of the Paradise promised to the righteous is that under it rivers flow; eternal is its fruit as well as its shade. That is the ˹ultimate˺ outcome for the righteous. But the outcome for the disbelievers is the Fire!

The description of Paradise which Allah has promised to those who fear Him is that rivers flow beneath its trees and palaces; its fruit is never-ending, and its shade never ceases nor diminishes. That is the reward of Paradise for those who feared Allah, and avoided His disobedience and performed His obligations; and the consequence for those who disbelieve in Allah is the Fire.

This verse describes Paradise as a place of flowing rivers, abundant and eternal fruits, and lasting shade, which is the reward for the righteous. In contrast, the disbelievers will face the Fire.

Verse 36

وَالَّذِينَ آتَيْنَاهُمُ الْكِتَابَ يَفْرَحُونَ بِمَا أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْكَ ۖ وَمِنَ الْأَحْزَابِ مَنْ يُنْكِرُ بَعْضَهُ ۚ قُلْ إِنَّمَا أُمِرْتُ أَنْ أَعْبُدَ اللَّهَ وَلَا أُشْرِكَ بِهِ ۚ إِلَيْهِ أَدْعُو وَإِلَيْهِ مَآبِ

˹The believers among˺ those who were given the Scripture rejoice at what has been revealed to you ˹O Prophet˺, while some ˹disbelieving˺ factions deny a portion of it. Say, “I have only been commanded to worship Allah, associating none with Him ˹in worship˺. To Him I invite ˹all˺, and to Him is my return.”

And those whom We have given the Scripture, from among the Jews and Christians, those who believe in you, like Abdullah ibn Salam and the Negus, rejoice in the Quran revealed to you because of its agreement with what they have. And from those who are allied against you in disbelief, like Al-Sayyid and Al-'Aqib, the two bishops of Najran, and Ka'b ibn al-Ashraf, are those who deny some of what is revealed to you. Say to them: Indeed, Allah has only commanded me to worship Him alone, and not to associate anything with Him. To His worship I invite people, and to Him is my return and destination.

Believers among those who received previous scriptures rejoice in the Quran, while some disbelievers reject parts of it. The Prophet is commanded to worship Allah alone, to invite all to Him, and to acknowledge that all will return to Him.

Verse 37

وَكَذَٰلِكَ أَنْزَلْنَاهُ حُكْمًا عَرَبِيًّا ۚ وَلَئِنِ اتَّبَعْتَ أَهْوَاءَهُمْ بَعْدَمَا جَاءَكَ مِنَ الْعِلْمِ مَا لَكَ مِنَ اللَّهِ مِنْ وَلِيٍّ وَلَا وَاقٍ

And so We have revealed it as an authority in Arabic. And if you were to follow their desires after ˹all˺ the knowledge that has come to you, there would be none to protect or shield you from Allah.

And just as We sent down the scriptures to the prophets in their language, We have sent down to you - O Messenger - the Quran in the Arabic language, so that you may judge with it. And if you were to follow the desires of the polytheists in worshipping other than Allah - after the truth that has come to you from Allah - then you would have no helper to aid you and protect you from His punishment.

The Quran was revealed in Arabic as a clear authority. The Prophet is warned against following the desires of disbelievers, emphasizing that there is no protector against Allah's punishment.

Verse 38

وَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا رُسُلًا مِنْ قَبْلِكَ وَجَعَلْنَا لَهُمْ أَزْوَاجًا وَذُرِّيَّةً ۚ وَمَا كَانَ لِرَسُولٍ أَنْ يَأْتِيَ بِآيَةٍ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِ اللَّهِ ۗ لِكُلِّ أَجَلٍ كِتَابٌ

We have certainly sent messengers before you ˹O Prophet˺ and blessed them with wives and offspring. It was not for any messenger to bring a sign without Allah’s permission. Every destined matter has a ˹set˺ time.

And if they say, "What is with you, O Messenger, that you marry women?" Then We have sent before you messengers from mankind, and We gave them spouses and offspring. And if they say, "If he were a messenger, he would have brought what we asked of miracles," then it is not within the power of a messenger to bring a miracle that his people desire except by the permission of Allah. For every matter that Allah has decreed there is a book and a term that Allah has written with Him, it does not precede nor lag.

Allah sent messengers before the Prophet, who had wives and children. Messengers can only perform miracles with Allah's permission. Every matter has a predetermined time.

Verse 39

يَمْحُو اللَّهُ مَا يَشَاءُ وَيُثْبِتُ ۖ وَعِنْدَهُ أُمُّ الْكِتَابِ

Allah eliminates and confirms what He wills, and with Him is the Master Record.

Allah eliminates what He wills of decrees and other things, and He keeps what He wills of them for a wisdom He knows. And with Him is the Mother of the Book, which is the Preserved Tablet.

Allah has the power to eliminate or confirm any matter as He wills. The *Master Record* (or *Preserved Tablet*) with Allah contains all decrees.

Verse 40

وَإِنْ مَا نُرِيَنَّكَ بَعْضَ الَّذِي نَعِدُهُمْ أَوْ نَتَوَفَّيَنَّكَ فَإِنَّمَا عَلَيْكَ الْبَلَاغُ وَعَلَيْنَا الْحِسَابُ

Whether We show you ˹O Prophet˺ some of what We threaten them with, or cause you to die ˹before that˺, your duty is only to deliver ˹the message˺. Judgment is for Us.

And if We show you - O Messenger - some of the punishment with which We have threatened your enemies, of disgrace and retribution in this world, then that is expedited for them. And if We cause you to die before you see that, then your only duty is to deliver the message, and upon Us is the reckoning and recompense.

Whether the Prophet sees the punishment of the disbelievers or not, his duty is to deliver the message. Allah is the sole Judge, and all will be accountable to Him.

Verse 41

أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا أَنَّا نَأْتِي الْأَرْضَ نَنْقُصُهَا مِنْ أَطْرَافِهَا ۚ وَاللَّهُ يَحْكُمُ لَا مُعَقِّبَ لِحُكْمِهِ ۚ وَهُوَ سَرِيعُ الْحِسَابِ

Do they not see that We gradually reduce their land from its borders? Allah decides—none can reverse His decision. And He is swift in reckoning.

Have not these disbelievers seen that We come to the land, diminishing it from its edges, by the Muslims conquering the lands of the polytheists and annexing them to the lands of the Muslims? And Allah judges; there is no repeller of His judgment and decree, and He is swift in reckoning, so let them not hasten the punishment, for everything that is coming is near.

The disbelievers are asked if they do not see how their land is being reduced at its borders, a reference to the expansion of the Muslim lands. Allah's judgment is irreversible and swift.

Verse 42

وَقَدْ مَكَرَ الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ فَلِلَّهِ الْمَكْرُ جَمِيعًا ۖ يَعْلَمُ مَا تَكْسِبُ كُلُّ نَفْسٍ ۗ وَسَيَعْلَمُ الْكُفَّارُ لِمَنْ عُقْبَى الدَّارِ

Those ˹disbelievers˺ before them ˹secretly˺ planned, but Allah has the ultimate plan. He knows what every soul commits. And the disbelievers will soon know who will have the ultimate outcome.

And those before them had plotted against their messengers, just as these have done with you, but to Allah belongs all planning, so He nullifies their plots and returns it to them with disappointment and regret. He knows, glory be to Him, what every soul earns of good or evil, so it will be recompensed for it. And the disbelievers will know - when they come before their Lord - to whom the praiseworthy outcome will be after this world. Indeed, it is for the followers of the messengers. And in this is a threat and warning for the disbelievers.

Disbelievers before them plotted against messengers, but Allah's plan is supreme. He knows what every soul commits, and disbelievers will realize the praiseworthy outcome is for the followers of the messengers.

Verse 43

وَيَقُولُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَسْتَ مُرْسَلًا ۚ قُلْ كَفَىٰ بِاللَّهِ شَهِيدًا بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَكُمْ وَمَنْ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ الْكِتَابِ

The disbelievers say, “You ˹Muḥammad˺ are no messenger.” Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “Allah is sufficient as a Witness between me and you, as is whoever has knowledge of the Scripture.”

And those who disbelieve say to the Prophet of Allah: -O Muhammad- Allah did not send you. Say to them: Sufficient is Allah as a witness to my truthfulness and your falsehood, and sufficient is the testimony of those who have knowledge of the Scripture from the Jews and Christians who believed in my message, and what I have come with from Allah, and followed the truth and declared that testimony, and did not conceal it.

This verse addresses the disbelievers' rejection of Prophet Muhammad's message. It asserts that Allah and those with knowledge of previous scriptures serve as witnesses to his truthfulness.
