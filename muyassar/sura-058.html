Sura 058

Verse 1

قَدْ سَمِعَ اللَّهُ قَوْلَ الَّتِي تُجَادِلُكَ فِي زَوْجِهَا وَتَشْتَكِي إِلَى اللَّهِ وَاللَّهُ يَسْمَعُ تَحَاوُرَكُمَا ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ سَمِيعٌ بَصِيرٌ

Indeed, Allah has heard the argument of the woman who pleaded with you ˹O Prophet˺ concerning her husband, and appealed to Allah. Allah has heard your exchange. Surely Allah is All-Hearing, All-Seeing.

Allah has heard the statement of Khawlah bint Tha'labah, who argued about her husband Aws ibn as-Samit, and regarding what he uttered in her case concerning Zihar, which is his saying to her: "You are to me like my mother's back," meaning in the prohibition of marriage, while she was imploring Allah Almighty to relieve her distress. And Allah hears your conversation and your argument. Indeed, Allah is All-Hearing of every statement, All-Seeing of everything, nothing is hidden from Him.

This verse highlights Allah's awareness and attentiveness to the complaints of a woman regarding her husband's words. It emphasizes that Allah hears all and sees all, and is aware of our private matters.

Verse 2

الَّذِينَ يُظَاهِرُونَ مِنْكُمْ مِنْ نِسَائِهِمْ مَا هُنَّ أُمَّهَاتِهِمْ ۖ إِنْ أُمَّهَاتُهُمْ إِلَّا اللَّائِي وَلَدْنَهُمْ ۚ وَإِنَّهُمْ لَيَقُولُونَ مُنْكَرًا مِنَ الْقَوْلِ وَزُورًا ۚ وَإِنَّ اللَّهَ لَعَفُوٌّ غَفُورٌ

Those of you who ˹sinfully˺ divorce their wives by comparing them to their mothers ˹should know that˺ their wives are in no way their mothers. None can be their mothers except those who gave birth to them. What they say is certainly detestable and false. Yet Allah is truly Ever-Pardoning, All-Forgiving.

Those who pronounce Zihar among you to their wives - where a man says to his wife, "You are to me like my mother's back" - meaning in the prohibition of marriage - they have disobeyed Allah and violated the law. Their wives are not their mothers in reality, but they are their wives. Their mothers are only those who gave them birth. And indeed, these pronouncers of Zihar are saying a false and terrible statement, the validity of which is not known. And Allah is Forgiving and Merciful to whoever has committed some violations, and then rectified them with sincere repentance.

This verse addresses the practice of 'Zihar,' where a man compares his wife to his mother. It clarifies that such a comparison is false and unacceptable in Islam, but Allah is forgiving for those who repent.

Verse 3

وَالَّذِينَ يُظَاهِرُونَ مِنْ نِسَائِهِمْ ثُمَّ يَعُودُونَ لِمَا قَالُوا فَتَحْرِيرُ رَقَبَةٍ مِنْ قَبْلِ أَنْ يَتَمَاسَّا ۚ ذَٰلِكُمْ تُوعَظُونَ بِهِ ۚ وَاللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌ

Those who divorce their wives in this manner, then ˹wish to˺ retract what they said, must free a slave before they touch each other. This ˹penalty˺ is meant to deter you. And Allah is All-Aware of what you do.

And those who forbid their wives to themselves through Zihar, and then retract their statement and resolve to have intercourse with their wives, then the expiation for this pronouncement falls on the husband- in this case, and that is the freeing of a believing slave, male or female, before he has intercourse with his wife whom he had declared as forbidden. That is Allah's ruling concerning who pronounces Zihar to his wife, you are admonished by it, O believers, so that you do not fall into Zihar and false statements, and to atone if you do fall into it, and not to return to it. And Allah is not unaware of anything from your deeds, and He will recompense you for them.

This verse outlines the expiation for 'Zihar,' requiring the freeing of a slave before intimacy is resumed. This is to act as a deterrent. Allah is aware of all actions.

Verse 4

فَمَنْ لَمْ يَجِدْ فَصِيَامُ شَهْرَيْنِ مُتَتَابِعَيْنِ مِنْ قَبْلِ أَنْ يَتَمَاسَّا ۖ فَمَنْ لَمْ يَسْتَطِعْ فَإِطْعَامُ سِتِّينَ مِسْكِينًا ۚ ذَٰلِكَ لِتُؤْمِنُوا بِاللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ ۚ وَتِلْكَ حُدُودُ اللَّهِ ۗ وَلِلْكَافِرِينَ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ

But if the husband cannot afford this, let him then fast two consecutive months before the couple touch each other. But if he is unable ˹to fast˺, then let him feed sixty poor people. This is to re-affirm your faith in Allah and His Messenger. These are the limits set by Allah. And the disbelievers will suffer a painful punishment.

And whoever does not find a slave to free, then he must fast for two consecutive months before he has intercourse with his wife. And whoever cannot fast for two months due to a legitimate excuse, then he must feed sixty poor people enough to satisfy them. That which we have made clear to you concerning the rulings of Zihar is in order that you believe in Allah and follow His Messenger and act upon what Allah has prescribed, and abandon what you were upon in your ignorance. And those mentioned rulings are the commands and limits of Allah, so do not transgress them. And for those who deny them, there is a painful punishment.

If freeing a slave is not possible, then fasting for two consecutive months is required before resuming intimacy. If that is not possible, feeding sixty poor people. These are the limits set by Allah, and those who deny will receive severe punishment.

Verse 5

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يُحَادُّونَ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ كُبِتُوا كَمَا كُبِتَ الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ ۚ وَقَدْ أَنْزَلْنَا آيَاتٍ بَيِّنَاتٍ ۚ وَلِلْكَافِرِينَ عَذَابٌ مُهِينٌ

Indeed, those who defy Allah and His Messenger will be debased, just like those before them. We have certainly sent down clear revelations. And the disbelievers will suffer a humiliating punishment.

Indeed, those who oppose Allah and His Messenger and disobey their command have been humiliated and disgraced, just as those before them from the nations who opposed Allah and His Messengers were humiliated. And We have revealed clear verses, the proof of which demonstrates that Allah's law and limits are truth, and for the deniers of those verses, there is a humiliating punishment in Hell.

This verse warns that those who defy Allah and His Messenger will be humiliated, similar to those who opposed them in the past. The Quran provides clear revelations for those who believe.

Verse 6

يَوْمَ يَبْعَثُهُمُ اللَّهُ جَمِيعًا فَيُنَبِّئُهُمْ بِمَا عَمِلُوا ۚ أَحْصَاهُ اللَّهُ وَنَسُوهُ ۚ وَاللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ شَهِيدٌ

On the Day Allah resurrects them all together, He will then inform them of what they have done. Allah has kept account of it all, while they have forgotten it. For Allah is a Witness over all things.

And remember - O Messenger - the Day of Resurrection, the day when Allah will resurrect all the dead, and gather the first and last in one place, and He will inform them of what they did of good and evil, which Allah has recorded and written in the Preserved Tablet, and He has kept it recorded for them in their records of deeds, while they have forgotten it. And Allah is Witness over everything, nothing is hidden from Him.

This verse describes the Day of Judgment when Allah will inform everyone of their deeds, both good and bad. Allah has recorded everything, and nothing is hidden from Him, even if we forget.

Verse 7

أَلَمْ تَرَ أَنَّ اللَّهَ يَعْلَمُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۖ مَا يَكُونُ مِنْ نَجْوَىٰ ثَلَاثَةٍ إِلَّا هُوَ رَابِعُهُمْ وَلَا خَمْسَةٍ إِلَّا هُوَ سَادِسُهُمْ وَلَا أَدْنَىٰ مِنْ ذَٰلِكَ وَلَا أَكْثَرَ إِلَّا هُوَ مَعَهُمْ أَيْنَ مَا كَانُوا ۖ ثُمَّ يُنَبِّئُهُمْ بِمَا عَمِلُوا يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيمٌ

Do you not see that Allah knows whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth? If three converse privately, He is their fourth. If five, He is their sixth. Whether fewer or more, He is with them wherever they may be. Then, on the Day of Judgment, He will inform them of what they have done. Surely Allah has ˹perfect˺ knowledge of all things.

Do you not know that Allah the Exalted knows everything in the heavens and the earth? There is no secret consultation of three of His creation, but He is their fourth with His knowledge and encompassing, nor of five but He is their sixth, nor less than these mentioned numbers nor more, but He is with them by His knowledge wherever they may be. Nothing of their affairs is hidden from Him. Then He will inform them, on the Day of Resurrection, of what they did of good and evil, and He will reward them for it. Indeed, Allah is Knowing of all things.

This verse emphasizes Allah's comprehensive knowledge and awareness. No matter how private a conversation may be, Allah is fully aware of it, and He will hold everyone accountable on the Day of Judgment. Allah's knowledge encompasses all things.

Verse 8

أَلَمْ تَرَ إِلَى الَّذِينَ نُهُوا عَنِ النَّجْوَىٰ ثُمَّ يَعُودُونَ لِمَا نُهُوا عَنْهُ وَيَتَنَاجَوْنَ بِالْإِثْمِ وَالْعُدْوَانِ وَمَعْصِيَتِ الرَّسُولِ وَإِذَا جَاءُوكَ حَيَّوْكَ بِمَا لَمْ يُحَيِّكَ بِهِ اللَّهُ وَيَقُولُونَ فِي أَنْفُسِهِمْ لَوْلَا يُعَذِّبُنَا اللَّهُ بِمَا نَقُولُ ۚ حَسْبُهُمْ جَهَنَّمُ يَصْلَوْنَهَا ۖ فَبِئْسَ الْمَصِيرُ

Have you not seen those who were forbidden from secret talks, yet they ˹always˺ return to what they were forbidden from, conspiring in sin, aggression, and disobedience to the Messenger? And when they come to you ˹O Prophet˺, they greet you not as Allah greets you, and say to one another, “Why does Allah not punish us for what we say?” Hell is enough for them—they will burn in it. And what an evil destination!

Have you not seen - O Messenger - those Jews who were forbidden to converse secretly in a way that raises doubt in the hearts of the believers, then they return to what they were forbidden, and converse secretly about what is sin, aggression, and defiance of the Messenger's command? And if these Jews come to you - O Messenger - for a matter of affairs, they greet you with other than the greeting that Allah has made a greeting for you, saying, "As-sam 'alayk," meaning: "Death be upon you." And they say among themselves: "Why does Allah not punish us for what we say to Muhammad if he is truly a messenger?” Hell is enough for them; they will enter it and suffer its heat. How evil is that destination!

This verse condemns those who were prohibited from secret talks but persisted in them, particularly when those talks involved sin and disobedience. It also highlights the insincere greetings they gave to the Prophet and their defiance, indicating their hypocrisy and inviting their punishment in Hell.

Verse 9

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا تَنَاجَيْتُمْ فَلَا تَتَنَاجَوْا بِالْإِثْمِ وَالْعُدْوَانِ وَمَعْصِيَتِ الرَّسُولِ وَتَنَاجَوْا بِالْبِرِّ وَالتَّقْوَىٰ ۖ وَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ الَّذِي إِلَيْهِ تُحْشَرُونَ

O believers! When you converse privately, let it not be for sin, aggression, or disobedience to the Messenger, but let it be for goodness and righteousness. And fear Allah, to Whom you will ˹all˺ be gathered.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, when you converse among yourselves secretly, do not converse about what is sinful in speech, or what is aggression against others, or defiance of the Messenger's command, and converse about what is good, obedience, and kindness, and fear Allah by complying with His commands and avoiding His prohibitions, for to Him alone is your return with all your deeds and words that He has enumerated for you, and He will recompense you for them.

Believers are instructed to ensure their private conversations focus on good and righteousness, and to avoid sin, aggression, and defiance. The verse reminds them of their accountability to Allah on the Day of Gathering, emphasizing the importance of righteous communication.

Verse 10

إِنَّمَا النَّجْوَىٰ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ لِيَحْزُنَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَلَيْسَ بِضَارِّهِمْ شَيْئًا إِلَّا بِإِذْنِ اللَّهِ ۚ وَعَلَى اللَّهِ فَلْيَتَوَكَّلِ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ

Secret talks are only inspired by Satan to grieve the believers. Yet he cannot harm them whatsoever except by Allah’s Will. So in Allah let the believers put their trust.

Indeed, secret conversations of sin and aggression are from the whispers of Satan, he is the one who adorns them and motivates towards them, to bring grief to the hearts of the believers, and that is not harmful to the believers except by the will and desire of Allah. And upon Allah alone should the believers rely.

Secret and harmful conversations are inspired by Satan to cause grief among believers. However, Satan can only cause harm by the will of Allah. Therefore, believers are urged to place their trust solely in Allah, highlighting the importance of faith in overcoming evil influences.

Verse 11

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا فِي الْمَجَالِسِ فَافْسَحُوا يَفْسَحِ اللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ انْشُزُوا فَانْشُزُوا يَرْفَعِ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مِنْكُمْ وَالَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْعِلْمَ دَرَجَاتٍ ۚ وَاللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌ

O believers! When you are told to make room in gatherings, then do so. Allah will make room for you ˹in His grace˺. And if you are told to rise, then do so. Allah will elevate those of you who are faithful, and ˹raise˺ those gifted with knowledge in rank. And Allah is All-Aware of what you do.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, when you are asked to make room in your gatherings, then make room. Allah will make room for you in this world and the Hereafter. And if you are asked - O believers - to rise from your gatherings for a matter of affairs in which there is good for you, then rise. Allah will raise the status of the sincere believers among you, and will raise the status of the people of knowledge many degrees in reward and ranks of approval. And Allah the Exalted is Aware of your deeds, nothing of them is hidden from Him, and He will recompense you for them. And in the verse is a mention of the status of scholars and their virtue, and the raising of their ranks.

This verse encourages believers to be accommodating in gatherings, making room for others when asked. Allah will reward them for their humility and generosity, and He will also elevate those with faith and knowledge. The verse acknowledges the value of both faith and learning.

Verse 12

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا نَاجَيْتُمُ الرَّسُولَ فَقَدِّمُوا بَيْنَ يَدَيْ نَجْوَاكُمْ صَدَقَةً ۚ ذَٰلِكَ خَيْرٌ لَكُمْ وَأَطْهَرُ ۚ فَإِنْ لَمْ تَجِدُوا فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ

O believers! When you consult the Messenger privately, give something in charity before your consultation. That is better and purer for you. But if you lack the means, then Allah is truly All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, when you intend to speak privately to the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, then offer charity to the needy before that. That is better for you because of the reward in it, and purer for your hearts from sins. If you do not find anything to give in charity, then there is no blame upon you; for Allah is Forgiving to His believing servants, Merciful to them.

This verse initially instructed believers to give charity before private consultations with the Prophet (peace be upon him). It emphasizes that charity is good and purifies the heart. However, it also acknowledges that if one cannot afford charity, Allah is Forgiving and Merciful.

Verse 13

أَأَشْفَقْتُمْ أَنْ تُقَدِّمُوا بَيْنَ يَدَيْ نَجْوَاكُمْ صَدَقَاتٍ ۚ فَإِذْ لَمْ تَفْعَلُوا وَتَابَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْكُمْ فَأَقِيمُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَآتُوا الزَّكَاةَ وَأَطِيعُوا اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ ۚ وَاللَّهُ خَبِيرٌ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ

Are you afraid of spending in charity before your private consultations ˹with him˺? Since you are unable to do so, and Allah has turned to you in mercy, then ˹continue to˺ establish prayer, pay alms-tax, and obey Allah and His Messenger. And Allah is All-Aware of what you do.

Did you fear poverty if you gave charity before your private conversation with the Messenger of Allah? So, since you did not do what you were commanded to do, and Allah has turned to you in forgiveness, and granted you permission not to do it, then be steadfast and persistent in establishing prayer, giving Zakat, and obeying Allah and His Messenger in all that you have been commanded. And Allah is Knowing of your deeds, and He will recompense you for them.

This verse acknowledges the believers' inability to consistently give charity before consultations, and Allah has eased this requirement. It then emphasizes the importance of prayer, charity, and obedience to Allah and His Messenger.

Verse 14

أَلَمْ تَرَ إِلَى الَّذِينَ تَوَلَّوْا قَوْمًا غَضِبَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِمْ مَا هُمْ مِنْكُمْ وَلَا مِنْهُمْ وَيَحْلِفُونَ عَلَى الْكَذِبِ وَهُمْ يَعْلَمُونَ

Have you not seen those ˹hypocrites˺ who ally themselves with a people with whom Allah is displeased? They are neither with you nor with them. And they swear to lies knowingly.

Have you not seen the hypocrites who have taken the Jews as friends and allies? And the hypocrites, in reality, are neither of the Muslims nor of the Jews, and they swear falsely that they are Muslims and that you are the Messenger of Allah, while they know that they are liars in what they have sworn.

This verse speaks of hypocrites who align themselves with those who have incurred Allah's displeasure. They are not true believers, nor are they with those they ally with, and they knowingly lie about their faith.

Verse 15

أَعَدَّ اللَّهُ لَهُمْ عَذَابًا شَدِيدًا ۖ إِنَّهُمْ سَاءَ مَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

Allah has prepared for them a severe punishment. Evil indeed is what they do.

Allah has prepared for these hypocrites a severe and painful punishment. Indeed, evil is what they used to do of hypocrisy and swearing falsely.

This verse warns that Allah has prepared a severe punishment for these hypocrites due to their hypocrisy and deceitful actions.

Verse 16

اتَّخَذُوا أَيْمَانَهُمْ جُنَّةً فَصَدُّوا عَنْ سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ فَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ مُهِينٌ

They have made their ˹false˺ oaths as a shield, hindering ˹others˺ from the cause of Allah. So they will suffer a humiliating punishment.

The hypocrites have taken their false oaths as a shield for them from being killed because of their disbelief, and to prevent the Muslims from fighting them and taking their wealth. Because of that, they have turned themselves and others away from the path of Allah, which is Islam. So, for them is a humiliating punishment in the Fire because of their arrogance towards belief in Allah and His Messenger and turning others away from His path.

The hypocrites use their false oaths as a shield to protect themselves and to hinder others from the path of Allah, leading to a humiliating punishment in the Hereafter.

Verse 17

لَنْ تُغْنِيَ عَنْهُمْ أَمْوَالُهُمْ وَلَا أَوْلَادُهُمْ مِنَ اللَّهِ شَيْئًا ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ أَصْحَابُ النَّارِ ۖ هُمْ فِيهَا خَالِدُونَ

Neither their wealth nor children will be of any help to them against Allah whatsoever. It is they who will be the residents of the Fire. They will be there forever.

Neither their wealth nor their children will avail the hypocrites against Allah's punishment at all. Those are the people of the Fire; they will enter it and abide therein forever; they will not exit from it. And this reward includes everyone who turns away from the religion of Allah by his word or deed.

Neither wealth nor children will protect the hypocrites from Allah's punishment. They are destined for the Fire, where they will abide forever.

Verse 18

يَوْمَ يَبْعَثُهُمُ اللَّهُ جَمِيعًا فَيَحْلِفُونَ لَهُ كَمَا يَحْلِفُونَ لَكُمْ ۖ وَيَحْسَبُونَ أَنَّهُمْ عَلَىٰ شَيْءٍ ۚ أَلَا إِنَّهُمْ هُمُ الْكَاذِبُونَ

On the Day Allah resurrects them all, they will ˹falsely˺ swear to Him as they swear to you, thinking they have something to stand on. Indeed, it is they who are the ˹total˺ liars.

On the Day of Resurrection, Allah will resurrect the hypocrites all together from their graves alive, and they will swear to Him that they were believers, just as they used to swear to you, O believers, in the world, and they believe that this will benefit them with Allah just as it benefited them in the world with the Muslims. Indeed, they are the ones who are extreme in lying to a degree that no others have reached.

On the Day of Resurrection, hypocrites will swear to Allah that they were believers, just as they did in life, falsely believing it will help them. However, they are the greatest of liars.

Verse 19

اسْتَحْوَذَ عَلَيْهِمُ الشَّيْطَانُ فَأَنْسَاهُمْ ذِكْرَ اللَّهِ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ حِزْبُ الشَّيْطَانِ ۚ أَلَا إِنَّ حِزْبَ الشَّيْطَانِ هُمُ الْخَاسِرُونَ

Satan has taken hold of them, causing them to forget the remembrance of Allah. They are the party of Satan. Surely Satan’s party is bound to lose.

Satan has overcome them and taken control over them, until they abandoned the commands of Allah and acting in obedience to Him. Those are the party of Satan and his followers. Indeed, the party of Satan are the losers in this world and the Hereafter.

Satan has overcome the hypocrites, causing them to forget Allah. They are the party of Satan, and those who follow Satan are destined to lose.

Verse 20

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يُحَادُّونَ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ أُولَٰئِكَ فِي الْأَذَلِّينَ

˹As for˺ those who defy Allah and His Messenger, they will definitely be among the most debased.

Indeed, those who oppose the command of Allah and His Messenger, they are among the humiliated, defeated, and disgraced in this world and the Hereafter.

Those who defy Allah and His Messenger will be among the most humiliated and disgraced in this world and the Hereafter.

Verse 21

كَتَبَ اللَّهُ لَأَغْلِبَنَّ أَنَا وَرُسُلِي ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ قَوِيٌّ عَزِيزٌ

Allah has decreed, “I and My messengers will certainly prevail.” Surely Allah is All-Powerful, Almighty.

Allah has decreed in the Preserved Tablet and judged that victory is for Him and for His Book and His Messengers and His believing servants. Indeed, Allah is Powerful, nothing can defeat Him; Honored over His creation.

Allah has decreed that He and His messengers will be victorious. Allah is All-Powerful and Almighty and cannot be defeated.

Verse 22

لَا تَجِدُ قَوْمًا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ يُوَادُّونَ مَنْ حَادَّ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ وَلَوْ كَانُوا آبَاءَهُمْ أَوْ أَبْنَاءَهُمْ أَوْ إِخْوَانَهُمْ أَوْ عَشِيرَتَهُمْ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ كَتَبَ فِي قُلُوبِهِمُ الْإِيمَانَ وَأَيَّدَهُمْ بِرُوحٍ مِنْهُ ۖ وَيُدْخِلُهُمْ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا عَنْهُ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ حِزْبُ اللَّهِ ۚ أَلَا إِنَّ حِزْبَ اللَّهِ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ

You will never find a people who ˹truly˺ believe in Allah and the Last Day loyal to those who defy Allah and His Messenger, even if they were their parents, children, siblings, or extended family. For those ˹believers˺, Allah has instilled faith in their hearts and strengthened them with a spirit from Him. He will admit them into Gardens under which rivers flow, to stay there forever. Allah is pleased with them and they are pleased with Him. They are the party of Allah. Indeed, Allah’s party is bound to succeed.

You will not find, [O Messenger], a people who believe in Allah and the Last Day and act according to what Allah has legislated for them, loving and befriending those who oppose Allah and His Messenger and disobey their commands, even if they were their fathers or their sons or their brothers or their relatives. Those are the ones whose hearts Allah has inscribed with faith and strengthened them with support from Him and victory over their enemies in this world, and He will admit them in the Hereafter into gardens beneath which rivers flow, abiding therein for an extended time that will never cease. Allah has granted them His pleasure, so He will not be displeased with them, and they are pleased with their Lord for what He has given them of honors and high ranks. Those are the party of Allah and His allies, and those are the ones who are the winners of happiness in this world and the Hereafter.

True believers will never prioritize relationships with those who oppose Allah and His Messenger, even if they are family. Allah instills faith in the hearts of believers and will reward them with Paradise. They are the party of Allah, and they will be successful.
