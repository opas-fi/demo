Sura 048

Verse 1

إِنَّا فَتَحْنَا لَكَ فَتْحًا مُبِينًا

Indeed, We have granted you a clear triumph ˹O Prophet˺

Indeed, We have granted you, [O Prophet], a clear victory, through which Allah will make your religion apparent, grant you victory over your enemy. It is the truce of “Al-Hudaybiyah”, which made people feel safe with each other, thus expanding the circle of invitation to the religion of Allah. It enabled those who wish to know the truth of Islam to learn about it, and during that time, people entered the religion of Allah in crowds; and that is why Allah called it a clear victory, meaning an apparent and obvious one.

This verse refers to a clear victory granted to Prophet Muhammad, which is understood to be the truce of Hudaybiyah. This treaty paved the way for wider acceptance of Islam.

Verse 2

لِيَغْفِرَ لَكَ اللَّهُ مَا تَقَدَّمَ مِنْ ذَنْبِكَ وَمَا تَأَخَّرَ وَيُتِمَّ نِعْمَتَهُ عَلَيْكَ وَيَهْدِيَكَ صِرَاطًا مُسْتَقِيمًا

so that Allah may forgive you for your past and future shortcomings, perfect His favour upon you, guide you along the Straight Path,

We have granted you that victory, and made it easy for you; so that Allah may forgive your past and future sins because of the many acts of obedience resulting from this victory, and because of the hardships you have endured. He may complete His favor upon you by making your religion apparent and granting you victory over your enemies. And He may guide you to a straight path of religion in which there is no crookedness, and Allah will grant you a mighty victory, in which Islam will not be weakened.

This verse explains the purpose of the victory: that Allah may forgive past and future shortcomings, perfect His favor, and guide the Prophet to the Straight Path, establishing the strength of Islam.

Verse 3

وَيَنْصُرَكَ اللَّهُ نَصْرًا عَزِيزًا

and so that Allah will help you tremendously.

We have granted you that victory, and made it easy for you; so that Allah may forgive your past and future sins because of the many acts of obedience resulting from this victory, and because of the hardships you have endured. He may complete His favor upon you by making your religion apparent and granting you victory over your enemies. And He may guide you to a straight path of religion in which there is no crookedness, and Allah will grant you a mighty victory, in which Islam will not be weakened.

This verse emphasizes that Allah will provide tremendous support to the Prophet, reinforcing the might and expansion of Islam.

Verse 4

هُوَ الَّذِي أَنْزَلَ السَّكِينَةَ فِي قُلُوبِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ لِيَزْدَادُوا إِيمَانًا مَعَ إِيمَانِهِمْ ۗ وَلِلَّهِ جُنُودُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ وَكَانَ اللَّهُ عَلِيمًا حَكِيمًا

He is the One Who sent down serenity upon the hearts of the believers so that they may increase even more in their faith. To Allah ˹alone˺ belong the forces of the heavens and the earth. And Allah is All-Knowing, All-Wise.

He is Allah who sent down tranquility into the hearts of the believers in Allah and His Messenger on the day of "Al-Hudaybiyah," so they were calmed, and certainty was established within them, so that they might increase in their belief in Allah and their adherence to His Messenger along with their belief and adherence. And to Allah, glory be to Him, belong the soldiers of the heavens and the earth, with whom He supports His believing servants. And Allah is Knowing of the interests of His creation, Wise in His planning and making.

Allah grants serenity to believers, strengthening their faith. He is the owner of all forces in the heavens and the earth, and He is All-Knowing and All-Wise.

Verse 5

لِيُدْخِلَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَالْمُؤْمِنَاتِ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا وَيُكَفِّرَ عَنْهُمْ سَيِّئَاتِهِمْ ۚ وَكَانَ ذَٰلِكَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ فَوْزًا عَظِيمًا

So He may admit believing men and women into Gardens under which rivers flow—to stay there forever—and absolve them of their sins. And that is a supreme achievement in the sight of Allah.

That Allah may admit the believing men and believing women into gardens beneath which rivers flow, to abide therein forever, and remove from them the evil of what they did, so He will not punish them for it. And that reward was with Allah salvation from every sorrow and success in every request.

Allah promises believing men and women gardens in Paradise, where they will live forever. Their sins are forgiven, and this is the supreme achievement in Allah's sight.

Verse 6

وَيُعَذِّبَ الْمُنَافِقِينَ وَالْمُنَافِقَاتِ وَالْمُشْرِكِينَ وَالْمُشْرِكَاتِ الظَّانِّينَ بِاللَّهِ ظَنَّ السَّوْءِ ۚ عَلَيْهِمْ دَائِرَةُ السَّوْءِ ۖ وَغَضِبَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَعَنَهُمْ وَأَعَدَّ لَهُمْ جَهَنَّمَ ۖ وَسَاءَتْ مَصِيرًا

Also ˹so that˺ He may punish hypocrite men and women and polytheistic men and women, who harbour evil thoughts of Allah. May ill-fate befall them! Allah is displeased with them. He has condemned them and prepared for them Hell. What an evil destination!

And Allah will punish the hypocrite men and hypocrite women, and the polytheist men and polytheist women, who think an evil thought of Allah—that He would not grant victory to His Prophet and the believers with him over their enemies, and that He would not make His religion apparent. Upon them falls the circle of torment and all that brings them pain, and Allah has become angry with them and cast them out from His mercy, and has prepared for them Hellfire; and it is an evil destination to which they will return.

Allah will punish hypocrites and polytheists who have evil thoughts about Him. They are under His displeasure, condemned and destined for Hell.

Verse 7

وَلِلَّهِ جُنُودُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ وَكَانَ اللَّهُ عَزِيزًا حَكِيمًا

To Allah ˹alone˺ belong the forces of the heavens and the earth. And Allah is Almighty, All-Wise.

And to Allah, glory be to Him, belong the soldiers of the heavens and the earth, with whom He supports His believing servants. And Allah is Mighty over His creation, Wise in managing their affairs.

This verse reiterates that the forces of the heavens and the earth belong to Allah, who is Almighty and Wise in managing all affairs.

Verse 8

إِنَّا أَرْسَلْنَاكَ شَاهِدًا وَمُبَشِّرًا وَنَذِيرًا

Indeed, ˹O Prophet,˺ We have sent you as a witness, a deliverer of good news, and a warner,

Indeed, We have sent you, [O Prophet], as a witness over your nation, conveying to them what We have sent you with, and as a bringer of good tidings of Paradise for those who obey you, and as a warner of the swift and deferred punishment for those who disobey you. So that you may believe in Allah and His Messenger, and support Allah by supporting His religion, and venerate Him, and glorify Him at the beginning and end of the day.

Prophet Muhammad is sent as a witness, a bearer of good news, and a warner, conveying Allah's message to mankind.

Verse 9

لِتُؤْمِنُوا بِاللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ وَتُعَزِّرُوهُ وَتُوَقِّرُوهُ وَتُسَبِّحُوهُ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا

so that you ˹believers˺ may have faith in Allah and His Messenger, support and honour him, and glorify Allah morning and evening.

Indeed, We have sent you, [O Prophet], as a witness over your nation, conveying to them what We have sent you with, and as a bringer of good tidings of Paradise for those who obey you, and as a warner of the swift and deferred punishment for those who disobey you. So that you may believe in Allah and His Messenger, and support Allah by supporting His religion, and venerate Him, and glorify Him at the beginning and end of the day.

This verse calls on believers to have faith in Allah and His Messenger, support and honor him, and glorify Allah morning and evening.

Verse 10

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يُبَايِعُونَكَ إِنَّمَا يُبَايِعُونَ اللَّهَ يَدُ اللَّهِ فَوْقَ أَيْدِيهِمْ ۚ فَمَنْ نَكَثَ فَإِنَّمَا يَنْكُثُ عَلَىٰ نَفْسِهِ ۖ وَمَنْ أَوْفَىٰ بِمَا عَاهَدَ عَلَيْهُ اللَّهَ فَسَيُؤْتِيهِ أَجْرًا عَظِيمًا

Surely those who pledge allegiance to you ˹O Prophet˺ are actually pledging allegiance to Allah. Allah’s Hand is over theirs. Whoever breaks their pledge, it will only be to their own loss. And whoever fulfils their pledge to Allah, He will grant them a great reward.

Indeed, those who pledge allegiance to you - O Prophet - at "Al-Hudaybiyah" for fighting are only pledging allegiance to Allah. They are making a covenant with Him seeking His Paradise and His pleasure. The Hand of Allah is over their hands. He is with them, hears their words, sees their place, and knows their hidden and apparent matters. So whoever breaks his pledge, then the harm of that returns to himself. And whoever fulfills what he has covenanted with Allah of patience when meeting the enemy in the path of Allah and in support of His Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, then Allah will give him a great reward which is Paradise. And in the verse is the establishment of the attribute of the Hand of Allah Almighty in a manner befitting Him, glory be to Him, without resemblance or modality.

This verse highlights that the pledge of allegiance to the Prophet is essentially a pledge to Allah. Allah's support is with those who uphold their commitments, while those who break it harm themselves. Those who fulfill their pledge will receive a great reward from Allah.

Verse 11

سَيَقُولُ لَكَ الْمُخَلَّفُونَ مِنَ الْأَعْرَابِ شَغَلَتْنَا أَمْوَالُنَا وَأَهْلُونَا فَاسْتَغْفِرْ لَنَا ۚ يَقُولُونَ بِأَلْسِنَتِهِمْ مَا لَيْسَ فِي قُلُوبِهِمْ ۚ قُلْ فَمَنْ يَمْلِكُ لَكُمْ مِنَ اللَّهِ شَيْئًا إِنْ أَرَادَ بِكُمْ ضَرًّا أَوْ أَرَادَ بِكُمْ نَفْعًا ۚ بَلْ كَانَ اللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرًا

The nomadic Arabs, who stayed behind, will say to you ˹O Prophet˺, “We were preoccupied with our wealth and families, so ask for forgiveness for us.” They say with their tongues what is not in their hearts. Say, “Who then can stand between you and Allah in any way, if He intends harm or benefit for you? In fact, Allah is All-Aware of what you do.

Those who lagged behind from the Bedouins will say to you - O Prophet - when you reproach them for not going out with you to "Mecca": "Our wealth and our families kept us busy, so ask your Lord to forgive us for our lagging behind." They say that with their tongues, while it has no truth in their hearts. Say to them: "Then who can avail you anything against Allah if He intends for you harm or good?" It is not as these hypocrites thought that Allah does not know what is hidden in their inner selves of hypocrisy. Rather, He, glory be to Him, is ever aware of what they do; nothing of the actions of His creation is hidden from Him.

This verse addresses the excuses of those who stayed behind from the Prophet's mission, claiming they were busy. It emphasizes that Allah knows what is in their hearts, and nothing can prevent Allah's will from affecting them. Allah is fully aware of their actions.

Verse 12

بَلْ ظَنَنْتُمْ أَنْ لَنْ يَنْقَلِبَ الرَّسُولُ وَالْمُؤْمِنُونَ إِلَىٰ أَهْلِيهِمْ أَبَدًا وَزُيِّنَ ذَٰلِكَ فِي قُلُوبِكُمْ وَظَنَنْتُمْ ظَنَّ السَّوْءِ وَكُنْتُمْ قَوْمًا بُورًا

The truth is: you thought that the Messenger and the believers would never return to their families again. And that was made appealing in your hearts. You harboured evil thoughts ˹about Allah˺, and ˹so˺ became a doomed people.”

And the matter is not as you claimed, that you were busy with wealth and family. Rather, you thought that the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, and those of his companions with him, would perish and never return to you. Satan made that fair-seeming in your hearts. And you thought an evil thought that Allah would not grant victory to His Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, and his companions over their enemies, and you were a ruined people with no good in you.

This verse reveals the true reason for the actions of those who stayed behind, which is their doubt in the success of the Prophet and his companions. They had evil thoughts about Allah's plans and were destined for failure as a result.

Verse 13

وَمَنْ لَمْ يُؤْمِنْ بِاللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ فَإِنَّا أَعْتَدْنَا لِلْكَافِرِينَ سَعِيرًا

And whoever does not believe in Allah and His Messenger, then We surely have prepared for the disbelievers a blazing Fire.

And whoever does not believe in Allah and what His Messenger, peace and blessings be upon him, brought and does not act according to His law, then he is an infidel who deserves punishment. Indeed, We have prepared for the disbelievers the punishment of the blazing fire.

This verse states that those who disbelieve in Allah and His Messenger will be punished in a blazing fire. This emphasizes the importance of faith and obedience to Allah's commandments.

Verse 14

وَلِلَّهِ مُلْكُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ يَغْفِرُ لِمَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيُعَذِّبُ مَنْ يَشَاءُ ۚ وَكَانَ اللَّهُ غَفُورًا رَحِيمًا

To Allah ˹alone˺ belongs the kingdom of the heavens and the earth. He forgives whoever He wills, and punishes whoever He wills. And Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.

And to Allah belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth and what is within them. He forgives whom He wills by His mercy, covering his sins, and He punishes whom He wills by His justice. And Allah is ever Forgiving to those who repent to Him, Merciful to them.

This verse proclaims Allah's absolute sovereignty over all creation. He forgives whom He wills out of His mercy, and punishes whom He wills out of His justice. Allah is ever-Forgiving and Most Merciful.

Verse 15

سَيَقُولُ الْمُخَلَّفُونَ إِذَا انْطَلَقْتُمْ إِلَىٰ مَغَانِمَ لِتَأْخُذُوهَا ذَرُونَا نَتَّبِعْكُمْ ۖ يُرِيدُونَ أَنْ يُبَدِّلُوا كَلَامَ اللَّهِ ۚ قُلْ لَنْ تَتَّبِعُونَا كَذَٰلِكُمْ قَالَ اللَّهُ مِنْ قَبْلُ ۖ فَسَيَقُولُونَ بَلْ تَحْسُدُونَنَا ۚ بَلْ كَانُوا لَا يَفْقَهُونَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا

Those who stayed behind will say, when you ˹believers˺ set out to take the spoils of war, “Let us accompany you.” They wish to change Allah’s promise. Say, ˹O Prophet,˺ “You will not accompany us. This is what Allah has said before.” They will then say, “In fact, you are driven by jealousy against us!” The truth is: they can hardly comprehend.

The ones who were left behind will say when you - O Prophet - and your companions set out for the spoils of "Khaybar" which Allah has promised you: "Let us go with you to 'Khaybar'." They intend thereby to change the promise of Allah to you. Say to them: "You will never go out with us to 'Khaybar' because Allah Almighty told us before our return to 'Medina' that the spoils of 'Khaybar' are for those who witnessed 'Al-Hudaybiyah' with us." Then they will say: "It is not as you say; Allah did not command you to do this. You are preventing us from going out with you out of envy from you, lest we obtain spoils with you.” And the matter is not as they claimed; rather, they understand little about Allah, what is for them, and what is against them of religious matters.

This verse describes the excuses of those who stayed behind, wanting to join the believers for spoils of war. They are told they cannot join because Allah has already decided who will receive those spoils. Their excuse is driven by jealousy and a lack of understanding of divine matters.

Verse 16

قُلْ لِلْمُخَلَّفِينَ مِنَ الْأَعْرَابِ سَتُدْعَوْنَ إِلَىٰ قَوْمٍ أُولِي بَأْسٍ شَدِيدٍ تُقَاتِلُونَهُمْ أَوْ يُسْلِمُونَ ۖ فَإِنْ تُطِيعُوا يُؤْتِكُمُ اللَّهُ أَجْرًا حَسَنًا ۖ وَإِنْ تَتَوَلَّوْا كَمَا تَوَلَّيْتُمْ مِنْ قَبْلُ يُعَذِّبْكُمْ عَذَابًا أَلِيمًا

Say to nomadic Arabs, who stayed behind, “You will be called ˹to fight˺ against a people of great might, who you will fight unless they submit. If you then obey, Allah will grant you a fine reward. But if you turn away as you did before, He will inflict upon you a painful punishment.”

Say to those of the Bedouins who stayed behind from fighting: You will be called to fight a people who are very strong in fighting. You will fight them or they will surrender without fighting. If you obey God in what He called you to do by fighting these people, He will give you Paradise. But if you disobey Him, as you did when you stayed behind from travelling with the Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, to Mecca, He will punish you with a painful punishment.

The nomadic Arabs who stayed behind are told they will be called to fight a powerful people. If they obey, they will receive a great reward, but if they disobey, they will face a painful punishment, as they had done previously.

Verse 17

لَيْسَ عَلَى الْأَعْمَىٰ حَرَجٌ وَلَا عَلَى الْأَعْرَجِ حَرَجٌ وَلَا عَلَى الْمَرِيضِ حَرَجٌ ۗ وَمَنْ يُطِعِ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ يُدْخِلْهُ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ ۖ وَمَنْ يَتَوَلَّ يُعَذِّبْهُ عَذَابًا أَلِيمًا

There is no blame on the blind, or the disabled, or the sick ˹for staying behind˺. And whoever obeys Allah and His Messenger will be admitted by Him into Gardens under which rivers flow. But whoever turns away will be subjected by Him to a painful punishment.

There is no blame upon the blind among you, O people, nor upon the lame, nor upon the sick for remaining behind from fighting with the believers, due to their inability. And whoever obeys Allah and His Messenger, He will admit him to gardens beneath which rivers flow, and whoever disobeys Allah and His Messenger, and remains behind from fighting with the believers, He will punish him with a painful and grievous punishment.

Those who are blind, disabled, or sick are excused from fighting. Those who obey Allah and His Messenger will be admitted to Paradise. Those who disobey will be punished.

Verse 18

لَقَدْ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ إِذْ يُبَايِعُونَكَ تَحْتَ الشَّجَرَةِ فَعَلِمَ مَا فِي قُلُوبِهِمْ فَأَنْزَلَ السَّكِينَةَ عَلَيْهِمْ وَأَثَابَهُمْ فَتْحًا قَرِيبًا

Indeed, Allah was pleased with the believers when they pledged allegiance to you ˹O Prophet˺ under the tree. He knew what was in their hearts, so He sent down serenity upon them and rewarded them with a victory at hand,

Certainly, Allah was pleased with the believers when they pledged allegiance to you, O Prophet, under the tree (and this is the Pledge of Good Pleasure in "Al-Hudaybiyah"). And Allah knew what was in the hearts of these believers of faith, truthfulness, and loyalty, so Allah sent down tranquility upon them and strengthened their hearts, and compensated them for what they missed with the treaty of "Al-Hudaybiyah" with an imminent victory, which is the conquest of "Khaybar," and many spoils that you will take from the wealth of the Jews of "Khaybar." And Allah is Mighty in His vengeance against His enemies, Wise in managing the affairs of His creation.

Allah was pleased with the believers who pledged allegiance to the Prophet under the tree. He knew the sincerity in their hearts and sent serenity upon them, rewarding them with an imminent victory.

Verse 19

وَمَغَانِمَ كَثِيرَةً يَأْخُذُونَهَا ۗ وَكَانَ اللَّهُ عَزِيزًا حَكِيمًا

and many spoils of war they will gain. For Allah is Almighty, All-Wise.

Certainly, Allah was pleased with the believers when they pledged allegiance to you, O Prophet, under the tree (and this is the Pledge of Good Pleasure in "Al-Hudaybiyah"). And Allah knew what was in the hearts of these believers of faith, truthfulness, and loyalty, so Allah sent down tranquility upon them and strengthened their hearts, and compensated them for what they missed with the treaty of "Al-Hudaybiyah" with an imminent victory, which is the conquest of "Khaybar," and many spoils that you will take from the wealth of the Jews of "Khaybar." And Allah is Mighty in His vengeance against His enemies, Wise in managing the affairs of His creation.

The believers were also granted many spoils of war, and Allah is All-Powerful and Wise in His actions.

Verse 20

وَعَدَكُمُ اللَّهُ مَغَانِمَ كَثِيرَةً تَأْخُذُونَهَا فَعَجَّلَ لَكُمْ هَٰذِهِ وَكَفَّ أَيْدِيَ النَّاسِ عَنْكُمْ وَلِتَكُونَ آيَةً لِلْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَيَهْدِيَكُمْ صِرَاطًا مُسْتَقِيمًا

Allah has promised you ˹believers˺ abundant spoils, which you will gain, so He hastened this ˹truce˺ for you. And He has held people’s hands back from ˹harming˺ you, so it may be a sign for the believers, and so He may guide you along the Straight Path.

Allah has promised you abundant spoils that you will take in their appointed times which Allah has determined for you, and He has hastened for you the spoils of "Khaybar," and He restrained the hands of the people from you, so no harm befell you from what your enemies concealed for you of fighting and warfare, and from harming those you left behind you in "Medina," and so that their defeat, your safety, and your spoils should be a sign by which you take heed, and by which you know that Allah is your Protector and Supporter, and guides you to a straight path in which there is no crookedness. And Allah has promised you another spoil which you could not have conquered. Allah Almighty is Able over it and it is under His control and dominion. And He has promised it to you and what He has promised must come to pass. And Allah is over all things Powerful; nothing can make Him incapable. If the disbelievers of Quraysh fought you in "Mecca," they would have been defeated and turned their backs to you, as the defeated do in battle, and they would not have found for themselves besides Allah a protector to support them in fighting you, nor a helper to assist them in fighting you.

Allah promised the believers many spoils, and He hastened the truce. He also protected them from harm so that they may see a sign and be guided to the Straight Path.

Verse 21
