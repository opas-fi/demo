Sura 047

Verse 1

الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا وَصَدُّوا عَنْ سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ أَضَلَّ أَعْمَالَهُمْ

Those who disbelieve and hinder ˹others˺ from the Way of Allah, He will render their deeds void.

Those who denied that Allah is the only true God, without any partners, and turned people away from His religion, Allah has nullified their deeds, made them void, and made them wretched because of it.

This verse highlights that those who reject faith and obstruct others from following God's path will have their deeds rendered worthless.

Verse 2

وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ وَآمَنُوا بِمَا نُزِّلَ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَهُوَ الْحَقُّ مِنْ رَبِّهِمْ ۙ كَفَّرَ عَنْهُمْ سَيِّئَاتِهِمْ وَأَصْلَحَ بَالَهُمْ

As for those who believe, do good, and have faith in what has been revealed to Muḥammad—which is the truth from their Lord—He will absolve them of their sins and improve their condition.

And those who believed in Allah and followed His law and believed in the Book that was sent down to Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, which is the truth without a doubt from their Lord, He has pardoned them and concealed from them what they did of evil deeds, so He will not punish them for it, and He has set their affairs right in this world and the hereafter.

This verse speaks of the believers who perform good deeds and believe in the divine revelation to Prophet Muhammad. They will have their sins forgiven and their condition improved by God.

Verse 3

ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا اتَّبَعُوا الْبَاطِلَ وَأَنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اتَّبَعُوا الْحَقَّ مِنْ رَبِّهِمْ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ يَضْرِبُ اللَّهُ لِلنَّاسِ أَمْثَالَهُمْ

This is because the disbelievers follow falsehood, while the believers follow the truth from their Lord. This is how Allah shows people their true state ˹of faith˺.

That straying and guidance is because those who disbelieved followed Satan and obeyed him, and those who believed followed the Messenger, peace and blessings be upon him, and what he brought of light and guidance, as Allah has made clear the actions of both groups, the people of disbelief and the people of faith, with what they deserve. He sets forth their examples for people, and attaches to each group of examples and forms what is appropriate.

This verse clarifies that the disbelievers follow falsehood while believers follow the truth from God. This difference reveals their distinct paths and states of faith.

Verse 4

فَإِذَا لَقِيتُمُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا فَضَرْبَ الرِّقَابِ حَتَّىٰ إِذَا أَثْخَنْتُمُوهُمْ فَشُدُّوا الْوَثَاقَ فَإِمَّا مَنًّا بَعْدُ وَإِمَّا فِدَاءً حَتَّىٰ تَضَعَ الْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا ۚ ذَٰلِكَ وَلَوْ يَشَاءُ اللَّهُ لَانْتَصَرَ مِنْهُمْ وَلَٰكِنْ لِيَبْلُوَ بَعْضَكُمْ بِبَعْضٍ ۗ وَالَّذِينَ قُتِلُوا فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ فَلَنْ يُضِلَّ أَعْمَالَهُمْ

So when you meet the disbelievers ˹in battle˺, strike ˹their˺ necks until you have thoroughly subdued them, then bind them firmly. Later ˹free them either as˺ an act of grace or by ransom until the war comes to an end. So will it be. Had Allah willed, He ˹Himself˺ could have inflicted punishment on them. But He does ˹this only to˺ test some of you by means of others. And those who are martyred in the cause of Allah, He will never render their deeds void.

So, when you meet - O believers - those who disbelieve in the battlefields, then strike their necks, until when you have weakened them by much killing and broken their strength, then secure the ties of the captives: either to bestow favor on them by freeing them without compensation, or to ransom themselves with money or otherwise, or to enslave them or kill them, and continue in that until the war ends. That is the ruling mentioned in the trial of the believers by the disbelievers and the alternation of days between them, and if Allah willed, He would have given victory to the believers over the disbelievers without fighting, but He made their punishment by your hands, so He prescribed Jihad to test you by them, and to grant victory to His religion by you. And those who are killed in the path of Allah from the believers, Allah will not invalidate the reward of their deeds. He will guide them during their lives in this world to His obedience and His pleasure, and He will set right their condition, their affairs, and their reward in this world and the hereafter, and He will admit them into Paradise. He has made it known to them and described it to them, and guided them to do what He commanded them - including martyrdom in His cause - then He has made known to them, when they enter Paradise, their dwellings in it.

This verse outlines the conduct during warfare. Believers are instructed to strike the necks of disbelievers until subdued, then to take captives, who can later be freed by grace or ransom. It also mentions the concept of testing through conflicts and that the deeds of martyrs will not be in vain.

Verse 7

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِنْ تَنْصُرُوا اللَّهَ يَنْصُرْكُمْ وَيُثَبِّتْ أَقْدَامَكُمْ

O believers! If you stand up for Allah, He will help you and make your steps firm.

O you who have believed in Allah and His Messenger and acted according to His law, if you support the religion of Allah by striving in His cause, and judging by His Book, and following His commands, and avoiding His prohibitions, Allah will grant you victory over your enemies and make your feet firm during fighting.

This verse assures believers that if they support God's cause, He will help them and make them steadfast.

Verse 8

وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا فَتَعْسًا لَهُمْ وَأَضَلَّ أَعْمَالَهُمْ

As for the disbelievers, may they be doomed and may He render their deeds void.

And those who disbelieve, then destruction is for them, and Allah has nullified the reward of their deeds; that is because they disliked the Book of Allah revealed to His Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, so they denied it, so He nullified their deeds, because they were in obedience to Satan.

This verse states that the disbelievers are doomed and their deeds are void because they rejected God's guidance.

Verse 9

ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّهُمْ كَرِهُوا مَا أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ فَأَحْبَطَ أَعْمَالَهُمْ

That is because they detest what Allah has revealed, so He has rendered their deeds void.

And those who disbelieve, then destruction is for them, and Allah has nullified the reward of their deeds; that is because they disliked the Book of Allah revealed to His Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, so they denied it, so He nullified their deeds, because they were in obedience to Satan.

This verse emphasizes that the reason for disbelievers' doom is their hatred for God's revelations, which nullifies their deeds.

Verse 10

أَفَلَمْ يَسِيرُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ فَيَنْظُرُوا كَيْفَ كَانَ عَاقِبَةُ الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ ۚ دَمَّرَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِمْ ۖ وَلِلْكَافِرِينَ أَمْثَالُهَا

Have they not travelled throughout the land to see what was the end of those before them? Allah annihilated them, and a similar fate awaits the disbelievers.

Have these disbelievers not traveled throughout the land, considering what befell the denying nations before them of punishment? Allah destroyed their homes, and for the disbelievers is the like of that consequence which befell those nations.

This verse prompts reflection on the fate of past nations who rejected God, warning disbelievers of a similar consequence.

Verse 11

ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّ اللَّهَ مَوْلَى الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَأَنَّ الْكَافِرِينَ لَا مَوْلَىٰ لَهُمْ

This is because Allah is the Patron of the believers while the disbelievers have no patron.

That is what We did with the two groups, the group of faith and the group of disbelief; because Allah is the protector and helper of the believers, and the disbelievers have no protector nor helper.

This verse contrasts the believers, who are supported by God, with the disbelievers, who have no protector.

Verse 12

إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُدْخِلُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ ۖ وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا يَتَمَتَّعُونَ وَيَأْكُلُونَ كَمَا تَأْكُلُ الْأَنْعَامُ وَالنَّارُ مَثْوًى لَهُمْ

Surely Allah will admit those who believe and do good into Gardens under which rivers flow. As for the disbelievers, they enjoy themselves and feed like cattle. But the Fire will be their home.

Indeed, Allah will admit those who believe in Allah and His Messenger and do righteous deeds to gardens beneath which rivers flow, as an honor for them. And the example of those who disbelieve is that they eat and enjoy themselves in this world like grazing livestock that have no concern except for grazing, and Hellfire is their abode and resting place.

This verse contrasts the destinies of believers and disbelievers. Believers, who do good, will be rewarded with Paradise, while disbelievers indulge in worldly pleasures like animals, with Hell as their final abode.

Verse 13

وَكَأَيِّنْ مِنْ قَرْيَةٍ هِيَ أَشَدُّ قُوَّةً مِنْ قَرْيَتِكَ الَّتِي أَخْرَجَتْكَ أَهْلَكْنَاهُمْ فَلَا نَاصِرَ لَهُمْ

˹Imagine, O Prophet,˺ how many societies We destroyed that were far superior in might than your society—which drove you out—and there was none to help them!

And how many a city was more powerful in might than your city - O Messenger, which is "Mecca" - that expelled you; We destroyed them with various kinds of torment, and they had no helper to help them against the punishment of Allah.

This verse highlights that many powerful societies were destroyed for their transgressions, despite their might. It serves as a warning that worldly power is no protection against Allah's decree.

Verse 14

أَفَمَنْ كَانَ عَلَىٰ بَيِّنَةٍ مِنْ رَبِّهِ كَمَنْ زُيِّنَ لَهُ سُوءُ عَمَلِهِ وَاتَّبَعُوا أَهْوَاءَهُمْ

Can those ˹believers˺ who stand on clear proof from their Lord be like those whose evil deeds are made appealing to them and ˹only˺ follow their desires?

Then is one who is upon clear evidence from his Lord and knowledge of His Oneness like one to whom Satan has made pleasing his evil deed and they have followed what their souls have called them to of disobedience to Allah and worshipping others than Him without proof or evidence? They are not equal.

This verse asks whether those who follow clear guidance are the same as those who are led astray by their desires and evil deeds. It emphasizes that true guidance comes from Allah.

Verse 15

The description of the Paradise promised to the righteous is that in it are rivers of fresh water, rivers of milk that never changes in taste, rivers of wine delicious to drink, and rivers of pure honey. There they will ˹also˺ have all kinds of fruit, and forgiveness from their Lord. ˹Can they be˺ like those who will stay in the Fire forever, left to drink boiling water that will tear apart their insides?

The description of Paradise which Allah has promised the righteous: In it are rivers of water that never changes, rivers of milk the taste of which never changes, rivers of wine delicious to those who drink, and rivers of purified honey. And for these righteous ones in this Paradise are all kinds of fruits and other things. And greater than that is the covering up and overlooking of their sins. Is one who is in this Paradise like one who is staying in the Fire and will never come out of it, and were given to drink water that was extremely hot, which then tore apart their intestines?

This verse describes the delights of Paradise, with rivers of pure water, milk, wine, and honey, as well as fruits and forgiveness. It contrasts this with Hell, where inhabitants suffer intensely, drinking boiling water that tears their insides.

This verse speaks of those who listen to the Prophet without understanding, later mocking his teachings. Their hearts are sealed, preventing them from accepting the truth, and they follow their own desires.
