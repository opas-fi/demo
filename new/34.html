Sura 34

Verse 1:
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ فِي الْآخِرَةِ ۚ وَهُوَ الْحَكِيمُ الْخَبِيرُ
Translation: Praise be to Allah, to Whom belong all things in the heavens and on earth: to Him be Praise in the Hereafter: and He is Full of Wisdom, acquainted with all things.
Commentary: This verse begins by stating that all praise belongs to Allah. He is the owner of everything that exists in the heavens and on the earth. Even if some people do not acknowledge Him, the universe and all creation are understood to praise Him. The verse also affirms that all praise will ultimately be directed to Him in the life to come. It emphasizes that Allah is full of wisdom in all His actions, managing the affairs of this life, the hereafter, and the entire universe with perfect wisdom. Furthermore, He is fully aware of everything that happens, with complete and accurate knowledge.
Finnish: Tämä jae alkaa toteamalla, että kaikki ylistys kuuluu Allahille. Hän on kaiken omistaja, mitä taivaissa ja maan päällä on. Vaikka jotkut ihmiset eivät tunnustaisi Häntä, universumin ja koko luomakunnan ymmärretään ylistävän Häntä. Jae vahvistaa myös, että kaikki ylistys suunnataan lopulta Hänelle tulevassa elämässä. Se korostaa, että Allah on täynnä viisautta kaikissa teoissaan, hoitaen tämän elämän, tulevan elämän ja koko universumin asioita täydellisellä viisaudella. Lisäksi Hän on täysin tietoinen kaikesta tapahtuvasta, täydellisellä ja tarkalla tiedolla.
تبدأ هذه الآية بذكر أن كل الحمد والثناء لله. هو مالك كل ما في السماوات والأرض. حتى لو لم يعترف به بعض الناس، فإن الكون وكل المخلوقات يُفهم أنها تحمده وتسبحه. وتؤكد الآية أيضًا أن كل الحمد سيكون في النهاية موجهًا إليه في الحياة الآخرة. وتشدد على أن الله كامل الحكمة في جميع أفعاله، يدبر شؤون هذه الحياة والآخرة والكون بأسره بحكمة بالغة. علاوة على ذلك، هو مطلع تمام الاطلاع على كل ما يحدث، بعلم كامل ودقيق.
Verse 2:
يَعْلَمُ مَا يَلِجُ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَمَا يَخْرُجُ مِنْهَا وَمَا يَنْزِلُ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ وَمَا يَعْرُجُ فِيهَا ۚ وَهُوَ الرَّحِيمُ الْغَفُورُ
Translation: He knows all that goes into the earth, and all that comes out thereof; all that comes down from the sky and all that ascends thereto and He is the Most Merciful, the Oft-Forgiving.
Commentary: Building upon the idea of Allah's knowledge from the previous verse, this verse provides a vivid illustration of its vastness. It describes Allah's awareness of everything that enters the earth (like seeds, rain, creatures), everything that emerges from it (plants, springs, gases), everything that descends from the sky (rain, light, divine commands), and everything that ascends to it (vapor, prayers, souls). This imagery conveys a sense of comprehensive knowledge beyond human capacity. Despite knowing everything about His creation, including their shortcomings, Allah is described as the Most Merciful and Oft-Forgiving.
Finnish: Edellisen jakeen ajatukseen Allahin tiedosta perustuen, tämä jae antaa elävän kuvauksen sen laajuudesta. Se kuvaa Allahin tietoisuutta kaikesta, mikä menee maan sisään (kuten siemenet, sade, olennot), kaikesta, mikä nousee sieltä (kasvit, lähteet, kaasut), kaikesta, mikä laskeutuu taivaasta (sade, valo, jumalalliset käskyt), ja kaikesta, mikä nousee sinne (höyry, rukoukset, sielut). Tämä kuvasto välittää tunteen kattavasta tiedosta, joka ylittää inhimillisen kyvyn. Huolimatta kaiken tietämisestä luomakunnastaan, mukaan lukien heidän puutteensa, Allahia kuvataan Armollisimpana ja Paljon Anteeksiantavana.
تكملة لفكرة علم الله من الآية السابقة، تقدم هذه الآية تصويرًا حيويًا لاتساع علمه. تصف الآية إحاطة علم الله بكل ما يدخل في الأرض (مثل البذور، المطر، الكائنات)، وكل ما يخرج منها (النباتات، الينابيع، الغازات)، وكل ما ينزل من السماء (المطر، الضوء، الأوامر الإلهية)، وكل ما يعرج إليها (البخار، الدعوات، الأرواح). هذا التصوير ينقل إحساسًا بعلم شامل يتجاوز القدرة البشرية. على الرغم من معرفته بكل شيء عن خلقه، بما في ذلك تقصيراتهم، يوصف الله بأنه الرحمن الرحيم والغفور الودود.
Verse 3:
وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَا تَأْتِينَا السَّاعَةُ ۖ قُلْ بَلَىٰ وَرَبِّي لَتَأْتِيَنَّكُمْ عَالِمِ الْغَيْبِ ۖ لَا يَعْزُبُ عَنْهُ مِثْقَالُ ذَرَّةٍ فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَلَا فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا أَصْغَرُ مِنْ ذَٰلِكَ وَلَا أَكْبَرُ إِلَّا فِي كِتَابٍ مُبِينٍ
Translation: The Unbelievers say, "Never to us will come the Hour": Say, "Nay! but most surely, by my Lord, it will come upon you;- by Him Who knows the unseen,- from Whom is not hidden the least little atom in the heavens or on earth: Nor is there anything less than that, or greater, but is in the Record Perspicuous:
Commentary: This verse contrasts the comprehensive knowledge of Allah described in the previous verses with the disbelief of those who deny the arrival of the Final Hour (Day of Judgment). They assert it will never come, but the Prophet is instructed to respond emphatically, confirming its certain arrival by Allah, who knows all that is unseen. Reaffirming Allah's perfect knowledge, the verse states that not even the weight of an atom in the heavens or on earth is hidden from Him, nor anything smaller or larger. Everything is recorded in a clear register. This serves to underscore the certainty of the Hour and the accountability that will follow, because Allah is aware of every detail.
Finnish: Tämä jae asettaa vastakkain edellisissä jakeissa kuvatun Allahin kattavan tiedon ja niiden epäuskovaisten epäuskouksen, jotka kieltävät Viimeisen hetken (Tuomionpäivän) saapumisen. He väittävät, ettei se koskaan tule, mutta Profeettaa kehotetaan vastaamaan painokkaasti vahvistaen sen varman saapumisen Allahin tahdosta, joka tietää kaiken näkymättömän. Vahvistaen Allahin täydellisen tiedon, jae toteaa, ettei edes atomin paino taivaissa tai maan päällä ole Häneltä piilossa, eikä mikään sitä pienempi tai suurempi. Kaikki on kirjattu selkeään rekisteriin. Tämä alleviivaa Hetken varmuutta ja seuraavaa vastuullisuutta, koska Allah on tietoinen jokaisesta yksityiskohdasta.
هذه الآية تعارض العلم الشامل لله الموصوف في الآيات السابقة بإنكار أولئك الذين لا يؤمنون بقدوم الساعة النهائية (يوم القيامة). يؤكدون أنها لن تأتي أبدًا، ولكن يوجه النبي بالرد القاطع، مؤكدًا وصولها المؤكد بإذن الله، الذي يعلم كل الغيب. وتأكيدًا لعلم الله الكامل، تذكر الآية أنه لا تخفى عليه مثقال ذرة في السماوات ولا في الأرض، ولا أصغر من ذلك ولا أكبر، إلا في كتاب مبين. هذا يؤكد يقين الساعة والمسؤولية التي ستتبعها، لأن الله مطلع على كل التفاصيل.
Verse 4:
لِيَجْزِيَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ لَهُمْ مَغْفِرَةٌ وَرِزْقٌ كَرِيمٌ
Translation: That He may reward those who believe and work deeds of righteousness: for such is Forgiveness and a Sustenance Most Generous."
Commentary: This verse explains one of the primary purposes for the inevitable arrival of the Final Hour, as stated in the previous verse: so that Allah may reward those who believe and live righteously. Such individuals are promised forgiveness for their mistakes and sins, and they will receive abundant and generous provisions in the hereafter. This highlights the justice and mercy that will be manifested on that Day.
Finnish: Tämä jae selittää yhden ensisijaisista tarkoituksista Viimeisen hetken väistämättömälle saapumiselle, kuten edellisessä jakeessa todettiin: jotta Allah voisi palkita ne, jotka uskovat ja tekevät vanhurskaita tekoja. Tällaisille yksilöille luvataan anteeksianto heidän virheistään ja synneistään, ja he saavat yltäkylläisiä ja anteliaita varoja tulevassa elämässä. Tämä korostaa oikeudenmukaisuutta ja armoa, jotka ilmenevät sinä päivänä.
هذه الآية توضح أحد الأغراض الرئيسية لقدوم الساعة الحتمية، كما ذكر في الآية السابقة: لكي يجزي الله الذين آمنوا وعملوا الصالحات. يوعد هؤلاء الأفراد بالمغفرة لزلاتهم وذنوبهم، وسيحصلون على رزق وفير وكريم في الآخرة. هذا يسلط الضوء على العدل والرحمة اللذين سيتجليان في ذلك اليوم.
Verse 5:
وَالَّذِينَ سَعَوْا فِي آيَاتِنَا مُعَاجِزِينَ أُولَٰئِكَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ مِنْ رِجْزٍ أَلِيمٌ
Translation: But those who strive against Our Signs, to frustrate them,- for such will be a Penalty,- a Punishment most humiliating.
Commentary: Following the mention of reward for the believers in the previous verse, this verse addresses the consequence for those who actively oppose Allah's messages and signs, trying to undermine or invalidate them. For such individuals, the inevitable Final Hour will bring a painful and humiliating punishment. This completes the explanation of why the Hour is necessary: to justly recompense both those who do good and those who strive against the truth.
Finnish: Edellisessä jakeessa mainitun uskovien palkinnon jälkeen tämä jae käsittelee seurausta niille, jotka aktiivisesti vastustavat Allahin sanomia ja merkkejä, yrittäen heikentää tai mitätöidä niitä. Tällaisille yksilöille väistämätön Viimeinen hetki tuo tuskallisen ja nöyryyttävän rangaistuksen. Tämä täydentää selityksen siitä, miksi Hetki on tarpeellinen: oikeudenmukaisesti palkitsemaan sekä ne, jotka tekevät hyvää, että ne, jotka taistelevat totuutta vastaan.
بعد ذكر جزاء المؤمنين في الآية السابقة، تتناول هذه الآية عاقبة الذين يسعون ضد آيات الله معاجزين (محاولين إبطالها أو إضعافها). لهؤلاء الأفراد، ستجلب الساعة النهائية الحتمية عذابًا أليمًا ومهينًا. هذا يكمل شرح سبب ضرورة الساعة: لتجزية عادلة لكل من أحسن، ولكل من عاند الحق.
Verse 6:
وَيَرَى الَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْعِلْمَ الَّذِي أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْكَ مِنْ رَبِّكَ هُوَ الْحَقَّ وَيَهْدِي إِلَىٰ صِرَاطِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَمِيدِ
Translation: And those to whom knowledge has come see that the (Revelation) sent down to thee from thy Lord - that is the Truth, and that it guides to the Path of the Exalted (in might), Worthy of all praise.
Commentary: Within the context of Allah's perfect knowledge and the coming judgment, this verse points out that people who possess true knowledge recognize the divine revelation given to the Prophet as the truth. This revelation, the Qur'an, guides to the path that leads to Allah, who is described as Exalted in power and worthy of all praise. This suggests that genuine understanding and wisdom naturally lead to the recognition of the truth and guidance provided by the Qur'an, aligning with the universal reality established by the Almighty.
Finnish: Allahin täydellisen tiedon ja tulevan tuomion yhteydessä tämä jae osoittaa, että ne ihmiset, joilla on todellista tietoa, tunnistavat Profeetalle lähetetyn jumalallisen ilmoituksen totuudeksi. Tämä ilmoitus, Koraani, ohjaa polulle, joka johtaa Allahiin, jota kuvataan Voimallisena ja Kaiken Ylistyksen Arvoisena. Tämä viittaa siihen, että aito ymmärrys ja viisaus johtavat luonnollisesti totuuden ja Koraanin antaman ohjauksen tunnistamiseen, sovitettuna Kaikkivaltiaan vahvistamaan universaaliin todellisuuteen.
في سياق علم الله الكامل والجزاء القادم، تشير هذه الآية إلى أن الذين أوتوا العلم الحق يرون أن الوحي الإلهي الذي أنزل على النبي هو الحق. هذا الوحي، وهو القرآن، يهدي إلى الطريق الذي يؤدي إلى الله، الذي يوصف بأنه العزيز (ذي القوة والعزة) والحميد (المستحق لكل الحمد). هذا يشير إلى أن الفهم الحقيقي والحكمة يؤديان بشكل طبيعي إلى إدراك الحقيقة والهدى الذي يقدمه القرآن، متوافقًا مع الحقيقة الكونية التي أرساها الخالق العظيم.
Verse 7:
وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا هَلْ نَدُلُّكُمْ عَلَىٰ رَجُلٍ يُنَبِّئُكُمْ إِذَا مُزِّقْتُمْ كُلَّ مُمَزَّقٍ إِنَّكُمْ لَفِي خَلْقٍ جَدِيدٍ
Translation: The Unbelievers say (in ridicule): "Shall we point out to you a man that will tell you, when ye are all scattered to pieces in disintegration, that ye shall (then be raised) in a New Creation?
Commentary: Returning to the theme of the disbelievers' denial of the hereafter, this verse describes their mocking reaction to the idea of resurrection. They speak derisively, inviting others to observe the Prophet, whom they portray as strange for claiming that people will be brought back to life in a new creation after their bodies have completely decomposed.
Finnish: Palaten takaisin epäuskovaisten kieltoon tuonpuoleisesta, tämä jae kuvaa heidän pilkkaavaa reaktiotaan ylösnousemuksen ajatukseen. He puhuvat ivallisesti, kutsuen muita tarkkailemaan Profeettaa, jota he pitävät outona väittäessään, että ihmiset herätetään takaisin eloon uuteen luomukseen sen jälkeen, kun heidän ruumiinsa ovat täysin hajonneet.
بالعودة إلى موضوع إنكار الكفار للحياة الآخرة، تصف هذه الآية رد فعلهم الساخر تجاه فكرة البعث. يتحدثون بازدراء، يدعون الآخرين ليروا النبي، الذي يصورونه على أنه غريب لمزاعمه بأن الناس سيعادون إلى الحياة في خلق جديد بعد أن تتفرق أجسادهم وتتحلل تمامًا.
Verse 8:
أَفْتَرَىٰ عَلَى اللَّهِ كَذِبًا أَمْ بِهِ جِنَّةٌ ۗ بَلِ الَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِالْآخِرَةِ فِي الْعَذَابِ وَالضَّلَالِ الْبَعِيدِ
Translation: "Has he invented a falsehood against Allah, or has a spirit (seized) him?"- Nay, it is those who believe not in the Hereafter, that are in (real) Penalty, and in farthest error.
Commentary: Continuing from the previous verse's portrayal of the disbelievers' reaction, they escalate their ridicule by questioning the Prophet's integrity or sanity. They suggest he has either fabricated a lie against Allah or is affected by madness. The verse refutes their claims, asserting that it is the disbelievers themselves, due to their refusal to believe in the hereafter, who are experiencing a real penalty and are deeply astray. This "penalty" could refer to their future punishment or perhaps the mental and spiritual distress resulting from living without the hope and accountability that belief in the hereafter provides.
Finnish: Jatkuen edellisen jakeen kuvauksesta epäuskovaisten reaktiosta, he lisäävät pilkkaansa kyseenalaistamalla Profeetan rehellisyyden tai mielenterveyden. He ehdottavat, että hän on joko keksinyt valheen Allahiin liittyen tai että jokin henki on ottanut hänet valtaansa. Jae kumoaa heidän väitteensä ja vahvistaa, että juuri epäuskovaiset itse, kieltäytymisensä vuoksi uskoa tuonpuoleiseen, kokevat todellisen rangaistuksen ja ovat syvästi eksyksissä. Tämä "rangaistus" voi viitata heidän tulevaan rangaistukseensa tai ehkä henkiseen ja hengelliseen ahdistukseen, joka johtuu elämisestä ilman toivoa ja vastuullisuutta, jonka usko tuonpuoleiseen tuo.
استكمالاً لتصوير رد فعل الكافرين في الآية السابقة، يصعدون من سخريتهم بالتشكيك في صدق النبي أو سلامة عقله. يقترحون أنه إما افترى كذبًا على الله أو أن به جنة (أُصيب بالجنون). ترد الآية على ادعاءاتهم، مؤكدة أن الكافرين أنفسهم، بسبب رفضهم الإيمان بالآخرة، هم الذين يعانون من عذاب حقيقي وضلال بعيد. قد يشير هذا "العذاب" إلى عقابهم المستقبلي أو ربما الضيق النفسي والروحي الناتج عن العيش بدون الأمل والمسؤولية اللذين يوفرهما الإيمان بالآخرة.
Verse 9:
أَفَلَمْ يَرَوْا إِلَىٰ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ إِنْ نَشَأْ نَخْسِفْ بِهِمُ الْأَرْضَ أَوْ نُسْقِطْ عَلَيْهِمْ كِسَفًا مِنَ السَّمَاءِ ۚ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَةً لِكُلِّ عَبْدٍ مُنِيبٍ
Translation: See they not what is before them and behind them, of the sky and the earth? If We wished, We could cause the earth to swallow them up, or cause a piece of the sky to fall upon them. Verily in this is a Sign for every devotee that turns to Allah (in repentance).
Commentary: Addressing the disbelievers' dismissal of future accountability, this verse shifts focus to Allah's immediate power evident in the physical world around them. It asks if they consider the sky and earth surrounding them. It warns that if Allah willed, He could easily cause the earth to swallow them or cause a piece of the sky to fall upon them. This serves as a potent reminder that Allah's power is not limited to a future Day of Judgment; He has the ability to bring about consequences in this life as well. These potential events and the wonders of the sky and earth serve as clear signs for anyone who reflects deeply and turns to Allah with sincerity and a desire for guidance.
Finnish: Käsitellen epäuskovaisten vähättelyä tulevasta vastuullisuudesta, tämä jae siirtää painopisteen Allahin välittömään voimaan, joka ilmenee heitä ympäröivässä fyysisessä maailmassa. Se kysyy, miettivätkö he taivasta ja maata ympärillään. Se varoittaa, että jos Allah niin tahtoisi, Hän voisi helposti saada maan nielemään heidät tai saada palan taivasta putoamaan heidän päälleen. Tämä toimii voimakkaana muistutuksena siitä, että Allahin voima ei rajoitu tulevaan Tuomionpäivään; Hänellä on kyky aiheuttaa seurauksia myös tässä elämässä. Nämä mahdolliset tapahtumat ja taivaan ja maan ihmeet toimivat selvinä merkkeinä kaikille, jotka pohtivat syvällisesti ja kääntyvät Allahin puoleen vilpittömästi ja ohjausta haluten.
مخاطبةً استخفاف الكافرين بالمسؤولية المستقبلية، تحول هذه الآية التركيز إلى قدرة الله الفورية الظاهرة في العالم المادي من حولهم. تسألهم عما إذا كانوا لا ينظرون إلى السماء والأرض المحيطة بهم. تحذر الآية من أنه لو شاء الله، يمكنه بسهولة أن يخسف بهم الأرض أو يسقط عليهم قطعًا من السماء. هذا بمثابة تذكير قوي بأن قدرة الله لا تقتصر على يوم القيامة المستقبلي؛ فلديه القدرة على جلب العواقب في هذه الحياة أيضًا. هذه الأحداث المحتملة وعجائب السماء والأرض بمثابة آيات واضحة لكل من يتفكر بعمق ويتوب إلى الله بصدق ورغبة في الهدى.
Verse 10:
وَلَقَدْ آتَيْنَا دَاوُودَ مِنَّا فَضْلًا ۖ يَا جِبَالُ أَوِّبِي مَعَهُ وَالطَّيْرَ ۖ وَأَلَنَّا لَهُ الْحَدِيدَ
Translation: We bestowed Grace aforetime on David from ourselves: "O ye Mountains! Sing ye back the Praises of Allah with him! and ye birds (also)! And We made the iron soft for him;-
Commentary: This verse introduces Prophet David, highlighting some special favors Allah granted him. It mentions that Allah bestowed grace upon him. One of these favors was that the mountains and birds were commanded to join him in praising Allah. This is seen as a sign of the unique connection Prophet David had with Allah and the response of creation to his devotion. Another favor mentioned is that Allah made iron soft for him, which was a special ability or miracle given to him. This verse sets the stage for describing the blessings given to prophets as examples of Allah's grace and power.
Finnish: Tämä jae esittelee profeetta Daavidin, korostaen erityisiä suosionosoituksia, jotka Allah hänelle myönsi. Mainitsee, että Allah antoi hänelle armonsa. Yksi näistä suosionosoituksista oli, että vuorille ja linnuille käskettiin yhtyä hänen kanssaan Allahin ylistämiseen. Tämä nähdään merkkinä profeetta Daavidin ainutlaatuisesta yhteydestä Allahiin ja luomakunnan vastauksesta hänen omistautumiseensa. Toinen mainittu suosionosoitus on, että Allah teki raudan pehmeäksi hänelle, mikä oli erityinen kyky tai ihme, joka hänelle annettiin. Tämä jae pohjustaa profeetoille annettujen siunausten kuvausta esimerkkeinä Allahin armosta ja voimasta.

Translation: Between them and the Cities on which We had poured our blessings, We had placed Cities in prominent positions, and between them We had appointed stages of journey in due proportion: "Travel therein, secure, by night and by day."
Commentary: Despite the initial loss of their gardens, the people of Saba still had other blessings. This verse highlights that Allah had made travel easy and safe for them, connecting their land to other prosperous regions (referred to as "Cities on which We had poured our blessings," often understood to be places like Makkah and Jerusalem). Allah had established well-defined, secure routes between these areas, with towns conveniently located at measured distances. This allowed travelers to journey safely and comfortably, day and night. This detail underscores the continued ease and convenience Allah had provided them, offering another opportunity for them to be grateful and perhaps reflect on His favors.

Translation: But they said: "Our Lord! Place longer distances between our journey-stages": but they wronged themselves (therein). Commentary: This verse describes the people of Saba's reaction to the ease of travel mentioned in the previous verse. This attitude is seen as arrogance and a form of "wronging themselves," as they were wishing for the removal of a favor. As a consequence of their ingratitude and this specific foolish request, Allah's judgment came upon them. They were utterly destroyed and scattered across the land, their story becoming merely a cautionary tale remembered by others. The verse concludes by pointing out that in the fate of the people of Saba are clear signs for anyone who is patiently enduring in times of hardship and deeply grateful in times of ease. This connects their story to the broader theme of patience and gratitude as essential qualities for a believer.

Verse 20:
وَلَقَدْ صَدَّقَ عَلَيْهِمْ إِبْلِيسُ ظَنَّهُ فَاتَّبَعُوهُ إِلَّا فَرِيقًا مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ
Translation: And on them did Satan prove true his idea, and they followed him, all but a party that believed.
Commentary: Following the account of past communities, this verse highlights how Satan (referred to here as Iblis) saw his assumption about people proven true: most of them followed his temptations, with the exception of a small group of believers. This illustrates a general pattern seen throughout communities – while many may stray, there is always a group that maintains faith and resists negative influences, showing that truth remains accessible to those who seek it, even in challenging times.

Verse 21:
وَمَا كَانَ لَهُ عَلَيْهِمْ مِنْ سُلْطَانٍ إِلَّا لِنَعْلَمَ مَنْ يُؤْمِنُ بِالْآخِرَةِ مِمَّنْ هُوَ مِنْهَا فِي شَكٍّ ۗ وَرَبُّكَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ حَفِيظٌ
Translation: But he had no authority over them,- except that We might test the man who believes in the Hereafter from him who is in doubt concerning it: and thy Lord doth watch over all things.

Nothing is hidden from His knowledge and He keeps watch over all things. This verse, like the end of the previous section, concludes by mentioning the afterlife and Allah's boundless knowledge and awareness, themes emphasized throughout this part of the Sura.

Verse 22:

Translation: Say: "Call upon other (gods) whom ye fancy, besides Allah: They have no power,- not the weight of an atom,- in the heavens or on earth: No (sort of) share have they therein, nor is any of them a helper to Allah.
Commentary: This verse begins with a challenge to those who associate partners with Allah. It asks them to call upon those they imagine to be partners with Allah and consider their actual power and status in the universe. The verse explains that these alleged partners possess absolutely no authority, not even equal to the weight of an atom, anywhere in the heavens or on the earth. They hold no share in any ownership or control of creation. Furthermore, Allah has no need for any helper from among them; He is self-sufficient and supreme. This verse sets the stage by establishing Allah's absolute and sole authority, contrasting it with the powerlessness of anything else that might be worshipped alongside Him.

Verse 23:
وَلَا تَنْفَعُ الشَّفَاعَةُ عِنْدَهُ إِلَّا لِمَنْ أَذِنَ لَهُ ۚ حَتَّىٰ إِذَا فُزِّعَ عَنْ قُلُوبِهِمْ قَالُوا مَاذَا قَالَ رَبُّكُمْ ۖ قَالُوا الْحَقَّ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْكَبِيرُ
Translation: "No intercession can avail in His Presence, except for those for whom He has granted permission. So far (is this the case) that, when terror is removed from their hearts (at the Day of Judgment, then) will they say, 'what is it that your Lord commanded?' they will say, 'That which is true and just; and He is the Most High Most Great'."
Commentary: Following the challenge about the lack of power of imagined partners, this verse addresses the concept of intercession, particularly as some might believe certain beings (like angels, as mentioned in the broader context) could intercede with Allah on their behalf. The verse clarifies that no intercession is effective with Allah unless He specifically grants permission for it. It describes a scene on the Day of Judgment where beings who are permitted to intercede, along with those hoping for intercession, wait in awe for Allah's command. When permission is finally given, the sheer majesty of the moment causes profound awe. After this awe subsides, those present ask what Allah has commanded, and the answer is simply, "The truth." This underscores that Allah's command is always just and true, reflecting His nature. The verse concludes by affirming that Allah is the Most High, the Supreme, attributes that highlight His absolute transcendence and authority, making it clear that intercession is entirely dependent on His will and permission. In Islamic belief, such attributes as 'Most High' and 'Supreme' are affirmed as they appear in the Qur’an, without likening Allah to His creation or explaining how they occur. Rather, they reflect His majesty and uniqueness in a way beyond human comprehension. Muslims believe Allah’s attributes are accepted as revealed, without interpreting them metaphorically or anthropomorphically.

Translation: Say: "Who gives you sustenance, from the heavens and the earth?" Say: "It is Allah; and certain it is that either we or ye are on right guidance or in manifest error!"
Commentary: Continuing the argument for Allah's oneness, this verse shifts focus from cosmic authority and future judgment to the tangible reality of daily life: sustenance. It prompts a reflection on the source of the provisions received from the heavens (like rain, sunlight) and the earth (plants, minerals, etc.). The expected and truthful answer is provided: it is Allah who provides sustenance. This undeniable fact serves as another proof of His sole Lordship. The verse then presents a clear and courteous distinction between those who recognize this truth and those who do not, stating that one group must be rightly guided while the other is clearly mistaken. This is presented not as an accusation, but as a simple, undeniable reality, encouraging reflection on which path one is on.

The commentary notes that most people do not understand this principle and mistakenly equate worldly prosperity with divine approval.

These varied accusations were attempts to distract people from the powerful and clear message of the Qur'an.

Verse 53:
وَقَدْ كَفَرُوا بِهِ مِنْ قَبْلُ ۖ وَيَقْذِفُونَ بِالْغَيْبِ مِنْ مَكَانٍ بَعِيدٍ
Translation: Seeing that they did reject Faith (entirely) before, and that they (continually) cast (slanders) on the unseen from a position far off?
Commentary: This verse reflects on the situation of those who are now experiencing the consequences of their actions, as mentioned in the preceding verses. I explain that their attempt to believe now is ineffective because they had completely rejected faith earlier, when they had the chance in their worldly life. While they were in a position of safety and perceived distance from the consequences, they used to mock and deny the unseen realities, such as the Day of Judgment, without any basis or evidence. Now that these unseen things are clear before them, they wish to believe, but it is too late.

Verse 54:
وَحِيلَ بَيْنَهُمْ وَبَيْنَ مَا يَشْتَهُونَ كَمَا فُعِلَ بِأَشْيَاعِهِمْ مِنْ قَبْلُ ۚ إِنَّهُمْ كَانُوا فِي شَكٍّ مُرِيبٍ
Translation: And between them and their desires, is placed a barrier, as was done in the past with their partisans: for they were indeed in suspicious (disquieting) doubt.
Commentary: This verse concludes the passage and the Sura. It explains that a barrier is placed between these individuals and what they now desperately desire – which is faith, hoping it might save them from the severe situation they face. I understand this is similar to what happened to earlier communities who also denied the truth and tried to escape Allah's punishment when it was inevitable. It highlights that during their lives, they were trapped in deep doubt about the truth and the Day of Judgment. Now, that doubt is replaced by certainty as they witness the reality. This verse emphasizes the finality of their situation and the justice of their consequence, reflecting their prior state of denial and doubt.
