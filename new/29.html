Sura 29

Verse 1:
الم
Translation: A. L. M.
Commentary: This verse consists of separate Arabic letters: Alif, Lam, and Mim. We understand that these letters appear at the beginning of certain chapters in the Qur'an to draw attention to the fact that this divine book is composed of the same familiar letters used by people every day. However, despite being made of these known components, humans cannot produce anything like the Qur'an, indicating its unique divine origin. Chapters that open with these letters typically speak about the Qur'an itself, either immediately after or later, highlighting its inimitable nature.
Finnish: Tämä jae koostuu erillisistä arabialaisista kirjaimista: Alif, Lam ja Mim. Ymmärrämme, että nämä kirjaimet esiintyvät tiettyjen Koraanin lukujen alussa kiinnittääkseen huomion siihen, että tämä jumalallinen kirja koostuu samoista tutuista kirjaimista, joita ihmiset käyttävät joka päivä. Vaikka se onkin tehty näistä tunnetuista komponenteista, ihmiset eivät kuitenkaan pysty tuottamaan mitään Koraanin kaltaista, mikä osoittaa sen ainutlaatuisen jumalallisen alkuperän. Luvut, jotka alkavat näillä kirjaimilla, puhuvat tyypillisesti Koraanista itsestään, joko heti perään tai myöhemmin, korostaen sen jäljittelemätöntä luonnetta.
هذه الآية تتكون من أحرف عربية منفصلة: ألف، لام، وميم. نفهم أن هذه الأحرف تظهر في بداية بعض السور في القرآن لتلفت الانتباه إلى أن هذا الكتاب الإلهي يتألف من نفس الحروف المألوفة التي يستخدمها الناس يومياً. ومع ذلك، على الرغم من أنها تتكون من هذه المكونات المعروفة، لا يستطيع البشر أن ينتجوا شيئاً يماثل القرآن، مما يدل على أصله الإلهي الفريد. السور التي تبدأ بهذه الأحرف عادة ما تتحدث عن القرآن نفسه، إما مباشرة بعد ذلك أو لاحقاً، مسلطة الضوء على طبيعته التي لا مثيل لها.
Verse 2:
أَحَسِبَ النَّاسُ أَنْ يُتْرَكُوا أَنْ يَقُولُوا آمَنَّا وَهُمْ لَا يُفْتَنُونَ
Translation: Do men think that they will be left alone on saying, "We believe", and that they will not be tested?
Commentary: Right after the opening letters, the Qur'an introduces a crucial concept about faith. It poses a question challenging the idea that merely saying "We believe" is enough. It asks if people imagine that by simply claiming faith, they will be left untouched and not face trials and tests. This sets the stage for understanding that belief is not just a declaration, but a profound commitment that will be proven through experience, leading into the discussion of testing in the next verse.
Finnish: Heti avauskirjainten jälkeen Koraani esittelee ratkaisevan käsitteen uskosta. Se esittää kysymyksen, joka haastaa ajatuksen siitä, että pelkkä "Uskomme" sanominen riittäisi. Se kysyy, kuvittelevatko ihmiset, että pelkästään uskoa väittämällä heidät jätetään rauhaan eivätkä kohtaa koettelemuksia ja testejä. Tämä asettaa pohjan ymmärrykselle, että usko ei ole pelkkä ilmoitus, vaan syvällinen sitoumus, joka todistetaan kokemuksen kautta, johtaen keskusteluun koettelemuksista seuraavassa jakeessa.
بعد الأحرف الافتتاحية مباشرة، يقدم القرآن مفهوماً حاسماً عن الإيمان. إنه يطرح سؤالاً يتحدى فكرة أن مجرد قول "آمنا" يكفي. يسأل عما إذا كان الناس يتصورون أنهم بمجرد ادعاء الإيمان سيتركون وشأنهم ولن يواجهوا التجارب والاختبارات. هذا يمهد الطريق لفهم أن الإيمان ليس مجرد إعلان، ولكنه التزام عميق سيتم إثباته من خلال التجربة، مما يقود إلى مناقشة الاختبار في الآية التالية.
Verse 3:
وَلَقَدْ فَتَنَّا الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ ۖ فَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ صَدَقُوا وَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ الْكَاذِبِينَ
Translation: We did test those before them, and Allah will certainly know those who are true from those who are false.
Commentary: Following the previous verse's question about testing, this verse confirms that trials are a universal and historical reality. It states that Allah certainly tested those who came before the current believers. The purpose of these tests is to distinguish between those who are truthful in their claim of faith and those who are not. While Allah knows what is in people's hearts beforehand, the test makes this reality apparent in practice, allowing people to be accountable for their actions and providing a clear example for humanity not to judge hidden intentions, which only Allah knows. This establishes the principle that trials are an essential part of the journey of faith.
Finnish: Edellisen jakeen koettelemuksia koskevan kysymyksen jälkeen tämä jae vahvistaa, että koetukset ovat universaali ja historiallinen todellisuus. Se toteaa, että Allah totisesti koetteli niitä, jotka tulivat ennen nykyisiä uskovaisia. Näiden testien tarkoituksena on erottaa toisistaan ne, jotka ovat totuudellisia uskontonsa ilmoituksessa, ja ne, jotka eivät. Vaikka Allah tietää, mitä ihmisten sydämissä on ennalta, testi tekee tämän todellisuuden selväksi käytännössä, antaen ihmisille mahdollisuuden olla vastuussa teoistaan ja tarjoten selkeän esimerkin ihmiskunnalle olla tuomitsematta piilotettuja aikeita, jotka vain Allah tietää. Tämä vakiinnuttaa periaatteen, että koetukset ovat olennainen osa uskon matkaa.
عقب السؤال في الآية السابقة عن الاختبار، تؤكد هذه الآية أن التجارب حقيقة عالمية وتاريخية. فهي تنص على أن الله اختبر بالتأكيد أولئك الذين أتوا قبل المؤمنين الحاليين. الغرض من هذه الاختبارات هو التمييز بين الصادقين في ادعائهم الإيمان وغير الصادقين. وبينما يعلم الله ما في قلوب الناس سلفاً، فإن الاختبار يظهر هذه الحقيقة عملياً، مما يسمح للناس بالمساءلة عن أفعالهم ويقدم مثالاً واضحاً للبشرية بعدم الحكم على النوايا الخفية التي يعلمها الله وحده. هذا يؤسس لمبدأ أن التجارب جزء أساسي من رحلة الإيمان.
Verse 4:
أَمْ حَسِبَ الَّذِينَ يَعْمَلُونَ السَّيِّئَاتِ أَنْ يَسْبِقُونَا ۚ سَاءَ مَا يَحْكُمُونَ
Translation: Do those who practise evil think that they will get the better of Us? Evil is their judgment!
Commentary: After discussing the testing of believers, the Qur'an turns its attention to those who commit evil deeds. This verse serves as a strong warning, parallel to the mention of testing believers, indicating that wicked individuals should not think they can escape Allah's reach or punishment. Their assumption that they can outwit or overcome divine justice is described as a terrible misjudgment. This verse assures believers undergoing tests that their oppressors will not ultimately succeed or escape consequences.
Finnish: Puhuttuaan uskovien koettelemisesta, Koraani kääntää huomionsa niihin, jotka tekevät pahoja tekoja. Tämä jae toimii voimakkaana varoituksena, rinnakkaisena maininnalle uskovien koettelemisesta, osoittaen, että pahojen yksilöiden ei pitäisi luulla voivansa paeta Allahin ulottuvuutta tai rangaistusta. Heidän oletustaan, että he voisivat oveluudella tai voittaa jumalallisen oikeuden, kuvataan kauheaksi harkintavirheeksi. Tämä jae vakuuttaa koetuksia läpikäyviä uskovaisia, että heidän sortajansa eivät lopulta onnistu tai pakene seurauksia.
بعد مناقشة اختبار المؤمنين، يلتفت القرآن إلى أولئك الذين يرتكبون الأفعال السيئة. هذه الآية بمثابة تحذير قوي، موازٍ لذكر اختبار المؤمنين، مشيرة إلى أن الأفراد الأشرار لا ينبغي أن يظنوا أنهم يستطيعون الهروب من قضاء الله أو عقابه. وصف افتراضهم بأنهم يستطيعون خداع العدالة الإلهية أو التغلب عليها بأنه حكم سيئ للغاية. هذه الآية تطمئن المؤمنين الذين يخضعون للاختبار بأن ظالميهم لن ينجحوا في النهاية أو يهربوا من العواقب.
Verse 5:
مَنْ كَانَ يَرْجُو لِقَاءَ اللَّهِ فَإِنَّ أَجَلَ اللَّهِ لَآتٍ ۚ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
Translation: For those whose hopes are in the meeting with Allah (in the Hereafter, let them strive); for the term (appointed) by Allah is surely coming and He hears and knows (all things).
Commentary: This verse offers reassurance to those who truly believe and look forward to meeting Allah in the afterlife. It encourages them to strive diligently, as the appointed time for this meeting, the Day of Judgment, is certainly coming. It reminds them that Allah hears all they say and knows all that is in their hearts and all their deeds, affirming His complete awareness of their aspirations and efforts. This connects the striving of believers to their ultimate hope and Allah's knowledge, providing a source of strength amidst tests.
Finnish: Tämä jae tarjoaa vakuutuksen niille, jotka todella uskovat ja odottavat innolla tapaamista Allahin kanssa tuonpuoleisessa. Se kannustaa heitä ponnistelemaan ahkerasti, sillä määrätty aika tälle tapaamiselle, tuomiopäivä, on varmasti tulossa. Se muistuttaa heitä siitä, että Allah kuulee kaiken, mitä he sanovat, ja tietää kaiken, mitä heidän sydämissään on, sekä kaikki heidän tekonsa, vahvistaen Hänen täydellisen tietoisuutensa heidän pyrkimyksistään ja ponnisteluistaan. Tämä yhdistää uskovien pyrkimykset heidän lopulliseen toivoonsa ja Allahin tietoon, tarjoten voimanlähteen koetusten keskellä.
هذه الآية تقدم طمأنينة لأولئك الذين يؤمنون حقاً ويتطلعون إلى لقاء الله في الآخرة. إنها تشجعهم على السعي بجد، لأن الموعد المحدد لهذا اللقاء، يوم القيامة، آتٍ يقيناً. تذكرهم بأن الله يسمع كل ما يقولون ويعلم كل ما في قلوبهم وكل أعمالهم، مؤكدة علمه التام بتطلعاتهم وجهودهم. هذا يربط سعي المؤمنين بأملهم النهائي وعلم الله، مقدماً مصدراً للقوة وسط الاختبارات.
Verse 6:
وَمَنْ جَاهَدَ فَإِنَّمَا يُجَاهِدُ لِنَفْسِهِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَغَنِيٌّ عَنِ الْعَالَمِينَ
Translation: And if any strive (with might and main), they do so for their own souls: for Allah is free of all needs from all creation.
Commentary: Building on the previous verse's call to strive, this verse clarifies who benefits from this effort. It states emphatically that when someone strives hard in the path of Allah, they are doing so for their own benefit, for their own souls. It emphasizes that Allah is completely independent and in no need of anyone's efforts or actions from all of creation. This perspective helps believers understand that their struggles and sacrifices are an investment in their own well-being and spiritual growth, reinforcing the personal value of their commitment during difficult tests.
Finnish: Rakentuen edellisen jakeen kehotukseen ponnistella, tämä jae selventää, kuka hyötyy tästä ponnistuksesta. Se toteaa painokkaasti, että kun joku ponnistelee kovasti Allahin tiellä, he tekevät niin omaksi hyväkseen, omille sieluilleen. Se korostaa, että Allah on täysin riippumaton eikä tarvitse kenenkään ponnistuksia tai tekoja koko luomakunnasta. Tämä näkökulma auttaa uskovaisia ymmärtämään, että heidän kamppailunsa ja uhrauksensa ovat investointi heidän omaan hyvinvointiinsa ja henkiseen kasvuunsa, vahvistaen heidän sitoumuksensa henkilökohtaista arvoa vaikeiden testien aikana.
استناداً إلى دعوة الآية السابقة للسعي، توضح هذه الآية من الذي يستفيد من هذا الجهد. إنها تنص بوضوح على أن من يجاهد (يسعى بجهد وعزم) في سبيل الله، فإنما يجاهد لنفسه، لروحه. وتؤكد أن الله غني عن العالمين ولا يحتاج إلى جهود أو أفعال أحد من خلقه. هذا المنظور يساعد المؤمنين على فهم أن صراعاتهم وتضحياتهم هي استثمار في رفاهيتهم ونموهم الروحي، مما يعزز القيمة الشخصية لالتزامهم خلال الاختبارات الصعبة.
Verse 7:
وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ لَنُكَفِّرَنَّ عَنْهُمْ سَيِّئَاتِهِمْ وَلَنَجْزِيَنَّهُمْ أَحْسَنَ الَّذِي كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ
Translation: Those who believe and work righteous deeds,- from them shall We blot out all evil (that may be) in them, and We shall reward them according to the best of their deeds.
Commentary: Following the discussion of striving and its benefit to the individual, this verse offers a promise of reward for those who successfully navigate the tests of faith. It assures that for those who truly believe and perform good deeds, Allah will remove their shortcomings or wrongdoings and will reward them generously, based on the very best of what they did. This serves as a powerful encouragement and comfort for believers enduring hardships, reminding them of the ultimate positive outcome awaiting their perseverance and good conduct.
Finnish: Seuraten keskustelua ponnistelusta ja sen hyödystä yksilölle, tämä jae tarjoaa lupauksen palkkiosta niille, jotka menestyksekkäästi selviävät uskon koetuksista. Se vakuuttaa, että niiltä, jotka todella uskovat ja tekevät oikeamielisiä tekoja, Allah pyyhkii pois kaikki mahdolliset pahuudet (erehdykset) heissä ja palkitsee heidät heidän parhaiden tekojensa mukaan. Tämä toimii voimakkaana rohkaisuna ja lohtuna uskovaisille, jotka kestävät vaikeuksia, muistuttaen heitä lopullisesta positiivisesta lopputuloksesta, joka odottaa heidän sinnikkyyttään ja hyvää käytöstään.
بعد مناقشة السعي وفائدته للفرد، تقدم هذه الآية وعداً بالمكافأة لأولئك الذين يجتازون اختبارات الإيمان بنجاح. تؤكد أنه بالنسبة لأولئك الذين يؤمنون حقاً ويعملون الصالحات، فإن الله سيكفر عنهم سيئاتهم (أخطائهم أو ذنوبهم) وسيجزيهم بأحسن ما كانوا يعملون. هذا بمثابة تشجيع قوي ومريح للمؤمنين الذين يتحملون الصعوبات، مذكراً إياهم بالنتيجة الإيجابية النهائية التي تنتظر مثابرتهم وسلوكهم الحسن.
Verse 8:
وَوَصَّيْنَا الْإِنْسَانَ بِوَالِدَيْهِ حُسْنًا ۖ وَإِنْ جَاهَدَاكَ لِتُشْرِكَ بِي مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِ عِلْمٌ فَلَا تُطِعْهُمَا ۚ إِلَيَّ مَرْجِعُكُمْ فَأُنَبِّئُكُمْ بِمَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ
Translation: We have enjoined on man kindness to parents: but if they (either of them) strive (to force) thee to join with Me (in worship) anything of which thou hast no knowledge, obey them not. Ye have (all) to return to me, and I will tell you (the truth) of all that ye did.
Commentary: Introducing a specific, often difficult, test, this verse addresses the significant relationship with parents. It clearly states that Allah has commanded people to be kind and good to their parents. However, it provides specific guidance for a challenging situation: if parents pressure their child to worship something alongside Allah, something they have no true knowledge or evidence for, the child should not obey them in this matter. It reminds everyone that ultimately, all will return to Allah, and He will inform them of everything they did, highlighting that the primary loyalty is to the Creator. This verse shows how faith can introduce tests even within the closest family bonds.
Finnish: Tämä jae, joka esittelee erityisen, usein vaikean, koetuksen, käsittelee merkityksellistä suhdetta vanhempiin. Se toteaa selvästi, että Allah on määrännyt ihmisiä olemaan ystävällisiä ja hyviä vanhemmilleen. Se antaa kuitenkin erityisiä ohjeita haastavaan tilanteeseen: jos vanhemmat painostavat lastaan palvomaan jotain Allahin rinnalla, jotain josta heillä ei ole todellista tietoa tai näyttöä, lapsen ei pidä totella heitä tässä asiassa. Se muistuttaa kaikkia, että lopulta kaikki palaavat Allahin luo, ja Hän kertoo heille kaikesta, mitä he tekivät, korostaen, että ensisijainen uskollisuus kuuluu Luojalle. Verse 9:
وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ لَنُدْخِلَنَّهُمْ فِي الصَّالِحِينَ
Translation: And those who believe and work righteous deeds,- them shall We admit to the company of the Righteous.
Commentary: This verse returns to the theme of the believers who have successfully navigated tests, including potentially difficult ones like conflicts related to faith within the family (as mentioned in the previous verse). It promises that those who hold firm to their belief and continue to do righteous deeds will be admitted into the company of the righteous people. This reinforces the idea that the community of believers, bound by their commitment to Allah, is the ultimate association, which holds true in this life and is perfected in the afterlife, where they will be gathered together.

Verse 10:
وَمِنَ النَّاسِ مَنْ يَقُولُ آمَنَّا بِاللَّهِ فَإِذَا أُوذِيَ فِي اللَّهِ جَعَلَ فِتْنَةَ النَّاسِ كَعَذَابِ اللَّهِ وَلَئِنْ جَاءَ نَصْرٌ مِنْ رَبِّكَ لَيَقُولُنَّ إِنَّا كُنَّا مَعَكُمْ ۚ أَوَلَيْسَ اللَّهُ بِأَعْلَمَ بِمَا فِي صُدُورِ الْعَالَمِينَ
Translation: Then there are among men such as say, "We believe in Allah"; but when they suffer affliction in (the cause of) Allah, they treat men's oppression as if it were the Wrath of Allah! And if help comes (to thee) from thy Lord, they are sure to say, "We have (always) been with you!" Does not Allah know best all that is in the hearts of all creation?
Commentary: In contrast to the steadfast believers described earlier, this verse highlights another type of person: those who claim faith easily but falter under pressure. It describes how some people declare belief, but when faced with hardship or oppression for their faith, they react dramatically, viewing human persecution as something unbearable, like the wrath of Allah itself. However, when circumstances change and help or victory comes from Allah, these same individuals quickly claim to have always been with the believers. The verse concludes by asking a rhetorical question, emphasizing that Allah is fully aware of what is in the hearts of everyone, revealing the insincerity of such individuals during testing times. This provides a clear contrast between true believers and those whose faith is weak or superficial when faced with difficulty.

Verse 11:

Translation: And Allah most certainly knows those who believe, and as certainly those who are Hypocrites.
Commentary: Following the discussion about how people are tested in their faith, this verse concludes that Allah has perfect knowledge of everyone. The tests and trials mentioned earlier serve to reveal to people themselves and to others what Allah already fully knows about their hearts and intentions. This verse underscores Allah's complete awareness of everyone's inner state.

Verse 12:
وَقَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اتَّبِعُوا سَبِيلَنَا وَلْنَحْمِلْ خَطَايَاكُمْ وَمَا هُمْ بِحَامِلِينَ مِنْ خَطَايَاهُمْ مِنْ شَيْءٍ ۖ إِنَّهُمْ لَكَاذِبُونَ
Translation: And the Unbelievers say to those who believe: "Follow our path, and we will bear (the consequences) of your faults." Never in the least will they bear their faults: in fact they are liars!
Commentary: This verse introduces a form of temptation or challenge faced by believers. It describes those who do not believe telling the believers to abandon their way and follow the path of disbelief instead. To entice them, they make a false promise, claiming they would bear the negative consequences, or "faults," of the believers' decision to switch paths. This is presented as a deceptive offer. The verse immediately states that they will not bear the faults of others at all and declares their claim to be untruthful. This sets up the next verse which explains the reality of accountability.
Finnish: Tämä jae esittelee eräänlaisen kiusauksen tai haasteen, jonka uskovaiset kohtaavat. Houkutellakseen heitä he antavat väärän lupauksen väittäen kantavansa uskovaisten päätöksen vaihtaa tietä negatiiviset seuraukset, eli "virheet". Tämä esitetään petollisena tarjouksena. Jae toteaa välittömästi, etteivät he missään nimessä kanna muiden virheitä, ja julistaa heidän väitteensä perättömäksi. Tämä pohjustaa seuraavaa jaetta, joka selittää vastuun todellisuuden.

Verse 13:
وَلَيَحْمِلُنَّ أَثْقَالَهُمْ وَأَثْقَالًا مَعَ أَثْقَالِهِمْ ۖ وَلَيُسْأَلُنَّ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ عَمَّا كَانُوا يَفْتَرُونَ
Translation: They will bear their own burdens, and (other) burdens along with their own, and on the Day of Judgments they will be called to account for their falsehoods.
Commentary: Building on the refutation in the previous verse, this verse clarifies the true nature of responsibility. It explains that those who urged others to abandon their faith will carry the full weight of their *own* actions and beliefs. In addition to their personal responsibility, they will also bear the burden of having misled others, meaning the negative consequences of the choices made by those they influenced to go astray will add to their own accountability. This highlights the seriousness of leading others away from the path of faith. Both those who mislead and those who are misled are accountable for their individual choices, but the misleaders carry an additional burden for their negative influence. The verse emphasizes that on the Day of Judgment, everyone will be held accountable for their actions, including the false claims they made, such as promising to bear the consequences for others. This reinforces the fundamental principle in Islam that each person is individually responsible before Allah for their deeds and beliefs.

Se selittää, että ne, jotka kehottivat toisia luopumaan uskosta, kantavat täyden painon *omistaan* teoistaan ja uskomuksistaan. Henkilökohtaisen vastuunsa lisäksi he kantavat myös taakan siitä, että ovat johtaneet toisia harhaan, mikä tarkoittaa, että niiden valintojen negatiiviset seuraukset, jotka heidän vaikutuksestaan ovat joutuneet harhaan, lisäävät heidän omaan vastuuseensa. Sekä ne, jotka johtavat harhaan, että ne, jotka joutuvat harhaan, ovat vastuussa yksilöllisistä valinnoistaan, mutta harhaanjohtajat kantavat lisätaakan negatiivisesta vaikutuksestaan. Jae korostaa, että tuomion päivänä jokainen joutuu vastaamaan teoistaan, mukaan lukien heidän tekemänsä valheelliset väitteet, kuten lupaus kantaa seuraukset toisten puolesta. Verse 14:

Translation: We (once) sent Noah to his people, and he tarried among them a thousand years less fifty: but the Deluge overwhelmed them while they (persisted in) sin.
Commentary: This verse begins a series of examples from history, illustrating the experiences of prophets and their communities. It tells us about Prophet Noah, who lived among his people for 950 years, inviting them to follow Allah's guidance. Despite his long efforts, only a few people believed. Because the majority of his people continued in their wrongdoing and rejected his message, Allah's punishment came upon them in the form of a great flood.

Verse 18:

Translation: See they not how Allah originates creation, then repeats it: truly that is easy for Allah.
Commentary: The focus shifts here to a broader message directed at those who deny the truth and the afterlife. This verse encourages reflection on the universe as evidence for Allah's power and ability to resurrect. It prompts people to observe how Allah initiates creation in the first place – how life begins and things come into being from nothing, a process humans cannot replicate or fully understand. It then asserts that the One who originates creation is certainly able to bring it back again, stating that this is easy for Allah, using a comparison understandable by human standards, though all things are equally easy for Allah.
Finnish: Tässä siirrytään laajempaan sanomaan, joka on suunnattu niille, jotka kieltävät totuuden ja tuonpuoleisen elämän. However, once they are back on dry land and feel secure in this world, they revert to associating partners with Allah. This illustrates that their acknowledgment of Allah's power is conditional on their worldly need or fear, rather than a consistent, sincere faith. It shows how the temporary nature of this life and its perceived safety cause them to forget the One who truly saves them.
