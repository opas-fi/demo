Sura 28

Verse 1:
طسم
Translation: Ta. Sin. Mim.
Commentary: This verse consists of three individual letters from the Arabic alphabet. The Qur'an begins several chapters with such letters. Their full meaning is known only to Allah, but they serve to show that the Qur'an is composed of these same basic letters, yet its verses contain profound meaning and beauty that human beings cannot replicate.
Finnish: Tämä jae koostuu kolmesta yksittäisestä kirjaimesta arabian aakkosista. Koraani aloittaa useita lukuja tällaisilla kirjaimilla. Niiden täysi merkitys on tunnettu vain Allahille, mutta ne osoittavat, että Koraani koostuu samoista peruskirjaimista, mutta sen jakeet sisältävät syvällistä merkitystä ja kauneutta, jota ihmiset eivät voi jäljitellä.
تتكون هذه الآية من ثلاثة أحرف مفردة من الأبجدية العربية. يبدأ القرآن عدة سور بمثل هذه الحروف. معناها الكامل معلوم لله وحده، لكنها تهدف إلى إظهار أن القرآن يتكون من نفس هذه الأحرف الأساسية، ومع ذلك فإن آياته تحتوي على معنى عميق وجمال لا يستطيع البشر محاكاته.
Verse 2:
تِلْكَ آيَاتُ الْكِتَابِ الْمُبِينِ
Translation: These are Verses of the Book that makes (things) clear.
Commentary: Following the opening letters, this verse describes the nature of the Qur'an itself, referred to here as "the Book." It emphasizes that these verses are clear and illuminating, providing guidance and making things understandable. This verse connects to the previous one by introducing the divine origin and nature of the Book whose verses follow.
Finnish: Aloituskirjainten jälkeen tämä jae kuvaa itse Koraanin luonnetta, johon tässä viitataan "Kirjana". Se korostaa, että nämä jakeet ovat selkeitä ja valaisevia, tarjoten opastusta ja tehden asioista ymmärrettäviä. Tämä jae liittyy edelliseen esittelemällä sen Kirjan jumalallisen alkuperän ja luonteen, jonka jakeet seuraavat.
عقِب الحروف الافتتاحية، تصف هذه الآية طبيعة القرآن نفسه، المشار إليه هنا بـ "الكتاب". تؤكد أن هذه الآيات واضحة ومبينة، توفر الهداية وتجعل الأمور مفهومة. تربط هذه الآية بالسابقة بتقديم الأصل الإلهي وطبيعة الكتاب الذي ستأتي آياته.
Verse 3:
نَتْلُو عَلَيْكَ مِنْ نَبَإِ مُوسَىٰ وَفِرْعَوْنَ بِالْحَقِّ لِقَوْمٍ يُؤْمِنُونَ
Translation: We rehearse to thee some of the story of Moses and Pharaoh in Truth, for people who believe.
Commentary: Moving from the description of the Qur'an, this verse announces that Allah will narrate a portion of the true history of Prophet Moses and Pharaoh. It highlights that this story is presented truthfully and is particularly for those who believe, as they are the ones who will benefit most from its lessons and guidance.
Finnish: Siirtyen Koraanin kuvauksesta, tämä jae ilmoittaa, että Allah kertoo osan profeetta Mooseksen ja faaraon todellisesta historiasta. Se korostaa, että tämä tarina esitetään totuudenmukaisesti ja on erityisesti niille, jotka uskovat, sillä he hyötyvät sen opetuksista ja ohjauksesta eniten.
انتقالا من وصف القرآن، تعلن هذه الآية أن الله سيروي جزءا من التاريخ الحقيقي للنبي موسى وفرعون. تسلط الضوء على أن هذه القصة تُعرض بصدق، وهي خاصة للمؤمنين، لأنهم هم الذين سيستفيدون أكثر من دروسها وهدايتها.
Verse 4:
إِنَّ فِرْعَوْنَ عَلَا فِي الْأَرْضِ وَجَعَلَ أَهْلَهَا شِيَعًا يَسْتَضْعِفُ طَائِفَةً مِنْهُمْ يُذَبِّحُ أَبْنَاءَهُمْ وَيَسْتَحْيِي نِسَاءَهُمْ ۚ إِنَّهُ كَانَ مِنَ الْمُفْسِدِينَ
Translation: Truly Pharaoh elated himself in the land and broke up its people into sections, depressing a small group among them: their sons he slew, but he kept alive their females: for he was indeed a maker of mischief.
Commentary: This verse sets the historical stage for the story of Prophet Moses, immediately following the introduction in verse 3. It describes the oppressive rule of Pharaoh in Egypt, detailing how he divided society and severely persecuted a particular group, the Children of Israel. This persecution included a brutal policy of killing their newborn male children while sparing the females, revealing his nature as a corrupt and oppressive ruler. This context of extreme tyranny is crucial for understanding the subsequent events.
Finnish: Tämä jae asettaa historiallisen näyttämön profeetta Mooseksen tarinalle, välittömästi jakeen 3 johdannon jälkeen. Se kuvaa faaraon sortavaa hallintoa Egyptissä, kertoen yksityiskohtaisesti, kuinka hän jakoi yhteiskunnan ja vainosi ankarasti tiettyä ryhmää, Israelin lapsia. Tämä vaino sisälsi raa'an politiikan, jossa tapettiin heidän vastasyntyneet poikalapsensa naaraspuolisten säästämiseksi, paljastaen hänen luonteensa turmeltuneena ja sortavana hallitsijana. Tämä äärimmäisen tyrannian konteksti on ratkaiseva seuraavien tapahtumien ymmärtämiseksi.
تضع هذه الآية المسرح التاريخي لقصة النبي موسى، مباشرة بعد المقدمة في الآية 3. تصف حكم فرعون القمعي في مصر، وتفصل كيف قسم المجتمع واضطهد بشدة فئة معينة، بني إسرائيل. شمل هذا الاضطهاد سياسة وحشية لقتل أبنائهم الذكور حديثي الولادة مع استبقاء الإناث، كاشفة عن طبيعته كحاكم فاسد وقمعي. هذا السياق من الطغيان الشديد حاسم لفهم الأحداث اللاحقة.
Verse 5:
وَنُرِيدُ أَنْ نَمُنَّ عَلَى الَّذِينَ اسْتُضْعِفُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ وَنَجْعَلَهُمْ أَئِمَّةً وَنَجْعَلَهُمُ الْوَارِثِينَ
Translation: And We wished to be Gracious to those who were being depressed in the land, to make them leaders (in Faith) and make them heirs,
Commentary: In contrast to the human tyranny described in verse 4, this verse introduces Allah's divine will. It states that Allah intended to bestow His favor upon the very people who were being oppressed, planning to elevate them to positions of leadership and make them inheritors of the land, signifying a complete reversal of their status under Pharaoh.
Finnish: Vastakohtana jakeessa 4 kuvatulle ihmisten tyrannialle, tämä jae esittelee Allahin jumalallisen tahdon. Se ilmoittaa, että Allah aikoi osoittaa suosiotaan juuri niitä kohtaan, joita sorrettiin, suunnitellen heidän korottamistaan johtotehtäviin ja tekemistä maan perillisiksi, mikä merkitsee täydellistä käännettä heidän asemassaan faaraon alaisuudessa.
على النقيض من الطغيان البشري الموصوف في الآية 4، تقدم هذه الآية إرادة الله الإلهية. تنص على أن الله أراد أن ينعم بفضله على أولئك الذين كانوا يتعرضون للقمع، خطط لرفعهم إلى مناصب قيادية وجعلهم ورثة الأرض، مما يدل على عكس كامل لحالتهم تحت حكم فرعون.
Verse 6:
وَنُمَكِّنَ لَهُمْ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَنُرِيَ فِرْعَوْنَ وَهَامَانَ وَجُنُودَهُمَا مِنْهُمْ مَا كَانُوا يَحْذَرُونَ
Translation: To establish a firm place for them in the land, and to show Pharaoh, Haman, and their hosts, at their hands, the very things against which they were taking precautions.
Commentary: Continuing from verse 5, this verse elaborates on Allah's plan to empower the oppressed group. It states the intention to give them security and dominance in the land. Furthermore, it reveals Allah's plan to use this group as the means to inflict upon Pharaoh, his advisor Haman, and their forces, the very outcome they feared and tried to prevent through their oppressive actions. This verse connects Allah's will (verse 5) to its direct impact on Pharaoh's regime.
Finnish: Jatkaen jakeesta 5, tämä jae tarkentaa Allahin suunnitelmaa sortetun ryhmän voimaannuttamiseksi. Se ilmoittaa aikomuksen antaa heille turvallisuus ja valta maassa. Lisäksi se paljastaa Allahin suunnitelman käyttää tätä ryhmää keinona kohdistaa faaraoon, hänen neuvonantajaansa Haamaniin ja heidän joukkoihinsa juuri sen seurauksen, jota he pelkäsivät ja yrittivät estää sortavilla teoillaan. Tämä jae yhdistää Allahin tahdon (jae 5) sen suoraan vaikutukseen faaraon hallintoon.
استمرارا من الآية 5، توضح هذه الآية خطة الله لتمكين الفئة المظلومة. تذكر نية منحهم الأمن والسيادة في الأرض. علاوة على ذلك، تكشف عن خطة الله في استخدام هذه الفئة كوسيلة ليحل بفرعون وهامان مستشاره وجنودهما، عين ما كانوا يخشونه وحاولوا منعه بأفعالهم القمعية. تربط هذه الآية بين إرادة الله (الآية 5) وتأثيرها المباشر على نظام فرعون.
Verse 7:
وَأَوْحَيْنَا إِلَىٰ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰ أَنْ أَرْضِعِيهِ ۖ فَإِذَا خِفْتِ عَلَيْهِ فَأَلْقِيهِ فِي الْيَمِّ وَلَا تَخَافِي وَلَا تَحْزَنِي ۖ إِنَّا رَادُّوهُ إِلَيْكِ وَجَاعِلُوهُ مِنَ الْمُرْسَلِينَ
Translation: So We sent this inspiration to the mother of Moses: "Suckle (thy child), but when thou hast fears about him, cast him into the river, but fear not nor grieve: for We shall restore him to thee, and We shall make him one of Our messengers."
Commentary: This verse begins the narrative of Prophet Moses's birth, illustrating how Allah's plan (mentioned in verses 5-6) begins to unfold. It describes a direct divine inspiration given to his mother, instructing her to take the seemingly contradictory action of placing her child in the river if she feared for his safety. Crucially, this instruction is accompanied by a powerful reassurance: she should not fear or grieve because Allah promises to return the child to her and make him one of His messengers. This sets the stage for the miraculous events that follow.
Finnish: Tämä jae aloittaa kertomuksen profeetta Mooseksen syntymästä, kuvaten kuinka Allahin suunnitelma (mainittu jakeissa 5-6) alkaa toteutua. Se kuvaa suoraa jumalallista ilmoitusta, joka annettiin hänen äidilleen, kehottaen häntä tekemään ilmeisen ristiriitaisen teon: panemaan lapsensa jokeen, jos hän pelkäsi hänen turvallisuutensa puolesta. Ratkaisevaa on, että tähän ohjeeseen liittyy voimakas vakuutus: hän ei saisi pelätä tai surra, koska Allah lupaa palauttaa lapsen hänelle ja tehdä hänestä yhden Hänen lähettiläistään. Tämä asettaa näyttämön seuraaville ihmeellisille tapahtumille.
تبدأ هذه الآية سرد ميلاد النبي موسى، موضحا كيف تبدأ خطة الله (المذكورة في الآيتين 5-6) في الظهور. تصف وحيا إلهيا مباشرا أُنزل على أمه، يأمرها باتخاذ إجراء يبدو متناقضا: بأن تضع طفلها في النهر إذا خافت عليه. والأهم من ذلك، أن هذا الأمر مصحوب بتأكيد قوي: ألا تخاف ولا تحزن، لأنا سنرده إليك ونجعله من المرسلين. هذا يمهد الطريق للأحداث المعجزة التي تتبع.
Verse 8:
فَالْتَقَطَهُ آلُ فِرْعَوْنَ لِيَكُونَ لَهُمْ عَدُوًّا وَحَزَنًا ۗ إِنَّ فِرْعَوْنَ وَهَامَانَ وَجُنُودَهُمَا كَانُوا خَاطِئِينَ
Translation: Then the people of Pharaoh picked him up (from the river): (It was intended) that (Moses) should be to them an adversary and a cause of sorrow: for Pharaoh and Haman and (all) their hosts were men of sin.
Commentary: Following the instruction given to his mother in verse 7, this verse describes what happened next: the child was found and retrieved by Pharaoh's own household. This seemingly dangerous turn of events was, however, part of Allah's plan. The verse explicitly states that the intention behind this was for the child to eventually become a direct challenge and source of distress for Pharaoh, Haman, and their sinful regime, highlighting how Allah turned the oppressor's caution against them.
Finnish: Jatkona äidilleen jakeessa 7 annetulle ohjeelle, tämä jae kuvaa, mitä seuraavaksi tapahtui: lapsen löysi ja nouti faaraon oma hoviväki. Tämä näennäisesti vaarallinen käänne oli kuitenkin osa Allahin suunnitelmaa. Jae ilmoittaa selvästi, että tämän takana oleva tarkoitus oli, että lapsesta tulisi lopulta suora haaste ja ahdistuksen lähde faaraolle, Haamanille ja heidän syntiselle hallinnolleen, korostaen, kuinka Allah käänsi sortajan varovaisuuden heitä vastaan.
بعد الأمر الذي أُعطي لأمه في الآية 7، تصف هذه الآية ما حدث بعد ذلك: عثر على الطفل وانتشله أهل فرعون أنفسهم. هذا التطور الذي بدا خطيرا كان، مع ذلك، جزءا من خطة الله. تنص الآية صراحة على أن القصد من وراء ذلك هو أن يصبح الطفل في نهاية المطاف تحديا مباشرا ومصدر ضيق لفرعون وهامان ونظامهما الخاطئ، مسلطة الضوء على كيف قلب الله حذر الطغاة عليهم.
Verse 9:
وَقَالَتِ امْرَأَتُ فِرْعَوْنَ قُرَّتُ عَيْنٍ لِي وَلَكَ ۖ لَا تَقْتُلُوهُ عَسَىٰ أَنْ يَنْفَعَنَا أَوْ نَتَّخِذَهُ وَلَدًا وَهُمْ لَا يَشْعُرُونَ
Translation: The wife of Pharaoh said: "(Here is) joy of the eye, for me and for thee: slay him not. It may be that he will be use to us, or we may adopt him as a son." And they perceived not (what they were doing)!
Commentary: Continuing the scene from verse 8, this verse reveals the immediate reaction within Pharaoh's household after finding the child. Pharaoh's wife felt an instant affection for him, seeing him as a source of joy and urging her husband not to harm him, suggesting they might adopt him. The verse concludes by noting their lack of awareness regarding the true significance of taking in this child, who was destined to be their undoing as mentioned in verse 8, illustrating Allah's subtle hand at work against their plans.
Finnish: Jatkaen kohtausta jakeesta 8, tämä jae paljastaa välittömän reaktion faaraon hoviväessä lapsen löytymisen jälkeen. Faaraon vaimo tunsi välitöntä kiintymystä häneen, pitäen häntä silmän ilona ja kehottaen miestään olemaan vahingoittamatta häntä, ehdottaen, että he voisivat adoptoida hänet. Jae päättyy huomautukseen heidän tietämättömyydestään tämän lapsen ottamisen todellisesta merkityksestä, joka oli määrätty heidän tuhokseen, kuten jakeessa 8 mainittiin, kuvaten Allahin hienovaraista kättä toiminnassa heidän suunnitelmiaan vastaan.
استمرارا للمشهد من الآية 8، تكشف هذه الآية رد الفعل الفوري داخل بيت فرعون بعد العثور على الطفل. شعرت امرأة فرعون بحب فوري له، رأت فيه قرة عين، وحثت زوجها على عدم إيذائه، مقترحة أنهم قد يتبنونه. تختتم الآية بالإشارة إلى عدم إدراكهم للمغزى الحقيقي لأخذ هذا الطفل، الذي كان مقدرا له أن يكون سبب هلاكهم كما ذكر في الآية 8، موضحا يد الله الخفية تعمل ضد خططهم.
Verse 10:
وَأَصْبَحَ فُؤَادُ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰ فَارِغًا ۖ إِنْ كَادَتْ لَتُبْدِي بِهِ لَوْلَا أَنْ رَبَطْنَا عَلَىٰ قَلْبِهَا لِتَكُونَ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ
Translation: But there came to be a void in the heart of the mother of Moses: She was going almost to disclose his (case), had We not strengthened her heart (with faith), so that she might remain a (firm) believer.
Commentary: While the child found protection in Pharaoh's household (verses 8-9), this verse shifts the focus back to the mother of Prophet Moses. It describes the intense anxiety and emotional emptiness she felt after casting her child into the river. Her distress was so profound that she was close to revealing the secret of her child's identity and survival. However, Allah intervened by strengthening her heart with faith, enabling her to remain steadfast and trust in His promise (from verse 7), despite the overwhelming difficulty of the situation.
Finnish: Vaikka lapsi löysi suojan faaraon hovista (jakeet 8-9), tämä jae siirtää huomion takaisin profeetta Mooseksen äitiin. Se kuvaa voimakasta ahdistusta ja emotionaalista tyhjyyttä, jonka hän tunsi heitettyään lapsensa jokeen. Hänen ahdistuksensa oli niin syvä, että hän oli lähellä paljastaa lapsensa henkilöllisyyden ja selviytymisen salaisuuden. Kuitenkin Allah puuttui asiaan vahvistamalla hänen sydäntään uskolla, mahdollistaen hänen pysyä vakaana ja luottaa Hänen lupaukseensa (jakeesta 7), huolimatta tilanteen ylivoimaisesta vaikeudesta.
بينما وجد الطفل الحماية في بيت فرعون (الآيات 8-9)، تحول هذه الآية التركيز مرة أخرى إلى أم النبي موسى. تصف القلق الشديد والفراغ العاطفي الذي شعرت به بعد إلقاء طفلها في النهر. كان ضيقها عميقا جدا حتى أنها كادت أن تكشف عن سر هوية طفلها وبقائه على قيد الحياة. ومع ذلك، تدخل الله بتعزيز قلبها بالإيمان، مما مكنها من البقاء ثابتة والوثوق بوعده (من الآية 7)، على الرغم من الصعوبة الغامرة للوضع.
Verse 11:
وَقَالَتْ لِأُخْتِهِ قُصِّيهِ ۖ فَبَصُرَتْ بِهِ عَنْ جُنُبٍ وَهُمْ لَا يَشْعُرُونَ
Translation: And she said to the sister of (Moses), "Follow him" so she (the sister) watched him in the character of a stranger. And they knew not.
Commentary: Following the guidance Allah had inspired in her heart, Moses’s mother placed her infant son in a chest and cast him into the river. Filled with worry, she then instructed her daughter, the infant’s sister, to follow the chest and see where it went. The sister obeyed, watching from a distance like a stranger, discreetly observing what would happen, while those who found him were unaware she was connected to the child.
Finnish: Noudattaen johdatusta, jonka Allah oli antanut hänen sydämeensä, Mooseksen äiti asetti vauvansa arkkuun ja laski hänet jokeen. Huolissaan tästä hän sitten käski tytärtään, vauvan siskoa, seurata arkkua ja nähdä minne se menee. Sisko totteli, tarkkaillen etäältä kuin vieras, huomaamattomasti seuraten mitä tapahtuisi, samalla kun ne jotka löysivät hänet, eivät tienneet hänen yhteydestään lapseen.
متبعة الإلهام الذي أوحاه الله في قلبها، وضعت أم موسى رضيعها في صندوق وألقته في النهر. ملؤها القلق، ثم أمرت ابنتها، أخت الرضيع، أن تتبع الصندوق وترى أين يذهب. أطاعت الأخت، وهي تراقبه عن بعد كالغريبة، تلاحظ ما يحدث بتكتم، بينما من وجدوه لم يكونوا على علم بارتباطها بالطفل.
Verse 12:
وَحَرَّمْنَا عَلَيْهِ الْمَرَاضِعَ مِنْ قَبْلُ فَقَالَتْ هَلْ أَدُلُّكُمْ عَلَىٰ أَهْلِ بَيْتٍ يَكْفُلُونَهُ لَكُمْ وَهُمْ لَهُ نَاصِحُونَ
Translation: And we ordained that he refused suck at first, until (His sister came up and) said: "Shall I point out to you the people of a house that will nourish and bring him up for you and be sincerely attached to him?"...
Commentary: As the story unfolds, we learn that Allah’s plan continued to work in remarkable ways. When the infant Moses was found by Pharaoh's household, they sought a wet-nurse for him. However, Allah willed that the baby refused to be nursed by any woman they brought, causing them distress and concern for the child. It was at this crucial moment, while they were seeking someone to nurse him, that his sister approached them and, seeing their need, offered to direct them to a family who could nourish and care for him properly. This moment sets the stage for the child to be returned to his own mother, as explained in the next verse.
Finnish: Kertomuksen edetessä saamme tietää, että Allahin suunnitelma jatkoi toteutumistaan ihmeellisillä tavoilla. Kun faraon hoviväki löysi vauva-Moseksen, he etsivät hänelle imettäjää. Kuitenkin Allah tahtoi, että vauva kieltäytyi imetettäväksi minkään naisen toimesta, jonka he toivat, aiheuttaen heille ahdistusta ja huolta lapsesta. Verse 43:

Finnish: Toisin kuin niiden kohtalo, jotka hylkäsivät Allahin sanomat ja tuhottiin (kuten Farao ja aikaisemmat sukupolvet), tämä jae korostaa Allahin armoa ja johdatusta Profeetta Moosesta ja hänen kansaansa kohtaan. Verse 44:
وَمَا كُنْتَ بِجَانِبِ الْغَرْبِيِّ إِذْ قَضَيْنَا إِلَىٰ مُوسَى الْأَمْرَ وَمَا كُنْتَ مِنَ الشَّاهِدِينَ
Translation: Thou wast not on the Western side when We decreed the Commission to Moses, nor wast thou a witness (of those events).

Finnish: Tämä jae osoittaa Profeetta Muhammadia ja huomauttaa, ettei hän ollut fyysisesti läsnä, kun Profeetta Mooses sai jumalallisia käskyjä Allahilta Siinainvuoren länsipuolella. Verse 45:
وَلَٰكِنَّا أَنْشَأْنَا قُرُونًا فَتَطَاوَلَ عَلَيْهِمُ الْعُمُرُ ۚ وَمَا كُنْتَ ثَاوِيًا فِي أَهْلِ مَدْيَنَ تَتْلُو عَلَيْهِمْ آيَاتِنَا وَلَٰكِنَّا كُنَّا مُرْسِلِينَ
Translation: But We raised up (new) generations, and long were the ages that passed over them; but thou wast not a dweller among the people of Madyan, rehearsing Our Signs to them; but it is We Who send messengers (with inspiration).
Commentary: Building on the previous verse, this verse emphasizes the vast span of time and the many generations that elapsed between the time of Prophet Moses and Prophet Muhammad. It specifically mentions the area of Madyan, where Moses also spent time. The point is reinforced: Prophet Muhammad did not live among these people or learn their history from them. His detailed knowledge comes from Allah, who raises generations, knows all ages, chooses messengers, and reveals information to them. This further underscores that the Qur'anic account is divinely sourced.
Finnish: Rakentuen edelliseen jakeeseen, tämä jae korostaa valtavaa ajanjaksoa ja monia sukupolvia, jotka kuluivat Profeetta Mooseksen ja Profeetta Muhammadin aikojen välillä. Se mainitsee erityisesti Midianin alueen, jossa Mooseskin vietti aikaa. Pointti vahvistetaan: Profeetta Muhammad ei elänyt näiden ihmisten parissa eikä oppinut heidän historiaansa heiltä. Hänen yksityiskohtainen tietonsa tulee Allahilta, Kuka nostaa sukupolvia, tuntee kaikki aikakaudet, valitsee sanansaattajia ja ilmoittaa tietoa heille. Tämä alleviivaa edelleen sitä, että Koraanin kuvaus on jumalallisesti peräisin.
استمراراً للآية السابقة، تؤكد هذه الآية على المدة الزمنية الشاسعة والأجيال العديدة التي مرت بين زمن النبي موسى والنبي محمد. وتذكر تحديداً منطقة مدين، حيث قضى موسى وقتاً أيضاً. ويتم التأكيد على النقطة التالية: النبي محمد لم يعش بين هؤلاء الناس ولم يتعلم تاريخهم منهم. بل علمه المفصل يأتي من الله، الذي ينشئ الأجيال، ويعلم كل العصور، ويختار الرسل، ويكشف لهم المعلومات. وهذا يؤكد مرة أخرى على أن الرواية القرآنية مصدرها إلهي.
Verse 46:
وَمَا كُنْتَ بِجَانِبِ الطُّورِ إِذْ نَادَيْنَا وَلَٰكِنْ رَحْمَةً مِنْ رَبِّكَ لِتُنْذِرَ قَوْمًا مَا أَتَاهُمْ مِنْ نَذِيرٍ مِنْ قَبْلِكَ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَتَذَكَّرُونَ

The comprehensive account of this event in the Qur'an is, therefore, evidence that this knowledge comes from Allah. The verse explains that sending Prophet Muhammad with this knowledge is an act of Allah's mercy to his people, providing them with a warner. This is significant because, for the Arabs, no messenger had come since Prophet Ishmael, their ancestor. The purpose of this warning is to give them an opportunity to reflect and be guided. This builds upon the reasons for sending a messenger mentioned previously.
Finnish: Tämä jae korostaa jälleen, että Profeetta Muhammad ei ollut läsnä Tur-vuorella, kun Allah puhui Profeetta Moosekselle. Tämän varoituksen tarkoituksena on antaa heille mahdollisuus pohdiskella ja tulla johdatetuiksi. Tämä rakentuu aiemmin mainittuihin syihin sanansaattajan lähettämiselle.

Verse 47:
وَلَوْلَا أَنْ تُصِيبَهُمْ مُصِيبَةٌ بِمَا قَدَّمَتْ أَيْدِيهِمْ فَيَقُولُوا رَبَّنَا لَوْلَا أَرْسَلْتَ إِلَيْنَا رَسُولًا فَنَتَّبِعَ آيَاتِكَ وَنَكُونَ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

This links directly to the purpose of warning mentioned in the preceding verse.

Lähettämällä Profeetan, Allah poistaa tämän tekosyyn, antaen heille täyden ja selkeän mahdollisuuden vastaanottaa sanoma ja uskoa ennen kuin kohtaavat seurauksia. Verse 48:

Finnish: Tämä jae asettaa vastakkain jakeen 47 mahdollisen anomuksen uskottomien todellisen reaktion kanssa, kun totuus Allahilta saapui Profeetta Muhammadin kautta. Verse 49:
قُلْ فَأْتُوا بِكِتَابٍ مِنْ عِنْدِ اللَّهِ هُوَ أَهْدَىٰ مِنْهُمَا أَتَّبِعْهُ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صَادِقِينَ

He states that he would follow such a book if they could truthfully present one. This challenge highlights the undisputed origin and guidance found in the Qur'an and the Torah, leaving no valid argument for rejecting them.
Finnish: Seuraten uskottomien hylkäämistä sekä Koraanista että Toorasta pelkkänä "taikuutena" jakeessa 48, tämä jae esittää heille suoran ja reilun haasteen. Hän ilmoittaa seuraavansa sellaista kirjaa, jos he voisivat sen totuudellisesti esittää. Tämä haaste korostaa Koraanin ja Tooran kiistatonta alkuperää ja ohjausta, jättäen jäljelle minkäänlaista pätevää perustetta niiden hylkäämiselle.

Verse 50:

Commentary: This verse describes the state of those who fail to respond to the clear challenge and evident truth presented in verse 49. It clarifies that their rejection isn't due to confusion or lack of evidence, but because they choose to follow their personal desires and whims instead of the guidance from Allah. The verse asks rhetorically who could be more lost than someone who abandons divine guidance for their own desires. It concludes by stating that Allah does not guide those who are intent on wrongdoing and injustice, indicating that their persistent rejection and following of desires makes them undeserving of guidance.
Finnish: Tämä jae kuvaa niiden tilaa, jotka eivät vastaa selkeään haasteeseen ja ilmeiseen totuuteen, joka esitettiin jakeessa 49. It states that all the comforts and luxuries of this worldly life are temporary and superficial. In contrast, what Allah has prepared for believers in the hereafter is far superior and eternal. The verse concludes by urging people to use their intellect and reason to understand this fundamental difference in value and make a wise choice about what to pursue. This directly addresses the worldly fears and attachment to ease mentioned in previous verses (like v. 57 and v. 58), presenting a higher, more enduring perspective.

This highlights the ultimate disparity between temporary worldly gain and eternal divine promise, urging reflection on which path is truly desirable and which leads to a fearful end.

Tämä korostaa perimmäistä eroavaisuutta väliaikaisen maallisen hyödyn ja ikuisen jumalallisen lupauksen välillä, kehottaen pohtimaan, kumpi polku on todella toivottava ja kumpi johtaa pelottavaan loppuun.

Verse 62:

Commentary: This verse depicts a scene from the Day of Judgment. This question is not because Allah does not know the answer, but to expose the futility and emptiness of their false beliefs and to humiliate them by showing that their alleged partners have no power or presence to help them on that crucial day. This marks a transition to discussing the consequences of denying Allah's oneness, a core theme related to the previous verses' discussion of guidance and worldly priorities.

This portrays their desperate attempt to absolve themselves of blame on the Day of Judgment, highlighting the breakdown of false alliances and the inability of anyone to bear the responsibility for another's sin before Allah.

Commentary: This verse was revealed at a time when Prophet Muhammad was making his journey from Makkah, the city he loved dearly, towards Madinah, having been compelled to leave his home by those who opposed him. This is a reassurance that Allah will support His messenger and the believers, ultimately ensuring their triumph over those who oppressed them. The reflection highlights this by drawing a parallel to Prophet Moses, who also returned to his land after fleeing, leading to the salvation of his people and the defeat of their oppressors. The verse then instructs the Prophet to entrust the final judgment regarding who is truly guided and who is in clear error to Allah, emphasizing that Allah knows best and will deal with each accordingly.

Verse 86:

Translation: And thou hadst not expected that the Book would be sent to thee except as a Mercy from thy Lord: Therefore lend not thou support in any way to those who reject (Allah's Message).
Commentary: Following the assurance of support in the previous verse, this verse emphasizes the nature of receiving the Qur'an itself. It explains that Prophet Muhammad had never expected or aspired to be given such a profound book and entrusted with this message. Its bestowal upon him was purely an act of Allah's mercy and grace, both upon him and upon all of humanity. This point highlights that messengerhood is a divine choice, granted by Allah to whom He wills, not something attainable through human ambition. Building on this, the verse then directly instructs the Prophet not to support those who reject Allah's message. This sets a clear boundary, emphasizing that the path of belief and the path of rejection are fundamentally different and cannot be aligned or mutually supported.

Verse 87:

Translation: And let nothing keep thee back from the Signs of Allah after they have been revealed to thee: and invite (men) to thy Lord, and be not of the company of those who join gods with Allah.
Commentary: This verse continues the instructions from the previous one, urging unwavering commitment to Allah's revelations (the "Signs of Allah"). It warns against allowing any opposition or influence to turn one away from these divine signs once they have been revealed. The commentary explains that those who oppose the message constantly try to divert believers, but believers must remain steadfast. The verse then gives a clear command: continue to invite people directly to Allah. This call should be unambiguous and solely directed towards belief in Allah, free from any other motives or interests. Finally, it gives a crucial warning: do not be among those who associate partners with Allah. This warning introduces a core principle of Islamic faith – the absolute oneness of Allah – which is further elaborated in the next verse.

Verse 88:
وَلَا تَدْعُ مَعَ اللَّهِ إِلَٰهًا آخَرَ ۘ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ كُلُّ شَيْءٍ هَالِكٌ إِلَّا وَجْهَهُ ۚ لَهُ الْحُكْمُ وَإِلَيْهِ تُرْجَعُونَ
Translation: And call not, besides Allah, on another god. There is no god but He. Everything (that exists) will perish except His own Face. To Him belongs the Command, and to Him will ye (all) be brought back.
Commentary: This verse reiterates the command from the end of the previous verse, emphasizing the prohibition against calling upon any deity alongside Allah. It then states the fundamental declaration of faith: "There is no god but He." This affirms the absolute oneness of Allah and His exclusive right to worship and obedience. The verse continues by declaring that "Everything (that exists) will perish except His own Face." This means that all of creation – wealth, power, life, the universe itself – is temporary and subject to decay and destruction. Only Allah, the Ever-Living, remains. In Islamic belief, attributes like "His own Face" are affirmed as they appear in the Qur'an, without likening Allah to His creation or attempting to explain how they occur. Rather, they reflect His majesty and uniqueness in a way beyond human comprehension. Muslims accept Allah’s attributes as revealed, without interpreting them metaphorically or anthropomorphically. The verse concludes by stating, "To Him belongs the Command, and to Him will you all be brought back." This signifies that all authority, judgment, and ultimate destiny rest solely with Allah. There is no escaping His decree or the final return to Him for accountability. This serves as a powerful conclusion, reinforcing the central themes of Allah's absolute oneness, His ultimate authority, and the reality of the return to Him, providing comfort and direction for those who uphold His message.
